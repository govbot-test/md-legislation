Section 1:
WES MOORE, Governor Ch. 434
Chapter 434
AN ACT concerning
3–1–1 Systems – Expansion Program and Oversight Board – Establishment
FOR the purpose of establishing the Maryland 3–1–1 Oversight Board in the Maryland
Information Network; establishing the 3–1–1 Program to use certain artificial
intelligence to answer certain questions and route certain calls in certain counties;
requiring the expansion of creation and implementation of a plan to expand the
3–1–1 Program to all of the counties in Maryland on or before a certain date;
establishing the 3–1–1 Technical Advisory Committee; and generally relating to
3–1–1 systems.
BY adding to
Article – Public Safety

Section 2:

(2022 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

SUBTITLE 3A. 3–1–1 NONEMERGENCY COMMUNITY INFORMATION AND REFERRAL
SYSTEMS.
1–3A–01.
(A) IN THIS SUBTITLE THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
INDICATED.
(B) “ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 3.5–801
OF THE STATE FINANCE AND PROCUREMENT ARTICLE.

(C) “BOARD” MEANS THE MARYLAND 3–1–1 OVERSIGHT BOARD
ESTABLISHED UNDER § 1–3A–02 OF THIS SUBTITLE.
(D) “CHATBOT” MEANS A COMPUTER PROGRAM AVAILABLE THROUGH A
PHONE NUMBER, A WEBSITE, OR AN APPLICATION THAT SIMULATES HUMAN
CONVERSATION THROUGH TEXT OR VOICE, USING ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE TO
RESPOND TO TYPED QUESTIONS USING INFORMATION FROM CURATED,

ISSUES ON A NONEMERGENCY BASIS.
(G) “VOICEBOT” MEANS A COMPUTER PROGRAM AVAILABLE THROUGH A
PHONE NUMBER THAT SIMULATES HUMAN CONVERSATION USING ARTIFICIAL
INTELLIGENCE TECHNOLOGY THAT:
(1) RESPONDS TO VOCAL QUESTIONS OVER THE PHONE USING
INFORMATION FROM CURATED, AGENCY–APPROVED SOURCES SUCH AS
GOVERNMENT WEBSITES, SERVICE CATALOGS, ORDINANCES, FORMS, AND
GEOGRAPHIC INFORMATION SYSTEM LAYERS; AND
(2) ROUTES CALLS TO APPROPRIATE DESTINATIONS.
1–3A–02.
(A) THERE IS A MARYLAND 3–1–1 OVERSIGHT BOARD IN THE MARYLAND
INFORMATION NETWORK.
(B) THE BOARD CONSISTS OF:
(1) TWO MEMBERS OF THE SENATE OF MARYLAND, APPOINTED BY
THE PRESIDENT OF THE SENATE;

Section 9:
3.5–801.
(a) In this subtitle the following words have the meanings indicated.
(c) “Artificial intelligence” means a machine–based system that:
(1) can, for a given set of human–defined objectives, make predictions,
recommendations, or decisions influencing real or virtual environments;
(2) uses machine and human–based inputs to perceive real and virtual
environments and abstracts those perceptions into models through analysis in an
automated manner; and
(3) uses model inference to formulate options for information or action.

Section 10:
SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That:
(a) There is a 3–1–1 Technical Advisory Committee.
(b) The Committee consists of the following members:
(1) one representative of a county that operates an existing 3–1–1 system,
designated by the Executive Director of the Maryland Association of Counties;
(2) one representative of a county that does not operate a 3–1–1 system,
designated by the Executive Director of the Maryland Association of Counties;
(3) one 9–1–1 Public Safety Answering Point Director, designated by the
Secretary of Emergency Management, with technical expertise from a county that operates
an existing 3–1–1 system, or their designee;
(4) one 9–1–1 Public Safety Answering Point Director, designated by the
Secretary of Emergency Management, with technical expertise from a county that does not
operate a 3–1–1 system, or their designee;
– 9 –
Ch. 434 2026 LAWS OF MARYLAND
(5) one geographic information systems expert who is not serving on the
Maryland 3–1–1 Oversight Board, appointed by the Secretary of Information Technology;
and
(6) the following members appointed by the Governor:
(i) one data interoperability expert; and
(ii) one expert in artificial intelligence applications in public service
deliveries.
(c) A member of the Committee:
(1) may not receive compensation as a member of the Committee; but
(2) is entitled to reimbursement for expenses under the Standard State
Travel Regulations, as provided in the State budget.
(d) The Committee shall provide technical guidance to the Maryland 3–1–1
Oversight Board and the 3–1–1 Program to avoid negative unintended consequences
related to interoperability among Maryland 3–1–1 voicebots and chatbots and existing
3–1–1, 2–1–1, 9–1–1, 9–8–8, and local nonemergency public safety systems.

Section 11:
SECTION 2. 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
July 1, 2026. Section 2 of this Act shall remain effective for a period of 2 years and 3 months
and, at the end of September 30, 2028, Section 2 of this Act, with no further action required
by the General Assembly, shall be abrogated and of no further force and effect.
Approved by the Governor, May 12, 2026.
