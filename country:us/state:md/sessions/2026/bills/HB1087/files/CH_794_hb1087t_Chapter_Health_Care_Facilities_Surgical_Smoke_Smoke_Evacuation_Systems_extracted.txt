Section 1:
WES MOORE, Governor Ch. 794
Chapter 794
AN ACT concerning
Health Care Facilities – Surgical Smoke – Smoke Evacuation Systems
FOR the purpose of requiring health care facilities that perform surgical procedures to
adopt and implement policies that require the use of a smoke evacuation system
during a surgical procedure that may generate surgical smoke; and generally
relating to smoke evacuation systems in health care facilities.
BY adding to
Article – Health – General

Section 2:
Section 19–1001 and 19–1002 to be under the new subtitle “Subtitle 10. Surgical
Smoke Evacuation Systems”
Annotated Code of Maryland
(2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

Section 3:
SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
Article – Health – General
SUBTITLE 10. SURGICAL SMOKE EVACUATION SYSTEMS.
19–1001.
(A) IN THIS SUBTITLE THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
INDICATED.
(B) “HEALTH CARE FACILITY” MEANS:
(1) A HOSPITAL, AS DEFINED IN § 19–301 OF THIS TITLE;
(2) A LIMITED SERVICE HOSPITAL, AS DEFINED IN § 19–301 OF THIS
TITLE;
(3) A RELATED INSTITUTION, AS DEFINED IN § 19–301 OF THIS TITLE;
(4) AN AMBULATORY SURGICAL FACILITY; OR
(5) A FREESTANDING MEDICAL FACILITY, AS DEFINED IN § 19–3A–01
OF THIS TITLE.
– 1 –
Ch. 794 2026 LAWS OF MARYLAND
(C) “SMOKE EVACUATION SYSTEM” MEANS EQUIPMENT THAT EFFECTIVELY
CAPTURES AND FILTERS SURGICAL SMOKE AT THE SITE OF ORIGIN BEFORE THE
SURGICAL SMOKE MAKES CONTACT WITH THE EYES OR RESPIRATORY TRACT OF
OCCUPANTS IN THE ROOM.
(D) “SURGICAL SMOKE” MEANS THE GASEOUS BY–PRODUCT PRODUCED BY
ENERGY–GENERATING DEVICES, INCLUDING SURGICAL PLUME, SMOKE PLUME,
BIO–AEROSOLS, LASER–GENERATED AIRBORNE CONTAMINANTS, OR
LUNG–DAMAGING DUST.
19–1002.
BY JANUARY 1, 2028, EACH HEALTH CARE FACILITY THAT PERFORMS
SURGICAL PROCEDURES SHALL ADOPT AND IMPLEMENT POLICIES THAT REQUIRE
THE USE OF A SMOKE EVACUATION SYSTEM DURING A SURGICAL PROCEDURE THAT
MAY GENERATE SURGICAL SMOKE.

Section 4:
SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
October 1, 2026.
Approved by the Governor, May 26, 2026.
