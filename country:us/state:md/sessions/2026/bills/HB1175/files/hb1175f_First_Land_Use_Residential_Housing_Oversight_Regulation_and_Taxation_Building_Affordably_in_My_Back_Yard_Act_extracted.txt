A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Land Use – Residential Housing – Oversight, Regulation, and Taxation
3 (Building Affordably in My Back Yard Act)
4 FOR the purpose of establishing certain policies and processes to facilitate the development
5 of residential housing in the State; requiring an entity that owns residential real
6 property in the State to make a certain certification to the Department of Housing
7 and Community Development; authorizing a local jurisdiction to establish an
8 administrative process to review, approve, modify, or deny certain housing
9 development project applications; providing for the establishment of certain housing
10 production targets; requiring certain local jurisdictions and the Commission to adopt
11 local project design guidelines for certain qualified affordable housing projects;
12 requiring local jurisdictions and the Commission to implement certain preapproval
13 building permit application processes for standardized model home designs;
14 authorizing a county to reduce the fee or tax rate of certain housing–sensitive taxes
15 or fees imposed on a qualified affordable housing project; authorizing a county to
16 increase the fee or tax rate of certain housing–sensitive taxes or fees on classes of
17 property that are not part of a qualified affordable housing project; limiting the
18 entities from which a certain person may accept offers on residential real property
19 during a certain period of time; authorizing a county, after making a certain
20 determination, to impose a transfer tax on the transfer of certain owner–occupied
21 properties; authorizing the Mayor and City Council of Baltimore City or the
22 governing body of a county to establish, by law, certain subclasses of real property
23 and to set a special property tax rate for the subclasses of real property; requiring
24 the Comptroller, in consultation with the State Department of Assessments and
25 Taxation, to evaluate and report on disincentives to homeowners to downsize or
26 transition to smaller homes; requiring the Department of Planning to conduct a
27 study and publish a comprehensive report on housing infrastructure; requiring
28 certain departments to conduct a comprehensive internal review of certain approval
29 processes that impact housing; and generally relating to the oversight, regulation,
30 development, and taxation of residential real estate.
