WES MOORE, Governor Ch. 164
Chapter 164
(Senate Bill 523)
AN ACT concerning
Department of the Environment Natural Resources – Water Resources –
Protection Identification of Vernal Pools
(Jack Cover Vernal Pools Act of 2026)
(Vernal Pool Wetlands Protection Act of 2026)
FOR the purpose of requiring the Department of the Environment to adopt regulations in
a certain manner Natural Resources in coordination with the Department of the
Environment to identify and protect certain vernal pools in the State in a certain
manner; requiring the Department, when issuing a certain permit, to protect certain
vernal pools that are located within the geographic area covered by the permit of
Natural Resources to create and maintain a certain list of vernal pools in the State
in a certain manner; requiring the Department of the Environment to designate staff
to receive training in vernal pool identification and to notify the Department of
Natural Resources under certain circumstances; and generally relating to the
regulatory protection of water resources and identification of vernal pools in the
State.

Preamble
WHEREAS, Vernal pools are unique, small–scale ecosystems that contribute
significantly to the biodiversity of Maryland by providing habitat for species that cannot
reproduce in alternative habitats, including the wood frog, mole salamanders, and fairy
shrimp; and
WHEREAS, Vernal pools provide a significant amount of ecosystem services as they
occur frequently throughout Maryland and have the capacity to replenish groundwater and
filter stormwater flows; and

WHEREAS, Nontidal wetlands that meet the three federal wetland delineation
criteria of hydrology, hydric soils, and hydrophytic vegetation, including vernal pools that
satisfy those criteria, are regulated and protected by the State; and
WHEREAS, Vernal pools that do not meet all three federal wetland delineation
criteria are not afforded the regulatory protections applicable to nontidal wetlands in the
State and may suffer impairments or losses without regulatory review or approval; now,
therefore,
SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
Article – Environment
5–205.
(A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
INDICATED.
(2) “AGRICULTURAL ACTIVITY” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN §
5–901 OF THIS TITLE.
(3) “FORESTRY ACTIVITY” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 5–901 OF
THIS TITLE.
(4) “NONTIDAL WETLAND” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 5–901 OF
THIS TITLE.
(5) “OBLIGATE SPECIES” MEANS AN ANIMAL SPECIES THAT DEPENDS
ON VERNAL POOLS AS A NECESSARY PART OF ITS LIFE CYCLE.
(6) “QUALIFIED VERNAL POOL” MEANS A CONFINED DEPRESSION
THAT:
(I) HAS SURFACE WATER FOR PART OF THE YEAR;
(II) PROVIDES A PERSISTENT OR BREEDING HABITAT FOR
OBLIGATE SPECIES, INCLUDING NATIVE AMPHIBIANS AND INVERTEBRATES; AND
(III) DOES NOT MEET THE CRITERIA FOR PROTECTION AS A
NONTIDAL WETLAND UNDER FEDERAL OR STATE LAW, INCLUDING THE FEDERAL
WETLAND DELINEATION CRITERIA OF HYDROLOGY, HYDRIC SOILS, AND
HYDROPHYTIC VEGETATION.

(7) (I) “REGULATED ACTIVITY” MEANS ANY OF THE FOLLOWING
ACTIVITIES IN A QUALIFIED VERNAL POOL OR WITHIN A BUFFER AROUND A
QUALIFIED VERNAL POOL:
1. THE REMOVAL, EXCAVATION, OR DREDGING OF SOIL,
SAND, GRAVEL, MINERALS, ORGANIC MATTER, OR MATERIALS OF ANY KIND;
2. THE CHANGING OF EXISTING DRAINAGE
CHARACTERISTICS, SEDIMENTATION PATTERNS, FLOW PATTERNS, OR FLOOD
RETENTION CHARACTERISTICS;
3. THE DISTURBANCE OF THE WATER LEVEL OR WATER
TABLE BY DRAINAGE, IMPOUNDMENT, OR OTHER MEANS;
4. THE DUMPING, DISCHARGING OF MATERIAL, OR
FILLING WITH MATERIAL, INCLUDING THE DRIVING OF PILES AND PLACING OF
OBSTRUCTIONS;
5. THE GRADING OR REMOVAL OF MATERIAL THAT
WOULD ALTER EXISTING TOPOGRAPHY; AND
6. THE DESTRUCTION OR REMOVAL OF PLANT LIFE
THAT WOULD ALTER THE CHARACTER OF A QUALIFIED VERNAL POOL.
(II) “REGULATED ACTIVITY” DOES NOT INCLUDE AN
AGRICULTURAL ACTIVITY OR FORESTRY ACTIVITY AS DEFINED IN THIS SECTION.
(B) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ADOPT REGULATIONS TO:
(1) ESTABLISH AND MAINTAIN A LIST OF QUALIFIED VERNAL POOLS
IN THE STATE;
(2) ESTABLISH PROTECTIONS RELATED TO THE PRESERVATION OF
QUALIFIED VERNAL POOLS IN THE STATE;
(3) ESTABLISH PROTECTIONS FOR BUFFERS AROUND QUALIFIED
VERNAL POOLS IN THE STATE; AND
(4) REQUIRE THAT A PERSON CONDUCTING A REGULATED ACTIVITY
RESULTING IN IMPAIRMENTS OR LOSSES TO A QUALIFIED VERNAL POOL OR A
BUFFER AROUND A QUALIFIED VERNAL POOL:

(I) AVOID IMPAIRMENTS AND LOSSES TO THE MAXIMUM
EXTENT PRACTICABLE;
(II) MINIMIZE IMPAIRMENTS AND LOSSES TO THE MAXIMUM
EXTENT PRACTICABLE WHERE AVOIDANCE IS NOT POSSIBLE; AND
(III) PROVIDE COMPENSATION OR OTHER MITIGATION AS
DETERMINED BY THE DEPARTMENT FOR ANY REMAINING UNAVOIDABLE
IMPAIRMENTS AND LOSSES.
(C) THE REGULATIONS ADOPTED UNDER SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION
SHALL APPLY ONLY TO QUALIFIED VERNAL POOLS THAT HAVE BEEN IDENTIFIED OR
DOCUMENTED BY:
(1) THE DEPARTMENT;
(2) A PERSON USING IDENTIFICATION METHODS APPROVED BY THE
DEPARTMENT, INCLUDING MAPPING OR OTHER SCIENTIFIC INFORMATION; OR
(3) A CERTIFIED WETLAND DELINEATOR DURING A SITE ASSESSMENT
CONDUCTED BEFORE THE ISSUANCE OF A PERMIT FOR A REGULATED ACTIVITY.
(D) (1) IN DEVELOPING THE REGULATIONS UNDER SUBSECTION (B) OF
THIS SECTION, THE DEPARTMENT SHALL, TO THE EXTENT PRACTICABLE:
(I) ENSURE THAT THE BUFFER SPECIFICATIONS FOR
QUALIFIED VERNAL POOLS BE CONSISTENT WITH THE BUFFER SPECIFICATIONS FOR
NONTIDAL WETLANDS UNDER § 5–906 OF THIS TITLE; AND
(II) ENSURE THAT THE COMPENSATION AND MITIGATION
REQUIREMENTS FOR IMPAIRMENTS AND LOSSES WITHIN QUALIFIED VERNAL POOLS
BE CONSISTENT WITH THE COMPENSATION AND MITIGATION REQUIREMENTS FOR
IMPAIRMENTS AND LOSSES WITHIN NONTIDAL WETLANDS UNDER §§ 5–909 AND
5–910 OF THIS TITLE.
(2) IN ADDITION TO THE REQUIREMENTS UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF
THIS SUBSECTION, THE DEPARTMENT MAY:
(I) USE MAPPING OR OTHER SCIENTIFIC INFORMATION TO
IDENTIFY ADDITIONAL QUALIFIED VERNAL POOLS FOR INCLUSION ON THE
DEPARTMENT’S LIST OF QUALIFIED VERNAL POOLS; AND

5–901.
(a) In this subtitle the following words have the meanings indicated.
(b) (1) “Agricultural activity” means aquaculture and farming activities.
(2) “Agricultural activity” includes:
(i) Plowing, tillage, cropping, seeding, cultivating, and harvesting
for the production of food and fiber products; and
(ii) The grazing of livestock.
(f) “Forestry activity” means planting, cultivating, thinning, harvesting, or any
other activity undertaken to use forest resources or to improve their quality or productivity.
(m) (1) “Nontidal wetland” means an area that is inundated or saturated by
surface water or groundwater at a frequency and duration sufficient to support, and that
under normal circumstances does support, a prevalence of vegetation typically adapted for
life in saturated soil conditions, commonly known as hydrophytic vegetation.
(2) The determination of whether an area is a nontidal wetland shall be
made in accordance with the publication known as the “Federal Manual for Identifying and
Delineating Jurisdictional Wetlands”, published in 1989 and as may be amended.
(3) “Nontidal wetlands” do not include tidal wetlands regulated under Title
16 of this article.
Article – Natural Resources
1–1002.

(A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
INDICATED.
(2) “FACULTATIVE SPECIES” MEANS AN ANIMAL SPECIES THAT USES
VERNAL POOLS IRREGULARLY.
(3) “OBLIGATE SPECIES” MEANS AN ANIMAL SPECIES THAT DEPENDS
ON VERNAL POOLS AS A NECESSARY PART OF ITS LIFE CYCLE.
(4) “TYPICAL YEAR” MEANS A YEAR IN WHICH RAINFALL TOTALS
MEET OR EXCEED THE AVERAGE YEARLY RAINFALL FROM THE PREVIOUS 30 YEARS
FOR THE LOCATION WITHIN WHICH THE VERNAL POOL IS LOCATED.
(5) “VERNAL POOL” MEANS A CONFINED DEPRESSION THAT:
(I) HAS SURFACE WATER FOR AT LEAST 2 CONSECUTIVE
MONTHS BETWEEN NOVEMBER AND MAY IN A TYPICAL YEAR;
(II) EXPERIENCES A DECREASE IN WATER LEVEL TO 6 INCHES
OR LESS BETWEEN JUNE AND SEPTEMBER IN A TYPICAL YEAR;
(III) PROVIDES A PERSISTENT OR BREEDING HABITAT FOR
VARIOUS ANIMAL SPECIES, INCLUDING NATIVE AMPHIBIANS AND INVERTEBRATES
THAT ARE FACULTATIVE SPECIES OR OBLIGATE SPECIES;
(IV) CONTAINS LESS THAN 30% AERIAL COVER OF EMERGENT
VEGETATION WHEN FULL;
(V) IS PREDOMINANTLY OR PARTIALLY SHADED BY WOODY
VEGETATION;
(VI) IS LESS THAN 5 ACRES IN SIZE; AND
(VII) MAY HAVE INTERMITTENT, TEMPORARY CONNECTIONS TO
OTHER BODIES OF WATER.
(B) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL:
(1) IN COORDINATION WITH THE DEPARTMENT OF THE
ENVIRONMENT, CREATE AND MAINTAIN A LIST OF VERNAL POOLS IN THE STATE;
(2) ESTABLISH CRITERIA AND PROCEDURES FOR IDENTIFYING AND
INCLUDING VERNAL POOLS ON THE LIST, INCLUDING:

(I) VERNAL POOLS IDENTIFIED BY THE DEPARTMENT IN
CARRYING OUT ITS DUTIES SET FORTH IN THIS ARTICLE;
(II) LOCATIONS THE DEPARTMENT DETERMINES MEET THE
DEFINITION OF A VERNAL POOL UNDER SUBSECTION (A) OF THIS SECTION; AND
(III) VERNAL POOLS IDENTIFIED BY THE DEPARTMENT OF THE
ENVIRONMENT IN ACCORDANCE WITH SUBSECTION (C) OF THIS SECTION;
(3) AUTHORIZE:
(I) USING MAPPING OR OTHER SCIENTIFIC INFORMATION TO
IDENTIFY ADDITIONAL VERNAL POOLS FOR INCLUSION ON THE LIST; AND
(II) CONSULTING WITH ANY STAKEHOLDER GROUP AS
APPROPRIATE, INCLUDING THE DEPARTMENT OF THE ENVIRONMENT AND THE
UNIVERSITY OF MARYLAND, TO IDENTIFY VERNAL POOLS FOR INCLUSION ON THE
LIST; AND
(4) PROVIDE FOR PUBLICATION OF THE LIST OF VERNAL POOLS ON
THE DEPARTMENT’S WEBSITE.
(C) (1) THE DEPARTMENT OF THE ENVIRONMENT SHALL DESIGNATE
STAFF TO RECEIVE TRAINING IN VERNAL POOL IDENTIFICATION.
(2) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL PROVIDE VERNAL POOL
IDENTIFICATION TRAINING TO THE STAFF DESIGNATED UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF
THIS SUBSECTION.
(3) IF, IN CARRYING OUT RESPONSIBILITIES UNDER TITLE 5,
SUBTITLE 9 OF THE ENVIRONMENT ARTICLE, STAFF TRAINED UNDER THIS
SUBSECTION IDENTIFY A LOCATION THAT MAY QUALIFY AS A VERNAL POOL, THE
DEPARTMENT OF THE ENVIRONMENT SHALL NOTIFY THE DEPARTMENT OF THE
LOCATION FOR POSSIBLE INCLUSION ON THE LIST ESTABLISHED UNDER
SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION.
(4) NOTHING IN THIS SECTION SHALL BE CONSTRUED TO LIMIT THE
AUTHORITY OF THE DEPARTMENT OF THE ENVIRONMENT TO REGULATE VERNAL
POOLS THAT MEET THE DEFINITION OF NONTIDAL WETLANDS UNDER TITLE 5,
SUBTITLE 9 OF THE ENVIRONMENT ARTICLE.
SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
October 1, 2026.
