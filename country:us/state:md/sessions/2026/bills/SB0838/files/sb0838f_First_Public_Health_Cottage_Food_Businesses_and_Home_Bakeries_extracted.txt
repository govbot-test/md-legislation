A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Public Health – Cottage Food Businesses and Home Bakeries
3 FOR the purpose of altering the definition of “cottage food product” for purposes of
4 provisions of law governing the sale of cottage food products to include nonpotentially
5 hazardous foods, rather than nonhazardous foods, as specified in regulations adopted
6 by the Maryland Department of Health; providing that a home bakery is not
7 required, under certain circumstances, to be licensed by the Maryland Department
8 of Health; applying certain provisions of law governing cottage food businesses to
9 home bakeries; and generally relating to cottage food business and home bakeries
10 products.
11 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
12 Article – Health – General
13 Section 21–301(a)
14 Annotated Code of Maryland
15 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
16 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
17 Article – Health – General
18 Section 21–301(b–2), (g)(3), and (h)(3) and 21–330.1
19 Annotated Code of Maryland
20 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
21 BY adding to
22 Article – Health – General
23 Section 21–301(h–1) and (h–2)
24 Annotated Code of Maryland
25 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
26 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
27 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

3 (a) In this subtitle the following words have the meanings indicated.
4 (b–2) “Cottage food product” means a [nonhazardous] NONPOTENTIALLY
5 HAZARDOUS food, as specified in regulations adopted by the Department, that is sold in
6 the State in accordance with § 21–330.1 of this subtitle and regulations adopted by the
7 Department:
8 (1) Directly to a consumer from a residence, at a farmer’s market, at a
9 public event, by personal delivery, or by mail delivery; or
10 (2) To a retail food store, including a grocery store, or a food cooperative.
11 (g) (3) “Food processing plant” does not include:
12 (i) A warehouse or distribution center that:
13 1. Does not process food; and
14 2. Stores only sealed containers of whole bean, ground or
15 instant coffee, leaf or instant teas, nondairy dehydrated whiteners, sugar, or sugar–free
16 sweeteners; [or]
17 (ii) A cottage food business; OR
18 (III) A HOME BAKERY.
19 (h) (3) “Food service facility” does not include:
20 (i) A kitchen in a private home where food is prepared at no charge
21 for guests in the home, for guests at a social gathering, or for service to unemployed,
22 homeless, or other disadvantaged populations;
23 (ii) A food preparation or serving area where food is prepared or
24 served only by an excluded organization;
25 (iii) A location in a farmer’s market or at a public festival or event
26 where raw agricultural products, as defined in § 21–304(d)(1)(iii) of this subtitle, are sold;
27 [or]
28 (iv) A cottage food business; OR

6 21–330.1 OF THIS SUBTITLE AND REGULATIONS ADOPTED BY THE DEPARTMENT;

15 PARTIALLY OR COMPLETELY BAKED; AND

24 (a) This section does not:
25 (1) Apply to a food establishment that is required to have a license under §
26 21–305 of this subtitle; or
27 (2) Exempt a cottage food business OR HOME BAKERY from any applicable
28 State or federal tax laws.

1 (b) A cottage food business OR HOME BAKERY is not required to be licensed by

2 PRODUCT was made.
3 (d) At the request of a cottage food business OR HOME BAKERY, the Department
4 shall provide to the cottage food business OR HOME BAKERY a unique identification
5 number that the cottage food business OR HOME BAKERY may use on the label of a cottage
6 food product OR HOME BAKERY PRODUCT under subsection (c) of this section.
7 (e) The owner of a cottage food business OR HOME BAKERY shall comply with all
8 applicable county and municipal laws and ordinances regulating the preparation,
9 processing, storage, and sale of cottage food products OR HOME BAKERY PRODUCTS.
10 (f) (1) The Department may investigate any complaint alleging that a cottage
11 food business OR HOME BAKERY has violated this section.
12 (2) On receipt of a complaint, a representative of the Department, at a
13 reasonable time, may enter and inspect the premises of a cottage food business OR HOME
14 BAKERY to determine compliance with this section.
15 (3) The owner of a cottage food business OR HOME BAKERY may not:
16 (i) Refuse to grant access to a representative who requests to enter
17 and inspect the premises of the cottage food business OR HOME BAKERY under paragraph
18 (2) of this subsection; or
19 (ii) Interfere with any inspection under paragraph (2) of this
20 subsection.
21 (4) An investigation of a cottage food business OR HOME BAKERY
22 conducted under this subsection may include sampling of a cottage food product OR HOME
23 BAKERY PRODUCT to determine if the cottage food product OR HOME BAKERY PRODUCT
24 is misbranded or adulterated.
25 (g) Before the owner of a cottage food business OR HOME BAKERY may sell a
26 cottage food product OR HOME BAKERY PRODUCT to a retail food store, the owner shall
27 submit to the Department:
28 (1) Documentation of the owner’s successful completion of a food safety
29 course approved by the Department; and
30 (2) The label that will be affixed to the cottage food product OR HOME
31 BAKERY PRODUCT in accordance with subsection (c)(2) of this section.
32 (h) Beginning on or before December 30, 2020, and every December 30 thereafter,
33 the Department shall report to the Senate Finance Committee and the House Health [and

1 Government Operations] Committee, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government
2 Article, on:
3 (1) The documentation and labels submitted under subsection (f) of this
4 section; and
5 (2) Any complaints received by the Department related to a cottage food
6 business [or], cottage food product, HOME BAKERY, OR HOME BAKERY PRODUCT.
7 (i) The Department shall adopt regulations to carry out this section.
8 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
9 October 1, 2026.
