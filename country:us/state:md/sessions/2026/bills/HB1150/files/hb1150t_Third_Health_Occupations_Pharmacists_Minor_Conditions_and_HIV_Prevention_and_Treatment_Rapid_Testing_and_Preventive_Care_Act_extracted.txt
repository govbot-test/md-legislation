1 AN ACT concerning
2 Health Occupations – Pharmacists – Minor Conditions and HIV Prevention and
3 Treatment
4 (Rapid Testing and Preventive Care Act)
5 FOR the purpose of authorizing pharmacists, under certain circumstances, to prescribe,
6 administer, and dispense certain drugs or devices and perform and interpret certain
7 laboratory tests; repealing provisions establishing and governing the
8 Nonoccupational Postexposure Prophylaxis (nPEP) Standing Order Program;
9 authorizing the Maryland Department of Health to establish certain statewide
10 written protocols relating to the treatment of minor conditions and the prevention
11 and treatment of human immunodeficiency virus; and generally relating to the scope
12 of practice of pharmacists.
13 BY repealing
14 Article – Health – General
15 Section 13–5301 through 13–5303 and the subtitle “Subtitle 53. Nonoccupational
16 Postexposure Prophylaxis (nPEP) Standing Order Program”
17 Annotated Code of Maryland
18 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
19 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
20 Article – Health Occupations
21 Section 12–101(a)

20 12–101.
21 (a) In this title the following words have the meanings indicated.
22 (x) (1) “Practice pharmacy” means to engage in any of the following activities:
23 (i) Providing pharmaceutical care;

25 devices;
26 (iii) Compounding or dispensing nonprescription drugs or devices;
27 (iv) Monitoring prescriptions for prescription and nonprescription
28 drugs or devices;
29 (v) Providing information, explanation, or recommendations to
30 patients and health care practitioners about the safe and effective use of prescription or
31 nonprescription drugs or devices;
32 (vi) Identifying and appraising problems concerning the use or
33 monitoring of therapy with drugs or devices;

5 § 12–509 of this title;

1 THAT MAY GUIDE CLINICAL DECISION MAKING THAT IS CLASSIFIED AS WAIVED

14 TECHNICIAN TRAINING PROGRAM UNDER THE SUPERVISION OF THE PHARMACIST
15 REGISTERED PHARMACY INTERN.
16 12–516.
17 (A) THE DEPARTMENT MAY ESTABLISH A STATEWIDE WRITTEN PROTOCOL
18 FOR THE DELIVERY OF PRE–EXPOSURE PROPHYLAXIS AND POSTEXPOSURE
19 PROPHYLAXIS MEDICATIONS FOR THE PREVENTION AND TREATMENT OF HUMAN
20 IMMUNODEFICIENCY VIRUS.
21 (B) IN ACCORDANCE WITH THE STATEWIDE WRITTEN PROTOCOL
22 ESTABLISHED UNDER SUBSECTION (A) OF THIS SECTION, A PHARMACIST MAY:
23 (1) PERFORM AND INTERPRET LABORATORY TESTS;
24 (2) CONDUCT ASSESSMENTS AND CONSULTATIONS;
25 (3) INITIATE, DISPENSE, OR ADMINISTER:
26 (I) PRE–EXPOSURE PROPHYLAXIS MEDICATIONS; OR
27 (II) POSTEXPOSURE PROPHYLAXIS MEDICATIONS; AND
28 (4) PROVIDE REFERRALS FOR ADDITIONAL CARE.
