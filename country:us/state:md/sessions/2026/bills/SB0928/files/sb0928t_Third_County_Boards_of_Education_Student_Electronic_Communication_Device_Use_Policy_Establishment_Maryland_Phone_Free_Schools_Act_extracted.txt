1 AN ACT concerning
2 County Boards of Education – Student Electronic Communication Device Use
3 Policy – Establishment
4 (Maryland Phone–Free Schools Act)
5 FOR the purpose of requiring each county board of education to develop and implement,
6 not later than a certain school year, a policy limiting prohibiting the use of an
7 electronic communication device by a student during the school day except under
8 certain circumstances; and generally relating to county boards of education and a
9 student electronic communication device use policy.
10 BY adding to
11 Article – Education
12 Section 7–137
13 Annotated Code of Maryland

1 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
2 INDICATED.
3 (2) “ACADEMIC SCHOOL DAY” MEANS ANY STRUCTURED OR
4 UNSTRUCTURED LEARNING EXPERIENCE THAT OCCURS FROM WHEN THE FIRST
5 BELL RINGS AT THE START OF THE SCHOOL DAY UNTIL THE DISMISSAL BELL RINGS
6 AT THE END OF THE SCHOOL DAY, INCLUDING INSTRUCTIONAL PERIODS, LUNCH,
7 RECESS, PASSING PERIODS, AND TRANSITIONAL PERIODS.
8 (3) (I) “ELECTRONIC COMMUNICATION DEVICE” MEANS ANY
9 PORTABLE ELECTRONIC DEVICE CARRIED OR TRANSPORTED BY A STUDENT TO
10 RECEIVE, SEND, OR RECORD VOICE, IMAGE, OR TEXT CONTENT, INCLUDING CELL
11 PHONES, TABLETS, LAPTOPS, SMART WATCHES, WIRELESS EARBUDS OR
12 HEADPHONES, GEOLOCATION DEVICES, AND ANY OTHER DEVICE WITH
13 COMMUNICATION FUNCTIONS.
14 (II) “ELECTRONIC COMMUNICATION DEVICE” DOES NOT
15 INCLUDE AN ELECTRONIC COMMUNICATION DEVICE PROVIDED BY A COUNTY BOARD
16 FOR INSTRUCTIONAL PURPOSES.
17 (4) “SOCIAL MEDIA PLATFORM” MEANS AN INTERNET–BASED
18 APPLICATION, WEBSITE, OR DIGITAL SERVICE THAT ENABLES USERS TO CREATE,
19 SHARE, OR VIEW CONTENT OR TO INTERACT WITH OTHER USERS THROUGH
20 PROFILES, ACCOUNTS, FEEDS, MESSAGING, COMMENTS, REACTIONS, OR SIMILAR
21 SOCIAL FEATURES, AND THAT IS PRIMARILY DESIGNED TO FACILITATE SOCIAL
22 INTERACTION, USER–GENERATED CONTENT, OR CONTENT DISTRIBUTION TO A
23 BROAD OR SEMI–PUBLIC AUDIENCE.
24 (4) (5) “STORE” MEANS TO SECURE IN A MANNER DETERMINED BY
25 THE COUNTY BOARD TO ENSURE THE ELECTRONIC COMMUNICATION DEVICE IS NOT
26 CARRIED ON THE STUDENT’S PERSON DURING THE ACADEMIC SCHOOL DAY.
27 (B) NOT LATER THAN THE 2027–2028 SCHOOL YEAR, EACH COUNTY BOARD
28 SHALL DEVELOP AND IMPLEMENT A POLICY TO LIMIT PROHIBIT THE USE OF
29 ELECTRONIC COMMUNICATION DEVICES BY STUDENTS DURING THE ACADEMIC
30 SCHOOL DAY.
31 (C) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN SUBSECTION (D) OF THIS SECTION, THE
32 POLICY DEVELOPED UNDER THIS SECTION SHALL:
33 (1) PROHIBIT A STUDENT FROM USING AN ELECTRONIC
34 COMMUNICATION DEVICE DURING THE ACADEMIC SCHOOL DAY;

1 (2) REQUIRE A STUDENT TO STORE THE STUDENT’S ELECTRONIC
2 COMMUNICATION DEVICE DURING THE ACADEMIC SCHOOL DAY;
3 (3) PROHIBIT A STUDENT FROM USING THE SOCIAL MEDIA
4 APPLICATIONS AND SOCIAL MEDIA WEBSITES DETERMINED BY THE COUNTY BOARD
5 ACCESSING OR USING A SOCIAL MEDIA PLATFORM DURING THE ACADEMIC SCHOOL
6 DAY; AND
7 (4) REQUIRE ADMINISTRATOR APPROVAL FOR THE USE OF A
8 PERSONAL ELECTRONIC COMMUNICATION DEVICE FOR EDUCATIONAL PURPOSES
9 WHEN A SCHOOL–ISSUED ELECTRONIC COMMUNICATION DEVICE IS UNAVAILABLE.
10 (D) THE POLICY DEVELOPED UNDER THIS SECTION MAY NOT PROHIBIT A
11 STUDENT FROM USING AN ELECTRONIC COMMUNICATION DEVICE:
12 (1) FOR ANY PURPOSE DOCUMENTED IN THE STUDENT’S
13 INDIVIDUALIZED EDUCATION PROGRAM OR 504 PLAN;
14 (2) TO MONITOR OR ADDRESS A STUDENT’S DOCUMENTED HEALTH
15 ISSUE;
16 (3) DURING AN EMERGENCY EVENT, IF EXPRESSLY AUTHORIZED BY
17 AN ADMINISTRATOR;
18 (4) WHEN DIRECTED BY AN ADMINISTRATOR FOR EDUCATIONAL
19 PURPOSES;
20 (5) (3) TO ACCESS LANGUAGE TRANSLATION TOOLS TO ENSURE
21 EDUCATIONAL EQUITY FOR MULTILINGUAL STUDENTS WHEN SCHOOL–ISSUED
22 ELECTRONIC COMMUNICATION DEVICES ARE NOT AVAILABLE OR SUFFICIENT FOR
23 THIS PURPOSE; OR
24 (6) (4) FOR THE PURPOSE OF MEETING CAREGIVING
25 RESPONSIBILITIES, AS APPROVED BY A PRINCIPAL.
26 (E) EACH COUNTY BOARD SHALL:
27 (1) ENGAGE PARENTS, STUDENTS, EDUCATORS, AND COMMUNITY
28 STAKEHOLDERS IN THE DEVELOPMENT AND REVISION OF THE POLICY, INCLUDING
29 DETERMINING ENFORCEMENT OF THE POLICY; AND
