Assigned to: Government, Labor, and Elections
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Insurance – Certificates of Guarantee for County Bond Requirements – All
3 Counties
4 FOR the purpose of authorizing all counties, rather than only specified counties, to honor
5 certificates of guarantee that are issued by certain associations or on behalf of certain
6 contractors in order to satisfy certain county bond requirements; providing that a
7 county is not required under certain provisions of law to honor certain certificates of
8 guarantee; and generally relating to certificates of guarantee.
9 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
10 Article – Insurance
11 Section 1–203
12 Annotated Code of Maryland
13 (2017 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
14 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
15 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
16 Article – Insurance
17 1–203.
18 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
19 (2) “Certificate of guarantee” means an instrument that is issued:
20 (i) by a nonprofit association of contractors, or its wholly owned
21 subsidiary that is approved to operate by [Baltimore County, Baltimore City, Calvert
22 County, Charles County, Howard County, Prince George’s County, Montgomery County,
23 St. Mary’s County, or Washington County, as appropriate] A COUNTY; and
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from exist in g l aw . *hb1216*
2 HOUSE BILL 1216
1 (ii) on behalf of a contractor for the purpose of satisfying:
2 1. county bond requirements for public improvements; or
3 2. other county bond requirements.
4 (3) “Contractor” means a person that, for a fixed price, commission, fee, or
5 percentage, undertakes to bid on or accepts or offers to accept orders or contracts to perform
6 or supervise the construction, improvement, or maintenance of a building, structure, or
7 road.
8 (b) This article does not apply to a certificate of guarantee.
9 (c) [Baltimore County, Baltimore City, Calvert County, Charles County, Howard
10 County, Montgomery County, Prince George’s County, St. Mary’s County, and Washington
11 County] A COUNTY:
12 (1) may honor certificates of guarantee; but
13 (2) [are] IS not required under this article to honor certificates of
14 guarantee.
15 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
16 October 1, 2026.
