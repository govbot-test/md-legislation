CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Supporting Inclusive Community Adaptation Act
3 FOR the purpose of requiring the Department of Natural Resources to facilitate community
4 participation in project planning and development; establishing the Resilience
5 Through Restoration Capital Grant Fund in the Department as a special, nonlapsing
6 fund to provide certain grant funding for certain projects that reduce the State’s
7 climate change vulnerabilities and enhance the resilience of the State’s communities
8 and public lands; requiring interest earnings of the Resilience Through Restoration
9 Capital Grant Fund to be credited to the Fund; requiring, by a certain date, the
10 Department to develop a website, guidance, and training for certain planning tools
11 and resources; requiring, by a certain date, the Department to take certain actions
12 relating to climate change mitigation, adaptation, and resilience projects on certain
13 land owned, leased, or managed by the Department; altering certain provisions of
14 law relating to the Shore Erosion Control Construction Loan Fund, including
15 altering the eligibility for loans, authorizing the Department to forgive loans under
16 certain circumstances, and requiring interest earnings of the Fund to be credited to
17 the Fund; and generally relating to community participation in project planning and
18 development and climate change resilience.

24 FUND IN THE DEPARTMENT.

27 ORGANIZATIONS FOR THE PLANNING, DESIGN, CONSTRUCTION, AND ADAPTIVE
28 MANAGEMENT OF NATURE–BASED RESTORATION PROJECTS THAT REDUCE THE

1 (C) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ADMINISTER THE FUND.

12 THE BENEFIT OF THE FUND.
13 (F) THE FUND MAY BE USED ONLY FOR:
14 (1) PROVIDING GRANTS TO STATE AGENCIES, LOCAL GOVERNMENTS,
15 AND NONPROFIT ORGANIZATIONS IN THE STATE FOR THE PLANNING, DESIGN,
16 CONSTRUCTION, AND ADAPTIVE MANAGEMENT OF NATURE–BASED RESTORATION
17 PROJECTS THAT REDUCE THE STATE’S CLIMATE CHANGE VULNERABILITIES AND
18 ENHANCE THE RESILIENCE OF THE STATE’S COMMUNITIES AND PUBLIC LANDS;
19 (2) SUPPORTING PROGRAMMATIC AND PROJECT–LEVEL ADAPTIVE
20 MANAGEMENT THAT REDUCES THE STATE’S OVERALL CLIMATE CHANGE

22 AND PUBLIC LANDS; AND
23 (3) ADMINISTRATIVE EXPENSES CALCULATED IN ACCORDANCE WITH

26 (1) SHALL ENHANCE THE ABILITY OF COMMUNITIES AND PUBLIC

28 SUCH AS STORMS AND MORE FREQUENT AND INTENSE RAIN EVENTS OR CHRONIC
29 HAZARDS SUCH AS NUISANCE FLOODING AND SEA LEVEL RISE;

8 (V) DUNE AND BEACH RESTORATION;

10 (VII) ANY OTHER NATURE–BASED PROJECT THAT PROVIDES
11 COMMUNITY AND ECOSYSTEM BENEFITS BY RESTORING, ENHANCING, OR CREATING
12 HABITAT.

16 THE FUND.

18 WITH THE STATE BUDGET.
