HOUSE BILL 1312
I1 6lr3105
By: Delegate Pruski
Introduced and read first time: February 12, 2026
Assigned to: Economic Matters
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Legal Tender – Specie – Establishment
3 FOR the purpose of authorizing certain persons to use specie for the payment of certain
4 debts, taxes, fees, or other obligations; prohibiting a person from being required to
5 accept specie as payment; and generally relating to establishing specie as legal
6 tender in the State.
7 BY adding to
8 Article – Financial Institutions
9 Section 12–1301 through 12–1304 to be under the new subtitle “Subtitle 13. Specie
10 as Legal Tender”
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
13 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
14 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
15 Article – Financial Institutions
16 SUBTITLE 13. SPECIE AS LEGAL TENDER.
17 12–1301.
18 (A) IN THIS SUBTITLE THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
19 INDICATED.
20 (B) (1) “LEGAL TENDER” MEANS A MEDIUM OF EXCHANGE RECOGNIZED
21 BY THE STATE FOR THE PAYMENT OF DEBTS, TAXES, FEES, AND OTHER
22 OBLIGATIONS.

2 HOUSE BILL 1312
1 (2) “LEGAL TENDER” INCLUDES SPECIE.
2 (C) “SPECIE” MEANS:
3 (1) GOLD OR SILVER COIN ISSUED BY THE UNITED STATES OR A
4 FOREIGN GOVERNMENT; OR
5 (2) GOLD OR SILVER IN THE FORM OF BARS OR OTHER PHYSICAL
6 FORM CERTIFIED AS AT LEAST 99% PURE.
7 12–1302.
8 THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY FINDS THAT:
9 (1) THE USE OF SPECIE AS A MEDIUM OF EXCHANGE HAS A
10 HISTORICAL BASIS IN FOSTERING ECONOMIC STABILITY;
11 (2) RECOGNIZING SPECIE AS LEGAL TENDER PROMOTES
12 COMPETITION IN THE MONETARY SYSTEM AND PROTECTS AGAINST RELIANCE ON
13 FIAT CURRENCY; AND
14 (3) ESTABLISHING MECHANISMS FOR THE USE OF PRECIOUS METAL
15 IN TRANSACTIONS ENHANCES ECONOMIC RESILIENCE IN THE STATE.
16 12–1303.
17 (A) SUBJECT TO SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION, A PERSON MAY USE
18 SPECIE FOR THE PAYMENT OF:
19 (1) DEBTS BETWEEN PRIVATE PARTIES, IF THE PARTIES MUTUALLY
20 AGREE TO USE SPECIE; AND
21 (2) TAXES, FEES, OR OTHER OBLIGATIONS OWED TO THE STATE OR A
22 POLITICAL SUBDIVISION, IF THE STATE OR POLITICAL SUBDIVISION AGREES TO
23 ACCEPT SPECIE AS PAYMENT.
24 (B) A PERSON MAY NOT BE REQUIRED TO ACCEPT SPECIE AS PAYMENT.
25 12–1304.
26 (A) THE COMMISSIONER MAY ADOPT REGULATIONS TO CARRY OUT THE
27 PROVISIONS OF THIS SUBTITLE.

1 (B) ON OR BEFORE OCTOBER 1, 2027, AND EACH OCTOBER 1 THEREAFTER,

4 REPORT DETAILING THE ECONOMIC IMPACT OF RECOGNIZING SPECIE AS LEGAL
