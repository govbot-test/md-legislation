CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Higher Education – Public Senior Higher Education Institutions – Direct
3 Admission Program and Study
4 FOR the purpose of establishing a direct admission program at participating public senior
5 higher education institutions for certain eligible high school students beginning in a
6 certain academic year; requiring the Maryland Higher Education Commission to
7 administer the direct admission program through a certain financial aid system and
8 the Common Application, the State Department of Education, and public senior
9 higher education institutions to establish eligibility requirements for the direct
10 admission program; requiring the State Department of Education and the
11 Commission jointly to establish a process for notifying the Commission of the high
12 school students who have met the college and career readiness standard certain
13 eligibility requirements each school year; requiring the Commission, in consultation
14 with the Department, to conduct a study on administering the direct admission
15 program; and generally relating to a direct admission program and public senior
16 higher education institutions.
17 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
18 Article – Education
19 Section 7–205.1(a) and (c)(2)
20 Annotated Code of Maryland
21 (2025 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
22 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
23 Article – Education

31 INSTITUTION TO WHICH THE STUDENT APPLIES.

6 HOUSE BILL 700
1 (a) The Maryland Higher Education Commission, in collaboration with the State
2 Department of Education, shall study and make recommendations on the administration
3 of the direct admission program established under § 15–101.1 of the Education Article, as
4 enacted by Section 1 of this Act.
5 (b) In conducting the study required under subsection (a) of this section, the
6 Commission shall:
7 (1) identify the financial, technical, legal, and operational mechanisms
8 needed to effectively implement the direct admission program;
9 (2) evaluate whether a system could be developed to integrate student
10 academic data with financial aid eligibility data to provide simultaneous admission and
11 affordability notifications to students; and
12 (3) create an implementation timeline for the direct admission program.
13 (c) On or before July 1, 2027, the Commission shall submit a final report of its
14 findings and conclusions to the General Assembly, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State
15 Government Article.
16 SECTION 2. 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
17 July 1, 2026. Section 2 of this Act shall remain effective for a period of 1 year and 1 month
18 and, at the end of July 31, 2027, Section 2 of this Act, with no further action required by
19 the General Assembly, shall be abrogated and of no further force and effect.
