CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Family Law – Child Custody Evaluators – Qualifications
3 FOR the purpose of establishing qualifications for an individual to be appointed by a court
4 as a custody evaluator; and generally relating to child custody and visitation.
5 BY adding to
6 Article – Family Law
7 Section 9–109
8 Annotated Code of Maryland
9 (2019 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
10 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
11 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
12 Article – Family Law
13 9–109.
14 (A) IN THIS SECTION, “CUSTODY EVALUATOR” MEANS AN INDIVIDUAL
15 APPOINTED OR APPROVED BY A COURT TO CONDUCT A CUSTODY EVALUATION.
16 (B) ON MOTION OF A PARTY OR CHILD’S COUNSEL, OR ON ITS OWN
17 INITIATIVE, A COURT MAY:

Underlining indicates amendments to bill.

2 SENATE BILL 222
1 (1) ORDER AN ASSESSMENT TO AID THE COURT IN EVALUATING THE
2 HEALTH, SAFETY, AND WELFARE OR BEST INTERESTS OF A CHILD IN A CONTESTED
3 CUSTODY OR VISITATION CASE; OR
4 (2) APPOINT A CUSTODY EVALUATOR DETERMINED BY THE COURT TO
5 BE COMPETENT TO CONDUCT A HOME STUDY OR AN EVALUATION OF A SPECIFIC
6 ISSUE.
7 (C) A COURT MAY NOT ORDER THE COST OF AN ASSESSMENT TO BE PAID, IN
8 WHOLE OR IN PART, BY A PARTY WITHOUT GIVING THE PARTIES NOTICE AND AN
9 OPPORTUNITY TO OBJECT.
10 (D) A COURT MAY NOT APPOINT AN INDIVIDUAL AS A CUSTODY EVALUATOR
11 UNLESS:
12 (1) THE INDIVIDUAL MEETS THE QUALIFICATIONS SPECIFIED IN
13 SUBSECTIONS (E) AND (F) OF THIS SECTION; OR
14 (2) THE COURT WAIVES THE QUALIFICATIONS IN ACCORDANCE WITH
15 SUBSECTION (G) OF THIS SECTION.
16 (E) (1) A CUSTODY EVALUATOR MUST BE:
17 (I) A PHYSICIAN LICENSED IN ANY STATE WHO IS
18 BOARD–CERTIFIED IN PSYCHIATRY OR HAS COMPLETED A PSYCHIATRY RESIDENCY
19 ACCREDITED BY THE ACCREDITATION COUNCIL FOR GRADUATE MEDICAL
20 EDUCATION OR A SUCCESSOR TO THAT COUNCIL;
21 (II) A MARYLAND–LICENSED PSYCHOLOGIST OR A
22 PSYCHOLOGIST WITH AN EQUIVALENT LEVEL OF LICENSURE IN ANY OTHER STATE;
23 (III) A MARYLAND–LICENSED CLINICAL MARRIAGE AND FAMILY
24 THERAPIST OR A CLINICAL MARRIAGE AND FAMILY THERAPIST WITH AN
25 EQUIVALENT LEVEL OF LICENSURE IN ANY OTHER STATE;
26 (IV) A MARYLAND–LICENSED CERTIFIED SOCIAL
27 WORKER–CLINICAL OR A CLINICAL SOCIAL WORKER WITH AN EQUIVALENT LEVEL
28 OF LICENSURE IN ANY OTHER STATE;
29 (V) 1. A MARYLAND–LICENSED GRADUATE OR MASTER
30 SOCIAL WORKER WITH AT LEAST 2 YEARS OF EXPERIENCE IN:
SENATE BILL 222 3
1 A. ONE OR MORE OF THE AREAS LISTED IN SUBSECTION
2 (F)(4) OF THIS SECTION;
3 B. CONDUCTING CUSTODY EVALUATIONS; OR
4 C. ANY COMBINATION OF THE EXPERIENCE DESCRIBED
5 IN ITEM A OR B OF THIS ITEM; OR
6 2. A GRADUATE OR MASTER SOCIAL WORKER WITH AN
7 EQUIVALENT LEVEL OF LICENSURE AND EXPERIENCE IN ANY OTHER STATE; OR
8 (VI) A MARYLAND–LICENSED CLINICAL PROFESSIONAL
9 COUNSELOR OR A CLINICAL PROFESSIONAL COUNSELOR WITH AN EQUIVALENT
10 LEVEL OF LICENSURE IN ANY OTHER STATE.
11 (2) A CUSTODY EVALUATOR SHALL COMPLY WITH ALL CONDITIONS
12 NECESSARY TO MAINTAIN PROFESSIONAL LICENSURE, INCLUDING COMPLETING
13 ALL MANDATORY CONTINUING EDUCATION REQUIREMENTS.
14 (F) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN SUBSECTION (G) OF THIS SECTION, A CUSTODY
15 EVALUATOR MUST HAVE:
16 (1) COMPLETED A TRAINING PROGRAM THAT CONFORMS TO
17 GUIDELINES ESTABLISHED BY THE ADMINISTRATIVE OFFICE OF THE COURTS;
18 (2) COMPLIED WITH THE CONTINUING EDUCATIONAL
19 REQUIREMENTS OF THE CUSTODY EVALUATOR’S FIELD;
20 (3) EXPERIENCE IN CONDUCTING OR OBSERVING CUSTODY
21 EVALUATIONS; AND
22 (4) DEMONSTRATED KNOWLEDGE OF AND EXPERIENCE IN THE
23 FOLLOWING TOPICS:
24 (I) DOMESTIC AND FAMILY VIOLENCE;
25 (II) CHILD NEGLECT AND ABUSE, INCLUDING SEXUAL ABUSE;
26 (III) CHILD AND ADULT DEVELOPMENT;
27 (IV) TRAUMA AND ITS IMPACT ON CHILDREN AND ADULTS;
28 (V) FAMILY DYNAMICS AND CONFLICT RESOLUTION;
4 SENATE BILL 222
1 (VI) DISABILITY–RELATED ISSUES; AND
2 (VII) THE IMPACT OF DIVORCE AND SEPARATION ON CHILDREN
3 AND ADULTS.
4 (G) A COURT MAY WAIVE THE REQUIREMENTS IN SUBSECTIONS (E) AND (F)
5 OF THIS SECTION FOR A COURT EMPLOYEE OR AN INDIVIDUAL UNDER CONTRACT
6 WITH THE COURT WHO:
7 (1) CONDUCTED CUSTODY EVALUATIONS FOR AT LEAST 14 YEARS
8 PRIOR TO JANUARY 1, 2025;
9 (2) HAS COMPLETED A TRAINING PROGRAM REQUIRED BY
10 SUBSECTION (F)(1) OF THIS SECTION; AND
11 (3) COMPLETES AT LEAST 20 HOURS PER YEAR OF CONTINUING
12 EDUCATION RELEVANT TO CONDUCTING CUSTODY EVALUATIONS.
13 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
14 1, 2026.
Approved:
