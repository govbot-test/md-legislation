1 (a) (1) A small business, nonprofit organization, or microenterprise may apply
2 for financial assistance under the Business Development Program.
3 (2) The Department shall review each application.
4 (b) An applicant may qualify for financial assistance for a project in a priority
5 funding area or an eligible opportunity zone if the application demonstrates that:
6 (1) the financial assistance from the Fund is the minimum amount
7 necessary to make the project financially feasible;
8 (2) the project is ready to proceed when it receives financial assistance from
9 the Business Development Program; and
10 (3) any food desert project includes a plan to seek out sources of
11 Maryland–grown produce and Maryland produced foods.
12 (c) [(1)] The Department shall provide written notice to the political subdivision
13 in which the proposed project is located.
14 [(2) Except as provided in paragraph (3) of this subsection, the Department
15 may not approve an application unless the political subdivision in which the proposed
16 project is located approves the application by:
17 (i) resolution; or
18 (ii) letter, delivered to the Department by the political subdivision’s
19 authorized designee, expressing support of the plan or project.
20 (3) If the Department does not receive notice of approval or denial of an
21 application from the affected jurisdictions within 45 15 days after notice of the proposed
22 project is given in accordance with paragraph (1) of this subsection, the Department may
23 approve the application.]
24 (d) Financial assistance under the Business Development Program may be
25 provided to a small business, nonprofit organization, or microenterprise as:
26 (1) a grant;
27 (2) a loan;
28 (3) a reduction in the principal obligation of or interest rate on a loan or
29 portion of a loan;
30 (4) a prepayment of interest on a subordinate or superior loan or portion of
31 a loan;
