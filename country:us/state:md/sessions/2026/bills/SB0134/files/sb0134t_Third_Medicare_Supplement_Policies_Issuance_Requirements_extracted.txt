2 PERIOD GUARANTEED ISSUE RIGHT, INCLUDING FOR PLANS ISSUED ON OR AFTER
3 JANUARY 1, 2020;
4 (III) APPLIES FOR THE MEDICARE SUPPLEMENT POLICY DURING
5 THE 63–DAY PERIOD FOLLOWING THE QUALIFYING EVENT FOR THE FEDERAL
6 SPECIAL ENROLLMENT PERIOD GUARANTEED ISSUE RIGHT; AND
7 (IV) SUBMITS EVIDENCE OF THE DATE OF THE QUALIFYING
8 EVENT FOR THE FEDERAL SPECIAL ENROLLMENT PERIOD GUARANTEED ISSUE
9 RIGHT WITH THE APPLICATION FOR A MEDICARE SUPPLEMENT POLICY.
10 [(3)] (5) (i) A carrier shall make available Medicare supplement
11 policy plans A and D to an individual who is under the age of 65 years but is eligible for
12 Medicare due to a disability, if an application for a Medicare supplement policy or certificate
13 is submitted:
14 1. during the 6–month period following the applicant’s
15 enrollment in Part B of Medicare; or
16 2. if the applicant is notified by Medicare of the applicant’s
17 retroactive enrollment in Medicare, during the 6–month period following notification of
18 enrollment in Medicare.
19 (ii) For a Medicare supplement policy plan A or D required to be
20 made available under subparagraph (i) of this paragraph, a carrier:
21 1. may not deny or condition the issuance or effectiveness of
22 a Medicare supplement policy plan A or D because of the health status, claims experience,
23 receipt of health care, or medical condition of the applicant; or
24 2. may not deny, reduce, or condition coverage to the
25 applicant for a Medicare supplement policy plan A or D because of the health status, claims
26 experience, or medical condition of the applicant or the use of medical care by the applicant.
27 (iii) For a Medicare supplement policy plan A required to be made
28 available under subparagraph (i) of this paragraph, a carrier may not charge individuals
29 who are under the age of 65 years, but are eligible for Medicare due to a disability, a rate
30 higher than the average of the premiums paid by all policyholders age 65 and older in the
31 State who are covered under that plan A policy form.
32 [(4)] (6) A carrier may elect to offer Medicare supplement policy plans to
33 individuals who are under the age of 65 years, but eligible for Medicare due to a disability,
34 in addition to the Medicare supplement policy plans A and D that are required to be offered
35 under paragraph [(3)(i)] (5)(I) of this subsection.
4 SENATE BILL 134
1 [(5)] (7) [Nothing in paragraph (3)] PARAGRAPH (5) of this subsection
2 may NOT be construed to require a carrier to offer a Medicare supplement policy plan to
3 individuals who are under the age of 65 years, but are eligible for Medicare due to a
4 disability, if the plan is not offered to individuals who are eligible for Medicare due to age.
5 [(6)] (8) (i) This paragraph applies only on and after July 1, 2023.
6 (ii) During the 30 days following the birthday of an individual
7 enrolled in a Medicare supplement policy, a carrier shall make available to the individual
8 different Medicare supplement policies with benefits that are equal to or less than the
9 benefits of the individual’s existing coverage.
10 (iii) 1. For purposes of this paragraph, a Medicare supplement
11 policy has equal or lesser benefits unless:
12 A. it contains one or more significant benefits not included in
13 the Medicare supplement policy being replaced; or
14 B. it contains the same significant benefits included in the
15 Medicare supplement policy being replaced but it reduces the cost–sharing responsibilities
16 of the enrollee for the benefits.
17 2. The Commissioner shall adopt regulations establishing a
18 matrix for identifying which Medicare supplement policies have equal or lesser benefits.
19 (iv) For a Medicare supplement policy required to be made available
20 under subparagraph (ii) of this paragraph, a carrier may not:
21 1. deny or condition the issuance or effectiveness of a
22 Medicare supplement policy, or discriminate in the pricing of the policy, because of the
23 health status, claims experience, or medical condition of the individual or the receipt of
24 health care by the individual; or
25 2. deny, reduce, or condition coverage to the individual for a
26 Medicare supplement policy because of the health status, claims experience, or medical
27 condition of the individual or the use of medical care by the individual.
28 (v) A carrier that offers Medicare supplement policies shall notify an
29 insured of the insured’s rights under this paragraph at least 30 days, but not more than 60
30 days, before the insured’s birthday.
31 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
32 1, 2026.
