A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Frederick County – Special Education Video and Audio Recording Program –
3 Established
4 FOR the purpose of establishing the Frederick County Special Education Video and Audio
5 Recording Program for the purpose of installing certain video and audio recording
6 devices in specialized instructional settings and certain related areas to document
7 certain incidents; and generally relating to the Frederick County Special Education
8 Video and Audio Recording Program.
9 BY adding to
10 Article – Education
11 Section 7–455
12 Annotated Code of Maryland
13 (2025 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
14 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
15 Article – Education
16 Section 8–401(a)(1) and (5)
17 Annotated Code of Maryland
18 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
19 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
20 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
21 Article – Education
22 7–455.
23 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
24 INDICATED.
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

2 HOUSE BILL 1545
1 (2) “BOARD” MEANS THE FREDERICK COUNTY BOARD OF
2 EDUCATION.
3 (3) “INCIDENT” MEANS THE FOLLOWING BEHAVIORS, SUSPECTED BY
4 A TEACHER, AN AIDE, A PARENT, OR A GUARDIAN OF A STUDENT:
5 (I) BULLYING;
6 (II) ABUSE;
7 (III) NEGLECT OF A STUDENT; OR
8 (IV) HARM TO AN EMPLOYEE OR A STUDENT BY AN EMPLOYEE
9 OR ANOTHER STUDENT.
10 (4) “PROGRAM” MEANS THE FREDERICK COUNTY SPECIAL
11 EDUCATION VIDEO AND AUDIO RECORDING PROGRAM.
12 (5) “SPECIAL EDUCATION” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 8–401 OF
13 THIS ARTICLE.
14 (6) “SPECIALIZED INSTRUCTIONAL SETTING” MEANS ANY SPACE
15 WHERE STUDENTS WITH DISABILITIES RECEIVE SPECIALIZED SERVICES OR CARE
16 FOR AT LEAST 50% OF THE INSTRUCTIONAL DAY, INCLUDING:
17 (I) “EXPRESSIONS” CLASSROOMS;
18 (II) “LEARNING FOR LIFE” CLASSROOMS; AND
19 (III) ANY OTHER ENVIRONMENT DESIGNATED FOR SPECIALIZED
20 DISABILITY SUPPORT.
21 (7) “UNBIASED THIRD PARTY” MEANS:
22 (I) A DESIGNATED REVIEWER WITHIN THE FREDERICK
23 COUNTY PUBLIC SCHOOLS DEPARTMENT OF SAFETY AND EMERGENCY
24 MANAGEMENT; OR
25 (II) AN EXTERNAL ENTITY NOT DIRECTLY ASSIGNED TO THE
26 SCHOOL WHERE A VIDEO OR AUDIO RECORDING ORIGINATED.
HOUSE BILL 1545 3
1 (B) (1) THERE IS A FREDERICK COUNTY SPECIAL EDUCATION VIDEO
2 AND AUDIO RECORDING PROGRAM.
3 (2) THE PROGRAM SHALL BE ADMINISTERED BY THE BOARD.
4 (C) (1) (I) THE BOARD SHALL INSTALL VIDEO RECORDING DEVICES IN
5 ALL SPECIALIZED INSTRUCTIONAL SETTINGS THAT MONITOR ALL AREAS OF THE
6 SPECIALIZED INSTRUCTIONAL SETTING.
7 (II) A VIDEO RECORDING DEVICE INSTALLED IN ACCORDANCE
8 WITH THIS PARAGRAPH MAY NOT RECORD INSIDE BATHROOM AREAS OR IN AREAS
9 THAT STUDENTS USE TO CHANGE CLOTHING.
10 (2) THE BOARD SHALL INSTALL AND MAINTAIN AUDIO RECORDING
11 DEVICES IN:
12 (I) RESTROOMS;
13 (II) AREAS THAT STUDENTS USE TO CHANGE CLOTHING; AND
14 (III) PLACES OF PHYSICAL CARE ATTACHED TO RESTROOMS AND
15 AREAS THAT STUDENTS USE TO CHANGE CLOTHING.
16 (3) THE FREDERICK COUNTY PUBLIC SCHOOLS DEPARTMENT OF
17 SAFETY AND EMERGENCY MANAGEMENT SHALL OPERATE AND MAINTAIN THE
18 VIDEO AND AUDIO RECORDING DEVICES INSTALLED IN ACCORDANCE WITH THIS
19 SUBSECTION.
20 (4) AN UNBIASED THIRD PARTY SHALL BE THE CUSTODIAN OF ALL
21 VIDEO AND AUDIO RECORDINGS.
22 (5) FREDERICK COUNTY PUBLIC SCHOOL SYSTEM PERSONNEL MAY
23 NOT BE A POINT OF CONTACT OR PRIMARY REVIEWER OF VIDEO OR AUDIO
24 RECORDINGS.
25 (6) IF A PERSON DISCOVERS THAT THE OPERATION OF A VIDEO OR
26 AUDIO RECORDING DEVICE HAS BEEN INTERRUPTED, THE PERSON SHALL SUBMIT A
27 WRITTEN REPORT TO THE UNBIASED THIRD PARTY AND THE BOARD THAT INCLUDES
28 A DESCRIPTION OF:
29 (I) HOW THE INTERRUPTION WAS DISCOVERED AND THE
30 LENGTH OF THE INTERRUPTION; AND
4 HOUSE BILL 1545
1 (II) THE REASON FOR THE INTERRUPTION, IF KNOWN.
2 (D) (1) THE BOARD SHALL:
3 (I) PROVIDE WRITTEN NOTICE OF THE USE OF A VIDEO AND
4 AUDIO RECORDING DEVICE:
5 1. TO THE PARENT OR LEGAL GUARDIAN OF EACH
6 STUDENT SUBJECT TO A RECORDING MADE BY A VIDEO OR AUDIO RECORDING
7 DEVICE AT LEAST 10 DAYS BEFORE ACTIVATING THE DEVICE; AND
8 2. IN THE STUDENT HANDBOOK; AND
9 (II) POST A SIGN IN A CONSPICUOUS LOCATION OUTSIDE EACH
10 RESTROOM WHERE AN AUDIO RECORDING DEVICE IS USED.
11 (2) (I) A PARENT OR LEGAL GUARDIAN MAY REQUEST THAT THE
12 CHILD OF THE PARENT OR LEGAL GUARDIAN BE EXEMPT FROM AUDIO RECORDINGS
13 IN THE AREAS SPECIFIED IN SUBSECTION (C)(2) OF THIS SECTION.
14 (II) A PARENT OR LEGAL GUARDIAN REQUESTING AN
15 EXEMPTION UNDER THIS PARAGRAPH SHALL:
16 1. DOCUMENT THE REQUEST IN THE STUDENT’S
17 INDIVIDUALIZED EDUCATION PROGRAM OR 504 PLAN; AND
18 2. HAVE THE OPPORTUNITY TO DISCUSS ALTERNATIVE
19 ARRANGEMENTS FOR THE STUDENT’S CARE.
20 (E) (1) VIDEO AND AUDIO RECORDINGS MAY NOT BE LIVE–STREAMED.
21 (2) AN UNBIASED THIRD PARTY SHALL REVIEW AT LEAST 15 MINUTES
22 OF VIDEO AND AUDIO RECORDINGS FROM EACH AREA SPECIFIED IN SUBSECTION
23 (C)(1) AND (2) OF THIS SECTION AT LEAST EVERY 90 CALENDAR DAYS.
24 (3) WITHIN 7 DAYS AFTER THE DATE OF AN INCIDENT, THE BOARD
25 MAY AUTHORIZE THE FOLLOWING INDIVIDUALS TO REVIEW THE VIDEO OR AUDIO
26 RECORDING:
27 (I) AN EMPLOYEE OF THE FREDERICK COUNTY PUBLIC
28 SCHOOL SYSTEM;
HOUSE BILL 1545 5
1 (II) THE PARENT OR LEGAL GUARDIAN OF A STUDENT WHO IS
2 THE SUBJECT OF THE INCIDENT;
3 (III) A LAW ENFORCEMENT OFFICER; OR
4 (IV) A REPRESENTATIVE FROM CHILD PROTECTIVE SERVICES.
5 (4) THE BOARD SHALL ENSURE THAT VIDEO AND AUDIO RECORDINGS
6 MADE UNDER THIS SECTION COMPLY WITH FEDERAL PRIVACY LAWS.
7 (F) (1) THE BOARD MAY NOT USE VIDEO OR AUDIO RECORDINGS MADE
8 UNDER THIS SECTION:
9 (I) FOR TEACHER EVALUATIONS; OR
10 (II) TO DOCUMENT A STUDENT IN VIOLATION OF A RULE OR
11 DISCIPLINARY CODE NOT RELATED TO THE INCIDENT, UNLESS THE BOARD
12 DETERMINES THAT THE RECORDING CAPTURES A SEPARATE INCIDENT THAT
13 REQUIRES A SEPARATE INVESTIGATION.
14 (2) RECORDINGS MADE UNDER THIS SECTION ARE CONFIDENTIAL
15 AND MAY NOT BE USED FOR ANY PURPOSE NOT SPECIFIED IN THIS SECTION.
16 (G) (1) THE FREDERICK COUNTY PUBLIC SCHOOLS DEPARTMENT OF
17 SAFETY AND EMERGENCY MANAGEMENT SHALL RETAIN ALL RECORDINGS MADE
18 UNDER THIS SECTION FOR AT LEAST 1 YEAR AFTER THE DATE OF THE RECORDING.
19 (2) IF A PARTY REQUESTS A REVIEW OF A RECORDING UNDER
20 SUBSECTION (E)(3) OF THIS SECTION, THE BOARD SHALL PRESERVE THE
21 RECORDING FOR 60 DAYS AFTER RECEIVING THE REQUEST AND UNTIL ALL LEGAL
22 AND ADMINISTRATIVE PROCEEDINGS ARE COMPLETE.
23 (3) ALL RECORDINGS MADE UNDER THIS SECTION SHALL BE STORED
24 ON A SECURE, CLOSED NETWORK MANAGED BY THE BOARD.
25 (H) (1) ON OR BEFORE DECEMBER 31, 2029, THE BOARD SHALL INSTALL
26 AT LEAST ONE VIDEO AND AUDIO RECORDING DEVICE IN ALL SPECIALIZED
27 INSTRUCTIONAL SETTINGS.
28 (2) ON OR BEFORE JUNE 30, 2030, THE BOARD SHALL INSTALL VIDEO
29 AND AUDIO RECORDING DEVICES IN CLASSROOMS THAT HAVE STUDENTS WHO ARE
30 PART OF:
6 HOUSE BILL 1545
1 (I) THE RESPONSIVE INTERVENTIONS FOR STUDENT
2 EXCELLENCE (RISE) PROGRAM; AND
3 (II) THE PYRAMID PROGRAM.
4 (3) ON OR BEFORE JUNE 30, 2031, THE BOARD SHALL COMPLETE
5 INSTALLATION OF VIDEO AND AUDIO RECORDING DEVICES.
6 (I) ON OR BEFORE JUNE 30, 2032, EACH SCHOOL IN THE COUNTY SHALL
7 EVALUATE THE PROGRAM AND SUBMIT A REPORT TO THE BOARD AND THE
8 DEPARTMENT ON THE SCHOOL’S PROGRESS TOWARD MEETING PROGRAM GOALS.
9 8–401.
10 (a) (1) In this subtitle the following words have the meanings indicated.
11 (5) “Special education” means specially designed instruction, at no cost to
12 parents, to meet the unique needs of a child with a disability, including:
13 (i) Instruction in the classroom, in the home, in hospitals and
14 institutions, and in other settings; and
15 (ii) Instruction in physical education.
16 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
17 1, 2026.
