Section 1:
L6, M4 6lr0816
CF HB 35
By: Senator Brooks
Introduced and read first time: January 19, 2026
Assigned to: Education, Energy, and the Environment
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Local Government – Regulatory Powers – Regulation of Tree of Heaven
3 FOR the purpose of authorizing the governing body of a county or municipality to adopt
4 certain ordinances to regulate trees of heaven; authorizing the governing body of a
5 county or municipality to establish a bounty program to incentivize the removal of
6 trees of heaven; and generally relating to local management of trees of heaven.
7 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
8 Article – Local Government
9 Section 1–1318
10 Annotated Code of Maryland
11 (2013 Volume and 2025 Supplement)
12 Preamble
13 WHEREAS, Invasive species are nonnative plants and animals whose introduction
14 causes harm to human health, environmental harm, or economic harm – typically through
15 unchecked growth; and
16 WHEREAS, The tree of heaven is a nonnative, deciduous hardwood tree that is
17 considered an invasive species throughout North America because the species spreads
18 quickly and aggressively, crowding out native plants with dense greenery while secreting
19 a chemical into the soil that is toxic to surrounding plants; and
20 WHEREAS, The tree of heaven is the preferred habitat for the spotted lanternfly,
21 another invasive species that is causing economic damage to the agricultural community,
22 stressing native plant populations, and generally acting as a nuisance statewide; now,
23 therefore,
24 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
25 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from exist in g l aw . *sb0266*
2 SENATE BILL 266
1 Article – Local Government
2 1–1318.
3 (a) (1) In this section[, “invasive] THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE
4 MEANINGS INDICATED.
5 (2) “INVASIVE bamboo” means bamboo that is characterized by
6 aggressive spreading behavior, including bamboo species in the genus Phyllostachys.
7 (3) “TREE OF HEAVEN” MEANS THE AILANTHUS ALTISSIMA TREE
8 SPECIES.
9 (b) This section applies to all counties and municipalities.
10 (c) The governing body of a county or municipality, by ordinance, may regulate
11 invasive bamboo OR TREES OF HEAVEN, including by prohibiting a person from:
12 (1) selling invasive bamboo OR TREES OF HEAVEN;
13 (2) planting invasive bamboo OR TREES OF HEAVEN; [and]
14 (3) allowing invasive bamboo to grow on the property of the person without
15 proper upkeep and appropriate containment measures, including barriers or trenches; AND
16 (4) ALLOWING TREES OF HEAVEN TO GROW ON THE PERSON’S
17 PROPERTY.
18 (d) The governing body of a county or municipality may provide for the
19 enforcement of an ordinance adopted under this section by:
20 (1) requiring any damages caused by invasive bamboo OR TREES OF
21 HEAVEN to be mitigated; and
22 (2) establishing a civil fine.
23 (E) THE GOVERNING BODY OF A COUNTY OR MUNICIPALITY MAY ESTABLISH
24 A BOUNTY PROGRAM TO INCENTIVIZE THE REMOVAL OF TREES OF HEAVEN.
25 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
26 October 1, 2026.
