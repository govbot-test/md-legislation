1 AN ACT concerning
2 Local Government – Regulatory Powers – Regulation of Tree of Heaven Invasive
3 Trees
4 FOR the purpose of authorizing the governing body of a county or municipality to adopt
5 certain ordinances to regulate trees tree of heaven and certain invasive trees;
6 authorizing the governing body of a county or municipality to establish a bounty
7 program certain programs to incentivize the removal of trees tree of heaven and
8 certain invasive trees; and generally relating to local management of trees tree of
9 heaven and certain invasive trees.
10 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
11 Article – Local Government
12 Section 1–1318
13 Annotated Code of Maryland
14 (2013 Volume and 2025 Supplement)

2 SENATE BILL 266
1 Preamble
2 WHEREAS, Invasive species are nonnative plants, pathogens, and animals whose
3 introduction causes harm to human health, environmental harm, or economic harm –
4 typically through unchecked growth spread; and
5 WHEREAS, The tree of heaven is a nonnative, deciduous hardwood tree that is
6 considered an invasive species throughout North America because the species spreads
7 quickly and aggressively, crowding out native plants with dense greenery while secreting
8 a chemical into the soil that is toxic to surrounding plants; and
9 WHEREAS, The tree of heaven is the preferred habitat for the spotted lanternfly,
10 another invasive species that is causing economic damage to the agricultural community,
11 stressing native plant populations, and generally acting as a nuisance statewide; and
12 WHEREAS, The trees on the Maryland Department of Agriculture’s Maryland
13 Prohibited Invasive Plant List have been determined to be invasive, nonnative trees that
14 cause harm to human health, environmental harm, or economic harm; now, therefore,
15 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
16 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
17 Article – Local Government
18 1–1318.
19 (a) (1) In this section[, “invasive] THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE
20 MEANINGS INDICATED.
21 (2) “INVASIVE bamboo” means NONNATIVE bamboo that is characterized
22 by aggressive spreading behavior, including bamboo species in the genus Phyllostachys.
23 (3) “LISTED INVASIVE TREE” MEANS ANY TREE ON THE “MARYLAND
24 PROHIBITED INVASIVE PLANTS PLANT LIST” POSTED BY THE DEPARTMENT OF
25 AGRICULTURE.
26 (4) “NATIVE TREE REPLACEMENT PROGRAM” MEANS A PROGRAM
27 WHEREBY A PERSON RECEIVES A NATIVE TREE IN EXCHANGE FOR REMOVING AND
28 ERADICATING AN INVASIVE TREE.
29 (3) (5) “TREE OF HEAVEN” MEANS THE AILANTHUS ALTISSIMA
30 TREE SPECIES.
31 (b) This section applies to all counties and municipalities.
SENATE BILL 266 3
1 (c) The governing body of a county or municipality, by ordinance, may regulate
2 invasive bamboo, LISTED INVASIVE TREES, OR TREES OF HEAVEN, including by
3 prohibiting a person from:
4 (1) selling invasive bamboo, LISTED INVASIVE TREES, OR TREES OF
5 HEAVEN;
6 (2) planting invasive bamboo, LISTED INVASIVE TREES, OR TREES OF
7 HEAVEN; [and]
8 (3) allowing invasive bamboo, LISTED INVASIVE TREES, to grow on the
9 property of the person without proper upkeep and appropriate containment measures,
10 including barriers or trenches; AND
11 (4) ALLOWING TREES OF HEAVEN OR OTHER LISTED INVASIVE TREES
12 TO GROW ON THE PERSON’S PROPERTY.
13 (d) The governing body of a county or municipality may provide for the
14 enforcement of an ordinance adopted under this section by:
15 (1) requiring any damages caused by invasive bamboo, LISTED INVASIVE
16 TREES, OR TREES OF HEAVEN OR OTHER LISTED INVASIVE TREES to be mitigated; and
17 (2) establishing a civil fine.
18 (E) THE GOVERNING BODY OF A COUNTY OR MUNICIPALITY MAY ESTABLISH
19 A BOUNTY OR NATIVE TREE REPLACEMENT PROGRAM TO INCENTIVIZE THE
20 REMOVAL AND ERADICATION OF TREES OF HEAVEN AND OTHER LISTED INVASIVE
21 TREES.
22 (F) A BOUNTY OR NATIVE TREE REPLACEMENT PROGRAM ESTABLISHED BY
23 A COUNTY OR MUNICIPALITY SHALL REQUIRE:
24 (1) AN INVASIVE TREE TO BE PROPERLY IDENTIFIED CONSISTENT
25 WITH THE SPECIES PAGE PUBLISHED BY THE UNIVERSITY OF MARYLAND
26 AGRICULTURE EXTENSION; AND
27 (2) THE INVASIVE SPECIES, AFTER BEING PROPERLY IDENTIFIED, TO
28 BE REMOVED OR ERADICATED ACCORDING TO PROFESSIONALLY ACCEPTED
29 METHODS OR CONSISTENT WITH METHODOLOGY ON THE SPECIES PAGE PUBLISHED
30 BY THE UNIVERSITY OF MARYLAND AGRICULTURE EXTENSION.
31 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
32 October 1, 2026.
