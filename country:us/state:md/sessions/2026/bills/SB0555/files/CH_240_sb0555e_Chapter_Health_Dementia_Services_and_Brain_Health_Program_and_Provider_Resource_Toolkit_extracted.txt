WES MOORE, Governor Ch. 240
Chapter 240
(Senate Bill 555)
AN ACT concerning
Health – Dementia Services and Brain Health Program and Clinical Provider
Resource Toolkit
FOR the purpose of altering the duties of the Virginia I. Jones Alzheimer’s Disease and
Related Dementias Council; establishing the Dementia Services and Brain Health
Program in the Maryland Department of Health to lead the State’s public health
efforts relating to brain health and dementia; requiring the Director of Dementia
Services and Brain Health to administer the Program; requiring the Program, rather
than the Director, to perform certain duties and staff the Council; requiring the
Program to oversee implementation of a certain State Plan and support and promote
the delivery of dementia–capable care; requiring the Maryland Department of
Health, in partnership with the Department of Aging, the Council, and other entities,
to develop a clinical provider resource toolkit for dementia care for health care
providers; and generally relating to Alzheimer’s disease and related dementias and
dementia services.
BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,

Section 13–3201, 13–3202, 13–3207, 13–32A–03, and 13–32A–06

13–32A–07.
(A) IN THIS SECTION, “TOOLKIT” MEANS THE CLINICAL PROVIDER
RESOURCE TOOLKIT FOR DEMENTIA CARE FOR HEALTH CARE PROVIDERS
DEVELOPED UNDER SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION.
(B) ON OR BEFORE JANUARY OCTOBER 1, 2027, THE DEPARTMENT, IN
PARTNERSHIP WITH THE DEPARTMENT OF AGING, THE COUNCIL, AND OTHER
PUBLIC OR PRIVATE ORGANIZATIONS WITH EXPERTISE IN ALZHEIMER’S DISEASE OR
RELATED DEMENTIAS SHALL ESTABLISH AND MAINTAIN A CLINICAL PROVIDER
RESOURCE TOOLKIT FOR DEMENTIA CARE FOR HEALTH CARE PROVIDERS.
(C) THE PURPOSE OF THE TOOLKIT IS TO PROVIDE A CENTRALIZED,
UP–TO–DATE RESOURCE TO SUPPORT HEALTH CARE PROVIDERS IN CARING FOR
PATIENTS WHO ARE AT RISK FOR OR WHO MAY HAVE DEMENTIA.
(D) THE TOOLKIT DEVELOPED UNDER SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION
SHALL INCLUDE INFORMATION ON:

(1) BE COMPOSED OF MATERIALS THAT CAN BE DEVELOPED USING
EXISTING RESOURCES AND EXPERTISE WITHIN THE DEPARTMENT OR OBTAINED
THROUGH PARTNERSHIPS WITH ANY OTHER PUBLIC OR PRIVATE ORGANIZATION OR
ENTITY ENTITIES AS DETERMINED NECESSARY BY THE DEPARTMENT; AND
(2) INCLUDE INFORMATION ON:
(1) (I) RISK FACTORS FOR ALZHEIMER’S DISEASE AND RELATED

SYNDROME (TRISOMY–21);
(2) (II) RISK REDUCTION STRATEGIES, INCLUDING INFORMATION
ON LIFESTYLE INTERVENTIONS TO REDUCE DEMENTIA RISK;
(3) (III) THE IMPORTANCE OF EARLY DETECTION AND DIAGNOSIS
OF ALZHEIMER’S DISEASE AND RELATED DEMENTIAS;
(4) (IV) VALIDATED ASSESSMENT TOOLS FOR THE DETECTION AND
DIAGNOSIS OF COGNITIVE IMPAIRMENT;
(5) (V) THE RACIAL AND ETHNIC DISPARITIES IN DETECTING,
DIAGNOSING, AND ACCESSING TREATMENT AND SERVICES;
(6) (VI) PERSON–CENTERED CARE DELIVERY AND RELEVANT
SOURCES OF CLINICAL PRACTICE GUIDELINES AND TOOLS;
(7) (VII) EFFECTIVE CARE PLANNING TOOLS, INCLUDING:
(I) 1. AVAILABLE REIMBURSEMENT TOOLS, INCLUDING
RELEVANT BILLING CODES, FOR INDIVIDUALS WITH COGNITIVE IMPAIRMENT;
(II) 2. REFERRAL PATHWAYS TO COMMUNITY–BASED
SERVICES AND CARE PLANNING RESOURCES; AND
(III) 3. INFORMATION ON TREATMENT OPTIONS AND
RELEVANT SOURCES FOR GUIDELINES; AND
(8) (VIII) CONTINUING EDUCATION OPPORTUNITIES RELATED TO
DEMENTIA CARE BEST PRACTICES.
(E) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL PROMOTE THE USE OF THE TOOLKIT ACROSS
HEALTH CARE SECTORS, INCLUDING:
