1 AN ACT concerning
2 Health – Dementia Services and Brain Health Program and Clinical Provider
3 Resource Toolkit
4 FOR the purpose of altering the duties of the Virginia I. Jones Alzheimer’s Disease and
5 Related Dementias Council; establishing the Dementia Services and Brain Health
6 Program in the Maryland Department of Health to lead the State’s public health
7 efforts relating to brain health and dementia; requiring the Director of Dementia
8 Services and Brain Health to administer the Program; requiring the Program, rather
9 than the Director, to perform certain duties and staff the Council; requiring the
10 Program to oversee implementation of a certain State Plan and support and promote
11 the delivery of dementia–capable care; requiring the Maryland Department of
12 Health, in partnership with the Department of Aging, the Council, and other entities,
13 to develop a clinical provider resource toolkit for dementia care for health care
14 providers; and generally relating to Alzheimer’s disease and related dementias and
15 dementia services.
16 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
17 Article – Health – General
18 Section 13–3201, 13–3202, 13–3207, 13–32A–03, and 13–32A–06
19 Annotated Code of Maryland
20 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
21 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
22 Article – Health – General
23 Section 13–3205, 13–3206, 13–32A–01, 13–32A–02, 13–32A–04, and 13–32A–05

1 (B) ON OR BEFORE JANUARY OCTOBER 1, 2027, THE DEPARTMENT, IN

7 UP–TO–DATE RESOURCE TO SUPPORT HEALTH CARE PROVIDERS IN CARING FOR

19 (2) (II) RISK REDUCTION STRATEGIES, INCLUDING INFORMATION
20 ON LIFESTYLE INTERVENTIONS TO REDUCE DEMENTIA RISK;
21 (3) (III) THE IMPORTANCE OF EARLY DETECTION AND DIAGNOSIS
22 OF ALZHEIMER’S DISEASE AND RELATED DEMENTIAS;
23 (4) (IV) VALIDATED ASSESSMENT TOOLS FOR THE DETECTION AND
24 DIAGNOSIS OF COGNITIVE IMPAIRMENT;
25 (5) (V) THE RACIAL AND ETHNIC DISPARITIES IN DETECTING,
26 DIAGNOSING, AND ACCESSING TREATMENT AND SERVICES;
27 (6) (VI) PERSON–CENTERED CARE DELIVERY AND RELEVANT
28 SOURCES OF CLINICAL PRACTICE GUIDELINES AND TOOLS;
29 (7) (VII) EFFECTIVE CARE PLANNING TOOLS, INCLUDING:

1 (I) 1. AVAILABLE REIMBURSEMENT TOOLS, INCLUDING
2 RELEVANT BILLING CODES, FOR INDIVIDUALS WITH COGNITIVE IMPAIRMENT;

4 SERVICES AND CARE PLANNING RESOURCES; AND
5 (III) 3. INFORMATION ON TREATMENT OPTIONS AND
6 RELEVANT SOURCES FOR GUIDELINES; AND
7 (8) (VIII) CONTINUING EDUCATION OPPORTUNITIES RELATED TO
8 DEMENTIA CARE BEST PRACTICES.

13 (3) HOSPITALS AND HEALTH SYSTEMS;

15 (5) HOSPITAL AND HEALTH SYSTEM ASSOCIATIONS;
16 (6) PHYSICIAN AND MEDICAL STUDENT ORGANIZATIONS;
17 (7) ORGANIZATIONS REPRESENTING THE ADVANCEMENT OF NURSES,
18 HEALTH CARE PROFESSIONALS, AND HEALTH CARE ORGANIZATIONS; AND
19 (8) ANY OTHER PUBLIC OR PRIVATE ORGANIZATION, ENTITY, OR
20 PART OF AN ORGANIZATION OR ENTITY THE DEPARTMENT DEEMS NECESSARY.
21 (F) ON OR BEFORE JANUARY 1 EACH YEAR, BEGINNING IN 2028, THE
22 DEPARTMENT SHALL REVIEW AND UPDATE THE INFORMATION IN THE TOOLKIT TO
23 REFLECT THE MOST RECENT AND ACCURATE AVAILABLE INFORMATION.
