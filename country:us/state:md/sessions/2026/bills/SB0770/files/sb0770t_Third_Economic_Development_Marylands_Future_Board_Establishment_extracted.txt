CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Economic Development – Maryland’s Future Board – Establishment
3 FOR the purpose of establishing the Maryland’s Future Board under the Department of
4 Commerce to develop, evaluate, and revise a certain visionary plan for the future of
5 Maryland by a certain date and make recommendations for projects based on the
6 plan to the Governor and the General Assembly; establishing the Maryland’s Future
7 Fund as a special, nonlapsing fund; requiring interest earnings of the Fund to be
8 credited to the Fund; requiring the Comptroller to study the trends for certain
9 industries and report to the General Assembly on or before a certain date; and
10 generally relating to the Maryland’s Future Board.
11 BY adding to
12 Article – Economic Development
13 Section 2.5–301 through 2.5–305 to be under the new subtitle “Subtitle 3. Maryland’s
14 Future Board”

20 Preamble
21 WHEREAS, Maryland is rich in assets, often ranking among the top in the nation
22 for its innovation, technology–based workforce, personal income levels, and research and
23 development activity; and
24 WHEREAS, Maryland, despite these significant competitive advantages, lags
25 behind the nation in economic growth, population growth, and personal income growth; and
26 WHEREAS, In 2025, Maryland’s gross domestic product growth was 3.8%, compared
27 to the national rate of 4.4%; and
28 WHEREAS, In 2025, Maryland’s personal income growth was 2.8%, compared to the
29 national rate of 3.3%; and
30 WHEREAS, In 2025, Maryland’s population growth over the precious 5 years was
31 1.4%, compared to the national rate of 2.8%; and
32 WHEREAS, Demographic changes, population shifts, and uncertainty at the federal
33 level, on which much of Maryland’s economy is dependent, makes planning for the future
34 more challenging; and
SENATE BILL 770 3
1 WHEREAS, It is incumbent on Maryland to plan for the future with intention and
2 purpose to ensure all residents have a more ideal place to work, learn, and live; and
3 WHEREAS, Priorities are often aimed at solving pressing and immediate needs,
4 however, best practices show that visionary planning is the key to producing better
5 outcomes, creating competitive advantages, and encouraging sustainable growth; now,
6 therefore,
7 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
8 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
9 Article – Economic Development
10 SUBTITLE 3. MARYLAND’S FUTURE BOARD.
11 2.5–301.
12 (A) IN THIS SUBTITLE THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
13 INDICATED.
14 (B) “BOARD” MEANS THE MARYLAND’S FUTURE BOARD.
15 (C) “FUND” MEANS THE MARYLAND’S FUTURE FUND.
16 (D) “PROJECT” INCLUDES:
17 (1) AN ALLOCATION OF FUNDS IN THE OPERATING BUDGET OF THE
18 STATE;
19 (2) AN ALLOCATION OF FUNDS IN THE CAPITAL BUDGET OF THE
20 STATE; AND
21 (3) THE IMPLEMENTATION OF ANY POLICY THAT WILL ADVANCE THE
22 STATE’S SOCIOECONOMIC WELLBEING AND RESILIENCY.
23 (E) “VISIONARY PLAN” MEANS A PLAN THAT ENVISIONS A STATE THAT IS
24 EQUITABLE, PROSPEROUS, LIVABLE, AND PROVIDES OPPORTUNITIES FOR ALL
25 RESIDENTS TO THRIVE, INCLUDING:
26 (1) A FORWARD–THINKING STRATEGY THAT WOULD INCORPORATE
27 CORE VALUES AND PRINCIPLES AS ENVISIONED BY THE BOARD;
28 (2) A STRATEGY THAT COVERS BROADLY ISSUES THAT THE STATE
29 FACES;
4 SENATE BILL 770
1 (3) A STRATEGY THAT IS NOT CONSTRAINED BY EXISTING
2 TECHNOLOGY, INFRASTRUCTURE, OR FISCAL CONDITIONS OF THE STATE; AND
3 (4) STRATEGIES THAT IMAGINE FUTURE TRENDS IN THE FOLLOWING
4 AREAS:
5 (I) ROBOTICS AND AUTOMATION;
6 (II) ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE;
7 (III) ADVANCED MANUFACTURING;
8 (IV) DATA AGGREGATIONS; AND
9 (V) OTHER INDUSTRY DISRUPTIVE TECHNOLOGIES.
10 2.5–302.
11 (A) THERE IS A MARYLAND’S FUTURE BOARD IN THE DEPARTMENT.
12 (B) (1) THE BOARD CONSISTS OF THE FOLLOWING MEMBERS:
13 (I) THREE MEMBERS APPOINTED BY THE GOVERNOR;
14 (II) THE COMPTROLLER, OR THE COMPTROLLER’S DESIGNEE;
15 (III) TWO MEMBERS APPOINTED BY THE PRESIDENT OF THE
16 SENATE; AND
17 (III) (IV) TWO MEMBERS APPOINTED BY THE SPEAKER OF THE
18 HOUSE.
19 (2) THE BOARD SHALL CONSIST OF INDIVIDUALS WHO,
20 COLLECTIVELY, ARE VISIONARY THINKERS FOR AN ECONOMIC FUTURE OF THE
21 STATE THAT ATTRACTS INDIVIDUALS WHO WANT TO LIVE, WORK, AND LEARN IN THE
22 STATE, INCLUDING:
23 (I) ENTREPRENEURS;
24 (II) MEMBERS OF ACADEMIA;
25 (III) ETHICISTS;
SENATE BILL 770 5
1 (IV) ARTISTS;
2 (V) PHILANTHROPISTS; AND
3 (VI) LEADERS IN:
4 1. LABOR ORGANIZATIONS;
5 2. BUSINESS;
6 3. HIGHER EDUCATION; AND
7 4. NONPROFIT ORGANIZATIONS.
8 (3) (I) WHEN APPOINTING MEMBERS CONSIDERATION SHALL BE
9 GIVEN TO GEOGRAPHIC REPRESENTATION.
10 (II) THE MEMBERS APPOINTED TO THE BOARD SHALL REFLECT
11 THE DEMOGRAPHICS OF THE STATE.
12 (C) (1) THE TERM OF A MEMBER APPOINTED TO THE BOARD IS 4 YEARS
13 AND BEGINS ON JULY 1.
14 (2) (I) A VACANCY SHALL BE FILLED IMMEDIATELY FOR THE
15 REMAINDER OF THE UNEXPIRED PORTION OF A TERM.
16 (II) AT THE END OF A TERM, A MEMBER CONTINUES TO SERVE
17 UNTIL A SUCCESSOR HAS BEEN APPOINTED.
18 (3) A MEMBER APPOINTED BY THE GOVERNOR, THE PRESIDENT OF
19 THE SENATE, OR THE SPEAKER OF THE HOUSE SERVES AT THE PLEASURE OF THE
20 APPOINTING OFFICER.
21 (D) (1) A MEMBER OF THE BOARD:
22 (I) MAY NOT RECEIVE COMPENSATION AS A MEMBER OF THE
23 BOARD; BUT
24 (II) IS ENTITLED TO REIMBURSEMENT FOR EXPENSES UNDER
25 THE STANDARD STATE TRAVEL REGULATIONS, AS PROVIDED IN THE STATE
26 BUDGET.
6 SENATE BILL 770
1 (2) (I) THE BOARD IS SUBJECT TO THE PUBLIC INFORMATION ACT
2 AND THE OPEN MEETINGS ACT.
3 (II) THE BOARD MEMBERS ARE SUBJECT TO THE MARYLAND
4 PUBLIC ETHICS LAW.
5 (E) THE BOARD SHALL MEET AT LEAST FOUR TIMES PER YEAR.
6 (F) THE BOARD SHALL CARRY OUT THE PURPOSE OF THIS SUBTITLE BY:
7 (1) DEVELOPING AND REVISING A VISIONARY PLAN UNDER § 2.5–303
8 OF THIS SUBTITLE;
9 (2) IN ALIGNMENT WITH THE VISIONARY PLAN, RECOMMENDING
10 PROJECTS THAT MOVE THE STATE FORWARD AND CONTRIBUTE TO ENSURING THE
11 ECONOMIC STRENGTH OF THE STATE INTO THE FUTURE; AND
12 (3) CONSULTING WITH ENTITIES AND STATE AGENCIES, AS NEEDED.
13 2.5–303.
14 (A) ON OR BEFORE JANUARY 1, 2027, THE BOARD SHALL DEVELOP A
15 VISIONARY PLAN FOR THE FUTURE OF THE STATE THAT CARRIES OUT THE PURPOSE
16 OF THIS SUBTITLE.
17 (B) THE BOARD SHALL EVALUATE THE VISIONARY PLAN AT LEAST
18 ANNUALLY AND REVISE THE PLAN ACCORDINGLY.
19 (C) THE BOARD SHALL SUBMIT THE VISIONARY PLAN TO THE GOVERNOR
20 AND THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY, IN ACCORDANCE WITH § 2–1257 OF THE STATE
21 GOVERNMENT ARTICLE, WITHIN 30 DAYS AFTER THE COMPLETION OF THE
22 VISIONARY PLAN OR A REVISION TO THE VISIONARY PLAN.
23 (D) ON OR BEFORE AUGUST 1, 2027, AND EACH AUGUST 1 THEREAFTER,
24 THE BOARD SHALL SUBMIT RECOMMENDATIONS FOR PROJECTS TO THE GOVERNOR
25 AND, IN ACCORDANCE WITH § 2–1257 OF THE STATE GOVERNMENT ARTICLE, THE
26 GENERAL ASSEMBLY.
27 2.5–304.
28 (A) THERE IS A MARYLAND’S FUTURE FUND.
SENATE BILL 770 7
1 (B) THE PURPOSE OF THE FUND IS TO FUND THE PROJECTS RECOMMENDED
2 BY THE BOARD MADE UNDER § 2.5–303 OF THIS SUBTITLE.
3 (C) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ADMINISTER THE FUND.
4 (D) (1) THE FUND IS A SPECIAL, NONLAPSING FUND THAT IS NOT
5 SUBJECT TO § 7–302 OF THE STATE FINANCE AND PROCUREMENT ARTICLE.
6 (2) THE STATE TREASURER SHALL HOLD THE FUND SEPARATELY,
7 AND THE COMPTROLLER SHALL ACCOUNT FOR THE FUND.
8 (E) THE FUND CONSISTS OF:
9 (1) MONEY APPROPRIATED IN THE STATE BUDGET TO THE FUND;
10 (2) INTEREST EARNINGS; AND
11 (3) ANY OTHER MONEY FROM ANY OTHER SOURCE ACCEPTED FOR
12 THE BENEFIT OF THE FUND.
13 (F) THE FUND MAY BE USED ONLY FOR PROJECTS RECOMMENDED BY THE
14 BOARD UNDER § 2.5–303 OF THIS SUBTITLE.
15 (G) (1) THE STATE TREASURER SHALL INVEST THE MONEY OF THE FUND
16 IN THE SAME MANNER AS OTHER STATE MONEY MAY BE INVESTED.
17 (2) ANY INTEREST EARNINGS OF THE FUND SHALL BE CREDITED TO
18 THE FUND.
19 (H) EXPENDITURES FROM THE FUND MAY BE MADE ONLY IN ACCORDANCE
20 WITH THE STATE BUDGET.
21 (I) MONEY EXPENDED FROM THE FUND IS SUPPLEMENTAL TO AND IS NOT
22 INTENDED TO TAKE THE PLACE OF FUNDING THAT OTHERWISE WOULD BE
23 APPROPRIATED FOR THE SAME PURPOSE.
24 2.5–305.
25 THE DEPARTMENT MAY ADOPT REGULATIONS NECESSARY TO CARRY OUT
26 THIS SUBTITLE.

17 provision of law, and unless inconsistent with a federal law, grant agreement, or other
18 federal requirement, or with the terms of a gift or settlement agreement, for fiscal years
19 2024 through 2028, net interest on all State money allocated by the State Treasurer under

21 otherwise entitled to receive interest earnings, as accounted for by the Comptroller, shall
22 accrue to the General Fund of the State, with the exception of the following funds:

25 (89) THE MARYLAND’S FUTURE FUND.
26 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That:
27 (a) (1) The Comptroller shall study the economic growth trends in Maryland,
28 including the trends for industries in which Maryland has a competitive advantage or any
29 other industries that have an outsized presence in Maryland.
30 (2) The study shall identify the growth that is needed in the competitive
31 industries to ensure the financial and socioeconomic well–being of the residents of
32 Maryland.
SENATE BILL 770 9
1 (3) The study may examine the social and economic factors that are
2 expected to significantly change over the next 10 years or more, including:
3 (i) state migration trends;
4 (ii) job replacement due to the rise in artificial intelligence; and
5 (iii) the need for universal basic income or advanced technology
6 workforce training.
7 (4) The Comptroller may contract with an independent consultant to assist
8 the Comptroller in the preparation of the study required under this subsection.
9 (b) On or before December 1, 2026, the Comptroller shall report to the General
10 Assembly, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government Article, on the result of
11 the study under subsection (a) of this section.
12 SECTION 2. 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
13 July 1, 2026. It shall remain effective for a period of 5 years and, at the end of June 30,
14 2031, this Act, with no further action required by the General Assembly, shall be abrogated
15 and of no further force and effect.
