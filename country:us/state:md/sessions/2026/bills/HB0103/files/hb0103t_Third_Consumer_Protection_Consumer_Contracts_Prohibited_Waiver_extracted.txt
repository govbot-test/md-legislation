CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Consumer Protection – Consumer Contracts – Prohibited Waiver Waivers
3 FOR the purpose of repealing certain exemptions relating to a prohibition on a consumer
4 contract that sets a shorter time to bring an action under or on the consumer contract
5 under certain circumstances; prohibiting a consumer contract from waiving,
6 limiting, impairing, or disclaiming any right conferred or created or a manner of
7 enforcement established statutory or punitive damages authorized by federal or
8 State law; establishing that a violation of this Act is an unfair, abusive, or deceptive
9 trade practice subject to enforcement and certain civil penalties; prohibiting a waiver
10 by agreement of certain provisions of this Act, except under certain circumstances;
11 and generally relating to consumer contracts.
12 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
13 Article – Commercial Law
14 Section 13–301(14)(xlvii)
15 Annotated Code of Maryland
16 (2025 Replacement Volume)
17 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
18 Article – Commercial Law
19 Section 13–301(14)(xlviii)
20 Annotated Code of Maryland
21 (2025 Replacement Volume)

12 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
13 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
14 Article – Commercial Law
15 13–301.
16 Unfair, abusive, or deceptive trade practices include any:
17 (14) Violation of a provision of:
18 (xlvii) Title 14, Subtitle 50 of this article; [or]
19 (xlviii) Section 13–411.1(c)(2) of the Transportation Article; or
20 (XLIX) SECTION 14–1328 OF THIS ARTICLE; OR
21 14–1328.
22 (a) (1) In this section[, “consumer] THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE
23 MEANINGS INDICATED.
24 (2) “CONSUMER” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 13–101 OF THIS
25 ARTICLE.
26 (3) “CONSUMER contract” means a contract involving the sale, lease, or
27 provision of goods or services that are for personal, family, or household purposes.
28 (b) [This section does not apply to:
29 (1) Any consumer contract provided by a business, including an affiliate or
30 a subsidiary of the business, that is doing business under a license, franchise agreement,
HOUSE BILL 103 3
1 certificate, or other authorization issued by a State agency, political subdivision, or public
2 utility commission;
3 (2) A service regulated by:
4 (i) The Maryland Public Service Commission;
5 (ii) The Federal Communications Commission; or
6 (iii) The Federal Energy Regulatory Commission; or
7 (3) A person or business licensed to provide security system services under
8 Title 18 of the Business Occupations and Professions Article.
9 (c)] A provision THE FOLLOWING PROVISIONS in a consumer contract that [sets
10 a shorter time to bring an action under or on the consumer contract than required by the
11 law of the State when the consumer contract is issued or delivered] WAIVES, LIMITS,
12 IMPAIRS, OR DISCLAIMS A RIGHT CONFERRED OR CREATED OR A MANNER OF
13 ENFORCEMENT ESTABLISHED BY FEDERAL OR STATE LAW is ARE against State public
14 policy, illegal, and void, INCLUDING A:
15 (1) A PROVISION THAT WAIVES, LIMITS, IMPAIRS, OR DISCLAIMS
16 STATUTORY OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES AUTHORIZED BY FEDERAL OR STATE LAW; AND
17 (2) A PROVISION THAT SETS A SHORTER TIME TO BRING AN ACTION
18 UNDER OR ON THE CONSUMER CONTRACT THAN REQUIRED BY STATE LAW WHEN
19 THE CONSUMER CONTRACT WAS ISSUED OR DELIVERED.
20 [(d)] (C) If a consumer contract contains a provision that is illegal under this
21 section:
22 (1) A court [may]:
23 (I) MAY not give effect to the provision; and
24 (II) SHALL ENFORCE THE REMAINDER OF THE CONSUMER
25 CONTRACT TO THE EXTENT PRACTICABLE; AND
26 (2) A defense to liability under the consumer contract may not be based on
27 the [shorter limitation period] PROVISION.
28 (D) (1) A VIOLATION OF THIS SECTION IS:
29 (I) AN UNFAIR, ABUSIVE, OR DECEPTIVE TRADE PRACTICE
30 WITHIN THE MEANING OF TITLE 13 OF THIS ARTICLE; AND
4 HOUSE BILL 103
1 (II) SUBJECT TO THE ENFORCEMENT AND PENALTY
2 PROVISIONS CONTAINED IN TITLE 13 OF THIS ARTICLE, EXCEPT § 13–411 OF THIS
3 ARTICLE.
4 (2) IN ADDITION TO AN ACTION FOR DAMAGES UNDER § 13–408 OF
5 THIS ARTICLE, A PERSON MAY BRING AN ACTION FOR DECLARATORY RELIEF OR AN
6 INJUNCTION TO PROHIBIT A PERSON WHO HAS ENGAGED OR IS ENGAGING IN A
7 VIOLATION OF THIS SECTION FROM ENGAGING IN OR CONTINUING TO ENGAGE IN
8 THE VIOLATION.
9 (E) (D) (1) THIS SECTION SHALL BE CONSTRUED TO APPLY EQUALLY
10 AND WITHOUT FAVORABLE TREATMENT TO A CONSUMER CONTRACT WITH OR
11 WITHOUT AN ARBITRATION PROVISION.
12 (2) THIS SECTION MAY NOT BE CONSTRUED TO:
13 (I) INVALIDATE AN ARBITRATION PROVISION THAT IS
14 ENFORCEABLE UNDER THE FEDERAL ARBITRATION ACT; OR
15 (II) PROHIBIT A PROVISION IN A CONSUMER CONTRACT THAT
16 GRANTS A CONSUMER A RIGHT OR A MANNER OF ENFORCEMENT MORE EXPANSIVE
17 OR EXTENSIVE THAN A RIGHT CONFERRED OR CREATED OR A MEANS OR MANNER OF
18 ENFORCEMENT ESTABLISHED CREATE A RIGHT OR REMEDY NOT OTHERWISE
19 PROVIDED BY FEDERAL OR STATE LAW.
20 (3) (I) A PROVISION OF THIS SECTION MAY NOT BE WAIVED BY
21 AGREEMENT.
22 (II) A CONSUMER MAY WAIVE A RIGHT CONFERRED OR
23 CREATED OR A MANNER OF ENFORCEMENT ESTABLISHED BY FEDERAL OR STATE
24 LAW AS PART OF A SETTLEMENT OF A DISPUTE OR AN ACTION, UNLESS OTHERWISE
25 PROHIBITED BY FEDERAL LAW.
26 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That, if any provision of this Act or
27 the application of any provision of this Act to any person or circumstance is held invalid for
28 any reason in a court of competent jurisdiction, the invalidity does not affect other
29 provisions or any other application of this Act that can be given effect without the invalid
30 provision or application, and for this purpose the provisions of this Act are declared
31 severable.
32 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall be construed to
33 apply only prospectively and may not be applied or interpreted to have any effect on or
34 application to any contract entered into before the effective date of this Act.

2 the later of October 1, 2026, or the taking effect of Chapter 194 of the Acts of the General
3 Assembly of 2025, if the effective date of Chapter 194 is amended.
