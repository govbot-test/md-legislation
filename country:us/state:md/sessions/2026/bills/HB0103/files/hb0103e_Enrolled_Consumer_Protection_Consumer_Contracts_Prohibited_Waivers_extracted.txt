1 AN ACT concerning
2 Consumer Protection – Consumer Contracts – Prohibited Waiver Waivers
3 FOR the purpose of repealing altering certain exemptions relating to a prohibition on a
4 consumer contract that sets a shorter time to bring an action under or on the
5 consumer contract under certain circumstances; prohibiting a consumer contract
6 from waiving, limiting, impairing, or disclaiming any right conferred or created or a
7 manner of enforcement established statutory or punitive damages certain remedies
8 if authorized by federal or State law, subject to certain exemptions and a certain
9 exception; establishing that a violation of this Act is an unfair, abusive, or deceptive
10 trade practice subject to enforcement and certain civil penalties; prohibiting a waiver
11 by agreement of certain provisions of this Act, except under certain circumstances;
12 and generally relating to consumer contracts.
13 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
14 Article – Commercial Law

3 (3) “CONSUMER contract” means a contract involving the sale, lease, or
4 provision of goods or services that are for personal, family, or household purposes.
5 (b) [This section does not apply to:
6 (1) Any consumer contract provided by a business, including an affiliate or
7 a subsidiary of the business, that is doing business under a license, franchise agreement,
8 certificate, or other authorization issued by a State agency, political subdivision, or public

17 (c)] (1) A provision THE EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN PARAGRAPH (2) OF THIS
18 SUBSECTION, THE FOLLOWING PROVISIONS in a consumer contract that [sets a shorter
19 time to bring an action under or on the consumer contract than required by the law of the
20 State when the consumer contract is issued or delivered] WAIVES, LIMITS, IMPAIRS, OR
21 DISCLAIMS A RIGHT CONFERRED OR CREATED OR A MANNER OF ENFORCEMENT
22 ESTABLISHED BY FEDERAL OR STATE LAW is ARE against State public policy, illegal,
23 and void, INCLUDING A:
24 (1) (I) A PROVISION THAT WAIVES, LIMITS, IMPAIRS, OR
25 DISCLAIMS STATUTORY OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES THE FOLLOWING REMEDIES IF
26 AUTHORIZED BY FEDERAL OR STATE LAW:
27 1. STATUTORY DAMAGES;
28 2. PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING FOR INTENTIONAL,
29 KNOWING, OR RECKLESS VIOLATIONS OF LAW;
30 3. DECLARATORY RELIEF; OR
31 4. INJUNCTIVE RELIEF; AND
4 HOUSE BILL 103
1 (2) (II) A PROVISION THAT SETS A SHORTER TIME TO BRING AN
2 ACTION UNDER OR ON THE CONSUMER CONTRACT THAN REQUIRED BY STATE LAW
3 WHEN THE CONSUMER CONTRACT WAS ISSUED OR DELIVERED.
4 (2) A PROVISION IN A CONSUMER CONTRACT MAY LIMIT RECOVERY OF
5 STATUTORY OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES AUTHORIZED BY FEDERAL OR STATE LAW TO
6 THE EXTENT OF THE APPLICABLE LIMIT OF INSURANCE COVERAGE.
7 [(d)] (C) If a consumer contract contains a provision that is illegal under this
8 section:
9 (1) A court [may]:
10 (I) MAY not give effect to the provision; and
11 (II) SHALL ENFORCE THE REMAINDER OF THE CONSUMER
12 CONTRACT TO THE EXTENT PRACTICABLE; AND
13 (2) A defense to liability under the consumer contract may not be based on
14 the [shorter limitation period] PROVISION.
15 (D) (1) A VIOLATION OF THIS SECTION IS:
16 (I) AN UNFAIR, ABUSIVE, OR DECEPTIVE TRADE PRACTICE
17 WITHIN THE MEANING OF TITLE 13 OF THIS ARTICLE; AND
18 (II) SUBJECT TO THE ENFORCEMENT AND PENALTY
19 PROVISIONS CONTAINED IN TITLE 13 OF THIS ARTICLE, EXCEPT § 13–411 OF THIS
20 ARTICLE.
21 (2) IN ADDITION TO AN ACTION FOR DAMAGES UNDER § 13–408 OF
22 THIS ARTICLE, A PERSON MAY BRING AN ACTION FOR DECLARATORY RELIEF OR AN
23 INJUNCTION TO PROHIBIT A PERSON WHO HAS ENGAGED OR IS ENGAGING IN A
24 VIOLATION OF THIS SECTION FROM ENGAGING IN OR CONTINUING TO ENGAGE IN
25 THE VIOLATION.
26 (E) (D) (E) (1) THIS SECTION SHALL BE CONSTRUED TO APPLY EQUALLY
27 AND WITHOUT FAVORABLE TREATMENT TO A CONSUMER CONTRACT WITH OR
28 WITHOUT AN ARBITRATION PROVISION.
29 (2) THIS SECTION MAY NOT BE CONSTRUED TO:
HOUSE BILL 103 5
1 (I) INVALIDATE AN ARBITRATION PROVISION THAT IS
2 ENFORCEABLE UNDER THE FEDERAL ARBITRATION ACT; OR
3 (II) PROHIBIT A PROVISION IN A CONSUMER CONTRACT THAT
4 GRANTS A CONSUMER A RIGHT OR A MANNER OF ENFORCEMENT MORE EXPANSIVE
5 OR EXTENSIVE THAN A RIGHT CONFERRED OR CREATED OR A MEANS OR MANNER OF
6 ENFORCEMENT ESTABLISHED CREATE A RIGHT OR REMEDY NOT OTHERWISE
7 PROVIDED BY FEDERAL OR STATE LAW.
8 (3) (I) A PROVISION OF THIS SECTION MAY NOT BE WAIVED BY
9 AGREEMENT.
10 (II) A CONSUMER MAY WAIVE A RIGHT CONFERRED OR
11 CREATED OR A MANNER OF ENFORCEMENT ESTABLISHED BY FEDERAL OR STATE
12 LAW AS PART OF A SETTLEMENT OF A DISPUTE OR AN ACTION, UNLESS OTHERWISE
13 PROHIBITED BY FEDERAL LAW.
14 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That, if any provision of this Act or
15 the application of any provision of this Act to any person or circumstance is held invalid for
16 any reason in a court of competent jurisdiction, the invalidity does not affect other
17 provisions or any other application of this Act that can be given effect without the invalid
18 provision or application, and for this purpose the provisions of this Act are declared
19 severable.
20 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall be construed to
21 apply only prospectively and may not be applied or interpreted to have any effect on or
22 application to any contract entered into before the effective date of this Act.
23 SECTION 4. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect on
24 the later of October 1, 2026, or the taking effect of Chapter 194 of the Acts of the General
25 Assembly of 2025, if the effective date of Chapter 194 is amended.
Approved:
