WES MOORE, Governor Ch. 424
Chapter 424
AN ACT concerning
Criminal Law – Interference With Access to or Egress From a Religious
Facility Obstruction or Interference With Exercise of Religious Beliefs –
Prohibition
FOR the purpose of prohibiting a person, acting alone or with others, from preventing
another from entering or exiting a religious facility; and generally relating to access
to or egress from religious facilities person from using force, threat of force, or
physical obstruction to interfere with or physically injure another while the other is
lawfully exercising or attempting to lawfully exercise religious beliefs at a place of
religious worship; and generally relating to obstruction or interference with the
exercise of religious beliefs.
BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
Article – Criminal Law

Section 2:
Section 10–204 and 10–302
Annotated Code of Maryland
(2021 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
BY adding to
Article – Criminal Law

Section 3:
Section 10–204.1
Annotated Code of Maryland
(2021 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

Section 4:
SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
Article – Criminal Law
10–204.
(a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
(2) (i) “Medical facility” means:

Section 5:
1. a facility as defined in § 10–101 of the Health – General
Article; or

Section 6:
2. a health care facility as defined in § 19–114 of the
Health – General Article.
– 1 –
Ch. 424 2026 LAWS OF MARYLAND
(ii) “Medical facility” includes an agency, clinic, or office operated
under the direction of the local health officer or under the regulatory authority of the
Maryland Department of Health.
(b) (1) This section does not apply to:
(i) the chief executive officer of the medical facility;
(ii) a designee of the chief executive officer of the medical facility;
(iii) an agent of the medical facility; or
(iv) a law enforcement officer.
(2) This section does not prohibit:
(i) speech; or
(ii) picketing in connection with a labor dispute as defined in §
4–301 of the Labor and Employment Article.
(c) A person may not intentionally act, alone or with others, to prevent another
from entering or exiting a medical facility by physically:
(1) detaining the other; or
(2) obstructing, impeding, or hindering the other’s passage.
(d) A person who violates this section is guilty of a misdemeanor and on conviction
is subject to imprisonment not exceeding 90 days or a fine not exceeding $1,000 or both.
10–204.1.
(A) (1) IN THIS SECTION, “RELIGIOUS FACILITY” MEANS REAL PROPERTY
THAT IS OWNED, LEASED, OR USED BY A RELIGIOUS ENTITY OR FOR ANY RELIGIOUS
PURPOSE.
(2) “RELIGIOUS FACILITY” INCLUDES:
(I) A PLACE OF WORSHIP;
(II) A CEMETERY;
(III) A RELIGIOUS SCHOOL, EDUCATIONAL FACILITY, OR
COMMUNITY CENTER; AND

(IV) THE GROUNDS ADJACENT TO THEM.
(B) (1) THIS SECTION DOES NOT APPLY TO:
(I) THE PERSON IN CHARGE OF THE RELIGIOUS FACILITY;
(II) A DESIGNEE OF THE PERSON IN CHARGE OF THE RELIGIOUS
FACILITY;
(III) AN AGENT OF THE RELIGIOUS FACILITY;
(IV) A LAW ENFORCEMENT OFFICER; OR
(V) A PERSON:

Section 7:
1. AUTHORIZED BY THE RELIGIOUS AUTHORITY TO
PROTECT THE RELIGIOUS FACILITY OR THE RELIGIOUS FACILITY’S ACTIVITIES; OR

Section 8:
2. OTHERWISE ACTING IN GOOD FAITH ON THE
RELIGIOUS FACILITY’S BEHALF TO PROTECT THE RELIGIOUS FACILITY OR THE
RELIGIOUS FACILITY’S ACTIVITIES.
(2) THIS SECTION DOES NOT PROHIBIT SPEECH.
(C) A RELIGIOUS FACILITY HAS SOLE DISCRETION IN DETERMINING WHO
MAY BE ON THE RELIGIOUS FACILITY’S PREMISES, AS DETERMINED BY THE
INTERNAL DICTATES OF THE RELIGIOUS ORGANIZATION.
(D) A PERSON MAY NOT INTENTIONALLY ACT, ALONE OR WITH OTHERS, TO
PREVENT ANOTHER FROM ENTERING OR EXITING A RELIGIOUS FACILITY BY
PHYSICALLY:
(1) DETAINING THE OTHER; OR
(2) OBSTRUCTING, IMPEDING, OR HINDERING THE OTHER’S PASSAGE.
(E) A PERSON WHO VIOLATES THIS SECTION IS GUILTY OF A MISDEMEANOR
AND ON CONVICTION IS SUBJECT TO IMPRISONMENT NOT EXCEEDING 90 DAYS OR A
FINE NOT EXCEEDING $1,000 OR BOTH.
10–302.

A person may not deface, damage, or destroy, or attempt or threaten to deface,
damage, or destroy, personal or real property that is owned, leased, or used by a religious
entity or for any religious purpose including:
(1) a place of worship;
(2) a cemetery;
(3) a religious school, educational facility, or community center; and
(4) the grounds adjacent to them.
10–204.1.
(A) A PERSON MAY NOT USE FORCE, THREAT OF FORCE, OR PHYSICAL
OBSTRUCTION TO INTERFERE WITH OR PHYSICALLY INJURE ANOTHER WHILE THE
OTHER IS LAWFULLY EXERCISING OR ATTEMPTING TO LAWFULLY EXERCISE THE
RIGHT OF RELIGIOUS FREEDOM UNDER THE FIRST AMENDMENT OF THE U.S.
CONSTITUTION AT A PLACE OF RELIGIOUS WORSHIP.
(B) A PERSON WHO VIOLATES THIS SECTION IS GUILTY OF A MISDEMEANOR
AND ON CONVICTION IS SUBJECT TO IMPRISONMENT NOT EXCEEDING 90 DAYS OR A
FINE NOT EXCEEDING $1,000 OR BOTH.

Section 9:
SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
October 1, 2026.
Approved by the Governor, May 12, 2026.
