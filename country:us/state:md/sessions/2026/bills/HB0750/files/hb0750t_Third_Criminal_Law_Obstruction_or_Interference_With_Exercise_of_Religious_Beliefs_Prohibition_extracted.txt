CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Criminal Law – Interference With Access to or Egress From a Religious
3 Facility Obstruction or Interference With Exercise of Religious Beliefs –
4 Prohibition
5 FOR the purpose of prohibiting a person, acting alone or with others, from preventing
6 another from entering or exiting a religious facility; and generally relating to access
7 to or egress from religious facilities person from using force, threat of force, or
8 physical obstruction to interfere with or physically injure another while the other is
9 lawfully exercising or attempting to lawfully exercise religious beliefs at a place of
10 religious worship; and generally relating to obstruction or interference with the
11 exercise of religious beliefs.
12 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
13 Article – Criminal Law
14 Section 10–204 and 10–302
15 Annotated Code of Maryland
16 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
17 BY adding to
18 Article – Criminal Law
19 Section 10–204.1
20 Annotated Code of Maryland
21 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

2 HOUSE BILL 750
1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – Criminal Law
4 10–204.
5 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
6 (2) (i) “Medical facility” means:
7 1. a facility as defined in § 10–101 of the Health – General
8 Article; or
9 2. a health care facility as defined in § 19–114 of the
10 Health – General Article.
11 (ii) “Medical facility” includes an agency, clinic, or office operated
12 under the direction of the local health officer or under the regulatory authority of the
13 Maryland Department of Health.
14 (b) (1) This section does not apply to:
15 (i) the chief executive officer of the medical facility;
16 (ii) a designee of the chief executive officer of the medical facility;
17 (iii) an agent of the medical facility; or
18 (iv) a law enforcement officer.
19 (2) This section does not prohibit:
20 (i) speech; or
21 (ii) picketing in connection with a labor dispute as defined in §
22 4–301 of the Labor and Employment Article.
23 (c) A person may not intentionally act, alone or with others, to prevent another
24 from entering or exiting a medical facility by physically:
25 (1) detaining the other; or
26 (2) obstructing, impeding, or hindering the other’s passage.
27 (d) A person who violates this section is guilty of a misdemeanor and on conviction
28 is subject to imprisonment not exceeding 90 days or a fine not exceeding $1,000 or both.
HOUSE BILL 750 3
1 10–204.1.
2 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION, “RELIGIOUS FACILITY” MEANS REAL PROPERTY
3 THAT IS OWNED, LEASED, OR USED BY A RELIGIOUS ENTITY OR FOR ANY RELIGIOUS
4 PURPOSE.
5 (2) “RELIGIOUS FACILITY” INCLUDES:
6 (I) A PLACE OF WORSHIP;
7 (II) A CEMETERY;
8 (III) A RELIGIOUS SCHOOL, EDUCATIONAL FACILITY, OR
9 COMMUNITY CENTER; AND
10 (IV) THE GROUNDS ADJACENT TO THEM.
11 (B) (1) THIS SECTION DOES NOT APPLY TO:
12 (I) THE PERSON IN CHARGE OF THE RELIGIOUS FACILITY;
13 (II) A DESIGNEE OF THE PERSON IN CHARGE OF THE RELIGIOUS
14 FACILITY;
15 (III) AN AGENT OF THE RELIGIOUS FACILITY;
16 (IV) A LAW ENFORCEMENT OFFICER; OR
17 (V) A PERSON:
18 1. AUTHORIZED BY THE RELIGIOUS AUTHORITY TO
19 PROTECT THE RELIGIOUS FACILITY OR THE RELIGIOUS FACILITY’S ACTIVITIES; OR
20 2. OTHERWISE ACTING IN GOOD FAITH ON THE
21 RELIGIOUS FACILITY’S BEHALF TO PROTECT THE RELIGIOUS FACILITY OR THE
22 RELIGIOUS FACILITY’S ACTIVITIES.
23 (2) THIS SECTION DOES NOT PROHIBIT SPEECH.
24 (C) A RELIGIOUS FACILITY HAS SOLE DISCRETION IN DETERMINING WHO
25 MAY BE ON THE RELIGIOUS FACILITY’S PREMISES, AS DETERMINED BY THE
26 INTERNAL DICTATES OF THE RELIGIOUS ORGANIZATION.

12 entity or for any religious purpose including:
13 (1) a place of worship;
14 (2) a cemetery;
15 (3) a religious school, educational facility, or community center; and
16 (4) the grounds adjacent to them.

18 (A) A PERSON MAY NOT USE FORCE, THREAT OF FORCE, OR PHYSICAL
19 OBSTRUCTION TO INTERFERE WITH OR PHYSICALLY INJURE ANOTHER WHILE THE
