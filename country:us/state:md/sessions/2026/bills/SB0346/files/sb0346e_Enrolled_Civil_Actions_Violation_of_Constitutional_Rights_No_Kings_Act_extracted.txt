CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Civil Actions – Violation of Constitutional Rights
3 (No Kings Act)
4 FOR the purpose of establishing certain civil liability for an individual who, under color of
5 law, deprives another, or causes or allows another to be deprived, of a right, a
6 privilege, or an immunity secured by the U.S. Constitution or the laws of the United
7 States; and generally relating to civil liability for the deprivation of Constitutional
8 rights.
9 BY adding to
10 Article – Courts and Judicial Proceedings
11 Section 3–2701 and 3–2702 to be under the new subtitle “Subtitle 27. Action for
12 Deprivation of Constitutional Rights”
13 Annotated Code of Maryland

2 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
3 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
4 Article – Courts and Judicial Proceedings
5 SUBTITLE 27. ACTION FOR DEPRIVATION OF CONSTITUTIONAL RIGHTS.
6 3–2701.
7 IN THIS SUBTITLE, (A) IN THIS SUBTITLE THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE
8 MEANINGS INDICATED.
9 (B) (1) “COVERED OFFICER” MEANS, EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN
10 PARAGRAPH (2) OF THIS SUBSECTION, AN OFFICER WHO MAY:
11 (I) MAKE AN ARREST WITH OR WITHOUT A WARRANT FOR
12 VIOLATIONS OF THE UNITED STATES CODE; AND
13 (II) CARRY FIREARMS IN THE PERFORMANCE OF THE OFFICER’S
14 DUTIES.
15 (2) “COVERED OFFICER” DOES NOT INCLUDE A LAW ENFORCEMENT
16 OFFICER, AS DEFINED IN § 1–101 OF THE PUBLIC SAFETY ARTICLE, ACTING UNDER
17 THE AUTHORITY OF A JOINT TASK FORCE THAT PRIMARILY ENFORCES THE UNITED
18 STATES CODE.
19 (C) “LAW LAW” INCLUDES:
20 (1) THE U.S. CONSTITUTION; AND
21 (2) THE MARYLAND DECLARATION OF RIGHTS;
22 (3) THE MARYLAND CONSTITUTION;
23 (4) (2) THE LAWS OF THE UNITED STATES; AND.
24 (5) THE LAWS OF MARYLAND.
25 3–2702.
26 (A) (1) AN AGGRIEVED PARTY OR THE OFFICE OF THE ATTORNEY
27 GENERAL MAY BRING AN ACTION AGAINST AN INDIVIDUAL A COVERED OFFICER
SENATE BILL 346 3
1 WHO, UNDER COLOR OF LAW, DEPRIVES THE AGGRIEVED PARTY OR CAUSES OR
2 ALLOWS THE AGGRIEVED PARTY TO BE DEPRIVED OR CAUSES OR ALLOWS THE
3 AGGRIEVED PARTY TO BE DEPRIVED OF A RIGHT, A PRIVILEGE, OR AN IMMUNITY
4 SECURED BY THE U.S. CONSTITUTION UNDER THE LAW.
5 (2) SUBJECT TO PARAGRAPH (3) OF THIS SUBSECTION, IN AN ACTION
6 UNDER THIS SECTION, AN AGGRIEVED PARTY AN AGGRIEVED PARTY OR THE OFFICE
7 OF THE ATTORNEY GENERAL MAY SEEK DAMAGES AND DECLARATORY AND
8 INJUNCTIVE RELIEF IN AN ACTION BROUGHT UNDER THIS SECTION.
9 (3) IN AN ACTION UNDER THIS SECTION AGAINST A JUDICIAL
10 OFFICER, INJUNCTIVE RELIEF IS AVAILABLE ONLY IF A DECLARATORY JUDGMENT
11 IS VIOLATED OR DECLARATORY RELIEF IS UNAVAILABLE.
12 (B) (1) A DEFENDANT IN AN ACTION UNDER THIS SECTION MAY ASSERT A
13 DEFENSE OF ABSOLUTE OR QUALIFIED IMMUNITY TO THE SAME EXTENT AS A
14 PERSON SUED UNDER 42 U.S.C. § 1983 UNDER LIKE CIRCUMSTANCES.
15 (2) THIS SECTION MAY NOT BE CONSTRUED TO WAIVE OR ABROGATE
16 ANY DEFENSE OF SOVEREIGN IMMUNITY OTHERWISE AVAILABLE A DEFENDANT IN
17 AN ACTION UNDER THIS SECTION MAY ASSERT ANY DEFENSE OF IMMUNITY THAT
18 WAS AVAILABLE AT THE TIME THE CAUSE OF ACTION ACCRUED.
19 (C) (1) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN PARAGRAPH (2) OF THIS SUBSECTION, A
20 COURT MAY AWARD REASONABLE FEES AND COSTS, INCLUDING ATTORNEY’S FEES
21 AND EXPERT WITNESS FEES, TO A PREVAILING PLAINTIFF UNDER THIS SECTION.
22 (2) A COURT MAY NOT AWARD FEES OR COSTS UNDER THIS SECTION
23 AGAINST A JUDICIAL OFFICER UNLESS THE JUDICIAL OFFICER ACTED CLEARLY IN
24 EXCESS OF THE JUDICIAL OFFICER’S JURISDICTION A COURT MAY AWARD
25 REASONABLE FEES AND COSTS, INCLUDING ATTORNEY’S FEES AND EXPERT WITNESS
26 FEES, TO A PREVAILING PLAINTIFF UNDER THIS SECTION.
27 (D) AN ACTION UNDER THIS SECTION SHALL BE FILED WITHIN 3 YEARS
28 AFTER THE CAUSE OF ACTION ACCRUES
29 (1) AN ACTION UNDER THIS SECTION SHALL BE FILED WITHIN 5
30 YEARS AFTER THE CAUSE OF ACTION ACCRUED.
31 (2) THIS SECTION MAY NOT BE CONSTRUED TO ABROGATE:
4 SENATE BILL 346
1 (I) ANY STATE OR LOCAL GOVERNMENT LIABILITY UNDER
2 STATE OR COMMON LAW FOR THE ACTIONS OF STATE OR LOCAL GOVERNMENT
3 EMPLOYEES; OR
4 (II) ANY STATUTORY WAIVER OF ANY DEFENSE, INCLUDING
5 IMMUNITY.
6 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall apply to any
7 civil action pending on the effective date of this Act.
8 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
9 October 1, 2026
10 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That, if any provision of this Act or
11 the application of any provision of this Act to any person or circumstance is held invalid for
12 any reason in a court of competent jurisdiction, the invalidity does not affect other
13 provisions or any other application of this Act that can be given effect without the invalid
14 provision or application, and for this purpose the provisions of this Act are declared
15 severable.
16 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
17 October 1, 2026.
