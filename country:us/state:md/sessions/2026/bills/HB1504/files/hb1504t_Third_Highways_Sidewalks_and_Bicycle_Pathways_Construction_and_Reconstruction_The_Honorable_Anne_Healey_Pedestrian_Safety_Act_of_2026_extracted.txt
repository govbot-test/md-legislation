CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Highways – Sidewalks and Bicycle Pathways – Construction and
3 Reconstruction
4 (The Honorable Anne Healey Pedestrian Safety Act of 2026)
5 FOR the purpose of requiring the State Highway Administration to prioritize certain
6 funding for the construction and reconstruction of sidewalks and bicycle pathways
7 to sidewalks and bicycle pathways that are, or are adjacent to highways that are,
8 subject to a complete streets policy or another similar Vision Zero program; requiring
9 a local government to request certain funding from the Department of
10 Transportation in a certain manner; authorizing certain State funding for the
11 construction or reconstruction of sidewalks or bicycle pathways under certain
12 circumstances; and generally relating to the construction and reconstruction of
13 sidewalks and bicycle pathways.
14 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
15 Article – Transportation
16 Section 8–630
17 Annotated Code of Maryland
18 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
19 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
20 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

1 Article – Transportation
2 8–630.
3 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
4 (2) “Municipal corporation” means a municipality as defined in § 1–101 of
5 the Local Government Article.
6 (3) “Urban highway” means a highway, other than an expressway, that is:
7 (i) 1. Constructed with a curb and gutter and an enclosed type
8 storm drainage system;
9 2. Located in an urban area and on which is located a public
10 facility that creates appreciable pedestrian traffic along the highway from adjacent areas;
11 3. Located within urban boundaries as defined by the U.S.
12 Census Bureau; or
13 4. Located within the boundaries of a municipal corporation;
14 and
15 (ii) Part of the State highway system.
16 (b) (1) Sidewalks shall be constructed at the time of construction or
17 reconstruction of an urban highway, or in response to the request of a local government
18 unless:
19 (i) The Administration determines that the cost or impacts of
20 constructing the sidewalks would be too great in relation to the need for them or their
21 probable use; or
22 (ii) The local government indicates that there is no need for
23 sidewalks.
24 (2) Sidewalks constructed under this section shall be consistent with area
25 master plans and transportation plans adopted by the local planning commission.
26 (c) (1) If sidewalks or bicycle pathways are constructed or reconstructed as
27 part of a roadway construction or reconstruction project, the Administration shall fund the
28 sidewalk or bicycle pathway construction or reconstruction as a part of the cost of the
29 roadway project.
30 (2) Except as provided in paragraphs (3) and (4) of this subsection, if
31 sidewalks or bicycle pathways are constructed or reconstructed in response to a request
32 from a local government IN ITS ANNUAL PRIORITY LETTER FOR THE DEPARTMENT’S
HOUSE BILL 1504 3
1 CONSIDERATION FOR INCLUSION IN THE CONSOLIDATED TRANSPORTATION
2 PROGRAM UNDER § 2–103.1 OF THIS ARTICLE and the adjacent roadway is not being
3 concurrently constructed or reconstructed, the cost to construct or reconstruct the sidewalk
4 or bicycle pathway shall be shared equally between the State and local governments.
5 (3) If sidewalks or bicycle pathways within a sustainable community as
6 defined in § 6–301 of the Housing and Community Development Article are constructed or
7 reconstructed in response to a request from a local government IN ITS ANNUAL PRIORITY
8 LETTER FOR THE DEPARTMENT’S CONSIDERATION FOR INCLUSION IN THE
9 CONSOLIDATED TRANSPORTATION PROGRAM UNDER § 2–103.1 OF THIS ARTICLE
10 and the adjacent roadway is not being concurrently constructed or reconstructed, the cost
11 to construct or reconstruct the sidewalk or bicycle pathway may be funded entirely by the
12 State.
13 (4) (i) This paragraph does not apply to a priority funding area that is
14 a sustainable community as defined in § 6–301 of the Housing and Community
15 Development Article.
16 (ii) IN DETERMINING FUNDING FOR THE CONSTRUCTION AND
17 RECONSTRUCTION OF SIDEWALKS AND BICYCLE PATHWAYS UNDER THIS
18 PARAGRAPH FROM THE FUNDING PROGRAMMED FOR SIDEWALKS AND BICYCLE
19 PATHWAYS BY THE DEPARTMENT IN THE CONSOLIDATED TRANSPORTATION
20 PROGRAM UNDER § 2–103.1 OF THIS ARTICLE, THE ADMINISTRATION SHALL
21 PRIORITIZE FUNDING FOR SIDEWALKS AND BICYCLE PATHWAYS THAT ARE, OR ARE
22 ADJACENT TO HIGHWAYS THAT ARE, SUBJECT TO:
23 1. A COMPLETE STREETS POLICY, AS DEFINED IN §
24 2–112 OF THIS ARTICLE; OR
25 2. ANOTHER SIMILAR VISION ZERO PROGRAM UNDER
26 SUBTITLE 10 OF THIS TITLE.
27 (III) If sidewalks or bicycle pathways within an area designated as a
28 priority funding area under § 5–7B–02 of the State Finance and Procurement Article are
29 constructed or reconstructed in response to a request from a local government IN ITS
30 ANNUAL PRIORITY LETTER FOR THE DEPARTMENT’S CONSIDERATION FOR
31 INCLUSION IN THE CONSOLIDATED TRANSPORTATION PROGRAM UNDER § 2–103.1
32 OF THIS ARTICLE and the adjacent roadway is not being concurrently constructed or
33 reconstructed, and if the Administration determines that construction would not occur
34 under this section due to insufficient contribution of funds by the local government, the cost
35 to construct or reconstruct the sidewalk or bicycle pathway shall be shared between the
36 State and local government as follows:
37 1. 75 percent of the cost shall be funded by the State; and
4 HOUSE BILL 1504
1 2. 25 percent of the cost shall be funded by the local
2 government.
3 [(iii)] (IV) If sidewalks or bicycle pathways within an area
4 designated as a priority funding area under § 5–7B–02 of the State Finance and
5 Procurement Article are constructed or reconstructed based on a determination by the
6 Administration that a substantial public safety risk or significant impediment to
7 pedestrian access exists and the adjacent roadway is not being concurrently constructed or
8 reconstructed, then:
9 1. The Administration shall categorize the sidewalk or
10 bicycle pathway construction project as “system preservation” and give corresponding
11 funding priority to the project; and
12 2. The cost to construct or reconstruct the sidewalk or bicycle
13 pathway AND TO ACQUIRE THE NECESSARY RIGHT–OF–WAY may be funded entirely by
14 the State.
15 (5) If sidewalks or bicycle pathways are being constructed or reconstructed
16 in response to a request from a local government IN ITS ANNUAL PRIORITY LETTER FOR
17 THE DEPARTMENT’S CONSIDERATION FOR INCLUSION IN THE CONSOLIDATED
18 TRANSPORTATION PROGRAM UNDER § 2–103.1 OF THIS ARTICLE and the adjacent
19 roadway is not being concurrently constructed or reconstructed, the local government shall:
20 (i) Provide public notice and opportunities for community
21 involvement prior to the construction of a sidewalk or bicycle pathway project; and
22 (ii) Secure any necessary right–of–way that may be needed beyond
23 the right–of–way already owned by the State.
24 (6) (i) Except as provided in subparagraph (ii) of this paragraph, after
25 sidewalks and bicycle pathways are constructed under this section, they shall be
26 maintained and repaired by the political subdivision in which they are located.
27 (ii) Subject to approval and the availability of funds, the
28 Administration promptly shall reimburse a political subdivision for the preapproved and
29 documented costs incurred in reconstructing a segment of a sidewalk or bicycle pathway
30 that has deteriorated to the extent that repair is not practical or desirable for public safety.
31 (d) The Administration may not construct any project that will result in the
32 severance or destruction of an existing major route for pedestrian transportation traffic,
33 unless the project provides for construction of a reasonable alternative route or such a route
34 already exists.
35 (e) The Administration shall develop guidelines jointly with local governments to
36 carry out the provisions of this section.

1 (f) The Administration shall maintain and repair all facilities for nighttime
2 illumination that:
3 (1) Are constructed by the Administration for the safe conduct of vehicular
4 traffic; and
5 (2) Exist adjacent to urban highways.
6 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
7 October 1, 2026.
