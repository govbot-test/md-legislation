2 SENATE BILL 333
1 IN ORDER TO STRENGTHEN ACCESS TO HEALTH CARE, AND IN RECOGNITION
2 OF THE ADVANCES IN THE DELIVERY IN HEALTH CARE, THE MEMBER STATES OF THE
3 INTERSTATE PODIATRIC MEDICAL LICENSURE COMPACT HAVE ALLIED IN COMMON
4 PURPOSE TO DEVELOP A COMPREHENSIVE PROCESS THAT COMPLEMENTS THE
5 EXISTING LICENSING AND REGULATORY AUTHORITY OF STATE PODIATRIC MEDICAL
6 BOARDS AND PROVIDES A STREAMLINED PROCESS THAT ALLOWS PODIATRISTS TO
7 BECOME LICENSED IN MULTIPLE STATES, THEREBY ENHANCING THE PORTABILITY
8 OF A PODIATRIC MEDICAL LICENSE AND ENSURING THE SAFETY OF PATIENTS. THE
9 COMPACT CREATES ANOTHER PATHWAY FOR LICENSURE AND DOES NOT
10 OTHERWISE CHANGE A STATE’S EXISTING PODIATRIC MEDICAL PRACTICE ACT.
11 THE COMPACT ALSO ADOPTS THE PREVAILING STANDARD FOR LICENSURE AND
12 AFFIRMS THAT THE PRACTICE OF PODIATRIC MEDICINE OCCURS WHERE THE
13 PATIENT IS LOCATED AT THE TIME OF THE PODIATRIST–PATIENT ENCOUNTER, AND
14 THEREFORE, REQUIRES THE PODIATRIST TO BE UNDER THE JURISDICTION OF THE
15 STATE PODIATRIC MEDICAL BOARD WHERE THE PATIENT IS LOCATED. STATE
16 PODIATRIC MEDICAL BOARDS THAT PARTICIPATE IN THE COMPACT RETAIN THE
17 JURISDICTION TO IMPOSE AN ADVERSE ACTION AGAINST A LICENSE TO PRACTICE
18 PODIATRIC MEDICINE IN THAT STATE ISSUED TO A PODIATRIST THROUGH THE
19 PROCEDURES IN THE COMPACT.

3 SECTION 3. ELIGIBILITY
4 (A) A PODIATRIST MUST MEET THE ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS AS
5 DEFINED IN SECTION 2(M) TO RECEIVE AN EXPEDITED LICENSURE UNDER THE
6 TERMS AND PROVISIONS OF THE COMPACT.
7 (B) A PODIATRIST WHO DOES NOT MEET THE REQUIREMENTS OF SECTION
8 2(M) MAY OBTAIN A LICENSE TO PRACTICE PODIATRIC MEDICINE IN A MEMBER
9 STATE IF THE INDIVIDUAL COMPLIES WITH ALL LAWS AND REQUIREMENTS, OTHER
10 THAN THE COMPACT, RELATING TO THE ISSUANCE OF A LICENSE TO PRACTICE
11 PODIATRIC MEDICINE IN THAT STATE.
12 SECTION 4. DESIGNATION OF STATE OF PRINCIPAL LICENSE
13 (A) A PODIATRIST SHALL DESIGNATE A MEMBER STATE AS THE STATE OF
14 PRINCIPAL LICENSE FOR PURPOSES OF REGISTRATION FOR EXPEDITED LICENSURE
15 THROUGH THE COMPACT IF THE PODIATRIST POSSESSES A FULL AND
16 UNRESTRICTED LICENSE TO PRACTICE PODIATRIC MEDICINE IN THAT STATE, AND
17 THE STATE IS:
18 (1) THE STATE OF PRINCIPAL RESIDENCE FOR THE PODIATRIST;
19 (2) THE STATE WHERE AT LEAST 25% OF THE PRACTICE OF
20 PODIATRIC MEDICINE OCCURS;
21 (3) THE LOCATION OF THE PODIATRIST’S EMPLOYER; OR
22 (4) IF NO STATE QUALIFIES UNDER SUBSECTION (1), SUBSECTION (2),
23 OR SUBSECTION (3), THE STATE DESIGNATED AS STATE OF RESIDENCE FOR
24 PURPOSE OF FEDERAL INCOME TAX.
25 (B) A PODIATRIST MAY REDESIGNATE A MEMBER STATE AS STATE OF
26 PRINCIPAL LICENSE AT ANY TIME, AS LONG AS THE STATE MEETS THE
27 REQUIREMENTS OF SUBSECTION (A).
28 (C) THE INTERSTATE COMMISSION IS AUTHORIZED TO DEVELOP RULES TO
29 FACILITATE REDESIGNATION OF ANOTHER MEMBER STATE AS THE STATE OF
30 PRINCIPAL LICENSE.

2 SENATE BILL 333
1 IN ORDER TO STRENGTHEN ACCESS TO HEALTH CARE, AND IN RECOGNITION
2 OF THE ADVANCES IN THE DELIVERY IN HEALTH CARE, THE MEMBER STATES OF THE
3 INTERSTATE PODIATRIC MEDICAL LICENSURE COMPACT HAVE ALLIED IN COMMON
4 PURPOSE TO DEVELOP A COMPREHENSIVE PROCESS THAT COMPLEMENTS THE
5 EXISTING LICENSING AND REGULATORY AUTHORITY OF STATE PODIATRIC MEDICAL
6 BOARDS AND PROVIDES A STREAMLINED PROCESS THAT ALLOWS PODIATRISTS TO
7 BECOME LICENSED IN MULTIPLE STATES, THEREBY ENHANCING THE PORTABILITY
8 OF A PODIATRIC MEDICAL LICENSE AND ENSURING THE SAFETY OF PATIENTS. THE
9 COMPACT CREATES ANOTHER PATHWAY FOR LICENSURE AND DOES NOT
10 OTHERWISE CHANGE A STATE’S EXISTING PODIATRIC MEDICAL PRACTICE ACT.
11 THE COMPACT ALSO ADOPTS THE PREVAILING STANDARD FOR LICENSURE AND
12 AFFIRMS THAT THE PRACTICE OF PODIATRIC MEDICINE OCCURS WHERE THE
13 PATIENT IS LOCATED AT THE TIME OF THE PODIATRIST–PATIENT ENCOUNTER, AND
14 THEREFORE, REQUIRES THE PODIATRIST TO BE UNDER THE JURISDICTION OF THE
15 STATE PODIATRIC MEDICAL BOARD WHERE THE PATIENT IS LOCATED. STATE
16 PODIATRIC MEDICAL BOARDS THAT PARTICIPATE IN THE COMPACT RETAIN THE
17 JURISDICTION TO IMPOSE AN ADVERSE ACTION AGAINST A LICENSE TO PRACTICE
18 PODIATRIC MEDICINE IN THAT STATE ISSUED TO A PODIATRIST THROUGH THE
19 PROCEDURES IN THE COMPACT.

3 SECTION 3. ELIGIBILITY
4 (A) A PODIATRIST MUST MEET THE ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS AS
5 DEFINED IN SECTION 2(M) TO RECEIVE AN EXPEDITED LICENSURE UNDER THE
6 TERMS AND PROVISIONS OF THE COMPACT.
7 (B) A PODIATRIST WHO DOES NOT MEET THE REQUIREMENTS OF SECTION
8 2(M) MAY OBTAIN A LICENSE TO PRACTICE PODIATRIC MEDICINE IN A MEMBER
9 STATE IF THE INDIVIDUAL COMPLIES WITH ALL LAWS AND REQUIREMENTS, OTHER
10 THAN THE COMPACT, RELATING TO THE ISSUANCE OF A LICENSE TO PRACTICE
11 PODIATRIC MEDICINE IN THAT STATE.
12 SECTION 4. DESIGNATION OF STATE OF PRINCIPAL LICENSE
13 (A) A PODIATRIST SHALL DESIGNATE A MEMBER STATE AS THE STATE OF
14 PRINCIPAL LICENSE FOR PURPOSES OF REGISTRATION FOR EXPEDITED LICENSURE
15 THROUGH THE COMPACT IF THE PODIATRIST POSSESSES A FULL AND
16 UNRESTRICTED LICENSE TO PRACTICE PODIATRIC MEDICINE IN THAT STATE, AND
17 THE STATE IS:
18 (1) THE STATE OF PRINCIPAL RESIDENCE FOR THE PODIATRIST;
19 (2) THE STATE WHERE AT LEAST 25% OF THE PRACTICE OF
20 PODIATRIC MEDICINE OCCURS;
21 (3) THE LOCATION OF THE PODIATRIST’S EMPLOYER; OR
22 (4) IF NO STATE QUALIFIES UNDER SUBSECTION (1), SUBSECTION (2),
23 OR SUBSECTION (3), THE STATE DESIGNATED AS STATE OF RESIDENCE FOR
24 PURPOSE OF FEDERAL INCOME TAX.
25 (B) A PODIATRIST MAY REDESIGNATE A MEMBER STATE AS STATE OF
26 PRINCIPAL LICENSE AT ANY TIME, AS LONG AS THE STATE MEETS THE
27 REQUIREMENTS OF SUBSECTION (A).
28 (C) THE INTERSTATE COMMISSION IS AUTHORIZED TO DEVELOP RULES TO
29 FACILITATE REDESIGNATION OF ANOTHER MEMBER STATE AS THE STATE OF
30 PRINCIPAL LICENSE.

8 TAKE ALL ACTIONS NECESSARY AND APPROPRIATE TO EFFECTUATE THE
9 COMPACT’S PURPOSES AND INTENT. THE PROVISIONS OF THE COMPACT AND THE
10 RULES PROMULGATED HEREUNDER SHALL HAVE STANDING AS STATUTORY LAW
11 BUT MAY NOT OVERRIDE EXISTING STATE AUTHORITY TO REGULATE THE PRACTICE
12 OF PODIATRIC MEDICINE.
13 (B) ALL COURTS SHALL TAKE JUDICIAL NOTICE OF THE COMPACT AND THE
14 RULES IN ANY JUDICIAL OR ADMINISTRATIVE PROCEEDING IN A MEMBER STATE
15 PERTAINING TO THE SUBJECT MATTER OF THE COMPACT WHICH MAY AFFECT THE
16 POWERS, RESPONSIBILITIES OR ACTIONS OF THE INTERSTATE COMMISSION.
17 (C) THE INTERSTATE COMMISSION SHALL BE ENTITLED TO RECEIVE ALL
18 SERVICES OF PROCESS IN ANY SUCH PROCEEDING, AND SHALL HAVE STANDING TO
19 INTERVENE IN THE PROCEEDING FOR ALL PURPOSES. FAILURE TO PROVIDE
20 SERVICE OF PROCESS TO THE INTERSTATE COMMISSION SHALL RENDER
21 JUDGEMENT OR ORDER VOID AS TO THE INTERSTATE COMMISSION, THE COMPACT,
22 OR PROMULGATED RULES.
23 SECTION 17. ENFORCEMENT OF INTERSTATE COMPACT
24 (A) THE INTERSTATE COMMISSION, IN THE REASONABLE EXERCISE OF ITS
25 DISCRETION, SHALL ENFORCE THE PROVISIONS AND RULES OF THE COMPACT.
26 (B) THE INTERSTATE COMMISSION MAY, BY MAJORITY VOTE OF THE
27 COMMISSIONERS PRESENT AND VOTING, INITIATE LEGAL ACTION IN THE UNITED
28 STATES COURT FOR THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA, OR, AT THE DISCRETION OF THE
29 INTERSTATE COMMISSION, IN THE FEDERAL DISTRICT WHERE THE INTERSTATE
30 COMMISSION HAS ITS PRINCIPAL OFFICES, TO ENFORCE COMPLIANCE WITH THE
31 PROVISIONS OF THE COMPACT, AND ITS PROMULGATED RULES AND BYLAWS,
32 AGAINST A MEMBER STATE IN DEFAULT. THE RELIEF SOUGHT MAY INCLUDE BOTH
33 INJUNCTIVE RELIEF AND DAMAGES. IN THE EVENT JUDICIAL ENFORCEMENT IS
34 NECESSARY, THE PREVAILING PARTY SHALL BE AWARDED ALL COSTS OF SUCH
35 LITIGATION INCLUDING REASONABLE ATTORNEY’S FEES.

1 (C) THE REMEDIES HEREIN MAY NOT BE THE EXCLUSIVE REMEDIES OF THE
2 INTERSTATE COMMISSION. THE INTERSTATE COMMISSION MAY AVAIL ITSELF OF

4 PROFESSION.
5 SECTION 18. DEFAULT PROCEDURES
6 (A) THE GROUNDS FOR DEFAULT INCLUDE, BUT ARE NOT LIMITED TO,
7 FAILURE OF A MEMBER BOARD TO PERFORM SUCH OBLIGATIONS OR
8 RESPONSIBILITIES IMPOSED UPON IT BY THE COMPACT, OR THE RULES AND
9 BYLAWS OF THE INTERSTATE COMMISSION PROMULGATED UNDER THE COMPACT.
10 (B) IF THE INTERSTATE COMMISSION DETERMINES THAT A MEMBER STATE
11 HAS DEFAULTED IN THE PERFORMANCE OF ITS OBLIGATIONS OR RESPONSIBILITIES
12 UNDER THE COMPACT, OR THE BYLAWS OR PROMULGATED RULES, THE INTERSTATE
13 COMMISSION SHALL:
14 (1) PROVIDE WRITTEN NOTICE TO THE DEFAULTING STATE AND
15 OTHER MEMBER STATES, OF THE NATURE OF THE DEFAULT, THE MEANS OF CURING
16 THE DEFAULT, AND ANY ACTION TAKEN BY THE INTERSTATE COMMISSION. THE
17 INTERSTATE COMMISSION SHALL SPECIFY THE CONDITIONS BY WHICH THE
18 DEFAULTING STATE MUST CURE ITS DEFAULT; AND
19 (2) PROVIDE REMEDIAL TRAINING AND SPECIFIC TECHNICAL
20 ASSISTANCE REGARDING THE DEFAULT.
21 (C) IF THE DEFAULTING STATE FAILS TO CURE THE DEFAULT, THE
22 DEFAULTING STATE SHALL BE TERMINATED FROM THE COMPACT UPON AN
23 AFFIRMATIVE VOTE OF THE MAJORITY OF THE COMMISSIONERS PRESENT AND
24 VOTING, AND ALL RIGHTS, PRIVILEGES, AND BENEFITS CONFERRED BY THE
25 COMPACT SHALL TERMINATE ON THE EFFECTIVE DATE OF TERMINATION. A CURE
26 OF THE DEFAULT DOES NOT RELIEVE THE OFFENDING STATE OF OBLIGATIONS OR
27 LIABILITIES INCURRED DURING THE PERIOD OF DEFAULT.
28 (D) TERMINATION OF MEMBERSHIP IN THE COMPACT SHALL BE IMPOSED
29 ONLY AFTER ALL OTHER MEANS OF SECURING COMPLIANCE HAVE BEEN
30 EXHAUSTED. NOTICE OF INTENT TO TERMINATE SHALL BE GIVEN BY THE
31 INTERSTATE COMMISSION TO THE GOVERNOR, THE MAJORITY AND MINORITY
32 LEADERS OF THE DEFAULTING STATE’S LEGISLATURE, AND EACH OF THE MEMBER
33 STATES.
34 (E) THE INTERSTATE COMMISSION SHALL ESTABLISH RULES AND
35 PROCEDURES TO ADDRESS LICENSES AND PODIATRISTS THAT ARE MATERIALLY

3 (F) THE MEMBER STATE WHICH HAS BEEN TERMINATED IS RESPONSIBLE

16 SECTION 19. DISPUTE RESOLUTION
17 (A) THE INTERSTATE COMMISSION SHALL ATTEMPT, UPON THE REQUEST
18 OF A MEMBER STATE, TO RESOLVE DISPUTES WHICH ARE SUBJECT TO THE COMPACT
19 AND WHICH ARISE AMONG MEMBER STATES OR MEMBER BOARDS.
20 (B) THE INTERSTATE COMMISSION SHALL PROMULGATE RULES
21 PROVIDING FOR BOTH MEDIATION AND BINDING DISPUTE RESOLUTION AS
22 APPROPRIATE.
23 SECTION 20. MEMBER STATES, EFFECTIVE DATE, AND
24 AMENDMENT
25 (A) ANY STATE IS ELIGIBLE TO BECOME A MEMBER OF THE COMPACT.
26 (B) THE COMPACT SHALL BECOME EFFECTIVE AND BINDING UPON
27 LEGISLATIVE ENACTMENT OF THE COMPACT INTO LAW BY NO LESS THAN FOUR
28 STATES. THEREAFTER, IT SHALL BECOME EFFECTIVE AND BINDING ON A STATE
29 UPON ENACTMENT OF THE COMPACT INTO LAW BY THAT STATE.
30 (C) THE GOVERNORS OF NON–MEMBER STATES, OR THEIR DESIGNEES,
31 SHALL BE WELCOME TO PARTICIPATE IN THE ACTIVITIES OF THE INTERSTATE
32 COMMISSION ON A NON–VOTING BASIS PRIOR TO ADOPTION OF THE COMPACT BY
33 ALL STATES.

1 (D) THE INTERSTATE COMMISSION MAY PROPOSE AMENDMENTS TO THE
2 COMPACT FOR ENACTMENT BY THE MEMBER STATES. NO AMENDMENT SHALL
3 BECOME EFFECTIVE AND BINDING UPON THE INTERSTATE COMMISSION AND OTHER
4 MEMBER STATES UNLESS AND UNTIL IT IS ENACTED INTO LAW BY UNANIMOUS
5 CONSENT OF THE MEMBER STATES.
6 SECTION 21. WITHDRAWAL
7 (A) ONCE EFFECTIVE, THE COMPACT SHALL CONTINUE IN FORCE AND
8 REMAIN BINDING UPON EACH AND EVERY MEMBER STATE; PROVIDED THAT A
9 MEMBER STATE MAY WITHDRAW FROM THE COMPACT BY SPECIFICALLY REPEALING
10 THE STATUTE WHICH ENACTED THE COMPACT INTO LAW.
11 (B) WITHDRAWAL FROM THE COMPACT SHALL BE BY THE ENACTMENT OF A
12 STATUTE REPEALING THE SAME, BUT MAY NOT TAKE EFFECT UNTIL ONE YEAR
13 AFTER THE EFFECTIVE DATE OF SUCH STATUTE AND UNTIL WRITTEN NOTICE OF THE
14 WITHDRAWAL HAS BEEN GIVEN BY THE WITHDRAWING STATE TO THE GOVERNOR OF
15 EACH OTHER MEMBER STATE.
16 (C) THE WITHDRAWING STATE SHALL IMMEDIATELY NOTIFY THE
17 CHAIRPERSON OF THE INTERSTATE COMMISSION IN WRITING UPON THE
18 INTRODUCTION OF LEGISLATION REPEALING THE COMPACT IN THE WITHDRAWING
19 STATE.
20 (D) THE INTERSTATE COMMISSION MAY NOTIFY THE OTHER MEMBER
21 STATES OF THE WITHDRAWING STATE’S INTENT TO WITHDRAW WITHIN SIXTY DAYS
22 OF ITS RECEIPT OF NOTICE PROVIDED UNDER SUBSECTION (C).
23 (E) THE WITHDRAWING STATE IS RESPONSIBLE FOR ALL DUES,
24 OBLIGATIONS AND LIABILITIES INCURRED THROUGHOUT THE EFFECTIVE DATE OF
25 WITHDRAWAL, INCLUDING OBLIGATIONS, THE PERFORMANCE OF WHICH EXTEND
26 BEYOND THE EFFECTIVE DATE OF WITHDRAWAL.
27 (F) REINSTATEMENT FOLLOWING WITHDRAWAL OF A MEMBER STATE
28 SHALL OCCUR UPON THE WITHDRAWING STATE REENACTING THE COMPACT OR
29 UPON SUCH LATER DATE AS DETERMINED BY THE INTERSTATE COMMISSION.
30 (G) THE INTERSTATE COMMISSION IS AUTHORIZED TO DEVELOP RULES TO
31 ADDRESS THE IMPACT OF THE WITHDRAWAL OF A MEMBER STATE ON LICENSES
32 GRANTED IN OTHER MEMBER STATES TO PODIATRISTS WHO DESIGNATED THE
33 WITHDRAWING MEMBER STATE AS THE STATE OF PRINCIPAL LICENSE.

22 SENATE BILL 333
1 SECTION 22. DISSOLUTION
2 (A) THE COMPACT SHALL BE DISSOLVED EFFECTIVE UPON THE DATE OF
3 THE WITHDRAWAL OR DEFAULT OF THE MEMBER STATE WHICH REDUCES THE
4 MEMBERSHIP OF THE COMPACT TO ONE MEMBER STATE.
5 (B) UPON THE DISSOLUTION OF THE COMPACT, THE COMPACT BECOMES
6 NULL AND VOID AND SHALL BE OF NO FURTHER FORCE OR EFFECT, AND THE
7 BUSINESS AND AFFAIRS OF THE INTERSTATE COMMISSION SHALL BE CONCLUDED,
8 AND SURPLUS FUNDS SHALL BE DISTRIBUTED IN ACCORDANCE WITH THE BYLAWS.
9 SECTION 23. SEVERABILITY AND CONSTRUCTION
10 (A) THE PROVISIONS OF THE COMPACT SHALL BE SEVERABLE, AND IF ANY
11 PHRASE, CLAUSE, SENTENCE, OR PROVISION IS DEEMED UNENFORCEABLE, THE
12 REMAINING PROVISIONS OF THE COMPACT SHALL BE ENFORCEABLE.
13 (B) THE PROVISIONS OF THE COMPACT SHALL BE LIBERALLY CONSTRUED
14 TO EFFECTUATE ITS PURPOSES.
15 (C) NOTHING IN THE COMPACT SHALL BE CONSTRUED TO PROHIBIT THE
16 APPLICABILITY OF OTHER INTERSTATE COMPACTS TO WHICH THE MEMBER STATES
17 ARE MEMBERS.
18 SECTION 24. BINDING EFFECT OF COMPACT AND OTHER LAWS
19 (A) NOTHING HEREIN PREVENTS THE ENFORCEMENT OF ANY OTHER LAW
20 OF A MEMBER STATE THAT IS NOT INCONSISTENT WITH THE COMPACT.
21 (B) ALL LAWS IN A MEMBER STATE IN CONFLICT WITH THE COMPACT ARE
22 SUPERSEDED TO THE EXTENT OF THE CONFLICT.
23 (C) ALL LAWFUL ACTIONS OF THE INTERSTATE COMMISSION, INCLUDING
24 ALL RULES AND BYLAWS PROMULGATED BY THE COMMISSION, ARE BINDING UPON
25 ALL MEMBER STATES.
26 (D) ALL AGREEMENTS BETWEEN THE INTERSTATE COMMISSION AND THE
27 MEMBER STATES ARE BINDING IN ACCORDANCE WITH THEIR TERMS.
28 (E) IN THE EVENT THAT ANY PROVISION OF THE COMPACT EXCEEDS THE
29 CONSTITUTIONAL LIMITS IMPOSED ON THE LEGISLATURE OF ANY MEMBER STATE,
30 SUCH PROVISION SHALL BE INEFFECTIVE TO THE EXTENT OF THE CONFLICT WITH
31 THE CONSTITUTIONAL PROVISION IN QUESTION IN THAT MEMBER STATE.
