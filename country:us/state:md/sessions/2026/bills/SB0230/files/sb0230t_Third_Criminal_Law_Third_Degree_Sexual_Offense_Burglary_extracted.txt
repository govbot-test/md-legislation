CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Criminal Law – Third–Degree Sexual Offense – Burglary
3 FOR the purpose of establishing that engaging in sexual contact with another without the
4 consent of the other in connection with a first–, second–, or third–degree burglary
5 constitutes sexual offense in the third degree; altering the definitions of “tier I sex
6 offender”, “tier II sex offender”, and “tier III sex offender” applicable to provisions
7 relating to sex offender registration to include certain acts constituting sexual
8 offense in the third degree; and generally relating to sexual offense in the third
9 degree.
10 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
11 Article – Criminal Law
12 Section 3–307
13 Annotated Code of Maryland
14 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
15 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
16 Article – Criminal Procedure
17 Section 11–701(a)
18 Annotated Code of Maryland
19 (2025 Replacement Volume)
20 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
21 Article – Criminal Procedure

4 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
5 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
6 Article – Criminal Law
7 3–307.
8 (a) A person may not:
9 (1) (i) engage in sexual contact with another without the consent of the
10 other; and
11 (ii) 1. employ or display a dangerous weapon, or a physical object
12 that the victim reasonably believes is a dangerous weapon;
13 2. suffocate, strangle, disfigure, or inflict serious physical
14 injury on the victim or another in the course of committing the crime;
15 3. threaten, or place the victim in fear, that the victim, or an
16 individual known to the victim, imminently will be subject to death, suffocation,
17 strangulation, disfigurement, serious physical injury, or kidnapping; [or]
18 4. commit the crime while aided and abetted by another; OR
19 5. COMMIT THE CRIME IN CONNECTION WITH A
20 BURGLARY IN THE FIRST DEGREE UNDER § 6–202 OF THIS ARTICLE, A BURGLARY IN
21 THE SECOND DEGREE UNDER § 6–203 OF THIS ARTICLE, OR A BURGLARY IN THE
22 THIRD DEGREE UNDER § 6–204 OF THIS ARTICLE;
23 (2) engage in sexual contact with another if the victim is a substantially
24 cognitively impaired individual, a mentally incapacitated individual, or a physically
25 helpless individual, and the person performing the act knows or reasonably should know
26 the victim is a substantially cognitively impaired individual, a mentally incapacitated
27 individual, or a physically helpless individual;
28 (3) engage in sexual contact with another if the victim is under the age of
29 14 years, and the person performing the sexual contact is at least 4 years older than the
30 victim;
31 (4) engage in a sexual act with another if the victim is 14 or 15 years old,
32 and the person performing the sexual act is at least 21 years old; or
SENATE BILL 230 3
1 (5) engage in vaginal intercourse with another if the victim is 14 or 15
2 years old, and the person performing the act is at least 21 years old.
3 (b) A person who violates this section is guilty of the felony of sexual offense in
4 the third degree and on conviction is subject to imprisonment not exceeding 10 years.
5 Article – Criminal Procedure
6 11–701.
7 (a) In this subtitle the following words have the meanings indicated.
8 (o) “Tier I sex offender” means a person who has been convicted of:
9 (1) conspiring to commit, attempting to commit, or committing a violation
10 of § 3–308 of the Criminal Law Article;
11 (2) conspiring to commit, attempting to commit, or committing a violation
12 of § 3–902 or § 11–208 of the Criminal Law Article, if the victim is a minor;
13 (3) CONSPIRING TO COMMIT, ATTEMPTING TO COMMIT, OR
14 COMMITTING A VIOLATION OF § 3–307(A)(1)(II)5 OF THE CRIMINAL LAW ARTICLE,
15 IF THE VICTIM IS NOT A MINOR;
16 [(3)] (4) a crime committed in a federal, military, tribal, or other
17 jurisdiction that, if committed in this State, would constitute one of the crimes listed in
18 [item (1) or (2)] ITEMS (1) THROUGH (3) of this subsection;
19 [(4)] (5) any of the following federal offenses:
20 (i) misleading domain names on the Internet under 18 U.S.C. §
21 2252B;
22 (ii) misleading words or digital images on the Internet under 18
23 U.S.C. § 2252C;
24 (iii) engaging in illicit conduct in foreign places under 18 U.S.C. §
25 2423(c);
26 (iv) failure to file a factual statement about an alien individual under
27 18 U.S.C. § 2424;
28 (v) transmitting information about a minor to further criminal
29 sexual conduct under 18 U.S.C. § 2425;

1 (vi) sex trafficking by force, fraud, or coercion under 18 U.S.C. § 1591;
2 or
3 (vii) travel with intent to engage in illicit conduct under 18 U.S.C. §
4 2423(b);
5 [(5)] (6) any military offense specified by the Secretary of Defense under
6 Section 115(A)(8)(C)(i) of Public Law 105–119 (codified at 10 U.S.C. § 951 Note) that is
7 similar to those offenses listed in item [(4)] (5) of this subsection; or
8 [(6)] (7) a crime in a court of Canada, Great Britain, Australia, New

12 the conviction occurred that, if the crime were committed in this State, would constitute

14 (p) “Tier II sex offender” means a person who has been convicted of:
15 (1) conspiring to commit, attempting to commit, or committing a violation
16 of § 3–307(a)(4) or (5), § 3–324, § 11–207, or § 11–209 of the Criminal Law Article;
17 (2) conspiring to commit, attempting to commit, or committing a violation
18 of § 3–1102, § 3–1103, § 11–303, § 11–305, § 11–306, or § 11–307 of the Criminal Law
19 Article, if the intended prostitute or victim is a minor;
20 (3) conspiring to commit, attempting to commit, or committing a violation
21 of § 3–314 or § 3–603 of the Criminal Law Article, if the victim is a minor who is at least 14
22 years old;
23 (4) CONSPIRING TO COMMIT, ATTEMPTING TO COMMIT, OR
