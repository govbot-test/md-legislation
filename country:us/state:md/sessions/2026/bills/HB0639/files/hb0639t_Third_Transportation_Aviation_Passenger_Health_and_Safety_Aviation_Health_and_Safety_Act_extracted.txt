CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Transportation – Aviation – Passenger Health and Safety
3 (Aviation Health and Safety Act)
4 FOR the purpose of requiring the Maryland Aviation Administration to develop certain
5 passenger forms for aircraft traveling with detained individuals; requiring the flight
6 crew on each aircraft to distribute and collect certain forms; requiring certain
7 aeronautical service providers to confirm that certain forms are on board an aircraft
8 before providing service to the aircraft that service a flight with detained individuals
9 to request to see a warrant for the detained individual, and to note whether there is
10 a warrant and the type of warrant; requiring certain aeronautical service providers
11 to compile, and a member of each flight crew to sign, compile and submit to the
12 Administration certain reports; prohibiting certain agreements with airlines that
13 transport detained individuals unless certain information is provided; prohibiting
14 the refueling of an aircraft if an individual on board is in any kind of restraint;
15 requiring that a restrained individual who is removed from an aircraft to refuel the
16 aircraft be provided certain services; and generally relating to health and safety of
17 aircraft passengers.
18 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
19 Article – Transportation
20 Section 5–101(a) and (b)
21 Annotated Code of Maryland
22 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

7 WHEREAS, In the event of an emergency that occurs at an airport facility, the
8 State’s fire, rescue, and emergency medical services, as well as the State’s health care
9 systems, are tasked with responding to these emergencies, and as such the responders and
10 health care systems require medical information about potential patients; now, therefore,
11 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
12 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

14 5–101.
15 (a) In this title the following words have the meanings indicated.
16 (b) “Administration” means the Maryland Aviation Administration.

19 INDICATED.
20 (2) “AERONAUTICAL SERVICES” INCLUDES:
21 (I) FUELING, HANGARING, TYING DOWN, OR PARKING AN
22 AIRCRAFT;
23 (II) AIRCRAFT RENTAL;
24 (III) AIRCRAFT MAINTENANCE; AND
25 (IV) FLIGHT INSTRUCTION.
26 (3) “AVIATION SERVICE COMPANY” MEANS A SINGLE–SERVICE FIXED
27 BASE OPERATOR (FBO) THAT OFFERS SPECIALIZED AERONAUTICAL SERVICES.
28 (4) (3) (I) “DETAINED INDIVIDUAL” MEANS A PASSENGER WHO
29 IS TRAVELING INVOLUNTARILY OR WHILE BEING HELD IN ANY KIND OF RESTRAINT.

6 BUSINESS THAT AN AIRPORT HAS GRANTED THE RIGHT TO:
7 (I) OPERATE AT AN AIRPORT FACILITY; AND
8 (II) PROVIDE AERONAUTICAL SERVICES.

12 PASSENGER ON AN AIRCRAFT WHO IS A DETAINED INDIVIDUAL THAT:

23 PROVIDES GUARDIANSHIP INFORMATION IF THE GUARDIAN IS NOT ON BOARD THE
24 AIRCRAFT;

8 HAVE SEEN THE FORM AND ARE CHOOSING NOT TO COMPLETE THE FORM.

10 SHALL BE:
11 (1) KEPT ON BOARD EACH AIRCRAFT; AND
12 (2) IN THE EVENT OF AN EMERGENCY, PROVIDED TO EMERGENCY

18 AVIATION SERVICE COMPANY SHALL INSPECT THE AIRCRAFT TO CONFIRM THAT THE
