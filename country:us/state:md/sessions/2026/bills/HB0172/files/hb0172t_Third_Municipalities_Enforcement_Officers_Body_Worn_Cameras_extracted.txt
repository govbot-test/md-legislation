1 AN ACT concerning
2 Municipalities – Enforcement Officers – Body–Worn Cameras
3 FOR the purpose of including a certain municipal enforcement officer in the definition of
4 “law enforcement officer” for purposes of a certain exception to prohibitions against
5 wiretapping and electronic surveillance relating to the use of body–worn cameras by
6 law enforcement officers; authorizing a municipality to adopt an ordinance or a
7 resolution authorizing a certain enforcement officer to utilize body–worn cameras;
8 requiring a municipality that adopts a certain ordinance or resolution to publish a
9 certain policy; and generally relating to enforcement officers and body–worn
10 cameras.

21 BY adding to

2 Section 6–103.1
3 Annotated Code of Maryland

5 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
6 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
7 Article – Courts and Judicial Proceedings
8 10–402.
9 (a) Except as otherwise specifically provided in this subtitle it is unlawful for any
10 person to:
11 (1) Willfully intercept, endeavor to intercept, or procure any other person
12 to intercept or endeavor to intercept, any wire, oral, or electronic communication;
13 (2) Willfully disclose, or endeavor to disclose, to any other person the
14 contents of any wire, oral, or electronic communication, knowing or having reason to know
15 that the information was obtained through the interception of a wire, oral, or electronic
16 communication in violation of this subtitle; or
17 (3) Willfully use, or endeavor to use, the contents of any wire, oral, or
18 electronic communication, knowing or having reason to know that the information was
19 obtained through the interception of a wire, oral, or electronic communication in violation
20 of this subtitle.
21 (c) (11) (i) 1. In this paragraph the following words have the meanings
22 indicated.
23 2. “Body–worn digital recording device” means a device worn
24 on the person of a law enforcement officer or a correctional officer that is capable of
25 recording video and intercepting oral communications.
26 3. “Electronic control device” has the meaning stated in §
27 4–109 of the Criminal Law Article.
28 4. “LAW ENFORCEMENT OFFICER” INCLUDES A
29 MUNICIPAL ENFORCEMENT OFFICER DULY AUTHORIZED UNDER § 6–103.1 OF THE
30 LOCAL GOVERNMENT ARTICLE TO USE A BODY–WORN CAMERA DURING THE
31 COURSE OF THE OFFICER’S DUTIES.
32 (ii) It is lawful under this subtitle for a law enforcement officer or a
33 correctional officer in the course of the officer’s regular duty to intercept an oral
34 communication with a body–worn digital recording device or an electronic control device
35 capable of recording video and oral communications if:
HOUSE BILL 172 3
1 1. The law enforcement officer or correctional officer is in
2 uniform or prominently displaying the officer’s badge or other insignia;
3 2. The law enforcement officer or correctional officer is
4 making reasonable efforts to conform to standards in accordance with § 3–511 of the Public
5 Safety Article or § 8–211 of the Correctional Services Article for the use of body–worn digital
6 recording devices or electronic control devices capable of recording video and oral
7 communications;
8 3. The law enforcement officer or correctional officer is a
9 party to the oral communication;
10 4. Law enforcement or the correctional officer notifies, as
11 soon as is practicable, the individual that the individual is being recorded, unless it is
12 unsafe, impractical, or impossible to do so; and
13 5. The oral interception is being made as part of a videotape
14 or digital recording.
15 (iii) Failure to notify under subparagraph (ii)4 of this paragraph does
16 not affect the admissibility in court of the recording if the failure to notify involved an
17 individual who joined a discussion in progress for which proper notification was previously
18 given.
19 Article – Local Government
20 6–103.1.
21 (A) IN THIS SECTION, “ENFORCEMENT OFFICER” MEANS AN EMPLOYEE OR
22 AGENT OF A MUNICIPALITY WHO IS AUTHORIZED TO ACT AS A CODE ENFORCEMENT,
23 PARKING ENFORCEMENT, OR TRAFFIC ENFORCEMENT OFFICER FOR THE
24 MUNICIPALITY.
25 (B) A MUNICIPALITY MAY ADOPT AN ORDINANCE OR A RESOLUTION
26 AUTHORIZING AN ENFORCEMENT OFFICER TO UTILIZE A BODY–WORN CAMERA
27 DURING THE COURSE OF THE ENFORCEMENT OFFICER’S DUTIES.
28 (C) A MUNICIPALITY THAT ADOPTS AN ORDINANCE OR A RESOLUTION
29 UNDER SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION SHALL DEVELOP AND PUBLISH A POLICY
30 FOR THE ISSUANCE AND USE OF A BODY–WORN CAMERA BY AN ENFORCEMENT
31 OFFICER THAT IS CONSISTENT WITH THE POLICY PUBLISHED BY THE MARYLAND
32 POLICE TRAINING AND STANDARDS COMMISSION UNDER § 3–511 OF THE PUBLIC
33 SAFETY ARTICLE FOR THE ISSUANCE AND USE OF BODY–WORN CAMERAS BY LAW
34 ENFORCEMENT OFFICERS.
4 HOUSE BILL 172
1 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
2 October 1, 2026.
