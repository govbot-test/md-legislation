CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Criminal Law – Interference With Access to or Egress From a Religious Facility
3 Obstruction or Interference With Exercise of Religious Beliefs – Prohibition
4 FOR the purpose of prohibiting a person, acting alone or with others, from preventing
5 another from entering or exiting a religious facility; and generally relating to access
6 to or egress from religious facilities person from using force, threat of force, or
7 physical obstruction to interfere with or physically injure another while the other is
8 lawfully exercising or attempting to lawfully exercise religious beliefs at a place of
9 religious worship; and generally relating to obstruction or interference with the
10 exercise of religious beliefs.

2 SENATE BILL 177
1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – Criminal Law
4 10–204.
5 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
6 (2) (i) “Medical facility” means:
7 1. a facility as defined in § 10–101 of the Health – General
8 Article; or
9 2. a health care facility as defined in § 19–114 of the Health
10 – General Article.
11 (ii) “Medical facility” includes an agency, clinic, or office operated
12 under the direction of the local health officer or under the regulatory authority of the
13 Maryland Department of Health.
14 (b) (1) This section does not apply to:
15 (i) the chief executive officer of the medical facility;
16 (ii) a designee of the chief executive officer of the medical facility;
17 (iii) an agent of the medical facility; or
18 (iv) a law enforcement officer.
19 (2) This section does not prohibit:
20 (i) speech; or
21 (ii) picketing in connection with a labor dispute as defined in §
22 4–301 of the Labor and Employment Article.
23 (c) A person may not intentionally act, alone or with others, to prevent another
24 from entering or exiting a medical facility by physically:
25 (1) detaining the other; or
26 (2) obstructing, impeding, or hindering the other’s passage.
27 (d) A person who violates this section is guilty of a misdemeanor and on conviction
28 is subject to imprisonment not exceeding 90 days or a fine not exceeding $1,000 or both.

SENATE BILL 177 3
1 10–204.1.
2 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION, “RELIGIOUS FACILITY” MEANS REAL PROPERTY
3 THAT IS OWNED, LEASED, OR USED BY A RELIGIOUS ENTITY OR FOR ANY RELIGIOUS
4 PURPOSE.
5 (2) “RELIGIOUS FACILITY” INCLUDES:
6 (I) A PLACE OF WORSHIP;
7 (II) A CEMETERY;
8 (III) A RELIGIOUS SCHOOL, EDUCATIONAL FACILITY, OR
9 COMMUNITY CENTER; AND
10 (IV) THE GROUNDS ADJACENT TO THEM.
11 (B) (1) THIS SECTION DOES NOT APPLY TO:
12 (I) THE CHIEF EXECUTIVE OFFICER OF THE RELIGIOUS
13 FACILITY;
14 (II) A DESIGNEE OF THE CHIEF EXECUTIVE OFFICER OF THE
15 RELIGIOUS FACILITY;
16 (III) AN AGENT OF THE RELIGIOUS FACILITY; OR
17 (IV) A LAW ENFORCEMENT OFFICER.
18 (2) THIS SECTION DOES NOT PROHIBIT SPEECH.
19 (C) A PERSON MAY NOT INTENTIONALLY ACT, ALONE OR WITH OTHERS, TO
20 PREVENT ANOTHER FROM ENTERING OR EXITING A RELIGIOUS FACILITY BY
21 PHYSICALLY:
22 (1) DETAINING THE OTHER; OR
23 (2) OBSTRUCTING, IMPEDING, OR HINDERING THE OTHER’S PASSAGE.
24 (D) A PERSON WHO VIOLATES THIS SECTION IS GUILTY OF A MISDEMEANOR
25 AND ON CONVICTION IS SUBJECT TO IMPRISONMENT NOT EXCEEDING 90 DAYS OR A
26 FINE NOT EXCEEDING $1,000 OR BOTH.

4 SENATE BILL 177
1 10–302.
2 A person may not deface, damage, or destroy, or attempt or threaten to deface,
3 damage, or destroy, personal or real property that is owned, leased, or used by a religious
4 entity or for any religious purpose including:
5 (1) a place of worship;
6 (2) a cemetery;
7 (3) a religious school, educational facility, or community center; and
8 (4) the grounds adjacent to them.
9 10–204.1.
10 (A) A PERSON MAY NOT USE FORCE, THREAT OF FORCE, OR PHYSICAL
11 OBSTRUCTION TO INTERFERE WITH OR PHYSICALLY INJURE ANOTHER WHILE THE
12 OTHER IS LAWFULLY EXERCISING OR ATTEMPTING TO LAWFULLY EXERCISE THE
13 RIGHT OF RELIGIOUS FREEDOM UNDER THE FIRST AMENDMENT OF THE U.S.
14 CONSTITUTION AT A PLACE OF RELIGIOUS WORSHIP.
15 (B) A PERSON WHO VIOLATES THIS SECTION IS GUILTY OF A MISDEMEANOR
16 AND ON CONVICTION IS SUBJECT TO IMPRISONMENT NOT EXCEEDING 90 DAYS OR A
17 FINE NOT EXCEEDING $1,000 OR BOTH.
