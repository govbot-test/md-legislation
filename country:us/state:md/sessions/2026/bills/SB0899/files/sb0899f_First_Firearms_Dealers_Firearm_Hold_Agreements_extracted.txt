Section 1:
E4 6lr3140
By: Senator Ready
Introduced and read first time: February 6, 2026
Assigned to: Judicial Proceedings
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Firearms Dealers – Firearm Hold Agreements
3 FOR the purpose of providing that a certain federally licensed firearms dealer may not be
4 held civilly liable for personal injury to or death of any person after the return of a
5 certain firearm to a certain firearm owner at the termination of a certain agreement,
6 with a certain exception; and generally relating to firearms dealers.
7 BY adding to
8 Article – Public Safety
9 Section 5–149
10 Annotated Code of Maryland
11 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
12 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
13 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
14 Article – Public Safety
15 5–149.
16 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
17 INDICATED.
18 (2) “FEDERALLY LICENSED FIREARMS DEALER” HAS THE MEANING
19 STATED IN § 5–701 OF THIS TITLE.
20 (3) “FIREARM HOLD AGREEMENT” MEANS A PRIVATE AGREEMENT
21 BETWEEN A FEDERALLY LICENSED FIREARMS DEALER AND A PERSON WHO OWNS A
22 FIREARM IN WHICH THE FEDERALLY LICENSED FIREARMS DEALER:
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from exist in g l aw . *sb0899*
2 SENATE BILL 899
1 (I) TAKES PHYSICAL POSSESSION OF THE FIREARM OWNER’S
2 LAWFULLY POSSESSED FIREARM OR FIREARMS AT THE FIREARM OWNER’S
3 REQUEST;
4 (II) HOLDS THE FIREARM OR FIREARMS FOR AN AGREED
5 PERIOD OF TIME; AND
6 (III) RETURNS THE FIREARM OR FIREARMS TO THE FIREARM
7 OWNER IN ACCORDANCE WITH THE TERMS OF THE AGREEMENT.
8 (B) THIS SECTION DOES NOT APPLY TO FIREARM TRANSFERS IN
9 ACCORDANCE WITH § 6–234 OF THE CRIMINAL PROCEDURE ARTICLE.
10 (C) (1) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN PARAGRAPH (2) OF THIS SUBSECTION, A
11 FEDERALLY LICENSED FIREARMS DEALER MAY NOT BE HELD CIVILLY LIABLE FOR
12 PERSONAL INJURY TO OR DEATH OF ANY PERSON AFTER THE RETURN OF A FIREARM
13 TO A FIREARM OWNER BY THE FEDERALLY LICENSED FIREARMS DEALER AT THE
14 TERMINATION OF A FIREARM HOLD AGREEMENT.
15 (2) THE IMMUNITY FROM LIABILITY PROVIDED UNDER PARAGRAPH
16 (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION DOES NOT APPLY TO PERSONAL INJURY OR DEATH CAUSED
17 BY UNLAWFUL CONDUCT ON THE PART OF THE FEDERALLY LICENSED FIREARMS
18 DEALER.
19 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
20 October 1, 2026.
