1 AN ACT concerning
2 Criminal Law – School Resource Officers – Prohibition on Sexual Activity With
3 Students
4 FOR the purpose of prohibiting a law enforcement officer from engaging in certain sexual
5 acts, sexual contact, or vaginal intercourse with a student enrolled in a school where
6 the law enforcement officer is assigned as a school resource officer; and generally
7 relating to school resource officers and prohibited activities with students.

2 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
3 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
4 Article – Criminal Law
5 3–314.
6 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
7 (5) “Law enforcement officer” has the meaning stated in § 1–101 of the
8 Public Safety Article.
9 (e) (1) Except as provided in paragraph (2) of this subsection, a law
10 enforcement officer may not engage in sexual contact, vaginal intercourse, or a sexual act
11 with a person:
12 (i) who is a victim, witness, or suspect in an open investigation that
13 the law enforcement officer is conducting, supervising, or assisting with if the law
14 enforcement officer knew or should have known that the person is a victim, witness, or
15 suspect in the investigation;
16 (ii) requesting assistance from or responding to the law enforcement
17 officer in the course of the law enforcement officer’s official duties; [or]
18 (iii) in the custody of the law enforcement officer; OR
19 (IV) WHO IS A STUDENT ENROLLED IN A SCHOOL WHERE THE
20 LAW ENFORCEMENT OFFICER IS ASSIGNED AS A SCHOOL RESOURCE OFFICER, AS
21 DEFINED IN § 7–1501 OF THE EDUCATION ARTICLE.
22 (2) Paragraph (1)(i) and (ii) of this subsection does not apply if the law
23 enforcement officer:
24 (i) had a prior existing legal sexual relationship with the person;
25 and
26 (ii) did not act under the color or pretense of office or under color of
27 official right when seeking consent to the vaginal intercourse, sexual act, or sexual contact.
28 (f) A person who violates this section is guilty of a misdemeanor and on conviction
29 is subject to imprisonment not exceeding 3 years or a fine not exceeding $3,000 or both.
30 (g) A sentence imposed for a violation of this section may be separate from and
31 consecutive to or concurrent with a sentence for another crime under § 3–303, § 3–304, or
