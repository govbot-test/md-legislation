A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Catalytic Revitalization Project Tax Credit – Alterations
3 FOR the purpose of altering eligibility for and the manner for claiming a certain credit
4 against the State income tax that certain persons may claim for certain construction
5 and rehabilitation costs for certain catalytic revitalization projects in the State;
6 altering certain limitations on the Secretary of Housing and Community
7 Development to accept certain applications and award certain tax credit certificates;
8 and generally relating to the catalytic revitalization project tax credit.
9 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
10 Article – Housing and Community Development
11 Section 4–1801(l) and 6–903(a)
12 Annotated Code of Maryland
13 (2019 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
14 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
15 Article – Housing and Community Development
16 Section 6–901, 6–902, and 6–903(b)
17 Annotated Code of Maryland
18 (2019 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

20 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
21 Article – Housing and Community Development
22 4–1801.
23 (l) “Workforce housing” means:

14 property in the State:
15 (1) [that was formerly owned by the State or the federal government;
16 (2)] the rehabilitation of which will foster economic, housing, and
17 community development within the community in which the property is located; and
18 (2) (I) 1. A. THAT WAS FORMERLY OWNED BY THE FEDERAL

20 B. THAT WAS FORMERLY OR IS CURRENTLY OWNED BY

24 OR

1 3. WITHIN A ONE–HALF MILE RADIUS OF A PASSENGER

4 7–101 OF THE TRANSPORTATION ARTICLE.
5 (D) “WORKFORCE HOUSING” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 4–1801 OF
6 THIS ARTICLE.

8 LEAST 20% OF THE RESIDENTIAL UNITS PRODUCED ARE WORKFORCE HOUSING
9 UNITS.

11 (a) [An] EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION, AN
12 individual, a nonprofit organization, or a business entity may claim a tax credit in an
13 amount equal to:
14 (1) for a project issued a single tax credit certificate on completion, 20% of
15 the amount stated in the final tax credit certificate issued by the Secretary under § 6–903
16 of this subtitle for 5 consecutive taxable years beginning with the taxable year in which a
17 final tax credit certificate is issued; or
18 (2) for a phased project issued a tax credit certificate on completion of a
19 phase, 100% of the amount stated in the final tax credit certificate issued by the Secretary
20 under § 6–903 of this subtitle for the taxable year in which the final tax credit certificate is
21 issued.
22 (b) IF THE PROJECT IS A WORKFORCE HOUSING PROJECT, AN INDIVIDUAL,
23 A NONPROFIT ORGANIZATION, OR A BUSINESS ENTITY MAY CLAIM THE TAX CREDIT
24 IN AN AMOUNT EQUAL TO:
25 (1) ONE–HALF OF THE AMOUNT STATED IN A FINAL TAX CREDIT
26 CERTIFICATE ISSUED BY THE SECRETARY UNDER § 6–903 OF THIS SUBTITLE THAT
27 IS ATTRIBUTABLE TO THE PRODUCTION OF THE WORKFORCE HOUSING UNITS IN THE
28 TAXABLE YEAR IN WHICH THE FINAL TAX CREDIT CERTIFICATE IS ISSUED AND THE
29 IMMEDIATELY FOLLOWING TAXABLE YEAR; AND
30 (2) ONE–THIRD OF THE AMOUNT STATED IN THE FINAL TAX CREDIT
31 CERTIFICATE ISSUED BY THE SECRETARY UNDER § 6–903 OF THIS SUBTITLE THAT
32 IS ATTRIBUTABLE TO PROJECT COSTS NOT ATTRIBUTABLE TO THE PRODUCTION OF
33 THE WORKFORCE HOUSING UNITS IN EACH OF THE 3 CONSECUTIVE TAXABLE YEARS
34 FOLLOWING THE TAXABLE YEARS DESCRIBED UNDER ITEM (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION.

1 (C) The Comptroller or the Secretary may not recapture a tax credit authorized
2 under this subtitle if:
3 (1) the catalytic revitalization project is transferred, in whole or in part, by
4 fee simple transfer of real property or by transfer of an ownership interest in the entity
5 that owns the project to a new owner; and
6 (2) the Secretary has issued a final tax credit certificate for the catalytic
7 revitalization project under § 6–903 of this subtitle.
8 [(c)] (D) The Secretary may issue multiple final tax credit certificates for a
9 project that is issued an initial tax certificate under this section if:
10 (1) the project has been determined by the Secretary to be in phases;
11 (2) a phase of the project has been completed;
12 (3) a final tax credit certificate is not issued for more than 20% of the
13 project’s eligible expenses to date; and
14 (4) a final tax credit certificate is not issued that would exceed the
15 aggregated limit of a project’s credit under § 6–903(b) of this subtitle.
16 6–903.
17 (a) (1) Subject to the limitations of paragraph (2) of this subsection and
18 subsection (b) of this section, on application by an individual, a nonprofit organization, or
19 a business entity, the Secretary shall issue:
20 (i) an initial tax credit certificate in an amount equal to 20% of the
21 individual’s, nonprofit organization’s, or business entity’s estimated new construction costs
22 and rehabilitation costs for the project; and
23 (ii) on completion of the catalytic revitalization project or a phase of
24 the catalytic revitalization project, a final tax credit certificate in an amount equal to 20%
25 of the individual’s, nonprofit organization’s, or business entity’s actual new construction
26 costs and rehabilitation costs for the project.
27 (2) For purposes of calculating the credit authorized under paragraph (1)
28 of this subsection, new construction costs:
29 (i) must be related to the construction of new structures that
30 connect, expand, or integrate the existing structures to be rehabilitated; and
31 (ii) may not exceed 50% of the total costs of the catalytic
32 revitalization project.

1 (b) (1) Except as provided in paragraph [(2)(ii)] (3)(II) of this subsection, the
2 Secretary may not:
3 (i) accept applications and award initial credit certificates for
4 catalytic revitalization projects more than once [within a 2–year period] EACH YEAR; or
5 (ii) issue tax credit certificates for credit amounts in the aggregate
6 totaling more than [$15,000,000 within a 2–year period] $30,000,000 EACH YEAR.
7 (2) THE SECRETARY MAY NOT ACCEPT APPLICATIONS AND AWARD
8 INITIAL CREDIT CERTIFICATES FOR CATALYTIC REVITALIZATION PROJECTS THAT
9 ARE TRANSIT–ORIENTED DEVELOPMENT PROJECTS OR WORKFORCE HOUSING
10 PROJECTS UNLESS THE SECRETARY CERTIFIES THAT THE STATE HAS NOT YET MET
11 CONTINUED PRODUCTION TARGETS FOR HOUSING UNITS.
12 (3) (i) The Secretary may revoke an initial credit certificate on a finding
13 by the Secretary that the recipient is not in compliance with the initial credit certificate or
14 an agreement with the Department regarding the initial credit certificate.
15 (ii) If the Secretary revokes an initial credit certificate under
16 subparagraph (i) of this paragraph, the Secretary may award an initial credit certificate to
17 another applicant, provided that the award does not exceed the amount of the revoked
18 initial credit certificate.
19 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
20 1, 2026, and shall be applicable to all initial credit certificates issued for the catalytic
21 revitalization project tax credit on or after July 1, 2026.
