2 Election Law – Early Voting Centers – Bus Stops

4 passengers to embark and disembark at the entrance of early voting centers located

2 HOUSE BILL 263
1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – Election Law
4 10–301.1.
5 (c) Each early voting center shall be open for voting as follows:
6 (1) beginning the second Thursday before a primary or general election
7 through the Thursday before the election; and
8 (2) during the hours between 7 a.m. and 8 p.m. each early voting day.
9 (E) (1) (I) THIS SUBSECTION APPLIES ONLY WITH RESPECT TO A
10 FIXED LOCAL BUS ROUTE FUNDED BY A COUNTY OR MUNICIPAL CORPORATION AND
11 OPERATED BY THE COUNTY OR MUNICIPAL CORPORATION OR A CONTRACTOR FOR
12 THE COUNTY OR MUNICIPAL CORPORATION.
13 (II) THIS SUBSECTION DOES NOT APPLY TO:
14 1. AN EXPRESS OR A COMMUTER BUS ROUTE THAT HAS
15 LIMITED STOPS BETWEEN ITS ORIGIN AND DESTINATION;
16 2. A BUS ROUTE OPERATED BY THE MARYLAND TRANSIT
17 ADMINISTRATION; OR
18 3. A BUS ROUTE OPERATED BY THE WASHINGTON
19 METROPOLITAN AREA TRANSIT AUTHORITY.
20 (2) IF AN EARLY VOTING CENTER IS LOCATED WITHIN ONE–HALF
21 MILE OF A FIXED LOCAL BUS ROUTE, EACH BUS OPERATING ON THAT ROUTE SHALL,
22 TO THE MAXIMUM EXTENT PRACTICABLE AND CONSISTENT WITH ACCESSIBILITY
23 REQUIREMENTS, ALLOW PASSENGERS TO EMBARK AND DISEMBARK AT THE
24 ENTRANCE OF THE EARLY VOTING CENTER ON THE DAYS THE CENTER IS OPEN FOR
25 VOTING UNDER SUBSECTION (C) OF THIS SECTION.
26 [(e)] (F) Beginning 30 days prior to each early voting period the State Board and
27 each local board shall undertake steps to inform the public about early voting and the
28 location of early voting centers in each county, including:
29 (1) a series of public service media announcements;
30 (2) mailings to all registered voters in each county; and

2 [(f)] (G) Except as expressly provided in this section, any provision of this article

5 with the requirements of this section for the conduct of early voting.
