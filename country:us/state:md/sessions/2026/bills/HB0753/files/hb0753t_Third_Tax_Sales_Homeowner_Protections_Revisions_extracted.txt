CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Tax Sales – Homeowner Protections – Revisions
3 FOR the purpose of requiring the State Tax Sale Ombudsman to develop a process to allow
4 a homeowner to designate a family member or another representative of the
5 homeowner to communicate and work with the Ombudsman on the homeowner’s
6 behalf; requiring the dwelling of a homeowner to be withheld from tax sale if the
7 homeowner has a documented terminal illness or medical hardship; increasing the
8 maximum value of a dwelling that a homeowner may reside in and be eligible for the
9 Homeowner Protection Program; requiring homeowners with terminal illness or
10 medical hardship to receive priority for enrollment in the Homeowner Protection
11 Program; and generally relating to protections for homeowners in tax sale.
12 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
13 Article – Tax – Property
14 Section 2–112(b) and (e), 14–811(i) and (j), 14–836(a) and (b)(4)(i)3., 14–883, and
15 14–884
16 Annotated Code of Maryland
17 (2019 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
18 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
19 Article – Tax – Property
20 Section 2–112(d), 14–811(h), and 14–885(a) and (b)
21 Annotated Code of Maryland

7 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
8 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
9 Article – Tax – Property
10 2–112.
11 (b) There is a State Tax Sale Ombudsman in the Department.
12 (d) The Ombudsman shall:
13 (1) assist homeowners to understand the process for collection of
14 delinquent taxes;
15 (2) actively assist homeowners to apply for tax credits, discount programs,
16 and other public benefits that may assist the homeowners to pay delinquent taxes and
17 improve their financial situation;
18 (3) refer homeowners to legal services, housing counseling, and other social
19 services that may assist homeowners to pay delinquent taxes and improve their financial
20 situation;
21 (4) maintain a website that functions as a clearinghouse for information
22 concerning:
23 (i) the process for collection of delinquent taxes; and
24 (ii) services and programs that are available to assist homeowners
25 to pay delinquent taxes and improve their financial situation; [and]
26 (5) maintain a toll–free telephone number that a homeowner may call to
27 obtain individualized personal assistance with delinquent taxes; AND
28 (6) DEVELOP A PROCESS TO ALLOW A HOMEOWNER TO DESIGNATE A
29 FAMILY MEMBER OR ANOTHER REPRESENTATIVE OF THE HOMEOWNER TO
30 COMMUNICATE AND WORK WITH THE OMBUDSMAN ON THE HOMEOWNER’S BEHALF.
HOUSE BILL 753 3
1 (e) A county may, by law, establish a County Tax Sale Ombudsman to fulfill all
2 the responsibilities of the State Tax Sale Ombudsman under subsection (d) of this section
3 with respect to homeowners within the county.
4 14–811.
5 (h) (1) In this subsection, “dwelling” and “homeowner” have the meanings
6 stated in § 9–105 of this article.
7 (2) The Mayor and City Council of Baltimore City or the governing body of
8 a county or municipal corporation may withhold from sale a dwelling owned by a
9 homeowner or occupied by an heir of a deceased homeowner who is low–income, at least 65
10 years old, or disabled if the homeowner or heir meets eligibility criteria established by the
11 county or municipal corporation.
12 (3) (I) THE MAYOR AND CITY COUNCIL OF BALTIMORE CITY OR
13 THE GOVERNING BODY OF A COUNTY SHALL WITHHOLD FROM SALE THE DWELLING
14 OF A HOMEOWNER WHO HAS A TERMINAL ILLNESS OR MEDICAL HARDSHIP AS
15 DOCUMENTED BY A PHYSICIAN WHO HAS TREATED OR EXAMINED THE HOMEOWNER.
16 (II) THE MAYOR AND CITY COUNCIL OF BALTIMORE CITY OR
17 THE GOVERNING BODY OF A COUNTY MAY ADOPT ADDITIONAL METHODS FOR
18 ESTABLISHING A TERMINAL ILLNESS OR MEDICAL HARDSHIP IN ACCORDANCE WITH
19 SUBPARAGRAPH (I) OF THIS SUBSECTION.
20 (II) THE DEPARTMENT AND THE STATE TAX SALE OMBUDSMAN
21 SHALL, IN CONSULTATION WITH THE MARYLAND DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH, DEFINE
22 “TERMINAL ILLNESS” AND “MEDICAL HARDSHIP” BY REGULATION FOR PURPOSES
23 OF SUBPARAGRAPH (I) OF THIS PARAGRAPH.
24 (III) THE COLLECTOR FOR EACH COUNTY SHALL ESTABLISH A
25 PROCESS FOR A HOMEOWNER, A FAMILY MEMBER OF A HOMEOWNER, OR ANOTHER
26 REPRESENTATIVE OF A HOMEOWNER TO APPLY FOR THE HOMEOWNER’S DWELLING
27 TO BE WITHHELD FROM SALE UNDER THIS PARAGRAPH.
28 (IV) FOR A HOMEOWNER’S DWELLING TO CONTINUE TO BE
29 WITHHELD FROM SALE UNDER THIS PARAGRAPH, THE HOMEOWNER, A FAMILY
30 MEMBER OF THE HOMEOWNER, OR ANOTHER REPRESENTATIVE OF THE
31 HOMEOWNER SHALL REAPPLY TO THE COLLECTOR IN THE SECOND YEAR AFTER THE
32 HOMEOWNER’S DWELLING WAS FIRST WITHHELD FROM SALE UNDER THIS
33 PARAGRAPH AND IN EVERY SECOND YEAR THEREAFTER.
34 (i) The collector shall withhold from sale the dwelling of a homeowner who is
35 enrolled in the Homeowner Protection Program established under Part VII of this subtitle.

1 (j) (1) The Mayor and City Council of Baltimore City or the governing body of
2 a county shall establish a registry for interested parties or the Tax Sale Ombudsman to
3 designate a property to be withheld from tax sale in accordance with this section.
4 (2) On request of the Mayor and City Council of Baltimore City or the
5 governing body of a county, the Department shall provide assistance in creating and
6 maintaining the registry required under paragraph (1) of this subsection.

14 14–883.
15 (a) In this part the following words have the meanings indicated.
16 (b) “Combined income” means the combined Maryland adjusted gross income of
17 all individuals who actually reside in a dwelling except an individual who:
18 (1) is a dependent of the homeowner under § 152 of the Internal Revenue
19 Code; or
20 (2) pays a reasonable amount for rent or room and board.
21 (c) “Dwelling” has the meaning stated in § 9–105 of this article.
22 (d) “Homeowner” means:
23 (1) a homeowner as defined in § 9–105 of this article; or
24 (2) if a homeowner as defined in § 9–105 of this article is deceased:
25 (i) the estate of the homeowner;
26 (ii) the personal representative of the homeowner; or
27 (iii) an heir or legatee of the homeowner who is entitled to inherit the
28 homeowner’s dwelling.

1 (e) “Ombudsman” means the State Tax Sale Ombudsman established under §
2 2–112 of this article.
3 (f) “Program” means the Homeowner Protection Program established under this
4 part.
5 (g) “Tax” has the meaning stated in § 14–801 of this subtitle.
6 14–884.
7 (a) There is a Homeowner Protection Program administered by the Ombudsman
8 in the Department.
9 (b) The purpose of the Program is to divert vulnerable homeowners from the
10 private tax lien sale process under Part III of this subtitle into an alternative program with
11 the primary purpose of:
12 (1) minimizing tax collection costs to homeowners;
13 (2) assisting homeowners to pay their taxes; and
14 (3) allowing homeowners to remain in their homes.
15 14–885.
16 (a) (1) To be eligible for the Program a homeowner shall:
17 (i) reside in a dwelling that has an assessed value of [$300,000]
18 $450,000 or less; and
19 (ii) have a combined income of $60,000 or less.
20 (2) The Department may establish, by regulation, additional eligibility
21 criteria for enrollment in the Program.
22 (b) (1) The Department shall establish, by regulation, a process to:
23 (1) (I) give priority for enrollment in the Program to homeowners who
24 are:
25 (i) 1. at least 60 years old; [or]
26 (ii) 2. currently receiving disability benefits from the federal
27 Social Security Disability Insurance program or the federal Supplemental Security Income
28 program; OR
6 HOUSE BILL 753
1 (III) 3. EXPERIENCING A DOCUMENTED TERMINAL ILLNESS
2 OR MEDICAL HARDSHIP; and
3 (2) (II) ensure that homeowners are enrolled in the Program who reside
4 in each county in the State.
5 (2) THE REGULATION ESTABLISHED UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS
6 SUBSECTION SHALL INCLUDE THE DEFINITIONS OF “TERMINAL ILLNESS” AND
7 “MEDICAL HARDSHIP” ADOPTED UNDER § 14–811 OF THIS SUBTITLE.
8 14–888.
9 (E) THE OMBUDSMAN SHALL DEVELOP A PROCESS TO ALLOW A
10 HOMEOWNER ENROLLED IN THE PROGRAM TO DESIGNATE A FAMILY MEMBER OR
11 ANOTHER REPRESENTATIVE OF THE HOMEOWNER TO COMMUNICATE AND WORK
12 WITH THE OMBUDSMAN ON THE HOMEOWNER’S BEHALF.
13 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
14 October 1, 2026.
