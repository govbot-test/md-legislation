CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Occupational Licensing and Certification – Criminal History –
3 Predetermination Review Process
4 FOR the purpose of providing that “department” does not include the Department of Public
5 Safety and Correctional Services for purposes of certain provisions prohibiting a
6 department from refusing to issue an occupational license or certificate based on a
7 certain conviction under certain circumstances; establishing a predetermination
8 review process for occupational licenses and certificates in certain departments of
9 State government; and generally relating to occupational licensing and certification.
10 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
11 Article – Criminal Procedure
12 Section 1–209
13 Annotated Code of Maryland
14 (2025 Replacement Volume)
15 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
16 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
17 Article – Criminal Procedure
18 1–209.
19 (a) (1) In this section, “department” means:

2 HOUSE BILL 557
1 (i) the Department of Agriculture;
2 (ii) the Department of the Environment;
3 (iii) the Maryland Department of Health;
4 (iv) the Department of Human Services; OR
5 (v) the Maryland Department of Labor[; or
6 (vi) the Department of Public Safety and Correctional Services].
7 (2) “Department” includes any unit of a department specified in paragraph
8 (1) of this subsection.
9 (b) This section does not apply to a person who:
10 (1) was previously convicted of a crime of violence, as defined in § 14–101
11 of the Criminal Law Article; OR
12 (2) IS APPLYING FOR LICENSURE OR LICENSE RENEWAL UNDER
13 TITLE 11, SUBTITLE 6 OF THE FINANCIAL INSTITUTIONS ARTICLE.
14 (c) It is the policy of the State to encourage the employment of nonviolent
15 ex–offenders and remove barriers to their ability to demonstrate fitness for occupational
16 licenses or certifications required by the State.
17 (d) Except as provided in subsection (f) of this section, a department may not deny
18 an occupational license or certificate to an applicant solely on the basis that the applicant
19 has previously been convicted of a crime, unless the department determines that:
20 (1) there is a direct relationship between the applicant’s previous
21 conviction and the specific occupational license or certificate sought; or
22 (2) the issuance of the license or certificate would involve an unreasonable
23 risk to property or to the safety or welfare of specific individuals or the general public.
24 (e) In making the determination under subsection (d) of this section, the
25 department shall consider:
26 (1) the policy of the State expressed in subsection (c) of this section;
27 (2) the specific duties and responsibilities required of a licensee or
28 certificate holder;
HOUSE BILL 557 3
1 (3) whether the applicant’s previous conviction has any impact on the
2 applicant’s fitness or ability to perform the duties and responsibilities authorized by the
3 license or certificate;
4 (4) the age of the applicant at the time of the conviction and the amount of
5 time that has elapsed since the conviction;
6 (5) the seriousness of the offense for which the applicant was convicted;
7 (6) other information provided by the applicant or on the applicant’s behalf
8 with regard to the applicant’s rehabilitation and good conduct; and
9 (7) the legitimate interest of the department in protecting property and the
10 safety and welfare of specific individuals or the general public.
11 (f) (1) This subsection does not apply to a conviction of a crime for which
12 registration on the sex offender registry is required under Title 11, Subtitle 7 of this article.
13 (2) If a period of 7 years or more has passed since an applicant completed
14 serving the sentence for a crime, including all imprisonment, mandatory supervision,
15 probation, and parole, and the applicant has not been charged with another crime other
16 than a minor traffic violation, as defined in § 10–101 of this article, during that time, a
17 department may not deny an occupational license or certificate to the applicant solely on
18 the basis that the applicant was previously convicted of the crime.
19 (G) (1) (I) AN INDIVIDUAL MAY FILE A REQUEST WITH A DEPARTMENT
20 FOR REVIEW OF THE INDIVIDUAL’S CRIMINAL HISTORY TO DETERMINE WHETHER
21 THE INDIVIDUAL’S CRIMINAL HISTORY WOULD DISQUALIFY THE INDIVIDUAL FROM
22 OBTAINING THE OCCUPATIONAL LICENSE OR CERTIFICATE BEING SOUGHT.
23 (II) THE DETERMINATION OF A DEPARTMENT REGARDING
24 WHETHER THE OCCUPATIONAL LICENSE OR CERTIFICATE WOULD BE APPROVED OR
25 DENIED TO THE INDIVIDUAL REQUESTING THE PREDETERMINATION UNDER
26 SUBPARAGRAPH (I) OF THIS PARAGRAPH SHALL BE BINDING ON THE DEPARTMENT
27 UNLESS THERE IS A SUBSEQUENT DIRECT AND MATERIAL ADVERSE CHANGE TO THE
28 INDIVIDUAL’S CRIMINAL HISTORY.
29 (III) WHEN CONDUCTING A PREDETERMINATION REVIEW
30 REQUESTED UNDER SUBPARAGRAPH (I) OF THIS PARAGRAPH, A DEPARTMENT
31 SHALL UTILIZE THE EXISTING RESOURCES OF THE DEPARTMENT.
32 (2) IF A DEPARTMENT DETERMINES THAT AN OCCUPATIONAL
33 LICENSE OR CERTIFICATE WOULD BE DENIED TO THE INDIVIDUAL UNDER
34 PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION, THE DEPARTMENT SHALL PROVIDE THE
4 HOUSE BILL 557
1 INDIVIDUAL WITH AN EXPLANATION FOR ITS DETERMINATION, INCLUDING THE
2 BASIS UNDER SUBSECTION (D) OF THIS SECTION.
3 (3) AN INDIVIDUAL MAY SUBMIT A REVISED REQUEST FOR A
4 PREDETERMINATION TO THE DEPARTMENT THAT MADE A PREDETERMINATION
5 UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION IF:
6 (I) 1 YEAR HAS PASSED SINCE THE INDIVIDUAL RECEIVED THE
7 PREDETERMINATION UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION; OR
8 (II) THERE IS A MATERIAL CHANGE TO THE INDIVIDUAL’S
9 CRIMINAL HISTORY.
10 (4) (I) A DEPARTMENT MAY CHARGE A CRIMINAL HISTORY REVIEW
11 FEE TO AN INDIVIDUAL TO CONDUCT A REVIEW UNDER THIS SECTION NOT
12 EXCEEDING $100.
13 (II) THE FEE CHARGED UNDER SUBPARAGRAPH (I) OF THIS
14 PARAGRAPH MAY BE WAIVED IF THE INDIVIDUAL’S INCOME IS AT OR BELOW 300%
15 OF THE FEDERAL POVERTY LEVEL, AS DETERMINED BY THE DISTRICT COURT OF
16 MARYLAND.
17 (H) NOTHING IN THIS SECTION MAY BE CONSTRUED TO:
18 (1) OVERRIDE, SUPERSEDE, OR INVALIDATE ANY COMPACT OR
19 AGREEMENT ALREADY IN PLACE WITH REGARD TO THE REGULATION OF ANY
20 PROFESSION OR OCCUPATION BY A DEPARTMENT; OR
21 (2) SUPERSEDE THE AUTHORITY OF A DEPARTMENT TO REQUIRE AN
22 APPLICANT OR A LICENSEE TO SUBMIT TO A CRIMINAL HISTORY RECORDS REVIEW
23 IN ACCORDANCE WITH THIS SECTION.
24 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That, on or before October 1, 2028,
25 the Department of Agriculture, the Department of the Environment, the Maryland
26 Department of Health, the Department of Human Services, and the Maryland Department
27 of Labor shall each report to the Senate Finance Committee and the House Economic
28 Matters Committee, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government Article, on the
29 implementation of Section 1 of this Act, including the number of predetermination requests
30 conducted and the costs associated with implementation.
31 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
32 1, 2026. It shall remain effective for a period of 3 years and, at the end of June 30, 2029,
33 this Act, with no further action required by the General Assembly, shall be abrogated and
34 of no further force and effect.
