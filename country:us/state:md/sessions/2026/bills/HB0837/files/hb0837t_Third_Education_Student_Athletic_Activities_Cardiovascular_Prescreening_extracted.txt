CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Education – Student Athletic Activities – Physical Examinations and
3 Cardiovascular Prescreening
4 FOR the purpose of requiring a certain physical examination used to determine the physical
5 fitness of a student seeking to participate in interscholastic sports to include a
6 certain cardiovascular prescreening; requiring certain health care providers who
7 perform certain physical examinations for students participating in interscholastic
8 sports to include a cardiovascular prescreening as part of the examination; requiring
9 the State Department of Education, in consultation with the Maryland Department
10 of Health to develop certain guidelines; requiring certain nonpublic schools that offer
11 athletic activities for students to develop policies and programs to provide certain
12 information on sudden cardiac arrest to certain persons participating in the
13 nonpublic school’s athletic programs; requiring a certain physical examination for
14 student participation in an interscholastic sport to include a cardiovascular
15 prescreening under certain circumstances; requiring an individual performing a
16 cardiovascular prescreening to make certain notices under certain circumstances;
17 and generally relating to student athletic activities, physical examinations, and
18 cardiovascular prescreening for interscholastic sports.

1 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
2 (2) (i) “Athletic activity” means:
3 1. Interscholastic athletics;
4 2. An athletic contest or competition that is sponsored by or
5 associated with a school; and
6 3. Practices and scrimmages.
7 (ii) “Athletic activity” includes cheerleading and sports sponsored by
8 school–affiliated organizations.
9 (3) “Sudden cardiac arrest” means a condition in which the heart suddenly
10 and unexpectedly stops beating.
11 (4) “Youth sports program” means a program organized for recreational
12 athletic competition or instruction for participants who are under the age of 19 years.
13 (b) Nothing in this section may be construed to create, establish, expand, reduce,
14 contract, or eliminate any civil liability on the part of a county board employee, school
15 employee, or youth sports program volunteer.
16 (c) (1) The Department shall develop policies and implement a program to
17 provide awareness to coaches, school personnel, students, and the parents or guardians of
18 students, in collaboration with the Maryland Department of Health, each county board, the
19 Maryland Public Secondary Schools Athletic Association, the Maryland Athletic Trainers’
20 Association, Parent Heart Watch, and licensed health care providers who treat heart
21 issues, on:
22 (i) The nature and warning signs of sudden cardiac arrest, including
23 fainting, difficulty breathing, chest pains, dizziness, and abnormal heart rate; and
24 (ii) The risks associated with continuing to play or practice after
25 experiencing a symptom of sudden cardiac arrest.
26 (2) The program shall include a process to verify that a coach has received
27 information on the program developed under paragraph (1) of this subsection.
28 (3) (i) Before a student enrolled in a public school system in the State
29 may participate in an authorized athletic activity, the county board shall provide a sudden
30 cardiac arrest symptoms and warning signs information sheet to the student and a parent
31 or guardian of the student.
