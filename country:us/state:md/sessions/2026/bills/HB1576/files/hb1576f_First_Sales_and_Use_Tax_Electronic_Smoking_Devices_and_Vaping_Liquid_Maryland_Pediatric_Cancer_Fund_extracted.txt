A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Sales and Use Tax – Electronic Smoking Devices and Vaping Liquid – Maryland
3 Pediatric Cancer Fund
4 FOR the purpose of increasing the sales and use tax on the sale of electronic smoking
5 devices and vaping liquid; requiring a certain amount of revenue from the sales and
6 use tax on electronic smoking devices and vaping liquid to be distributed to the
7 Maryland Pediatric Cancer Fund; and generally relating to the sales and use tax on
8 electronic smoking devices and vaping liquid.
9 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
10 Article – Health – General
11 Section 20–120
12 Annotated Code of Maryland
13 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
14 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
15 Article – Tax – General
16 Section 2–1302.4 and 11–104(j)
17 Annotated Code of Maryland
18 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
19 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
20 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
21 Article – Health – General
22 20–120.
23 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

2 HOUSE BILL 1576
1 (2) “Fund” means the Maryland Pediatric Cancer Fund established under
2 this section.
3 (3) “Pediatric cancer research” includes research to develop and advance
4 the understanding of pediatric cancer and the techniques and modalities effective in the
5 prevention, cure, screening, and treatment of pediatric cancer.
6 (b) (1) There is a Maryland Pediatric Cancer Fund.
7 (2) The Fund consists of:
8 (i) Money appropriated in the State budget to the Fund;
9 (II) MONEY DISTRIBUTED TO THE FUND UNDER § 2–1302.4 OF
10 THE TAX – GENERAL ARTICLE; and
11 [(ii)] (III) Any donations or other money from any other source
12 accepted for the benefit of the Fund.
13 (3) The Secretary shall administer the Fund.
14 (4) The Fund shall be used only for pediatric cancer research and
15 administrative expenses as provided in subsection (c) of this section.
16 (5) The Fund shall be maintained for the purposes stated in this section
17 and unspent portions of the Fund shall remain in the Fund and may not revert to the
18 General Fund of the State.
19 (6) Money expended from the Fund for pediatric cancer research is
20 supplemental and is not intended to take the place of funding that would otherwise be
21 appropriated to the Department for pediatric cancer research.
22 (7) All expenditures from the Fund shall be made only in accordance with
23 an appropriation approved by the General Assembly in the annual State budget or through
24 an approved budget amendment under §§ 7–209 and 7–210 of the State Finance and
25 Procurement Article.
26 (8) (i) For fiscal year 2023, the Governor shall include in the annual
27 budget bill an appropriation of $5,000,000 to the Fund.
28 (ii) If $5,000,000 is not included in the annual budget bill for fiscal
29 year 2023, then the Governor shall include in the annual budget bill for fiscal year 2024 an
30 appropriation of $5,000,000 to the Fund.
31 (c) (1) (i) Each year, the Secretary may:
HOUSE BILL 1576 3
1 1. Distribute funds from the Fund to a promotional account
2 to be allocated to a State–based childhood cancer organization to be used to promote further
3 donations to the Fund and to educate the State scientific community about the
4 opportunities for childhood cancer research through the Fund; and
5 2. Use funds from the Fund to administer the Fund,
6 including for staff and administrative costs associated with the Commission established
7 under § 20–121 of this title.
8 (ii) The amounts distributed and used under subparagraph (i) of this
9 paragraph may not exceed a total of 5% of the Fund.
10 (2) (i) After making the distribution allowed under paragraph (1) of
11 this subsection, the Secretary shall use the remainder of the Fund only to provide grants
12 to eligible physicians, hospitals, laboratories, educational institutions, and other
13 organizations and persons to conduct pediatric cancer research.
14 (ii) Money received from the grant program under this section may
15 not be used to pay for institutional overhead expenses, organizational expenses, or charges
16 that are not directly related to pediatric cancer research or expenses arising from
17 community–based support services.
18 (d) (1) On or before August 31 each year, the Secretary shall submit a report
19 to the General Assembly, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government Article, on
20 the administration of the Fund.
21 (2) The report required under this subsection shall include:
22 (i) A description of promotional efforts undertaken with money from
23 the Fund; and
24 (ii) A detailed accounting of the use of the Fund.
25 (e) The Secretary shall adopt regulations to implement a pediatric cancer grant
26 program under this section.
27 Article – Tax – General
28 2–1302.4.
29 After making the distributions required under §§ 2–1301 through 2–1302.2 of this
30 subtitle, of the sales and use tax collected [from the sale of electronic smoking devices, as
31 defined in] UNDER § 11–104(j) of this article, the Comptroller shall distribute:
32 (1) FROM THE SALE OF ELECTRONIC SMOKING DEVICES:
4 HOUSE BILL 1576
1 (I) the revenue attributable to a tax rate of 8% to the Blueprint for
2 Maryland’s Future Fund established under § 5–206 of the Education Article; AND
3 (II) THE REVENUE ATTRIBUTABLE TO A TAX RATE OF 10% TO
4 THE MARYLAND PEDIATRIC CANCER FUND ESTABLISHED UNDER § 20–120 OF THE
5 HEALTH – GENERAL ARTICLE; AND
6 (2) FROM THE SALE OF VAPING LIQUID SOLD IN A CONTAINER THAT
7 CONTAINS 5 MILLILITERS OR LESS OF VAPING LIQUID, THE REVENUE
8 ATTRIBUTABLE TO A TAX RATE OF 30% TO THE MARYLAND PEDIATRIC CANCER
9 FUND ESTABLISHED UNDER § 20–120 OF THE HEALTH – GENERAL ARTICLE.
10 11–104.
11 (j) (1) (i) In this subsection, the following words have the meanings
12 indicated.
13 (ii) “Electronic smoking device” has the meaning stated in §
14 16.7–101 of the Business Regulation Article.
15 (iii) “Tobacco pipe” means a pipe made primarily of meerschaum,
16 wood, or porcelain, with a bowl designed to be used without a screen or filter.
17 (iv) “Vaping liquid” has the meaning stated in § 16.7–101 of the
18 Business Regulation Article.
19 (2) Except as provided in paragraph (3) of this subsection, the sales and
20 use tax rate is [20%] 30% of the taxable price for electronic smoking devices.
21 (3) The sales and use tax for vaping liquid sold in a container that contains
22 5 milliliters or less of vaping liquid is [60%] 90% of the taxable price.
23 (4) The sales and use tax for tobacco pipes is 12% of the taxable price.
24 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
25 1, 2026.
