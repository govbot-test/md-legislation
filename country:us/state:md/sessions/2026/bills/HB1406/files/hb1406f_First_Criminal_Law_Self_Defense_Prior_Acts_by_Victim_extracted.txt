E1 6lr3086
By: Delegates Smith, Embry, and Ruff
Introduced and read first time: February 13, 2026
Assigned to: Judiciary
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Criminal Law – Self–Defense – Prior Acts by Victim
3 FOR the purpose of authorizing a person charged with a certain offense to assert a certain
4 defense without proving a certain element of that defense if the victim had previously
5 committed a certain act against the person; and generally relating to self–defense.
6 BY adding to
7 Article – Criminal Law
8 Section 1–403
9 Annotated Code of Maryland
10 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
11 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
12 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
13 Article – Criminal Law
14 1–403.
15 A PERSON CHARGED WITH MURDER OR MANSLAUGHTER IN ANY DEGREE OR A
16 CRIME UNDER TITLE 3, SUBTITLE 2 OF THIS ARTICLE MAY ASSERT THE DEFENSE
17 THAT THE PERSON ACTED IN SELF–DEFENSE AND NEED NOT PROVE THAT THE
18 PERSON BELIEVED DANGER WAS IMMINENT OR IMMEDIATE IF THE VICTIM HAD
19 PREVIOUSLY COMMITTED:
20 (1) AN ASSAULT IN ANY DEGREE AGAINST THE PERSON; OR
21 (2) AN ACT CONSTITUTING SEXUALLY ASSAULTIVE BEHAVIOR
22 AGAINST THE PERSON, AS DEFINED IN § 10–923 OF THE COURTS ARTICLE.
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

2 HOUSE BILL 1406
1 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
2 October 1, 2026.
