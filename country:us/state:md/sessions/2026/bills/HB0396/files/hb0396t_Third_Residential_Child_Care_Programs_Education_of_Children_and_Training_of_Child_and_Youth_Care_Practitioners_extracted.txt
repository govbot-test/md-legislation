CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Residential Child Care Programs – Education of Children and Training of Child
3 and Youth Care Practitioners
4 FOR the purpose of repealing the maximum age for children in certain residential child
5 care programs who receive educational support; requiring operators of certain
6 residential child care programs to provide certain educational opportunities to the
7 children for whom they have legal custody or care and control; requiring that
8 applicants to be child and youth care practitioners undergo certain training;
9 prohibiting an individual who holds a certificate as a residential child and youth care
10 practitioner from being required to complete certain training under certain
11 circumstances; and generally relating to residential child care programs.
12 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
13 Article – Education
14 Section 7–309
15 Annotated Code of Maryland
16 (2025 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
17 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
18 Article – Health Occupations
19 Section 20–302.1

9 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
10 Article – Education
11 7–309.
12 (a) (1) In this section, “residential child care program” means a program that:
13 (i) Provides care for children 24 hours a day within a structured set
14 of services and activities designed to achieve objectives related to the needs of the children
15 served; and
16 (ii) Is licensed by the Maryland Department of Health, the
17 Department of Human Services, or the Department of Juvenile Services.
18 (2) A “residential child care program” includes:
19 (i) Group homes;
20 (ii) Alternative living units; and
21 (iii) Emergency shelter care.
22 (b) Each licensed operator of a residential child care program who has legal
23 custody or care and control of a child who is at least 5 years old and under the age of 16
24 years and receives State funding as provided in § 5–526 of the Family Law Article:
25 (1) Shall enroll the child in the local school system where the residential
26 child care program is located unless the residential child care program operates an
27 approved educational program in accordance with the licensing regulations that govern the
28 residential child care program ENSURE THE CHILD REMAINS ENROLLED IN THE
29 SCHOOL IN WHICH THE CHILD WAS PREVIOUSLY ENROLLED, UNLESS A
30 DETERMINATION IS MADE BY A LOCAL DEPARTMENT OF SOCIAL SERVICES THAT
31 REMAINING ENROLLED IS NOT IN THE CHILD’S BEST INTEREST, THEN:

4 APPROVED EDUCATIONAL PROGRAM IN ACCORDANCE WITH THE LICENSING
5 REGULATIONS THAT GOVERN RESIDENTIAL CHILD CARE PROGRAMS, THE CHILD
6 SHALL BE ENROLLED IN THAT PROGRAM;
7 (2) Shall expeditiously initiate and monitor the transfer of the academic
8 records of a child in the operator’s care from the transferring school to [insure] ENSURE
9 that the academic records are transferred to the school that the child will be attending
10 while living in the operator’s care;
11 (3) (i) May request a meeting with the child’s teachers; [and]

17 EXTRACURRICULAR OPPORTUNITIES TO SUPPORT THE CHILD;
18 (4) (3) Shall sign the child’s report card, [insure] ENSURE that the

20 record; AND
21 (5) (4) SHALL ENSURE THAT THE CHILD IS PROVIDED:
22 (I) REGULARLY SCHEDULED TIME TO STUDY TO SUPPORT THE
23 CHILD’S ACADEMIC WORK;

25 SKILLS PRACTICE, AND TEST PREPARATION IN ORDER TO REINFORCE EDUCATIONAL
26 CONCEPTS TAUGHT TO THE CHILD;
27 (III) ENCOURAGEMENT AND SUPPORT FOR PARTICIPATION IN
28 EXTRACURRICULAR ACTIVITIES;
29 (IV) FOR A CHILD WITH AN INDIVIDUALIZED EDUCATION
30 PROGRAM UNDER THE FEDERAL INDIVIDUALS WITH DISABILITIES EDUCATION ACT

32 APPLICABLE EDUCATIONAL GOALS TO COMPLETE WITHIN THE RESIDENTIAL CHILD

6 COMPLETE ASSIGNMENTS THAT REQUIRE COMPUTER SOFTWARE OR THE INTERNET.
7 Article – Health Occupations
8 20–302.1.
9 (a) To qualify for a certificate as a residential child and youth care practitioner,
10 an applicant shall be an individual who meets the requirements of this section.
11 (b) The applicant shall be of good moral character.
12 (c) The applicant shall have completed:
13 (1) A State and national criminal history records check; and
14 (2) Unless waived by the Board under § 20–303(d) of this subtitle, a child
15 protective services background clearance.
16 (d) The applicant shall be:
17 (1) At least 21 years old; or
18 (2) At least 18 years old and have earned at least an associate’s or
19 bachelor’s degree from an accredited college or university.
20 (e) The applicant shall have:
21 (1) A high school diploma or equivalent and have successfully completed
22 an approved training program; and
23 (2) (i) At least 2 years’ experience in the human service field; or
24 (ii) An associate’s or bachelor’s degree from an accredited college or
25 university.
26 (f) (1) (I) Except as provided in paragraph (2) of this subsection, the
27 applicant shall have successfully completed a training program approved by the Board.
28 (II) THE BOARD’S PROGRAM SHALL INCLUDE TRAINING ON:

1 1. THE ROLE OF THE RESIDENTIAL CHILD AND YOUTH
2 CARE PRACTITIONER;
3 2. CHILD DEVELOPMENT;
4 3. CHILDREN WITH SPECIAL NEEDS DISABILITIES;
5 4. RECOGNIZING DIFFERENCES IN PROCESSING
6 INFORMATION AND THE DIVERSE WAYS CHILDREN LEARN;
7 5. RECOGNIZING BEHAVIORS THAT IMPACT A CHILD’S
8 ABILITY TO LEARN AND PRACTICE SOCIAL NORMS;
9 6. CHILD ABUSE AND NEGLECT IDENTIFICATION AND
10 REPORTING;
11 7. SUICIDE RISK ASSESSMENT AND PREVENTION;
12 8. APPROVED FORMS OF DISCIPLINE AND BEHAVIORAL
13 MANAGEMENT TECHNIQUES, INCLUDING THE USE OF BEHAVIORAL INTERVENTION
14 PLANS UNDER TITLE 7, SUBTITLE 11 OF THE EDUCATION ARTICLE;
15 9. COMMUNICATION SKILLS;
16 10. PARENTING ISSUES, COLLABORATION WITH
17 FAMILIES, AND THE SUPPORT OF CHILDREN;
18 11. THE PSYCHOSOCIAL AND EMOTIONAL NEEDS OF
19 CHILDREN, FAMILIAL RELATIONSHIPS, AND THE IMPACT OF SEPARATION;
20 12. EMERGENCY PREPAREDNESS AND SAFETY
21 PRACTICES;
22 13. ANNUAL FIRST AID TRAINING, INCLUDING
23 CERTIFICATION IN CARDIOPULMONARY RESUSCITATION;
24 14. MEDICATION MANAGEMENT;
25 15. INFECTION CONTROL; AND
26 16. IF APPLICABLE, FOOD PREPARATION AND
27 NUTRITION.

1 (2) (i) An applicant who has an associate’s or bachelor’s degree from an
2 accredited college or university may be waived from the training program requirement, if
3 the applicant passes an examination and meets other requirements established by the
4 Board under this subtitle.
5 (ii) The Board shall establish requirements and procedures for
6 waiving the training program requirement for an applicant under subparagraph (i) of this
7 paragraph.
8 (g) The applicant shall pass an examination given by the Board under this
9 subtitle.

12 (1) Applies for certification on or before October 1, 2015; and
13 (2) Presents to the Board satisfactory evidence that the applicant worked

15 October 1, 2015.
16 Article – Human Services
17 8–704.
18 A contract awarded or renewed between an agency and a provider for a residential
19 child care program shall:
20 (8) require the provider to comply with § 7–309 of the Education Article;
21 (10) require the residential child care program to have certified residential
22 child and youth care practitioners, as required under § 20–301 of the Health Occupations
23 Article; and
24 (11) require the residential child care program to have a certified program
25 administrator as required under § 20–301 of the Health Occupations Article.
26 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That an individual who holds a
27 certificate as a residential child and youth care practitioner on July 1, 2026, may not be
28 required to complete:
29 (1) any program of training required by § 20–302.1 of the Health
30 Occupations Article, as enacted by Section 1 of this Act, that the individual has already
31 completed; and

1 (2) any new program of training required by § 20–302.1 of the Health
2 Occupations Article, as enacted by Section 1 of this Act, until the individual’s certificate is
3 due for renewal in accordance with § 20–310 of the Health Occupations Article.
4 SECTION 2. 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
5 July 1, 2026.
