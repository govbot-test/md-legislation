CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Business Regulation – Rounding Cash Transactions – Authorization
3 FOR the purpose of authorizing a merchant to round the portion of the price of a certain
4 cash transactions transaction for which a customer pays using cash in a certain
5 manner or round the amount of change due to a customer in a certain manner;
6 authorizing an employer to round a wage that the employer pays using cash in a
7 certain manner; providing that certain provisions of this Act shall preempt existing
8 regulations, rules, and ordinances that conflict with certain provisions of this Act;
9 and generally relating to rounding cash transactions.
10 BY adding to
11 Article – Business Regulation
12 Section 1–601 through 1–607 1–606 to be under the new subtitle “Subtitle 6.
13 Rounding Cash Transactions in a Trade or Business”

15 (2024 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
16 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
17 Article – Labor and Employment
18 Section 3–502(a)
19 Annotated Code of Maryland
20 (2025 Replacement Volume)
21 BY adding to
22 Article – Labor and Employment

4 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
5 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
6 Article – Business Regulation
7 SUBTITLE 6. ROUNDING CASH TRANSACTIONS IN A TRADE OR BUSINESS.
8 1–601.
9 (A) IN THIS SUBTITLE THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
10 INDICATED.
11 (B) (1) “CASH” MEANS UNITED STATES COINS AND CURRENCY.
12 (2) “CASH” DOES NOT INCLUDE:
13 (I) A PAPER INSTRUMENT OTHER THAN A FEDERAL RESERVE
14 NOTE; OR
15 (II) A GIFT CARD.
16 (C) “CUSTOMER” MEANS A PURCHASER OF A GOOD OR SERVICE FROM A
17 MERCHANT.
18 (D) “MERCHANT” MEANS A PERSON ENGAGED IN THE TRADE OR BUSINESS
19 OF THE SALE OF GOODS OR SERVICES.
20 (E) “TOTAL PRICE PRICE” MEANS THE PRICE AMOUNT DUE AND PAYABLE
21 BY A CUSTOMER IS RESPONSIBLE FOR PAYING FOR A GOOD OR SERVICE AFTER A
22 MERCHANT:
23 (1) SUBTRACTS ANY DISCOUNT OR DEDUCTION; AND
24 (2) APPLIES ANY APPLICABLE TAX OR FEE; AND
25 (3) ROUNDS THE TRANSACTION CONSISTENT WITH THIS SUBTITLE.
26 1–602.
27 THIS SUBTITLE:
SENATE BILL 893 3
1 (1) DOES NOT APPLY TO A CASH TRANSACTION FOR A GOOD OR
2 SERVICE IN EXCHANGE FOR PAID WITH EXACT CHANGE; AND
3 (2) MAY NOT BE CONSTRUED TO AUTHORIZE A MERCHANT TO ROUND
4 A PRICE FOR A GOOD OR SERVICE IN A MANNER THAT ALTERS THE CALCULATION OF
5 ANY TAX OR FEE APPLICABLE TO THE TRANSACTION.
6 1–603.
7 (A) THIS SECTION APPLIES TO:
8 (1) THE PORTION OF THE PRICE OF AN IN–PERSON, CASH
9 TRANSACTION IF THE PRICE TOTALS AT LEAST 5 CENTS; OR
10 (2) OR A TELEPHONE, MAIL, OR INTERNET TRANSACTION FOR
11 WHICH A CUSTOMER PAYS USING CASH IF THE PRICE TOTALS AT LEAST 5 CENTS.
12 (B) NOTWITHSTANDING ANY OTHER PROVISION OF LAW, A MERCHANT
13 DOING BUSINESS IN THE STATE MAY:
14 (1) DETERMINE THE TOTAL PRICE OF ROUND THE PRICE FOR A GOOD
15 OR SERVICE, AFTER SUBTRACTING ANY DISCOUNT OR DEDUCTION FROM THE PRICE
16 AND APPLYING ANY APPLICABLE TAX OR FEE TO THE PRICE, IN THE FOLLOWING
17 MANNER:
18 (I) IF THE PRICE ENDS IN 1 CENT, 2 CENTS, 6 CENTS, OR 7
19 CENTS, BY ROUNDING THE PRICE DOWN TO THE NEAREST CENT DIVISIBLE BY FIVE;
20 OR
21 (II) IF THE PRICE ENDS IN 3 CENTS, 4 CENTS, 8 CENTS, OR 9
22 CENTS, BY ROUNDING THE PRICE UP TO THE NEAREST CENT DIVISIBLE BY FIVE; OR
23 (2) ROUND THE AMOUNT OF CHANGE DUE TO A CUSTOMER UP OR
24 DOWN CONSISTENT WITH ITEM (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION.
25 1–604.
26 (A) THIS SECTION APPLIES TO:
27 (1) THE PORTION OF THE PRICE OF AN IN–PERSON, CASH
28 TRANSACTION IF THE PRICE TOTALS LESS THAN 5 CENTS; OR
4 SENATE BILL 893
1 (2) OR A TELEPHONE, MAIL, OR INTERNET TRANSACTION FOR
2 WHICH A PERSON CUSTOMER PAYS USING CASH IF THE PRICE TOTALS LESS THAN 5
3 CENTS.
4 (B) NOTWITHSTANDING ANY OTHER PROVISION OF LAW, A MERCHANT
5 DOING BUSINESS IN THE STATE MAY DETERMINE THE TOTAL PRICE OF ROUND THE
6 PRICE FOR A GOOD OR SERVICE, AFTER SUBTRACTING ANY DISCOUNT OR
7 DEDUCTION FROM THE PRICE AND APPLYING ANY APPLICABLE TAX OR FEE TO THE
8 PRICE, BY ROUNDING THE PRICE UP TO 5 CENTS.
9 1–605.
10 AN EMPLOYER, AS DEFINED IN § 3–501 OF THE LABOR AND EMPLOYMENT
11 ARTICLE, MAY ROUND A WAGE, AS DEFINED IN § 3–501 OF THE LABOR AND
12 EMPLOYMENT ARTICLE, THAT THE EMPLOYER PAYS USING CASH CONSISTENT WITH
13 THIS SUBTITLE.
14 1–606. 1–605.
15 THIS SUBTITLE SUPERSEDES ANY REGULATION, RULE, OR ORDINANCE THAT
16 CONFLICTS WITH THIS SUBTITLE.
17 1–607. 1–606.
18 THE SECRETARY MAY ADOPT REGULATIONS TO CARRY OUT THE PROVISIONS
19 OF THIS SUBTITLE.
20 Article – Labor and Employment
21 3–502.
22 (a) (1) Each employer:
23 (i) shall set regular pay periods; and
24 (ii) except as provided in paragraph (2) of this subsection, shall pay
25 each employee at least once in every 2 weeks or twice in each month.
26 (2) An employer may pay an administrative, executive, or professional
27 employee less frequently than required under paragraph (1)(ii) of this subsection.
28 (G) AN EMPLOYER MAY ROUND A WAGE THAT THE EMPLOYER PAYS USING
29 CASH, AS DEFINED IN § 1–601 OF THE BUSINESS REGULATION ARTICLE,
30 CONSISTENT WITH TITLE 1, SUBTITLE 6 OF THE BUSINESS REGULATION ARTICLE.

4 each of the two Houses of the General Assembly, and shall take effect from the date it is
