A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 State Board of Dental Examiners – Disciplinary Action and Unannounced
3 Inspections
4 FOR the purpose of authorizing the State Board of Dental Examiners to conduct
5 unannounced inspections of the office of certain dentists to determine compliance
6 with certain guidelines for infection control; altering the grounds for discipline of
7 dentists, dental hygienists, and dental and dental hygienist license applicants;
8 authorizing the Board to take disciplinary action against certified expanded function
9 dental assistants and certificate applicants under certain circumstances; and
10 generally relating to the State Board of Dental Examiners.
11 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
12 Article – Health Occupations
13 Section 4–205(a)(6) and 4–315(a)(30), (37), and (38) and (c)(18)
14 Annotated Code of Maryland
15 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
16 BY adding to
17 Article – Health Occupations
18 Section 4–315(a)(39) and (d)
19 Annotated Code of Maryland
20 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
21 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
22 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
23 Article – Health Occupations
24 4–205.
25 (a) In addition to the powers set forth elsewhere in this title, the Board may:

1 (6) On receipt of a written and signed complaint, including a referral from
2 the Commissioner of Labor and Industry, conduct an unannounced inspection of the office
3 of a dentist, other than an office of a dentist in a hospital, related institution, freestanding
4 medical facility, or a freestanding birthing center, to determine compliance at that office
5 with the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention’s guidelines on [universal]
6 STANDARD precautions AND THE CENTERS FOR DISEASE CONTROL AND
7 PREVENTION’S GUIDELINES FOR INFECTION CONTROL IN DENTAL HEALTH–CARE
8 SETTINGS; and
9 4–315.
10 (a) Subject to the hearing provisions of § 4–318 of this subtitle, the Board may
11 deny a general license to practice dentistry, a limited license to practice dentistry, or a
12 teacher’s license to practice dentistry to any applicant, reprimand any licensed dentist,
13 place any licensed dentist on probation, or suspend or revoke the license of any licensed
14 dentist, if the applicant or licensee:
15 (30) Except in an emergency life–threatening situation where it is not
16 feasible or practicable, fails to comply with the Centers for Disease Control and
17 Prevention’s guidelines on [universal] STANDARD precautions OR THE CENTERS FOR
18 DISEASE CONTROL AND PREVENTION’S GUIDELINES FOR INFECTION CONTROL IN
19 DENTAL HEALTH–CARE SETTINGS;
20 (37) Accepts or tenders rebates or splits fees in violation of § 4–103(c) of this
21 title; [or]
22 (38) Allows a dental assistant to assist in the practice of dentistry:
23 (i) In an unauthorized manner in violation of this title or
24 regulations adopted by the Board;
25 (ii) Without specifically instructing the certified dental assistant to
26 perform an intraoral procedure that the certified dental assistant is authorized to perform;
27 or
28 (iii) Failing to provide direct supervision of a dental assistant; OR
29 (39) EMPLOYS, SUPERVISES, OR USES THE SERVICES OF AN
30 INDIVIDUAL AS AN EXPANDED FUNCTION DENTAL ASSISTANT WHO IS NOT CERTIFIED
31 BY THE BOARD UNDER § 4–301.1 OF THIS SUBTITLE.

33 deny a general license to practice dental hygiene, a teacher’s license to practice dental
34 hygiene, or a temporary license to practice dental hygiene to any applicant, reprimand any
