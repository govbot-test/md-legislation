CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Criminal Procedure – Evidence – Protecting Artists’ Creative Expression
3 (PACE Act)
4 FOR the purpose of providing that the creative expression of a criminal defendant or
5 juvenile respondent is not admissible against the defendant or respondent unless the
6 court makes certain findings, subject to a certain exception; and generally relating
7 to the admissibility of creative expression in criminal or juvenile proceedings.
8 BY adding to
9 Article – Courts and Judicial Proceedings
10 Section 10–926
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

2 SENATE BILL 475
1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – Courts and Judicial Proceedings
4 10–926.
5 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION, “CREATIVE EXPRESSION” MEANS THE
6 EXPRESSION OR APPLICATION OF CREATIVITY OR IMAGINATION IN THE
7 PRODUCTION OR ARRANGEMENT OF FORMS, SOUNDS, WORDS, MOVEMENTS, OR
8 SYMBOLS THAT IS ELIGIBLE FOR FEDERAL COPYRIGHT PROTECTION UNDER 17
9 U.S.C. § 102.
10 (2) “CREATIVE EXPRESSION” INCLUDES:
11 (I) MUSIC;
12 (II) DANCE;
13 (III) PERFORMANCE ART;
14 (IV) VISUAL ART;
15 (V) POETRY;
16 (VI) LITERATURE; AND
17 (VII) FILM.
18 (B) IN ANY CRIMINAL PROCEEDING OR JUVENILE PROCEEDING, THE
19 CREATIVE EXPRESSION OF A DEFENDANT OR RESPONDENT IS NOT ADMISSIBLE
20 AGAINST THE DEFENDANT OR RESPONDENT UNLESS THE COURT FINDS, BY A
21 PREPONDERANCE OF THE EVIDENCE, THAT:
22 (1) (I) THE DEFENDANT OR RESPONDENT INTENDED THE
23 CREATIVE EXPRESSION TO BE LITERAL, RATHER THAN FIGURATIVE OR FICTIONAL;
24 OR
25 (II) IF THE CREATIVE EXPRESSION IS DERIVATIVE, THE
26 DEFENDANT INTENDED TO ADOPT THE LITERAL MEANING OF THE CREATIVE
27 EXPRESSION AS THEIR OWN;

SENATE BILL 475 3
1 (2) THE CREATIVE EXPRESSION REFERS TO THE SPECIFIC FACTS OF
2 THE ALLEGED OFFENSE; AND THERE IS A CLOSE TEMPORAL AND FACTUAL NEXUS
3 BETWEEN THE CREATIVE EXPRESSION AND THE ALLEGED OFFENSE;
4 (3) THE CREATIVE EXPRESSION IS RELEVANT TO A DISPUTED ISSUE
5 OF FACT.; AND
6 (4) THE PROBATIVE VALUE OF THE CREATIVE EXPRESSION
7 OUTWEIGHS ITS PREJUDICIAL EFFECT.
8 (C) NOTHING IN THIS SECTION PRECLUDES THE ADMISSION OF CREATIVE
9 EXPRESSION IN JUVENILE CASES FOR THE PURPOSES OF EVALUATING,
10 RECOMMENDING, OR ORDERING REFERRAL TO MENTAL HEALTH SERVICES OR
11 DIVERSION PROGRAMS.
12 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
13 October 1, 2026.
