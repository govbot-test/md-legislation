5 the annual budget bill an appropriation for the Program that is at least a certain

7 to the Walter Sondheim Jr. Public Service Internship Scholarship Program.

5 Article – Education
6 18–1702.
7 (a) (1) There is a scholarship program known as the Walter Sondheim Jr.
8 Public Service Internship Scholarship Program in the State.
9 (2) THE PROGRAM CONSISTS OF AT LEAST THE FOLLOWING PUBLIC
10 SERVICE INTERN FELLOWSHIPS:
11 (I) THE GOVERNOR’S SUMMER INTERNSHIP PROGRAM;
12 (II) THE SONDHEIM MARYLAND NONPROFIT LEADERSHIP
13 INTERNSHIP PROGRAM; AND
14 (III) THE SONDHEIM PUBLIC SERVICE LAW FELLOWS
15 PROGRAM.
16 (b) The purpose of the Program is to assist college and graduate students to
17 explore public service career opportunities through internships.
18 (c) The Shriver Center shall administer the Program.
19 (d) An institution of higher education in the State may nominate eligible students
20 to participate in the Program on or before:
21 (1) January 1 of each year for summer and fall internships; and
22 (2) October 1 of each year for spring internships.
23 (e) Priority for participation in the Program shall be given to an eligible student
24 who:
25 (1) Is a resident of the State;
26 (2) Has demonstrated an interest in a career in public service; and
27 (3) Assists in providing:
28 (i) Legal services:

1 1. To low–income residents in the State who cannot afford
2 legal services; or
3 2. In a public service position;
4 (ii) Social work services to low–income residents in the State;
5 (iii) Nursing services in nursing shortage areas in the State as
6 defined in § 18–802 of this title; or
7 (iv) Other services in the public or nonprofit sectors in which there is
8 a shortage of qualified practitioners to low–income or underserved residents or areas of the
9 State.
10 (f) (1) Subject to paragraph (2) of this subsection, for eligible students who
11 have agreed to serve in a public service internship, the Shriver Center shall award
12 scholarships on or before:
13 (i) January 15 of each year for spring internships;
14 (ii) May 1 of each year for summer internships; and
15 (iii) August 1 of each year for fall internships.
16 (2) Funds for the scholarships awarded under this subtitle shall be as
17 provided in the State budget.
18 (g) Subject to the availability of funds, the scholarship award under the Program
19 shall be at least $2,000 and no more than $5,000.
20 (h) The Shriver Center shall serve as a clearinghouse for public and nonprofit
21 entities that wish to hire public service interns participating in the Program.
