CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Voting Rights Act of 2026 – Counties and Municipal Corporations
3 FOR the purpose of prohibiting the imposition or application of a method for electing the
4 governing body of a county or municipal corporation that impairs the ability of
5 members of a protected class to elect candidates of the members’ choice or influence
6 the outcome of an election by diluting or abridging the rights of voters who are
7 members of a protected class; and generally relating to voting rights of members of
8 protected classes in elections in counties and municipal corporations.
9 BY adding to
10 Article – Election Law
11 Section 15.7–101 through 15.7–107 to be under the new title “Title 15.7. Voting
12 Rights – Counties and Municipal Corporations”
13 Annotated Code of Maryland
14 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

2 SENATE BILL 255
1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – Election Law
4 TITLE 15.7. VOTING RIGHTS – COUNTIES AND MUNICIPAL CORPORATIONS.
5 15.7–101.
6 (A) IN THIS TITLE THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
7 INDICATED.
8 (B) “POLARIZED VOTING” MEANS VOTING IN WHICH THERE IS A
9 DIFFERENCE IN THE CHOICE OF CANDIDATES OR OTHER ELECTORAL CHOICES THAT
10 ARE PREFERRED BY VOTERS IN A PROTECTED CLASS AND IN THE CHOICE OF
11 CANDIDATES AND ELECTORAL CHOICES THAT ARE PREFERRED BY ANOTHER CLASS
12 OR SET OF CLASSES.
13 (C) “PROTECTED CLASS” MEANS A CLASS OF VOTERS WHO ARE MEMBERS
14 OF A RACE, COLOR, OR LANGUAGE MINORITY GROUP OR A CLASS OF TWO OR MORE
15 OF THOSE GROUPS.
16 15.7–102.
17 (A) THIS TITLE APPLIES ONLY TO ELECTIONS IN A COUNTY OR MUNICIPAL
18 CORPORATION.
19 (B) THIS TITLE DOES NOT APPLY TO STATEWIDE ELECTIONS.
20 15.7–103.
21 (A) A METHOD FOR ELECTING THE GOVERNING BODY OF A COUNTY OR
22 MUNICIPAL CORPORATION MAY NOT BE IMPOSED OR APPLIED IN A MANNER THAT
23 IMPAIRS THE ABILITY OF MEMBERS OF A PROTECTED CLASS TO ELECT CANDIDATES
24 OF THE MEMBERS’ CHOICE OR THE MEMBERS’ ABILITY TO INFLUENCE THE
25 OUTCOME OF AN ELECTION AS A RESULT OF THE DILUTION OR THE ABRIDGEMENT
26 OF THE RIGHTS OF VOTERS WHO ARE MEMBERS OF A PROTECTED CLASS.
27 (B) A VIOLATION OF SUBSECTION (A) OF THIS SECTION IS ESTABLISHED IF:
28 (1) ELECTIONS IN A COUNTY OR MUNICIPAL CORPORATION EXHIBIT
29 POLARIZED VOTING; AND
SENATE BILL 255 3
1 (2) THE METHOD OF ELECTION DILUTES OR ABRIDGES THE VOTING
2 STRENGTH OF MEMBERS OF A PROTECTED CLASS TO ELECT A CANDIDATE OF THE
3 MEMBERS’ CHOICE OR THE MEMBERS’ ABILITY TO INFLUENCE THE OUTCOME OF AN
4 ELECTION, AS DEMONSTRATED BY THE EXISTENCE OF ONE OR MORE METHODS OF
5 ELECTION THAT COULD BE CONSTITUTIONALLY ADOPTED THAT WOULD LIKELY
6 MITIGATE THE DILUTION.
7 15.7–104.
8 (A) TO DETERMINE WHETHER THERE IS POLARIZED VOTING UNDER §
9 15.7–103(B)(1) OF THIS TITLE, THE COURT SHALL CONSIDER:
10 (1) THE METHODOLOGIES FOR ESTIMATING GROUP VOTING
11 BEHAVIOR, AS APPROVED IN FEDERAL CASE LAW, TO ENFORCE THE FEDERAL
12 VOTING RIGHTS ACT OF 1965;
13 (2) ELECTIONS OF THE GOVERNING BODY OF THE COUNTY OR
14 MUNICIPAL CORPORATION;
15 (3) BALLOT QUESTION ELECTIONS;
16 (4) ELECTIONS IN WHICH AT LEAST ONE CANDIDATE IS A MEMBER OF
17 A PROTECTED CLASS; AND
18 (5) OTHER ELECTORAL CHOICES THAT AFFECT THE RIGHTS AND
19 PRIVILEGES OF MEMBERS OF A PROTECTED CLASS.
20 (B) (1) ELECTIONS THAT ARE CONDUCTED BEFORE THE FILING OF AN
21 ACTION TO ENFORCE § 15.7–103(A) OF THIS TITLE ARE MORE PROBATIVE TO
22 ESTABLISH THE EXISTENCE OF POLARIZED VOTING THAN ELECTIONS CONDUCTED
23 AFTER THE FILING OF AN ACTION.
24 (2) THE FOLLOWING MAY NOT PRECLUDE A FINDING OF POLARIZED
25 VOTING THAT RESULTS IN AN UNEQUAL OPPORTUNITY FOR A PROTECTED CLASS TO
26 ELECT CANDIDATES OF THE MEMBERS’ CHOICE:
27 (I) THE ELECTION OF CANDIDATES WHO ARE MEMBERS OF A
28 PROTECTED CLASS AND WHO WERE ELECTED BEFORE THE FILING OF AN ACTION TO
29 ENFORCE § 15.7–103(A) OF THIS TITLE;
30 (II) NONQUANTITATIVE OR NONSTATISTICAL EVIDENCE; OR
31 (III) LOW VOTER TURNOUT OR VOTER REGISTRATION RATES
32 AMONG MEMBERS OF THE PROTECTED CLASS.
4 SENATE BILL 255
1 (3) TO ESTABLISH THE EXISTENCE OF POLARIZED VOTING, THE
2 COURT MAY NOT BE REQUIRED TO:
3 (I) USE A SET NUMBER OR COMBINATION OF ELECTIONS; OR
4 (II) CONSIDER THE CAUSES OF OR REASONS FOR POLARIZED
5 VOTING, INCLUDING PARTISAN EXPLANATIONS OR DISCRIMINATORY INTENT.
6 (C) THE FACT THAT MEMBERS OF A PROTECTED CLASS ARE NOT
7 GEOGRAPHICALLY COMPACT OR CONCENTRATED MAY NOT PRECLUDE A FINDING OF
8 POLARIZED VOTING OR A VIOLATION OF § 15.7–103(A) OF THIS TITLE, BUT IT MAY
9 BE A FACTOR THAT IS CONSIDERED IN DETERMINING AN APPROPRIATE REMEDY.
10 15.7–105.
11 IN DETERMINING WHETHER A VIOLATION OF § 15.7–103(A) OF THIS TITLE HAS
12 OCCURRED WITH RESPECT TO A PROTECTED CLASS, THE COURT MAY CONSIDER
13 EVIDENCE RELEVANT TO WHETHER PROTECTED CLASS MEMBERS ARE VULNERABLE
14 TO OR OTHERWISE AT RISK OF VOTING DISCRIMINATION, INCLUDING:
15 (1) THE HISTORY AND EFFECTS OF DISCRIMINATION AGAINST THE
16 PROTECTED CLASS; AND
17 (2) THE EXTENT TO WHICH PROTECTED CLASS MEMBERS ENCOUNTER
18 BARRIERS, DISPARITIES, OR HOSTILITY REGARDING POLITICAL PARTICIPATION
19 AND CIVIC LIFE.
20 15.7–106.
21 (A) THE OFFICE OF THE ATTORNEY GENERAL AND ANY OTHER PERSON MAY
22 BRING AN ACTION TO ENFORCE § 15.7–103 OF THIS TITLE IN THE COUNTY WHERE
23 THE VIOLATION ALLEGEDLY OCCURRED FOR INJUNCTIVE RELIEF, DAMAGES, OR
24 OTHER RELIEF.
25 (B) (1) SUBJECT TO PARAGRAPH (2) OF THIS SUBSECTION, IF THE COURT
26 FINDS A VIOLATION OF THIS TITLE, THE COURT SHALL HAVE BROAD AUTHORITY TO
27 ORDER APPROPRIATE REMEDIES THAT ARE TAILORED TO ADDRESS THE VIOLATION.
28 (2) A COURT MAY NOT ORDER THE ADOPTION OF A METHOD OF
29 ELECTION THAT IS INCONSISTENT WITH THE METHODS OF ELECTION IN USE IN
30 COUNTIES IN THE STATE WITHOUT THE CONSENT OF THE RELEVANT JURISDICTION.
SENATE BILL 255 5
1 (C) (1) THE COURT SHALL CONSIDER REMEDIES PROPOSED BY ANY
2 PARTIES TO THE ACTION OR INTERESTED PARTIES.
3 (2) THE COURT MAY NOT GIVE DEFERENCE OR PRIORITY TO A
4 PROPOSED REMEDY BECAUSE IT IS PROPOSED BY A COUNTY OR MUNICIPAL
5 CORPORATION.
6 (D) A COURT MAY GRANT PRELIMINARY RELIEF REQUESTED UNDER THIS
7 SECTION REGARDING AN UPCOMING ELECTION IF THE COURT DETERMINES:
8 (1) THAT THE PARTY IS MORE LIKELY THAN NOT TO SUCCEED ON THE
9 MERITS; AND
10 (2) IT IS POSSIBLE TO IMPLEMENT AN APPROPRIATE REMEDY THAT
11 WOULD RESOLVE THE VIOLATION ALLEGED UNDER THIS SECTION BEFORE THE
12 ELECTION.
13 15.7–107.
14 (A) IN AN ACTION TO ENFORCE THIS TITLE, THE COURT MAY AWARD
15 REASONABLE ATTORNEY’S FEES AND LITIGATION COSTS, INCLUDING EXPERT
16 WITNESS FEES AND EXPENSES TO A PRIVATE PLAINTIFF THAT PREVAILED IN THE
17 ACTION.
18 (B) A PRIVATE PLAINTIFF WILL BE DEEMED TO HAVE PREVAILED IN AN
19 ACTION WHEN, AS A RESULT OF THE ACTION, THE COUNTY OR MUNICIPAL
20 CORPORATION YIELDS SOME OR ALL OF THE RELIEF SOUGHT IN THE ACTION.
21 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That, if a court issues a holding
22 that affects the application of a provision of this Act, the ruling does not affect other
23 provisions or any other application of this Act that can be given effect without the affected
24 provision or application, and for this purpose the provisions of this Act are declared
25 severable.
26 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act is an emergency
27 measure, is necessary for the immediate preservation of the public health or safety, has
28 been passed by a yea and nay vote supported by three–fifths of all the members elected to
29 each of the two Houses of the General Assembly, and shall take effect from the date it is
30 enacted.
