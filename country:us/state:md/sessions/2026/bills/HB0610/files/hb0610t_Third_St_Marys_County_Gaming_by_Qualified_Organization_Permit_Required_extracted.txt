1 AN ACT concerning
2 St. Mary’s County – Gaming by Qualified Organization – License Permit
3 Required
4 FOR the purpose of requiring a qualified organization to hold a gaming license permit
5 whenever the qualified organization conducts operates a certain gaming device in St.
6 Mary’s County; authorizing the County Commissioners of St. Mary’s County to adopt
7 certain regulations; requiring the Board to designate a person to administer the
8 issuance of gaming licenses permits in the county; and generally relating to gaming
9 in St. Mary’s County.

1 (b) “County commissioners” means the [Board of] County Commissioners of St.
2 Mary’s County.
3 (c) “Designee” means a person designated by the county commissioners to
4 administer the issuance of bingo licenses OR GAMING LICENSES PERMITS in the county.
5 (d) (1) “GAMING DEVICE” MEANS A MECHANISM FOR PLAYING A GAME OF
6 CHANCE.
7 (2) “Gaming device” includes a paddle wheel, wheel of fortune, chance book,
8 bingo, Nevada card, and a stamp machine.
9 (2) (3) “Gaming device” does not include a slot machine, as defined in §
10 12–301 of this article.
11 (e) “Gaming event” means a carnival, bazaar, or raffle.
12 (f) “Qualified organization” means:
13 (1) a volunteer fire company; or
14 (2) a bona fide:
15 (i) religious organization;
16 (ii) fraternal organization;
17 (iii) civic organization;
18 (iv) war veterans’ organization; or
19 (v) charitable organization.
20 (g) “Sheriff” means the Sheriff of St. Mary’s County.
21 13–2111.
22 (A) SUBJECT TO SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION, A QUALIFIED
23 ORGANIZATION MUST HOLD A GAMING LICENSE PERMIT WHENEVER THE QUALIFIED
24 ORGANIZATION CONDUCTS OPERATES A GAMING DEVICE IN ACCORDANCE WITH
25 THIS SUBTITLE.
26 (B) A qualified organization may hold a gaming event and may operate a gaming
27 device if an individual or group of individuals does not:
28 (1) benefit financially from the operation of the gaming device; or

1 (2) receive from the operation of the gaming device any proceeds for
2 personal use or benefit.
3 (C) THE COUNTY COMMISSIONERS MAY ADOPT REGULATIONS, IN
4 CONSULTATION WITH THE DESIGNEE UNDER SUBSECTION (D) OF THIS SECTION, TO
5 ADMINISTER AND ENFORCE GAMING ACTIVITIES AUTHORIZED THE GAMING DEVICE

7 (D) THE COUNTY COMMISSIONERS SHALL DESIGNATE A PERSON TO
