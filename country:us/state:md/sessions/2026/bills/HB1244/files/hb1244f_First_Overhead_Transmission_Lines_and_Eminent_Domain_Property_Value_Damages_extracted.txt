A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Overhead Transmission Lines and Eminent Domain – Property Value – Damages
3 FOR the purpose of authorizing the owner of property used for residential purposes to bring
4 an action to recover damages incurred as a result of the value of the property being
5 diminished from the use of eminent domain to take property and construct an
6 overhead transmission line within a certain distance of the property owner’s
7 residence; requiring that reasonable fees be awarded to counsel for the defendant in
8 a condemnation proceeding and that the costs be charged against the plaintiff under
9 certain circumstances; and generally relating to overhead transmission lines and
10 eminent domain.

13 Section 12–104(h)

1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – Real Property
4 12–104.
5 (H) (1) THE OWNER OF PROPERTY THAT IS USED FOR RESIDENTIAL
6 PURPOSES MAY BRING AN ACTION IN A COURT OF COMPETENT JURISDICTION FOR
7 DAMAGES INCURRED AS THE RESULT OF THE VALUE OF THAT PROPERTY BEING
8 DIMINISHED BY THE TAKING OF ANOTHER PROPERTY IN ACCORDANCE WITH THIS
9 SUBTITLE IF THE PROPERTY BEING TAKEN:
10 (I) IS LOCATED WITHIN 300 FEET OF THE OWNER’S RESIDENCE;
11 AND
12 (II) WAS TAKEN IN CONJUNCTION WITH THE CONSTRUCTION OF
13 AN OVERHEAD TRANSMISSION LINE OR RELATED INFRASTRUCTURE.
14 (2) A PROPERTY OWNER WHO BRINGS AN ACTION UNDER THIS
15 SUBSECTION AND IS AWARDED DAMAGES MAY ALSO SEEK, AND THE COURT MAY
16 AWARD, REASONABLE ATTORNEY’S FEES.
17 (3) A PROPERTY OWNER MAY BRING AN ACTION UNDER PARAGRAPH
18 (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION ON THE ENTERING OF THE FINAL DECISION IN A
19 CONDEMNATION PROCEEDING.
20 12–105.
21 (b) (1) (I) The fair market value of property in a condemnation proceeding
22 is the price as of the valuation date for the highest and best use of the property which a
23 vendor, willing but not obligated to sell, would accept for the property, and which a
24 purchaser, willing but not obligated to buy, would pay, excluding any increment in value
25 proximately caused by the public project for which the property condemned is needed. [In
26 addition, fair]
27 (II) FAIR market value includes any amount by which the price
28 reflects a diminution in value occurring between the effective date of legislative authority
29 for the acquisition of the property and the date of actual taking if the trier of facts finds
30 that the diminution in value was proximately caused by the public project for which the
31 property condemned is needed, or by announcements or acts of the plaintiff or its officials
32 concerning the public project, and was beyond the reasonable control of the property owner.
