CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Maryland Military Department – Supervision of State Active Duty Personnel
3 FOR the purpose of providing for command and control of State active duty personnel
4 during mobilizations of the Maryland National Guard; and generally relating to the
5 Maryland Military Department.
6 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
7 Article – Public Safety
8 Section 13–702
9 Annotated Code of Maryland
10 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
11 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
12 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
13 Article – Public Safety
14 13–702.
15 (a) This section applies to ALL UNITS OF the [Maryland Defense Force and the
16 National Guard] DEPARTMENT, INCLUDING THE NATIONAL GUARD, THE MARYLAND
17 DEFENSE FORCE, AND STATE OPERATIONS.
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from existing law.
Underlining indicates amendments to bill.
Strike out indicates matter stricken from the bill by amendment or deleted from the law by
amendment. *sb0128*
2 SENATE BILL 128
1 (b) The Governor may order the militia into State active duty:
2 (1) in times of or on reasonable apprehension of imminent public crisis,
3 disaster, rioting, catastrophe, insurrection, invasion, tumult, or breach of peace;
4 (2) when martial law is declared;
5 (3) to enforce the laws; or
6 (4) to carry on any function of the militia of the State.
7 (c) (1) To enforce the laws, a member of the militia in State active duty has all
8 the authority of a peace or law enforcement officer.
9 (2) The authority of the member extends throughout the State during the
10 State active duty.
11 (D) DURING STATE ACTIVE DUTY, MEMBERS OF THE DEPARTMENT ARE
12 UNDER THE OPERATIONAL COMMAND AND CONTROL OF THE ADJUTANT GENERAL,
13 OR ANY OFFICER OF THE NATIONAL GUARD DESIGNATED BY THE ADJUTANT
14 GENERAL, WHETHER THE ADJUTANT GENERAL OR DESIGNEE IS:
15 (1) IN STATE ACTIVE DUTY; OR
16 (2) IN A FEDERAL STATUS IN WHICH THE GOVERNOR IS THE
17 COMMANDER IN CHIEF.
18 [(d)] (E) Whenever the militia is in State active duty, the ranking officer of the
19 militia ordered into State active duty or that officer’s subordinates on State active duty
20 shall:
21 (1) cooperate with local law enforcement authorities; or
22 (2) if the exigencies of the case require and subject only to order from the
23 Governor:
24 (i) direct and control local law enforcement authorities and the
25 Department of State Police; and
26 (ii) assume all the powers vested in these subordinated law
27 enforcement authorities.
28 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
29 1, 2026.
SENATE BILL 128 3
1 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act is an emergency
2 measure, is necessary for the immediate preservation of the public health or safety, has
3 been passed by a yea and nay vote supported by three–fifths of all the members elected to
4 each of the two Houses of the General Assembly, and shall take effect from the date it is
5 enacted.
