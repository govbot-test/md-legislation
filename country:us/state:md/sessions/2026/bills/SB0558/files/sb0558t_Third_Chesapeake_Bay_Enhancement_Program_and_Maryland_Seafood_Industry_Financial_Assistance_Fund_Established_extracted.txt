CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Chesapeake Bay Enhancement Program – Established
3 Chesapeake Bay – Enhancement Program and Maryland Seafood Industry
4 Financial Assistance Fund – Established
5 FOR the purpose of establishing the Chesapeake Bay Enhancement Program to provide
6 funding for certain oyster propagation and replenishment projects that mitigate the
7 effects on the State’s oyster population of certain transportation projects on the
8 State’s oyster population; establishing the Maryland Seafood Industry Financial
9 Assistance Fund to provide financial assistance to certain commercial watermen and
10 small businesses; prohibiting the Department of Natural Resources from making
11 certain approvals for tidal fish licenses or authorizations of certain borrowers; and
12 generally relating to the Chesapeake Bay Enhancement Program.
13 BY adding to
14 Article – Natural Resources
15 Section 4–1020.1
16 Annotated Code of Maryland
17 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
18 BY adding to
19 Article – Economic Development

amendment. *sb0558*
2 SENATE BILL 558
1 Section 10–526.1 and 10–526.2
2 Annotated Code of Maryland
3 (2024 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
4 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
5 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
6 Article – Natural Resources
7 4–1020.1.
8 (A) IN THIS SECTION, “PROGRAM” MEANS THE CHESAPEAKE BAY
9 ENHANCEMENT PROGRAM.
10 (B) THERE IS A CHESAPEAKE BAY ENHANCEMENT PROGRAM IN THE
11 DEPARTMENT.
12 (C) THE PURPOSE OF THE PROGRAM IS TO PROVIDE FUNDING FOR OYSTER
13 PROPAGATION AND REPLENISHMENT PROJECTS THAT MITIGATE THE EFFECTS ON
14 THE STATE’S OYSTER POPULATION OF TRANSPORTATION PROJECTS THAT
15 MAINTAIN OR EXPAND THE HELEN DELICH BENTLEY PORT OF BALTIMORE,
16 INCLUDING:
17 (1) DREDGING;
18 (2) THE CONSTRUCTION OR MAINTENANCE OF:
19 (I) PIERS;
20 (II) BRIDGES; OR
21 (III) SHIPPING CHANNELS; AND
22 (3) CONSTRUCTION AT THE HELEN DELICH BENTLEY PORT OF
23 BALTIMORE OF TRANSPORTATION PROJECTS ON THE STATE’S OYSTER
24 POPULATION.
25 (D) FOR FISCAL YEAR 2028 AND EACH FISCAL YEAR THEREAFTER, THE
26 GOVERNOR SHALL INCLUDE IN THE ANNUAL BUDGET BILL AN APPROPRIATION OF
27 $2,000,000 $2,250,000 TO THE PROGRAM FROM THE TRANSPORTATION TRUST
28 FUND UNDER § 3–216 OF THE TRANSPORTATION ARTICLE.
29 (E) (1) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ADMINISTER THE FUNDS
30 APPROPRIATED UNDER SUBSECTION (D) OF THIS SECTION.
SENATE BILL 558 3
1 (2) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ALLOCATE THE FUNDS APPROPRIATED
2 UNDER SUBSECTION (D) OF THIS SECTION TO SUPPORT OYSTER PROPAGATION AND
3 REPLENISHMENT AND PROGRAM MANAGEMENT.
4 (3) NOTWITHSTANDING PARAGRAPH (2) OF THIS SUBSECTION, THE
5 DEPARTMENT SHALL ALLOCATE AT LEAST $1,500,000 OF THE FUNDS
6 APPROPRIATED UNDER SUBSECTION (D) OF THIS SECTION FOR OYSTER
7 PROPAGATION AND REPLENISHMENT PROJECTS.
8 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That the Laws of Maryland read
9 as follows:
10 Article – Economic Development
11 10–526.1.
12 (A) IN THIS SECTION, “FUND” MEANS THE MARYLAND SEAFOOD INDUSTRY
13 FINANCIAL ASSISTANCE FUND.
14 (B) THERE IS A MARYLAND SEAFOOD INDUSTRY FINANCIAL ASSISTANCE
15 FUND.
16 (C) THE PURPOSE OF THE FUND IS TO PROVIDE FINANCIAL ASSISTANCE,
17 INCLUDING LOANS, TO COMMERCIAL WATERMEN AND SMALL BUSINESSES IN THE
18 SEAFOOD INDUSTRY.
19 (D) THE CORPORATION SHALL ADMINISTER THE FUND.
20 (E) (1) THE FUND IS A SPECIAL, NONLAPSING FUND THAT IS NOT
21 SUBJECT TO § 7–302 OF THE STATE FINANCE AND PROCUREMENT ARTICLE.
22 (2) THE STATE TREASURER SHALL HOLD THE FUND SEPARATELY,
23 AND THE COMPTROLLER SHALL ACCOUNT FOR THE FUND.
24 (F) THE FUND CONSISTS OF:
25 (1) MONEY APPROPRIATED IN THE STATE BUDGET TO THE FUND;
26 (2) FEES AND LOAN REPAYMENTS, INCLUDING INTEREST, RELATED
27 TO THE FINANCIAL ASSISTANCE UNDER THIS SECTION;
28 (3) MONEY MADE AVAILABLE FROM THE FEDERAL, STATE, OR LOCAL
29 GOVERNMENT; AND
4 SENATE BILL 558
1 (4) ANY OTHER MONEY FROM ANY OTHER SOURCE ACCEPTED FOR
2 THE BENEFIT OF THE FUND.
3 (G) THE FUND MAY BE USED ONLY FOR:
4 (1) FINANCIAL ASSISTANCE, INCLUDING LOANS; AND
5 (2) THE COSTS OF THE CORPORATION TO ADMINISTER THIS SECTION.
6 (H) THE STATE TREASURER SHALL INVEST THE MONEY OF THE FUND IN
7 THE SAME MANNER AS OTHER STATE MONEY MAY BE INVESTED.
8 (I) EXPENDITURES FROM THE FUND MAY BE MADE ONLY IN ACCORDANCE
9 WITH THE STATE BUDGET.
10 (J) FOR FINANCIAL ASSISTANCE, INCLUDING LOANS, PROVIDED UNDER
11 THIS SECTION, THE CORPORATION SHALL DETERMINE THE FOLLOWING:
12 (1) THE APPLICATION AND AWARD PROCESS;
13 (2) ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA;
14 (3) THE AMOUNT OF THE FINANCIAL ASSISTANCE, INCLUDING LOANS,
15 FOR EACH APPLICANT;
16 (4) THE TERMS AND CONDITIONS OF FINANCIAL ASSISTANCE,
17 INCLUDING LOANS;
18 (5) THE AMOUNT OF LOAN FORGIVENESS THAT THE CORPORATION
19 MAY PROVIDE FOR LOAN REPAYMENT PERFORMANCE; AND
20 (6) ANY OTHER PROVISIONS NECESSARY TO CARRY OUT THIS
21 SECTION.
22 10–526.2.
23 (A) (1) IF A BORROWER WHO HOLDS A TIDAL FISH LICENSE UNDER §
24 4–701 OF THE NATURAL RESOURCES ARTICLE IS DELINQUENT IN THE REPAYMENT
25 OF A LOAN UNDER THIS SUBTITLE, THE CORPORATION SHALL NOTIFY THE
26 DEPARTMENT OF NATURAL RESOURCES.
SENATE BILL 558 5
1 (2) IF A BORROWER UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION
2 BECOMES COMPLIANT WITH THE REPAYMENT OF A LOAN UNDER THIS SUBTITLE,
3 THE CORPORATION SHALL NOTIFY THE DEPARTMENT OF NATURAL RESOURCES.
4 (B) NOTWITHSTANDING § 4–701 OF THE NATURAL RESOURCES ARTICLE,
5 AFTER RECEIVING A NOTIFICATION UNDER SUBSECTION (A)(1) OF THIS SECTION
6 AND UNTIL RECEIVING A NOTIFICATION UNDER SUBSECTION (A)(2) OF THIS
7 SECTION, THE DEPARTMENT OF NATURAL RESOURCES MAY NOT APPROVE A
8 TRANSFER OF THE TIDAL FISH LICENSE OR AUTHORIZATION.
9 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That Section 2 of this Act shall take
10 effect July 1, 2026.
11 SECTION 2. 4. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That, except as provided in
12 Section 3 of this Act, this Act shall take effect October 1, 2026.
Approved:
