1 AN ACT concerning
2 Sales and Use Tax – Precious Metal Bullion or Coins – Exemption
3 FOR the purpose of altering the definition of “precious metal bullion or coins” for purposes
4 of eligibility for an exemption from the sales and use tax for the sale of precious metal
5 bullion or coins; expanding the exemption by repealing requirements a requirement
6 that the sale price exceed a certain amount and the sale occur at the Baltimore
7 Convention Center; and generally relating to a sales and use tax exemption for
8 precious metal bullion and coins.

10 Article – Tax – General
11 Section 11–214.1

2 SENATE BILL 309
1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – Tax – General
4 11–214.1.
5 (a) In this section:
6 (1) “precious metal bullion or coins” means:
7 (i) any precious metal that has gone through a refining process and
8 is in a state or condition such that its value PRIMARILY depends on its precious metal
9 content and not on its form; or
10 (ii) except as provided in paragraph (2) of this subsection, monetized
11 bullion, coins, or other forms of money that:
12 1. are manufactured from precious metals; and
13 2. are or have in the past been used as a medium of exchange
14 under the laws of the State, the United States, or a foreign nation; and
15 (2) “precious metal bullion or coins” does not include jewelry or a work of
16 art made of precious metal bullion or coins.
17 (b) The sales and use tax does not apply to a sale of precious metal bullion or coins
18 [if:
19 (1) the sale price is greater than $1,000; and
20 (2) the sale occurs at the Baltimore Convention Center].
21 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
22 1, 2026.
