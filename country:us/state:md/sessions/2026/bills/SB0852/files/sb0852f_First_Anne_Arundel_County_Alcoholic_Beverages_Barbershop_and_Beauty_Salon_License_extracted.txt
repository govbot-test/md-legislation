A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Anne Arundel County – Alcoholic Beverages – Barbershop and Beauty Salon
3 License
4 FOR the purpose of authorizing a holder of a barbershop and beauty salon license in Anne
5 Arundel County to provide a certain amount of liquor for on–premises consumption
6 in addition to beer and wine; and generally relating to alcoholic beverages licenses
7 in Anne Arundel County.
8 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
9 Article – Alcoholic Beverages and Cannabis
10 Section 11–102
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2024 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
13 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
14 Article – Alcoholic Beverages and Cannabis
15 Section 11–1002
16 Annotated Code of Maryland
17 (2024 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
18 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
19 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
20 Article – Alcoholic Beverages and Cannabis
21 11–102.
22 This title applies only in Anne Arundel County.
23 11–1002.
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from exist in g l aw . *sb0852*
2 SENATE BILL 852
1 (a) There is a barbershop and beauty salon beer [and], wine, AND LIQUOR
2 license.
3 (b) The Board may issue the license to a holder of:
4 (1) a barbershop permit issued under § 4–501 of the Business Occupations
5 and Professions Article; or
6 (2) a beauty salon permit issued under § 5–501 of the Business Occupations
7 and Professions Article.
8 (c) The license authorizes the license holder to provide not more than 12 ounces
9 of beer [or], 5 ounces of wine by the glass, OR 1.7 OUNCES OF LIQUOR for on–premises
10 consumption by a barbershop or beauty salon customer:
11 (1) when the customer is being provided:
12 (i) a barbering service under Title 4 of the Business Occupations
13 and Professions Article; or
14 (ii) a cosmetology service under Title 5 of the Business Occupations
15 and Professions Article; or
16 (2) while the customer is attending a fund–raising event at the barbershop
17 or beauty salon.
18 (d) A license holder may serve beer [and], wine, AND LIQUOR for on–premises
19 consumption during normal business hours but not later than 9 p.m.
20 (e) A license holder need not obtain a Sunday license under § 11–2004 of this title
21 to provide beer [and], wine, AND LIQUOR on Sunday.
22 (f) The annual license fee is $100.
23 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
24 1, 2026.
