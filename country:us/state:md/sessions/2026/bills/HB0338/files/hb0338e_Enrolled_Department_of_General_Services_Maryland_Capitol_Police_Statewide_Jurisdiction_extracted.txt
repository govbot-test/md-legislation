2 Department of General Services – Maryland Capitol Police – Statewide
3 Jurisdiction
4 FOR the purpose of altering the jurisdiction of the Maryland Capitol Police of the
5 Department of General Services; and generally relating to the Maryland Capitol
6 Police and the Department of General Services.
7 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
8 Article – State Finance and Procurement
9 Section 4–601 and 4–605

11 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

2 HOUSE BILL 338
1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – State Finance and Procurement
4 4–601.
5 (a) Subject to subsection [(b)] (C) of this section, the Department has
6 STATEWIDE jurisdiction over and full police authority for the enforcement of the criminal
7 laws and the parking and motor vehicle laws as to the operation, maintenance, and
8 protection of:
9 (1) [buildings and grounds that, on June 30, 1984, were administered by
10 the Office of Annapolis Public Buildings and Grounds, and extending to the surrounding
11 area that encompasses 1,000 feet in any direction from the boundary of those buildings and
12 grounds;
13 (2) buildings and grounds that, on June 30, 1984, were administered by the
14 Office of Baltimore Public Buildings and Grounds, and extending to the surrounding area
15 that encompasses 1,000 feet in any direction from the boundary of those buildings and
16 grounds;
17 (3) multiservice centers designated by law or by the Board of Public Works
18 and extending to the surrounding area that encompasses 1,000 feet in any direction from
19 the boundary of those multiservice centers;
20 (4) buildings and grounds that, on June 30, 2019, were administered by the
21 Maryland Department of Labor, and extending to the surrounding area that encompasses
22 1,000 feet in any direction from the boundary of those buildings;
23 (5)] State–owned or State–leased buildings and grounds and extending to
24 the surrounding area that encompasses [1,000] 2,000 feet in any direction from the
25 boundary of those buildings and grounds; and
26 [(6)] (2) other public improvements or grounds designated by law or by
27 the Board of Public Works.
28 (B) (1) SUBJECT TO PARAGRAPHS (2) AND (3) OF THIS SUBSECTION, AND
29 IN ADDITION TO THE POWERS CONFERRED UNDER SUBSECTION (A) OF THIS
30 SECTION, A POLICE OFFICER OF THE MARYLAND CAPITOL POLICE WITHIN THE
31 DEPARTMENT MAY INVESTIGATE AND OTHERWISE ENFORCE STATEWIDE THE
32 CRIMINAL LAWS, TITLE 12 OF THE CRIMINAL PROCEDURE ARTICLE, AND PARKING
33 AND MOTOR VEHICLE LAWS.
HOUSE BILL 338 3
1 (2) IF A POLICE OFFICER OF THE MARYLAND CAPITOL POLICE
2 WITHIN THE DEPARTMENT EXERCISES THE POWERS GRANTED UNDER PARAGRAPH
3 (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION, NOTIFICATION OF THE INVESTIGATION AND
4 ENFORCEMENT SHALL BE PROVIDED AS SOON AS REASONABLY PRACTICABLE AND
5 AS APPLICABLE TO:
6 (I) TO THE SECRETARY OF STATE POLICE OR THE DESIGNEE
7 OF THE SECRETARY, REASONABLY IN ADVANCE OF THE INVESTIGATION OR
8 ENFORCEMENT, UNLESS AN EMERGENCY OR EXIGENCY EXISTS; AND
9 (II) TO THE FOLLOWING, AS APPLICABLE, AFTER THE
10 INVESTIGATION OR ENFORCEMENT:
11 (I) 1. THE CHIEF OF POLICE OF A LAW ENFORCEMENT
12 AGENCY LISTED IN § 1–101(C)(1)(II) OF THE PUBLIC SAFETY ARTICLE OR THE
13 DESIGNEE OF THE CHIEF OF POLICE;
14 (II) 2. IN A COUNTY IN WHICH THE DEPARTMENT OF STATE
15 POLICE PROVIDES GENERAL LAW ENFORCEMENT:
16 1. A. THE COMMANDER OF THE LOCAL BARRACKS FOR THE
17 DEPARTMENT OF STATE POLICE OR THE DESIGNEE OF THE COMMANDER; AND
18 2. B. THE SECRETARY OF STATE POLICE OR THE
19 DESIGNEE OF THE SECRETARY;
20 (III) 3. IN BALTIMORE CITY:
21 1. A. THE POLICE COMMISSIONER OR THE DESIGNEE OF
22 THE POLICE COMMISSIONER; AND
23 2. B. THE SHERIFF OR THE DESIGNEE OF THE SHERIFF;
24 AND
25 (IV) 1. 4. A. THE SECRETARY OF STATE POLICE OR THE
26 DESIGNEE OF THE SECRETARY;
27 2. B. THE CHIEF OF POLICE OF THE MARYLAND TRANSIT
28 ADMINISTRATION POLICE FORCE OR THE DESIGNEE OF THE CHIEF;
29 3. C. THE CHIEF OF POLICE OF THE MARYLAND
30 DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH SECURED TRANSPORT AND INVESTIGATION UNIT OR
31 THE DESIGNEE OF THE CHIEF; AND
4 HOUSE BILL 338
1 4. D. THE SUPERINTENDENT OF THE MARYLAND
2 DEPARTMENT OF NATURAL RESOURCES POLICE OR THE DESIGNEE OF THE
3 SUPERINTENDENT.
4 (3) NOTIFICATION REQUIRED UNDER PARAGRAPH (2) OF THIS
5 SUBSECTION SHALL BE MADE IN ACCORDANCE WITH ADOPTED STATE
6 REGULATIONS, POLICIES, PROCEDURES, AND MEMORANDA OF UNDERSTANDING.
7 [(b)] (C) Consistent with Article 8 of the Maryland Declaration of Rights, with
8 respect to State legislative buildings in the City of Annapolis, the presiding officers of the
9 General Assembly shall have final authority over the use of and access to the buildings by
10 the members of the General Assembly, their staff, and Department of Legislative Services
11 personnel.
12 4–605.
13 (a) (1) In accordance with the provisions of the State Personnel and Pensions
14 Article, the Secretary may establish a police and security force, known as the Maryland
15 Capitol Police of the Department of General Services, WITH STATEWIDE JURISDICTION
16 to protect PUBLIC OFFICIALS, people, and property [on or about improvements, grounds,
17 and multiservice centers under the jurisdiction of the Department, and in the surrounding
18 areas of the buildings and grounds in the State as described] in ACCORDANCE WITH §
19 4–601 of this subtitle.
20 (2) (i) The Secretary shall appoint the Chief of the Maryland Capitol
21 Police of the Department of General Services.
22 (ii) The Chief of the Maryland Capitol Police of the Department of
23 General Services serves at the pleasure of the Secretary.
24 (iii) The Chief of the Maryland Capitol Police of the Department of
25 General Services shall supervise and direct the affairs and operations of the Maryland
26 Capitol Police.
27 (3) The Maryland Capitol Police of the Department of General Services
28 may include sworn police officers.
29 (b) A member of the Maryland Capitol Police of the Department of General
30 Services may not be involuntarily transferred from a site to another site.
31 (c) Subject to subsection (d) of this section, a Maryland Capitol Police officer has
32 all the powers granted to a peace officer and a police officer of the State.
HOUSE BILL 338 5
1 (d) A Maryland Capitol Police officer may exercise the powers described in
2 subsection (c) of this section within the Department’s jurisdiction as specified in § 4–601 of
3 this subtitle provided the police officer:
4 (1) meets the legal requirements set forth by the Maryland Police Training
5 and Standards Commission; and
6 (2) is designated by the Department as a police officer.
7 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That before a police officer of the
8 Maryland Capitol Police within the Department of General Services exercises the powers
9 granted under this Act in Baltimore City, the Maryland Capitol Police must enter a
10 memorandum of understanding with the Baltimore Police Department.
11 SECTION 2. 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
12 July 1, 2026.
Approved:
________________________________________________________________________________
Governor.
________________________________________________________________________________
Speaker of the House of Delegates.

President of the Senate.
