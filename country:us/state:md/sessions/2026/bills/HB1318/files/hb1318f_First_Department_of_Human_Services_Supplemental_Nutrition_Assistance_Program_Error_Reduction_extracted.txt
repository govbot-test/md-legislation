A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Department of Human Services – Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program –
3 Error Reduction
4 FOR the purpose of requiring the Department of Human Services to develop a certain
5 process to address Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program administrative
6 errors; and generally relating to the administration of the Supplemental Nutrition
7 Assistance Program.
8 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
9 Article – Human Services
10 Section 5–501
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2019 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
13 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
14 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
15 Article – Human Services
16 5–501.
17 (a) (1) The Department may implement a Supplemental Nutrition Assistance
18 Program in accordance with the federal Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program.
19 (2) The Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program shall include:
20 (i) a Restaurant Meals Program in accordance with § 5–505 of this
21 subtitle; and
22 (ii) a Heat and Eat Program in accordance with § 5–506 of this

2 HOUSE BILL 1318
1 subtitle.
2 (b) The State shall bear the nonfederal portion of the administrative costs of the
3 Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program for each county.
4 (c) Each local department shall administer the Supplemental Nutrition
5 Assistance Program:
6 (1) under the supervision and control of the Department; and
7 (2) in accordance with the regulations of the Department and federal law.
8 (d) If a household includes an individual who is at least 60 years old and receives
9 a federally funded benefit in an amount less than $50 per month under the Supplemental
10 Nutrition Assistance Program, the State shall provide a supplement to increase the total
11 benefit to $50 per month.
12 (E) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL:
13 (1) DEVELOP A COMPREHENSIVE PROCESS TO:
14 (I) SYSTEMATICALLY ANALYZE SUPPLEMENTAL NUTRITION
15 ASSISTANCE PROGRAM ADMINISTRATIVE ERRORS;
16 (II) FORMULATE STRATEGIES, INCLUDING IMPROVEMENTS IN
17 THE ELIGIBILITY DETERMINATION PROCESS, TO REDUCE ADMINISTRATIVE ERRORS;
18 AND
19 (III) MONITOR IMPLEMENTATION OF THE STRATEGIES
20 DEVELOPED UNDER THIS SUBSECTION; AND
21 (2) WORK TOWARD ACHIEVING ERROR RATES EQUAL TO THE LEVEL
22 ACHIEVED BY THE BEST PERFORMING STATE IN THE UNITED STATES, AS
23 DETERMINED BY THE UNITED STATES DEPARTMENT OF AGRICULTURE.
24 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
25 October 1, 2026.
