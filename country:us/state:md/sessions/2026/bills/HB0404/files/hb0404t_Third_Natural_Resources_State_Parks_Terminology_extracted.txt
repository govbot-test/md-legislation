4 5–2A–02(f) of this subtitle; and
5 (iii) Any collaboration between the Department and the Department
6 of Transportation and local transportation agencies to improve bicycle, sidewalk, and trail
7 access to State parks.
8 5–1012.
9 (a) In this section, “partnership park” means a unit of the State park system
10 managed by the Department in partnership with a local government, a bicounty agency, or
11 a nonprofit organization.
12 (b) The Department may enter into:
13 (1) A memorandum of understanding with a local government, bicounty
14 agency, or nonprofit organization to establish a partnership park in the State; or
15 (2) A partnership agreement with a local government, bicounty agency, or
16 nonprofit organization to manage a partnership park in the State.
17 (c) (1) The Department shall establish the Freedman’s State [Historical] Park
18 as a partnership park that seeks to educate the public about and preserve and interpret
19 the lives and experiences of Black Americans both before and after the abolition of slavery.
20 (2) Freedman’s State [Historical] Park shall be held by the State as a State
21 park under the protection and administration of the Department.
22 (d) Freedman’s State [Historical] Park shall consist of properties in northeastern
23 Montgomery County in or near the communities of Brookeville, Olney, Sandy Spring, Zion,
24 and Laytonsville that are:
25 (1) Owned by the State and designated by the Department as part of the
26 park; and
27 (2) (i) Owned by a local government, a bicounty agency, or a nonprofit
28 organization; and
29 (ii) Designated as part of the park by a partnership agreement
30 between the owner of the property and the State.
31 (e) (1) The Department shall establish a stakeholder advisory committee to
32 advise the Department on the scope and management of Freedman’s State [Historical]
33 Park.
