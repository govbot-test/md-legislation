CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 State Board of Examiners in Optometry – Examination Requirements and Time
3 Period for Inactive Status
4 FOR the purpose of clarifying that applicants for licensure as an optometrist and certain
5 applicants for reinstatement are required to pass all sections of each part of a
6 licensure examination; repealing the requirement that the State Board of Examiners
7 in Optometry give examinations to licensure applicants; repealing the requirement
8 that a licensure applicant pay to the Board a reexamination fee set by the Board for
9 each reexamination; prohibiting the Board from placing a licensee on inactive status
10 for more than a certain period of time; and generally relating to the State Board of
11 Examiners in Optometry.
12 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
13 Article – Health Occupations
14 Section 11–302(e), 11–304, 11–305, and 11–310
15 Annotated Code of Maryland
16 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
17 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
18 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
19 Article – Health Occupations
20 11–302.

15 (i) The anatomy, physiology, and pathology of the human eye; and

20 (i) Contained in any textbook; or
21 (ii) Taught at any accredited educational institution that teaches
22 optics, optometry, and other related subjects.

28 (a) Subject to the provisions of this section, the Board may waive the examination
29 requirements of this title for an individual who is licensed to practice optometry in any
30 other state.
HOUSE BILL 380 3
1 (b) The Board may grant a waiver under this section only if the applicant:
2 (1) Pays the application fee required by the Board under § 11–303 of this
3 subtitle;
4 (2) Provides adequate evidence that the applicant:
5 (i) Meets the qualifications otherwise required by this title,
6 including, for 3 years before applying, the continuing education requirements of the other
7 state in which the applicant has been practicing optometry, or, if the other state does not
8 have any continuing education requirement, the applicant shall meet the equivalent of 3
9 years of the continuing education requirements of this subtitle in courses approved by the
10 Board;
11 (ii) Meets the terms and conditions that the Board establishes in
12 subsections (c) and (d) of this section;
13 (iii) At the time the applicant was licensed in the other state, was
14 qualified to take the examination that then was required by the laws of this State; and
15 (iv) Qualified for a license in the other state by passing an
16 examination given by the board of optometry in that state;
17 (3) Presents a copy of the license, certified by the issuing board; and
18 (4) Has not failed an examination [given] REQUIRED by the Board within
19 the previous 5 years.
20 (c) (1) The Board may waive the examination requirements of this subtitle for
21 an applicant who:
22 (i) If so required by the Board under subsection (d) of this section,
23 passes an examination [administered] REQUIRED by the Board; and
24 (ii) For 3 years immediately before applying, has practiced
25 optometry actively.
26 (2) The Board may waive the requirement of practicing optometry actively
27 for an applicant who for 3 years:
28 (i) Was teaching optometry;
29 (ii) Was a uniformed services optometrist;
30 (iii) Was a supervisor or administrative optometrist; or
31 (iv) Was a researcher in optometry.
4 HOUSE BILL 380
1 (d) (1) Within 1 year of receipt of an application, the Board may require that
2 the applicant pass an examination.
3 (2) The Board shall determine the scope, form, and passing score for
4 examinations under this section.
5 11–310.
6 (a) (1) The Board shall place a licensee on inactive status, if the licensee
7 submits to the Board:
8 (i) An application for inactive status on the form required by the
9 Board; and
10 (ii) The inactive status fee set by the Board.
11 (2) THE BOARD MAY NOT PLACE A LICENSEE ON INACTIVE STATUS
12 FOR MORE THAN 6 CONSECUTIVE YEARS.
13 [(2)] (3) The Board shall issue a license to an individual who is on inactive
14 status if the individual:
15 (i) Meets any continuing education requirements set by the Board;
16 and
17 (ii) Pays to the Board the reinstatement fee set by the Board.
18 (b) The Board may reinstate the license of an optometrist whose license has been
19 expired for less than 5 years and who has not been put on inactive status, if the optometrist:
20 (1) Has met the continuing education requirements set by the Board;
21 (2) Meets the renewal requirements of § 11–308 of this subtitle; and
22 (3) Pays to the Board the reinstatement fee set by the Board.
23 (c) The Board may not reinstate the license of an optometrist whose license has
24 been expired for 5 years or more and who has not been put on inactive status, unless the
25 optometrist:
26 (1) Passes ALL SECTIONS OF EACH PART OF an examination
27 [administered] AS REQUIRED by the Board; and
28 (2) Otherwise meets the requirements of subsection (b) of this section.
