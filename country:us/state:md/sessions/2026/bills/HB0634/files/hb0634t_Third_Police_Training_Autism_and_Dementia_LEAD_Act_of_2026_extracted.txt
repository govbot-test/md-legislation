1 AN ACT concerning
2 Police Training – Autism and Dementia
3 (LEAD Act of 2026)
4 FOR the purpose of requiring the Maryland Police Training and Standards Commission to
5 require entrance–level and in–service police training to include certain training
6 regarding individuals with intellectual and developmental disabilities, dementia,
7 and autism; and generally relating to police training.
8 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,

10 Section 3–207(a)(16)
11 Annotated Code of Maryland

14 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

2 HOUSE BILL 634
1 (a) The Commission has the following powers and duties:
2 (16) to require, for entrance–level police training and, as determined by the
3 Commission, for in–service level training conducted by the State and each county and
4 municipal police training school, that the curriculum and minimum courses of study
5 include, consistent with established law enforcement standards and federal and State
6 constitutional provisions:
7 (i) training in lifesaving techniques, including Cardiopulmonary
8 Resuscitation (CPR);
9 (ii) training in the proper level and use of force as set forth in the
10 Maryland Use of Force Statute under § 3–524 of this title;
11 (iii) training regarding sensitivity to cultural and gender diversity;
12 [and]
13 (iv) training regarding individuals with physical, intellectual,
14 developmental, and psychiatric disabilities; AND
15 (V) TRAINING REGARDING INDIVIDUALS WITH INTELLECTUAL
16 AND DEVELOPMENTAL DISABILITIES, DEMENTIA, AND AUTISM, INCLUDING
17 TRAINING FOR:
18 1. LOCATING AN INDIVIDUAL WHO IS WANDERING,
19 ELOPING, OR OTHERWISE MISSING;
20 2. SEARCHING NEAR BODIES OF WATER;
21 3. SENSORY–AWARE APPROACHES;
22 4. REUNIFICATION;
23 5. DOCUMENTATION; AND
24 6. INTERAGENCY COORDINATION;
25 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
26 October 1, 2026.
