HOUSE BILL 1569

By: Delegates Young and Woorman
Introduced and read first time: February 13, 2026
Assigned to: Environment and Transportation
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Parking Enforcement – Vehicles in Custody or Control of Auto Repair or
3 Storage Facility
4 FOR the purpose of establishing that if a parking citation is issued for a motor vehicle while
5 the motor vehicle is in the custody or control of an auto repair or storage facility, the
6 auto repair or storage facility, and not the registered owner, is liable for the parking
7 citation and any associated fines or late fees; prohibiting an auto repair or storage
8 facility from requiring a customer to waive the customer’s rights established under
9 this Act as a condition of service; and generally relating to parking citations issued
10 to motor vehicles in the custody or control of an auto repair or storage facility.
11 BY adding to
12 Article – Transportation
13 Section 26–302.1
14 Annotated Code of Maryland
15 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
16 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
17 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
18 Article – Transportation
19 26–302.1.
20 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
21 INDICATED.
22 (2) “AUTO REPAIR OR STORAGE FACILITY” MEANS A PERSON
23 ENGAGED IN THE REPAIR, SERVICING, STORAGE, OR MAINTENANCE OF MOTOR
24 VEHICLES FOR COMPENSATION.

1 (3) “CUSTODY OR CONTROL” MEANS THE POSSESSION OF A MOTOR
2 VEHICLE UNDER A REPAIR ORDER, SERVICE AGREEMENT, OR OTHER
3 AUTHORIZATION FROM THE REGISTERED OWNER.
4 (B) (1) IF A PARKING CITATION IS ISSUED FOR A MOTOR VEHICLE WHILE
5 THE MOTOR VEHICLE IS IN THE CUSTODY OR CONTROL OF AN AUTO REPAIR OR
6 STORAGE FACILITY:
7 (I) THE REGISTERED OWNER OF THE MOTOR VEHICLE MAY NOT
8 BE HELD LIABLE FOR THE PARKING VIOLATION AND ANY ASSOCIATED FINE OR LATE
9 FEE ARISING FROM THE PARKING CITATION; AND
10 (II) THE AUTO REPAIR OR STORAGE FACILITY MAY BE HELD
11 LIABLE FOR THE PARKING VIOLATION.
12 (2) IT IS AN AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE TO LIABILITY FOR A PARKING
13 CITATION IF THE REGISTERED OWNER OF THE MOTOR VEHICLE FOR WHICH THE
14 CITATION WAS ISSUED DEMONSTRATES THAT, AT THE TIME OF THE VIOLATION, THE
15 MOTOR VEHICLE WAS IN THE CUSTODY OR CONTROL OF AN AUTO REPAIR OR
16 STORAGE FACILITY.
17 (3) IF A REGISTERED OWNER IS FOUND TO BE NOT LIABLE FOR A
18 PARKING CITATION BY ASSERTING THE AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE ESTABLISHED
19 UNDER PARAGRAPH (2) OF THIS SUBSECTION, A COURT SHALL IMMEDIATELY WAIVE
20 ALL FINES AND FEES AND DISMISS THE CITATION AGAINST THE REGISTERED
21 OWNER, REGARDLESS OF WHETHER LIABILITY IS TRANSFERABLE TO THE AUTO
22 REPAIR OR STORAGE FACILITY.
23 (C) IF SUFFICIENT EVIDENCE IS AVAILABLE TO INDICATE THAT A MOTOR
24 VEHICLE WAS IN THE CUSTODY OR CONTROL OF AN AUTO REPAIR OR STORAGE
25 FACILITY AT THE TIME THE PARKING ENFORCEMENT AGENCY ISSUED THE
26 CITATION, THE PARKING ENFORCEMENT AGENCY MAY REISSUE THE CITATION FOR
27 THE PARKING VIOLATION DIRECTLY TO THE AUTO REPAIR OR STORAGE FACILITY.
28 (D) AN AUTO REPAIR OR STORAGE FACILITY MAY NOT REQUIRE A
29 CUSTOMER TO WAIVE THE CUSTOMER’S RIGHTS UNDER THIS SECTION AS A
30 CONDITION OF SERVICE.
31 (E) THIS SECTION DOES NOT LIMIT THE AUTHORITY OF A PARKING
32 ENFORCEMENT AGENCY TO ENFORCE PARKING LAWS UNDER ITS JURISDICTION.
33 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
34 October 1, 2026.
