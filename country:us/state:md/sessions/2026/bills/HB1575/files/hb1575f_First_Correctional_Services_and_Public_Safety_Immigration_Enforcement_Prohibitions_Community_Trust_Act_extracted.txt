A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Correctional Services and Public Safety – Immigration Enforcement –
3 Prohibitions
4 (Community Trust Act)
5 FOR the purpose of prohibiting employees and agents of State and local correctional
6 facilities from taking certain actions related to immigration enforcement, subject to
7 certain exceptions; prohibiting law enforcement agents from taking certain actions
8 related to immigration enforcement, subject to certain exceptions; and generally
9 relating to immigration enforcement.
10 BY adding to
11 Article – Correctional Services
12 Section 8–805
13 Annotated Code of Maryland
14 (2025 Replacement Volume)
15 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
16 Article – Criminal Procedure
17 Section 5–104
18 Annotated Code of Maryland
19 (2025 Replacement Volume)
20 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
21 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
22 Article – Correctional Services

1 8–805.
2 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
3 INDICATED.
4 (2) “CIVIL IMMIGRATION VIOLATION” MEANS A VIOLATION OF
5 FEDERAL CIVIL IMMIGRATION LAW.
6 (3) “COURT ORDER” DOES NOT INCLUDE A DOCUMENT ISSUED BY:
7 (I) THE UNITED STATES DEPARTMENT OF HOMELAND
8 SECURITY;
9 (II) THE UNITED STATES DEPARTMENT OF JUSTICE; OR
10 (III) ANY SUCCESSOR AGENCY.
11 (4) “JUDICIAL WARRANT” DOES NOT INCLUDE A DOCUMENT ISSUED
12 BY:
13 (I) THE UNITED STATES DEPARTMENT OF HOMELAND
14 SECURITY;
15 (II) THE UNITED STATES DEPARTMENT OF JUSTICE; OR
16 (III) ANY SUCCESSOR AGENCY.
17 (B) (1) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN PARAGRAPHS (2) AND (3) OF THIS
18 SUBSECTION, AN EMPLOYEE OR AGENT OF A STATE OR LOCAL CORRECTIONAL
19 FACILITY MAY NOT, IN THE COURSE OF THE EMPLOYEE’S OR AGENT’S DUTIES:
20 (I) INQUIRE ABOUT OR INVESTIGATE AN INDIVIDUAL’S
21 CITIZENSHIP, IMMIGRATION STATUS, OR PLACE OF BIRTH;
22 (II) DETAIN OR PROLONG THE DETENTION OF AN INDIVIDUAL:
23 1. FOR THE PURPOSE OF INVESTIGATING THE
24 INDIVIDUAL’S CITIZENSHIP OR IMMIGRATION STATUS;
25 2. BASED ON THE SUSPICION THAT THE INDIVIDUAL HAS
26 COMMITTED A CIVIL IMMIGRATION VIOLATION; OR
HOUSE BILL 1575 3
1 3. AT THE REQUEST OF FEDERAL IMMIGRATION
2 AUTHORITIES UNLESS PRESENTED WITH A VALID JUDICIAL WARRANT;
3 (III) NOTIFY FEDERAL IMMIGRATION AUTHORITIES THAT AN
4 INDIVIDUAL IS IN CUSTODY UNLESS REQUIRED BY A VALID COURT ORDER OR
5 JUDICIAL WARRANT;
6 (IV) TRANSFER AN INDIVIDUAL TO FEDERAL IMMIGRATION
7 AUTHORITIES UNLESS PRESENTED WITH A VALID JUDICIAL WARRANT; OR
8 (V) COERCE, INTIMIDATE, OR THREATEN AN INDIVIDUAL BASED
9 ON THE ACTUAL OR PERCEIVED CITIZENSHIP OR IMMIGRATION STATUS OF THE
10 INDIVIDUAL OR ANY OTHER PERSON.
11 (2) NOTHING IN THIS SUBSECTION SHALL PREVENT AN EMPLOYEE OR
12 AGENT OF A STATE OR LOCAL CORRECTIONAL FACILITY FROM INQUIRING ABOUT
13 INFORMATION THAT IS MATERIAL TO A ROUTINE BOOKING PROCEDURE.
14 (3) IF THE CITIZENSHIP OR IMMIGRATION STATUS OF AN INDIVIDUAL
15 IS RELEVANT TO A PROTECTION ACCORDED TO THE INDIVIDUAL UNDER STATE OR
16 FEDERAL LAW, OR SUBJECT TO A REQUIREMENT IMPOSED BY INTERNATIONAL
17 TREATY, AN EMPLOYEE OR AGENT OF A STATE OR LOCAL CORRECTIONAL FACILITY
18 MAY:
19 (I) NOTIFY THE INDIVIDUAL OF THE PROTECTION OR
20 REQUIREMENT; OR
21 (II) PROVIDE THE INDIVIDUAL WITH AN OPPORTUNITY TO
22 VOLUNTARILY DISCLOSE THE INDIVIDUAL’S CITIZENSHIP OR IMMIGRATION STATUS
23 FOR THE PURPOSE OF RECEIVING THE PROTECTION OR COMPLYING WITH THE
24 REQUIREMENT.
25 (C) UNLESS REQUIRED BY STATE OR FEDERAL LAW OR A COURT ORDER, AN
26 EMPLOYEE OR AGENT OF A STATE OR LOCAL CORRECTIONAL FACILITY MAY NOT:
27 (1) IN THE COURSE OF THE EMPLOYEE’S OR AGENT’S DUTIES,
28 PROVIDE INFORMATION TO FEDERAL IMMIGRATION AUTHORITIES; OR
29 (2) ALLOW FEDERAL IMMIGRATION AUTHORITIES TO ACCESS:
30 (I) AN AREA OF A STATE OR LOCAL CORRECTIONAL FACILITY
31 NOT ACCESSIBLE TO THE PUBLIC; OR
4 HOUSE BILL 1575
1 (II) RECORDS RELATING TO A STATE OR LOCAL CORRECTIONAL
2 FACILITY THAT ARE NOT ACCESSIBLE TO THE PUBLIC.
3 (D) (1) EACH STATE AND LOCAL CORRECTIONAL FACILITY SHALL ADOPT
4 A POLICY CONSISTENT WITH THE REQUIREMENTS OF THIS SECTION.
5 (2) THE POLICY REQUIRED BY THIS SUBSECTION SHALL INCLUDE
6 PENALTIES FOR AN EMPLOYEE OR AGENT WHO VIOLATES THE REQUIREMENTS OF
7 THIS SECTION.
8 (E) AN INDIVIDUAL WHO HAS BEEN SUBJECTED TO A VIOLATION OF THIS
9 SECTION MAY BRING AN ACTION FOR:
10 (1) ACTUAL DAMAGES;
11 (2) PUNITIVE DAMAGES; AND
12 (3) INJUNCTIVE RELIEF.

14 5–104.
15 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
16 (2) “Civil immigration violation” means a violation of federal civil
17 immigration law.
18 (3) “COURT ORDER” DOES NOT INCLUDE A DOCUMENT ISSUED BY:
19 (I) THE UNITED STATES DEPARTMENT OF HOMELAND
20 SECURITY;
21 (II) THE UNITED STATES DEPARTMENT OF JUSTICE; OR
22 (III) ANY SUCCESSOR AGENCY.
23 [(3)] (4) “Family member” means a relative by blood, adoption, or
24 marriage.
25 [(4)] (5) “Household member” means a person who lives with another or
26 is a regular presence in the home of another.

1 [(5)] (6) (i) “Law enforcement agent” means an individual who is
2 certified by the Maryland Police Training and Standards Commission under § 3–209 of the
3 Public Safety Article.
4 (ii) “Law enforcement agent” does not include an agent or employee
5 of a State correctional facility or a local correctional facility.
6 [(6)] (7) “Local correctional facility” has the meaning stated in § 1–101 of
7 the Correctional Services Article.
8 [(7)] (8) “State correctional facility” has the meaning stated in § 1–101 of
9 the Correctional Services Article.
10 (b) (1) In this subsection, “arrest” does not include a routine booking
11 procedure.
12 (2) Except as provided in paragraphs (3) and (4) of this subsection, a law
13 enforcement agent may not, during the performance of regular police functions:
14 (i) inquire about an individual’s citizenship, immigration status, or
15 place of birth during a stop, a search, or an arrest;
16 (ii) detain, or prolong the detention of, an individual:
17 1. for the purpose of investigating the individual’s
18 citizenship or immigration status; or
19 2. based on the suspicion that the individual has committed
20 a civil immigration violation;
21 (iii) transfer an individual to federal immigration authorities unless
22 [required by federal law; or] PRESENTED WITH A VALID JUDICIAL WARRANT;
23 (iv) coerce, intimidate, or threaten any individual based on the actual
24 or perceived citizenship or immigration status of the individual or:
25 1. the individual’s family member;
26 2. the individual’s household member;
27 3. the individual’s legal guardian; or
28 4. another individual for whom the individual is a legal
29 guardian; OR
6 HOUSE BILL 1575
1 (V) NOTIFY FEDERAL IMMIGRATION AUTHORITIES THAT AN
2 INDIVIDUAL IS IN CUSTODY UNLESS REQUIRED BY A VALID COURT ORDER.
3 (3) Nothing in this subsection shall prevent a law enforcement agent from
4 inquiring about any information that is material to a criminal investigation.
5 (4) If the citizenship or immigration status of an individual is relevant to a
6 protection accorded to the individual under State or federal law, or subject to a requirement
7 imposed by international treaty, a law enforcement agent may:
8 (i) notify the individual of the protection or requirement; and
9 (ii) provide the individual an opportunity to voluntarily disclose the
10 individual’s citizenship or immigration status for the purpose of receiving the protection or
11 complying with the requirement.
12 (C) (1) EACH LAW ENFORCEMENT AGENCY IN THE STATE SHALL ADOPT A
13 POLICY CONSISTENT WITH THE REQUIREMENTS OF THIS SECTION.
14 (2) THE POLICY REQUIRED BY THIS SUBSECTION SHALL INCLUDE
15 PENALTIES FOR AN EMPLOYEE OR AGENT WHO VIOLATES THE REQUIREMENTS OF
16 THIS SECTION.
17 (D) AN INDIVIDUAL WHO HAS BEEN SUBJECTED TO A VIOLATION OF THIS
18 SECTION MAY BRING AN ACTION FOR:
19 (1) ACTUAL DAMAGES;
20 (2) PUNITIVE DAMAGES; AND
21 (3) INJUNCTIVE RELIEF.
22 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
23 October 1, 2026.
