A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Law Enforcement – Use of Facial Recognition Technology – Images Captured by
3 Camera Affixed to Dwelling Exterior
4 FOR the purpose of authorizing the use of facial recognition technology by law enforcement
5 to investigate the commission or attempted commission of certain crimes if the image
6 used by facial recognition technology was captured by a certain camera affixed to the
7 exterior of a dwelling; and generally relating to the use of facial recognition
8 technology by law enforcement.

10 Article – Criminal Procedure
11 Section 2–501

1 (b) (1) “Facial recognition technology” means a computer program, a service,
2 or any other technology that analyzes facial features and is used by or at the direction of a
3 law enforcement agency for the identification, verification, or persistent tracking of
4 individuals in still or video images for use in criminal investigations.
5 (2) “Facial recognition technology” does not include technology:
6 (i) used only for the analysis of facial features to grant or deny
7 access to an electronic device; or
8 (ii) that uses an automated or semiautomated process only for the
9 purpose of redacting a recording or an image for release or disclosure outside a law
10 enforcement agency to protect the privacy of a subject depicted in the recording or image if
11 the process does not generate or result in the retention of any biometric data or surveillance
12 information.
13 (c) “Law enforcement agency” has the meaning stated in § 2–101 of the Public
14 Safety Article.

20 than the commission of or the attempt to commit:

22 Law Article;

25 [(2)] (3) (i) Except as provided in subparagraph (ii) of this paragraph,
26 an image being evaluated using facial recognition technology may be compared only to
27 images contained in:
28 1. the database of images of driver’s license and
29 identification card photos maintained by the Motor Vehicle Administration or the
30 department of motor vehicles of another state; or

1 2. a database of mugshot photos maintained by a local, State,
2 or federal law enforcement agency or a law enforcement agency of another country.
3 (ii) An image being evaluated using facial recognition technology
4 may be compared to an image contained in a database other than a database described in
5 subparagraph (i) of this paragraph if:
6 1. the law enforcement agency conducting the investigation
7 has entered into an agreement with the entity that maintains the database governing the
8 methods by which images in the database are collected; and
9 2. the agreement provides that the agreement will be
10 terminated in the event that the entity maintaining the database commits a material
11 breach of the provisions governing the methods by which images in the database are
12 collected.
13 [(3)] (4) Before being used for any purpose in relation to a criminal
14 investigation, a result generated by facial recognition technology shall be independently
15 verified by an individual who has completed training in accordance with § 2–505 of this
16 subtitle.
17 (b) A police officer or other employee or agent of a law enforcement agency may
18 not use facial recognition technology to identify an individual solely based on:
19 (1) the police officer’s, employee’s, or agent’s personal interest not related
20 to legitimate duties or objectives of the law enforcement agency;
21 (2) the individual’s political or social beliefs or activities;
22 (3) the individual’s participation in lawful activities; or
23 (4) the individual’s race, color, religious beliefs, sexual orientation, gender,
24 disability, national origin, or status as being homeless.
25 (c) (1) Except as provided in paragraph (2) of this subsection and subject to
26 paragraph (3) of this subsection, a result generated by facial recognition technology in
27 violation of this section and all other evidence subsequently derived from the result may
28 not be introduced by the State for any purpose in a criminal court proceeding or in a juvenile
29 court proceeding under Title 3, Subtitle 8A of the Courts Article.
30 (2) Evidence derived from a result generated in violation of subsection
31 (a)(1) OR (2) of this section may be introduced for a purpose described in § 2–502 of this
32 subtitle if the court finds that the evidence would otherwise be subject to a legally valid
33 exception to the exclusionary rule.
