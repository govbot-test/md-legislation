1 AN ACT concerning
2 Prince George’s County – Alcoholic Beverages – Class B–SEC (Small Event
3 Center) License
4 PG 312–26
5 FOR the purpose of establishing a Class B–SEC (small event center) beer, wine, and liquor
6 license in Prince George’s County; authorizing the Board of License Commissioners
7 for Prince George’s County to issue the license for use in certain small event centers
8 in the county; limiting the number of Class B–SEC licenses that the Board may issue
9 per license year and per State legislative district; and generally relating to alcoholic
10 beverages licenses in Prince George’s County.
11 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
12 Article – Alcoholic Beverages and Cannabis
13 Section 26–102
14 Annotated Code of Maryland
15 (2024 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
16 BY adding to
17 Article – Alcoholic Beverages and Cannabis
18 Section 26–1013.1
19 Annotated Code of Maryland
20 (2024 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

2 HOUSE BILL 443
1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – Alcoholic Beverages and Cannabis
4 26–102.
5 This title applies only in Prince George’s County.
6 26–1013.1.
7 (A) IN THIS SECTION, “SMALL EVENT CENTER” MEANS A VENUE THAT
8 OPERATES ON A PER–EVENT BASIS OR ON A LIMITED SCHEDULE AND DOES NOT
9 MAINTAIN STANDARD DAILY HOURS OF OPERATION.
10 (B) THERE IS A CLASS B–SEC (SMALL EVENT CENTER) BEER, WINE, AND
11 LIQUOR LICENSE.
12 (C) THE BOARD MAY ISSUE THE LICENSE FOR USE IN A SMALL EVENT
13 CENTER THAT HAS KITCHEN AND DINING FACILITIES TO ACCOMMODATE BETWEEN
14 75 AND 125 110 INDIVIDUALS, INCLUSIVE.
15 (D) THE LICENSE AUTHORIZES THE LICENSE HOLDER TO SELL BEER, WINE,
16 AND LIQUOR FOR ON–PREMISES CONSUMPTION TO INDIVIDUALS ATTENDING AN
17 EVENT AT THE SMALL EVENT CENTER DESCRIBED IN THE LICENSE.
18 (E) THE BOARD MAY NOT ISSUE MORE THAN:
19 (1) 24 LICENSES IN A LICENSE YEAR; OR
20 (2) 3 LICENSES TO APPLY IN EACH STATE LEGISLATIVE DISTRICT IN
21 THE COUNTY AT ANY ONE TIME.
22 (F) THE SMALL EVENT CENTER SHALL HAVE:
23 (1) FOR A SMALL EVENT CENTER APPROVED FOR OR UNDER
24 CONSTRUCTION, A MINIMUM CAPITAL INVESTMENT, NOT INCLUDING THE COST OF
25 LAND, BUILDINGS, OR LEASING, OF $500,000 $350,000 FOR SMALL EVENT CENTER
26 DINING FACILITIES AND KITCHEN EQUIPMENT; AND
27 (2) FOR A SMALL EVENT CENTER THAT IS OPEN TO THE PUBLIC FOR
28 EVENTS, A MINIMUM APPRAISED VALUE, NOT INCLUDING THE COST OF LAND,
29 BUILDINGS, OR LEASING, OF $350,000 FOR SMALL EVENT CENTER DINING
30 FACILITIES AND KITCHEN EQUIPMENT; AND
HOUSE BILL 443 3
1 (3) FOOD AVAILABLE DURING THE HOURS OF OPERATION FOR AN
2 EVENT.
3 (G) THE LICENSE HOLDER MAY SELL BEER, WINE, AND LIQUOR DURING THE
4 HOURS AND DAYS AS SET OUT FOR A CLASS B BEER, WINE, AND LIQUOR LICENSE
5 UNDER § 26–2004 OF THIS TITLE, SUBJECT TO COUNTY USE AND OCCUPANCY LAWS
6 FOR HOURS OF OPERATION.
7 (H) THE ANNUAL LICENSE FEE IS $3,500.
8 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
9 1, 2026.
Approved:
