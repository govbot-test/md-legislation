1 WHEREAS, The report identifies significant disparities in affordability in
2 majority–minority and lower–income zip codes and acknowledges that geographic and
3 nondriving rating factors may contribute to those disparities; and
4 WHEREAS, Despite identifying territorial rating and other nondriving rating
5 factors as key areas of concern, the workgroup did not reach consensus and did not provide
6 specific legislative recommendations to modify or reform the use of territorial rating in
7 Maryland; and
8 WHEREAS, On numerous major policy questions affecting affordability, including
9 territorial rating, low–cost insurance options, and relief mechanisms for high–cost
10 communities, the workgroup recommended only further study and deferred the
11 development of concrete legislative proposals; and
12 WHEREAS, The General Assembly finds that additional work is necessary to
13 produce data–driven, actionable recommendations that will reduce premiums for Maryland
14 motorists, reduce unjustified geographic and racial disparities, and provide the legislature
15 with specific policy options to reform rating practices in the private passenger automobile
16 insurance market; now, therefore,

21 (a) (1) The Maryland Insurance Administration shall establish a workgroup
22 to study:
23 (i) the affordability of private passenger automobile insurance in
24 the State, including factors that contribute to premium rate increases and trends in rate
25 increases;
26 (ii) policy options to provide greater transparency regarding
27 automobile insurance premiums and to increase the affordability of automobile insurance
28 in the State;
29 (iii) ways in which the term “affordability” has been or may be
30 defined in the context of the establishment of private passenger automobile insurance
31 premium rates;
32 (iv) methodologies by which affordability can be considered in
33 establishing private passenger automobile insurance rates consistent with rate making
34 principles codified in the Insurance Article; and
