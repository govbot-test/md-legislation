2 SENATE BILL 754
1 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
2 Article – Land Use
3 Section 7–101
4 Annotated Code of Maryland
5 (2012 Volume and 2025 Supplement)
6 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
7 That Section(s) 1–101(k) through (t) of Article – Land Use of the Annotated Code of
8 Maryland be renumbered to be Section(s) 1–101(l) through (u), respectively.
9 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That the Laws of Maryland read
10 as follows:
11 Article – Land Use
12 1–101.
13 (a) In this division the following words have the meanings indicated.
14 (K) “NATURAL RESOURCE” INCLUDES LAND, WATER, FORESTS, WETLANDS,
15 FLOODPLAINS, HABITAT AREAS, SENSITIVE AREAS, AND OTHER AREAS THAT
16 PROVIDE ECOLOGICAL, HYDROLOGICAL, OR CONSERVATION VALUE.
17 7–101.
18 To encourage the preservation of natural resources or the provision of affordable
19 housing and to facilitate orderly development and growth, a local jurisdiction that exercises
20 authority granted by this division may enact, and is encouraged to enact, local laws
21 providing for or requiring:
22 (1) the planning, staging, or provision of adequate public facilities and
23 affordable housing;
24 (2) off–site improvements or the dedication of land for public facilities
25 essential for a development;
26 (3) THE PRESERVATION OF NATURAL RESOURCES THROUGH:
27 (I) THE TRANSFER OF DEVELOPMENT RIGHTS;
28 (II) CONSERVATION OR PRESERVATION EASEMENTS;
29 (III) OPEN SPACE OR AGRICULTURAL LAND PRESERVATION
30 PROGRAMS;
31 (IV) CLUSTER OR CONSERVATION SUBDIVISION DESIGN;
SENATE BILL 754 3
1 (V) RIPARIAN BUFFER, FLOODPLAIN, OR WETLAND
2 PROTECTION REQUIREMENTS; AND
3 (VI) OTHER LAND USE OR ZONING MECHANISMS DESIGNED TO
4 PROVIDE LONG–TERM OR PERMANENT PROTECTION OF NATURAL RESOURCES;
5 [(3)] (4) moderately priced dwelling unit programs;
6 [(4)] (5) mixed use developments;
7 [(5)] (6) cluster developments;
8 [(6)] (7) planned unit developments;
9 [(7)] (8) alternative subdivision requirements that:
10 (i) meet minimum performance standards set by the local
11 jurisdiction; and
12 (ii) reduce infrastructure costs;
13 [(8)] (9) floating zones;
14 [(9)] (10) incentive zoning; and
15 [(10)] (11) performance zoning.
16 7–103.
17 The authority granted under this subtitle is not intended to limit a local jurisdiction’s
18 authority to:
19 (1) exercise any planning and zoning powers not expressly authorized
20 under this subtitle; or
21 (2) adopt other methods to:
22 (i) facilitate orderly development and growth;
23 (ii) encourage the preservation of natural resources; or
24 (iii) provide affordable housing.
25 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
26 October 1, 2026.
