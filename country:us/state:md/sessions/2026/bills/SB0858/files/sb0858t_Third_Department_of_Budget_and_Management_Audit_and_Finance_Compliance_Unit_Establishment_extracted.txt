25 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
26 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
27 Article – State Finance and Procurement
28 3–101.
29 (a) In this title the following words have the meanings indicated.
30 (b) “Department” means the Department of Budget and Management.
31 (c) “Secretary” means the Secretary of Budget and Management.
32 SUBTITLE 4. AUDIT AND FINANCE COMPLIANCE UNIT.
33 3–401.
SENATE BILL 858 3
1 (A) IN THIS SUBTITLE, THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
2 INDICATED.
3 (B) “AGENCY” MEANS AN ENTITY OF THE EXECUTIVE BRANCH OF STATE
4 GOVERNMENT.
5 (C) “UNIT” MEANS THE AUDIT AND FINANCE COMPLIANCE UNIT.
6 3–402.
7 THERE IS AN AUDIT AND FINANCE COMPLIANCE UNIT IN THE DEPARTMENT.
8 3–403.
9 (A) IN ACCORDANCE WITH § 2–1224(H)(1) OF THE STATE GOVERNMENT
10 ARTICLE, THE GOVERNOR SHALL IMPLEMENT SYSTEMS AND PROCESSES TO
11 MONITOR THE EFFORTS OF THE EXECUTIVE DEPARTMENTAL UNITS TO CORRECT
12 AUDIT FINDINGS REPORTED BY THE OFFICE OF LEGISLATIVE AUDITS.
13 (B) THE UNIT IS RESPONSIBLE FOR MONITORING THE EFFORTS OF
14 AGENCIES EXECUTIVE DEPARTMENTAL UNITS IN CORRECTING AUDIT FINDINGS
15 REPORTED BY THE OFFICE OF LEGISLATIVE AUDITS IN ACCORDANCE WITH TITLE
16 2, SUBTITLE 12, PART IV OF THE STATE GOVERNMENT ARTICLE.
17 3–404.
18 (A) IN CARRYING OUT ITS RESPONSIBILITIES, THE UNIT SHALL ASSIST
19 AGENCIES EXECUTIVE DEPARTMENTAL UNITS WITH THE RESOLUTION OF AUDIT
20 FINDINGS BY:
21 (1) PROVIDING OBJECTIVE ASSESSMENT OF CORRECTIVE ACTIONS;
22 AND
23 (2) MONITORING PROGRESS IN THE IMPLEMENTATION OF
24 CORRECTIVE ACTIONS.
25 (B) THE UNIT MAY IMPLEMENT PROACTIVE STEPS TO ADDRESS AND
26 PREVENT AUDIT FINDINGS THROUGH ADVICE AND SUPPORT PROVIDED TO
27 AGENCIES PROVIDE GUIDANCE, COORDINATION, AND TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE TO
28 EXECUTIVE DEPARTMENTAL UNITS TO STRENGTHEN EFFORTS TO ADDRESS AND
29 PREVENT AUDIT FINDINGS AND TO STRENGTHEN INTERNAL CONTROLS AND
30 COMPLIANCE PRACTICES.
4 SENATE BILL 858
1 3–405.
2 (A) THE UNIT SHALL PROVIDE DIRECT ASSISTANCE TO AGENCIES
3 EXECUTIVE DEPARTMENTAL UNITS WITH:
4 (1) FOUR OR MORE REPEAT AUDIT FINDINGS; OR
5 (2) AS REQUESTED BY THE JOINT AUDIT AND EVALUATION
6 COMMITTEE UNSATISFACTORY AUDITS.
7 (B) THE DIRECT ASSISTANCE PROVIDED UNDER SUBSECTION (A) OF THIS
8 SECTION SHALL INCLUDE:
9 (1) ADVICE AND SUPPORT TO DEVELOP CORRECTIVE ACTIONS AS
10 SOON AS PRACTICABLE, A REVIEW OF THE TIMELINE DEVELOPED BY THE
11 EXECUTIVE DEPARTMENTAL UNIT FOR THE IMPLEMENTATION OF CORRECTIVE
12 ACTIONS FOR EACH RECOMMENDATION TO ENSURE THAT THE TIMELINE HAS
13 ACHIEVABLE COMPLETION DATES;
14 (2) A REVIEW OF RESOLUTION ACTIONS ON EACH FINDING WITH A
15 FOCUS ON REPEAT AND SIGNIFICANT FINDINGS TO PROVIDE ADVICE AND SUPPORT
16 IN ASSISTING WITH THE DEVELOPMENT OF CORRECTIVE ACTIONS;
17 (3) PERFORMING SAMPLE TESTS OF IMPLEMENTED CORRECTIVE
18 ACTIONS, AS NEEDED; AND
19 (4) MONITORING PROGRESS IN OF THE IMPLEMENTATION OF
20 CORRECTION ACTIONS UNTIL THE LEGISLATIVE AUDITORS RETURN TO BEGIN THE
21 NEXT RELATED FISCAL COMPLIANCE AUDIT.
22 (C) (1) THE UNIT SHALL ESTABLISH A SPECIALIZED TEAM TO PROVIDE
23 THE DIRECT ASSISTANCE REQUIRED UNDER THIS SECTION.
24 (2) THE SPECIALIZED TEAM ESTABLISHED IN PARAGRAPH (1) OF
25 THIS SUBSECTION MAY INCLUDE CURRENT STATE EMPLOYEES, CURRENT
26 CONTRACTUAL EMPLOYEES, AND EXTERNAL CONTRACTORS.
27 3–406.
28 (A) THE UNIT SHALL MAINTAIN A DASHBOARD ON CURRENT AND REPEAT
29 AUDIT FINDINGS ON ITS WEBSITE.
SENATE BILL 858 5
1 (B) (1) THE DASHBOARD REQUIRED UNDER SUBSECTION (A) OF THIS
2 SECTION SHALL INCLUDE ALL AUDIT FINDINGS FOR AGENCIES UNDER
3 EXAMINATION EACH YEAR, ORGANIZED BY UNITS OF THE AGENCY.
4 (2) FOR EACH FINDING, THE DASHBOARD SHALL INCLUDE:
5 (A) ON OR BEFORE OCTOBER 1 EACH YEAR, THE UNIT SHALL SUBMIT AN
6 ANNUAL REPORT TO THE JOINT AUDIT AND EVALUATION COMMITTEE, IN
7 ACCORDANCE WITH § 2–1257 OF THE STATE GOVERNMENT ARTICLE, ON ALL AUDIT
8 FINDINGS FOR EXECUTIVE DEPARTMENTAL UNITS UNDER EXAMINATION.
9 (B) THE REPORT SHALL INCLUDE THE FOLLOWING INFORMATION FOR
10 EACH FINDING:
11 (I) (1) THE RECOMMENDATIONS OF THE OFFICE OF
12 LEGISLATIVE AUDITS;
13 (II) (2) THE NUMBER OF YEARS THAT A REPEAT FINDING
14 REMAINS UNRESOLVED;
15 (III) (3) THE PROJECTED COMPLETION DATE OF THE
16 RESOLUTION;
17 (IV) (4) INFORMATION ON UNRESOLVED FINDINGS,
18 INCLUDING THE REASON A FINDING IS UNRESOLVED; AND
19 (V) (5) A GRAPHIC REPRESENTATION OF THE PROGRESS OF
20 CORRECTIVE ACTIONS THAT INCLUDES WHETHER THE FINDING IS UNRESOLVED, IN
21 PROGRESS, OR RESOLVED.
22 (C) THE DASHBOARD SHALL BE FULLY OPERATIONAL ON OR BEFORE
23 OCTOBER 1, 2027.
24 3–407.
25 THE SECRETARY MAY ADOPT REGULATIONS TO CARRY OUT THIS SUBTITLE.
26 Article – State Government
27 2–1224.
28 (h) (1) The Governor and the Chief Justice of the Supreme Court of Maryland
29 shall implement systems and processes to monitor the efforts of the Executive
6 SENATE BILL 858
1 Departmental Units and the Judiciary, respectively, to correct audit findings reported by
2 the Office of Legislative Audits.
3 (i) (1) A report auditing a unit of State or local government shall have any
4 cybersecurity findings redacted in a manner consistent with auditing best practices before
5 the report is made available to the public.
6 (2) AN UNREDACTED CYBERSECURITY FINDING IN A REPORT
7 AUDITING A UNIT OF STATE OR LOCAL GOVERNMENT MAY BE VIEWED BY:
8 (I) MEMBERS OF THE JOINT AUDIT AND EVALUATION
9 COMMITTEE;
10 (II) MEMBERS OF THE JOINT COMMITTEE ON CYBERSECURITY,
11 INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY, AND BIOTECHNOLOGY;
12 (III) THE CHAIR AND THE SUBCOMMITTEE CHAIRS OF THE
13 SENATE BUDGET AND TAXATION COMMITTEE;
14 (IV) THE CHAIR AND THE SUBCOMMITTEE CHAIRS OF THE
15 HOUSE APPROPRIATIONS COMMITTEE;
16 (V) THE GOVERNOR, THE GOVERNOR’S CHIEF OF STAFF, AND
17 THE GOVERNOR’S DEPUTY CHIEFS OF STAFF; AND
18 (VI) THE AUDIT AND FINANCE COMPLIANCE UNIT IN THE
19 DEPARTMENT OF BUDGET AND MANAGEMENT.
20 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That, on or before October 1, 2026,
21 and October 1, 2027, the Audit and Finance Compliance Unit shall report to the Joint Audit
22 and Evaluation Committee, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government Article,
23 on the status of the dashboard required under § 3–406 of the State Finance and
24 Procurement Article, as enacted by Section 1 of this Act.
25 SECTION 3. 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
26 July 1, 2026.
