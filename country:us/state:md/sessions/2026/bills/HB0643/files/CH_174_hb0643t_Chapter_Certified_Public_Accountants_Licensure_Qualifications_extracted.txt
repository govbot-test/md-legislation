WES MOORE, Governor Ch. 174
Chapter 174
AN ACT concerning
Certified Public Accountants – Licensure – Qualifications
FOR the purpose of altering certain educational and experiential qualifications for a license
to practice certified public accountancy; and generally relating to licensure
qualifications for certified public accountants.
BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
Article – Business Occupations and Professions

Section 2:
Section 2–302 and 2–303
Annotated Code of Maryland
(2018 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

Section 3:
SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
Article – Business Occupations and Professions
2–302.
(a) To qualify for a license, an applicant shall be an individual who meets the
requirements of this section.
(b) The applicant shall be of good character and reputation.
(c) The applicant shall be at least 18 years old.
(d) The applicant shall meet the educational requirements under § 2–303 of this
subtitle.
(e) Except as otherwise provided in this subtitle, the applicant shall pass an
examination given by the Board under this subtitle.
(f) The applicant for an initial license shall complete practical work experience
that is approved by the Board and that:
(1) is obtained over a period of:
(i) not more than [3] 6 years; and

Board considers to constitute:
(1) a major in accounting; or
(2) a substantial equivalent to a major in accounting, of which required
credits or courses may be taken at any 2– or 4–year regionally accredited institution of
higher education.
(c) The degree required under this section shall be awarded by a school, college,

(1) is a member of the Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of

Programs;
(3) is accredited by or is a constituent unit of an institution accredited by:
(i) the Middle States Association of Colleges and Schools; or
(ii) the equivalent regional accrediting association for other regional
areas; or
(4) is recognized and approved by the Board.

the Board may accept as final the determination of that institution.
