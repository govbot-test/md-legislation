CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Residential Child Care Programs – Education of Children and Training of Child
3 and Youth Care Practitioners
4 FOR the purpose of repealing requirements that operators of certain residential child care
5 programs enroll a child for whom they have legal custody or care and control in a
6 certain school and initiate and monitor the transfer of certain records of the child;
7 requiring operators of certain residential child care programs to provide certain
8 educational opportunities to the children for whom they have legal custody or care
9 and control; requiring that applicants to be child and youth care practitioners
10 undergo certain training; requiring an individual who has a certain certification on
11 a certain date to complete certain training before the individual’s certificate is
12 renewed; and generally relating to residential child care programs.

8 Article – Education
9 7–309.
10 (a) (1) In this section, “residential child care program” means a program that:
11 (i) Provides care for children 24 hours a day within a structured set
12 of services and activities designed to achieve objectives related to the needs of the children
13 served; and
14 (ii) Is licensed by the Maryland Department of Health, the
15 Department of Human Services, or the Department of Juvenile Services.
16 (2) A “residential child care program” includes:
17 (i) Group homes;
18 (ii) Alternative living units; and
19 (iii) Emergency shelter care.
20 (b) Each licensed operator of a residential child care program who has legal
21 custody or care and control of a child who is at least 5 years old and under the age of 16
22 years and receives State funding as provided in § 5–526 of the Family Law Article:
23 (1) Shall enroll the child in the local school system where the residential
24 child care program is located unless the residential child care program operates an
25 approved educational program in accordance with the licensing regulations that govern the
26 residential child care program;
27 (2) Shall expeditiously initiate and monitor the transfer of the academic
28 records of a child in the operator’s care from the transferring school to [insure] ENSURE
29 that the academic records are transferred to the school that the child will be attending
30 while living in the operator’s care;
31 (3) (1) (i) May request a meeting with the child’s teachers; [and]

13 (1) A high school diploma or equivalent and have successfully completed

15 (2) (i) At least 2 years’ experience in the human service field; or
16 (ii) An associate’s or bachelor’s degree from an accredited college or
17 university.
