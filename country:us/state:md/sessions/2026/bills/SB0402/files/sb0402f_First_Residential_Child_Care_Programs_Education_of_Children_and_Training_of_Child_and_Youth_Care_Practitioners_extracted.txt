A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Residential Child Care Programs – Education of Children and Training of Child
3 and Youth Care Practitioners
4 FOR the purpose of requiring operators of certain residential child care programs to provide
5 certain educational opportunities to the children for whom they have legal custody
6 or care and control; requiring that applicants to be child and youth care practitioners
7 undergo certain training; and generally relating to residential child care programs.
8 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
9 Article – Education
10 Section 7–309
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2025 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
13 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
14 Article – Health Occupations
15 Section 20–302.1
16 Annotated Code of Maryland
17 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
18 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
19 Article – Human Services
20 Section 8–704(8), (10), and (11)
21 Annotated Code of Maryland
22 (2019 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
23 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
24 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
25 Article – Education

1 7–309.
2 (a) (1) In this section, “residential child care program” means a program that:
3 (i) Provides care for children 24 hours a day within a structured set
4 of services and activities designed to achieve objectives related to the needs of the children
5 served; and
6 (ii) Is licensed by the Maryland Department of Health, the
7 Department of Human Services, or the Department of Juvenile Services.
8 (2) A “residential child care program” includes:
9 (i) Group homes;
10 (ii) Alternative living units; and
11 (iii) Emergency shelter care.
12 (b) Each licensed operator of a residential child care program who has legal
13 custody or care and control of a child who is at least 5 years old and under the age of 16
14 years and receives State funding as provided in § 5–526 of the Family Law Article:
15 (1) Shall enroll the child in the local school system where the residential
16 child care program is located unless the residential child care program operates an
17 approved educational program in accordance with the licensing regulations that govern the
18 residential child care program;
19 (2) Shall expeditiously initiate and monitor the transfer of the academic
20 records of a child in the operator’s care from the transferring school to [insure] ENSURE
21 that the academic records are transferred to the school that the child will be attending
22 while living in the operator’s care;
23 (3) (i) May request a meeting with the child’s teachers; [and]
24 (ii) Shall meet the child’s teachers at the time of enrollment and at
25 any other time the school or a teacher requests TO DETERMINE THE BEST WAYS TO
26 SUPPORT THE CHILD; and
27 (III) SHALL CONTACT THE CHILD’S TEACHER WITH ANY
28 QUESTIONS ABOUT SCHOOL ASSIGNMENTS, THE CHILD’S BEHAVIOR AT SCHOOL, AND
29 EXTRACURRICULAR OPPORTUNITIES TO SUPPORT THE CHILD;
30 (4) Shall sign the child’s report card, [insure] ENSURE that the report card
31 is returned to school, and include a copy of the report card in the child’s case record; AND
SENATE BILL 402 3
1 (5) SHALL ENSURE THAT THE CHILD IS PROVIDED:
2 (I) REGULARLY SCHEDULED TIME TO STUDY TO SUPPORT THE
3 CHILD’S ACADEMIC WORK;
4 (II) ASSISTANCE WITH HOMEWORK ASSIGNMENTS, PROJECTS,
5 AND TEST PREPARATION IN ORDER TO REINFORCE EDUCATIONAL CONCEPTS
6 TAUGHT TO THE CHILD;
7 (III) ENCOURAGEMENT AND SUPPORT FOR PARTICIPATION IN
8 EXTRACURRICULAR ACTIVITIES;
9 (IV) FOR A CHILD WITH AN INDIVIDUALIZED EDUCATION
10 PROGRAM UNDER THE FEDERAL INDIVIDUALS WITH DISABILITIES EDUCATION ACT
11 OR A 504 PLAN UNDER § 504 OF THE FEDERAL REHABILITATION ACT OF 1973,
12 APPLICABLE EDUCATIONAL GOALS TO COMPLETE WITHIN THE RESIDENTIAL CHILD
13 CARE PROGRAM THAT ARE CONSISTENT WITH THE CHILD’S PROGRAM OR PLAN;
14 (V) FOR A CHILD WITH A BEHAVIORAL INTERVENTION PLAN
15 UNDER SUBTITLE 11 OF THIS TITLE, BEHAVIORAL SUPPORTS AND GOALS THAT
16 REINFORCE THE GOALS OF THE CHILD’S PLAN IN THE SCHOOL SETTING; AND
17 (VI) FOR CHILDREN DUALLY ENROLLED IN A HIGH SCHOOL AND
18 AN INSTITUTION OF HIGHER EDUCATION, ACCESS TO A COMPUTER IN ORDER TO
19 COMPLETE ASSIGNMENTS THAT REQUIRE COMPUTER SOFTWARE OR THE INTERNET.
20 Article – Health Occupations
21 20–302.1.
22 (a) To qualify for a certificate as a residential child and youth care practitioner,
23 an applicant shall be an individual who meets the requirements of this section.
24 (b) The applicant shall be of good moral character.
25 (c) The applicant shall have completed:
26 (1) A State and national criminal history records check; and
27 (2) Unless waived by the Board under § 20–303(d) of this subtitle, a child
28 protective services background clearance.
29 (d) The applicant shall be:
30 (1) At least 21 years old; or
4 SENATE BILL 402
1 (2) At least 18 years old and have earned at least an associate’s or
2 bachelor’s degree from an accredited college or university.
3 (e) The applicant shall have:
4 (1) A high school diploma or equivalent and have successfully completed
5 an approved training program; and
6 (2) (i) At least 2 years’ experience in the human service field; or
7 (ii) An associate’s or bachelor’s degree from an accredited college or
8 university.
9 (f) (1) (I) Except as provided in paragraph (2) of this subsection, the
10 applicant shall have successfully completed a training program approved by the Board.
11 (II) THE BOARD’S PROGRAM SHALL INCLUDE TRAINING ON:
12 1. THE ROLE OF THE RESIDENTIAL CHILD AND YOUTH
13 CARE PRACTITIONER;
14 2. CHILD DEVELOPMENT;
15 3. CHILDREN WITH SPECIAL NEEDS;
16 4. RECOGNIZING DIFFERENCES IN PROCESSING
17 INFORMATION AND THE DIVERSE WAYS CHILDREN LEARN;
18 5. RECOGNIZING BEHAVIORS THAT IMPACT A CHILD’S
19 ABILITY TO LEARN AND PRACTICE SOCIAL NORMS;
20 6. CHILD ABUSE AND NEGLECT IDENTIFICATION AND
21 REPORTING;
22 7. SUICIDE RISK ASSESSMENT AND PREVENTION;
23 8. APPROVED FORMS OF DISCIPLINE AND BEHAVIORAL
24 MANAGEMENT TECHNIQUES, INCLUDING THE USE OF BEHAVIORAL INTERVENTION
25 PLANS UNDER TITLE 7, SUBTITLE 11 OF THE EDUCATION ARTICLE;
26 9. COMMUNICATION SKILLS;
27 10. PARENTING ISSUES, COLLABORATION WITH
28 FAMILIES, AND THE SUPPORT OF CHILDREN;
SENATE BILL 402 5
1 11. THE PSYCHOSOCIAL AND EMOTIONAL NEEDS OF
2 CHILDREN, FAMILIAL RELATIONSHIPS, AND THE IMPACT OF SEPARATION;
3 12. EMERGENCY PREPAREDNESS AND SAFETY
4 PRACTICES;
5 13. ANNUAL FIRST AID TRAINING, INCLUDING
6 CERTIFICATION IN CARDIOPULMONARY RESUSCITATION;
7 14. MEDICATION MANAGEMENT;
8 15. INFECTION CONTROL; AND
9 16. IF APPLICABLE, FOOD PREPARATION AND
10 NUTRITION.
11 (2) (i) An applicant who has an associate’s or bachelor’s degree from an
12 accredited college or university may be waived from the training program requirement, if
13 the applicant passes an examination and meets other requirements established by the
14 Board under this subtitle.
15 (ii) The Board shall establish requirements and procedures for
16 waiving the training program requirement for an applicant under subparagraph (i) of this
17 paragraph.
18 (g) The applicant shall pass an examination given by the Board under this
19 subtitle.
20 (h) The Board shall waive the education, experience, training, and examination
21 requirements of this section for an applicant who:
22 (1) Applies for certification on or before October 1, 2015; and
23 (2) Presents to the Board satisfactory evidence that the applicant worked
24 as a residential child and youth care practitioner in the State for at least 2 years before
25 October 1, 2015.
26 Article – Human Services
27 8–704.
28 A contract awarded or renewed between an agency and a provider for a residential
29 child care program shall:
30 (8) require the provider to comply with § 7–309 of the Education Article;

1 (10) require the residential child care program to have certified residential
2 child and youth care practitioners, as required under § 20–301 of the Health Occupations
3 Article; and
4 (11) require the residential child care program to have a certified program
5 administrator as required under § 20–301 of the Health Occupations Article.
6 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
7 1, 2026.
