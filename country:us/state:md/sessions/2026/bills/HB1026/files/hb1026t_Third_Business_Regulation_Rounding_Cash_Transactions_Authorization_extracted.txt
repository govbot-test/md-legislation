CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Business Regulation – Rounding Cash Transactions – Authorization
3 FOR the purpose of authorizing a merchant to round certain cash transactions in a certain
4 manner or round the amount of change due to a customer in a certain manner;
5 authorizing an employer to round a wage that the employer pays using cash in a
6 certain manner; providing that certain provisions of this Act shall preempt existing
7 regulations, rules, and ordinances that conflict with certain provisions of this Act;
8 and generally relating to rounding cash transactions.

3 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
4 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
5 Article – Business Regulation
6 SUBTITLE 6. ROUNDING CASH TRANSACTIONS IN A TRADE OR BUSINESS.
7 1–601.
8 (A) IN THIS SUBTITLE THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
9 INDICATED.
10 (B) (1) “CASH” MEANS UNITED STATES COINS AND CURRENCY.
11 (2) “CASH” DOES NOT INCLUDE:
12 (I) A PAPER INSTRUMENT OTHER THAN A FEDERAL RESERVE
13 NOTE; OR
14 (II) A GIFT CARD.
15 (C) “CUSTOMER” MEANS A PURCHASER OF A GOOD OR SERVICE FROM A
16 MERCHANT.
17 (D) “MERCHANT” MEANS A PERSON ENGAGED IN THE TRADE OR BUSINESS
18 OF THE SALE OF GOODS OR SERVICES.
19 (E) “TOTAL PRICE” MEANS THE PRICE A CUSTOMER IS RESPONSIBLE FOR
20 PAYING FOR A GOOD OR SERVICE AFTER A MERCHANT:
21 (1) SUBTRACTS ANY DISCOUNT OR DEDUCTION;
22 (2) APPLIES ANY APPLICABLE TAX OR FEE; AND
23 (3) ROUNDS THE TRANSACTION CONSISTENT WITH THIS SUBTITLE.
24 1–602.
25 THIS SUBTITLE DOES NOT APPLY TO A CASH TRANSACTION FOR A GOOD OR
26 SERVICE IN EXCHANGE FOR EXACT CHANGE.

HOUSE BILL 1026 3
1 1–603.
2 (A) THIS SECTION APPLIES TO:
3 (1) AN IN–PERSON, CASH TRANSACTION IF THE PRICE TOTALS AT
4 LEAST 5 CENTS; OR
5 (2) A TELEPHONE, MAIL, OR INTERNET TRANSACTION FOR WHICH A
6 CUSTOMER PAYS USING CASH IF THE PRICE TOTALS AT LEAST 5 CENTS.
7 (B) NOTWITHSTANDING ANY OTHER PROVISION OF LAW, A MERCHANT
8 DOING BUSINESS IN THE STATE MAY:
9 (1) DETERMINE THE TOTAL PRICE OF A GOOD OR SERVICE, AFTER
10 SUBTRACTING ANY DISCOUNT OR DEDUCTION FROM THE PRICE AND APPLYING ANY
11 APPLICABLE TAX OR FEE TO THE PRICE, IN THE FOLLOWING MANNER:
12 (I) IF THE PRICE ENDS IN 1 CENT, 2 CENTS, 6 CENTS, OR 7
13 CENTS, BY ROUNDING THE PRICE DOWN TO THE NEAREST CENT DIVISIBLE BY FIVE;
14 OR
15 (II) IF THE PRICE ENDS IN 3 CENTS, 4 CENTS, 8 CENTS, OR 9
16 CENTS, BY ROUNDING THE PRICE UP TO THE NEAREST CENT DIVISIBLE BY FIVE; OR
17 (2) ROUND THE AMOUNT OF CHANGE DUE TO A CUSTOMER UP OR
18 DOWN CONSISTENT WITH ITEM (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION.
19 1–604.
20 (A) THIS SECTION APPLIES TO:
21 (1) AN IN–PERSON, CASH TRANSACTION IF THE PRICE TOTALS LESS
22 THAN 5 CENTS; OR
23 (2) A TELEPHONE, MAIL, OR INTERNET TRANSACTION FOR WHICH A
24 PERSON PAYS USING CASH IF THE PRICE TOTALS LESS THAN 5 CENTS.
25 (B) NOTWITHSTANDING ANY OTHER PROVISION OF LAW, A MERCHANT
26 DOING BUSINESS IN THE STATE MAY DETERMINE THE TOTAL PRICE OF A GOOD OR
27 SERVICE, AFTER SUBTRACTING ANY DISCOUNT OR DEDUCTION FROM THE PRICE
28 AND APPLYING ANY APPLICABLE TAX OR FEE TO THE PRICE, BY ROUNDING THE
29 PRICE UP TO 5 CENTS.
