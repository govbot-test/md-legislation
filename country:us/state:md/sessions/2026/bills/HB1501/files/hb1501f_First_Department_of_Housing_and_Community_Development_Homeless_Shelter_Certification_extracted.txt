By: Delegate Fair
Introduced and read first time: February 13, 2026
Assigned to: Economic Matters
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Department of Housing and Community Development – Homeless Shelter
3 Certification
4 FOR the purpose of requiring the Department of Housing and Community Development to
5 operate a certification program for homeless shelters; prohibiting a certain homeless
6 shelter from operating without a homeless shelter certification after a certain
7 deadline; requiring the Department to develop certain requirements for certain
8 homeless shelter certification classes; requiring the Department to perform on–site
9 monitoring of certain homeless shelters and implement a certain grievance process;
10 and generally relating to the certification of homeless shelters.
11 BY adding to
12 Article – Housing and Community Development
13 Section 6–1401 through 6–1405 to be under the new subtitle “Subtitle 14. Homeless
14 Shelter Certification”
15 Annotated Code of Maryland
16 (2019 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
17 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
18 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
19 Article – Housing and Community Development
20 SUBTITLE 14. HOMELESS SHELTER CERTIFICATION.
21 6–1401.
22 (A) IN THIS SUBTITLE THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
23 INDICATED.
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

2 HOUSE BILL 1501
1 (B) “DISASTER OR HAZARD EMERGENCY SHELTER” MEANS A TEMPORARY
2 SHELTER THAT IS A PART OF A COUNTY’S EMERGENCY OPERATIONS PLAN AND THAT
3 OPENS AT A SPECIFIC TEMPERATURE DESIGNATION, DURING INCLEMENT WEATHER,
4 OR DUE TO A NATURAL DISASTER OR HAZARD.
5 (C) (1) “HOMELESS SHELTER” MEANS A FACILITY THAT IS OPERATED
6 FOR THE PRIMARY PURPOSE OF PROVIDING TEMPORARY OVERNIGHT SLEEPING
7 ACCOMMODATIONS, MEALS, BASIC NEEDS ASSISTANCE, AND SUPPORTIVE SERVICES
8 FOR CASE MANAGEMENT AND HOUSING NAVIGATION TO HOMELESS INDIVIDUALS.
9 (2) “HOMELESS SHELTER” INCLUDES:
10 (I) A YEAR–ROUND SHELTER;
11 (II) A SAFE HAVEN SHELTER;
12 (III) A TRANSITIONAL SHELTER;
13 (IV) A SEASONAL EMERGENCY SHELTER; AND
14 (V) A DISASTER OR HAZARD EMERGENCY SHELTER OR
15 EMERGENCY MASS CARE SHELTER.
16 (D) “MEDICAL OR SPECIALIZED CARE SHELTER” MEANS A YEAR–ROUND
17 SHELTER THAT PROVIDES A HIGHER LEVEL OF CARE TO MEET THE UNIQUE HEALTH
18 CARE, PERSONAL CARE, ACTIVITIES OF DAILY LIVING, OR MOBILITY NEEDS OF
19 SHELTER RESIDENTS.
20 (E) “MOTEL– OR HOTEL–BASED SHELTER” MEANS A MOTEL OR HOTEL USED
21 TO SUPPLEMENT TRADITIONAL SHELTER OPTIONS WHEN SHELTERS ARE FULL OR
22 WHEN A FAMILY HAS INDIVIDUAL NEEDS THAT MAKE A TRADITIONAL SHELTER AN
23 UNSAFE OR UNVIABLE OPTION.
24 (F) “SAFE HAVEN SHELTER” MEANS A SUPPORTIVE SHELTER FUNDED BY
25 THE U.S. DEPARTMENT OF HOUSING AND URBAN DEVELOPMENT THAT PROVIDES
26 A 24–HOUR RESIDENCE FOR HOMELESS INDIVIDUALS WITH SEVERE MENTAL
27 ILLNESS.
28 (G) “SEASONAL EMERGENCY SHELTER” MEANS A CONGREGATE OR
29 DORM–STYLE SHELTER WITH BEDS OR WHOLE FACILITIES THAT OPEN FOR AN
30 ENTIRE SEASON OR FOR A PREDETERMINED TIME PERIOD BUT ARE NOT PART OF
31 THE COUNTY’S EMERGENCY OPERATIONS PLAN.
HOUSE BILL 1501 3
1 (H) “TRANSITIONAL SHELTER” MEANS A SHELTER THAT TYPICALLY
2 PROVIDES ACCOMMODATIONS FOR UP TO 2 YEARS.
3 (I) “YEAR–ROUND SHELTER” MEANS A SHELTER THAT IS OPEN
4 YEAR–ROUND AND HAS RESIDENTS OCCUPYING THE SHELTER EVERY NIGHT,
5 INCLUDING A SAFE HAVEN SHELTER, TRANSITIONAL SHELTER, AND MEDICAL OR
6 SPECIALIZED CARE SHELTER.
7 6–1402.
8 (A) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL OPERATE A CERTIFICATION PROGRAM FOR
9 HOMELESS SHELTERS.
10 (B) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ESTABLISH A TIERED SYSTEM FOR HOMELESS
11 SHELTER CERTIFICATIONS BASED ON:
12 (1) THE NUMBER OF INDIVIDUALS SERVED BY THE HOMELESS
13 SHELTER;
14 (2) THE TYPE AND DURATION OF SHELTER PROVIDED; AND
15 (3) THE TYPE AND INTENSITY OF SERVICES PROVIDED.
16 (C) THE TIERED SYSTEM REQUIRED UNDER SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS
17 SECTION SHALL PRIORITIZE THE CERTIFICATION OF HOMELESS SHELTERS IN THE
18 FOLLOWING ORDER:
19 (1) YEAR–ROUND SHELTERS, INCLUDING SAFE HAVEN SHELTERS,
20 TRANSITIONAL SHELTERS, OR MEDICAL OR SPECIALIZED CARE SHELTERS;
21 (2) SEASONAL SHELTERS;
22 (3) MOTEL– OR HOTEL–BASED SHELTERS; AND
23 (4) OTHER SHELTERS.
24 6–1403.
25 (A) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ESTABLISH A STAGGERED APPROVAL
26 TIMELINE TO ENSURE THAT HOMELESS SHELTER CERTIFICATION APPLICATIONS
27 ARE PROCESSED EFFICIENTLY, QUALITY SITE VISITS AND INSPECTIONS ARE
28 CONDUCTED, TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE IS PROVIDED TO SHELTER OPERATORS, AND
29 CERTIFICATIONS ARE ISSUED IN A TIMELY MANNER.
4 HOUSE BILL 1501
1 (B) THE STAGGERED APPROVAL PROCESS SHALL INCLUDE:
2 (1) NOTIFYING OPERATORS OF EXISTING HOMELESS SHELTERS OF
3 EACH SHELTER’S CLASSIFICATION UNDER § 6–1402 OF THIS SUBTITLE;
4 (2) ASSIGNING HOMELESS SHELTERS INTO COHORTS; AND
5 (3) ASSIGNING APPLICATION AND CERTIFICATION DEADLINES.
6 (C) A HOMELESS SHELTER MAY NOT OPERATE WITHOUT A HOMELESS
7 SHELTER CERTIFICATION AFTER THE APPLICABLE CERTIFICATION DEADLINE HAS
8 PASSED.
9 6–1404.
10 THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ESTABLISH REQUIREMENTS FOR EACH HOMELESS
11 SHELTER CERTIFICATION CLASS THAT INCLUDE:
12 (1) REQUIREMENTS FOR ORGANIZATIONAL, INCORPORATION, AND
13 GOVERNANCE DOCUMENTS;
14 (2) REQUIREMENTS FOR FISCAL, ADMINISTRATIVE, AND LEGAL
15 DOCUMENTS;
16 (3) OVERSIGHT VISIT AND INSPECTION REQUIREMENTS;
17 (4) INTERIM UPDATE REPORT REQUIREMENTS;
18 (5) PROCEDURES FOR THE RENEWAL OF A HOMELESS SHELTER
19 CERTIFICATION;
20 (6) SUBMISSION METHODS FOR APPLICATIONS AND REPORTS;
21 (7) CIRCUMSTANCES FOR THE CONDITIONAL APPROVAL OF A
22 HOMELESS SHELTER CERTIFICATION;
23 (8) GROUNDS FOR THE DENIAL, SUSPENSION, OR REVOCATION OF A
24 HOMELESS SHELTER CERTIFICATION; AND
25 (9) PENALTIES FOR A HOMELESS SHELTER THAT OPERATES WITHOUT
26 A CERTIFICATION AFTER THE APPLICABLE CERTIFICATION DEADLINE HAS PASSED.
HOUSE BILL 1501 5
1 6–1405.
2 (A) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL PERFORM ON–SITE MONITORING TO
3 DETERMINE IF A HOMELESS SHELTER MEETS HOMELESS SHELTER CERTIFICATION
4 REQUIREMENTS ESTABLISHED UNDER § 6–1404 OF THIS SUBTITLE.
5 (B) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL DEVELOP AND IMPLEMENT A GRIEVANCE
6 PROCESS FOR HOMELESS INDIVIDUALS AND HOMELESS SHELTER RESIDENTS TO
7 NOTIFY THE DEPARTMENT OF ALLEGED VIOLATIONS OF THIS SUBTITLE.
8 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
9 1, 2026.
