CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Prince George’s Community College – Aerospace Technology Studies Program
3 and Aviation Systems Technology Programs – Feasibility Study
4 FOR the purpose of requiring the Board of Community College Trustees for Prince George’s
5 County, in consultation with the Maryland Higher Education Commission, the
6 Department of Commerce, and the Prince George’s County Economic Development
7 Corporation, to establish an Aerospace Technology Studies Program at Prince
8 George’s Community College, in consultation with certain entities, to complete a
9 certain feasibility study and program design assessment for aerospace and aviation
10 systems technology workforce training programs; and generally relating to Prince
11 George’s Community College.
12 BY adding to
13 Article – Education
14 Section 16–420
15 Annotated Code of Maryland
16 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
17 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
18 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
19 Article – Education

2 SENATE BILL 198
1 16–420.
2 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
3 INDICATED.
4 (2) “BOARD” MEANS THE BOARD OF COMMUNITY COLLEGE
5 TRUSTEES FOR PRINCE GEORGE’S COUNTY.
6 (3) “PROGRAM” MEANS THE AEROSPACE TECHNOLOGY STUDIES
7 PROGRAM.
8 (B) THE BOARD, IN CONSULTATION WITH THE COMMISSION, THE
9 DEPARTMENT OF COMMERCE, AND THE PRINCE GEORGE’S COUNTY ECONOMIC
10 DEVELOPMENT CORPORATION, SHALL ESTABLISH AN AEROSPACE TECHNOLOGY
11 STUDIES PROGRAM.
12 (C) (1) THE BOARD, IN CONSULTATION WITH INDUSTRY EXPERTS, SHALL
13 DEVELOP THE PROGRAM CURRICULUM TO INCLUDE:
14 (I) AEROSPACE SYSTEMS TECHNOLOGY;
15 (II) AVIONICS AND ELECTRONICS;
16 (III) ADVANCED MANUFACTURING; AND
17 (IV) ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE IN AEROSPACE.
18 (2) THE PROGRAM SHALL PROVIDE:
19 (I) WORKFORCE–BASED LEARNING, INCLUDING INTERNSHIPS
20 AND APPRENTICESHIPS; AND
21 (II) A PATHWAY FROM HIGH SCHOOL TO COLLEGE AND
22 CAREERS FOR INDIVIDUALS WITH AN INTEREST IN PURSUING A CAREER IN
23 AEROSPACE TECHNOLOGY.
24 (D) THE BOARD SHALL ENTER INTO MEMORANDA OF UNDERSTANDING TO
25 ESTABLISH FORMAL INDUSTRY PARTNERSHIPS WITH AT LEAST ONE AEROSPACE
26 AND TECHNOLOGY EMPLOYER IN PRINCE GEORGE’S COUNTY.
27 (E) THE ESTABLISHMENT OF THE PROGRAM AT PRINCE GEORGE’S
28 COMMUNITY COLLEGE IS SUBJECT TO PROGRAM APPROVAL BY THE COMMISSION
29 UNDER TITLE 11, SUBTITLE 2 OF THIS ARTICLE.
SENATE BILL 198 3
1 (F) (1) ON OR BEFORE DECEMBER 15, 2026, AND EACH DECEMBER 15
2 THEREAFTER UNTIL THE PROGRAM HAS BEEN ESTABLISHED, THE BOARD SHALL
3 SUBMIT A REPORT TO THE COMMISSION, THE PRINCE GEORGE’S COUNTY
4 EXECUTIVE, THE MARYLAND AEROSPACE TECHNOLOGY COMMISSION, AND, IN
5 ACCORDANCE WITH § 2–1257 OF THE STATE GOVERNMENT ARTICLE, THE GENERAL
6 ASSEMBLY ON THE STATUS OF ESTABLISHING THE PROGRAM.
7 (2) ON OR BEFORE OCTOBER 1 OF EACH YEAR AFTER THE
8 PROGRAM’S ESTABLISHMENT, THE BOARD SHALL SUBMIT A REPORT TO THE
9 COMMISSION, THE PRINCE GEORGE’S COUNTY EXECUTIVE, THE MARYLAND
10 AEROSPACE TECHNOLOGY COMMISSION, AND, IN ACCORDANCE WITH § 2–1257 OF
11 THE STATE GOVERNMENT ARTICLE, THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY ON INDUSTRY
12 PLACEMENT AND OUTCOMES OF THE PROGRAM IN THE IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING
13 ACADEMIC YEAR.
14 (a) Prince George’s Community College shall conduct a comprehensive feasibility
15 study and program design assessment to establish an aerospace systems technology
16 workforce training program and an aviation systems technology workforce training
17 program.
18 (b) The study shall include an examination of:
19 (1) the workforce demand for aerospace manufacturing, aerospace systems
20 technology, and aviation systems technology regionally and statewide;
21 (2) the current and projected needs of private sector employers engaged in
22 aerospace, aviation, engineering, defense, and advanced manufacturing in Prince George’s
23 County and the State;
24 (3) the appropriate associate degree, credit certificate, and noncredit
25 workforce credential structures that are aligned with high–value industry degrees,
26 certificates, and credentials;
27 (4) opportunities for stackable credentials that are eligible for federal or
28 State financial aid;
29 (5) the facilities, laboratories, simulators, equipment, and infrastructure
30 that are required to deliver the programs;
31 (6) faculty and instructor credential and certification requirements,
32 including recruitment and workforce development needs;
33 (7) apprenticeship, internship, and other work–based learning
34 opportunities with private sector employers;
4 SENATE BILL 198
1 (8) start–up costs, operating costs, and any long–term sustainability
2 considerations; and
3 (9) the recommended sequencing for program implementation.
4 (c) In conducting the study, Prince George’s Community College shall consult
5 with:
6 (1) private sector employers in aerospace, aviation, engineering, defense,
7 and advanced manufacturing;
8 (2) the Maryland Aerospace and Technology Commission under the
9 Department of Commerce;
10 (3) State and regional workforce development entities;
11 (4) the Maryland Department of Labor; and
12 (5) other economic development or industry stakeholders, as appropriate.
13 (d) The study shall include structured industry engagement, including employer
14 roundtables, workforce charrettes, and demand–validation sessions to ensure alignment
15 with current and projected labor markets.
16 (e) On or before December 1, 2027, Prince George’s Community College shall
17 report to the Senate Education, Energy, and the Environment Committee and the House
18 Ways and Means Committee, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government Article,
19 on:
20 (1) the findings of the workforce demand analysis;
21 (2) recommended models for degree, certificate, and noncredit workforce
22 credential programs;
23 (3) the investments that would be required for facilities, laboratories,
24 simulators, equipment, and infrastructure;
25 (4) faculty and staff plans;
26 (5) potential industry partnerships;
27 (6) a proposed implementation timeline; and
28 (7) a funding and sustainability plan.
29 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That, for fiscal year 2028, the
30 Governor shall include in the annual budget bill an appropriation of $100,000 to Prince
SENATE BILL 198 5
1 George’s Community College to complete the feasibility study and program design
2 assessment required under Section 1 of this Act, including for industry workforce analysis,
3 technical program design consulting, employer engagement, curriculum framework
4 development, and planning for facilities, laboratories, simulators, equipment, and
5 infrastructure investment.
6 SECTION 2. 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
7 July 1, 2026. It shall remain effective for a period of 2 years and, at the end of June 30,
8 2028, this Act, with no further action required by the General Assembly, shall be abrogated
9 and of no further force and effect.
Approved:
