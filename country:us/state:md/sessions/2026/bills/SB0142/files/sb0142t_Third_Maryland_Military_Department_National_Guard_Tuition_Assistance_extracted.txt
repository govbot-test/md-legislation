1 (b) (1) To the extent that funds are provided in the State budget, the
2 Department may provide assistance equal to 100% of the cost of in–State tuition AND
3 TRAINING ASSISTANCE for any regularly scheduled undergraduate credit course,
4 graduate credit course, professional credit course, vocational–technical course, or trade
5 course for any active member attending an institution who is certified as eligible by the
6 Adjutant General.
7 (2) Subject to paragraph (5) of this subsection, a member who receives
8 assistance under paragraph (1) of this subsection for an undergraduate credit,
9 vocational–technical, or trade OR CAREER course shall remain an active member for at
10 least 2 years following the completion of the course.
11 (3) Subject to paragraph (5) of this subsection, a member who receives
12 assistance under paragraph (1) of this subsection for a graduate or professional credit
13 course shall remain an active member for at least 4 years following the completion of the
14 course.
15 (4) Subject to paragraph (5) of this subsection, if a member receives
16 assistance under paragraph (1) of this subsection, and is a member of a unit that has been
17 disbanded on or after September 1, 2013, due to budgetary cuts, Base Realignment and
18 Closure, or any other reason, the member may satisfy the requirements of paragraph (2) or
19 paragraph (3) of this subsection by transferring to another active duty, reserve, or National
20 Guard Unit in the State or in another state.
21 (5) If a member who receives assistance under paragraph (1) of this
22 subsection is offered early separation by the military following the disbanding of the
23 member’s unit due to budget cuts, Base Realignment and Closure, or any other reason, the
24 member is excused from the requirements of paragraph (2) or paragraph (3) of this
25 subsection.
26 (c) (1) The Adjutant General may not certify a member as eligible unless the
27 member is:
28 (i) enlisted and has at least 24 months remaining to serve on the
29 current enlistment of the member; or
30 (ii) an officer or warrant officer and agrees in writing to serve for a
31 minimum of 24 months.
32 (2) The 24–month requirement runs from the first day of classes for the
33 semester.
34 (d) If a recipient of tuition assistance under this section is discharged from the
35 National Guard for a reason designated by the Adjutant General, the assistance terminates
36 and the member shall reimburse the Department the amount of tuition assistance received
37 for that semester within 30 days of discharge.
