A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Maryland Military Department – National Guard – Tuition Assistance
3 FOR the purpose of expanding the institutions and types of expenses in connection with
4 which tuition assistance may be provided for certain eligible Maryland National
5 Guard service members; and generally relating to tuition assistance for members of
6 the Maryland National Guard.
7 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
8 Article – Public Safety
9 Section 13–405
10 Annotated Code of Maryland
11 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
12 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
13 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
14 Article – Public Safety
15 13–405.
16 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
17 (2) “Institution” means:
18 (i) any campus of the University System of Maryland, any
19 community college established under Title 16 of the Education Article, Morgan State
20 University, or St. Mary’s College;
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

1 (ii) any private institution of higher education that grants a member
2 a tuition waiver of at least 50%;
3 (iii) any public postsecondary vocational–technical or trade school; or
4 (iv) any private postsecondary vocational–technical [or trade school
5 that grants a member a tuition waiver of at least 50%], TRADE, OR CAREER SCHOOL
6 RECOGNIZED BY THE MARYLAND HIGHER EDUCATION COMMISSION OR THE
7 MARYLAND APPRENTICESHIP AND TRAINING COUNCIL.
8 (3) “Member” means an individual who:
9 (i) is regularly enlisted in the National Guard; or
10 (ii) holds a commission in the National Guard.
11 (4) (I) “TRAINING ASSISTANCE” MEANS EXPENSES DIRECTLY
12 RELATED TO PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT RESOURCES REQUIRED FOR
13 PARTICIPATION IN A COURSE OR PROGRAM AT AN INSTITUTION.
14 (II) “TRAINING ASSISTANCE” INCLUDES:
15 1. INDUSTRY–REQUIRED TOOLS, TECHNOLOGY, AND
16 PROFESSIONAL EQUIPMENT;
17 2. PERSONAL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT, UNIFORMS,
18 AND SPECIALIZED CLOTHING;
19 3. CERTIFICATION AND LICENSING COSTS NECESSARY
20 FOR EMPLOYMENT; AND
21 4. TRADE–RELATED PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT
22 MATERIALS, SOFTWARE, AND DIGITAL RESOURCES.
23 (5) (i) “Tuition” means the basic instructional charge for
24 undergraduate, graduate, professional, vocational–technical, and trade school credit
25 courses and related fees at an institution.
26 (ii) “Tuition” does not include charges for self–supporting programs.
27 (b) (1) To the extent that funds are provided in the State budget, the
28 Department may provide assistance equal to 100% of the cost of in–State tuition AND
29 TRAINING ASSISTANCE for any regularly scheduled undergraduate credit course,
30 graduate credit course, professional credit course, vocational–technical course, or trade
SENATE BILL 142 3
1 course for any active member attending an institution who is certified as eligible by the
2 Adjutant General.
3 (2) Subject to paragraph (5) of this subsection, a member who receives
4 assistance under paragraph (1) of this subsection for an undergraduate credit,
5 vocational–technical, or trade OR CAREER course shall remain an active member for at
6 least 2 years following the completion of the course.
7 (3) Subject to paragraph (5) of this subsection, a member who receives
8 assistance under paragraph (1) of this subsection for a graduate or professional credit
9 course shall remain an active member for at least 4 years following the completion of the
10 course.
11 (4) Subject to paragraph (5) of this subsection, if a member receives
12 assistance under paragraph (1) of this subsection, and is a member of a unit that has been
13 disbanded on or after September 1, 2013, due to budgetary cuts, Base Realignment and
14 Closure, or any other reason, the member may satisfy the requirements of paragraph (2) or
15 paragraph (3) of this subsection by transferring to another active duty, reserve, or National
16 Guard Unit in the State or in another state.
17 (5) If a member who receives assistance under paragraph (1) of this
18 subsection is offered early separation by the military following the disbanding of the
19 member’s unit due to budget cuts, Base Realignment and Closure, or any other reason, the
20 member is excused from the requirements of paragraph (2) or paragraph (3) of this
21 subsection.
22 (c) (1) The Adjutant General may not certify a member as eligible unless the
23 member is:
24 (i) enlisted and has at least 24 months remaining to serve on the
25 current enlistment of the member; or
26 (ii) an officer or warrant officer and agrees in writing to serve for a
27 minimum of 24 months.
28 (2) The 24–month requirement runs from the first day of classes for the
29 semester.
30 (d) If a recipient of tuition assistance under this section is discharged from the
31 National Guard for a reason designated by the Adjutant General, the assistance terminates
32 and the member shall reimburse the Department the amount of tuition assistance received
33 for that semester within 30 days of discharge.
34 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
35 October 1, 2026.
