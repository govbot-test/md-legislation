A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Maryland Medical Assistance Program – State Pharmacy Benefits Manager
3 FOR the purpose of requiring, on or before a certain date, the Maryland Department of
4 Health to select and contract with a State pharmacy benefits manager; requiring
5 that each managed care contract entered into or renewed after a certain date require
6 the managed care organization to contract with and use the State pharmacy benefits
7 manager; requiring the Department to engage an independent consultant for a
8 certain purpose; and generally relating to a State pharmacy benefits manager for the
9 Maryland Medical Assistance Program.
10 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
11 Article – Health – General
12 Section 15–103(a)(1)
13 Annotated Code of Maryland
14 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
15 BY adding to
16 Article – Health – General
17 Section 15–103(g)
18 Annotated Code of Maryland
19 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
20 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
21 Article – Insurance
22 Section 15–1601(a), (p), and (q)
23 Annotated Code of Maryland
24 (2017 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
25 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
26 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

3 (a) (1) The Secretary shall administer the Maryland Medical Assistance

5 (G) (1) (I) IN THIS SUBSECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE
6 MEANINGS INDICATED.
7 (II) “PHARMACY BENEFITS MANAGER” HAS THE MEANING
8 STATED IN § 15–1601 OF THE INSURANCE ARTICLE.
9 (III) “SPREAD PRICING” MEANS THE MODEL OF PRESCRIPTION
10 DRUG PRICING IN WHICH THE PHARMACY BENEFITS MANAGER CHARGES THE
11 PROGRAM OR A MANAGED CARE ORGANIZATION A CONTRACTED PRICE FOR A
12 PRESCRIPTION DRUG THAT DIFFERS FROM THE AMOUNT THE PHARMACY BENEFITS
13 MANAGER DIRECTLY OR INDIRECTLY PAYS THE PHARMACIST OR PHARMACY FOR
14 THE PRESCRIPTION DRUG DISPENSED BY THE PHARMACIST OR PHARMACY.
15 (IV) “STATE PHARMACY BENEFITS MANAGER” MEANS THE
16 PHARMACY BENEFITS MANAGER CONTRACTED BY THE DEPARTMENT UNDER THIS
17 SECTION TO ADMINISTER PHARMACY BENEFITS FOR ALL PROGRAM RECIPIENTS IN
18 THE STATE, INCLUDING THOSE ENROLLED IN A MANAGED CARE ORGANIZATION.
19 (2) (I) ON OR BEFORE JULY 1, 2028, THE DEPARTMENT SHALL
20 SELECT AND CONTRACT WITH A SINGLE THIRD–PARTY ADMINISTRATOR TO SERVE
21 AS THE STATE PHARMACY BENEFITS MANAGER.
22 (II) EACH MANAGED CARE CONTRACT THAT IS ENTERED INTO
23 OR RENEWED BY THE DEPARTMENT ON OR AFTER JULY 1, 2028, SHALL REQUIRE
24 THE MANAGED CARE ORGANIZATION TO CONTRACT WITH AND USE THE STATE
25 PHARMACY BENEFITS MANAGER FOR THE PURPOSE OF ADMINISTERING ALL
26 PHARMACY BENEFITS FOR PROGRAM RECIPIENTS ENROLLED WITH THE MANAGED
27 CARE ORGANIZATION.
28 (3) THE CONTRACT ENTERED INTO UNDER PARAGRAPH (2)(I) OF THIS
29 SECTION SHALL:
30 (I) ESTABLISH THE STATE PHARMACY BENEFITS MANAGER’S
31 FIDUCIARY DUTY OWED TO THE DEPARTMENT;
32 (II) REQUIRE THE USE OF PASS–THROUGH PRICING;
HOUSE BILL 1384 3
1 (III) REQUIRE THE STATE PHARMACY BENEFITS MANAGER TO
2 USE THE COMMON FORMULARY, REIMBURSEMENT METHODOLOGIES, AND
3 DISPENSING FEES NEGOTIATED BY THE DEPARTMENT WITH INPUT FROM
4 STAKEHOLDERS;
5 (IV) REQUIRE TRANSPARENCY IN DRUG COSTS, REBATES
6 COLLECTED AND PAID, DISPENSING FEES PAID, ADMINISTRATIVE FEES, AND ALL
7 OTHER CHARGES, FEES, COSTS, AND HOLDBACKS; AND
8 (V) PROHIBIT SPREAD PRICING.
9 (4) (I) TO ADVISE THE DEPARTMENT IN SELECTING AND
10 CONTRACTING WITH THE SINGLE THIRD–PARTY ADMINISTRATOR UNDER
11 PARAGRAPH (2)(I) OF THIS SUBSECTION, THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ENGAGE AN
12 INDEPENDENT CONSULTANT WITH DIRECT EXPERIENCE:
13 1. ADVISING MEDICAID FRAUD CONTROL UNITS; AND
14 2. WORKING WITH STATES THAT HAVE ESTABLISHED A
15 SINGLE PHARMACY BENEFITS MANAGER FOR THEIR MEDICAID PROGRAM.
16 (II) THE INDEPENDENT CONSULTANT ENGAGED UNDER
17 SUBPARAGRAPH (I) OF THIS PARAGRAPH MAY NOT BE ENGAGED BY A MANAGED
18 CARE ORGANIZATION OR BY ANY PHARMACY BENEFITS MANAGER CONTRACTED
19 WITH A MANAGED CARE ORGANIZATION.
20 Article – Insurance
21 15–1601.
22 (a) In this subtitle the following words have the meanings indicated.
23 (p) (1) “Pharmacy benefits management services” means:
24 (i) the procurement of prescription drugs at a negotiated rate for
25 dispensation within the State to beneficiaries;
26 (ii) the administration or management of prescription drug coverage
27 provided by a purchaser for beneficiaries; and
28 (iii) any of the following services provided with regard to the
29 administration of prescription drug coverage:
30 1. mail service pharmacy;
4 HOUSE BILL 1384
1 2. claims processing, retail network management, and
2 payment of claims to pharmacies for prescription drugs dispensed to beneficiaries;
3 3. clinical formulary development and management services;
4 4. rebate contracting and administration;
5 5. patient compliance, therapeutic intervention, and generic
6 substitution programs; or
7 6. disease management programs.
8 (2) “Pharmacy benefits management services” does not include any service
9 provided by a nonprofit health maintenance organization that operates as a group model,
10 provided that the service:
11 (i) is provided solely to a member of the nonprofit health
12 maintenance organization; and
13 (ii) is furnished through the internal pharmacy operations of the
14 nonprofit health maintenance organization.
15 (q) “Pharmacy benefits manager” means a person that performs pharmacy
16 benefits management services.
17 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
18 October 1, 2026.
