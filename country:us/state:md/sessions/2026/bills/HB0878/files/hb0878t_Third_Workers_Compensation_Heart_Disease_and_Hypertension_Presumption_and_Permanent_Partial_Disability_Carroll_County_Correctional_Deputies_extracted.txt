CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Workers’ Compensation – Heart Disease and Hypertension Presumption and
3 Permanent Partial Disability – Carroll County Correctional Deputies
4 FOR the purpose of extending the presumption of compensability under the workers’
5 compensation law to include, subject to certain conditions, Carroll County
6 correctional deputies who suffer from heart disease or hypertension resulting in
7 partial disability or death; altering a certain definition of “public safety employee”
8 for the purpose of applying a certain workers’ compensation provision relating to
9 permanent partial disability benefits to correctional deputies employed by Carroll
10 County; and generally relating to compensability of Carroll County correctional
11 deputies under the workers’ compensation law.
12 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
13 Article – Labor and Employment
14 Section 9–503(b) and (e) and 9–628(a)
15 Annotated Code of Maryland
16 (2025 Replacement Volume)
17 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
18 Article – Labor and Employment
19 Section 9–628(h) and 9–629
20 Annotated Code of Maryland
21 (2025 Replacement Volume)

2 HOUSE BILL 878
1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – Labor and Employment
4 9–503.
5 (b) (1) A paid police officer employed by an airport authority, a county, the
6 Maryland–National Capital Park and Planning Commission, a municipality, or the State,
7 a deputy sheriff of Montgomery County, or, subject to paragraph (2) of this subsection, a
8 deputy sheriff of Anne Arundel County, Anne Arundel County detention officer, deputy
9 sheriff of Baltimore City, CARROLL COUNTY CORRECTIONAL DEPUTY, Montgomery
10 County correctional officer, Prince George’s County deputy sheriff, Prince George’s County
11 correctional officer, or deputy sheriff of Allegany County is presumed to be suffering from
12 an occupational disease that was suffered in the line of duty and is compensable under this
13 title if:
14 (i) the police officer, deputy sheriff, or correctional officer is
15 suffering from heart disease or hypertension; and
16 (ii) the heart disease or hypertension results in partial or total
17 disability or death.
18 (2) (i) A deputy sheriff of Anne Arundel County, Anne Arundel County
19 detention officer, deputy sheriff of Baltimore City, CARROLL COUNTY CORRECTIONAL
20 DEPUTY, Montgomery County correctional officer, Prince George’s County deputy sheriff,
21 or Prince George’s County correctional officer is entitled to the presumption under this
22 subsection only to the extent that the individual suffers from heart disease or hypertension
23 that is more severe than the individual’s heart disease or hypertension condition existing
24 prior to the individual’s employment as a deputy sheriff of Anne Arundel County, Anne
25 Arundel County detention officer, deputy sheriff of Baltimore City, CARROLL COUNTY
26 CORRECTIONAL DEPUTY, Montgomery County correctional officer, Prince George’s
27 County deputy sheriff, or Prince George’s County correctional officer.
28 (ii) To be eligible for the presumption under this subsection, a deputy
29 sheriff of Anne Arundel County, Anne Arundel County detention officer, deputy sheriff of
30 Baltimore City, CARROLL COUNTY CORRECTIONAL DEPUTY, Montgomery County
31 correctional officer, Prince George’s County deputy sheriff, or Prince George’s County
32 correctional officer, as a condition of employment, shall submit to a medical examination to
33 determine any heart disease or hypertension condition existing prior to the individual’s
34 employment as a deputy sheriff of Anne Arundel County, Anne Arundel County detention
35 officer, deputy sheriff of Baltimore City, CARROLL COUNTY CORRECTIONAL DEPUTY,
36 Montgomery County correctional officer, Prince George’s County deputy sheriff, or Prince
37 George’s County correctional officer.
HOUSE BILL 878 3
1 (e) (1) Except as provided in paragraph (2) of this subsection, any paid
2 firefighter, paid firefighting instructor, sworn member of the Office of the State Fire
3 Marshal, paid police officer, forest ranger, park ranger, wildlife ranger, paid law
4 enforcement employee of the Department of Natural Resources, deputy sheriff of Anne
5 Arundel County, Anne Arundel County detention officer, park police officer of the
6 Maryland–National Capital Park and Planning Commission, deputy sheriff of Montgomery
7 County, deputy sheriff of Baltimore City, CARROLL COUNTY CORRECTIONAL DEPUTY,
8 Montgomery County correctional officer, deputy sheriff of Prince George’s County, or Prince
9 George’s County correctional officer who is eligible for benefits under subsection (a), (b), (c),
10 or (d) of this section or the dependents of those individuals shall receive the benefits in
11 addition to any benefits that the individual or the dependents of the individual are entitled
12 to receive under the retirement system in which the individual was a participant at the
13 time of the claim.
14 (2) The benefits received under this title shall be adjusted so that the
15 weekly total of those benefits and retirement benefits does not exceed the weekly salary
16 that was paid to an individual specified under paragraph (1) of this subsection.
17 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That the Laws of Maryland read
18 as follows:
19 Article – Labor and Employment
20 9–628.
21 (a) In this section, “public safety employee” means:
22 (1) a firefighter, firefighting instructor, or paramedic employed by:
23 (i) a municipal corporation;
24 (ii) a county;
25 (iii) the State;
26 (iv) the State Airport Authority; or
27 (v) a fire control district;
28 (2) a volunteer firefighter or volunteer ambulance, rescue, or advanced life
29 support worker who is a covered employee under § 9–234 of this title and who provides
30 volunteer fire or rescue services to:
31 (i) a municipal corporation;
32 (ii) a county;

1 (iii) the State;
2 (iv) the State Airport Authority; or
3 (v) a fire control district;
4 (3) a police officer employed by:
5 (i) a municipal corporation;
6 (ii) a county;
7 (iii) the State;
8 (iv) the State Airport Authority;
9 (v) the Maryland–National Capital Park and Planning Commission;
10 or
11 (vi) the Washington Metropolitan Area Transit Authority;
12 (4) a Prince George’s County deputy sheriff or correctional officer;
13 (5) a Montgomery County deputy sheriff or correctional officer;
14 (6) an Allegany County deputy sheriff;
15 (7) a Howard County deputy sheriff;
16 (8) an Anne Arundel County deputy sheriff or detention officer;
17 (9) a Baltimore County correctional officer or detention officer;
18 (10) a Baltimore County deputy sheriff, but only when the deputy sheriff
19 sustains an accidental personal injury that arises out of and in the course and scope of
20 performing duties directly related to:
21 (i) courthouse security;
22 (ii) prisoner transportation;
23 (iii) service of warrants;
24 (iv) personnel management; or
25 (v) other administrative duties;

1 (11) a State correctional officer;
2 (12) a Baltimore City deputy sheriff; [or]
3 (13) a Harford County deputy sheriff, correctional officer, or detention
4 officer; OR
5 (14) A CARROLL COUNTY CORRECTIONAL DEPUTY.
6 (h) If a public safety employee is awarded compensation for less than 75 weeks,
7 the employer or its insurer shall pay the public safety employee compensation at the rate
8 set for an award of compensation for a period greater than or equal to 75 weeks but less
9 than 250 weeks under § 9–629 of this subtitle.
10 9–629.
11 If a covered employee is awarded compensation for a period equal to or greater than
12 75 weeks but less than 250 weeks, the employer or its insurer shall pay the covered
13 employee weekly compensation that equals two–thirds of the average weekly wage of the
14 covered employee but does not exceed one–third of the State average weekly wage.
15 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That, notwithstanding the
16 provisions of § 9–503(b)(2) of the Labor and Employment Article, as enacted by Section 1 of
17 this Act, a Carroll County correctional deputy who is employed on or before September 30,
18 2026:
19 (1) as a condition of continued employment shall provide to the Carroll
20 County Sheriff’s Office on or before December 31, 2026, a copy of a medical report disclosing
21 and describing any existing heart disease or hypertension from which the correctional
22 deputy may be suffering; and
23 (2) is entitled to the presumption under § 9–503(b) of the Labor and
24 Employment Article, as enacted by Section 1 of this Act, only to the extent that the
25 individual suffers from heart disease or hypertension that is more severe than the
26 individual’s heart disease or hypertension condition existing as of the date of the medical
27 report provided under item (1) of this section.
28 SECTION 4. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That Section 2 of this Act shall be
29 construed to apply only prospectively and may not be applied or interpreted to have any
30 effect on or application to any claims arising from events occurring before the effective date
31 of this Act.
32 SECTION 5. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
33 October 1, 2026.
