A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Property Tax – Credit for Dwelling House of Disabled Veterans and Surviving
3 Spouses – Alterations
4 FOR the purpose of altering a certain income limitation and certain disability rating
5 percentages used to establish eligibility for a certain property tax credit against the
6 county or municipal corporation property tax imposed on the dwelling house of
7 certain disabled veterans and their surviving spouses; and generally relating to a
8 property tax credit for the dwelling house of a disabled veteran and a surviving
9 spouse.
10 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
11 Article – Tax – Property
12 Section 9–265
13 Annotated Code of Maryland
14 (2019 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
15 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
16 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
17 Article – Tax – Property
18 9–265.
19 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
20 (2) “Disabled veteran” means an individual who:
21 (i) is honorably discharged or released under honorable
22 circumstances from active military, naval, or air service as defined in 38 U.S.C. § 101; and
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

1 (ii) 1. has been declared by the U.S. Department of Veterans
2 Affairs to have a permanent service–connected disability of at least 50% that results from
3 blindness or any other disabling cause that:
4 A. is reasonably certain to continue for the life of the veteran;
5 and
6 B. was not caused or incurred by misconduct of the veteran;
7 or
8 2. has been declared by the U.S. Department of Veterans
9 Affairs to have a nonpermanent service–connected disability of 100% that results from
10 blindness or any other disabling cause that was not caused or incurred by misconduct of
11 the veteran.
12 (3) “Dwelling house”:
13 (i) means real property that is:
14 1. the legal residence of a disabled veteran; and
15 2. occupied by not more than two families; and
16 (ii) includes the lot or curtilage and structures necessary to use the
17 real property as a residence.
18 (b) The Mayor and City Council of Baltimore City or the governing body of a
19 county or municipal corporation may grant, by law, a property tax credit under this section
20 against the county or municipal corporation property tax imposed on a dwelling house if:
21 (1) the dwelling house is owned by a disabled veteran;
22 (2) the disabled veteran’s federal adjusted gross income for the
23 immediately preceding taxable year does not exceed:
24 (I) $100,000, IF FILING AN INDIVIDUAL INCOME TAX RETURN;
25 OR
26 (II) $200,000, IF FILING A JOINT INCOME TAX RETURN; and
27 (3) the application requirements of subsection (d) of this section are met.
28 (c) The property tax credit granted under this section shall equal:
29 (1) 50% of the county or municipal corporation property tax imposed on the
30 dwelling house if the disabled veteran’s service–connected disability rating is at least [75%]
SENATE BILL 980 3
1 70% and the disabled veteran does not qualify for a property tax exemption under § 7–208
2 of this article; or
3 (2) 25% of the county or municipal corporation property tax imposed on the
4 dwelling house if the disabled veteran’s service–connected disability rating is at least 50%
5 but not more than [74%] 69%.
6 (d) (1) A disabled veteran shall apply for the property tax credit under this
7 section by providing to the county or municipal corporation:
8 (i) a copy of the disabled veteran’s discharge certificate from active
9 military, naval, or air service; and
10 (ii) on the form provided by the county or municipal corporation, a
11 certification of the disabled veteran’s disability from the U.S. Department of Veterans
12 Affairs.
13 (2) The disabled veteran’s certificate of disability may not be inspected by
14 individuals other than:
15 (i) the disabled veteran; or
16 (ii) appropriate employees of the county or municipal corporation.
17 (e) The Mayor and City Council of Baltimore City or the governing body of a
18 county or municipal corporation may, by law, continue to provide the property tax credit
19 under this section to the surviving spouse of the disabled veteran.
20 (f) The Mayor and City Council of Baltimore City or the governing body of a
21 county or municipal corporation may provide, by law, for:
22 (1) the duration of the tax credit;
23 (2) regulations and procedures for the application and uniform processing
24 of requests for the tax credit;
25 (3) the definition of surviving spouse and the amount and duration of the
26 tax credit for the surviving spouse;
27 (4) notwithstanding subsection (a)(2)(ii)2 of this section, eligibility criteria
28 for the credit allowed under this section that limits the credit to individuals described under
29 subsection (a)(2)(ii)1 of this section; and
30 (5) any other provision necessary to carry out the tax credit under this
31 section.
4 SENATE BILL 980
1 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect June
2 1, 2026, and shall be applicable to all taxable years beginning after June 30, 2026.
