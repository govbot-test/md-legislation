CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Economic Development – Maryland’s Future Board – Establishment
3 FOR the purpose of establishing the Maryland’s Future Board under the Department of
4 Commerce to develop, evaluate, and revise a certain visionary plan for the future of
5 Maryland by a certain date and make recommendations for projects based on the
6 plan to the Governor and the General Assembly; establishing the Maryland’s Future
7 Fund as a special, nonlapsing fund; requiring interest earnings of the Fund to be
8 credited to the Fund; requiring the Comptroller to study the trends for certain
9 industries and report to the General Assembly on or before a certain date; and
10 generally relating to the Maryland’s Future Board.
11 BY adding to
12 Article – Economic Development
13 Section 2.5–301 through 2.5–305 to be under the new subtitle “Subtitle 3. Maryland’s
14 Future Board”

27 WHEREAS, Maryland is rich in assets, often ranking among the top in the nation
28 for its innovation, technology–based workforce, personal income levels, and research and
29 development activity; and
30 WHEREAS, Maryland, despite these significant competitive advantages, lags
31 behind the nation in economic growth, population growth, and personal income growth; and
32 WHEREAS, In 2025, Maryland’s gross domestic product growth was 3.8%, compared
33 to the national rate of 4.4%; and

1 WHEREAS, In 2025, Maryland’s population growth over the precious 5 years was
2 1.4%, compared to the national rate of 2.8%; and
3 WHEREAS, Demographic changes, population shifts, and uncertainty at the federal
4 level, on which much of Maryland’s economy is dependent, makes planning for the future

1 (a) (1) The Comptroller shall study the economic growth trends in Maryland,
2 including the trends for industries in which Maryland has a competitive advantage or any
3 other industries that have an outsized presence in Maryland.
4 (2) The study shall identify the growth that is needed in the competitive
5 industries to ensure the financial and socioeconomic well–being of the residents of Maryland.
6 (3) The study may examine the social and economic factors that are expected
7 to significantly change over the next 10 years or more, including:
8 (i) state migration trends;
9 (ii) job replacement due to the rise in artificial intelligence; and
10 (iii) the need for universal basic income or advanced technology
11 workforce training.
12 (4) The Comptroller may contract with an independent consultant to assist
13 the Comptroller in the preparation of the study required under this paragraph.
14 (b) On or before December 1, 2026, the Comptroller shall report to the General
15 Assembly, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government Article, on the result of the
16 study under subsection (a) of this section.
