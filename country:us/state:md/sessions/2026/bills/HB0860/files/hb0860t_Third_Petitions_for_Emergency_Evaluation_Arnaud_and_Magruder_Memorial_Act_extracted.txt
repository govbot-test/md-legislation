CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Petitions for Emergency Evaluation
3 (Arnaud and Magruder Memorial Act)
4 FOR the purpose of specifying that a petition for emergency evaluation is effective for a
5 certain period of time; authorizing a court, on a certain motion and for good cause
6 shown on certain findings, to extend a petition for a certain period of time; requiring
7 a court to include the date and time the petition expires on the petition; requiring
8 peace officers to comply with a certain provision of law when executing a petition;
9 authorizing peace officers to act in accordance with certain best practices and to be
10 joined by a mobile crisis team under certain circumstances when executing a
11 petition; and generally relating to petitions for emergency evaluation.
12 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
13 Article – Health – General
14 Section 10–623 and 10–624(a)
15 Annotated Code of Maryland
16 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
17 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
18 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
19 Article – Health – General

2 (a) If the petitioner under Part IV of this subtitle is not a physician, psychologist,
3 clinical social worker, licensed clinical professional counselor, clinical nurse specialist in
4 psychiatric and mental health nursing, psychiatric nurse practitioner, licensed clinical
5 marriage and family therapist, health officer or designee of a health officer, or peace officer,
6 the petitioner shall present the petition to the court for immediate review.
7 (b) After review of the petition, the court shall endorse the petition if the court
8 finds probable cause to believe that the emergency evaluee has shown the symptoms of a
9 mental disorder and that the individual presents a danger to the life or safety of the
10 individual or of others.
11 (c) If the court does not find probable cause, the court shall indicate that fact on
12 the petition, and no further action may be taken under the petition.
13 (D) (1) SUBJECT TO PARAGRAPH (2) OF THIS SUBSECTION, THE PETITION
14 SHALL BE EFFECTIVE FOR 5 DAYS AFTER BEING ENDORSED BY THE COURT UNDER
15 SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION.
16 (2) (I) SUBJECT TO SUBPARAGRAPH (II) OF THIS PARAGRAPH, ON
17 A MOTION, THE COURT MAY EXTEND THE PETITION FOR GOOD CAUSE SHOWN BASED
18 ON THE PRESENTING BEHAVIOR OF THE INDIVIDUAL FOR UP TO 5 DAYS AT A TIME,
19 BUT NOT TO EXCEED A TOTAL OF 30 DAYS FROM THE DATE THE PETITION WAS
20 ENDORSED BY THE COURT UNDER SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION, IF THE COURT
21 FINDS:
22 1. THERE WAS GOOD CAUSE AS TO WHY THE PETITION
23 COULD NOT BE SERVED BEFORE THE EXPIRATION OF THE PETITION; AND
24 2. THE GROUNDS AND CONDITIONS THAT
25 NECESSITATED THE ISSUANCE OF THE INITIAL PETITION STILL EXIST.
26 (II) 1. A MOTION UNDER SUBPARAGRAPH (I) OF THIS
27 PARAGRAPH MUST BE MADE BY THE ORIGINAL PETITIONER.
28 2. EACH EXTENSION SHALL BE BASED ON A SEPARATE
29 MOTION.
30 (3) A PETITION ENDORSED OR EXTENDED UNDER THIS SUBSECTION
31 SHALL BE SERVED EXPEDITIOUSLY.
32 (E) THE COURT SHALL INCLUDE ON THE PETITION THE DATE AND TIME THE
33 PETITION EXPIRES.
HOUSE BILL 860 3
1 10–624.
2 (a) (1) A peace officer shall take an emergency evaluee to the nearest
3 emergency facility if the peace officer has a petition under Part IV of this subtitle that:
4 (i) 1. Has been endorsed by a court [within the last 5 days]; AND
5 2. IS IN EFFECT; or
6 (ii) Is signed and submitted by a physician, psychologist, clinical
7 social worker, licensed clinical professional counselor, clinical nurse specialist in
8 psychiatric and mental health nursing, psychiatric nurse practitioner, licensed clinical
9 marriage and family therapist, health officer or designee of a health officer, or peace officer.
10 (2) The petition required under paragraph (1) of this subsection may be:
11 (i) In the form of an electronic record; and
12 (ii) Transmitted and received electronically.
13 (3) A WHEN EXECUTING A PETITION, A PEACE OFFICER SHALL:
14 (I) SHALL ACT IN ACCORDANCE WITH § 3–524 OF THE PUBLIC
15 SAFETY ARTICLE WHEN EXECUTING A PETITION;
16 (II) MAY ACT IN ACCORDANCE WITH BEST PRACTICES ON HOW
17 TO TAKE AN EMERGENCY EVALUEE TO AN EMERGENCY FACILITY; AND
18 (III) SUBJECT TO THE DISCRETION OF THE PEACE OFFICER, MAY
19 BE JOINED BY A MOBILE CRISIS TEAM WHEN PRACTICABLE.
20 [(3)] (4) To the extent practicable, a peace officer shall notify the
21 emergency facility in advance that the peace officer is bringing an emergency evaluee to
22 the emergency facility.
23 [(4)] (5) After a peace officer brings the emergency evaluee to an
24 emergency facility, the peace officer need not stay unless, because the emergency evaluee
25 is violent, emergency facility personnel ask the supervisor of the peace officer to have the
26 peace officer stay.
27 [(5)] (6) A peace officer shall stay until the supervisor responds to the
28 request for assistance. If the emergency evaluee is violent, the supervisor shall allow the
29 peace officer to stay.
30 [(6)] (7) If emergency facility personnel ask that a peace officer stay, a
31 physician shall examine the emergency evaluee as promptly as possible.
