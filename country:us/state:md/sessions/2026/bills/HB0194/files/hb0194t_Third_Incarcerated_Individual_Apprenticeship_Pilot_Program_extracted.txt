CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Incarcerated Job Training Act
3 Incarcerated Individual Apprenticeship Pilot Program
4 FOR the purpose of requiring the Department of Public Safety and Correctional Services,
5 in consultation with the Department of Labor, to develop and offer an apprenticeship
6 program in skilled trades for incarcerated individuals; and generally relating to an
7 incarcerated individual apprenticeship program and the employment of incarcerated
8 individuals an Incarcerated Individual Apprenticeship Pilot Program to provide
9 incarcerated individuals at the Baltimore City Correctional Center the opportunity
10 to serve as paid apprentices; and generally relating to the Incarcerated Individual
11 Apprenticeship Pilot Program.
12 BY adding to
13 Article – Correctional Services
14 Section 3–901 through 3–908 to be under the new subtitle “Subtitle 9. Incarcerated
15 Individual Apprenticeship Program”

1 SUBTITLE 9. INCARCERATED INDIVIDUAL APPRENTICESHIP PROGRAM.
2 3–901.
3 IN THIS SUBTITLE, “APPRENTICESHIP PROGRAM” MEANS THE

5 3–902.
6 (A) THERE IS AN INCARCERATED INDIVIDUAL APPRENTICESHIP PROGRAM
7 IN THE DEPARTMENT.
8 (B) THE APPRENTICESHIP PROGRAM IS AN INDEPENDENT PROGRAM THAT
9 IS SEPARATE FROM MARYLAND CORRECTIONAL ENTERPRISES UNDER SUBTITLE 5
10 OF THIS TITLE.
11 3–903.
12 (A) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL DEVELOP AN APPRENTICESHIP PROGRAM TO
13 PROVIDE INCARCERATED INDIVIDUALS THE OPPORTUNITY TO SERVE AS PAID
14 APPRENTICES IN AN AGENCY OR A UNIT OF STATE OR LOCAL GOVERNMENT OR WITH
15 A PRIVATE ENTITY.
16 (B) THE APPRENTICESHIP PROGRAM SHALL PRIORITIZE EMPLOYMENT IN
17 SKILLED TRADES WITH THE PURPOSE OF ASSISTING INCARCERATED INDIVIDUALS
18 TO DEVELOP MARKETABLE JOB SKILLS AND TO PROVIDE INCARCERATED
19 INDIVIDUALS WITH THE OPPORTUNITY TO EARN MONEY THAT CAN BE USED ONCE
20 THE INCARCERATED INDIVIDUAL IS RELEASED FROM INCARCERATION.
21 (C) EMPLOYERS PARTICIPATING IN THE APPRENTICESHIP PROGRAM MAY
22 INCLUDE AN AGENCY OR A UNIT OF STATE OR LOCAL GOVERNMENT AND PRIVATE
23 ENTITIES.
24 (D) (1) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL MAKE ASSIGNMENTS FOR
25 APPRENTICESHIP PROGRAM PARTICIPANTS TO ELIGIBLE EMPLOYERS WHO ARE
26 PARTICIPATING IN THE APPRENTICESHIP PROGRAM.
27 (2) AN EMPLOYER MAY REJECT THE DEPARTMENT’S PROPOSED
28 ASSIGNMENT OR CEASE PARTICIPATION IN THE APPRENTICESHIP PROGRAM AT ANY
29 TIME.

1 (c) The Department, in consultation with the Department of Labor, shall develop
2 the Program to provide incarcerated individuals at the Baltimore City Correctional Center
3 the opportunity to serve as paid apprentices in an agency or a unit of State or local
4 government or with a private entity.
5 (d) The Department shall make assignments for Program participants to eligible
6 employers who are participating in the Program.
7 (e) A person that employs an incarcerated individual under the Program shall
8 pay a compensation rate of not less than the State minimum wage under § 3–413(c) of the
9 Labor and Employment Article.
10 (f) The Division of Correction shall provide for, or make arrangements that it
11 considers to be adequate and proper to provide for, the guarding, transporting, lodging,
12 feeding, clothing, and medical and other care of an incarcerated individual while the
13 incarcerated individual is participating in the Program.
14 (g) If an incarcerated individual successfully completes the Program, prior to the
15 individual’s scheduled release, the Department or the Program shall provide the
16 incarcerated individual with a certificate demonstrating the incarcerated individual’s
17 successful completion of the Program.
18 (h) On or before January 1, 2029, the Department shall report to the General
19 Assembly, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government Article, on the
20 implementation of this section.
21 (i) The Department may adopt regulations to carry out the provisions of this Act.
22 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
