A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Education – Engagement Policies and School Compact
3 (Maryland Public Education Parental Partnership Act)
4 FOR the purpose of requiring each county board of education to develop a parent and family
5 engagement policy on or before the start of a certain school year; requiring each
6 public school in the State to develop a Parent–School Compact beginning in a certain
7 school year; requiring the State Department of Education to provide certain guidance
8 and technical assistance to county boards and public schools in developing a certain
9 parent and family engagement policy and Parent–School Compact; and generally
10 relating to parent and family engagement in student education in public schools.
11 BY adding to
12 Article – Education
13 Section 4–148
14 Annotated Code of Maryland
15 (2025 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
16 Preamble
17 WHEREAS, Parental involvement is a critical component of a high–quality
18 education and contributes significantly to the academic achievement of students; and
19 WHEREAS, Federal law recognizes the importance of comprehensive parent and
20 family engagement by requiring that all schools that receive funding through Title I, Part
21 A of the Every Student Succeeds Act develop a comprehensive plan to engage parents; and
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

2 HOUSE BILL 1334
1 WHEREAS, The General Assembly recognizes the myriad benefits of robust parental
2 engagement and believes that parental engagement is a critical component to the education
3 of all children in each public school; now, therefore,
4 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
5 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
6 Article – Education
7 4–148.
8 (A) (1) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN PARAGRAPH (5) OF THIS SUBSECTION,
9 ON OR BEFORE THE START OF THE 2027–2028 SCHOOL YEAR, EACH COUNTY BOARD
10 SHALL DEVELOP A COMPREHENSIVE PARENT AND FAMILY ENGAGEMENT POLICY.
11 (2) THE PURPOSE OF THE PARENT AND FAMILY ENGAGEMENT POLICY
12 IS TO:
13 (I) PROVIDE THE PARENTS OF STUDENTS ENROLLED IN THE
14 LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM WITH MEANINGFUL OPPORTUNITIES TO PARTICIPATE IN
15 THE DECISIONS OF THE COUNTY BOARD AND COUNTY SUPERINTENDENT THAT
16 PERTAIN TO THE EDUCATION OF STUDENTS WITHIN THE LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM;
17 AND
18 (II) ASSIST PARENTS AND PUBLIC SCHOOLS IN COLLABORATING
19 ON THE EDUCATION OF STUDENTS ENROLLED IN EACH PUBLIC SCHOOL.
20 (3) THE PARENT AND FAMILY ENGAGEMENT POLICY DEVELOPED
21 UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION SHALL:
22 (I) BE ESTABLISHED IN CONSULTATION WITH THE PARENTS OF
23 STUDENTS ENROLLED IN THE LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM;
24 (II) ESTABLISH A FRAMEWORK FOR A PARENT–SCHOOL
25 COMPACT TO BE USED BY EACH PUBLIC SCHOOL IN THE LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM IN
26 ACCORDANCE WITH THE REQUIREMENTS OF SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION,
27 INCLUDING GENERAL PROCEDURAL RULES FOR THE CREATION OF THE COMPACT;
28 (III) PROVIDE TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE TO PARENTS SEEKING TO
29 PARTICIPATE IN THE DEVELOPMENT OF THE PARENT–SCHOOL COMPACT,
30 INCLUDING BY PROVIDING TRANSLATION SERVICES AND TRAINING;
HOUSE BILL 1334 3
1 (IV) BUILD CAPACITY IN EACH SCHOOL FOR SUBSTANTIVE
2 PARENTAL ENGAGEMENT, INCLUDING BY PROVIDING TRAINING, MATERIALS, AND
3 SUPPORT FOR SCHOOL STAFF WHO SEEK TO ENGAGE PARENTS;
4 (V) COORDINATE AND INTEGRATE THE FAMILY AND PARENT
5 ENGAGEMENT STRATEGIES ADOPTED AS PART OF THE POLICY WITH OTHER
6 RELEVANT FEDERAL AND STATE LAWS AND POLICIES;
7 (VI) PROVIDE AN OPPORTUNITY, AT LEAST ONCE A YEAR, FOR
8 PARENTS TO EVALUATE THE POLICY FOR EFFECTIVENESS AND CONTRIBUTE
9 FEEDBACK TO IMPROVE THE POLICY; AND
10 (VII) BE REVISED AT LEAST ONCE A YEAR TO REMOVE BARRIERS
11 TO PARENTAL INVOLVEMENT AND ADDRESS THE NEEDS OF PARENTS SEEKING TO
12 ASSIST IN THE EDUCATION OF THEIR CHILDREN.
13 (4) THE PARENT AND FAMILY ENGAGEMENT POLICY SHALL BE
14 DOCUMENTED IN WRITING AND PUBLISHED ON THE WEBSITE OF THE COUNTY
15 BOARD.
16 (5) IF THE COUNTY BOARD HAS AN EXISTING PARENT AND FAMILY
17 ENGAGEMENT POLICY, INCLUDING A POLICY ESTABLISHED IN ACCORDANCE WITH
18 THE FEDERAL EVERY STUDENT SUCCEEDS ACT, THE COUNTY BOARD MAY AMEND
19 THAT POLICY TO MEET THE REQUIREMENTS OF PARAGRAPH (3) OF THIS
20 SUBSECTION.
21 (B) (1) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN PARAGRAPH (6) OF THIS SUBSECTION,
22 BEGINNING IN THE 2028–2029 SCHOOL YEAR, EACH PUBLIC SCHOOL SHALL
23 DEVELOP A PARENT–SCHOOL COMPACT.
24 (2) THE PURPOSE OF A COMPACT IS TO CLEARLY DEFINE THE
25 RESPONSIBILITIES AND ROLES OF SCHOOL STAFF, PARENTS, AND STUDENTS IN THE
26 EDUCATION OF EACH STUDENT, INCLUDING:
27 (I) THE COUNTY BOARD’S AND SCHOOL’S RESPONSIBILITY TO
28 PROVIDE HIGH–QUALITY CURRICULUM AND INSTRUCTION ALIGNED TO THE STATE
29 STANDARDS AND CURRICULAR FRAMEWORKS IN A SUPPORTIVE AND EFFECTIVE
30 LEARNING ENVIRONMENT;
31 (II) THE WAYS PARENTS ARE RESPONSIBLE FOR SUPPORTING
32 THEIR STUDENT’S LEARNING AND ACADEMIC PROGRESS, INCLUDING MONITORING
33 ATTENDANCE, HOMEWORK ASSISTANCE AND COMPLETION, AND PARTICIPATION IN
34 EXTRACURRICULAR ACTIVITIES;
4 HOUSE BILL 1334
1 (III) A PARENT’S RIGHT TO KNOW THE QUALIFICATIONS AND
2 CREDENTIALS OF THE EDUCATORS TEACHING OR PROVIDING EDUCATIONAL OR
3 OTHER SERVICES TO THEIR STUDENT, INCLUDING TIMELY NOTIFICATION THAT AN
4 ASSIGNED TEACHER OF 4 OR MORE WEEKS HOLDS A CONDITIONAL TEACHING
5 LICENSE OR IS TEACHING A SUBJECT MATTER FOR WHICH THE TEACHER DOES NOT
6 HOLD A LICENSE; AND
7 (IV) THE SCHOOL’S RESPONSIBILITY TO PROVIDE MEANINGFUL
8 OPPORTUNITIES FOR ONGOING COMMUNICATION BETWEEN A STUDENT’S PARENTS
9 AND TEACHERS, INCLUDING, TO THE EXTENT PRACTICABLE, IN THE LANGUAGE
10 THAT THE PARENT SPEAKS:
11 1. ANNUAL PARENT–TEACHER CONFERENCES;
12 2. FREQUENT REPORTS ON STUDENT ACADEMIC
13 PROGRESS; AND
14 3. REGULAR OPPORTUNITIES FOR THE PARENT TO
15 MEET WITH SCHOOL STAFF.
16 (3) IN DEVELOPING A COMPACT UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS
17 SUBSECTION, THE SCHOOL AND PARENTS SHALL ENSURE THE COMPACT:
18 (I) FOLLOWS THE COMPREHENSIVE PARENT AND FAMILY
19 ENGAGEMENT POLICY DEVELOPED BY THE COUNTY BOARD;
20 (II) IS DEVELOPED USING A COLLABORATIVE PROCESS,
21 INCLUDING THE COLLECTION AND USE OF INPUT FROM THE SCHOOL AND PARENTS
22 REGARDING PLANNING, ORGANIZATION, REVIEW, IMPLEMENTATION, DETERMINING
23 THE INITIAL TERMS, AND AN AMENDMENT PROCESS; AND
24 (III) IS WRITTEN USING PLAIN AND CLEAR LANGUAGE
25 UNDERSTANDABLE TO ALL PARENTS, INCLUDING, TO THE EXTENT PRACTICABLE, IN
26 THE LANGUAGE THAT PARENTS SPEAK.
27 (4) (I) EACH SCHOOL SHALL CONVENE AN ANNUAL MEETING FOR
28 PARENTS TO:
29 1. REVIEW AND DISCUSS THE TERMS AND CONTENT OF
30 THE COMPACT;
HOUSE BILL 1334 5
1 2. UPDATE PARENTS ON THE SCHOOL’S ACADEMIC
2 PROGRAMS AND CURRICULUM USED FOR VARIOUS ACADEMIC SUBJECTS,
3 INCLUDING OPPORTUNITIES FOR PARENTS TO REVIEW CURRICULAR MATERIALS;
4 AND
5 3. INFORM PARENTS OF THE ASSESSMENTS USED TO
6 MEASURE STUDENT AND SCHOOL PROGRESS AND THE EXPECTED LEVELS OF
7 PROFICIENCY IN REPORTED SUBJECTS.
8 (II) DURING THE ANNUAL MEETING, THE SCHOOL SHALL:
9 1. PROVIDE PARENTS WITH TRAINING MATERIALS TO
10 ASSIST PARENTS SEEKING TO IMPROVE STUDENT ACHIEVEMENT; AND
11 2. OFFER PARENTS MEANINGFUL OPPORTUNITIES TO
12 PROVIDE FEEDBACK ON THE TERMS OF THE COMPACT, INCLUDING PROPOSING
13 AMENDMENTS TO THE COMPACT.
14 (III) THE ANNUAL MEETING SHALL:
15 1. TO THE EXTENT PRACTICABLE, HOLD DISCUSSIONS IN
16 LANGUAGES THAT PARENTS SPEAK; AND
17 2. BE SCHEDULED AT A CONVENIENT TIME FOR
18 PARENTS AND CONSIDER FLEXIBLE MODES OF MEETINGS IN ORDER TO MAXIMIZE
19 PARENTAL PARTICIPATION.
20 (IV) THE SCHOOL SHALL SEEK TO MAXIMIZE THE
21 PARTICIPATION OF PARENTS WITH DIVERSE NEEDS IN THE ANNUAL MEETING,
22 INCLUDING THE PARENTS OF:
23 1. STUDENTS WITH DISABILITIES;
24 2. ENGLISH LANGUAGE LEARNERS;
25 3. HOMELESS CHILDREN; AND
26 4. STUDENTS WITH FEDERAL VISAS.
27 (5) THE COMPACT SHALL BE POSTED IN A CONSPICUOUS PLACE ON
28 THE WEBSITE OF THE PUBLIC SCHOOL AND UPDATED EACH TIME THE COMPACT IS
29 REVISED OR AMENDED, INCLUDING A NOTATION OF THE TIME AND DATE OF THE
30 REVISION OR AMENDMENT.

9 (2) EACH PUBLIC SCHOOL SHALL SUBMIT TO THE COUNTY BOARD A

15 (E) THE DEPARTMENT MAY ADOPT REGULATIONS TO IMPLEMENT THE
