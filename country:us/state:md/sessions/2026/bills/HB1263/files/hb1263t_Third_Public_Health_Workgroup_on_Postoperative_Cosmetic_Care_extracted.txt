1 AN ACT concerning
2 Public Health – Workgroup on Postoperative Cosmetic Care
3 FOR the purpose of establishing the Workgroup on Postoperative Cosmetic Care to study
4 the regulation of postoperative cosmetic and body–altering services provided by
5 nonphysician practitioners; and generally relating to the Workgroup on
6 Postoperative Cosmetic Care.
7 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
8 That:
9 (a) There is a Workgroup on Postoperative Cosmetic Care to study the regulation
10 of postoperative cosmetic and body–altering services provided by nonphysician
11 practitioners with a focus on patient safety, consumer protection, and continuity of care.
12 (b) The Workgroup consists of the following members:
13 (1) the Secretary of Health, or the Secretary’s designee;
14 (2) the Secretary of Labor, or the Secretary’s designee;
15 (3) the President of the State Board of Nursing, or the President’s designee;

1 (5) assess training and education requirements for postoperative cosmetic
2 care;
3 (6) review best practices and regulatory models from other states;
4 (7) consider options for certification, registration, or professional
5 recognition for providers of postsurgical cosmetic care, including alignment with existing
6 licensing boards;
7 (8) make recommendations that prioritize consumer protection and patient
8 safety;
9 (9) avoid recommending the creation of a new licensure program unless
10 necessary; and
11 (10) consider low–cost and nonbureaucratic options.
12 (g) In carrying out its duties under subsection (f) of this section, the Workgroup
13 shall focus on consumer protection, patient safety, and continuity of care over industry
14 expansion.
15 (h) (1) On or before December 1, 2026, the Workgroup shall submit an interim
16 report of its findings and recommendations to the Governor and, in accordance with §
17 2–1257 of the State Government Article, the President of the Senate and the Speaker of the
18 House of Delegates.
19 (2) On or before December 1, 2027, the Workgroup shall submit a final
20 report of its findings and recommendations to the Governor and, in accordance with §
21 2–1257 of the State Government Article, the President of the Senate and the Speaker of the
22 House of Delegates.
23 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That the Workgroup on
24 Postoperative Cosmetic Care is not a regulatory board and may not establish a licensure
25 program without legislative approval.
26 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
27 1, 2026. It shall remain effective for a period of 2 years and, at the end of June 30, 2028,
28 this Act, with no further action required by the General Assembly, shall be abrogated and
29 of no further force and effect.
