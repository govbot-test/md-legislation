CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Higher Education – Nonresident Tuition – Exemption for Spouses and
3 Dependents of State or Local Public Safety Employees
4 (Maryland Fallen Heroes Tuition Benefits Act)
5 FOR the purpose of exempting spouses and financially dependent children of certain State
6 or local public safety employees from paying out–of–state or out–of–county tuition at
7 a public institution of higher education in the State; and generally relating to
8 nonresident tuition exemptions for spouses and financially dependent children of
9 State or local public safety employees.
10 BY adding to
11 Article – Education
12 Section 15–106.12
13 Annotated Code of Maryland
14 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
15 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
16 Article – Education
17 Section 18–601(a)(1) and (5)
18 Annotated Code of Maryland
19 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
20 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
21 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from existing law.
Underlining indicates amendments to bill.
Strike out indicates matter stricken from the bill by amendment or deleted from the law by
amendment. *sb0212*
2 SENATE BILL 212
1 Article – Education
2 15–106.12.
3 (A) IN THIS SECTION, “STATE OR LOCAL PUBLIC SAFETY EMPLOYEE” HAS
4 THE MEANING STATED IN § 18–601 OF THIS ARTICLE.
5 (B) NOTWITHSTANDING THE STATE OR COUNTY IN WHICH THE SPOUSE OR
6 THE CHILD RESIDES, A SPOUSE OR A FINANCIALLY DEPENDENT CHILD OF A STATE
7 OR LOCAL PUBLIC SAFETY EMPLOYEE WHO DIES AS A RESULT OF OR IN THE COURSE
8 OF PERFORMANCE OF DUTIES IS EXEMPT FROM PAYING OUT–OF–STATE OR
9 OUT–OF–COUNTY TUITION AT A PUBLIC INSTITUTION OF HIGHER EDUCATION IN THE
10 STATE.
11 (C) THE COMMISSION SHALL ADOPT REGULATIONS TO IMPLEMENT THIS
12 SECTION.
13 18–601.
14 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
15 (5) “State or local public safety employee” means a person who is employed
16 in the State as:
17 (i) A career or volunteer member of a:
18 1. Fire department;
19 2. Ambulance company or squad; or
20 3. Rescue company or squad;
21 (ii) A law enforcement officer;
22 (iii) A correctional officer; or
23 (iv) A member of the Maryland National Guard who was a resident
24 of this State at the time of death.
25 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
26 1, 2026.
