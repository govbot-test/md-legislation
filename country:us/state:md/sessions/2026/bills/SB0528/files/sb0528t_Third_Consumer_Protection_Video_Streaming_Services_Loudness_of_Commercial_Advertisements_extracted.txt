CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Consumer Protection – Video Streaming Services – Loudness of Commercial
3 Advertisements
4 FOR the purpose of prohibiting a certain video streaming service from transmitting to a
5 consumer in the State the audio of a commercial advertisement in a manner that is
6 louder than the audio of the accompanying video programming or video content of
7 the video streaming service; and generally relating to the regulation of video
8 streaming services.
9 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
10 Article – Commercial Law
11 Section 13–301(14)(xlvii)
12 Annotated Code of Maryland
13 (2025 Replacement Volume)
14 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
15 Article – Commercial Law
16 Section 13–301(14)(xlviii)
17 Annotated Code of Maryland
18 (2025 Replacement Volume)
19 BY adding to
20 Article – Commercial Law
21 Section 13–301(14)(xlix) and 14–1330
22 Annotated Code of Maryland

2 SENATE BILL 528
1 (2025 Replacement Volume)
2 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
3 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
4 Article – Commercial Law
5 13–301.
6 Unfair, abusive, or deceptive trade practices include any:
7 (14) Violation of a provision of:
8 (xlvii) Title 14, Subtitle 50 of this article; [or]
9 (xlviii) Section 13–411.1(c)(2) of the Transportation Article; or
10 (XLIX) SECTION 14–1330 OF THIS ARTICLE; OR
11 14–1330.
12 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
13 INDICATED.
14 (2) “CONSUMER” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 13–101 OF THIS
15 ARTICLE.
16 (3) “VIDEO PROGRAMMING” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN 47 U.S.C.
17 § 613.
18 (4) (I) “VIDEO STREAMING SERVICE” MEANS AN ENTITY THAT
19 TRANSMITS DIRECTLY TO A CONSUMER USING AN INTERNET PROTOCOL–BASED
20 SYSTEM:
21 1. VIDEO PROGRAMMING OR VIDEO CONTENT; AND
22 2. COMMERCIAL ADVERTISEMENTS TRANSMITTED
23 ALONG WITH THE VIDEO PROGRAMMING OR THE VIDEO CONTENT.
24 (II) “VIDEO STREAMING SERVICE” DOES NOT INCLUDE:
25 1. A TELEVISION BROADCAST STATION;
26 2. A CABLE OPERATOR; OR
SENATE BILL 528 3
1 3. ANY OTHER TELEVISION MULTICHANNEL VIDEO
2 PROGRAMMING DISTRIBUTOR;
3 4. A SERVICE THAT MAKES AVAILABLE PROGRAMMING
4 OR VIDEO CONTENT WITHOUT COMMERCIAL ADVERTISEMENTS; OR
5 5. A WEBSITE OR APPLICATION THAT DOES NOT MAKE
6 VIDEO PROGRAMMING AVAILABLE TO CONSUMERS AS ITS PRIMARY PURPOSE.
7 (B) A VIDEO STREAMING SERVICE MAY NOT TRANSMIT TO A CONSUMER IN
8 THE STATE THE AUDIO OF A COMMERCIAL ADVERTISEMENT IN A MANNER THAT IS
9 LOUDER THAN THE AUDIO OF THE ACCOMPANYING VIDEO PROGRAMMING OR VIDEO
10 CONTENT BEING TRANSMITTED BY THE VIDEO STREAMING SERVICE.
11 (C) A VIDEO STREAMING SERVICE SHALL BE CONSIDERED IN COMPLIANCE
12 WITH THIS SECTION IF THE VIDEO STREAMING SERVICE COMPLIES WITH
13 REGULATIONS THAT ARE ADOPTED:
14 (1) BY THE FEDERAL COMMUNICATIONS COMMISSION UNDER THE
15 FEDERAL COMMERCIAL ADVERTISEMENT LOUDNESS MITIGATION ACT; AND
16 (2) FOR THE PURPOSE OF REGULATING THE AUDIO LOUDNESS OF
17 COMMERCIAL ADVERTISEMENTS OF TELEVISION BROADCAST STATIONS, CABLE
18 OPERATORS, AND OTHER TELEVISION PROGRAMMING DISTRIBUTORS.
19 (D) A VIOLATION OF THIS SECTION IS:
20 (1) AN UNFAIR, ABUSIVE, OR DECEPTIVE TRADE PRACTICE WITHIN
21 THE MEANING OF TITLE 13 OF THIS ARTICLE; AND
22 (2) SUBJECT TO THE ENFORCEMENT AND PENALTY PROVISIONS
23 CONTAINED IN TITLE 13 OF THIS ARTICLE, EXCEPT §§ 13–407, 13–408, AND 13–411
24 OF THIS ARTICLE.
25 (E) THIS SECTION MAY NOT BE CONSTRUED TO AUTHORIZE A PRIVATE
26 RIGHT OF ACTION UNDER THIS SECTION OR ANY OTHER LAW.
27 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
28 October 1, 2026.
