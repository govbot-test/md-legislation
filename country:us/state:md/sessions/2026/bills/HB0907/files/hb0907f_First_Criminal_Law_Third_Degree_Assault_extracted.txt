A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Criminal Law – Third–Degree Assault
3 FOR the purpose of establishing that it is a misdemeanor to intentionally cause offensive
4 contact, engage in conduct tending to put another in fear of offensive contact, or
5 attempt to cause offensive contact; altering a certain list of convictions that are
6 eligible for expungement under certain circumstances; and generally relating to
7 assault.
8 BY adding to
9 Article – Criminal Law
10 Section 3–203.1
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
13 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
14 Article – Criminal Law

16 Annotated Code of Maryland
17 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
18 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
19 Article – Courts and Judicial Proceedings
20 Section 4–301(b) and 4–302(d)
21 Annotated Code of Maryland
22 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
23 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
24 Article – Criminal Procedure
25 Section 10–110(a)(1)(vii)
26 Annotated Code of Maryland
27 (2025 Replacement Volume)

2 HOUSE BILL 907
1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – Criminal Law
4 3–203.1.
5 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION, “OFFENSIVE CONTACT” MEANS
6 NONCONSENSUAL PHYSICAL CONTACT THAT A REASONABLE PERSON WOULD FIND
7 TO BE OFFENSIVE.
8 (2) “OFFENSIVE CONTACT” DOES NOT INCLUDE:
9 (I) CONTACT THAT RESULTS IN PHYSICAL INJURY;
10 (II) CONTACT THAT CAUSES A RISK OF SERIOUS PHYSICAL
11 INJURY;
12 (III) A DOMESTICALLY RELATED CRIME, AS DEFINED IN § 6–233
13 OF THE CRIMINAL PROCEDURE ARTICLE; OR
14 (IV) A SEXUAL CRIME UNDER SUBTITLE 3 OF THIS TITLE.
15 (B) A PERSON MAY NOT:
16 (1) INTENTIONALLY CAUSE OFFENSIVE CONTACT;
17 (2) ENGAGE IN CONDUCT INTENDING TO PUT ANOTHER IN FEAR OF
18 OFFENSIVE CONTACT; OR
19 (3) ATTEMPT TO CAUSE OFFENSIVE CONTACT.
20 (C) A PERSON WHO VIOLATES THIS SECTION IS GUILTY OF THE
21 MISDEMEANOR OF ASSAULT IN THE THIRD DEGREE AND ON CONVICTION IS SUBJECT
22 TO IMPRISONMENT NOT EXCEEDING 90 DAYS OR A FINE NOT EXCEEDING $500 OR
23 BOTH.
24 3–206.
25 (a) An indictment, information, other charging document, or warrant for a crime
26 described in § 3–202, § 3–203, or § 3–205 of this subtitle is sufficient if it substantially
27 states:
HOUSE BILL 907 3
1 “(name of defendant) on (date) in (county) assaulted (name of victim) in the........
2 degree or (describe other violation) in violation of (section violated) against the peace,
3 government, and dignity of the State.”.
4 (b) If the general form of indictment or information described in subsection (a) of
5 this section is used to charge a crime described in § 3–202, § 3–203, or § 3–205 of this
6 subtitle in a case in the circuit court, the defendant, on timely demand, is entitled to a bill
7 of particulars.
8 (c) A charge of assault in the first degree also charges a defendant with assault
9 in the second degree.
10 (D) (1) UNLESS SPECIFICALLY CHARGED BY THE STATE, ASSAULT IN THE
11 THIRD DEGREE UNDER § 3–203.1 OF THIS SUBTITLE IS NOT A LESSER INCLUDED
12 CRIME OF ANY OTHER CRIME.
13 (2) A CHARGING DOCUMENT OR WARRANT FOR A CRIME DESCRIBED
14 IN § 3–203.1 OF THIS SUBTITLE IS SUFFICIENT IF IT SUBSTANTIALLY STATES:
15 “(NAME OF DEFENDANT) ON (DATE) IN (COUNTY) COMMITTED ASSAULT IN THE
16 THIRD DEGREE AGAINST (NAME OF VICTIM) IN VIOLATION OF § 3–203.1 OF THE
17 CRIMINAL LAW ARTICLE AGAINST THE PEACE, GOVERNMENT, AND DIGNITY OF THE
18 STATE.”.
19 [(d)] (E) (1) To be found guilty of reckless endangerment under § 3–204 of
20 this subtitle, a defendant must be charged specifically with reckless endangerment.
21 (2) A charging document for reckless endangerment under § 3–204 of this
22 subtitle is sufficient if it substantially states:
23 “(name of defendant) on (date) in (county) committed reckless endangerment in
24 violation of § 3–204 of the Criminal Law Article against the peace, government, and dignity
25 of the State.”.
26 (3) If more than one individual is endangered by the conduct of the
27 defendant, a separate charge may be brought for each individual endangered.
28 (4) A charging document containing a charge of reckless endangerment
29 under § 3–204 of this subtitle may:
30 (i) include a count for each individual endangered by the conduct of
31 the defendant; or
32 (ii) contain a single count based on the conduct of the defendant,
33 regardless of the number of individuals endangered by the conduct of the defendant.

2 this subsection is used to charge reckless endangerment under § 3–204 of this subtitle in a

13 THIS SUBTITLE.
