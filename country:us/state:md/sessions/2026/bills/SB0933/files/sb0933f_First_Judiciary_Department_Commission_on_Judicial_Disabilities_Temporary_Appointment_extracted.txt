Section 1:
D1 CONSTITUTIONAL AMENDMENT 6lr0674
By: Chair, Judicial Proceedings Committee
Introduced and read first time: February 6, 2026
Assigned to: Judicial Proceedings
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Judiciary Department – Commission on Judicial Disabilities – Temporary
3 Appointment
4 FOR the purpose of withdrawing and repealing a certain proposed Constitutional
5 amendment relating to temporary appointments to the Commission on Judicial
6 Disabilities; allowing the chair of the Commission to appoint a former member to
7 temporarily fill a certain vacancy on the Commission; and allowing the Governor, on
8 certain request of the chair, to appoint a temporary substitute member or extend the
9 term of an existing member of the Commission.
10 BY withdrawing, recalling, and repealing
11 Chapters 848 and 849 of the Acts of the General Assembly of 2025
12 BY proposing an amendment to the Maryland Constitution
13 Article IV – Judiciary Department
14 Section 4A and 4B
15 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
16 (Three–fifths of all the members elected to each of the two Houses concurring), That
17 Chapters 848 and 849 of the Acts of the General Assembly of 2025 be and they are hereby
18 withdrawn and recalled for appropriate legislative action.
19 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That Chapters 848 and 849 of the
20 Acts of the General Assembly of 2025 be and they are hereby repealed.
21 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY
22 OF MARYLAND, (Three–fifths of all the members elected to each of the two Houses
23 concurring), That it be proposed that the Maryland Constitution read as follows:
24 Article IV – Judiciary Department
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

2 SENATE BILL 933
1 4A.
2 (a) There is a Commission on Judicial Disabilities composed of eleven persons
3 appointed by the Governor of Maryland, by and with the advice and consent of the Senate.
4 (b) The members of the Commission shall be citizens and residents of this State.
5 (c) (1) Three members of the Commission shall be appointed from among the
6 judges of the State, with one member representing the appellate courts, one member
7 representing the Circuit Courts, and one member representing the District Court.
8 (2) Three members shall be appointed from among those persons who are
9 admitted to practice law in the State, who have been so engaged for at least seven years,
10 and who are not judges of any court.
11 (3) Five members shall represent the public, who may not be active or
12 retired judges, who are not admitted to practice law in this State, and who may not have a
13 financial relationship with or receive compensation from a judge or a person admitted to
14 practice law in this State.
15 (4) The composition of the Commission should reflect the race, gender, and
16 geographic diversity of the population of the State.
17 (d) [The] SUBJECT TO THE PROVISIONS OF §4B(A)(5) OF THIS ARTICLE, THE
18 term of office of each member is four years commencing on January 1 following the
19 expiration of the member’s predecessor’s term. A member may not serve more than two
20 four–year terms, or for more than a total of ten years if appointed to fill a vacancy.
21 (e) A member’s membership automatically terminates:
22 (1) When any member of the Commission appointed from among judges in
23 the State ceases to be a judge;
24 (2) When any member appointed from among those admitted to practice
25 law becomes a judge;
26 (3) When any member representing the public becomes a judge or is
27 admitted to the practice of law in this State or has a financial relationship with or receives
28 compensation from a judge or a person admitted to practice law in this State; or
29 (4) When any member ceases to be a resident of the State.
30 (f) Any vacancies on the Commission shall be filled for the unexpired term by the
31 Governor in the same manner as for making of appointments to the Commission and
32 subject to the same qualifications which were applicable to the person causing the vacancy.
SENATE BILL 933 3
1 (g) A member of the Commission may not receive any compensation for the
2 member’s services as such but shall be allowed any expenses necessarily incurred in the
3 performance of the member’s duties as such member.
4 4B.
5 (a) (1) The Commission on Judicial Disabilities has the power to:
6 (i) Investigate complaints against any justice or judge of the
7 Supreme Court of Maryland, any intermediate courts of appeal, the circuit courts, the
8 District Court of Maryland, or the orphans’ court; and
9 (ii) Conduct hearings concerning such complaints, administer oaths
10 and affirmations, issue process to compel the attendance of witnesses and the production
11 of evidence, and require persons to testify and produce evidence by granting them immunity
12 from prosecution or from penalty or forfeiture.
13 (2) The Commission has the power to issue a reprimand and the power to
14 recommend to the Supreme Court of Maryland the removal, censure, or other appropriate
15 disciplining of a justice or judge or, in an appropriate case, retirement.
16 (3) All proceedings, testimony, and evidence before the Commission shall
17 be confidential and privileged, except as provided by rule of the Supreme Court of
18 Maryland; the record and any proceeding filed with the Supreme Court of Maryland shall
19 lose its confidential character, except as ordered by the Supreme Court of Maryland.
20 (4) No justice or judge shall participate as a member of the Commission in
21 any proceedings involving that justice’s or judge’s own conduct, and the Governor shall
22 appoint another justice or judge as a substitute member of the Commission for those
23 proceedings.
24 (5) (I) IN THE EVENT OF A MEMBER’S RECUSAL OR
25 DISQUALIFICATION OR THE EXPIRATION OF A MEMBER’S TERM WITHOUT A
26 REPLACEMENT, THE COMMISSION CHAIR MAY:
27 1. RECALL A FORMER MEMBER TO SERVE AS A
28 TEMPORARY SUBSTITUTE MEMBER; OR
29 2. REQUEST THAT THE GOVERNOR APPOINT A
30 TEMPORARY SUBSTITUTE MEMBER OR EXTEND A CURRENT MEMBER’S TERM.
31 (II) ON REQUEST OF THE COMMISSION CHAIR MADE UNDER
32 THIS PARAGRAPH, THE GOVERNOR MAY APPOINT A TEMPORARY SUBSTITUTE
33 MEMBER FOR A SPECIFIED PERIOD OR EXTEND A CURRENT MEMBER’S TERM UNTIL
34 A REPLACEMENT IS APPOINTED.
4 SENATE BILL 933
1 (III) ANY TEMPORARY SUBSTITUTE MEMBER:
2 1. IS SUBJECT TO THE SAME QUALIFICATIONS THAT
3 WERE APPLICABLE TO THE MEMBER FOR WHOM THE SUBSTITUTE IS TEMPORARILY
4 APPOINTED;
5 2. MAY NOT HAVE BEEN REJECTED BY THE SENATE FOR
6 APPOINTMENT TO THE COMMISSION; AND
7 3. IF THE MEMBER IS A JUDGE OR AN ATTORNEY, MUST
8 BE A MEMBER IN GOOD STANDING OF THE MARYLAND BAR.
9 (6) The Supreme Court of Maryland shall prescribe by rule the means to
10 implement and enforce the powers of the Commission and the practice and procedure before
11 the Commission.
12 (b) (1) Upon any recommendation of the Commission, the Supreme Court of
13 Maryland, after a hearing and upon a finding of misconduct while in office, or of persistent
14 failure to perform the duties of the office, or of conduct prejudicial to the proper
15 administration of justice, may remove the justice or judge from office or may censure or
16 otherwise discipline the justice or judge, or the Supreme Court of Maryland, after hearing
17 and upon a finding of disability which is or is likely to become permanent and which
18 seriously interferes with the performance of the justice’s or judge’s duties, may retire the
19 justice or judge from office.
20 (2) A justice or judge removed under this section, and the justice’s or
21 judge’s surviving spouse, shall have the rights and privileges accruing from the justice’s or
22 judge’s judicial service only to the extent prescribed by the order of removal.
23 (3) A justice or judge retired under this section shall have the rights and
24 privileges prescribed by law for other retired justices or judges.
25 (4) No justice of the Supreme Court of Maryland shall sit in judgment in
26 any hearing involving that justice’s own conduct.
27 (c) This section is alternative to, and cumulative with, the methods of retirement
28 and removal provided in Sections 3 and 4 of this Article, and in Section 26 of Article III of
29 this Constitution.
30 SECTION 4. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That the General Assembly
31 determines that the amendment to the Maryland Constitution proposed by Section 3 of this
32 Act affects multiple jurisdictions and that the provisions of Article XIV, § 1 of the Maryland
33 Constitution concerning local approval of constitutional amendments do not apply.
34 SECTION 5. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That the amendment to the
35 Maryland Constitution proposed by Section 3 of this Act shall be submitted to the qualified
SENATE BILL 933 5
1 voters of the State at the next general election to be held in November 2026 for adoption or
2 rejection in accordance with Article XIV of the Maryland Constitution. At that general
3 election, the vote on the proposed amendment to the Constitution shall be by ballot, and on
4 each ballot there shall be printed the words “For the Constitutional Amendment” and
5 “Against the Constitutional Amendment”, as now provided by law. Immediately after the
6 election, all returns shall be made to the Governor of the vote for and against the proposed
7 amendment, as directed by Article XIV of the Maryland Constitution, and further
8 proceedings had in accordance with Article XIV.
