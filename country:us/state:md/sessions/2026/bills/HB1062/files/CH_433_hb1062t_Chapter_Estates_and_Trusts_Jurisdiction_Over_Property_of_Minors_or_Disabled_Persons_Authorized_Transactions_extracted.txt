WES MOORE, Governor Ch. 433
Chapter 433
AN ACT concerning
Estates and Trusts – Jurisdiction Over Property of Minors or Disabled Persons –
Authorized Transactions
FOR the purpose of authorizing a circuit court to authorize or direct a transaction that
grants access to financial records or establishes eligibility for government assistance
programs for a minor or disabled person under a certain circumstance; and generally
relating to assuming jurisdiction over the property of a minor or disabled person.
BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
Article – Estates and Trusts

Section 2:
Section 13–204
Annotated Code of Maryland
(2022 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

Section 3:
SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
Article – Estates and Trusts
13–204.
(a) (1) If a basis exists as described in § 13–201 of this subtitle for assuming
jurisdiction over the property of a minor or disabled person, the circuit court, without
appointing a guardian, may authorize or direct a transaction with respect to the property,
service, or care arrangement of the minor or disabled person.
(2) The transactions described under paragraph (1) of this subsection
include:
(i) Payment, delivery, deposit, or retention of funds or property;
(ii) Sale, mortgage, lease, or other transfer of property;
(iii) Purchase of contracts for an annuity, life care, training, or
education;
(iv) Making the election to take an elective share of an estate subject
to election under § 3–403 of this article; [or]
(V) GRANTING ACCESS TO FINANCIAL RECORDS;
