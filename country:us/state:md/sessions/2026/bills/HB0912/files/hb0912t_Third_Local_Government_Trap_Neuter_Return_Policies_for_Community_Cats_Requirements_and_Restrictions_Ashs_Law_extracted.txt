CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Local Government – Trap–Neuter–Return Policies for Community Cats –
3 Requirements and Restrictions
4 (Ash’s Law)
5 FOR the purpose of establishing that a certain prohibition against abandoning certain
6 provisions of law prohibiting the abandonment or certain neglect of an animal does
7 do not apply to a person who is providing care to a community cat under a certain
8 trap–neuter–return policy; establishing that a local law that establishes a
9 trap–neuter–return policy is subject to certain requirements and restrictions; and
10 generally relating to trap–neuter–return policies for community cats.
11 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
12 Article – Criminal Law
13 Section 10–604 and 10–612
14 Annotated Code of Maryland
15 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
16 BY adding to
17 Article – Local Government
18 Section 1–1321
19 Annotated Code of Maryland
20 (2013 Volume and 2025 Supplement)
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

Underlining indicates amendments to bill.
Strike out indicates matter stricken from the bill by amendment or deleted from the law by
amendment. *hb0912*
2 HOUSE BILL 912
1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – Criminal Law
4 10–604.
5 (a) [A] SUBJECT TO SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION, A person may not:
6 (1) overdrive or overload an animal;
7 (2) deprive an animal of necessary sustenance;
8 (3) inflict unnecessary suffering or pain on an animal;
9 (4) cause, procure, or authorize an act prohibited under item (1), (2), or (3)
10 of this subsection; or
11 (5) if the person has charge or custody of an animal, as owner or otherwise,
12 unnecessarily fail to provide the animal with:
13 (i) nutritious food in sufficient quantity;
14 (ii) necessary veterinary care;
15 (iii) proper drink;
16 (iv) proper air;
17 (v) proper space;
18 (vi) proper shelter; or
19 (vii) proper protection from the weather.
20 (b) A PERSON WHO PROVIDES CARE TO A COMMUNITY CAT UNDER A
21 TRAP–NEUTER–RETURN POLICY IN ACCORDANCE WITH § 1–1321 OF THE LOCAL
22 GOVERNMENT ARTICLE MAY NOT BE DEEMED AS HAVING CHARGE OR CUSTODY OF
23 THE COMMUNITY CAT UNDER SUBSECTION (A)(5) OF THIS SECTION.
24 (C) (1) A person who violates this section is guilty of a misdemeanor and on
25 conviction is subject to imprisonment not exceeding 90 days or a fine not exceeding $1,000
26 or both.
27 (2) As a condition of sentencing, the court may order a defendant convicted
28 of violating this section to:
HOUSE BILL 912 3
1 (i) participate in and pay for psychological counseling; and
2 (ii) pay, in addition to any other fines and costs, all reasonable costs
3 incurred in removing, housing, treating, or euthanizing an animal confiscated from the
4 defendant.
5 (3) As a condition of probation, the court may prohibit a defendant from
6 owning, possessing, or residing with an animal.
7 10–612.
8 (a) THIS SECTION DOES NOT APPLY TO A PERSON WHO IS PROVIDING CARE
9 TO A COMMUNITY CAT UNDER A TRAP–NEUTER–RETURN POLICY IN ACCORDANCE
10 WITH § 1–1321 OF THE LOCAL GOVERNMENT ARTICLE.
11 (B) A person who owns, possesses, or has custody of a domestic animal may not
12 drop or leave the animal on a road, in a public place, or on private property with the intent
13 to abandon the animal.
14 [(b)] (C) A person who violates this section is guilty of a misdemeanor and on
15 conviction is subject to a fine not exceeding $100.
16 Article – Local Government
17 1–1321.
18 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
19 INDICATED.
20 (2) (I) “COMMUNITY CAT” MEANS A FREE–ROAMING CAT THAT
21 MAY BE CARED FOR BY ONE OR MORE RESIDENTS OF THE IMMEDIATE AREA WITHIN
22 WHICH THE CAT IS LOCATED.
23 (II) “COMMUNITY CAT” INCLUDES A FERAL CAT.
24 (3) (I) “COMMUNITY CAT CAREGIVER” MEANS A PERSON WHO
25 PROVIDES CARE TO A COMMUNITY CAT, INCLUDING FOOD, SHELTER, OR MEDICAL
26 CARE, UNDER A TRAP–NEUTER–RETURN POLICY IN ACCORDANCE WITH THIS
27 SECTION.
28 (II) “COMMUNITY CAT CAREGIVER” DOES NOT INCLUDE A
29 PERSON WHO IS CONSIDERED THE OWNER, HARBORER, CONTROLLER, OR KEEPER
30 OF A COMMUNITY CAT.
4 HOUSE BILL 912
1 (4) “EARTIP” MEANS A PROCEDURE THAT:
2 (I) REMOVES THE DISTAL ONE–QUARTER OF THE LEFT EAR OF
3 A COMMUNITY CAT;
4 (II) IS PERFORMED USING ANESTHESIA UNDER THE
5 SUPERVISION OF A LICENSED VETERINARIAN; AND
6 (III) IS DESIGNED TO IDENTIFY A COMMUNITY CAT AS BEING
7 STERILIZED AND VACCINATED FOR RABIES.
8 (5) (I) “TRAP–NEUTER–RETURN” MEANS TO HUMANELY TRAP,
9 STERILIZE, VACCINATE FOR RABIES, EARTIP, AND RETURN A COMMUNITY CAT TO
10 THE LOCATION WHERE THE CAT WAS ORIGINALLY TRAPPED.
11 (II) “TRAP–NEUTER–RETURN” INCLUDES PROVIDING CARE TO
12 A COMMUNITY CAT.
13 (B) THIS SECTION APPLIES TO ALL COUNTIES AND MUNICIPALITIES.
14 (C) A LOCAL LAW THAT ESTABLISHES A TRAP–NEUTER–RETURN POLICY:
15 (1) MAY NOT REQUIRE ANY REGISTRATION OR CERTIFICATION
16 PROCESS FOR A COMMUNITY CAT CAREGIVER;
17 (2) SHALL AUTHORIZE THE TRAPPING OF A COMMUNITY CAT TO
18 STERILIZE, VACCINATE FOR RABIES, AND EARTIP THE COMMUNITY CAT IN
19 ACCORDANCE WITH FEDERAL OR STATE LAW;
20 (3) SHALL AUTHORIZE A COMMUNITY CAT CAREGIVER TO RECLAIM
21 AN IMPOUNDED COMMUNITY CAT WITHOUT PROOF OF OWNERSHIP FOR THE
22 PURPOSE OF:
23 (I) CARRYING OUT A TRAP–NEUTER–RETURN POLICY; OR
24 (II) RETURNING AN EARTIPPED CAT TO THE LOCATION WHERE
25 THE CAT WAS ORIGINALLY TRAPPED;
26 (4) UNLESS VETERINARY CARE IS REQUIRED OR A LOCATION IS
27 DEEMED UNSAFE FOR RETURN OF AN EARTIPPED CAT, SHALL REQUIRE THAT:
28 (I) AN EARTIPPED CAT THAT IS RECEIVED BY A LOCAL SHELTER
29 BE RETURNED TO THE LOCATION WHERE THE CAT WAS ORIGINALLY TRAPPED; AND
HOUSE BILL 912 5
1 (II) AN EARTIPPED CAT THAT IS TRAPPED BE RETURNED
2 ON–SITE; AND
3 (5) SHALL ESTABLISH TRAP–NEUTER–RETURN AS THE PREFERRED
4 DISPOSITION OF AN IMPOUNDED COMMUNITY CAT.
5 (D) THIS SECTION SUPERSEDES AND PREEMPTS ANY LAW, ORDINANCE,
6 POLICY, OR REGULATION OF ANY LOCAL GOVERNMENT BODY, AND ANY POLICY OR
7 RULE OF ANY PERSON OR CORPORATION, THAT PROHIBITS OR RESTRICTS THE
8 PRACTICE OF TRAP–NEUTER–RETURN.
9 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
10 October 1, 2026.
Approved:
