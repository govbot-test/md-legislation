1 (e) A member of the Commission:
2 (1) may not receive compensation as a member of the Commission; but
3 (2) is entitled to reimbursement for expenses under the Standard State
4 Travel Regulations, as provided in the State budget.
5 (f) The Commission shall study and make legislative, regulatory, programmatic,
6 or other recommendations regarding:
7 (1) best practices, standards, and guidelines:
8 (i) to prevent, detect, and suppress lithium–ion battery fires in:
9 1. consumer and transportation applications; and
10 2. utility applications, with review and consideration of the
11 National Fire Prevention Association 855 Standards for Grid Scale Storage and Safety;
12 (ii) to prevent, detect, and suppress lithium–ion fires at recycling
13 facilities;
14 (iii) for reusing, recycling, and decommissioning lithium–ion
15 batteries; and
16 (iv) for collecting, storing, and recycling lithium–ion batteries;
17 (2) the viability of extended producer responsibility for lithium–ion
18 batteries;
19 (3) training, education, and other information to better inform the public
20 and first responders regarding lithium–ion battery safety;
21 (4) battery risks at the Port of Baltimore and on roads, rail, and tunnels
22 throughout Maryland;
23 (5) the impacts of battery risk on insurance providers and insurance
24 coverage availability;
25 (6) the integration of pending federal regulations and emerging
26 third–party standards; and
27 (7) any other global issues the Commission may consider useful for
28 enhancing the safety and reuse of batteries in the State.
29 (g) At the discretion of the Commission, the Commission may seek advice and
30 consultation from outside individuals and entities.
