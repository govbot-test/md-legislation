CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Correctional Services – Restrictive Housing – Individuals With Developmental
3 or Intellectual Disabilities
4 FOR the purpose of authorizing requiring a certain clinical professional to conduct a certain
5 assessment for an incarcerated individual at intake at a State correctional facility;
6 authorizing a certain clinical professional to make a certain recommendation about
7 a certain incarcerated individual; prohibiting a State correctional facility from
8 putting a certain incarcerated individual in restrictive housing for more than a
9 certain number of days unless the individual commits a certain prohibited act; and
10 generally relating to restrictive housing.
11 BY adding to
12 Article – Correctional Services
13 Section 9–614.3
14 Annotated Code of Maryland
15 (2025 Replacement Volume)
16 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
17 Article – Health – General
18 Section 7–101(a), (g), and (n)
19 Annotated Code of Maryland
20 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

2 HOUSE BILL 310
1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – Correctional Services
4 9–614.3.
5 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
6 INDICATED.
7 (2) “CLINICAL PROFESSIONAL” MEANS AN INDIVIDUAL WHO IS
8 TRAINED AND OR LICENSED IN MENTAL HEALTH.
9 (3) “DEVELOPMENTAL DISABILITY” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN §
10 7–101 OF THE HEALTH – GENERAL ARTICLE.
11 (4) “INTELLECTUAL DISABILITY” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN §
12 7–101 OF THE HEALTH – GENERAL ARTICLE.
13 (5) “PROHIBITED ACT” INCLUDES:
14 (I) CAUSING OR ATTEMPTING TO CAUSE SERIOUS PHYSICAL
15 INJURY TO OR DEATH OF ANOTHER PERSON;
16 (II) MAKING AN IMMINENT THREAT OF SERIOUS PHYSICAL
17 INJURY OR DEATH TO ANOTHER PERSON WHEN:
18 1. THE INDIVIDUAL MAKING THE THREAT HAS A
19 HISTORY OF CAUSING PHYSICAL INJURY OR DEATH; AND
20 2. THE COMMISSIONER OF CORRECTION REASONABLY
21 DETERMINES THAT THERE IS A STRONG LIKELIHOOD THAT THE PERSON WILL CARRY
22 OUT A THREAT OF SERIOUS PHYSICAL INJURY OR DEATH;
23 (III) COMPELLING OR ATTEMPTING TO COMPEL ANOTHER
24 PERSON, BY FORCE OR THREAT OF FORCE, TO ENGAGE IN A SEXUAL ACT;
25 (IV) EXTORTING ANOTHER, BY FORCE OR THREAT OF FORCE,
26 FOR PROPERTY OR MONEY;
27 (V) COERCING ANOTHER, BY FORCE OR THREAT OF FORCE, TO
28 VIOLATE A RULE;
HOUSE BILL 310 3
1 (VI) LEADING, ORGANIZING, INCITING, OR ATTEMPTING TO
2 CAUSE A RIOT, AN INSURRECTION, OR ANY OTHER SIMILARLY SERIOUS
3 DISTURBANCE THAT RESULTS IN THE TAKING OF A HOSTAGE, MAJOR PROPERTY
4 DAMAGE, OR PHYSICAL HARM TO ANOTHER PERSON;
5 (VII) PROCURING DEADLY WEAPONS OR OTHER DANGEROUS
6 CONTRABAND THAT POSE A SERIOUS THREAT TO THE SECURITY OF THE
7 INSTITUTION; AND
8 (VIII) ESCAPING, ATTEMPTING TO ESCAPE, OR FACILITATING AN
9 ESCAPE FROM A CORRECTIONAL FACILITY OR ESCAPING OR ATTEMPTING TO
10 ESCAPE WHILE UNDER SUPERVISION OUTSIDE A CORRECTIONAL FACILITY.
11 (6) (I) “RESTRICTIVE HOUSING” MEANS A FORM OF PHYSICAL
12 SEPARATION THAT HAS NOT BEEN REQUESTED BY THE INCARCERATED INDIVIDUAL
13 IN WHICH THE INCARCERATED INDIVIDUAL IS PLACED IN A LOCKED ROOM OR CELL
14 FOR APPROXIMATELY 22 HOURS OR MORE OUT OF A 24–HOUR PERIOD.
15 (II) “RESTRICTIVE HOUSING” INCLUDES ADMINISTRATIVE
16 SEGREGATION AND DISCIPLINARY SEGREGATION.
17 (III) “RESTRICTIVE HOUSING” DOES NOT INCLUDE:
18 1. MEDICAL ISOLATION;
19 2. MENTAL–HEALTH OBSERVATION OR CRISIS
20 STABILIZATION;
21 3. SUICIDE WATCH;
22 4. PROTECTIVE CUSTODY REQUESTED BY THE
23 INCARCERATED INDIVIDUAL; OR
24 5. QUARANTINE OR PUBLIC–HEALTH ISOLATION.
25 (B) THIS SECTION APPLIES TO A STATE CORRECTIONAL FACILITY.
26 (C) (1) DURING THE INTAKE ASSESSMENT MEDICAL SCREENING
27 CONDUCTED ON ADMISSION TO A STATE CORRECTIONAL FACILITY FOR A NEW
28 INCARCERATED INDIVIDUAL, AN APPLICABLE CLINICAL PROFESSIONAL MAY SHALL,
29 AS PART OF THE STANDARD INTAKE SCREENING PROCESS, ASSESS THE
30 INCARCERATED INDIVIDUAL FOR INDICATORS OF A DEVELOPMENTAL DISABILITY
31 OR AN INTELLECTUAL DISABILITY.
4 HOUSE BILL 310
1 (2) (I) BASED ON THE ASSESSMENT UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF
2 THIS SUBSECTION, THE CLINICAL PROFESSIONAL MAY RECOMMEND TO THE
3 CORRECTIONAL FACILITY THAT THE INCARCERATED INDIVIDUAL SHOULD NOT BE
4 PLACED IN RESTRICTIVE HOUSING FOR MORE THAN 15 CONSECUTIVE DAYS IN A
5 30–DAY PERIOD.
6 (II) IF A CLINICAL PROFESSIONAL MAKES A RECOMMENDATION
7 DESCRIBED IN SUBPARAGRAPH (I) OF THIS PARAGRAPH, THE CORRECTIONAL
8 FACILITY MAY NOT PLACE AN INCARCERATED INDIVIDUAL IN RESTRICTIVE
9 HOUSING FOR MORE THAN 15 CONSECUTIVE DAYS IN A 30–DAY PERIOD.
10 (D) THE 15 CONSECUTIVE DAY LIMIT ON RESTRICTIVE HOUSING UNDER
11 THIS SECTION DOES NOT APPLY TO AN INCARCERATED INDIVIDUAL WHO COMMITS
12 A PROHIBITED ACT.
13 Article – Health – General
14 7–101.
15 (a) In this title the following words have the meanings indicated.
16 (g) “Developmental disability” means a severe chronic disability of an individual
17 that:
18 (1) Is attributable to a physical or mental impairment, other than the sole
19 diagnosis of mental illness, or to a combination of mental and physical impairments;
20 (2) Is manifested before the individual attains the age of 22;
21 (3) Is likely to continue indefinitely;
22 (4) Results in an inability to live independently without external support
23 or continuing and regular assistance; and
24 (5) Reflects the need for a combination and sequence of special,
25 interdisciplinary, or generic care, treatment, or other services that are individually planned
26 and coordinated for the individual.
27 (n) “Intellectual disability” means a developmental disability that is evidenced by
28 significantly subaverage intellectual functioning and impairment in the adaptive behavior
29 of an individual.
30 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
31 October 1, 2026.
