8 and generally relating to standards and procedures under the Chesapeake and
9 Atlantic Coastal Bays Critical Area Protection Program.

17 OF MARYLAND, That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
18 Article – Natural Resources
19 8–1802.
20 (a) (1) In this subtitle the following words have the meanings indicated.
21 (2) “ADJACENT” MEANS, WITH RESPECT TO AREAS WITH DIFFERENT
22 LAND CLASSIFICATIONS:
23 (I) SHARING A COMMON BOUNDARY LINE; OR
24 (II) SEPARATED BY A PUBLIC OR PRIVATE STREET, ROAD,
25 RIGHT–OF–WAY, OR OTHER UTILITY OR ACCESS EASEMENT.
26 8–1806.
27 (a) The Commission has all powers necessary for carrying out the purposes of this
28 subtitle, including the following:
29 (1) In accordance with Title 2, Subtitle 5 (Joint Committee on
30 Administrative, Executive and Legislative Review) and Title 10, Subtitle 1 (Administrative
31 Procedure Act) of the State Government Article, to adopt and amend regulations BY
32 AFFIRMATIVE VOTE OF THE COMMISSION AND as authorized under this subtitle for the
33 administration and enforcement of the State and local programs;
HOUSE BILL 258 3
1 (2) To conduct hearings in connection with policies, proposed programs,
2 and proposed regulations or amendments to regulations; and
3 (3) To contract for consultant or other services.
4 (b) Regulations adopted or amended under subsection (a)(1) of this section shall:
5 (1) Establish comprehensive standards and procedures for:
6 (xiv) Directives for local program development and implementation,
7 with respect to:
8 2. The [6–year] 10–YEAR comprehensive review of a local
9 critical area program;
10 8–1808.1.
11 (c) (2) When locating new intensely developed or limited development areas,
12 local jurisdictions shall use the following standards:
13 (i) Locate a new intensely developed area in a limited development
14 area or [adjacent] CONTIGUOUS AND ADJOINING to an existing intensely developed area;
15 (ii) Locate a new limited development area [adjacent] CONTIGUOUS
16 AND ADJOINING to an existing limited development area or an intensely developed area;
17 (iii) Locate a new limited development area or an intensely developed
18 area in a manner that minimizes impacts to a habitat protection area as defined in COMAR
19 27.01.09, and in an area and manner that optimizes benefits to water quality;
20 (iv) Locate a new intensely developed area or a limited development
21 area in a resource conservation area at least 300 feet beyond the landward edge of tidal
22 wetlands or tidal waters, unless the local jurisdiction proposes, and the Commission
23 approves, alternative measures for enhancement of water quality and habitat that provide
24 greater benefits to the resources;
25 (v) Locate new intensely developed areas and limited development
26 areas in a manner that minimizes their impacts to the defined land uses of the resource
27 conservation area;
28 (vi) Locate new intensely developed areas and limited development
29 areas outside of areas vulnerable to climate change unless the local jurisdiction proposes
30 and the Commission approves:
31 1. Areas identified by the local jurisdiction as vulnerable to
32 climate change as required under § 8–1808(c)(1)(iii)16 of this subtitle; and
4 HOUSE BILL 258
1 2. Measures that:
2 A. Assess climate resiliency and vulnerability; and
3 B. Incorporate siting, design, construction, and other natural
4 features to significantly enhance climate resiliency and reduce vulnerability;
5 (vii) Except as provided in item (ix) of this paragraph, no more than
6 one–half of the expansion allocated in the criteria of the Commission may be located in
7 resource conservation areas;
8 (viii) New intensely developed or limited development areas involving
9 the use of growth allocation shall conform to all criteria of the Commission and shall be
10 designated on the comprehensive zoning map submitted by the local jurisdiction as part of
11 its application to the Commission for program approval or at a later date in compliance
12 with § 8–1809(g) of this subtitle; and
13 (ix) In Calvert, Caroline, Cecil, Charles, Dorchester, Kent, Queen
14 Anne’s, St. Mary’s, Somerset, Talbot, Wicomico, and Worcester counties, if the county is
15 unable to utilize a portion of the growth allocated to the county in items (i) and (ii) of this
16 paragraph within or [adjacent] CONTIGUOUS AND ADJOINING to existing intensely
17 developed or limited development areas as demonstrated in the local plan approved by the
18 Commission, then that portion of the allocated expansion which cannot be so located may
19 be located in the resource conservation area in addition to the expansion allocated in item
20 (vii) of this paragraph. A developer shall be required to cluster any development in an area
21 of expansion authorized under this paragraph.
22 8–1809.
23 (j) (1) As often as necessary but not more than 4 times per calendar year, each
24 local jurisdiction may propose program amendments and program refinements to its
25 adopted program.
26 (2) A change to a critical area designation may be granted by a local
27 approving authority on proof of mistake if the proposed critical area classification:
28 (i) Conforms to the State critical area mapping criteria;
29 (ii) 1. Is based on land uses or natural features in existence [as]:
30 A. AS of December 1, 1985, FOR THE CHESAPEAKE BAY
31 CRITICAL AREA; or
32 B. AS OF JUNE 1, 2002, FOR THE ATLANTIC COASTAL
33 BAYS CRITICAL AREA; OR
HOUSE BILL 258 5
1 2. For areas included in the critical area due to remapping,
2 is based on land uses or natural features in existence at the time of the remapping;
3 (iii) Follows the local jurisdiction’s documented mapping
4 methodology for critical area classifications at the time of original program adoption; and
5 (iv) Is consistent with the purposes, policies, and goals of this subtitle
6 and all criteria of the Commission.
7 (r) (1) Proposed program refinements shall be determined as provided in this
8 subsection.
9 (2) (i) Within 30 days of the Commission’s acceptance of a proposal to
10 change an adopted program, the chair, on behalf of the Commission, may determine that
11 the proposed change is a program refinement.
12 (ii) The chair shall notify the Commission of that determination at
13 the next meeting or a subsequent meeting if the local jurisdiction requests, and the chair
14 approves, an extension.
15 (iii) If a proposed change that was specifically submitted as a
16 program refinement is not acted on by the chair within the 30–day period UNDER
17 SUBPARAGRAPH (I) OF THIS PARAGRAPH OR APPROVED FOR AN EXTENSION UNDER
18 SUBPARAGRAPH (II) OF THIS PARAGRAPH, the Commission shall notify the appropriate
19 local jurisdiction that the proposed change has been deemed to be a program amendment.
20 (3) (i) The Commission may vote to override the chair’s determination
21 only at the first Commission meeting where a quorum is present following the chair’s
22 notification to the Commission.
23 (ii) If the chair’s determination is overridden, the proposed change
24 is deemed a program amendment, which shall be decided by the Commission in accordance
25 with the procedures for program amendments provided in this section, except that the
26 Commission shall act on the program amendment within [90] 130 days after a vote to
27 override the chair.
28 (iii) If the chair’s determination is not overridden, within 10 working
29 days after the opportunity to override the chair’s decision under subparagraph (i) of this
30 paragraph, the chair shall:
31 1. Determine if the program refinement is consistent with
32 the purposes, policies, goals, and provisions of this subtitle, and all criteria of the
33 Commission; and
34 2. A. Approve the proposed program refinement and
35 notify the local jurisdiction;
6 HOUSE BILL 258
1 B. Deny the program refinement;
2 C. Approve the proposed program refinement subject to one
3 or more conditions; or
4 D. Return the proposed program refinement back to the local
5 jurisdiction with a list of the changes to be made.
6 (iv) If the chair approves a proposed program refinement subject to
7 one or more conditions under subparagraph (iii)2C of this paragraph, the local jurisdiction
8 shall notify the Commission within 60 days of its intent to adopt the conditions.
9 (4) A local jurisdiction shall incorporate an approved program refinement
10 and any required conditions into its adopted program within 120 days of receiving notice
11 from the chair that the program refinement has been approved.
12 8–1815.1.
13 (b) Subject to subsection (f) of this section, if a person cuts or clears or plans to
14 cut or clear trees within the Chesapeake Bay Critical Area or Atlantic Coastal Bays Critical
15 Area in violation of an approved local critical area program or of regulations adopted by the
16 Commission, the chair may bring an action, or the local jurisdiction may bring an action or
17 request that the chair of the Commission refer the matter to the Attorney General to bring
18 an action:
19 (1) To require the person to replant trees where the cutting or clearing
20 occurred in accordance with a plan prepared by the State Forester, a [registered
21 professional] LICENSED forester, [or] a [registered] LICENSED landscape architect, OR
22 ANY OTHER QUALIFIED PROFESSIONAL APPROVED BY THE DEPARTMENT;
23 (2) To restrain the planned violation; or
24 (3) For damages:
25 (i) To be assessed by a circuit court in an amount equal to the
26 estimated cost of replanting trees; and
27 (ii) To be paid to the [Department] LOCAL JURISDICTION by the
28 person found to have violated the provisions of this subsection.
29 (e) On the request of a local jurisdiction or the chair of the Commission, the State
30 Forester, a [registered professional] LICENSED forester, [or] a [registered] LICENSED
31 landscape architect, OR ANY OTHER QUALIFIED PROFESSIONAL APPROVED BY THE
32 DEPARTMENT may prepare, oversee, and approve the final implementation of a plan to:
HOUSE BILL 258 7
1 (1) Replant trees in any part of the Chesapeake Bay Critical Area where
2 trees in the Chesapeake Bay Critical Area are cut or cleared in violation of subsection (b)
3 of this section; and
4 (2) Replant trees in any part of the Atlantic Coastal Bays Critical Area
5 where trees in the Atlantic Coastal Bays Critical Area are cut or cleared in violation of
6 subsection (b) of this section.
7 SECTION 2. 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
8 October 1, 2026.
