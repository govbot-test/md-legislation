1 AN ACT concerning
2 Law Enforcement Officers’ Pension System – Military Service Credit Eligibility
3 – Helicopter Pilots
4 FOR the purpose of authorizing certain members of the Law Enforcement Officers’ Pension
5 System assigned as helicopter pilots in a State law enforcement aviation unit to
6 receive credit for certain military service if the member receives military service
7 credit in another retirement system; prohibiting an individual from receiving credit
8 more than a certain number of times; and generally relating to members of the Law
9 Enforcement Officers’ Pension System receiving military service credit.
10 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
11 Article – State Personnel and Pensions
12 Section 38–104
13 Annotated Code of Maryland
14 (2024 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
15 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
16 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
17 Article – State Personnel and Pensions
18 38–104.
19 (a) (1) This section applies to an individual who is:

1 (i) a member of a State system; or
2 (ii) a former member of a State system who is eligible to receive a
3 benefit under § 29–302 or § 29–303 of this article.
4 (2) This section does not apply to a retiree of a State system.
5 (b) Except as provided in [subsection (c)] SUBSECTIONS (C) AND (E) of this
6 section, an individual described in subsection (a) of this section, who has not met the
7 conditions set forth in § 38–103(a)(2) through (4) of this subtitle, is entitled to receive
8 service credit for military service only on the attainment of 10 years of service credit earned
9 through employment as a member of a State system.
10 (c) (1) An individual described in subsection (a) of this section may not receive
11 credit for that military service if the individual receives credit for military service from
12 another retirement system for which retirement benefits have been or will be received by
13 the individual.
14 (2) Paragraph (1) of this subsection does not apply to:
15 (i) credit for military service provided under:
16 1. the Social Security Act;
17 2. the National Railroad Retirement Act; or
18 3. Title 3 or Title 10, Chapter 1223, §§ 12731 through 12737
19 of the United States Code; [or]
20 (ii) disability payments from a pension or retirement system; OR
21 (III) A MEMBER OR FORMER MEMBER OF THE LAW
22 ENFORCEMENT OFFICERS’ PENSION SYSTEM WHO IS OR WAS ASSIGNED AS A
23 HELICOPTER PILOT IN A STATE LAW ENFORCEMENT AVIATION UNIT.
24 (3) (i) Military service credit under this section may not exceed 5 years.
25 (ii) Subject to subparagraph (i) of this paragraph, a member of the
26 Maryland National Guard or of a reserve component of the armed forces of the United
27 States who has not been activated under Title 10 of the United States Code, shall receive
28 service credit at the rate of 4 months for each full year of service with the Maryland
29 National Guard or with a reserve component of the armed forces of the United States, not
30 to exceed a total of 36 months.
SENATE BILL 491 3
1 (iii) Subject to subparagraph (i) of this paragraph, an individual shall
2 receive service credit at the rate of 4 months for each full year of attendance at a United
3 States service academy, not to exceed a total of 16 months.
4 (d) (1) Except as provided in paragraph (2) of this subsection, the service credit
5 for military service that an individual receives under this section shall be applied to the
6 individual’s retirement allowance using the accrual rate in effect at the time the individual
7 retires from a State system.
8 (2) If an individual applies for military service credit on or after July 1,
9 2011, the service credit for military service that the individual receives under this section
10 shall be applied to the individual’s retirement allowance using the accrual rate in effect at
11 the time the individual submits an application for military service credit to the State
12 Retirement Agency.
13 (E) AN INDIVIDUAL MAY NOT RECEIVE SERVICE CREDIT UNDER THIS
14 SECTION MORE THAN ONCE.
15 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
16 1, 2026.
