A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Criminal Law – Homicide by Motor Vehicle or Vessel – Restitution to Child
3 (Bentley’s Law)
4 FOR the purpose of requiring a person to pay certain restitution to a child if the person
5 violated the prohibition against operating a motor vehicle or vessel while impaired
6 or under the influence resulting in the death of the child’s parent or guardian;
7 establishing requirements and procedures for the determination and payment of
8 restitution under this Act; and generally relating to restitution.
9 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
10 Article – Criminal Law
11 Section 2–503(a), 2–504(a), 2–505(a), and 2–506(a)
12 Annotated Code of Maryland
13 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
14 BY adding to
15 Article – Criminal Procedure
16 Section 11–606.1
17 Annotated Code of Maryland
18 (2025 Replacement Volume)

24 negligently driving, operating, or controlling a motor vehicle or vessel while:

16 Article – Criminal Procedure
17 11–606.1.
18 (A) SUBJECT TO SUBSECTION (D) OF THIS SECTION, THE COURT SHALL
19 ORDER AN INDIVIDUAL CONVICTED OF A VIOLATION OF AN OFFENSE UNDER TITLE
20 2, SUBTITLE 5 OF THE CRIMINAL LAW ARTICLE RESULTING IN THE DEATH OF A
21 PARENT OR GUARDIAN OF A CHILD TO PAY RESTITUTION TO THE CHILD.
22 (B) (1) IN DETERMINING THE AMOUNT OF RESTITUTION ORDERED
23 UNDER SUBSECTION (A) OF THIS SECTION, THE COURT SHALL CONSIDER THE
24 CHILD’S:
25 (I) FINANCIAL NEEDS AND RESOURCES;
26 (II) STANDARD OF LIVING;
27 (III) PHYSICAL AND EMOTIONAL CONDITION AND NEEDS; AND
28 (IV) PHYSICAL AND LEGAL CUSTODY ARRANGEMENTS.

1 (2) IN ADDITION TO THE CONSIDERATIONS UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF

8 (C) (1) SUBJECT TO PARAGRAPH (2) OF THIS SUBSECTION, THE FULL
9 AMOUNT OF RESTITUTION ORDERED BY THE COURT SHALL BE DIVIDED INTO

12 GRADUATED FROM A SECONDARY SCHOOL; OR
13 (II) THE MAJORITY OF THE CLASS OF WHICH THE CHILD IS A
14 MEMBER WHEN THE CHILD REACHES THE AGE OF 18 YEARS HAS GRADUATED FROM

16 (2) (I) SUBJECT TO SUBPARAGRAPH (II) OF THIS PARAGRAPH, IF
17 AN INDIVIDUAL ORDERED TO PAY RESTITUTION UNDER THIS SECTION IS
18 INCARCERATED AND UNABLE TO PAY, PAYMENT SHALL BEGIN WITHIN 1 YEAR AFTER
19 THE INDIVIDUAL’S RELEASE FROM INCARCERATION.
20 (II) IF THE PERIOD OF RESTITUTION UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF
21 THIS SUBSECTION ENDS WHILE THE INDIVIDUAL ORDERED TO PAY RESTITUTION IS
22 INCARCERATED AND THE RESTITUTION IS NOT PAID IN FULL, PAYMENT UNDER THIS
23 SUBSECTION SHALL CONTINUE UNTIL THE RESTITUTION IS PAID IN FULL.
24 (D) (1) IF THE SURVIVING PARENT OR GUARDIAN OF A CHILD OF THE
25 VICTIM BRINGS AN ACTION AGAINST THE INDIVIDUAL SEEKING DAMAGES BEFORE
26 AN ORDER OF RESTITUTION IS ENTERED UNDER THIS SECTION, AND THE PARENT OR
27 GUARDIAN OBTAINS A FAVORABLE JUDGMENT, THE COURT MAY NOT ORDER
28 RESTITUTION.
29 (2) IF THE SURVIVING PARENT OR GUARDIAN OF A CHILD OF THE
30 VICTIM BRINGS AN ACTION AGAINST THE INDIVIDUAL AFTER AN ORDER OF
31 RESTITUTION IS ENTERED UNDER THIS SECTION, AND THE PARENT OR GUARDIAN
32 OBTAINS A FAVORABLE JUDGMENT, THE COURT SHALL OFFSET THE RESTITUTION
33 BY THE AMOUNT OF THE FAVORABLE JUDGMENT.
