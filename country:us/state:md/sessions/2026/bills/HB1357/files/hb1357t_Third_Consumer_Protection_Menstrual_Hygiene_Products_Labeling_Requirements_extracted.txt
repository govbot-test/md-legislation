1 AN ACT concerning
2 Consumer Protection – Menstrual Hygiene Products – Labeling Requirements
3 FOR the purpose of requiring manufacturers of menstrual hygiene products to include a
4 list of ingredients on each package of menstrual hygiene products sold in the State;
5 and generally relating to labeling of ingredients of menstrual hygiene products.
6 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
7 Article – Commercial Law
8 Section 13–301(14)(xlvii)

2 HOUSE BILL 1357
1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – Commercial Law
4 13–301.
5 Unfair, abusive, or deceptive trade practices include any:
6 (14) Violation of a provision of:
7 (xlvii) Title 14, Subtitle 50 of this article; [or]
8 (xlviii) Section 13–411.1(c)(2) of the Transportation Article; or
9 (XLIX) SECTION 14–1330 OF THIS ARTICLE; OR
10 14–1330.
11 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
12 INDICATED.
13 (2) “DIVISION” MEANS THE DIVISION OF CONSUMER PROTECTION
14 OF THE OFFICE OF THE ATTORNEY GENERAL.
15 (2) (3) “INGREDIENT” MEANS ANY INTENTIONALLY ADDED
16 SUBSTANCE IN A MENSTRUAL HYGIENE PRODUCT THAT SERVES A TECHNICAL OR
17 FUNCTIONAL PURPOSE.
18 (3) (4) “MENSTRUAL HYGIENE PRODUCT” MEANS A DISPOSABLE
19 OR REUSABLE PRODUCT USED FOR THE PURPOSE OF ABSORBING OR CONTAINING
20 MENSTRUAL BLOOD AND TISSUE MENSTRUATION OR VAGINAL DISCHARGE,
21 INCLUDING SANITARY PADS, TAMPONS, AND MENSTRUAL CUPS.
22 (B) (1) ON OR BEFORE APRIL 1, 2028, A A MANUFACTURER OF
23 MENSTRUAL HYGIENE PRODUCTS SHALL INCLUDE A LIST OF ALL INGREDIENTS
24 LISTED IN ORDER OF PREDOMINANCE AND DISPLAYED PROMINENTLY IN
25 CONSPICUOUS TYPE ON EACH CONSUMER–FACING PACKAGE OF MENSTRUAL
26 HYGIENE PRODUCTS SOLD IN THE STATE.
27 (2) A MANUFACTURER MAY LIST A PROPRIETARY INGREDIENT OR
28 COMBINATION OF INGREDIENTS BY THEIR COMMON NAME TO PROTECT
29 CONFIDENTIALITY.
HOUSE BILL 1357 3
1 (C) A VIOLATION OF THIS SECTION IS:
2 (1) AN UNFAIR OR DECEPTIVE TRADE PRACTICE WITHIN THE
3 MEANING OF TITLE 13 OF THIS ARTICLE; AND
4 (2) SUBJECT TO THE ENFORCEMENT AND PENALTY PROVISIONS
5 CONTAINED IN TITLE 13 OF THIS ARTICLE.
6 (C) (1) A MANUFACTURER THAT VIOLATES THIS SECTION IS SUBJECT TO
7 A CIVIL PENALTY NOT EXCEEDING $1,000.
8 (2) THE DIVISION SHALL PAY ALL PENALTIES COLLECTED BY THE
9 DIVISION UNDER THIS SUBSECTION INTO THE GENERAL FUND OF THE STATE WITH
10 THE INTENT THAT THE PENALTIES BE USED TO FULLY OFFSET ANY COSTS INCURRED
11 BY THE DIVISION IN CONNECTION WITH THE ENFORCEMENT OF THIS SECTION.
12 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
13 October 1, 2026.
Approved:
