1 AN ACT concerning
2 Baltimore City – Alcoholic Beverages – Related Event Promoter’s Permit
3 FOR the purpose of establishing a related event promoter’s permit in Baltimore City for
4 the Kappa Alpha Psi 2027 Conclave; authorizing the Board of License
5 Commissioners for Baltimore City to issue a related event promoter’s permit to
6 certain applicants under certain circumstances; requiring a holder of a State
7 caterer’s license to obtain a certain Class C per diem license from the Board before
8 the holder may act as a participating license holder at a related event; and generally
9 relating to a related event promoter’s permit in Baltimore City.
10 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
11 That:
12 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
13 (2) “Board” means the Board of License Commissioners for Baltimore City.
14 (3) “Kappa Alpha Psi Conclave” means the Kappa Alpha Psi Fraternity,
15 Inc. 2027 Conclave and 88th grand chapter meeting for Kappa Alpha Psi Fraternity, Inc.
16 (4) “License holder” means the holder of an alcoholic beverages license
17 issued by the Board of License Commissioners for Baltimore City.
18 (5) (i) “Related event” means an event related to the Kappa Alpha Psi
19 Conclave in which:

6 (ii) “Related event” includes an event for which tickets are sold to

11 (b) (1) A related event promoter or participating license holder on behalf of a
12 related event promoter shall apply for a permit from the Board before the related event
13 promoter may publicize, sell tickets for, organize, operate, produce, or stage a related event.
14 (2) A holder of a State caterer’s license shall obtain a Class C per diem beer,
15 beer and wine, or beer, wine, and liquor license from the Board before the holder may act
16 as a participating license holder at a related event.
17 (c) (1) Except as provided in paragraph (2) of this subsection, the Board may
18 grant the permit to an applicant who submits an application to the Board as provided under
19 Title 4 of the Alcoholic Beverages and Cannabis Article at least 30 days before the date of
20 the related event.
21 (2) Before being granted the permit, an applicant shall:
22 (i) obtain written consent from a designee of Visit Baltimore;
23 (ii) if required based on the type of premises to be used:
24 1. obtain a special event permit from the Baltimore City
25 Department of Transportation; and
26 2. provide a copy of the special event permit to the Board;
27 and
28 (iii) provide a completed application that:
29 1. is dated and notarized, and signed by each license holder
30 that will participate in the related event;
31 2. lists each premises for which the related event will be held;

1 3. is accompanied by any other document that the Board
2 requires.
3 (3) An individual who applies for and is issued the permit is not required
4 to be a resident of or registered to vote in Baltimore City.
5 (4) Within 7 days after receiving an application, the Board shall grant or
6 deny the permit or request more information from the applicant.
7 (5) A permit may not be altered within 14 days before the
8 related event is scheduled to take place.
9 (d) The permit authorizes the related event promoter and participating license
10 holder to conduct a related event.
11 (e) The permit for the related event may be in effect for the time stated on the
12 special event permit required under subsection (c)(2) of this section.
13 (f) The Board may adopt regulations establishing requirements for:
14 (1) conducting a related event, including health and safety standards to be
15 met by the related event promoter and participating license holder; and
16 (2) providing public notice of a related event at the premises of
17 participating license holders by the related event promoter or participating license holders.
18 (g) (1) The application fee is $50, payable on the submission of the application.
19 (2) The permit fee, payable when the permit is granted, is:
20 (i) $500, if the Baltimore City Fire Marshal has determined that the
21 maximum capacity for the proposed location is less than 300 persons; or
22 (ii) $1,500, if the Baltimore City Fire Marshal has determined that
23 the maximum capacity for the proposed location is 300 or more persons.
24 (h) For the offense of publicizing, selling tickets or imposing a cover charge for,
25 organizing, operating, producing, facilitating, or staging a related event with the knowledge
26 or a reason to know that a related event promoter’s permit required under this section has
27 not been obtained, the Board shall impose a fine of not less than $1,000 and not more than
28 $3,000 or suspend the license or both.
29 (i) For the offense by a holder of a State caterer’s license of participating in a
30 related event without first obtaining a Class C per diem beer, beer and wine, or beer, wine,
31 and liquor license as required under this section, the Executive Director of the Alcohol,
