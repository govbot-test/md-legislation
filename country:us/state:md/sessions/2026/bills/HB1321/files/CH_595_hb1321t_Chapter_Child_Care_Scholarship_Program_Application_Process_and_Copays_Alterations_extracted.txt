WES MOORE, Governor Ch. 595
Chapter 595
(House Bill 1321)
AN ACT concerning
Child Care Scholarship Program – Enrollment Application Process and Copays
– Alterations
FOR the purpose of prohibiting an enrollment freeze in the Child Care Scholarship
Program from applying to certain individuals; requiring the State Department of
Education to establish a waitlist for the Program under certain conditions; requiring
the Department to give priority for enrollment in the Program to certain individuals
on the waitlist; requiring the Department to identify, inform, and assist requiring
the State Department of Education to identify, inform, and assist Child Care
Scholarship Program applicants who qualify for enrollment in publicly funded
prekindergarten, Head Start, and Early Head Start subject to certain conditions;
repealing a process for presumptive eligibility in the Program; limiting copays for
certain individuals under the Program subject to certain conditions; requiring copays
for certain individuals under the Program subject to certain conditions; and
generally relating to the Child Care Scholarship Program.

SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That, in order to fulfill the
requirement to share information about publicly funded prekindergarten slots with
applicants to the Child Care Scholarship Program under § 9.5–111(e) of the Education
Article, as enacted by Section 1 of this Act, the State Department of Education:
(1) may, before the date on which the Department implements the Early
Childhood Pre–K Enrollment System for publicly funded prekindergarten, provide
information on available publicly funded prekindergarten slots in the county in which the
applicant resides as of the most recent enrollment count; and
(2) shall, beginning on the date the Department implements the Early
Childhood Pre–K Enrollment System for publicly funded prekindergarten, provide
up–to–date information on available publicly funded prekindergarten slots in the county in
which the applicant resides.
SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That:
(a) If the State Department of Education implements a system of copayments for
the Child Care Scholarship Program in accordance with § 9.5–113(d) of the Education
Article, as enacted by Section 1 of this Act, the Department shall provide ample notice of
incoming copayments, notifying current scholarship recipients and the child care providers
that accept the scholarships multiple times, beginning with a first notice at least 90 days
before a system of copayments is implemented.
(b) (1) On or before December 1, 2027, the State Department of Education
shall report to the General Assembly, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government
Article, on a system of copayments for the Child Care Scholarship Program adopted in
accordance with § 9.5–113(d) of the Education Article, as enacted by Section 1 of this Act.
(2) The report shall include information on:
(i) income bands used to generate copayments;
(ii) the number of scholarships in each income band; and
(iii) the average copayment in each income band.
SECTION 4. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That:
(a) The State Department of Education shall monitor the waitlist for the Child
Care Scholarship Program in effect on July 1, 2026.
