CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Health Insurance – Ovarian Cancer Prevention With Salpingectomy – Required
3 Coverage and Prohibited Cost Sharing
4 FOR the purpose of requiring certain insurers, nonprofit health service plans, and health
5 maintenance organizations to provide individuals with coverage for opportunistic
6 salpingectomy coverage for ovarian cancer prevention; prohibiting the insurers,
7 nonprofit health service plans, and health maintenance organizations from imposing
8 a copayment, coinsurance, or deductible requirement on the salpingectomy coverage;
9 and generally relating to insurance coverage for salpingectomies for ovarian cancer
10 prevention.

2 HOUSE BILL 633
1 (A) THIS SECTION APPLIES TO:
2 (1) INSURERS AND NONPROFIT HEALTH SERVICE PLANS THAT
3 PROVIDE HOSPITAL, MEDICAL, OR SURGICAL BENEFITS TO INDIVIDUALS OR GROUPS
4 ON AN EXPENSE–INCURRED BASIS UNDER HEALTH INSURANCE POLICIES THAT ARE
5 ISSUED OR DELIVERED IN THE STATE; AND
6 (2) HEALTH MAINTENANCE ORGANIZATIONS THAT PROVIDE
7 HOSPITAL, MEDICAL, OR SURGICAL BENEFITS TO INDIVIDUALS OR GROUPS UNDER
8 CONTRACTS THAT ARE ISSUED OR DELIVERED IN THE STATE.
9 (B) AN ENTITY SUBJECT TO THIS SECTION SHALL PROVIDE AN INDIVIDUAL
10 WITH COVERAGE FOR OPPORTUNISTIC SALPINGECTOMY COVERAGE FOR OVARIAN
11 CANCER PREVENTION.
12 (C) (1) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN PARAGRAPH (2) OF THIS SUBSECTION,
13 AN ENTITY SUBJECT TO THIS SECTION MAY NOT IMPOSE A COPAYMENT,
14 COINSURANCE, OR DEDUCTIBLE REQUIREMENT ON SALPINGECTOMY COVERAGE
15 FOR OVARIAN CANCER PREVENTION.
16 (2) IF AN INSURED OR ENROLLEE IS COVERED UNDER A
17 HIGH–DEDUCTIBLE HEALTH PLAN, AS DEFINED IN 26 U.S.C. § 223, AN ENTITY
18 SUBJECT TO THIS SECTION MAY SUBJECT SALPINGECTOMY COVERAGE FOR
19 OVARIAN CANCER PREVENTION TO THE DEDUCTIBLE REQUIREMENT OF THE
20 HIGH–DEDUCTIBLE PLAN.
21 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That Section 1 of this Act shall
22 apply to all policies, contracts, and health benefit plans issued, delivered, or renewed in the
23 State on or after January 1, 2027.
24 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That, on or before December 1,
25 2026, the Maryland Health Care Commission shall:
26 (1) conduct a mandated benefits study of coverage of salpingectomy that is
27 not opportunistic for individuals who are not at high risk of ovarian cancer, including
28 consideration of existing practice guidance issued by the American College of Obstetricians
29 and Gynecologists and the Society of Gynecologic Oncology as well as the differences in
30 efficacy of nonopportunistic salpingectomy versus the efficacy of opportunistic
31 salpingectomy for individuals who are not at high risk; and
32 (2) report the findings of the study to the Senate Finance Committee and
33 the House Health Committee, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government Article.
