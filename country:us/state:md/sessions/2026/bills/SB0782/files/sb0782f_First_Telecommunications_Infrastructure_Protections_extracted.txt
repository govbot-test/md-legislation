A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Telecommunications Infrastructure – Protections
3 FOR the purpose of requiring certain junk dealers, scrap metal processors, and antique
4 dealers to register and provide a description including certain information of
5 purchased telecommunications equipment with a certain law enforcement official;
6 prohibiting certain junk dealers, scrap metal processors, and antique dealers from
7 purchasing telecommunications equipment other than by check mailed to the seller’s
8 address; prohibiting the sale or disposal of certain telecommunications equipment
9 within a certain period; prohibiting certain individuals from destroying
10 telecommunications infrastructure in a certain manner; and generally relating to
11 telecommunications equipment and infrastructure.

1 (A) THIS SECTION APPLIES TO ALL JUNK DEALERS, SCRAP METAL

3 (B) (1) EACH JUNK DEALER, SCRAP METAL PROCESSOR, OR ANTIQUE
4 DEALER SUBJECT TO THIS SECTION WHO PURCHASES TELECOMMUNICATIONS

10 (2) THE DESCRIPTION SHALL INCLUDE:
11 (I) THE DATE AND TIME OF PURCHASE;
12 (II) THE NAME AND ADDRESS OF THE SELLER; AND
13 (III) THE LICENSE TAG NUMBER OF ANY VEHICLE USED.
14 (C) A JUNK DEALER, SCRAP METAL PROCESSOR, OR ANTIQUE DEALER
15 SUBJECT TO THIS SECTION WHO PURCHASES TELECOMMUNICATIONS EQUIPMENT
16 MAY PURCHASE TELECOMMUNICATIONS EQUIPMENT ONLY BY ISSUING A CHECK
17 THAT IS MAILED TO THE SELLER’S ADDRESS.
18 (D) TELECOMMUNICATIONS EQUIPMENT MAY NOT BE SOLD OR OTHERWISE
19 DISPOSED OF FOR A PERIOD OF 30 DAYS AFTER THE DATE OF PURCHASE.
20 (E) A PERSON WHO VIOLATES THIS SECTION:
21 (1) IS SUBJECT TO REVOCATION OF ANY STATE JUNK LICENSE HELD
22 BY THE PERSON; AND
23 (2) IS GUILTY OF A MISDEMEANOR AND, ON CONVICTION, IS SUBJECT
