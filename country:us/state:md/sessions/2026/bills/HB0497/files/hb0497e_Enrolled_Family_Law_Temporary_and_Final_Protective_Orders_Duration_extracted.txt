CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Family Law – Temporary and Final Protective Orders – Duration and Relief
3 FOR the purpose of extending altering the time period during which a temporary protective
4 order is effective after service of the order; extending altering the time period for
5 during which a final protective order hearing is required to be held after service of
6 the temporary protective order; providing that a final protective order may order the
7 respondent to pay to a person eligible for relief certain losses or expenses incurred
8 as a result of abuse; providing that a certain monetary award does not preclude a
9 petitioner from bringing a subsequent claim for certain losses or expenses; and
10 generally relating to temporary and final protective orders.
11 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
12 Article – Family Law
13 Section 4–505(a)(1)(i) and 4–506(a)

15 Article – Family Law
16 4–505.
17 (a) (1) (i) If, after a hearing on a petition, whether ex parte or otherwise, a
18 judge finds that there are reasonable grounds to believe that a person eligible for relief has
19 been abused, the judge may enter a temporary protective order to protect any person
20 eligible for relief from abuse.
21 (c) (1) Except as otherwise provided in this subsection, the temporary
22 protective order shall be effective for not more than [7] 14 days after service ISSUANCE of
23 the order.
24 (2) The judge may extend the temporary protective order as needed, but
25 not to exceed 6 months, to effectuate service of the order where necessary to provide
26 protection or for other good cause.
27 (3) If the court is closed on the day on which the temporary protective order
28 is due to expire, the temporary protective order shall be effective until the second day on
29 which the court is open, by which time the court shall hold a final protective order hearing.
30 4–506.
31 (a) A respondent under § 4–505 of this subtitle shall have an opportunity to be
32 heard on the question of whether the judge should issue a final protective order.
33 (b) (1) (i) The temporary protective order shall state the date and time of
34 the final protective order hearing.
