5 of education to certain education support professionals beginning requiring the State

1 6–306.
2 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
3 INDICATED.
4 (2) “EDUCATION SUPPORT PROFESSIONAL” MEANS A
5 NONCERTIFICATED PUBLIC SCHOOL EMPLOYEE DESIGNATED AS PART OF A
6 NONSUPERVISORY BARGAINING UNIT UNDER SUBTITLE 5 OF THIS TITLE.
7 (3) “PUBLIC SCHOOL EMPLOYEE” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN §
8 6–501 OF THIS TITLE.
9 (B) NOTWITHSTANDING § 3–413 OF THE LABOR AND EMPLOYMENT
10 ARTICLE, BEGINNING JULY 1, 2028, EACH COUNTY BOARD SHALL PAY TO EACH
11 EDUCATION SUPPORT PROFESSIONAL A MINIMUM WAGE RATE OF AT LEAST $25.00
12 PER HOUR.
13 (C) THE STATE BOARD MAY ADOPT REGULATIONS NECESSARY TO CARRY
14 OUT THE PROVISIONS OF THIS SECTION.
15 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That, on
16 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
17 (2) “Education support professional” means a noncertificated public school
18 employee designated as part of a nonsupervisory bargaining unit under Title 6, Subtitle 5
19 of the Education Article.
20 (3) “Public school employee” has the meaning stated in § 6–501 of the
21 Education Article.
22 (b) (1) The State Department of Education shall study the wages of education
23 support professionals and calculate the cost of implementing a $25.00 per hour minimum
24 wage.
25 (2) The calculation of the cost of implementation shall:
26 (i) include, for each education support professional position, filled
27 and unfilled, that has an hourly wage of less than $25.00, the cost to raise the hourly wage
28 to $25.00; and
29 (ii) exclude cost assumptions for each education support profession
30 position, filled and unfilled, that has an hourly wage of $25.00 or more.
