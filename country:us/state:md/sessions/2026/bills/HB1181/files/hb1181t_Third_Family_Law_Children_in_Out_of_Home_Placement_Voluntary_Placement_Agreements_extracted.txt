4 FOR the purpose of altering provisions relating to children who are placed in an

7 Unlicensed Settings and Pediatric Hospital Overstays; requiring the Department of
8 Human Services and the Maryland Department of Health to jointly submit a certain
9 report concerning children who are placed in an out–of–home placement under a
10 voluntary placement agreement; and generally relating to children in out–of–home
11 placement and voluntary placement agreements.

12 Preamble
13 WHEREAS, Maryland’s Voluntary Placement Agreement system was designed to
14 allow families to access out–of–home treatment for children with serious mental illness or
15 developmental disabilities without permanently surrendering custody; and
16 WHEREAS, Current statutory and administrative procedures have resulted in
17 significant delays in placement, often exceeding 60 to 90 days, contributing to extended
18 pediatric inpatient stays, emergency department boarding, and unnecessary family
19 distress; and
20 WHEREAS, The local care team process, while intended to coordinate interagency
21 services, has proven largely ineffective in facilitating timely or appropriate supports, and
22 its required involvement has become a systemic bottleneck; and
23 WHEREAS, The 2022 General Assembly enacted legislation intended to streamline
24 access to psychiatric residential treatment for Medicaid–eligible children by clarifying the
25 authority of local behavioral health authorities to approve the educational component of
26 residential placements; however, implementation challenges and unresolved interagency
27 roles have limited the realization of that intent, perpetuating delays and inequities for
28 families seeking timely psychiatric residential treatment; and
29 WHEREAS, The General Assembly recognizes the urgent need to codify timelines,
30 clarify agency accountability, and eliminate duplicative procedural barriers to ensure that
31 children in crisis receive timely, clinically appropriate care; now, therefore,
32 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
33 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

12 Preamble
13 WHEREAS, Maryland’s Voluntary Placement Agreement system was designed to
14 allow families to access out–of–home treatment for children with serious mental illness or
15 developmental disabilities without permanently surrendering custody; and
16 WHEREAS, Current statutory and administrative procedures have resulted in
17 significant delays in placement, often exceeding 60 to 90 days, contributing to extended
18 pediatric inpatient stays, emergency department boarding, and unnecessary family
19 distress; and
20 WHEREAS, The local care team process, while intended to coordinate interagency
21 services, has proven largely ineffective in facilitating timely or appropriate supports, and
22 its required involvement has become a systemic bottleneck; and
23 WHEREAS, The 2022 General Assembly enacted legislation intended to streamline
24 access to psychiatric residential treatment for Medicaid–eligible children by clarifying the
25 authority of local behavioral health authorities to approve the educational component of
26 residential placements; however, implementation challenges and unresolved interagency
27 roles have limited the realization of that intent, perpetuating delays and inequities for
28 families seeking timely psychiatric residential treatment; and
29 WHEREAS, The General Assembly recognizes the urgent need to codify timelines,
30 clarify agency accountability, and eliminate duplicative procedural barriers to ensure that
31 children in crisis receive timely, clinically appropriate care; now, therefore,
32 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
