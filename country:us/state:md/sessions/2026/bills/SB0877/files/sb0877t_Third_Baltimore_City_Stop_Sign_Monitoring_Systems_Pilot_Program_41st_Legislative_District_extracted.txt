1 AN ACT concerning
2 Baltimore City – Stop Sign Monitoring Systems Pilot Program – Mt. Washington
3 Neighborhood 41st Legislative District
4 FOR the purpose of authorizing the use of stop sign monitoring systems in certain school
5 zones in Baltimore City under the pilot program authorized for Prince George’s
6 County, certain school zones in Baltimore City, and the City of Takoma Park, if
7 authorized by local law; requiring that a certain percentage of the fines collected
8 under this Act be distributed to the local management board for Baltimore City to be
9 used only for certain purposes; and generally relating to the use of stop sign
10 monitoring systems in Baltimore City.
11 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
12 Article – Courts and Judicial Proceedings
13 Section 7–302(e)(6)

15 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

17 Article – Transportation
18 Section 21–707.1(a)
19 Annotated Code of Maryland
20 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
21 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
22 Article – Transportation

8 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
9 Article – Courts and Judicial Proceedings
10 7–302.
11 (e) (6) From the fines collected by Baltimore City or the District Court as a
12 result of violations enforced by a stop sign monitoring system located in Baltimore City,
13 50% shall be:
14 (i) Distributed to the local management board for Baltimore City;

18 providing youth programming and services for youth living in or attending a school located
19 in the 45th legislative district; [or]
20 2. From the fines collected as a result of violations enforced
21 by a stop sign monitoring system located in the 46th legislative district, used only for
22 providing youth programming and services for youth living in or attending a school located
23 in the 46th legislative district; OR

25 VIOLATIONS ENFORCED BY A STOP SIGN MONITORING SYSTEM LOCATED IN THE MT.

27 ONLY FOR PROVIDING YOUTH PROGRAMMING AND SERVICES FOR YOUTH LIVING IN
28 OR ATTENDING A SCHOOL LOCATED IN THE 41ST LEGISLATIVE DISTRICT.

30 21–707.1.
31 (a) This section applies only in:
32 (1) Prince George’s County[, the];
SENATE BILL 877 3
1 (2) THE MT. WASHINGTON NEIGHBORHOOD IN THE 41ST
2 LEGISLATIVE DISTRICT IN BALTIMORE CITY;
3 (3) THE 45th 41ST, 45TH, and 46th Legislative Districts in Baltimore
4 City[,]; and [the]
5 (4) (3) THE City of Takoma Park.
6 (b) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
7 (2) “Agency” means a law enforcement agency that is authorized to issue a
8 citation for a violation of the Maryland Vehicle Law or of local traffic laws or regulations.
9 (3) (i) “Owner” means the registered owner of a motor vehicle or lessee
10 of a motor vehicle under a lease of 6 months or more.
11 (ii) “Owner” does not include:
12 1. A motor vehicle leasing company; or
13 2. A holder of a special registration plate issued under Title
14 13, Subtitle 9, Part III of this article.
15 (4) “Recorded image” means images recorded by a stop sign monitoring
16 system:
17 (i) On:
18 1. Two or more photographs;
19 2. Two or more microphotographs;
20 3. Two or more electronic images;
21 4. Videotape; or
22 5. Any other medium; and
23 (ii) Showing a motor vehicle and, on at least one image or portion of
24 tape, clearly identifying the registration plate number of the motor vehicle.
25 (5) “Stop sign monitoring system” means a device designed to capture a
26 recorded image of a violation.
27 (6) (i) “Violation” means a failure to come to a complete stop at a stop
28 sign in violation of § 21–707 of this subtitle.
4 SENATE BILL 877
1 (ii) “Violation” does not include any action a driver is instructed to
2 take by a police officer.
3 (c) (1) Subject to paragraphs (2) through (5) of this subsection, an agency may
4 use stop sign monitoring systems:
5 (i) On highways located in a school zone maintained by a local
6 jurisdiction, if authorized by the governing body of the local jurisdiction; or
7 (ii) On State highways located in a school zone, if authorized by the
8 State Highway Administration.
9 (2) A stop sign monitoring system:
10 (i) May not be used in a local jurisdiction under this section unless
11 its use is authorized by the governing body of the local jurisdiction by local law enacted
12 after reasonable notice and a public hearing; and
13 (ii) In Prince George’s County, may only be used at a location
14 approved by the Prince George’s County Council.
15 (3) Prince George’s County shall prioritize the placement of stop sign
16 monitoring systems within municipalities that have high violation rates.
17 (4) Before beginning use of stop sign monitoring systems, an agency shall
18 publish notice that the agency has adopted the use of stop sign monitoring systems on its
19 website and, in Baltimore City and Prince George’s County only, in a newspaper of general
20 circulation in the jurisdiction in which the stop sign monitoring system will be used.
21 (5) (i) A local jurisdiction that implements a stop sign monitoring
22 system shall prominently place signs on highways within the local jurisdiction providing
23 notice that stop sign monitoring systems are used in the local jurisdiction.
24 (ii) The State Highway Administration shall place signs prominently
25 providing notice that stop sign monitoring systems are in use on State highways.
26 Chapter 571 of the Acts of 2025
27 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That, on or before December 1,
28 2027, the Baltimore City Department of Transportation and the City of Takoma Park,
29 respectively, shall each report to the Governor and, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State
30 Government Article, the General Assembly on:
31 (1) through October 1, 2027:
SENATE BILL 877 5
1 (i) the time period during which stop sign monitoring systems were
2 in use in the local jurisdiction; and
3 (ii) the number of warnings and citations issued as a result of
4 violations recorded by a stop sign monitoring system in the local jurisdiction over the
5 reported time period, by location and date;
6 (2) (i) the costs associated with implementing and operating stop sign
7 monitoring systems; and
8 (ii) the revenue collected on a monthly basis as a result of violations
9 recorded by stop sign monitoring systems;
10 (3) appropriate locations for the deployment of stop sign monitoring
11 systems;
12 (4) the performance and reliability of stop sign monitoring systems used by
13 the local jurisdiction; and
14 (5) the effectiveness of stop sign monitoring systems in reducing violations,
15 crashes, and pedestrian injuries in the local jurisdiction and in areas where the stop sign
16 monitoring systems were implemented and used.
17 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
18 1, 2026. It shall remain effective for a period of 3 years and, at the end of June 30, 2029,
19 this Act, with no further action required by the General Assembly, shall be abrogated and
20 of no further force and effect.
Approved:
