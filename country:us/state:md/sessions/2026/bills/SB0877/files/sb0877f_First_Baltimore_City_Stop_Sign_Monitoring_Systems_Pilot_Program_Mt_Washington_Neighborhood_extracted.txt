A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Baltimore City – Stop Sign Monitoring Systems Pilot Program – Mt. Washington
3 Neighborhood
4 FOR the purpose of authorizing the use of stop sign monitoring systems in certain school
5 zones in Baltimore City under the pilot program authorized for Prince George’s
6 County, certain school zones in Baltimore City, and the City of Takoma Park, if
7 authorized by local law; requiring that a certain percentage of the fines collected
8 under this Act be distributed to the local management board for Baltimore City to be
9 used only for certain purposes; and generally relating to the use of stop sign
10 monitoring systems in Baltimore City.
11 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
12 Article – Courts and Judicial Proceedings
13 Section 7–302(e)(6)
14 Annotated Code of Maryland
15 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
16 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
17 Article – Transportation
18 Section 21–707.1(a)
19 Annotated Code of Maryland
20 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
21 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
22 Article – Transportation
23 Section 21–707.1(b) and (c)
24 Annotated Code of Maryland
25 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
26 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
27 Chapter 571 of the Acts of the General Assembly of 2025

25 21–707.1.
26 (a) This section applies only in:
27 (1) Prince George’s County[, the];
28 (2) THE MT. WASHINGTON NEIGHBORHOOD IN THE 41ST
29 LEGISLATIVE DISTRICT IN BALTIMORE CITY;
30 (3) THE 45th and 46th Legislative Districts in Baltimore City[,]; and [the]
31 (4) THE City of Takoma Park.
SENATE BILL 877 3
1 (b) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
2 (2) “Agency” means a law enforcement agency that is authorized to issue a
3 citation for a violation of the Maryland Vehicle Law or of local traffic laws or regulations.
4 (3) (i) “Owner” means the registered owner of a motor vehicle or lessee
5 of a motor vehicle under a lease of 6 months or more.
6 (ii) “Owner” does not include:
7 1. A motor vehicle leasing company; or
8 2. A holder of a special registration plate issued under Title
9 13, Subtitle 9, Part III of this article.
10 (4) “Recorded image” means images recorded by a stop sign monitoring
11 system:
12 (i) On:
13 1. Two or more photographs;
14 2. Two or more microphotographs;
15 3. Two or more electronic images;
16 4. Videotape; or
17 5. Any other medium; and
18 (ii) Showing a motor vehicle and, on at least one image or portion of
19 tape, clearly identifying the registration plate number of the motor vehicle.
20 (5) “Stop sign monitoring system” means a device designed to capture a
21 recorded image of a violation.
22 (6) (i) “Violation” means a failure to come to a complete stop at a stop
23 sign in violation of § 21–707 of this subtitle.
24 (ii) “Violation” does not include any action a driver is instructed to
25 take by a police officer.
26 (c) (1) Subject to paragraphs (2) through (5) of this subsection, an agency may
27 use stop sign monitoring systems:
4 SENATE BILL 877
1 (i) On highways located in a school zone maintained by a local
2 jurisdiction, if authorized by the governing body of the local jurisdiction; or
3 (ii) On State highways located in a school zone, if authorized by the
4 State Highway Administration.
5 (2) A stop sign monitoring system:
6 (i) May not be used in a local jurisdiction under this section unless
7 its use is authorized by the governing body of the local jurisdiction by local law enacted
8 after reasonable notice and a public hearing; and
9 (ii) In Prince George’s County, may only be used at a location
10 approved by the Prince George’s County Council.
11 (3) Prince George’s County shall prioritize the placement of stop sign
12 monitoring systems within municipalities that have high violation rates.
13 (4) Before beginning use of stop sign monitoring systems, an agency shall
14 publish notice that the agency has adopted the use of stop sign monitoring systems on its
15 website and, in Baltimore City and Prince George’s County only, in a newspaper of general
16 circulation in the jurisdiction in which the stop sign monitoring system will be used.
17 (5) (i) A local jurisdiction that implements a stop sign monitoring
18 system shall prominently place signs on highways within the local jurisdiction providing
19 notice that stop sign monitoring systems are used in the local jurisdiction.
20 (ii) The State Highway Administration shall place signs prominently
21 providing notice that stop sign monitoring systems are in use on State highways.
22 Chapter 571 of the Acts of 2025
23 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That, on or before December 1,
24 2027, the Baltimore City Department of Transportation and the City of Takoma Park,
25 respectively, shall each report to the Governor and, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State
26 Government Article, the General Assembly on:
27 (1) through October 1, 2027:
28 (i) the time period during which stop sign monitoring systems were
29 in use in the local jurisdiction; and
30 (ii) the number of warnings and citations issued as a result of
31 violations recorded by a stop sign monitoring system in the local jurisdiction over the
32 reported time period, by location and date;
SENATE BILL 877 5
1 (2) (i) the costs associated with implementing and operating stop sign
2 monitoring systems; and
3 (ii) the revenue collected on a monthly basis as a result of violations
4 recorded by stop sign monitoring systems;
5 (3) appropriate locations for the deployment of stop sign monitoring
6 systems;
7 (4) the performance and reliability of stop sign monitoring systems used by
8 the local jurisdiction; and
9 (5) the effectiveness of stop sign monitoring systems in reducing violations,
10 crashes, and pedestrian injuries in the local jurisdiction and in areas where the stop sign
11 monitoring systems were implemented and used.
12 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
13 1, 2026. It shall remain effective for a period of 3 years and, at the end of June 30, 2029,
14 this Act, with no further action required by the General Assembly, shall be abrogated and
15 of no further force and effect.
