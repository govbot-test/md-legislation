A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Vehicle Laws – Bicycles, Motor Scooters, and EPAMDs – Overtaking and Passing
3 (Pass Pedestrians Safely Act)
4 FOR the purpose of authorizing a person to operate an electric personal assistive mobility
5 device (EPAMD) that is equipped with certain audible devices; requiring an operator
6 of a bicycle, a motor scooter, or an EPAMD to exercise due care to avoid a collision
7 with another vehicle or a pedestrian and maintain a certain distance, if practicable,
8 when passing a vehicle or pedestrian; requiring an operator of a bicycle, a motor
9 scooter, or an EPAMD to give a certain audible warning before passing a pedestrian;
10 establishing that a violation of the distance or audible warning requirements is a
11 secondary offense subject to a certain civil penalty; and generally relating to
12 overtaking and passing requirements for bicycles, motor scooters, and EPAMDs.
13 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
14 Article – Transportation
15 Section 21–1207
16 Annotated Code of Maryland
17 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
18 BY adding to
19 Article – Transportation
20 Section 21–1209.1
21 Annotated Code of Maryland
22 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
23 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
24 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
25 Article – Transportation
26 21–1207.

2 HOUSE BILL 1567
1 (a) (1) If a bicycle or a motor scooter is used on a highway at any time when,
2 due to insufficient light or unfavorable atmospheric conditions, persons and vehicles on the
3 highway are not clearly discernible at a distance of 1,000 feet, the bicycle or motor scooter
4 shall be equipped:
5 (i) On the front, with a lamp that emits a white light visible from a
6 distance of at least 500 feet to the front; and
7 (ii) On the rear, with a red reflector of a type approved by the
8 Administration and visible from all distances from 600 feet to 100 feet to the rear when
9 directly in front of lawful upper beams of head lamps on a motor vehicle.
10 (2) A bicycle or bicyclist may be equipped with a functioning lamp that acts
11 as a reflector and emits a red light or a flashing amber light visible from a distance of 500
12 feet to the rear instead of or in addition to the red reflector required by paragraph (1) of
13 this subsection.
14 (b) Subject to subsection (c) of this section, a person may operate a bicycle [or], a
15 motor scooter, OR AN EPAMD that is equipped with a bell, HORN, or other device capable
16 of giving a signal audible for a distance of at least 100 feet.
17 (c) A bicycle [or], A motor scooter, OR AN EPAMD may not be equipped with nor
18 may any person use on a bicycle, A MOTOR SCOOTER, OR AN EPAMD any siren or
19 whistle.
20 (d) Every bicycle and motor scooter shall be equipped with a braking system
21 capable of stopping from a speed of 10 miles per hour within 15 feet on dry, level, clean
22 pavement.
23 21–1209.1.
24 (A) A PERSON OPERATING A BICYCLE, A MOTOR SCOOTER, OR AN EPAMD
25 SHALL:
26 (1) EXERCISE DUE CARE TO AVOID COLLIDING WITH A VEHICLE OR
27 PEDESTRIAN; AND
28 (2) WHEN OVERTAKING AND PASSING A VEHICLE OR PEDESTRIAN, IF
29 PRACTICABLE, PASS SAFELY AT A DISTANCE OF NOT LESS THAN 3 FEET.
30 (B) AN OPERATOR OF A BICYCLE, A MOTOR SCOOTER, OR AN EPAMD
31 SHALL GIVE AN AUDIBLE WARNING BY VOICE OR BELL, HORN, OR OTHER DEVICE
32 BEFORE OVERTAKING AND PASSING A PEDESTRIAN.
HOUSE BILL 1567 3
1 (C) (1) A POLICE OFFICER MAY ENFORCE THIS SECTION ONLY AS A
2 SECONDARY ACTION WHEN THE POLICE OFFICER DETAINS A PERSON OPERATING A
3 BICYCLE, A MOTOR SCOOTER, OR AN EPAMD FOR A SUSPECTED VIOLATION OF
4 ANOTHER PROVISION OF THE CODE.
5 (2) A PERSON WHO IS FOUND LIABLE FOR A VIOLATION OF THIS
6 SECTION IS SUBJECT TO A CIVIL PENALTY OF $25.
7 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
8 October 1, 2026.
