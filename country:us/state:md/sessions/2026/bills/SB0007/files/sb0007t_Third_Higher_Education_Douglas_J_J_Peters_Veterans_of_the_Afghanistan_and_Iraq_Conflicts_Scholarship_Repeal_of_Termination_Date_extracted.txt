1 AN ACT concerning
2 Higher Education – Douglas J. J. Peters Veterans of the Afghanistan and Iraq
3 Conflicts Scholarship – Repeal of Termination Date
4 FOR the purpose of repealing the prohibition on awarding the Douglas J. J. Peters Veterans
5 of the Afghanistan and Iraq Conflicts Scholarship after a certain date; repealing the
6 limitation on renewing the Douglas J. J. Peters Veterans of the Afghanistan and Iraq
7 Conflicts Scholarship if the scholarship recipient received an initial scholarship
8 before a certain date; and generally relating to the Douglas J. J. Peters Veterans of
9 the Afghanistan and Iraq Conflicts Scholarship.

3 (a) For purposes of this section, an individual served in the Afghanistan or Iraq
4 conflict if the individual was an active service member who served in:
5 (1) Afghanistan or contiguous air space, as defined in federal regulations,
6 on or after October 24, 2001; or
7 (2) Iraq or contiguous waters or air space, as defined in federal regulations,
8 on or after March 19, 2003.

10 Scholarship.

12 the individual:
13 (1) Is a resident of Maryland;
14 (2) (i) Is accepted for admission or enrolled in the regular
15 undergraduate program at an eligible institution; or
16 (ii) Is accepted for admission or enrolled in a 2–year terminal
17 certificate program in which the course work is acceptable for transfer credit for an
18 accredited baccalaureate program in an eligible institution; and
19 (3) (i) 1. Is a veteran who served in the Afghanistan or Iraq conflict;
20 2. Is an active service member who served in the Afghanistan
21 or Iraq conflict; or
22 3. Is a member of a reserve component who was activated as
23 a result of the Afghanistan or Iraq conflict described in subsection (a) of this section; or
24 (ii) Is a son, daughter, stepson, stepdaughter, or spouse of:
25 1. A veteran or an active service member who is serving or
26 has served in the Afghanistan or Iraq conflict; or
27 2. A member of the reserve component who was activated as
28 a result of the Afghanistan or Iraq conflict described in subsection (a) of this section.

1 (d) A scholarship awarded under this section may supplement, but is not
2 contingent on, any federal education benefits for which a recipient qualifies as a result of
3 an individual’s service or status as a dependent of an active service member or of a veteran.
4 (e) (1) Each scholarship may be used, at any eligible institution, to pay for
5 educational expenses as defined by the Office, including:
6 (i) Tuition and mandatory fees; and
7 (ii) Room and board.
8 (2) The annual amount of a scholarship may not exceed 50% of the
9 equivalent annual tuition, mandatory fees, and room and board of a resident

13 full–time resident undergraduate.
14 (f) A scholarship recipient shall maintain a grade point average of at least 2.5 on
15 a 4.0 scale.
16 (g) Each recipient of a scholarship under this section may hold the award for 5
17 years of full–time study or 8 years of part–time study.
