CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Hospitals – Emergency Pregnancy–Related Medical Conditions – Procedures
3 FOR the purpose of requiring a hospital to conduct screening on a patient presenting at an
4 emergency department of the hospital to determine whether the patient has an
5 emergency pregnancy–related medical condition; establishing requirements and
6 prohibitions related to the treatment and transfer of a patient who has an emergency
7 pregnancy–related medical condition; requiring a hospital to allow the termination
8 of a pregnancy in certain circumstances; prohibiting a hospital from taking adverse
9 action against a provider for not transferring a patient who is not stabilized or
10 against a hospital employee if the employee reports a violation of this Act; and
11 generally relating to emergency pregnancy–related medical conditions and hospitals.

13 Article – Health – General
14 Section 19–342.1

1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – Health – General
4 19–342.1.
5 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
6 INDICATED.
7 (2) “EMERGENCY PREGNANCY–RELATED MEDICAL CONDITION”
8 MEANS A MEDICAL CONDITION THAT PRESENTS IN A PREGNANT PATIENT THROUGH
9 ACUTE SYMPTOMS OF SUFFICIENT SEVERITY AND FOR WHICH THE ABSENCE OF
10 IMMEDIATE MEDICAL ATTENTION COULD BE REASONABLY EXPECTED TO RESULT IN:
11 (I) PLACING THE HEALTH OF THE PATIENT IN SERIOUS
12 JEOPARDY;
13 (II) SERIOUS IMPAIRMENT TO BODILY FUNCTIONS; OR
14 (III) SERIOUS DYSFUNCTION OF ANY BODILY ORGAN OR BODY
15 PART.
16 (3) “STABILIZE” MEANS, FOR AN EMERGENCY PREGNANCY–RELATED
17 MEDICAL CONDITION, TO PROVIDE THE MEDICAL TREATMENT NECESSARY TO
18 ALLEVIATE THE CONDITION OR ENSURE, WITHIN REASONABLE MEDICAL
19 PROBABILITY, THAT NO MATERIAL DETERIORATION OF THE CONDITION IS LIKELY
20 TO RESULT FROM OR OCCUR DURING THE TRANSFER OF THE PATIENT FROM THE
21 FACILITY.
22 (B) THIS SECTION APPLIES ONLY TO A HOSPITAL WITH AN EMERGENCY
23 DEPARTMENT.
24 (C) IF A HOSPITAL DETERMINES THAT A PATIENT HAS AN EMERGENCY
25 PREGNANCY–RELATED MEDICAL CONDITION, THE HOSPITAL SHALL:
26 (1) USING THE STAFF AND FACILITIES AVAILABLE TO THE HOSPITAL,
27 PROVIDE FURTHER EXAMINATION AND THE TREATMENT REQUIRED TO STABILIZE
28 THE EMERGENCY PREGNANCY–RELATED MEDICAL CONDITION, INCLUDING THE
29 TERMINATION OF A PREGNANCY WHEN THE TERMINATION IS MEDICALLY
30 NECESSARY TO STABILIZE THE PATIENT; OR
31 (2) TRANSFER THE PATIENT TO ANOTHER MEDICAL FACILITY.

SENATE BILL 169 3
1 (D) A HOSPITAL SHALL ALLOW THE TERMINATION OF A PREGNANCY AT THE
2 HOSPITAL IF THE PATIENT’S TREATING HEALTH CARE PRACTITIONER DETERMINES
3 TERMINATION IS MEDICALLY NECESSARY TO STABILIZE A PATIENT.
4 (E) (1) A HOSPITAL IS CONSIDERED TO HAVE MET THE REQUIREMENTS
5 OF THIS SECTION IF, AFTER OFFERING FURTHER EXAMINATION AND TREATMENT OR
6 TRANSFER TO THE PATIENT OR THE PATIENT’S REPRESENTATIVE AND INFORMING
7 THE PATIENT OR PATIENT’S REPRESENTATIVE OF THE RISKS AND BENEFITS OF
8 FURTHER EXAMINATION AND TREATMENT OR TRANSFER:
9 (I) A PATIENT OR THE PATIENT’S REPRESENTATIVE REFUSES
10 TO CONSENT TO FURTHER EXAMINATION AND TREATMENT; OR
11 (II) A PATIENT OR THE PATIENT’S REPRESENTATIVE REFUSES
12 TO CONSENT TO A TRANSFER TO ANOTHER MEDICAL FACILITY.
13 (2) A HOSPITAL SHALL TAKE REASONABLE STEPS TO SECURE
14 WRITTEN INFORMED CONSENT TO THE REFUSAL OF AN EXAMINATION OR
15 TREATMENT OR TRANSFER UNDER THIS SUBSECTION FROM THE PATIENT OR THE
16 PATIENT’S REPRESENTATIVE.
17 (F) IF A PATIENT HAS AN EMERGENCY PREGNANCY–RELATED MEDICAL
18 CONDITION THAT HAS NOT BEEN STABILIZED, THE HOSPITAL MAY NOT TRANSFER
19 THE PATIENT UNLESS THE TRANSFER IS DONE CONSISTENT WITH 42 U.S.C. §
20 1395DD.
21 (G) A HOSPITAL MAY NOT PENALIZE OR TAKE OTHER ADVERSE ACTION,
22 INCLUDING AN ACTION RELATED TO DISCHARGE, PROMOTION, DEMOTION,
23 SUSPENSION, COMPENSATION, TRAINING OPPORTUNITIES, STAFF PRIVILEGES, OR
24 ADMITTING PRIVILEGES, AGAINST:
25 (1) A TREATING HEALTH CARE PROVIDER IF THE PROVIDER REFUSES
26 TO AUTHORIZE THE TRANSFER OF A PATIENT WITH AN EMERGENCY
27 PREGNANCY–RELATED MEDICAL CONDITION THAT HAS NOT BEEN STABILIZED;
28 (2) A TREATING HEALTH CARE PROVIDER IF THE PROVIDER’S
29 TREATMENT OF THE PATIENT IS CONSISTENT WITH THE MEDICAL STANDARDS OF
30 CARE THAT, IN THE PROVIDER’S CLINICAL JUDGMENT, WERE NECESSARY TO
31 STABILIZE THE PATIENT; OR
32 (3) A HOSPITAL EMPLOYEE IF THE EMPLOYEE REPORTS A VIOLATION
33 OF THIS SECTION.
