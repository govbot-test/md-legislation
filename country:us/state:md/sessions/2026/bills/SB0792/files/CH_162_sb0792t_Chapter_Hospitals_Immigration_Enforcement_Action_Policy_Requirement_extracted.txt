Section 1:
WES MOORE, Governor Ch. 162
Chapter 162
AN ACT concerning
Hospitals – Immigration Enforcement Action – Policy Requirement
FOR the purpose of requiring the State Health Services Cost Review Commission to develop
and publish a model policy relating to immigration enforcement actions at hospitals;
requiring certain hospitals in the State to develop a policy relating to immigration
enforcement actions; and generally relating to hospitals and immigration
enforcement policies.
BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
Article – Health – General

Section 2:
Section 19–201(a) and (c) and 19–207(a)
Annotated Code of Maryland
(2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
Article – Health – General

Section 3:
Section 19–207(b)
Annotated Code of Maryland
(2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
BY adding to
Article – Health – General

Section 4:
Section 19–306
Annotated Code of Maryland
(2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

Section 5:

19–201.
(a) In this subtitle the following words have the meanings indicated.

(i) Health care costs;
(ii) The financial status of any facility; or

19–306.
(A) (1) THE REQUIREMENTS OF THIS SUBSECTION DO NOT APPLY TO A
HOSPITAL OPERATED BY A UNIT OF STATE OR LOCAL GOVERNMENT REQUIRED TO
IMPLEMENT A POLICY CONSISTENT WITH THE GUIDANCE OF THE ATTORNEY
GENERAL UNDER § 6–111 OF THE STATE GOVERNMENT ARTICLE.
(2) A HOSPITAL SHALL ADOPT:
(1) ADOPT A POLICY DESCRIBING THE PROTOCOL OF THE HOSPITAL
WHEN THERE IS AN IMMIGRATION ENFORCEMENT ACTION AT THE HOSPITAL ON OR
BEFORE THE EARLIER OF:
(I) JANUARY 1, 2027; OR
(II) 3 MONTHS AFTER THE COMMISSION PUBLISHES A MODEL
POLICY UNDER § 19–207(B)(10) OF THIS TITLE. CONSISTENT WITH THE GUIDANCE
OF THE ATTORNEY GENERAL ISSUED UNDER § 6–111 OF THE STATE GOVERNMENT
ARTICLE;
– 4 –

(B) A HOSPITAL SHALL:
(1) (2) CONSPICUOUSLY POST COPIES OF MAKE THE HOSPITAL’S
IMMIGRATION ENFORCEMENT ACTION POLICY ON THE HOSPITAL’S WEBSITE
AVAILABLE TO ALL HOSPITAL STAFF MEMBERS; AND
(2) (3) PROVIDE ANNUAL TRAINING TO ALL HOSPITAL STAFF
MEMBERS TO ENSURE THE STAFF’S KNOWLEDGE AND UNDERSTANDING OF THE
IMMIGRATION ENFORCEMENT ACTION POLICY.

Section 15:
SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect June
1, 2026.
Approved by the Governor, April 28, 2026.
