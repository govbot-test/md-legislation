CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Hospitals – Clinical Staffing Committees and Plans – Establishment
3 (Safe Staffing Act of 2026)
4 FOR the purpose of requiring certain hospitals licensed in the State to establish and
5 maintain a clinical staffing committee and to implement a clinical staffing plan;
6 requiring each clinical staffing committee to develop a clinical staffing plan; and
7 generally relating to hospitals and clinical staffing committees and plans.
8 BY adding to
9 Article – Health – General
10 Section 19–393 through 19–397 to be under the new part “Part XIII. Clinical Staffing
11 Committees and Plans”
12 Annotated Code of Maryland
13 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
14 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
15 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

3 19–393.
4 (A) IN THIS PART THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
5 INDICATED.
6 (B) (1) “ANCILLARY MEMBER OF THE FRONTLINE TEAM” MEANS AN
7 INDIVIDUAL WHOSE PRIMARY DUTIES INCLUDE SUPPORTING INDIVIDUALS WHO
8 PROVIDE DIRECT PATIENT CARE.
9 (2) “ANCILLARY MEMBER OF THE FRONTLINE TEAM” INCLUDES:
10 (I) A DIETARY WORKER;
11 (II) A PATIENT CARE TECHNICIAN; AND
12 (III) ANY OTHER NONLICENSED STAFF ASSISTING WITH PATIENT
13 CARE.
14 (C) “CLINICAL STAFFING STAFFING COMMITTEE” MEANS A COMMITTEE
15 ESTABLISHED BY A HOSPITAL IN ACCORDANCE WITH SUBSECTION (C) OF THIS
16 SECTION § 19–394(B) OF THIS SUBTITLE.
17 (D) “CLINICAL STAFFING STAFFING PLAN” MEANS A PLAN DEVELOPED
18 UNDER SUBSECTION (D) OF THIS SECTION § 19–394(C) OF THIS SUBTITLE.
19 19–394.
20 (A) THIS PART DOES NOT APPLY TO STATE HOSPITALS.
21 (B) (1) EACH HOSPITAL LICENSED UNDER THIS TITLE SHALL ESTABLISH
22 AND MAINTAIN A CLINICAL STAFFING COMMITTEE THAT HAS EQUAL MEMBERSHIP
23 FROM MANAGEMENT AND EMPLOYEES.
24 (2) EACH CLINICAL STAFFING COMMITTEE ESTABLISHED UNDER
25 THIS PART SHALL INCLUDE AT LEAST ONE OF THE FOLLOWING INDIVIDUALS:
26 (I) A CERTIFIED NURSING ASSISTANT;
27 (II) A DIETARY AIDE;
28 (III) AN EMERGENCY ROOM NURSE;

12 (C) EACH CLINICAL STAFFING COMMITTEE SHALL DEVELOP A CLINICAL
13 STAFFING PLAN THAT MEETS PATIENT NEEDS.
14 (D) WHEN DEVELOPING A CLINICAL STAFFING PLAN, THE CLINICAL
15 STAFFING COMMITTEE SHALL CONSIDER:
16 (1) EXISTING STAFFING LEVELS;
17 (2) METHODS TO SECURE COVERAGE NEEDS AS NECESSARY;
18 (3) EXISTING GAPS IN STAFFING AND HOW TO ADDRESS THE GAPS;
19 (4) INPUT FROM INDIVIDUALS WITH SPECIFIC EXPERTISE IN THE
20 STAFFING CLASSIFICATIONS;
21 (4) (5) ANY AVAILABLE EVIDENCE–BASED STAFFING STANDARDS;
22 AND
23 (5) (6) A SCHEDULE FOR PERIODIC REVIEW OF THE STAFFING
24 PLAN.
25 (E) ON OR BEFORE JULY 1 EACH YEAR, EACH HOSPITAL, THROUGH THE
26 CLINICAL STAFFING COMMITTEE, SHALL CONDUCT A REVIEW OF THE CLINICAL
27 STAFFING PLAN TO:

1 (1) EVALUATE THE EFFECTIVENESS OF THE CLINICAL STAFFING
2 PLAN DURING THE IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING YEAR;
3 (2) UPDATE THE CLINICAL STAFFING PLAN TO ENSURE THAT THE

7 (F) IN DEVELOPING THE CLINICAL STAFFING PLAN, THE CLINICAL
8 STAFFING COMMITTEE SHALL CONSIDER:
9 (1) THE AVERAGE NUMBER OF PATIENTS ON EACH UNIT ON EACH
10 SHIFT DURING THE IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING YEAR AND RELEVANT INFORMATION
11 REGARDING PATIENT DISCHARGES, POTENTIAL ADMISSIONS, AND TRANSFERS;
12 (2) THE AVERAGE LEVEL OF ACUITY FOR PATIENTS ON EACH UNIT ON
13 EACH SHIFT DURING THE IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING YEAR AND THE
14 CORRESPONDING LEVEL OF NURSING CARE REQUIRED; AND
15 (3) AN ESTIMATE OF THE APPROPRIATE COMBINATION OF SKILL,
16 EXPERIENCE LEVEL, AND SPECIALTY CERTIFICATION OR TRAINING OF STAFF FOR
17 EACH UNIT ON EACH SHIFT THAT IS REQUIRED TO ADEQUATELY PROVIDE CARE.
18 19–395.
19 (A) ON OR BEFORE JANUARY 1 EACH YEAR, BEGINNING IN 2028, EACH
20 HOSPITAL SHALL:
21 (1) IMPLEMENT THE CLINICAL STAFFING PLAN ADOPTED UNDER §
22 19–394 OF THIS SUBTITLE; AND
23 (2) ASSIGN PERSONNEL TO EACH PATIENT CARE UNIT IN
24 ACCORDANCE WITH THE CLINICAL STAFFING PLAN.
25 (B) A REGISTERED NURSE, A LICENSED PRACTICAL NURSE, AN ANCILLARY
26 MEMBER OF THE FRONTLINE TEAM, OR AN APPLICABLE EXCLUSIVE
27 REPRESENTATIVE MAY SUBMIT A COMPLAINT TO THE CLINICAL STAFFING
28 COMMITTEE REGARDING ANY VARIATION WHERE PERSONNEL ASSIGNMENT IN A
29 PATIENT CARE UNIT IS NOT IN ACCORDANCE WITH THE ADOPTED CLINICAL
30 STAFFING PLAN.
31 (C) THE CLINICAL STAFFING COMMITTEE SHALL DETERMINE, BY A
32 MAJORITY VOTE, WHETHER A COMPLAINT HAS BEEN ADEQUATELY RESOLVED.

13 19–397.

25 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That each hospital shall establish

27 as enacted by Section 1 of this Act, on or before January 1, 2027.
28 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That each clinical staffing
