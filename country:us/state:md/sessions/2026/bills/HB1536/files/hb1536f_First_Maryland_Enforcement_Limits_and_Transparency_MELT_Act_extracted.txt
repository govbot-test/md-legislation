Section 1:
D3, P2, J2 6lr2638
By: Delegates Lopez, Amprey, Boafo, Kaufman, Lewis, Martinez, Moreno, Taveras,
White Holland, Wims, and Woorman
Introduced and read first time: February 13, 2026
Assigned to: Judiciary
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Maryland Enforcement Limits and Transparency (MELT) Act
3 FOR the purpose of prohibiting a person from voluntarily assisting in immigration
4 enforcement under certain circumstances and requiring a person who assists
5 immigration enforcement to make and maintain certain records; authorizing a
6 person injured by a violation of this Act to bring a certain civil suit; authorizing the
7 Attorney General to make a certain investigation and to take certain actions to
8 enforce this Act; authorizing the Board of Public Works to take certain actions
9 relating to a procurement contract between the State and a person who violates this
10 Act and establishing a certain requirement for persons entering into a State
11 procurement contract; authorizing certain licensing entities to take certain actions
12 against a licensee that violates this Act and requiring certain applicants and
13 licensees to make a certain certified statement; and generally relating to civil
14 liability for assisting immigration enforcement.
15 BY adding to
16 Article – Courts and Judicial Proceedings
17 Section 3–2701 through 3–2705 to be under the new subtitle “Subtitle 27. Injury
18 Caused by Voluntary Immigration Enforcement Cooperation”
19 Annotated Code of Maryland
20 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
21 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
22 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
23 Article – Courts and Judicial Proceedings
24 SUBTITLE 27. INJURY CAUSED BY VOLUNTARY IMMIGRATION ENFORCEMENT
25 COOPERATION.
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

2 HOUSE BILL 1536
1 3–2701.
2 (A) IN THIS SUBTITLE THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
3 INDICATED.
4 (B) “AID IN IMMIGRATION ENFORCEMENT” MEANS:
5 (1) PROVIDING ACCESS TO AN AREA THAT WOULD OTHERWISE
6 REQUIRE A JUDICIAL WARRANT FOR LAW ENFORCEMENT TO ACCESS;
7 (2) SHARING PERSONAL INFORMATION OF ANOTHER WITH
8 IMMIGRATION AUTHORITIES; OR
9 (3) DELAYING THE DEPARTURE OF OR DETAINING ANOTHER BY
10 FORCE OR DECEIT WITH THE INTENT TO MAKE THE INDIVIDUAL ACCESSIBLE TO
11 IMMIGRATION AUTHORITIES.
12 (C) (1) “JUDICIAL WARRANT” MEANS A WARRANT SIGNED BY A JUDGE,
13 MAGISTRATE, OR DISTRICT COURT COMMISSIONER OF COMPETENT JURISDICTION.
14 (2) “JUDICIAL WARRANT” DOES NOT INCLUDE AN ADMINISTRATIVE
15 WARRANT, INCLUDING AN IMMIGRATION AND CUSTOMS ENFORCEMENT FORM
16 I–200 OR FORM I–205.
17 (D) “LICENSE” MEANS AN OCCUPATIONAL OR PROFESSIONAL LICENSE
18 ISSUED TO AN INDIVIDUAL BY THE STATE, INCLUDING A LICENSE ISSUED UNDER
19 THE BUSINESS OCCUPATIONS AND PROFESSIONS ARTICLE, THE BUSINESS
20 REGULATION ARTICLE, AND THE HEALTH OCCUPATIONS ARTICLE.
21 (E) “LICENSING AUTHORITY” MEANS A STATE ENTITY THAT ISSUES A
22 LICENSE TO AN INDIVIDUAL.
23 (F) “PERSON” INCLUDES:
24 (1) THE STATE;
25 (2) A UNIT OF LOCAL GOVERNMENT;
26 (3) A COUNTY SHERIFF; AND
27 (4) ANY AGENCY, OFFICER, EMPLOYEE, CONTRACTOR, OR AGENT OF
28 THE STATE OR A UNIT OF LOCAL GOVERNMENT.
HOUSE BILL 1536 3
1 3–2702.
2 (A) EXCEPT AS REQUIRED BY JUDICIAL WARRANT OR STATE OR FEDERAL
3 LAW, A PERSON MAY NOT KNOWINGLY AID IN IMMIGRATION ENFORCEMENT IN A
4 MANNER THAT VIOLATES THE CONSTITUTIONAL RIGHTS OF ANOTHER.
5 (B) FOR EVERY INTERACTION WITH IMMIGRATION ENFORCEMENT, A
6 PERSON SHALL:
7 (1) DOCUMENT THE PERSON’S INSTANCES OF COOPERATION WITH
8 IMMIGRATION ENFORCEMENT;
9 (2) RETAIN ALL RECORDS OF INSTANCES OF COOPERATION FOR NOT
10 FEWER THAN 5 YEARS AFTER EACH OCCURRENCE; AND
11 (3) NOTIFY ANY INDIVIDUAL WHOSE INFORMATION WAS SHARED,
12 INCLUDING, TO THE EXTENT PERMITTED BY LAW, WHAT INFORMATION WAS SHARED.
13 (C) (1) A PERSON HARMED BY A VIOLATION OF SUBSECTION (A) OF THIS
14 SECTION MAY BRING AN ACTION AGAINST ANY PERSON THAT:
15 (I) VIOLATED THIS SECTION; OR
16 (II) AIDED ANOTHER IN COMMITTING A VIOLATION OF THIS
17 SECTION.
18 (2) IN AN ACTION UNDER THIS SUBSECTION, A COURT MAY AWARD:
19 (I) ECONOMIC AND NONECONOMIC DAMAGES;
20 (II) DECLARATORY RELIEF;
21 (III) INJUNCTIVE RELIEF; AND
22 (IV) REASONABLE ATTORNEY’S FEES.
23 3–2703.
24 (A) THE ATTORNEY GENERAL MAY INVESTIGATE AN ALLEGED VIOLATION
25 OF THIS SUBTITLE BY:
26 (1) ISSUING SUBPOENAS;
4 HOUSE BILL 1536
1 (2) ADMINISTERING OATHS;
2 (3) COMPELLING THE ATTENDANCE AND TESTIMONY OF WITNESSES;
3 AND
4 (4) COMPELLING THE PRODUCTION OF RECORDS, BOOKS, PAPERS,
5 CONTRACTS, AND OTHER DOCUMENTS.
6 (B) A SUBPOENA ISSUED UNDER THIS SECTION SHALL BE SERVED BY:
7 (1) CERTIFIED MAIL, RETURN RECEIPT REQUESTED;
8 (2) ANY ADULT WHO IS NOT A PARTY TO THE PROCEEDING; OR
9 (3) THE SHERIFF OR DEPUTY SHERIFF OF THE COUNTY IN WHICH THE
10 SUBPOENA IS ISSUED.
11 (C) IF A PERSON FAILS OR REFUSES TO COMPLY WITH A SUBPOENA, THE
12 ATTORNEY GENERAL MAY APPLY TO A CIRCUIT COURT IN THE COUNTY IN WHICH
13 THE SUBPOENA WAS SERVED TO ENFORCE COMPLIANCE.
14 (D) IF THE ATTORNEY GENERAL DETERMINES THAT A VIOLATION OF THIS
15 SUBTITLE HAS OCCURRED, THE ATTORNEY GENERAL SHALL:
16 (1) PUBLISH:
17 (I) A SUMMARY OF THE ACTIVITY CONSTITUTING THE
18 VIOLATION;
19 (II) IF THE VIOLATION IS ONGOING OR LIKELY TO RECUR, THE
20 REMEDIAL MEASURES NECESSARY TO CORRECT THE VIOLATION OR PREVENT A
21 RECURRENCE OF THE VIOLATION; AND
22 (III) ANY OTHER RELEVANT INFORMATION; AND
23 (2) REFER THE MATTER TO:
24 (I) ANY LICENSING ENTITY THAT HOLDS AUTHORITY OVER A
25 LICENSE HELD BY THE VIOLATOR; AND
26 (II) FOR A MATTER THAT INVOLVES FEDERAL TORT CLAIMS,
27 THE FEDERAL DEPARTMENT OF JUSTICE.
HOUSE BILL 1536 5
1 (E) (1) SUBJECT TO PARAGRAPH (2) OF THIS SUBSECTION, IF THE
2 ATTORNEY GENERAL HAS REASON TO BELIEVE THAT A VIOLATION OF THIS
3 SUBTITLE HAS OCCURRED AND IS LIKELY TO RECUR, THE ATTORNEY GENERAL MAY
4 BRING AN ACTION TO ENFORCE THIS SUBTITLE IN THE CIRCUIT COURT FOR ANY
5 COUNTY IN WHICH THE VIOLATION IS ALLEGED TO HAVE OCCURRED.
6 (2) (I) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN SUBPARAGRAPH (II) OF THIS
7 PARAGRAPH, THE ATTORNEY GENERAL MAY BRING AN ACTION UNDER THIS
8 SUBSECTION ONLY AFTER:
9 1. CONDUCTING AN INVESTIGATION IN ACCORDANCE
10 WITH THIS SECTION;
11 2. AT LEAST 30 DAYS BEFORE BRINGING THE ACTION,
12 PROVIDING NOTICE TO THE GOVERNING BODY OF THE COUNTY WHERE THE
13 VIOLATION IS ALLEGED TO HAVE OCCURRED; AND
14 3. MAKING REASONABLE EFFORTS TO NOTIFY AN
15 ALLEGED VIOLATOR.
16 (II) THE ATTORNEY GENERAL MAY BRING AN ACTION TO
17 ENFORCE THIS SUBTITLE WITHOUT COMPLYING WITH SUBPARAGRAPH (I) OF THIS
18 PARAGRAPH IF THE ATTORNEY GENERAL DETERMINES THAT A DELAY POSES AN
19 IMMINENT AND SERIOUS THREAT TO LIFE, HEALTH, OR PUBLIC SAFETY.
20 (3) AS PART OF AN ACTION TO ENFORCE THIS SUBTITLE, THE
21 ATTORNEY GENERAL SHALL PROVIDE WRITTEN CERTIFICATION OF COMPLIANCE
22 WITH PARAGRAPH (2) OF THIS SUBSECTION.
23 (4) (I) IN AN ACTION UNDER THIS SUBSECTION, THE COURT MAY
24 AWARD:
25 1. ECONOMIC AND NONECONOMIC DAMAGES TO ANY
26 PERSON HARMED BY A VIOLATION OF THIS SUBTITLE;
27 2. INJUNCTIVE RELIEF; AND
28 3. DECLARATIVE RELIEF.
29 (II) IN ADDITION TO ANY AWARD OF DAMAGES IMPOSED BY THE
30 COURT, A PERSON WHO VIOLATES THIS SECTION IS SUBJECT TO A CIVIL PENALTY
31 NOT EXCEEDING $25,000 FOR EACH VIOLATION.
6 HOUSE BILL 1536
1 (III) IN AN ACTION BROUGHT BY THE ATTORNEY GENERAL
2 UNDER THIS SUBSECTION, THE ATTORNEY GENERAL IS ENTITLED TO RECOVER THE
3 COSTS OF THE ACTION FOR THE USE OF THE STATE.
4 3–2704.
5 (A) IF A PERSON THAT HAS ENTERED INTO A PROCUREMENT CONTRACT
6 WITH THE STATE VIOLATES § 3–2702 OF THIS SUBTITLE, THE BOARD OF PUBLIC
7 WORKS MAY:
8 (1) VOID THE PROCUREMENT CONTRACT;
9 (2) DECLINE TO RENEW THE PROCUREMENT AT EXPIRATION; OR
10 (3) IMPOSE CONDITIONS ON THE CONTINUATION OF THE
11 PROCUREMENT CONTRACT.
12 (B) ANY PROCUREMENT CONTRACT WITH THE STATE SHALL INCLUDE A
13 REQUIREMENT THAT THE PERSON ENTERING INTO THE PROCUREMENT CONTRACT:
14 (1) NOT VOLUNTARILY AID IN IMMIGRATION ENFORCEMENT IN
15 VIOLATION OF THIS SUBTITLE; AND
16 (2) RESPECT ALL RIGHTS OF OTHERS THAT ARE SECURED BY LAW.
17 3–2705.
18 (A) AN APPLICABLE LICENSING AUTHORITY MAY INSTITUTE A
19 DISCIPLINARY PROCEEDING AGAINST, AND MAY SUSPEND OR REVOKE THE
20 LICENSURE OF A LICENSEE, IF THE LICENSEE VIOLATES:
21 (1) § 3–2702 OF THIS SUBTITLE;
22 (2) THE REQUIREMENTS OF THIS SUBTITLE; OR
23 (3) AN INDIVIDUAL’S RIGHTS SECURED BY THE MARYLAND
24 CONSTITUTION OR THE MARYLAND DECLARATION OF RIGHTS.
25 (B) A LICENSEE OR AN APPLICANT FOR A LICENSE SHALL, BEFORE A
26 LICENSE IS ISSUED OR RENEWED AND AS A PART OF THE APPLICABLE LICENSING
27 PROCEDURE, CERTIFY IN WRITING THAT THE APPLICANT OR LICENSEE WILL
28 RESPECT ALL RIGHTS OF OTHERS THAT ARE SECURED BY LAW.
