A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Family Law – Final Protective Orders – Duration
3 FOR the purpose of requiring all relief granted under a final protective order to remain
4 effective against a respondent on probation during the period of the respondent’s
5 probation; and generally relating to final protective orders.
6 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
7 Article – Family Law
8 Section 4–506(j)
9 Annotated Code of Maryland
10 (2019 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

16 subsection, all relief granted in a final protective order shall be effective for the period

20 stated in the order, not to exceed 2 years if:
