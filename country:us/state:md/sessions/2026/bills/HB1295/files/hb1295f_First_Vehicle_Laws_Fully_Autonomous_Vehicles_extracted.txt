13 21–1501.
14 (A) IN THIS SUBTITLE THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
15 INDICATED.
16 (B) “AUTOMATED DRIVING SYSTEM” MEANS THE HARDWARE AND
17 SOFTWARE THAT ARE COLLECTIVELY CAPABLE OF PERFORMING THE ENTIRE
18 DYNAMIC DRIVING TASK ON A SUSTAINED BASIS, REGARDLESS OF WHETHER IT IS
19 LIMITED TO A SPECIFIC OPERATIONAL DESIGN DOMAIN.
20 (C) “DYNAMIC DRIVING TASK” MEANS ALL OF THE REAL–TIME
21 OPERATIONAL FUNCTIONS REQUIRED TO OPERATE A MOTOR VEHICLE ON A
22 HIGHWAY, INCLUDING:
23 (1) LATERAL VEHICLE MOTION CONTROL THROUGH STEERING;
24 (2) LONGITUDINAL MOTION CONTROL THROUGH ACCELERATION
25 AND DECELERATION;
26 (3) MONITORING THE DRIVING ENVIRONMENT THROUGH OBJECT
27 AND EVENT DETECTION, RECOGNITION, CLASSIFICATION, AND RESPONSE
28 PREPARATION;

1 (4) OBJECT AND EVENT RESPONSE EXECUTION;
2 (5) MANEUVER PLANNING; AND
3 (6) SIGNALING WITH LIGHTS OR GESTURES.
4 (D) (1) “FULLY AUTONOMOUS VEHICLE” MEANS A MOTOR VEHICLE
5 EQUIPPED WITH AN AUTOMATED DRIVING SYSTEM DESIGNED TO FUNCTION
6 WITHOUT A HUMAN DRIVER.
7 (2) “FULLY AUTONOMOUS VEHICLE” INCLUDES A MOTOR VEHICLE
8 EQUIPPED WITH A LEVEL 4 OR 5 SYSTEM AS DEFINED BY “TAXONOMY AND
9 DEFINITIONS FOR TERMS RELATED TO DRIVING AUTOMATION SYSTEMS FOR
10 ON–ROAD MOTOR VEHICLES” PUBLISHED BY THE SOCIETY OF AUTOMOTIVE
11 ENGINEERS INTERNATIONAL IN APRIL 2021.
12 (E) “HUMAN DRIVER” MEANS AN INDIVIDUAL IN A VEHICLE WITH A VALID
13 LICENSE TO OPERATE A MOTOR VEHICLE WHO IS ABLE TO PERFORM THE DYNAMIC
14 DRIVING TASK.
15 (F) “MINIMAL RISK CONDITION” MEANS A STABLE, STOPPED CONDITION TO
16 WHICH A HUMAN DRIVER OR AN AUTOMATED DRIVING SYSTEM MAY BRING A FULLY
17 AUTONOMOUS VEHICLE AFTER PERFORMING THE DYNAMIC DRIVING TASK
18 FALLBACK TO REDUCE THE RISK OF A CRASH WHEN A GIVEN TRIP CANNOT OR
19 SHOULD NOT BE CONTINUED.
20 (G) “OPERATIONAL DESIGN DOMAIN” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN §
21 15–901 OF THIS ARTICLE.

29 AUTONOMOUS VEHICLE WILL ACHIEVE A MINIMAL RISK CONDITION;
