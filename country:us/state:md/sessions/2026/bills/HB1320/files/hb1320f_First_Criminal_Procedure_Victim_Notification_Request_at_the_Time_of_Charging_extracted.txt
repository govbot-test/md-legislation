A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Criminal Procedure – Victim Notification – Request at the Time of Charging
3 FOR the purpose of requiring a law enforcement officer or a District Court commissioner to
4 inform a victim or a victim’s representative of the ability of the victim or the victim’s
5 representative to request a certain notification at a certain time; establishing a
6 process for a victim or a victim’s representative to receive a certain notification; and
7 generally relating to victim notification.

1 (3) “Statewide DNA database system” has the meaning stated in § 2–501
2 of the Public Safety Article.
3 (4) “Victim” means a person who suffers actual or threatened physical,
4 emotional, or financial harm as a direct result of a crime or delinquent act.
5 (5) “Victim’s representative” includes a family member or guardian of a
6 victim who is:
7 (i) a minor;
8 (ii) deceased; or
9 (iii) disabled.
10 (b) (1) On first contact with a victim or victim’s representative, a law
11 enforcement officer, District Court commissioner, or juvenile intake officer shall give the
12 victim or the victim’s representative the pamphlet described in § 11–914(9)(i) of this title.
13 (2) ON FIRST CONTACT WITH A VICTIM OR VICTIM’S REPRESENTATIVE
14 IN A CRIMINAL CASE, A LAW ENFORCEMENT OFFICER OR DISTRICT COURT
15 COMMISSIONER SHALL INFORM THE VICTIM OR THE VICTIM’S REPRESENTATIVE OF
16 THE ABILITY OF THE VICTIM OR THE VICTIM’S REPRESENTATIVE TO REQUEST
17 NOTIFICATION THROUGH THE MARYLAND ELECTRONIC COURTS (MDEC) SYSTEM
18 OF A DEFENDANT’S CUSTODY STATUS AND COURT HEARINGS.
19 (3) IF A LAW ENFORCEMENT OFFICER RECEIVES A REQUEST FOR
20 NOTIFICATION FROM A VICTIM OR VICTIM’S REPRESENTATIVE UNDER THIS
21 SUBSECTION, THE LAW ENFORCEMENT OFFICER SHALL PROVIDE THE REQUEST TO
22 THE DISTRICT COURT COMMISSIONER AT THE TIME OF CHARGING.
23 (4) (I) ON RECEIVING A REQUEST FOR NOTIFICATION FROM A
24 VICTIM, A VICTIM’S REPRESENTATIVE, OR A LAW ENFORCEMENT OFFICER UNDER
25 THIS SUBSECTION, A DISTRICT COURT COMMISSIONER SHALL ENTER THE REQUEST
26 INTO THE MDEC SYSTEM.
27 (II) AFTER THE DISTRICT COURT COMMISSIONER ENTERS THE
28 REQUEST INTO THE MDEC SYSTEM, THE STATE’S VICTIM INFORMATION AND
29 NOTIFICATION EVERYDAY VENDOR SHALL NOTIFY THE VICTIM OR THE VICTIM’S
30 REPRESENTATIVE OF THE DEFENDANT’S RELEASE BY THE COMMISSIONER, OTHER
31 RELEASE FROM CUSTODY, AND SCHEDULED COURT HEARINGS.
32 (d) (1) Within 10 days after the filing or the unsealing of an indictment or
33 information in circuit court, whichever is later, the prosecuting attorney shall:

1 (i) mail or deliver to the victim or victim’s representative the
2 pamphlet described in § 11–914(9)(ii) of this title and the notification request form
3 described in § 11–914(10) of this title; and
4 (ii) certify to the clerk of the court that the prosecuting attorney has
5 complied with this paragraph or is unable to identify the victim or victim’s representative.
6 (2) If the prosecuting attorney files a petition alleging that a child is
7 delinquent for committing an act that could only be tried in the circuit court if committed
8 by an adult, the prosecuting attorney shall:
9 (i) inform the victim or victim’s representative of the right to
10 request restitution under § 11–606 of this title;
11 (ii) mail or deliver to the victim or victim’s representative the
12 notification request form described in § 11–914(10) of this title; and
13 (iii) certify to the clerk of the juvenile court that the prosecuting
14 attorney has complied with this paragraph or is unable to identify the victim or victim’s
15 representative.
16 (3) For cases described under this subsection, the prosecuting attorney
17 may provide a State’s witness in the case with the guidelines for victims, victims’
18 representatives, and witnesses available under §§ 11–1001 through 11–1004 of this title.
19 (4) WITHIN 10 DAYS AFTER THE FILING OF A CRIMINAL INFORMATION
20 IN THE DISTRICT COURT, THE PROSECUTING ATTORNEY SHALL INFORM THE VICTIM
21 OR THE VICTIM’S REPRESENTATIVE OF THE ABILITY OF THE VICTIM OR THE VICTIM’S
22 REPRESENTATIVE TO REQUEST NOTIFICATION THROUGH THE MDEC SYSTEM OF A
23 DEFENDANT’S CUSTODY STATUS AND COURT HEARINGS.
24 (e) (1) A victim or victim’s representative may:
25 (i) file a completed notification request form with the prosecuting
26 attorney; or
27 (ii) follow the MDEC system protocol to request notice.
28 (2) (i) If the jurisdiction has not implemented the MDEC system, the
29 prosecuting attorney shall send a copy of the completed notification request form to the
30 clerk of the circuit court or juvenile court.
31 (ii) If the jurisdiction has implemented the MDEC system and the
32 victim or victim’s representative has filed a completed notification request form, the
33 prosecuting attorney shall electronically file the form with the clerk of the circuit court or
34 juvenile court in the MDEC system.

1 (3) By filing a completed notification request form or completing the MDEC
