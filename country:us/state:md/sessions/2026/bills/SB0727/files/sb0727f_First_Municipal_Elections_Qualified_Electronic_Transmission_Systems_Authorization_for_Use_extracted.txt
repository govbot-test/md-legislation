G1, L3 6lr3398
CF HB 1066
By: Senators Lewis Young and Brooks
Introduced and read first time: February 6, 2026
Assigned to: Education, Energy, and the Environment
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Municipal Elections – Qualified Electronic Transmission Systems –
3 Authorization for Use
4 FOR the purpose of authorizing a municipality to conduct a municipal election through the
5 use of a qualified electronic transmission system; prohibiting a municipality from
6 using information received from a voter using a qualified electronic transmission
7 system for certain purposes; and generally relating to the use of qualified electronic
8 transmission systems in municipal elections.
9 BY adding to
10 Article – Local Government
11 Section 4–107.1
12 Annotated Code of Maryland
13 (2013 Volume and 2025 Supplement)
14 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
15 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
16 Article – Local Government
17 4–107.1.
18 (A) IN THIS SECTION, “QUALIFIED ELECTRONIC TRANSMISSION SYSTEM”
19 MEANS AN ELECTRONIC TRANSMISSION SYSTEM THROUGH WHICH A QUALIFIED
20 VOTER MAY RECEIVE, MARK, AND RETURN A BALLOT AND THAT MAINTAINS
21 REASONABLE DATA SECURITY AND VOTER INTEGRITY PROTECTIONS, INCLUDING:
22 (1) SECURITY AUDITABILITY;
23 (2) END–TO–END ENCRYPTION;
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from exist in g l aw . *sb0727*
2 SENATE BILL 727
1 (3) END–TO–END VERIFIABILITY;
2 (4) MULTIFACTOR AUTHENTICATION;
3 (5) AIR–GAPPING;
4 (6) A PROCESS FOR PRINTING DECRYPTED BALLOTS TO CREATE A
5 PAPER RECORD; AND
6 (7) BIOMETRIC OR EQUIVALENT DIGITAL IDENTITY VERIFICATION
7 TECHNOLOGIES.
8 (B) A MUNICIPALITY MAY CONDUCT A MUNICIPAL ELECTION THROUGH THE
9 USE OF A QUALIFIED ELECTRONIC TRANSMISSION SYSTEM.
10 (C) A MUNICIPALITY MAY NOT USE INFORMATION RECEIVED FROM A VOTER
11 USING A QUALIFIED ELECTRONIC TRANSMISSION SYSTEM IN A MUNICIPAL
12 ELECTION FOR ANY PURPOSE OTHER THAN ELECTION ADMINISTRATION AND VOTER
13 PARTICIPATION STATISTICS.
14 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
15 1, 2026.
