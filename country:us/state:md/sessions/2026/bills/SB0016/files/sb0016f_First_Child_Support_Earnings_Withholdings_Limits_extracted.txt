A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Child Support – Earnings Withholdings Limits
3 FOR the purpose of specifying that the amount of earnings withholding or a wage
4 withholding order for child support may not exceed a certain percentage of the
5 obligor’s disposable earnings under certain circumstances; requiring an earnings
6 withholding order or earnings withholding notice sent to an obligor’s employer to
7 include information on certain limits on earnings withholding; authorizing an
8 employee to contest the amount of an earnings withholding order based on certain
9 limits; and generally relating to limits on the amount of earnings withholding for
10 child support obligors.
11 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
12 Article – Family Law
13 Section 10–101(a) and (b)
14 Annotated Code of Maryland
15 (2019 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
16 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
17 Article – Family Law
18 Section 10–122, 10–128, 10–138, and 12–102.3
19 Annotated Code of Maryland
20 (2019 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
21 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
22 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
23 Article – Family Law
24 10–101.

2 SENATE BILL 16
1 (a) In this title the following words have the meanings indicated.
2 (b) “Administration” means the Child Support Administration of the Department
3 of Human Services.
4 10–122.
5 (a) IN THIS SECTION, “DISPOSABLE EARNINGS” MEANS THAT PART OF THE
6 EARNINGS OF AN INDIVIDUAL REMAINING AFTER THE DEDUCTION FROM THOSE
7 EARNINGS OF ANY AMOUNTS REQUIRED BY LAW TO BE WITHHELD.
8 (B) The amount of the earnings withholding [shall]:
9 (1) SHALL be enough to pay the support and any arrearage included in the
10 payments required by the support order; [and]
11 (2) SHALL include any arrearage accrued since the support order; AND
12 (3) MAY NOT EXCEED 25% OF THE OBLIGOR’S DISPOSABLE EARNINGS
13 FOR THE COMBINED SUPPORT ORDER AND ARREARAGE IF THE OBLIGOR’S
14 INDIVIDUAL INCOME FOR THE CURRENT YEAR DOES NOT EXCEED 250% OF THE
15 FEDERAL POVERTY GUIDELINES, UNLESS THE OBLIGOR WAS DETERMINED TO BE
16 VOLUNTARILY IMPOVERISHED AT THE TIME OF THE MOST RECENT COURT ORDER.
17 [(b)] (C) (1) (i) When arrearages under subsection [(a)(2)] (B)(2) of this
18 section are part of an earnings withholding order or earnings withholding notice, the total
19 arrearage withheld shall be in one lump–sum payment or apportioned over a period of time.
20 (ii) The amount of the arrearage withheld under subparagraph (i) of
21 this paragraph shall be determined by the tribunal or, in a case in which the
22 Administration is providing support services, by the Administration.
23 (2) The amount of arrears under subsection [(a)(2)] (B)(2) of this section
24 apportioned to each payment shall be at least $1 but not more than 25% of the current
25 support payment.
26 [(c)] (D) If there is more than one earnings withholding order or earnings
27 withholding notice against a single obligor, the Administration shall allocate amounts
28 available for withholding, giving priority to current support, up to the limits imposed by
29 the federal Consumer Credit Protection Act.
30 10–128.
31 (a) An earnings withholding order or an earnings withholding notice sent to the
32 obligor’s employer shall:
SENATE BILL 16 3
1 (1) be a separate document, and not include any other orders or pleadings;
2 and
3 (2) include only the following information:
4 (i) the amount to be withheld from the obligor’s earnings including
5 explanation of the application of the federal Consumer Credit Protection Act limits OR THE
6 LIMITS UNDER § 10–122 OF THIS SUBTITLE;
7 (ii) that subject to further orders of the tribunal, the employer is
8 required to withhold the stated amount on a regular and continuing basis commencing on
9 the beginning of the next pay period after receipt of the earnings withholding order or the
10 earnings withholding notice;
11 (iii) that the employer may deduct and retain from the employee’s
12 earnings an additional $2 for each deduction made under the earnings withholding order
13 or earnings withholding notice;
14 (iv) that the net amount withheld is to be sent promptly to the State
15 disbursement unit; and
16 (v) any other information that the employer needs to comply with
17 the earnings withholding order or earnings withholding notice.
18 (b) An earnings withholding order or earnings withholding notice is binding on
19 each present and future employer of the obligor on whom a copy of the earnings withholding
20 order or earnings withholding notice is served.
21 (c) Subject to federal law, an earnings withholding order or earnings withholding
22 notice under this Part III of this subtitle has priority over any other lien or legal process.
23 (d) The copy of the earnings withholding order or earnings withholding notice
24 served on the employer of the obligor shall contain a statement that upon willful violation
25 of the earnings withholding order or earnings withholding notice the employer shall be
26 subject to civil penalties.
27 10–138.
28 (a) Upon request of the obligor, the court shall immediately authorize service of
29 an earnings withholding order.
30 (b) Notwithstanding any other provision of this Part III, a court may at any time
31 issue an earnings withholding order, in a contempt or other proceeding, if:
32 (1) the recipient or the support enforcement agency has filed a petition that
33 includes a request for an earnings withholding order; and
4 SENATE BILL 16
1 (2) the obligor is in arrears in support payments of more than 30 days.
2 (c) A hearing shall be held if the obligor appears and contests the issuance of the
3 order.
4 (d) The amount of the wage withholding order entered under this subsection:
5 (1) shall be enough to pay the support as originally entered by the court;
6 [and]
7 (2) may include a part of the arrearage; AND
8 (3) MAY NOT EXCEED 25% OF THE OBLIGOR’S DISPOSABLE EARNINGS
9 FOR THE COMBINED SUPPORT ORDER AND ARREARAGE IF THE OBLIGOR’S
10 INDIVIDUAL INCOME FOR THE CURRENT YEAR DOES NOT EXCEED 250% OF THE
11 FEDERAL POVERTY GUIDELINES, UNLESS THE OBLIGOR WAS DETERMINED TO BE
12 VOLUNTARILY IMPOVERISHED AT THE TIME OF THE MOST RECENT COURT ORDER.
13 12–102.3.
14 (a) (1) This section applies to administrative contests of withholdings from an
15 employee’s earnings made by an employer for the purpose of complying with this title.
16 (2) Nothing in this section may be construed to limit an employee’s right to
17 judicially contest an underlying court order requiring the employee to provide health
18 insurance coverage for the employee’s child.
19 (b) (1) An employee may only contest a withholding under this section based
20 on a mistake of fact.
21 (2) The only issues that may be contested are:
22 (i) the identity of the employee;
23 (ii) whether there is an underlying court order requiring the
24 employee to provide health insurance coverage for the employee’s child;
25 (iii) that the amount of the withholding exceeds the limits of the
26 federal Consumer Credit Protection Act OR THE LIMITS UNDER § 10–122 OF THIS
27 ARTICLE; and
28 (iv) that the child for whom health insurance coverage is sought is
29 emancipated.
SENATE BILL 16 5
1 (c) An employee may contest a withholding by sending a written request for an
2 investigation to the Administration within 15 days after receiving notice of the withholding
3 from the employer.
4 (d) If an employee requests an investigation, the Administration:
5 (1) shall conduct an investigation within 15 days after the request; and
6 (2) on completion of the investigation, shall notify the employee of the
7 results of the investigation and the employee’s right to appeal the decision of the
8 Administration to the Office of Administrative Hearings.
9 (e) (1) (i) An employee may appeal the Administration’s decision to the
10 Office of Administrative Hearings by filing a written request for a hearing with the
11 Administration or the Office of Administrative Hearings.
12 (ii) The request for a hearing shall be made:
13 1. on a form provided by the Administration; and
14 2. within 15 days after receiving the written results of the
15 Administration’s investigation.
16 (2) The only issues that may be contested in an administrative hearing are:
17 (i) the identity of the employee;
18 (ii) whether there is an underlying court order requiring the
19 employee to provide health insurance coverage for the employee’s child;
20 (iii) that the amount of the withholding exceeds the limits of the
21 federal Consumer Credit Protection Act OR THE LIMITS UNDER § 10–122 OF THIS
22 ARTICLE; and
23 (iv) that the child for whom health insurance coverage is sought is
24 emancipated.
25 (3) An appeal under this section shall be conducted in accordance with Title
26 10, Subtitle 2 of the State Government Article.
27 (f) Enrollment of the employee’s child may not be stayed or terminated until the
28 employer receives written notice that the contest is resolved in the employee’s favor.
29 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
30 October 1, 2026.
