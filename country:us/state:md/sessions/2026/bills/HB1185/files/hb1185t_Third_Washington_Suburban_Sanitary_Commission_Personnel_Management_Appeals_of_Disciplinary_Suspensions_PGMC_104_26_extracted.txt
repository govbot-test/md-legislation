2 Washington Suburban Sanitary Commission – Personnel Management –
3 Disciplinary Actions Appeals of Disciplinary Suspensions
4 PG/MC 104–26
5 FOR the purpose of requiring the Washington Suburban Sanitary Commission to take
6 certain actions before suspending or removing an employee of the Commission;
7 providing that the Commission may take any disciplinary action not later than
8 within a certain number of days after the Commission acquires knowledge of the
9 misconduct for which the disciplinary action is imposed; authorizing an employee of
10 the Washington Suburban Sanitary Commission who is suspended for disciplinary
11 purposes to appeal to the Office of Administrative Hearings; requiring the Office to
12 dispose of a case or conduct a hearing and issue a final decision in an appeal under
13 this Act; and generally relating to the Washington Suburban Sanitary Commission.

15 16–101.

18 18–120.1.
19 (A) BEFORE TAKING ANY DISCIPLINARY ACTION UNDER § 18–121 OR §
20 18–123 OF THIS SUBTITLE, THE COMMISSION SHALL:
21 (1) INVESTIGATE THE ALLEGED MISCONDUCT;
22 (2) MEET WITH THE EMPLOYEE;
23 (3) CONSIDER ANY MITIGATING CIRCUMSTANCES;
24 (4) DETERMINE THE APPROPRIATE DISCIPLINARY ACTION, IF ANY, TO
25 BE IMPOSED; AND
26 (5) GIVE THE EMPLOYEE A WRITTEN NOTICE OF THE DISCIPLINARY
27 ACTION TO BE TAKEN AND THE EMPLOYEE’S APPEAL RIGHTS.
28 (B) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN SUBSECTION (C) OF THIS SECTION, THE
29 COMMISSION MAY IMPOSE ANY DISCIPLINARY ACTION NOT LATER THAN 30 DAYS

10 18–121.
11 (a) (1) The Commission may suspend an employee for disciplinary purposes
12 for up to 30 days.
13 [(b)] (2) Each suspension shall be without pay.
14 [(c)] (3) An employee may not be suspended for disciplinary purposes for more
15 than 30 days in a calendar year.
16 (B) AN EMPLOYEE WHO IS SUSPENDED FOR DISCIPLINARY PURPOSES MAY
17 APPEAL TO THE OFFICE OF ADMINISTRATIVE HEARINGS IN ACCORDANCE WITH §
18 4–401 OF THE STATE PERSONNEL AND PENSIONS ARTICLE.
19 18–123.
20 (a) An employee may not be permanently removed except for cause and after an
21 opportunity to be heard.
22 (b) An employee who is permanently removed may appeal to the Office of
23 Administrative Hearings in accordance with § 4–401 of the State Personnel and Pensions
24 Article.
25 (c) An employee may not be permanently removed from the merit system because
26 of religious or political opinions or affiliations.
