1 AN ACT concerning
2 Election Law – Election Misinformation, Election Disinformation, and
3 Deepfakes
4 FOR the purpose of requiring and authorizing the State Administrator of Elections to take
5 certain actions if the State Administrator receives a credible report that election
6 misinformation or election disinformation has been or is being communicated,
7 disseminated, or distributed; authorizing the State Board of Elections to file a certain
8 civil action related to election misinformation or election disinformation; prohibiting
9 a person, under certain circumstances, from knowingly or with reckless disregard,
10 creating, using, or disseminating a deepfake to produce materially false information;
11 and generally relating to election misinformation, election disinformation, and
12 deepfakes.
13 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,

10 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
11 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
12 Article – Election Law
13 2–110.
14 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
15 (2) “Election disinformation” means incorrect or misleading information
16 regarding the time, place, or manner of an election, election results, or voting rights in the
17 State that is knowingly and deliberately disseminated.
18 (3) “Election misinformation” means incorrect or misleading information
19 regarding the time, place, or manner of an election, election results, or voting rights in the
20 State.
21 (b) (1) The State Board shall maintain a portal on the State Board’s website
22 that the public may use to report election misinformation and election disinformation.
23 (2) The State Board shall conduct a periodic review of material submitted
24 by the public through the portal and, to the extent necessary, issue corrective information
25 or refer submissions to the State Prosecutor.
26 (C) (1) (I) IF THE STATE ADMINISTRATOR RECEIVES A CREDIBLE
27 REPORT THAT ELECTION MISINFORMATION OR ELECTION DISINFORMATION,
28 INCLUDING A DEEPFAKE, HAS BEEN OR IS BEING COMMUNICATED, DISSEMINATED,
29 OR DISTRIBUTED, THE STATE ADMINISTRATOR:
30 (I) 1. SHALL COMMUNICATE, DISSEMINATE, OR
31 DISTRIBUTE TO THE PUBLIC ACCURATE INFORMATION TO CORRECT THE ELECTION
32 MISINFORMATION OR ELECTION DISINFORMATION;
33 (II) 2. EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN SUBPARAGRAPH (II) OF THIS
34 PARAGRAPH, MAY SEEK AN INJUNCTION IN ACCORDANCE WITH THE MARYLAND

1 RULES FOR THE REMOVAL OF THE ELECTION MISINFORMATION OR ELECTION
2 DISINFORMATION FROM ANY ONLINE PLATFORM; AND
3 (III) 3. MAY ISSUE A SUBPOENA IN ACCORDANCE WITH THE
4 FEDERAL STORED COMMUNICATIONS ACT, IF APPLICABLE, FOR RECORDS RELATED
5 TO THE DISSEMINATION OR DISTRIBUTION OF THE ELECTION MISINFORMATION OR
6 ELECTION DISINFORMATION, INCLUDING INFORMATION RELATED TO THE
7 TARGETED AUDIENCE.
8 (II) THE STATE ADMINISTRATOR MAY NOT SEEK AN
9 INJUNCTION UNDER SUBPARAGRAPH (I)2 OF THIS PARAGRAPH AGAINST AN
10 INTERACTIVE COMPUTER SERVICE, AS DEFINED IN 47 U.S.C. § 230, FOR CONTENT
11 DISSEMINATED BY ANOTHER PERSON THROUGH THE INTERACTIVE COMPUTER
12 SERVICE.
13 (2) (I) THE STATE BOARD MAY NOT FILE A CIVIL ACTION UNDER
14 THIS PARAGRAPH AGAINST A PERSON:
15 1. EXEMPT FROM THE REQUIREMENTS OF § 16–905 OF
16 THIS ARTICLE; OR
17 2. WHO SEEKS TO DETECT, REPORT, PREVENT,
18 INVESTIGATE, RESPOND TO, OR PROSECUTE PERSONS RESPONSIBLE FOR SECURITY
19 BREACHES, IDENTITY THEFT, FRAUD, HARASSMENT, OR OTHER MALICIOUS OR
20 ILLEGAL ACTIVITY.
21 (II) THE STATE BOARD, IN CONSULTATION WITH THE STATE
22 ADMINISTRATOR, MAY FILE A CIVIL ACTION IN A COURT OF COMPETENT
23 JURISDICTION WITHIN THE STATE AGAINST A PERSON, CAMPAIGN, POLITICAL
24 ACTION COMMITTEE, OR OTHER LEGAL ENTITY IF THE STATE BOARD:
25 (I) 1. FINDS THAT THE ENTITY PUBLISHED PERSON
26 CREATED, USED, OR DISSEMINATED ELECTION MISINFORMATION OR ELECTION
27 DISINFORMATION:
28 A. ELECTION DISINFORMATION; OR
29 B. WITH RECKLESS DISREGARD, ELECTION
30 MISINFORMATION; AND
31 (II) 2. INCURS COSTS TO COMMUNICATE, DISSEMINATE, OR
32 DISTRIBUTE TO THE PUBLIC ACCURATE INFORMATION TO CORRECT THE ELECTION
33 MISINFORMATION OR ELECTION DISINFORMATION.

1 (3) (I) THE EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN SUBPARAGRAPH (II) OF THIS

7 CIVIL ACTION AGAINST A PERSON EXEMPT FROM THE REQUIREMENTS OF § 16–905
8 OF THIS ARTICLE.

10 FEES IN A CIVIL ACTION FILED UNDER PARAGRAPH (2)(II) OF THIS SUBSECTION
11 ONLY BASED ON A SHOWING BY CLEAR AND CONVINCING EVIDENCE THAT THE
12 PERSON, CAMPAIGN, POLITICAL ACTION COMMITTEE, OR OTHER LEGAL ENTITY
13 CREATED, USED, OR DISSEMINATED:
14 (I) ELECTION DISINFORMATION; OR
15 (II) WITH RECKLESS DISREGARD, ELECTION MISINFORMATION.
16 16–905.
17 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
18 INDICATED.
19 (2) “DEEPFAKE” MEANS AN IMAGE, AN AUDIO RECORDING, OR A
20 VIDEO RECORDING THAT HAS BEEN INTENTIONALLY CREATED OR MANIPULATED
21 WITH THE USE OF GENERATIVE ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE OR OTHER DIGITAL
22 TECHNOLOGY TO CREATE A REALISTIC BUT FALSE DEPICTION OF A PERSON THAT
23 AN ORDINARY PERSON WOULD CONCLUDE IS AN ACTUAL VISUAL OR AUDIO
24 REPRESENTATION OF THE PERSON DEPICTED.
25 (3) “INFLUENCE” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 16–201 OF THIS
26 TITLE.
27 (B) THIS SECTION DOES NOT APPLY:
28 (1) WITH RESPECT TO A DEEPFAKE THAT IS SATIRE OR PARODY;
29 (2) TO A RADIO OR TELEVISION BROADCASTING STATION, INCLUDING
30 A CABLE OR SATELLITE TELEVISION OPERATOR, PROGRAMMER, OR PRODUCER, OR
31 AN AFFILIATE OF THE RADIO OR TELEVISION BROADCASTING STATION, THAT
32 BROADCASTS A DECEPTIVE AND FRAUDULENT DEEPFAKE IF:
SENATE BILL 141 5
1 (I) THE BROADCAST IS REQUIRED BY THE FEDERAL LAW THAT
2 REQUIRES BROADCASTERS TO AIR ADVERTISEMENTS FROM LEGALLY QUALIFIED
3 CANDIDATES;
4 (II) THE BROADCAST:
5 1. IS PART OF A BONA FIDE NEWSCAST, A NEWS
6 INTERVIEW, A NEWS DOCUMENTARY, OR ON–THE–SPOT COVERAGE OF BONA FIDE
7 NEWS EVENTS; AND
8 2. CLEARLY IDENTIFIES TO THE AUDIENCE THROUGH
9 CONTENT OR A DISCLOSURE IN A MANNER THAT CAN BE EASILY HEARD OR READ BY
10 THE AVERAGE LISTENER OR VIEWER THAT THE DEEPFAKE MAY CONTAIN DECEPTIVE
11 AUDIO OR VISUAL MEDIA; OR
12 (III) THE BROADCASTER HAS:
13 1. BEEN PAID TO BROADCAST THE DEEPFAKE; AND
14 2. MADE A GOOD FAITH EFFORT TO ESTABLISH THAT
15 THE DEEPFAKE IS NOT A DECEPTIVE AND FRAUDULENT DEEPFAKE; OR
16 (3) TO A WEBSITE OR REGULARLY PUBLISHED NEWSPAPER,
17 MAGAZINE, OR OTHER PERIODICAL OF GENERAL CIRCULATION, INCLUDING AN
18 INTERNET OR ELECTRONIC PUBLICATION, THAT ROUTINELY CARRIES NEWS AND
19 COMMENTARY OF GENERAL INTEREST AND THAT PUBLISHES A DECEPTIVE AND
20 FRAUDULENT DEEPFAKE IF THE WEBSITE OR PERIODICAL CLEARLY STATES THAT
21 THE MATERIALLY DECEPTIVE AUDIO OR VISUAL MEDIA DOES NOT ACCURATELY
22 REPRESENT THE SPEECH OR CONDUCT OF THE CANDIDATE.; OR
23 (4) TO ANY ONLINE PUBLISHER OR SERVICE PROVIDER FOR ACTS
24 THAT WOULD BE SUBJECT TO IMMUNITY UNDER 47 U.S.C. § 230.
25 (C) A PERSON MAY NOT KNOWINGLY OR WITH RECKLESS DISREGARD USE
26 CREATE, USE, OR DISSEMINATE A DEEPFAKE TO PRODUCE MATERIALLY FALSE
27 INFORMATION IF:
28 (1) THE PERSON HAS THE INTENT TO USE THE DEEPFAKE TO:
29 (1) (I) IMPEDE, INFLUENCE, PREVENT, OR ATTEMPT TO IMPEDE,
30 INFLUENCE, OR PREVENT A VOTER’S DECISION TO CAST A BALLOT;

1 (2) (II) MISREPRESENT FACTS RELATING TO VOTER
2 REGISTRATION, VOTING METHODS, ELECTION RESULTS, ELECTORAL PROCESSES,
3 OR OFFICIAL DUTIES OF AN ELECTION OFFICIAL; OR
4 (3) (III) INDUCE OR ATTEMPT TO INDUCE ANOTHER INDIVIDUAL TO
5 SIGN OR NOT SIGN A PETITION FOR A QUESTION TO APPEAR ON A BALLOT.; OR
6 (IV) INFLUENCE OR ATTEMPT TO INFLUENCE A VOTER’S
7 DECISION WHETHER TO VOTE FOR OR AGAINST A CANDIDATE OR BALLOT ISSUE; AND
8 (2) THE CREATION, USE, OR DISSEMINATION OF THE DEEPFAKE
9 RESULTS, OR HAS THE INTENT TO RESULT, IN HARM TO A VOTER, POTENTIAL VOTER,
10 OR PETITION.
11 (D) A PERSON WHO VIOLATES SUBSECTION (C) OF THIS SECTION IS GUILTY
12 OF A MISDEMEANOR AND ON CONVICTION IS SUBJECT TO A FINE OF NOT MORE THAN
13 $5,000 OR IMPRISONMENT FOR NOT MORE THAN 5 YEARS OR BOTH.
14 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect June
15 1, 2026.
