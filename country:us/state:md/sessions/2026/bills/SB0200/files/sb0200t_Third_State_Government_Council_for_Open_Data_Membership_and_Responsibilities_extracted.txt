3 (b) “Council” means the Council [on] FOR Open Data.
4 (c) (1) “Data” means [final versions of] statistical or factual information that:
5 (i) are in alphanumeric or geospatial form reflected in a list, table,
6 graph, chart, map, or other nonnarrative format that can be digitally transmitted or
7 processed;
8 (ii) are regularly created or maintained by or on behalf of a
9 governmental entity; and
10 (iii) records a measurement, transaction, or determination or provide
11 information on government services, initiatives, and resources related to the mission of the
12 covered governmental entity.
13 (2) “Data” does not include draft versions of statistical or factual
14 information that are used for internal analysis by a governmental entity.

1 (e) (1) This subsection applies to members of the Council appointed under
2 subsection [(b)(28)] (B)(4) and [(29)] (5) of this section.
3 (2) The term of a member is 4 years[, except that five members may serve
4 an initial 3–year term as required by the terms provided for staggered members of the
5 Council on July 1, 2014].
6 (3) At the end of a term, a member continues to serve until a successor is
7 appointed and qualifies.
8 (4) A member who is appointed after a term has begun serves only for the
9 rest of the term and until a successor is appointed and qualifies.
10 (5) A member may not serve more than two consecutive terms.
11 (6) The Governor may remove a member for neglect of duty, incompetence,
12 or misconduct.
13 (f) A member of the Council may not receive compensation but is entitled to
14 reimbursement for expenses under the Standard State Travel Regulations as provided in
15 the State budget.
16 (g) [(1)] The [Secretary of Information Technology] STATE CHIEF DATA
17 OFFICER is the Chair of the Council.
18 [(2) The Director of the Governor’s Office of Performance Improvement is
19 the Vice Chair of the Council.]
20 (h) The staffing responsibilities of the Council shall be shared by the Department
21 of Information Technology[, the Governor’s Office of Performance Improvement,] and any
22 [other] staff designated by the Governor.
23 (i) The Council may establish workgroups as necessary to complete the duties of
24 the Council.
25 (j) The Council shall meet at least twice each year.
26 10–1504.
27 (a) The Council shall promote the policy established under § 10–1502 of this
28 subtitle by:
29 (1) providing guidance and policy recommendations and when appropriate
30 recommend legislation and regulations for:
6 SENATE BILL 200
1 (i) procedures, standards, and other deliverables for open data,
2 including for open data portals;
3 (ii) promotion, advertising, and marketing of open data; and
4 (iii) best practices for sharing open data while taking into account
5 privacy and security concerns;
6 (2) ADVISING ON DATA INITIATIVES AND PRIORITIES;
7 (3) IDENTIFYING AND EXPLORING THE MEANS BY WHICH
8 PARTNERSHIPS MAY BE DEVELOPED AND STRENGTHENED TO CONNECT MARYLAND
9 DATA WITH COMMUNITIES AND SOCIAL SERVICE ORGANIZATIONS;
10 [(2)] (4) coordinating the appropriate staff at each State entity for the
11 development, maintenance, and use of open data and open data portals;
12 [(3)] (5) (i) identifying the collective cost of operating and investing in
13 open data and funding mechanisms to support open data; and
14 (ii) advising the Governor and General Assembly on budget matters
15 related to open data;
16 [(4)] (6) inviting and encouraging local entities and the legislative and
17 judicial branches to:
18 (i) use open data portals established by State entities;
19 (ii) create their own open data portals; and
20 (iii) adopt policies consistent with the policy established under §
21 10–1502 of this subtitle;
22 [(5)] (7) establishing a plan for providing all open data to the public at no
23 cost;
24 [(6)] (8) advocating for sound records management and data preservation
25 practices; and
26 [(7)] (9) making recommendations to ensure that the purchase of new
27 data processing devices, systems, and software by the State includes a review of compliance
28 with the open data policy established under § 10–1502 of this subtitle and interoperability
29 with current technology used by the State.

1 (b) On or before January 10 of each year, the Council shall report to the Governor
2 and the General Assembly, in accordance with § 2–1257 of this article, on the activities of
3 the Council for the previous year and any recommendations for legislation.
4 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
5 1, 2026.
