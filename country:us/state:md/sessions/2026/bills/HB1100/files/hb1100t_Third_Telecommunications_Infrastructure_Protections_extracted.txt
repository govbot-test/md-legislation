1 AN ACT concerning
2 Telecommunications Infrastructure – Protections
3 FOR the purpose of requiring certain junk dealers, scrap metal processors, and antique
4 dealers to register and provide a description including certain information of
5 purchased telecommunications equipment with a certain law enforcement official;
6 prohibiting certain junk dealers, scrap metal processors, and antique dealers from
7 purchasing telecommunications equipment other than by check mailed to the seller’s
8 address; prohibiting the sale or disposal of certain telecommunications equipment
9 within a certain period; prohibiting certain individuals from destroying
10 telecommunications infrastructure in a certain manner; and generally relating to
11 telecommunications equipment and infrastructure.

13 Article – Business Regulation
14 Section 17–1001(a) and (f)(2)

12 17–1010.1.
13 (A) THIS SECTION APPLIES TO ALL JUNK DEALERS, SCRAP METAL
14 PROCESSORS, AND ANTIQUE DEALERS WHO ARE RESIDENTS OF THE STATE.
15 (B) (1) EACH JUNK DEALER, SCRAP METAL PROCESSOR, OR ANTIQUE
16 DEALER SUBJECT TO THIS SECTION WHO PURCHASES TELECOMMUNICATIONS
17 EQUIPMENT SHALL REGISTER WITH THE SHERIFF OR OTHER LAW ENFORCEMENT
18 OFFICIAL DESIGNATED BY THE GOVERNING BODY OF THE COUNTY IN WHICH THE
19 BUSINESS OF THE JUNK DEALER, SCRAP METAL PROCESSOR, OR ANTIQUE DEALER
20 IS LOCATED A COMPLETE DESCRIPTION OF THE TELECOMMUNICATIONS
21 EQUIPMENT.
22 (2) THE DESCRIPTION SHALL INCLUDE:
23 (I) THE DATE AND TIME OF PURCHASE;
24 (II) THE NAME AND ADDRESS OF THE SELLER; AND
25 (III) THE LICENSE TAG NUMBER OF ANY VEHICLE USED.
26 (C) A JUNK DEALER, SCRAP METAL PROCESSOR, OR ANTIQUE DEALER
27 SUBJECT TO THIS SECTION WHO PURCHASES TELECOMMUNICATIONS EQUIPMENT
28 MAY PURCHASE TELECOMMUNICATIONS EQUIPMENT ONLY BY ISSUING A CHECK
29 THAT IS MAILED TO THE SELLER’S ADDRESS.
4 HOUSE BILL 1100
1 (D) TELECOMMUNICATIONS EQUIPMENT MAY NOT BE SOLD OR OTHERWISE
2 DISPOSED OF FOR A PERIOD OF 30 DAYS AFTER THE DATE OF PURCHASE.
3 (E) A PERSON WHO VIOLATES THIS SECTION:
4 (1) IS SUBJECT TO REVOCATION OF ANY STATE JUNK LICENSE HELD
5 BY THE PERSON; AND
6 (2) IS GUILTY OF A MISDEMEANOR AND, ON CONVICTION, IS SUBJECT
7 TO A FINE NOT EXCEEDING $2,000.
8 Article – Criminal Law
9 6–305.1.
10 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
11 INDICATED.
12 (2) “TELECOMMUNICATIONS COMPANY” MEANS A COMPANY THAT
13 PROVIDES BROADBAND INTERNET, CABLE TELEVISION, TELEPHONE, OR OTHER
14 WIRELESS COMMUNICATION SERVICES.
15 (3) (I) “TELECOMMUNICATIONS INFRASTRUCTURE” MEANS ITEMS
16 THAT ARE USED TO PROVIDE BROADBAND INTERNET, CABLE TELEVISION,
17 TELEPHONE, OR OTHER WIRELESS COMMUNICATION SERVICES.
18 (II) “TELECOMMUNICATIONS INFRASTRUCTURE” INCLUDES:
19 1. ANTENNAE;
20 2. FIBER OPTIC AND COPPER CABLES;
21 3. HEADENDS;
22 4. TELEPHONE POLES;
23 5. RADIO TRANSCEIVERS;
24 6. RADIO TOWERS;
25 7. WIRELESS SUPPORT STRUCTURES;
26 8. SMALL CELL FACILITIES; AND
HOUSE BILL 1100 5
1 9. OFFICE FACILITIES.
2 (B) THIS SECTION DOES NOT APPLY TO:
3 (1) AN EMPLOYEE OF A TELECOMMUNICATIONS COMPANY OR A
4 PERSON ACTING UNDER THE AUTHORITY OF A TELECOMMUNICATIONS COMPANY;
5 OR
6 (2) SUPERVISION AND CONTROL OF A TELECOMMUNICATIONS
7 COMPANY AND ITS MATERIAL, EQUIPMENT, OR FACILITIES BY A POLITICAL
8 SUBDIVISION WITHIN WHICH THE TELECOMMUNICATIONS COMPANY IS DOING
9 BUSINESS.
10 (C) A PERSON MAY NOT RECKLESSLY OR WILLFULLY AND MALICIOUSLY
11 DEFACE, DAMAGE, OR DESTROY TELECOMMUNICATIONS INFRASTRUCTURE.
12 (D) A PERSON WHO VIOLATES THIS SECTION IS GUILTY OF A FELONY
13 MISDEMEANOR AND ON CONVICTION IS SUBJECT TO IMPRISONMENT NOT
14 EXCEEDING 6 MONTHS OR A FINE NOT EXCEEDING $5,000 $500 OR BOTH.
15 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
16 October 1, 2026.
