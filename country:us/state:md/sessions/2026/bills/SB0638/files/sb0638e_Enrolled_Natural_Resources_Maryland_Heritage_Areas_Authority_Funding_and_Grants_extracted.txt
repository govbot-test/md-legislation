29 (I) DEVELOP management plans in recognized heritage areas; AND

32 (B) IN AWARDING A GRANT OR LOAN UNDER SUBSECTION (A) OF THIS
33 SECTION, THE AUTHORITY MAY REQUIRE MATCHING FUNDS IN WHATEVER
34 PROPORTION THE AUTHORITY CONSIDERS APPROPRIATE.

21 (a) There is a Maryland Heritage Areas Authority Financing Fund.
22 (b) (1) The Authority shall use the Fund as a continuing, nonlapsing, revolving
23 fund for carrying out the purposes of this subtitle.
24 (2) No part of the Fund may revert or be credited to the General Fund or
25 to any other special fund of the State.
26 (c) The Authority shall place all of the following receipts in the Fund:
27 (1) Proceeds from the sale of bonds;
28 (2) Revenues that the Authority collects or receives from any source under
29 this subtitle; and
4 SENATE BILL 638
1 (3) Any additional revenue, gift, donation, or money received or paid to it
2 from any other source authorized by law.
3 (d) The Authority shall pay all expenses and make all expenditures from the
4 Fund.
5 (e) (1) The Authority may pledge and charge all or a portion of the receipts of
6 the Fund for the payment of:
7 (i) Debt service on bonds of the Authority; and
8 (ii) All reasonable charges and expenses related to borrowing by the
9 Authority and management of the obligations of the Authority.
10 (2) A pledge made under paragraph (1) of this subsection is effective as
11 provided in § 13–1119 of this subtitle and any applicable resolution of the Authority.
12 (f) The State Treasurer shall:
13 (1) Invest and reinvest the Fund in the same manner as State funds; and
14 (2) Transfer any investment earnings to the credit of the Fund.
15 (g) (1) In this subsection, “Program Open Space funds transferred to the
16 Authority” means the money appropriated to the Fund from Program Open Space funds
17 under § 5–903(a) of the Natural Resources Article.
18 (2) Except as provided in paragraph (3) of this subsection, Program Open
19 Space funds transferred to the Authority may not be used to pay the operating expenses of
20 the Authority, debt service of bonds issued by the Authority, or administrative expenses
21 related to bonds issued by the Authority.
22 (3) (i) Up to [10%] 7% OR $600,000, WHICHEVER AMOUNT IS
23 GREATER, of Program Open Space funds transferred to the Authority may be used to pay
24 the operating expenses of the Authority.
25 (ii) Up to 50% of Program Open Space funds transferred to the
26 Authority may be expended for debt service on bonds issued by the Authority.
27 (iii) For fiscal year 2012 only, an additional $500,000 of Program
28 Open Space funds transferred to the Authority may be used to pay operating expenses in
29 the Department of Planning.
30 (iv) For fiscal year 2026 only, an additional $340,000 of Program
31 Open Space funds transferred to the Authority may be used to pay operating expenses in
32 the Department of Planning.

1 Article – Natural Resources
2 5–903.
3 (a) (1) [(i)] Of the funds distributed to Program Open Space under § 13–209
4 of the Tax – Property Article, up to $3,000,000 may be transferred by an appropriation in
5 the State budget, or by an amendment to the State budget under Title 7, Subtitle 2 of the
6 State Finance and Procurement Article, to the Maryland Heritage Areas Authority
7 Financing Fund established under Title 13, Subtitle 11 of the Financial Institutions Article
8 to be used for the purposes provided in that subtitle.
9 [(ii) Of the amount transferred under subparagraph (i) of this
10 paragraph, up to $300,000 may be distributed to the Maryland Historical Trust within the
11 Department of Planning to be awarded as noncapital historic preservation grants.]
12 (2) (i) 1. Of the remaining funds not appropriated under paragraph
13 [(1)(i)] (1) of this subsection:
14 A. One half of the funds shall be used for recreation and open
15 space purposes by the Department and the Historic St. Mary’s City Commission; and
16 B. 20% of the funds or $21,000,000, whichever is greater,
17 shall be appropriated to the Forest and Park Service in the Department to operate State
18 forests and parks.
19 2. Except as otherwise provided in this section, any funds the
20 General Assembly appropriates to the State under this subsection shall be used only for
21 land acquisition projects.
22 (iv) In addition to the $3,000,000 under paragraph [(1)(i)] (1) of this
23 subsection that may be transferred to the Maryland Heritage Areas Authority Financing
24 Fund, up to [$3,000,000] $9,000,000 of the State’s share of funds available under
25 subparagraph (i)1A of this paragraph may be transferred by an appropriation in the State
26 budget or by an amendment to the State budget under Title 7, Subtitle 2 of the State
27 Finance and Procurement Article to the Maryland Heritage Areas Authority Financing
28 Fund established under Title 13, Subtitle 11 of the Financial Institutions Article to be used
29 for the purposes provided in that subtitle.
30 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
31 1, 2026.
