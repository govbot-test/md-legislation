By: Delegate Solomon (By Request – Joint Audit and Evaluation Committee)
Introduced and read first time: February 13, 2026
Assigned to: Government, Labor, and Elections and Judiciary
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Department of State Police – Centralized Background Check Division
3 FOR the purpose of establishing the Centralized Background Check Division in the
4 Department of State Police; requiring the Division to require each unit of State and
5 local government to take certain actions; requiring the Division to provide or
6 coordinate certain support services to units of State and local government; providing
7 for the process and confidentiality of a position–related background investigation;
8 requiring a certain unit of State or local government to include a certain condition of
9 funding for a regulated position funded by the unit; providing for the loss of a certain
10 contract or debarment under certain circumstances for a violation of this Act; and
11 generally relating to the Centralized Background Check Division.
12 BY adding to
13 Article – Public Safety
14 Section 2–901 through 2–906 to be under the new subtitle “Subtitle 9. Centralized
15 Background Check Division”
16 Annotated Code of Maryland
17 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
18 BY adding to
19 Article – State Finance and Procurement
20 Section 16–203(e)
21 Annotated Code of Maryland
22 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
23 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
24 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
25 Article – Public Safety
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

2 HOUSE BILL 1419
1 SUBTITLE 9. CENTRALIZED BACKGROUND CHECK DIVISION.
2 2–901.
3 (A) IN THIS SUBTITLE THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
4 INDICATED.
5 (B) “DESIGNATED INDIVIDUAL” MEANS AN EMPLOYEE OF A UNIT OF STATE
6 OR LOCAL GOVERNMENT RESPONSIBLE FOR ALL POSITION–RELATED BACKGROUND
7 INVESTIGATION PROCESSES AND PROCEDURES.
8 (C) “DISQUALIFYING EVENT” MEANS AN EVENT, INCLUDING AN ARREST, AN
9 ADJUDICATION, A CONVICTION, OR A REQUIREMENT FOR REGISTRATION AS A SEX
10 OFFENDER, FOR WHICH AN INDIVIDUAL IS INELIGIBLE TO HOLD A REGULATED
11 POSITION.
12 (D) “DIVISION” MEANS THE CENTRALIZED BACKGROUND CHECK
13 DIVISION.
14 (E) (1) “EMPLOYEE OR SERVICE PROVIDER” MEANS AN INDIVIDUAL WHO
15 PROVIDES SERVICES TO AN EMPLOYER.
16 (2) “EMPLOYEE OR SERVICE PROVIDER” INCLUDES:
17 (I) A PAID OR UNPAID VOLUNTEER OR INTERN;
18 (II) A CONTRACTOR, SUBCONTRACTOR, OR VENDOR; AND
19 (III) ANY OTHER INDIVIDUAL WHO PROVIDES GOODS OR
20 SERVICES TO A UNIT OF STATE OR LOCAL GOVERNMENT AS DETERMINED BY THE
21 DIVISION.
22 (F) “REGULATED POSITION” MEANS A POSITION AS AN EMPLOYEE OR
23 SERVICE PROVIDER WITHIN OR FUNDED BY A UNIT OF STATE OR LOCAL
24 GOVERNMENT THAT:
25 (1) IS REQUIRED BY STATE OR LOCAL LAW, REGULATION, OR POLICY
26 TO HAVE A POSITION–RELATED BACKGROUND INVESTIGATION; AND
27 (2) PROHIBITED ACTIVITY WOULD DISQUALIFY AN INDIVIDUAL FROM
28 HOLDING.
29 2–902.
HOUSE BILL 1419 3
1 (A) THERE IS A CENTRALIZED BACKGROUND CHECK DIVISION IN THE
2 DEPARTMENT.
3 (B) THE DIVISION, IN COORDINATION WITH THE DEPARTMENT OF PUBLIC
4 SAFETY AND CORRECTIONAL SERVICES, IS RESPONSIBLE FOR OVERSIGHT OF
5 POSITION–RELATED BACKGROUND INVESTIGATIONS OF INDIVIDUALS WHO HOLD OR
6 HAVE BEEN SELECTED TO HOLD REGULATED POSITIONS.
7 (C) IN COORDINATION WITH THE DEPARTMENT OF PUBLIC SAFETY AND
8 CORRECTIONAL SERVICES, THE DIVISION SHALL:
9 (1) ESTABLISH STANDARDS AND BEST PRACTICES FOR THE
10 CENTRALIZATION AND EFFICIENCY OF BACKGROUND INVESTIGATION PROCESSES
11 STATEWIDE;
12 (2) WORK WITH STATE AGENCIES AND LOCAL GOVERNMENTS TO
13 ENSURE THAT REQUIRED POSITION–RELATED BACKGROUND INVESTIGATIONS ARE
14 CONDUCTED FOR INDIVIDUALS WHO HOLD APPROPRIATE POSITIONS AND ROLES;
15 (3) ENSURE THAT ALL POSITION–RELATED BACKGROUND
16 INVESTIGATIONS ARE ACCOMPANIED BY ENROLLMENT IN THE FEDERAL BUREAU
17 OF INVESTIGATION NEXT GENERATION IDENTIFICATION RAP BACK PROGRAM; AND
18 (4) ADOPT ANY RULE, REGULATION, AND POLICY NECESSARY TO
19 IMPLEMENT THIS SUBTITLE.
20 (D) THERE IS AN EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR OF THE DIVISION, APPOINTED BY
21 THE SECRETARY.
22 (E) THE DIVISION SHALL REQUIRE EACH UNIT OF STATE AND LOCAL
23 GOVERNMENT TO:
24 (1) APPOINT A DESIGNATED INDIVIDUAL TO OVERSEE THE
25 POSITION–RELATED BACKGROUND INVESTIGATION PROCESS AND REPORT TO THE
26 DIVISION ON ALL BACKGROUND INVESTIGATION–RELATED ISSUES;
27 (2) NOTIFY THE DIVISION WHEN THE DESIGNATED INDIVIDUAL OR A
28 REGULATED POSITION HAS CHANGED; AND
29 (3) REQUIRE THE DESIGNATED INDIVIDUAL TO UNDERGO TRAINING
30 ON THE APPROPRIATE ROLE AND RESPONSIBILITIES OF A DESIGNATED INDIVIDUAL.
4 HOUSE BILL 1419
1 (F) THE DIVISION SHALL PROVIDE OR COORDINATE SUPPORT SERVICES TO
2 UNITS OF STATE AND LOCAL GOVERNMENT, INCLUDING:
3 (1) TECHNICAL SERVICES AND SUPPORT FOR THE IMPLEMENTATION
4 OF RAP BACK; AND
5 (2) PROVIDING ADVICE ON THE PROTECTION OF ALL INDIVIDUALS,
6 INCLUDING MINORS, BY STRENGTHENING HIRING POLICIES AND BACKGROUND
7 INVESTIGATION PROCEDURES.
8 2–903.
9 FOR EACH EMPLOYEE OR SERVICE PROVIDER SUBJECT TO A
10 POSITION–RELATED BACKGROUND INVESTIGATION FOR A REGULATED POSITION:
11 (1) THE DESIGNATED INDIVIDUAL FOR A UNIT OF STATE OR LOCAL
12 GOVERNMENT SHALL PROVIDE THE DIVISION ALL INFORMATION REQUIRED TO
13 CONDUCT THE POSITION–RELATED BACKGROUND INVESTIGATION; AND
14 (2) THE DIVISION SHALL CONDUCT A POSITION–RELATED
15 BACKGROUND INVESTIGATION PROMPTLY ON RECEIPT OF A REQUEST FROM A UNIT
16 OF STATE OR LOCAL GOVERNMENT.
17 2–904.
18 (A) IN PERFORMING A POSITION–RELATED BACKGROUND INVESTIGATION
19 UNDER THIS SUBTITLE, THE DIVISION MAY REQUEST THE ASSISTANCE, AS NEEDED,
20 OF THE CENTRAL REPOSITORY, THE POLICE COMMISSIONER OF BALTIMORE CITY,
21 THE CHIEF OF POLICE IN ANY COUNTY OR MUNICIPALITY MAINTAINING A POLICE
22 FORCE, OR THE SHERIFF IN A COUNTY NOT MAINTAINING A POLICE FORCE.
23 (B) THE DIVISION SHALL UPDATE INITIAL CRIMINAL HISTORY RECORDS
24 FOR A POSITION–RELATED BACKGROUND INVESTIGATION UNDER THIS SECTION IN
25 ACCORDANCE WITH FEDERAL REQUIREMENTS FOR THE FEDERAL BUREAU OF
26 INVESTIGATION NEXT GENERATION IDENTIFICATION RAP BACK PROGRAM,
27 APPLICABLE REGULATIONS, AND OTHER REQUIREMENTS UNDER THIS SECTION.
28 (C) THE DIVISION SHALL PROMPTLY TRANSMIT THE RESULTS OF THE
29 POSITION–RELATED BACKGROUND INVESTIGATION AND ANY SUBSEQUENT UPDATE
30 TO:
31 (1) THE UNIT OF STATE OR LOCAL GOVERNMENT THAT REQUESTED
32 THE POSITION–RELATED BACKGROUND INVESTIGATION; AND
HOUSE BILL 1419 5
1 (2) THE INDIVIDUAL WHO IS THE SUBJECT OF THE
2 POSITION–RELATED BACKGROUND INVESTIGATION.
3 (D) THE RESULTS OF, AND ANY RECORD OBTAINED THROUGH, A
4 POSITION–RELATED BACKGROUND INVESTIGATION UNDER THIS SECTION,
5 INCLUDING ANY SUBSEQUENT UPDATE:
6 (1) ARE CONFIDENTIAL AND SHALL BE TREATED AS A PERSONNEL
7 RECORD;
8 (2) MAY NOT BE DISSEMINATED, EXCEPT AS REQUIRED BY LAW; AND
9 (3) MAY BE USED ONLY FOR THE PURPOSES AUTHORIZED BY THIS
10 SUBTITLE.
11 (E) AN INDIVIDUAL WHO IS THE SUBJECT OF A POSITION–RELATED
12 BACKGROUND INVESTIGATION UNDER THIS SUBTITLE MAY CONTEST THE RESULTS
13 OF AN INVESTIGATION AS PROVIDED IN § 10–223 OF THE CRIMINAL PROCEDURE
14 ARTICLE.
15 2–905.
16 (A) A UNIT OF STATE OR LOCAL GOVERNMENT THAT PROVIDES FUNDING
17 FOR A REGULATED POSITION SHALL REQUIRE, AS A CONDITION OF FUNDING, THAT
18 THE PROGRAM OR ENTITY THAT RECEIVES THE FUNDING DISCLOSE TO THE UNIT
19 THE NAME AND DATE OF BIRTH OF EACH INDIVIDUAL EMPLOYED OR SUPERVISED BY
20 THE PROGRAM OR ENTITY IF THE INDIVIDUAL HOLDS OR IS SELECTED TO HOLD A
21 REGULATED POSITION.
22 (B) ON OR BEFORE OCTOBER 1, 2027, AND EACH OCTOBER 1 THEREAFTER,
23 EACH UNIT OF STATE AND LOCAL GOVERNMENT SHALL REPORT TO THE DIVISION
24 THE TOTAL NUMBER OF REGULATED POSITIONS, DISQUALIFYING EVENTS FOR EACH
25 POSITION, AND DATA ON THE EMPLOYEES OR SERVICE PROVIDERS DISQUALIFIED
26 FOR THE MOST RECENT STATE FISCAL YEAR.
27 (C) ON OR BEFORE DECEMBER 1, 2027, AND EACH DECEMBER 1
28 THEREAFTER, THE DIVISION SHALL REPORT TO THE GOVERNOR AND, IN
29 ACCORDANCE WITH § 2–1257 OF THE STATE GOVERNMENT ARTICLE, THE GENERAL
30 ASSEMBLY ON THE FOLLOWING INFORMATION SUBMITTED TO THE DIVISION UNDER
31 SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION FOR EACH UNIT OF STATE AND LOCAL
32 GOVERNMENT:
6 HOUSE BILL 1419
1 (1) THE TOTAL NUMBER OF POSITION–RELATED BACKGROUND
2 INVESTIGATIONS FOR EACH REGULATED POSITION;
3 (2) THE TOTAL NUMBER OF ALERTS RECEIVED THROUGH THE
4 FEDERAL BUREAU OF INVESTIGATION NEXT GENERATION IDENTIFICATION RAP
5 BACK PROGRAM, AND WHAT ACTION, IF ANY, WAS TAKEN; AND
6 (3) INFORMATION REGARDING COMPLIANCE WITH REPORTING
7 UNDER SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION.
8 2–906.
9 (A) INFORMATION ACCESSED OR OBTAINED UNDER THIS SECTION IS
10 SUBJECT TO THE CONFIDENTIALITY REQUIREMENTS OF § 2–1240 OF THE STATE
11 GOVERNMENT ARTICLE.
12 (B) THIS SUBTITLE MAY NOT BE CONSTRUED TO REQUIRE ANY EMPLOYEE
13 OR SERVICE PROVIDER WITHIN OR FUNDED BY A UNIT OF STATE OR LOCAL
14 GOVERNMENT TO UNDERGO A POSITION–RELATED BACKGROUND INVESTIGATION IF
15 THE EMPLOYEE OR SERVICE PROVIDER IS EMPLOYED BY OR PROVIDING SERVICES
16 TO A UNIT OF STATE OR LOCAL GOVERNMENT BEFORE OCTOBER 1, 2026.
17 Article – State Finance and Procurement
18 16–203.
19 (E) A PERSON THAT FAILS TO COMPLY WITH A REQUIREMENT UNDER TITLE
20 2, SUBTITLE 9 OF THE PUBLIC SAFETY ARTICLE MAY BE SUBJECT TO CONTRACT
21 CANCELLATION OR DEBARMENT BY A UNIT OF STATE OR LOCAL GOVERNMENT OR
22 THE BOARD, IF APPLICABLE.
23 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
24 October 1, 2026.
