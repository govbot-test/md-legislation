2 Hunting – Lead and Lead–Based Ammunition – Phase–Out Restrictions of
3 Venison Donation
4 FOR the purpose of requiring the Department of Natural Resources to require, through a
5 certain phasing–out process, the use of nonlead ammunition for the hunting of all
6 game species on or before a certain date prohibit the donation of certain processed
7 venison to certain entities except under certain circumstances; establishing a certain
8 penalty for a first violation of this Act, subject to a certain termination date; and
9 generally relating to phasing out the use of lead or lead–based ammunition for
10 hunting venison donation.

2 SENATE BILL 181
1 Preamble
2 WHEREAS, The 1991 federal ban on the use of lead ammunition in waterfowl
3 hunting is considered one of the greatest conservation and animal welfare achievements in
4 wildlife management in the last century; and
5 WHEREAS, The federal ban on the use of lead ammunition in waterfowl hunting
6 saves over 1.4 million ducks annually from lead poisoning, enabling larger fall flights of
7 ducks and geese, and increasing opportunities for hunters; and
8 WHEREAS, England, Scotland, and Wales will phase out the use of lead ammunition
9 by 2029 in recognition of the serious cumulative health and environmental impacts on
10 humans and animals; and
11 WHEREAS, Over 500 peer–reviewed studies recognize that there is no safe level of
12 lead exposure for humans; and
13 WHEREAS, Peer–reviewed studies have shown that individuals working at shooting
14 ranges have developed severe toxicity and increased health challenges as a result of
15 exposure to lead ammunition; and
16 WHEREAS, Peer–reviewed studies have proven that there is a direct connection
17 between the use of lead ammunition and an increase in health, neurological, emotional,
18 and intellectual deficiencies in humans; and
19 WHEREAS, It has been shown that the shattering of lead ammunition upon striking
20 bone in an animal can spread into dozens of lead fragments that are not readily visible by
21 the naked eye, but are detectable using X–ray technology; and
22 WHEREAS, Over 50,000 deer are killed by firearms in the State annually, and the
23 meat is consumed by hunters and family members; and
24 WHEREAS, Venison is donated in substantial amounts to food banks, and those
25 receiving the donated meat may be unknowingly ingesting lead; and
26 WHEREAS, Wildlife, such as the bald eagle, are being poisoned by ingesting lead
27 particles that are left in abandoned pieces of animal carcasses; and
28 WHEREAS, The health care system, educational system, and criminal justice system
29 are being unnecessarily burdened by exposure to and ingestion of lead ammunition; now,
30 therefore,
