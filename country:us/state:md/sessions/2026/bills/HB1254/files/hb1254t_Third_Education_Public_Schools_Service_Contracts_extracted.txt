CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Education – Public Schools – Service Contracts and Professional Development
3 FOR the purpose of requiring establishing the policy of the State to use public school
4 employees to perform functions for public schools in preference to contracting with
5 the private sector; requiring, under certain conditions, a county board of education
6 that seeks to enter into a service contract to submit to the State Department of
7 Education certain information to justify the need for the service contract, and to
8 submit a certain plan of assistance for employees adversely affected by the service
9 contract; requiring, on or before a certain date, the Department to design and develop
10 a new system of professional development for paraeducators and other education
11 support professionals; requiring, on or before a certain date, each county board to
12 provide the new system of professional development to each paraeducator and
13 education support professional employed in a public school in the county; and
14 generally relating to public school service contracts and professional development.

16 Article – Education

18 “Subtitle 12. Public School Service Contracts”

3 SUBTITLE 12. PUBLIC SCHOOL SERVICE CONTRACTS.
4 6–1201.
5 (A) IN THIS SECTION SUBTITLE, “SERVICE CONTRACT” MEANS A
6 PROCUREMENT CONTRACT FOR SERVICES THAT WILL BE PROVIDED TO A PUBLIC
7 SCHOOL.
8 6–1202.
9 THE POLICY OF THE STATE IS TO USE PUBLIC SCHOOL EMPLOYEES TO
10 PERFORM FUNCTIONS FOR PUBLIC SCHOOLS IN PREFERENCE TO CONTRACTING
11 WITH THE PRIVATE SECTOR TO PERFORM THOSE FUNCTIONS.
12 6–1203.
13 A COUNTY BOARD THAT SEEKS A SERVICE CONTRACT IS EXEMPT FROM THE
14 PREFERENCE STATED IN § 6–1202 OF THIS SUBTITLE IF:
15 (1) A CONFLICT OF INTEREST WOULD RESULT IF A PUBLIC SCHOOL
16 EMPLOYEE WERE TO PERFORM THE SERVICES SPECIFIED IN THE CONTRACT;
17 (2) THE NATURE OF THE SERVICES MEETS STANDARDS ESTABLISHED
18 BY EACH COUNTY BOARD FOR EMERGENCY CONTRACT PROCUREMENT;
19 (3) THE SERVICES CONTRACTED FOR ARE INCIDENTAL TO THE
20 PURCHASE OR LEASE OF PERSONAL OR REAL PROPERTY; OR
21 (4) A CLEAR NEED EXISTS TO OBTAIN AN UNBIASED FINDING OR
22 OPINION.
23 6–1204.
24 (B) (A) A COUNTY BOARD THAT SEEKS TO ENTER INTO A SERVICE
25 CONTRACT THAT IS NOT EXEMPT UNDER § 6–1203 OF THIS SUBTITLE SHALL SUBMIT
26 TO THE DEPARTMENT THE INFORMATION REQUIRED UNDER THIS SECTION.
27 (C) (B) THE COUNTY BOARD SHALL SUBMIT A DEMONSTRATION THAT THE
28 COUNTY BOARD HAS TAKEN FORMAL AND POSITIVE STEPS TO CONSIDER

12 6–133.
13 (A) (1) THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY FINDS THAT PARAEDUCATORS AND
14 OTHER EDUCATION SUPPORT PROFESSIONALS BENEFIT FROM IN–PERSON
15 TRAINING AS PART OF THE HIRING AND ONBOARDING PROCESS.
16 (2) IT IS THE INTENT OF THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY THAT IN–PERSON
17 TRAINING FOR PARAEDUCATORS AND OTHER EDUCATION SUPPORT
18 PROFESSIONALS BE EXPANDED IN PUBLIC SCHOOLS TO AT LEAST 2 DAYS OF
19 HIGH–QUALITY PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT DURING EACH SCHOOL YEAR.
20 (B) (1) SUBJECT TO PARAGRAPHS (2) AND (3) OF THIS SUBSECTION, ON

23 EDUCATION SUPPORT PROFESSIONALS THAT IS PAID AND OCCURS DURING
24 WEEKDAY SCHOOL HOURS.

26 INCLUDE:

28 TEACHERS WORKING AS A TEAM TO IMPROVE STUDENT OUTCOMES;
29 (II) TRAINING FOR EDUCATION SUPPORT PROFESSIONALS ON
