A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Procurement – Department of General Services – Real Estate Advisory
3 Committee
4 FOR the purpose of establishing a Real Estate Advisory Committee in the Department of
5 General Services to conduct a review of certain real estate transactions and make
6 certain recommendations; requiring certain real estate transactions submitted to the
7 Board of Public Works for approval to include certain information; and generally
8 relating to real estate transactions in State government.
9 BY adding to
10 Article – State Finance and Procurement
11 Section 4–417
12 Annotated Code of Maryland
13 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
14 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
15 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
16 Article – State Finance and Procurement
17 4–417.
18 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
19 INDICATED.
20 (2) “ADVISORY COMMITTEE” MEANS THE REAL ESTATE ADVISORY
21 COMMITTEE.
22 (3) “BOARD” MEANS THE BOARD OF PUBLIC WORKS.
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

2 HOUSE BILL 1557
1 (4) “REAL ESTATE TRANSACTION” MEANS A LEASE, LEASE RENEWAL,
2 OR PURCHASE OF PROPERTY BY THE STATE GOVERNED BY §§ 4–411 THROUGH
3 4–416 OF THIS SUBTITLE WITH A TOTAL EXPECTED COST GREATER THAN $500,000
4 PER YEAR.
5 (B) THERE IS A REAL ESTATE ADVISORY COMMITTEE IN THE
6 DEPARTMENT.
7 (C) THE ADVISORY COMMITTEE CONSISTS OF THE FOLLOWING MEMBERS:
8 (1) THE SECRETARY, OR THE SECRETARY’S DESIGNEE;
9 (2) SIX MEMBERS WITH EXPERTISE IN REAL ESTATE PURCHASES,
10 LEASES, AND LEASE RENEWALS:
11 (I) TWO OF WHOM SHALL BE APPOINTED BY THE GOVERNOR;
12 (II) TWO OF WHOM SHALL BE APPOINTED BY THE PRESIDENT OF
13 THE SENATE; AND
14 (III) TWO OF WHOM SHALL BE APPOINTED BY THE SPEAKER OF
15 THE HOUSE; AND
16 (3) AS EX OFFICIO, NONVOTING MEMBERS:
17 (I) ONE MEMBER OF THE SENATE OF MARYLAND, APPOINTED
18 BY THE PRESIDENT OF THE SENATE; AND
19 (II) ONE MEMBER OF THE HOUSE OF DELEGATES, APPOINTED
20 BY THE SPEAKER OF THE HOUSE.
21 (D) THE SECRETARY SHALL DESIGNATE THE CHAIR OF THE ADVISORY
22 COMMITTEE.
23 (E) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL PROVIDE STAFF FOR THE ADVISORY
24 COMMITTEE.
25 (F) A MEMBER OF THE ADVISORY COMMITTEE:
26 (1) MAY NOT RECEIVE COMPENSATION AS A MEMBER OF THE
27 ADVISORY COMMITTEE; BUT
HOUSE BILL 1557 3
1 (2) IS ENTITLED TO REIMBURSEMENT FOR EXPENSES UNDER THE
2 STANDARD STATE TRAVEL REGULATIONS, AS PROVIDED IN THE STATE BUDGET.
3 (G) THE ADVISORY COMMITTEE SHALL:
4 (1) PRIOR TO ANY FORMAL ACTION ON A REAL ESTATE TRANSACTION
5 BY A STATE AGENCY, ADVISE THE STATE AGENCY OF ANY ISSUES OF CONCERN
6 REGARDING THE REAL ESTATE TRANSACTION;
7 (2) ON THE SELECTION BY THE DEPARTMENT OF A LEAD OFFEROR,
8 IMMEDIATELY BEGIN REAL ESTATE TRANSACTION REVIEW;
9 (3) NOT LESS THAN 1 YEAR PRIOR TO THE EXPIRATION OF A LEASE OF
10 PROPERTY UNDER ITEM (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION, VOTE TO:
11 (I) MAKE A RECOMMENDATION AS TO WHETHER THE REAL
12 ESTATE TRANSACTION SHOULD MOVE FORWARD FOR SUBMISSION TO THE BOARD;
13 OR
14 (II) MAKE A RECOMMENDATION AS TO WHETHER A REAL ESTATE
15 TRANSACTION OR SPECIFIC PROVISIONS OF A REAL ESTATE TRANSACTION SHOULD
16 BE REJECTED, REVISED, OR REMOVED PRIOR TO SUBMISSION TO THE BOARD FOR
17 APPROVAL; AND
18 (4) ADVISE THE DEPARTMENT ON THE DEPARTMENT’S LEASE
19 PROCUREMENT PROCEDURES.
20 (H) A REAL ESTATE TRANSACTION OR SPECIFIC PROVISIONS NOT
21 RECOMMENDED FOR APPROVAL BY THE ADVISORY COMMITTEE UNDER
22 SUBSECTION (G) OF THIS SECTION MAY BE RESUBMITTED TO THE ADVISORY
23 COMMITTEE FOR ADDITIONAL REVIEW.
24 (I) EACH STATE AGENCY THAT SUBMITS A REQUEST FOR PROPOSALS FOR
25 A REAL ESTATE TRANSACTION TO THE ADVISORY COMMITTEE SHALL INCLUDE WITH
26 THE AGENCY’S SUBMISSION:
27 (1) A FULL COST BENEFIT ANALYSIS COMPARING SIMILAR OR
28 COMPARABLE PROPERTIES AND PURCHASE OPTIONS, INCLUDING A COMPARISON
29 OF LEASE OPTIONS WITH PURCHASE OPTIONS, AND LEASE CONSOLIDATION
30 OPTIONS;
31 (2) AN ANALYSIS OF STATE AGENCY PARKING NEEDS AND COSTS,
32 INCLUDING ANY RELATED CONSIDERATIONS REGARDING THE PROPERTY;
4 HOUSE BILL 1557
1 (3) DOCUMENTS THAT JUSTIFY:
2 (I) REAL ESTATE TERMS IN EXCESS OF TIME OR COST IN EXCESS
3 OF ORDINARY AND REGULAR STATE CONTRACT TERMS;
4 (II) RENT ESCALATION RATES IN EXCESS OF MARKET RATES;
5 (III) CONSTRUCTION COSTS AND COSTS FOR ANY OTHER PART OF
6 THE REAL ESTATE TRANSACTION, WITH CONSIDERATION FOR THE
7 REASONABLENESS AND COST COMPETITIVENESS OF EACH ITEM;
8 (IV) REMOVAL OF ANY STANDARD REAL ESTATE PROVISIONS,
9 INCLUDING THE RIGHT TO TERMINATE THE LEASE OR TRANSACTION; AND
10 (V) ANY ISSUES REGARDING NONCOMPETITIVE
11 PROCUREMENTS;
12 (4) A FULL OR SIGNIFICANT RENOVATION COST ANALYSIS, DISTINCT
13 FROM THE OVERALL LEASE COST BENEFIT ANALYSIS, INCLUDING:
14 (I) A JUSTIFICATION FOR NONCOMPETITIVE PROCUREMENT
15 OF GOODS AND SERVICES;
16 (II) A DEMONSTRATION THAT COSTS ARE NECESSARY,
17 REASONABLE, AND APPROPRIATE; AND
18 (III) A DEMONSTRATION THAT FULL FUNDING IS AVAILABLE FOR
19 ALL RENOVATIONS; AND
20 (5) LANDLORD PAYMENT DOCUMENTATION, INCLUDING:
21 (I) TOTAL PAYMENTS TO DATE TO THE LANDLORD;
22 (II) PRIOR LEASE PAYMENTS;
23 (III) TAX CREDITS OR OTHER STATE BENEFITS GRANTED TO THE
24 LANDLORD; AND
25 (IV) TAX CREDITS OR OTHER STATE BENEFITS FOR THE
26 LOCATION OF THE REAL ESTATE TRANSACTION.
HOUSE BILL 1557 5
1 (J) A REAL ESTATE TRANSACTION THAT IS SUBMITTED TO THE BOARD FOR
2 APPROVAL SHALL INCLUDE:
3 (1) THE ADVISORY COMMITTEE’S RECOMMENDATIONS ON A REAL
4 ESTATE TRANSACTION OR SPECIFIC PROVISIONS OF A REAL ESTATE TRANSACTION;
5 AND
6 (2) ALL INFORMATION SPECIFIED UNDER SUBSECTION (I) OF THIS
7 SECTION.
8 (K) (1) ON OR BEFORE JANUARY 1 EACH YEAR, THE ADVISORY
9 COMMITTEE SHALL SUBMIT AN ANNUAL REPORT TO THE GOVERNOR AND, IN
10 ACCORDANCE WITH § 2–1257 OF THE STATE GOVERNMENT ARTICLE, THE GENERAL
11 ASSEMBLY.
12 (2) THE REPORT SHALL INCLUDE:
13 (I) A LIST OF ALL CURRENT REAL ESTATE TRANSACTIONS BY
14 THE STATE GOVERNED BY §§ 4–318 THROUGH 4–321 OF THIS TITLE AND §§ 4–411
15 THROUGH 4–416 OF THIS SUBTITLE, INCLUDING THE TERM OF DURATION AND
16 COSTS ASSOCIATED WITH EACH REAL ESTATE TRANSACTION;
17 (II) THE TOTAL NUMBER OF STATE AGENCIES THAT SUBMITTED
18 REQUESTS FOR PROPOSALS FOR REAL ESTATE TRANSACTIONS TO THE ADVISORY
19 COMMITTEE;
20 (III) THE FINAL DISPOSITION OF EACH REQUEST FOR
21 PROPOSALS FOR A REAL ESTATE TRANSACTION;
22 (IV) THE SUBSEQUENT ACTION TAKEN BY THE AGENCY
23 FOLLOWING FINAL DISPOSITION OF EACH REQUEST FOR PROPOSALS FOR A REAL
24 ESTATE TRANSACTION; AND
25 (V) FOLLOWING BOARD APPROVAL, THE STATUS OF REAL
26 ESTATE TRANSACTIONS PUBLISHED IN EMARYLAND MARKETPLACE, LISTING
27 WHETHER THE REAL ESTATE TRANSACTION PUBLICATION WAS ON TIME OR LATE.
28 (3) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL PUBLISH ALL DATA REQUIRED IN THE
29 ANNUAL REPORT UNDER THIS SUBSECTION ON THE DEPARTMENT’S WEBSITE OR ON
30 A PUBLICLY ACCESSIBLE ONLINE DASHBOARD FOR PUBLIC REVIEW.

1 (L) NOTHING IN THIS SECTION MAY BE CONSTRUED TO PROHIBIT A STATE
2 AGENCY FROM SUBMITTING OR LIMIT THE AUTHORITY OF A STATE AGENCY TO
3 SUBMIT A REAL ESTATE TRANSACTION TO THE BOARD FOR APPROVAL.
4 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
5 1, 2026.
