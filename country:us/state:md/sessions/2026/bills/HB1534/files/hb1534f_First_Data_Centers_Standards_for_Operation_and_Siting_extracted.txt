Section 1:
M3, L6, M4 6lr3427
By: Delegate Miller
Introduced and read first time: February 13, 2026
Assigned to: Environment and Transportation
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Data Centers – Standards for Operation and Siting
3 FOR the purpose of requiring an owner or operator of a data center to comply with certain
4 standards under certain circumstances; requiring the Department of the
5 Environment to establish by regulation certain sound and vibration controls and
6 requirements for backup power generation for data centers; authorizing a local
7 health department and the Department to issue an emergency stop work order under
8 certain circumstances; prohibiting a local approving authority from authorizing the
9 construction of a data center on certain agricultural land; requiring a certain
10 applicant to submit a certain report to a local approving authority under certain
11 circumstances; and generally relating to standards for the operation and siting of
12 data centers.
13 BY adding to
14 Article – Environment
15 Section 19–101 and 19–102 to be under the new title “Title 19. Data Centers”
16 Annotated Code of Maryland
17 (2014 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
18 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
19 Article – Land Use
20 Section 1–401(a) and (c)
21 Annotated Code of Maryland
22 (2012 Volume and 2025 Supplement)
23 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
24 Article – Land Use
25 Section 1–401(b)(27) through (32)
26 Annotated Code of Maryland
27 (2012 Volume and 2025 Supplement)

14 Article – Environment
15 TITLE 19. DATA CENTERS.
16 19–101.
17 (A) IN THIS TITLE THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
18 INDICATED.
19 (B) “DATA CENTER” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 11–239 OF THE TAX –
20 GENERAL ARTICLE.
21 (C) “DBA” MEANS DECIBELS OF SOUND AS DETERMINED BY THE
22 A–WEIGHTING NETWORK.
23 (D) “DBC” MEANS DECIBELS OF SOUND AS DETERMINED BY THE
24 C–WEIGHTING NETWORK.
25 19–102.
26 (A) IN ADDITION TO ANY OTHER FEDERAL, STATE, OR LOCAL
27 REQUIREMENTS, AN OWNER OR OPERATOR OF A DATA CENTER SHALL:
28 (1) IF THE DATA CENTER IS LOCATED WITHIN A 2,500–FOOT RADIUS
29 OF THE BOUNDARY OF A PRIMARY OR SECONDARY SCHOOL PROPERTY:
30 (I) ENSURE ELECTRICAL SUBSTATIONS AND OVERHEAD
31 TRANSMISSION LINES THAT HAVE A VOLTAGE CARRYING CAPACITY IN EXCESS OF 69
HOUSE BILL 1534 3
1 KILOVOLTS MAINTAIN A MINIMUM 1,000–FOOT SETBACK FROM ANY PLAYGROUND
2 OR ATHLETIC FIELD ON THE SCHOOL PROPERTY;
3 (II) BEFORE OPERATING THE DATA CENTER AND ON REQUEST
4 FROM A SCHOOL, PROVIDE:
5 1. FOR THE INSTALLATION OF HEPA–ONLY FILTERS
6 AND MERV 13+ FILTERS IN THE SCHOOL’S HEATING, VENTILATION, AND
7 AIR–CONDITIONING SYSTEM; AND
8 2. FUNDING FOR ANNUAL COSTS RELATED TO
9 MAINTAINING AND REPLACING THE FILTERS; AND
10 (III) INSTALL AND MAINTAIN A PUBLIC–FACING AIR QUALITY
11 SENSOR NETWORK AT THE AIR INTAKES OF THE SCHOOL TO MONITOR PARTICULATE
12 MATTER 2.5, NITROGEN DIOXIDE, AND CARBON MONOXIDE; AND
13 (2) ENSURE COMPLIANCE WITH STANDARDS ESTABLISHED BY THE
14 DEPARTMENT UNDER SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION.
15 (B) (1) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ESTABLISH BY REGULATION SOUND
16 AND VIBRATION CONTROLS AND REQUIREMENTS FOR BACKUP POWER GENERATION
17 FOR DATA CENTERS.
18 (2) THE REGULATIONS ESTABLISHED BY THE DEPARTMENT UNDER
19 PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION SHALL:
20 (I) SET SOUND LEVEL LIMITS THAT PROHIBIT DBC FROM
21 EXCEEDING DBA BY MORE THAN 10 DECIBELS OF SOUND; AND
22 (II) FOR SCHOOL AND RESIDENTIAL PROPERTY, SET SOUND
23 LEVEL LIMITS THAT MAY NOT EXCEED:
24 1. 45 DBA; OR
25 2. 55 DBC;
26 (III) SET PEAK PARTICLE VELOCITY LIMITS THAT MAY NOT
27 EXCEED 0.02 INCHES PER SECOND AT ANY SCHOOL FOUNDATION;
28 (IV) REQUIRE ALL MECHANICAL EQUIPMENT TO USE
29 SPRING–ISOLATED FOUNDATIONS WITH 95% EFFICIENCY;
4 HOUSE BILL 1534
1 (V) REQUIRE EACH BACKUP GENERATOR WITHIN 1 MILE OF
2 THE BOUNDARY OF A SCHOOL OR RESIDENTIAL PROPERTY TO MEET TIER 4
3 EMISSION STANDARDS AND CERTIFICATION REQUIREMENTS ESTABLISHED BY THE
4 U.S. ENVIRONMENTAL PROTECTION AGENCY;
5 (VI) FOR A DATA CENTER THAT REQUIRES MORE THAN 50
6 MEGAWATTS, REQUIRE ON–SITE BATTERY ENERGY STORAGE; AND
7 (VII) PROHIBIT NONEMERGENCY BACKUP GENERATOR TESTING
8 BETWEEN THE HOURS OF 7:30 A.M. AND 4:30 P.M., MONDAY THROUGH FRIDAY.
9 (C) A LOCAL HEALTH DEPARTMENT OR THE DEPARTMENT MAY ISSUE AN
10 EMERGENCY STOP WORK ORDER IF:
11 (1) ANY SOUND OR VIBRATION LIMIT SET FOR SCHOOL PROPERTY BY
12 THE DEPARTMENT IN REGULATION IS EXCEEDED FOR MORE THAN 4 HOURS DURING
13 SCHOOL HOURS; OR
14 (2) AN AIR QUALITY MONITOR DETECTS AN INCREASE OF 20% OR
15 MORE IN POLLUTANTS DURING BACKUP GENERATOR TESTING.
16 (D) (1) A PERSON WHO VIOLATES THIS SECTION IS SUBJECT TO A CIVIL
17 PENALTY OF $10,000.
18 (2) IN ADDITION TO A PENALTY IMPOSED UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF
19 THIS SUBSECTION, IF AN OWNER OR OPERATOR OF A DATA CENTER HAS VIOLATED
20 THIS SECTION MULTIPLE TIMES, THE DEPARTMENT MAY REVOKE OR SUSPEND ANY
21 PERMIT ISSUED BY THE DEPARTMENT TO THE DATA CENTER UNDER THIS ARTICLE.

9 SUBTITLE 6. DATA CENTER SITING.
10 7–601.
11 IN THIS SUBTITLE, “DATA CENTER” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 11–239 OF
12 THE TAX – GENERAL ARTICLE.
13 7–602.
14 (A) SUBJECT TO SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION, A LOCAL APPROVING
15 AUTHORITY MAY NOT AUTHORIZE CONSTRUCTION OF A DATA CENTER:
16 (1) ON LAND WITH SOILS CLASSIFIED AS U.S. DEPARTMENT OF
17 AGRICULTURE SOIL CAPABILITY CLASS I OR CLASS II UNLESS THERE IS NO OTHER
18 LAND IN THE LOCAL JURISDICTION THAT IS AVAILABLE AND CLASSIFIED AS AN
19 INDUSTRIAL ZONE;
20 (2) ON LAND ENCUMBERED BY AN AGRICULTURAL EASEMENT;
21 (3) IN A RURAL LEGACY AREA, AS DEFINED IN § 5–9A–02 OF THE
22 NATURAL RESOURCES ARTICLE; OR
23 (4) IF CONSTRUCTION OF THE DATA CENTER WOULD RESULT IN A
24 VIOLATION OF TITLE 19 OF THE ENVIRONMENT ARTICLE.
25 (B) BEFORE A LOCAL APPROVING AUTHORITY MAY APPROVE AN
26 APPLICATION FOR THE CONSTRUCTION OF A DATA CENTER ON LAND THAT HAS NOT
27 BEEN PREVIOUSLY DEVELOPED, AN APPLICANT SHALL SUBMIT TO THE LOCAL
28 APPROVING AUTHORITY A REPORT THAT VERIFIES THAT THE APPLICANT
29 EVALUATED DECOMMISSIONED INDUSTRIAL OR POWER PLANT SITES BEFORE
30 SUBMITTAL OF THE APPLICATION.
