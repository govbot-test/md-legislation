2 SENATE BILL 610
1 (a) The Department of Natural Resources, in coordination with the Invasive
2 Catfish Advisory Committee, shall establish the Invasive Blue Catfish Pilot Program.
3 (b) The purpose of the Pilot Program is to curb the spread of the invasive blue
4 catfish in the Chesapeake Bay and its tributaries.
5 (c) The Pilot Program consists of:
6 (1) the For–Hire/Commercial Blue Catfish Program;
7 (2) the Mid– and Lower–Bay Blue Catfish Trotline Program; [and]
8 (3) THE BLUE CATFISH GILL NET PROGRAM; AND
9 [(3)] (4) any other program that the Department determines to be
10 effective in curbing the spread of blue catfish in the Chesapeake Bay and its tributaries.
11 (d) (1) Under the For–Hire/Commercial Blue Catfish Program, the
12 Department may:
13 (i) authorize eligible participants to run for–hire and commercial
14 fishing trips simultaneously; and
15 (ii) make any regulatory or administrative changes it believes will
16 increase the commercial harvest of blue catfish.
17 (2) (i) Under the Mid– and Lower–Bay Blue Catfish Trotline Program,
18 the Department shall authorize the use of finfish trotlines south of the William Preston
19 Lane Jr. Memorial Bridge to the maximum extent feasible to harvest blue catfish.
20 (ii) The Department may restrict the areas south of the William
21 Preston Lane Jr. Memorial Bridge where finfish trotlines may be used to harvest blue
22 catfish based on potential bycatch, user conflicts, or other issues it considers relevant.
23 (e) In implementing the Pilot Program, the Department shall consider for the
24 harvest of blue catfish:
25 (1) authorizing the use of electrofishing;
26 (2) authorizing the use of finfish trotlines of up to 2,400 feet in length;
27 (3) eliminating any limits on the number of hooks used on a finfish trotline;
28 and
29 (4) authorizing the use of hooks that are not corrodible.
SENATE BILL 610 3
1 (f) The Department shall:
2 (1) expand the use of practices determined to be effective under the Pilot
3 Program throughout the duration of the Pilot Program; and
4 (2) continue to implement and update the Pilot Program until the
5 termination date of this Act.
6 (g) On or before December 1, [2025] 2026, AND EACH DECEMBER 1
7 THEREAFTER, the Department shall report to the [Governor] SENATE COMMITTEE ON
8 EDUCATION, ENERGY, AND THE ENVIRONMENT and THE HOUSE ENVIRONMENT
9 AND TRANSPORTATION COMMITTEE, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State
10 Government Article, [the General Assembly] on [its] THE ACTIVITIES OF THE PILOT
11 PROGRAM DURING THE IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING YEAR, INCLUDING findings and
12 recommendations under the Pilot Program.

8 Article – Natural Resources
9 4–701.2.
10 (a) There is a commercial blue and flathead catfish [finfish trotline] license.
11 (b) (1) The license authorizes the holder to catch for sale [blue and flathead]
12 IN THE TIDAL WATERS OF THE STATE:
13 (I) BLUE CATFISH USING, AS DEFINED IN DEPARTMENT
14 REGULATIONS:
15 1. A FINFISH TROTLINE; OR
16 2. A GILL NET WITH A MESH SIZE OF NOT MORE THAN 7
17 INCHES; AND
18 (II) FLATHEAD catfish [in the tidal waters of the State] using a
19 finfish trotline, as defined in Department regulations.
20 (2) A licensee shall fish in accordance with Department regulations.
21 (c) A person who wishes to obtain the license shall complete and submit an
22 application for the license to the Department or any person designated by the Department.
23 (d) An applicant for the license does not need to hold a tidal fish license under §
24 4–701 of this subtitle to be eligible for the license.
25 (e) The term of the license is 1 year from September 1 through August 31 the
26 following year.
27 (f) The annual license fee is $15.
28 (g) A licensee may not transfer a license issued under this section.
29 (h) A licensee shall have a commercial blue and flathead catfish [finfish trotline]
30 license in the licensee’s possession when the licensee fishes under the license.
