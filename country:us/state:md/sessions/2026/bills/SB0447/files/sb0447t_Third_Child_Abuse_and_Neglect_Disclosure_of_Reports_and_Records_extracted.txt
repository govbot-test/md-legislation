2 Child Abuse and Neglect – Disclosure of Reports and Records
3 FOR the purpose of expanding the circumstances under which a report or record concerning
4 child abuse or neglect may be disclosed by the Department of Human Services to
5 certain individuals; and generally relating to disclosure of reports and records of
6 child abuse and neglect.

1 (a) Except as otherwise provided in Title 5, Subtitles 7 and 12 of the Family Law
2 Article, § 1–203 of this subtitle, and this section, a person may not disclose a report or
3 record concerning child abuse or neglect.
4 (c) A report or record concerning child abuse or neglect:
5 (1) may be disclosed on request to:
6 (i) personnel of the Social Services Administration or a local
7 department of social services, law enforcement personnel, and members of
8 multidisciplinary case consultation teams, including an addiction specialist as defined in
9 Title 5, Subtitle 12 of the Family Law Article or § 5–314 of this article, who are investigating
10 a report of known or suspected child abuse or neglect or providing services to or assessing
11 a child or family that is the subject of the report;
12 (ii) local or State officials responsible for the administration of child
13 protective services, juvenile services, or child care, foster care, or adoption licensing,
14 approval, or regulations, as necessary to carry out their official functions;
15 (iii) the State Council on Child Abuse and Neglect or its designee, the
16 State Citizens Review Board for Children or its designee, or a child fatality review team,
17 as necessary to carry out their official functions;
18 (iv) a person who is the alleged abuser or neglector, if that person is
19 responsible for the child’s welfare and provisions are made for the protection of the identity
20 of the reporter or any other person whose life or safety is likely to be endangered by
21 disclosing the information;
22 (v) a licensed practitioner who, or an agency, institution, or program
23 that, is providing treatment or care to a child who is the subject of a report of child abuse
24 or neglect for a purpose relevant to the treatment or care;
25 (vi) a parent or other person who has permanent or temporary care
26 and custody of the child, if provisions are made for the protection of the identity of the
27 reporter or any other person whose life or safety is likely to be endangered by disclosing the
28 information;
29 (vii) 1. the appropriate public school superintendent or the
30 principal or equivalent employee of a nonpublic school that holds a certificate of approval
31 from the State or is registered with the State Department of Education to carry out
32 appropriate personnel or administrative actions following a report of suspected child abuse
33 involving a student committed by:
34 A. a public school employee in that school system;
35 B. an employee of that nonpublic school;
