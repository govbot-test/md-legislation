SENATE BILL 911
M3 6lr3337
By: Senator Mautz
Introduced and read first time: February 6, 2026
Assigned to: Education, Energy, and the Environment
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Environment – Weather Engineering – Prohibition
3 FOR the purpose of prohibiting a person from injecting, releasing, or dispersing a chemical
4 compound, substance, or apparatus into the atmosphere within the State for the
5 purpose of affecting the temperature, weather, climate, or intensity of the sunlight;
6 requiring the Department of the Environment, in consultation with the Maryland
7 Department of Emergency Management, to establish a method for evaluating a
8 report of a certain weather engineering activity and investigate a report under
9 certain circumstances; and generally relating to weather engineering.
10 BY adding to
11 Article – Environment
12 Section 5–1501 through 5–1504 to be under the new subtitle “Subtitle 15. Weather
13 Engineering”
14 Annotated Code of Maryland
15 (2013 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
16 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
17 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
18 Article – Environment
19 SUBTITLE 15. WEATHER ENGINEERING.
20 5–1501.
21 IN THIS SUBTITLE, “WEATHER ENGINEERING ACTIVITY” MEANS AN ACTIVITY
22 THAT IS PROHIBITED UNDER § 5–1502 OF THIS SUBTITLE.
23 5–1502.

2 SENATE BILL 911
1 A PERSON MAY NOT INJECT, RELEASE, OR DISPERSE, BY ANY MEANS, A
2 CHEMICAL COMPOUND, SUBSTANCE, OR APPARATUS INTO THE ATMOSPHERE
3 WITHIN THE STATE FOR THE PURPOSE OF AFFECTING THE TEMPERATURE,
4 WEATHER, CLIMATE, OR INTENSITY OF THE SUNLIGHT.
5 5–1503.
6 (A) A PERSON WHO OBSERVES A WEATHER ENGINEERING ACTIVITY MAY
7 REPORT THE ACTIVITY TO THE DEPARTMENT ONLINE OR BY TELEPHONE, MAIL, OR
8 E–MAIL.
9 (B) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL MAKE AVAILABLE ON ITS WEBSITE AN E–MAIL
10 ADDRESS AND ONLINE FORM FOR THE REPORTING OF A WEATHER ENGINEERING
11 ACTIVITY.
12 (C) THE DEPARTMENT, IN CONSULTATION WITH THE MARYLAND
13 DEPARTMENT OF EMERGENCY MANAGEMENT, SHALL:
14 (1) ESTABLISH A METHOD FOR EVALUATING REPORTS MADE UNDER
15 THIS SECTION; AND
16 (2) INVESTIGATE ANY REPORT THAT THE DEPARTMENT DEEMS IN
17 NEED OF FURTHER REVIEW.
18 5–1504.
19 (A) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION, A PERSON
20 WHO VIOLATES § 5–1502 OF THIS SUBTITLE IS GUILTY OF A FELONY AND ON
21 CONVICTION IS SUBJECT TO A FINE NOT EXCEEDING $100,000.
22 (B) AN AIRCRAFT OPERATOR OR CONTROLLER WHO PARTICIPATES IN A
23 WEATHER ENGINEERING ACTIVITY IS GUILTY OF A FELONY AND ON CONVICTION IS
24 SUBJECT TO A FINE NOT EXCEEDING $5,000 OR IMPRISONMENT NOT EXCEEDING 5
25 YEARS OR BOTH.
26 (C) EACH VIOLATION OF THIS SUBTITLE IS A SEPARATE OFFENSE.
27 (D) MONEY COLLECTED FROM FINES IMPOSED UNDER THIS SECTION SHALL
28 BE DEPOSITED INTO THE BAY RESTORATION FUND ESTABLISHED UNDER § 9–1605.2
29 OF THIS ARTICLE.
