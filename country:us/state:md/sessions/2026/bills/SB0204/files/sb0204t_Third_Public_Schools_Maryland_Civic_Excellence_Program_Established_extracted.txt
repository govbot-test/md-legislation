CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Public Schools – Maryland Civic Excellence Program – Established
3 FOR the purpose of establishing the Maryland Civic Excellence Program in the State
4 Department of Education for certain public high school students; requiring the State
5 Board of Education to establish the criteria and requirements for the Program in
6 regulation; authorizing a local school system to participate in the Program to
7 recognize students who meet criteria related to student proficiency in civics and civic
8 literacy public high school students for their civic readiness and civic engagement;
9 and generally relating to the Maryland Civic Excellence Program.
10 BY adding to
11 Article – Education
12 Section 7–137
13 Annotated Code of Maryland
14 (2025 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
15 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
16 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
17 Article – Education
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from existing law.
Underlining indicates amendments to bill.
Strike out indicates matter stricken from the bill by amendment or deleted from the law by
amendment. *sb0204*
2 SENATE BILL 204
1 7–137.
2 (A) IN THIS SECTION, “PROGRAM” MEANS THE MARYLAND CIVIC
3 EXCELLENCE PROGRAM.
4 (B) (1) THERE IS A MARYLAND CIVIC EXCELLENCE PROGRAM IN THE
5 DEPARTMENT THAT RECOGNIZES THE CIVIC READINESS AND CIVIC ENGAGEMENT
6 OF PUBLIC HIGH SCHOOL STUDENTS IN THE STATE.
7 (2) THE PURPOSE OF THE PROGRAM IS TO RECOGNIZE STUDENTS
8 AND PUBLIC SCHOOLS THAT PROMOTE THE CIVIC READINESS AND CIVIC
9 ENGAGEMENT OF STUDENTS IN PUBLIC SCHOOLS IN THE STATE.
10 (C) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ADMINISTER THE PROGRAM.
11 (D) (C) NOT LATER THAN THE 2027–2028 SCHOOL YEAR, THE
12 DEPARTMENT STATE BOARD, IN CONSULTATION WITH EXPERTS AND
13 STAKEHOLDERS, SHALL DEVELOP GUIDELINES TO ASSIST PARTICIPATING LOCAL
14 SCHOOL SYSTEMS TO DETERMINE STUDENT PROFICIENCY IN CIVICS AND CIVIC
15 LITERACY ADOPT REGULATIONS TO ESTABLISH THE CRITERIA AND REQUIREMENTS
16 FOR AWARDING A SEAL OF CIVIC EXCELLENCE TO A QUALIFIED STUDENT WHO
17 ATTENDS A PUBLIC HIGH SCHOOL IN A PARTICIPATING LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM.
18 (E) (D) (1) THIS SUBSECTION APPLIES ONLY TO A A LOCAL SCHOOL
19 SYSTEM THAT PARTICIPATES MAY CHOOSE TO PARTICIPATE IN THE PROGRAM.
20 (2) A PUBLIC HIGH SCHOOL IN A PARTICIPATING LOCAL SCHOOL
21 SYSTEM SHALL UTILIZE THE GUIDELINES ESTABLISHED UNDER SUBSECTION (D) OF
22 THIS SECTION TO ESTABLISH:
23 (I) A MARYLAND CIVIC EXCELLENCE PROGRAM IN SCHOOLS;
24 (II) APPLICATION CRITERIA TO ADMINISTER THE PROGRAM;
25 AND
26 (III) CRITERIA FOR AWARDING A STUDENT THE SEAL OF CIVIC
27 EXCELLENCE MAY NOT OPT OUT OF PARTICIPATING IN THE PROGRAM.
28 (F) (E) A PARTICIPATING LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM THAT DETERMINES A
29 PUBLIC HIGH SCHOOL STUDENT HAS FULFILLED THE CRITERIA UNDER THE
30 PROGRAM MAY ESTABLISHED IN REGULATIONS ADOPTED IN ACCORDANCE WITH
31 SUBSECTION (C) OF THIS SECTION SHALL:
32 (1) AWARD THE STUDENT A SEAL OF CIVIC EXCELLENCE; AND
SENATE BILL 204 3
1 (2) RECOGNIZE THE AWARD BY AWARDING A CORD OR MEDAL OR BY
2 ATTACHING OTHER INSIGNIA ON THE STUDENT’S HIGH SCHOOL DIPLOMA.
3 (G) (F) A PARTICIPATING LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM MAY COLLABORATE
4 WITH NONPROFIT ORGANIZATIONS, CIVIC ORGANIZATIONS, OR INSTITUTIONS OF
5 HIGHER EDUCATION TO SUPPORT THE IMPLEMENTATION OF THE PROGRAM.
6 (H) (G) ON OR BEFORE JULY 1, 2028, AND EACH JULY 1 THEREAFTER,
7 EACH PARTICIPATING LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM SHALL SUBMIT TO THE DEPARTMENT
8 A REPORT ON THE PROGRAM BASED ON THE MOST RECENTLY COMPLETED SCHOOL
9 YEAR.
10 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
11 1, 2026.
