1 AN ACT concerning
2 Public Schools – Maryland Civic Excellence Program – Established
3 FOR the purpose of establishing the Maryland Civic Excellence Program in the State
4 Department of Education for certain public high school students; requiring the State
5 Board of Education to establish the criteria and requirements for the Program in
6 regulation; requiring that a certain capstone project be included in the Program;
7 authorizing a local school system to participate in the Program to recognize students
8 who meet criteria related to student proficiency in civics and civic literacy public
9 high school students for their civic readiness and civic engagement; and generally
10 relating to the Maryland Civic Excellence Program.

1 Article – Education

3 Annotated Code of Maryland

5 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
6 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
7 Article – Education
8 7–137.
9 (A) IN THIS SECTION, “PROGRAM” MEANS THE MARYLAND CIVIC
10 EXCELLENCE PROGRAM.
11 (B) (1) THERE IS A MARYLAND CIVIC EXCELLENCE PROGRAM IN THE
12 DEPARTMENT THAT RECOGNIZES THE CIVIC READINESS AND CIVIC ENGAGEMENT
13 OF PUBLIC HIGH SCHOOL STUDENTS IN THE STATE.
14 (2) THE PURPOSE OF THE PROGRAM IS TO RECOGNIZE STUDENTS
15 AND PUBLIC SCHOOLS THAT PROMOTE THE CIVIC READINESS AND CIVIC
16 ENGAGEMENT OF STUDENTS IN PUBLIC SCHOOLS IN THE STATE.
17 (C) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ADMINISTER THE PROGRAM.
18 (D) (C) (1) NOT LATER THAN THE 2027–2028 SCHOOL YEAR, THE
19 DEPARTMENT STATE BOARD, IN CONSULTATION WITH EXPERTS AND
20 STAKEHOLDERS, SHALL DEVELOP GUIDELINES TO ASSIST PARTICIPATING LOCAL
21 SCHOOL SYSTEMS TO DETERMINE STUDENT PROFICIENCY IN CIVICS AND CIVIC
22 LITERACY ADOPT REGULATIONS TO ESTABLISH THE CRITERIA AND REQUIREMENTS
23 FOR AWARDING A SEAL OF CIVIC EXCELLENCE TO A QUALIFIED STUDENT WHO
24 ATTENDS A PUBLIC HIGH SCHOOL IN A PARTICIPATING LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM.
25 (2) THE CRITERIA ESTABLISHED BY THE STATE BOARD SHALL
26 INCLUDE A REQUIREMENT THAT, IN ORDER TO RECEIVE A SEAL OF CIVIC
27 EXCELLENCE, A STUDENT MUST COMPLETE A CAPSTONE PROJECT THROUGH AN
28 EXTRACURRICULAR ACTIVITY THAT IS FOCUSED ON CIVIC SERVICE IN STATE,
29 COUNTY, OR LOCAL GOVERNMENT.
30 (E) (D) (1) THIS SUBSECTION APPLIES ONLY TO A A LOCAL SCHOOL
31 SYSTEM THAT PARTICIPATES MAY CHOOSE TO PARTICIPATE IN THE PROGRAM.
SENATE BILL 204 3
1 (2) A PUBLIC HIGH SCHOOL IN A PARTICIPATING LOCAL SCHOOL
2 SYSTEM SHALL UTILIZE THE GUIDELINES ESTABLISHED UNDER SUBSECTION (D) OF
3 THIS SECTION TO ESTABLISH:
4 (I) A MARYLAND CIVIC EXCELLENCE PROGRAM IN SCHOOLS;
5 (II) APPLICATION CRITERIA TO ADMINISTER THE PROGRAM;
6 AND
7 (III) CRITERIA FOR AWARDING A STUDENT THE SEAL OF CIVIC
8 EXCELLENCE MAY NOT OPT OUT OF PARTICIPATING IN THE PROGRAM.
9 (3) IF A LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM CHOOSES TO PARTICIPATE IN THE
10 PROGRAM, A PUBLIC HIGH SCHOOL SHALL IMPLEMENT THE PROGRAM, IN
11 ACCORDANCE WITH THE REGULATIONS ADOPTED BY THE STATE BOARD, BY
12 INCORPORATING A CIVIC SERVICE CAPSTONE PROJECT INTO AN EXTRACURRICULAR
13 ACTIVITY AT THE SCHOOL THAT:
14 (I) IS AN EXISTING EXTRACURRICULAR ACTIVITY AT THE TIME
15 THE LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM CHOOSES TO PARTICIPATE IN THE PROGRAM; OR
16 (II) IS A NEW EXTRACURRICULAR ACTIVITY THAT IS SUPERVISED
17 BY A PAID STAFF MEMBER.
18 (F) (E) A PARTICIPATING LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM THAT DETERMINES A
19 PUBLIC HIGH SCHOOL STUDENT HAS FULFILLED THE CRITERIA UNDER THE
20 PROGRAM MAY ESTABLISHED IN REGULATIONS ADOPTED IN ACCORDANCE WITH
21 SUBSECTION (C) OF THIS SECTION SHALL:
22 (1) AWARD THE STUDENT A SEAL OF CIVIC EXCELLENCE; AND
23 (2) RECOGNIZE THE AWARD BY AWARDING A CORD OR MEDAL OR BY
24 ATTACHING OTHER INSIGNIA ON THE STUDENT’S HIGH SCHOOL DIPLOMA.
25 (G) (F) A PARTICIPATING LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM MAY COLLABORATE
26 WITH NONPROFIT ORGANIZATIONS, CIVIC ORGANIZATIONS, OR INSTITUTIONS OF
27 HIGHER EDUCATION TO SUPPORT THE IMPLEMENTATION OF THE PROGRAM.
28 (H) (G) ON OR BEFORE JULY 1, 2028, AND EACH JULY 1 THEREAFTER,
29 EACH PARTICIPATING LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM SHALL SUBMIT TO THE DEPARTMENT
30 A REPORT ON THE PROGRAM BASED ON THE MOST RECENTLY COMPLETED SCHOOL
31 YEAR.
4 SENATE BILL 204
1 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
2 1, 2026.
Approved:
