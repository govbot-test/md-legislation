6 (A) ON WRITTEN REQUEST OF A COUNTY SUPERINTENDENT OR A
7 DETERMINATION BY THE STATE SUPERINTENDENT THAT IMMEDIATE ACTION IS
8 NECESSARY, THE STATE SUPERINTENDENT MAY DECLARE A PROLONGED STATE OF
9 EMERGENCY THAT PREVENTS REGULAR, IN–PERSON ATTENDANCE AT A PUBLIC

12 (2) A CIVIL DISASTER;
13 (3) A PUBLIC HEALTH EMERGENCY; OR
14 (4) A SPECIFIC SECURITY THREAT IDENTIFIED BY FEDERAL, STATE,
15 OR LOCAL LAW ENFORCEMENT.

19 (I) THE MARYLAND DEPARTMENT OF EMERGENCY

21 (II) THE DEPARTMENT OF STATE POLICE; OR
22 (III) THE MARYLAND DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH;
23 (2) ISSUE THE DECLARATION IN WRITING;
24 (3) INDICATE IN THE DECLARATION:
25 (I) THE NATURE OF THE EMERGENCY; AND
26 (II) THE SPECIFIC LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEMS OR INDIVIDUAL

24 7–14A–02. 7–14A–03.
25 (a) If there is a prolonged state of emergency AFFECTING THE LOCAL SCHOOL
26 SYSTEM, a county board may transition the ONE OR MORE public schools in the county to
27 virtual education under an approved virtual education plan, subject to the limitations of
28 this section.
29 (b) (1) (i) On or before June 1, 2024, a county board shall, in consultation
30 with county administrators and school staff, adopt a virtual education plan for use during
31 a prolonged state of emergency AFFECTING THE LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM.

1 (ii) A county board shall use the county board’s continuity of learning
2 plan in effect during the 2020–2021 school year to form a virtual education plan.
3 (2) A virtual education plan shall include the following components:
4 (i) A staffing and personnel assignment plan;
5 (ii) A student instruction plan;
6 (iii) A student assessment and learning support plan;
7 (iv) A social and emotional learning plan;
8 (v) A community communications plan;
9 (vi) A technology plan;
10 (vii) A nutritional and health services plan; and
11 (viii) A plan for return to in–person instruction.
12 (c) A county board shall update the virtual education plan every 2 years.
13 (d) (1) If a prolonged state of emergency AFFECTING THE LOCAL SCHOOL
14 SYSTEM requires the cessation of in–school attendance at a public school and the county
15 board determines virtual education is required, a county board immediately shall
16 implement the virtual education plan and transition the public school to virtual education.
17 (2) (i) A county board may adjust the virtual education plan to fit the
18 specific needs of the schools affected and the specific circumstances of the emergency.
19 (ii) A county board shall submit an adjusted plan to the State Board
20 as soon as practicable.
21 (e) (1) Within 10 calendar days of receipt of a virtual education plan, the State
22 Board shall:
23 (i) Accept the plan; or
24 (ii) Return the plan to the county board for specific modification.
25 (2) If an adjusted virtual education plan is returned, the county board shall
26 modify and resubmit the plan within 5 calendar days after the date the plan is returned.
27 (f) The county board shall publish the most recent version of the virtual
28 education plan on the county board’s website.
