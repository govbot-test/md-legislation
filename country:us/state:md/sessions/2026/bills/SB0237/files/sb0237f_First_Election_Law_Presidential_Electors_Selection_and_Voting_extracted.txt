A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Election Law – Presidential Electors – Selection and Voting
3 FOR the purpose of altering the procedures for the selection of presidential elector
4 nominees and alternate presidential elector nominees; requiring the State
5 Administrator of Elections to preside at a certain meeting of certain presidential
6 electors; altering the procedures for the meeting of presidential electors; establishing
7 procedures for the preparation, delivery, signing, and transmission of an amended
8 certificate of ascertainment after the vote of the State’s presidential electors under
9 certain circumstances; and generally relating to presidential electors.

5 Article – Election Law
6 [8–504.] 8–505.
7 (a) (1) At the general election for President and Vice President of the United
8 States there shall be elected, in accordance with subsection (b) of this section, the number
9 of presidential electors to which this State is entitled.
10 (2) Presidential electors shall be elected at large by the voters of the entire
11 State.
12 (b) (1) The names of the candidates for the office of presidential elector may
13 not be printed on the ballot.
14 (2) A vote for the candidates for President and Vice President of a political
15 party shall be considered to be and counted as a vote for each of the NOMINEES FOR
16 presidential [electors] ELECTOR of the political party [nominated] SUBMITTED in
17 accordance with § 8–503 of this subtitle.
18 (3) A VOTE FOR AN UNAFFILIATED CANDIDATE FOR PRESIDENT AND
19 VICE PRESIDENT SHALL BE CONSIDERED TO BE AND COUNTED AS A VOTE FOR EACH
20 OF THE NOMINEES FOR PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR SUBMITTED BY THE UNAFFILIATED
21 PRESIDENTIAL CANDIDATE TO THE STATE BOARD IN ACCORDANCE WITH § 8–503 OF
22 THIS SUBTITLE.
23 [8–505.] 8–508.
24 (a) (1) The individuals elected to the office of presidential elector shall meet in
25 the State House in the City of Annapolis on the day provided by the Constitution and laws
26 of the United States.
27 (2) The conduct of the meeting shall be consistent with the requirements
28 of federal law.
29 (b) (1) Before proceeding to perform the duties of their office, the presidential
30 electors who are present shall fill any vacancy in the office of PRESIDENTIAL elector IN
31 ACCORDANCE WITH § 8–507 OF THIS SUBTITLE, whether the vacancy is caused by
32 absence or other reason.
33 (2) An individual appointed to fill a vacancy is entitled to all rights and

1 privileges of the duly elected electors.
2 (c) (1) After taking the oath prescribed by Article I, § 9 of the Maryland
3 Constitution before the Clerk of the Supreme Court of Maryland or, in the Clerk’s absence,
4 before one of the Clerk’s deputies, the presidential electors shall cast their votes for the
5 candidates for President and Vice President who received a plurality of the votes cast in
6 the State of Maryland.
7 (2) THE STATE ADMINISTRATOR SHALL PROVIDE EACH
8 PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR WITH A PRESIDENTIAL AND VICE PRESIDENTIAL BALLOT.
9 (3) EACH PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR SHALL MARK THE PRESIDENTIAL
10 ELECTOR’S PRESIDENTIAL AND VICE PRESIDENTIAL BALLOTS WITH THE
11 PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR’S SIGNATURE AND THE PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR’S
12 LEGIBLY PRINTED NAME.
13 (D) (1) EACH PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR SHALL PRESENT BOTH
14 COMPLETED BALLOTS TO THE STATE ADMINISTRATOR.
15 (2) THE STATE ADMINISTRATOR SHALL EXAMINE THE BALLOTS
16 PRESENTED UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION AND ACCEPT AS CAST ALL
17 BALLOTS OF PRESIDENTIAL ELECTORS WHOSE VOTES ARE CONSISTENT WITH THEIR
18 PLEDGES EXECUTED UNDER § 8–504 OR § 8–507(D) OF THIS SUBTITLE.
19 (3) THE STATE ADMINISTRATOR MAY NOT COUNT EITHER A
20 PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR’S PRESIDENTIAL OR VICE PRESIDENTIAL BALLOT IF THE
21 PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR HAS NOT MARKED BOTH BALLOTS OR HAS MARKED A
22 BALLOT IN VIOLATION OF THE PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR’S PLEDGE.
23 (E) A PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR WHO REFUSES TO PRESENT A BALLOT,
24 PRESENTS AN UNMARKED BALLOT, OR PRESENTS A BALLOT MARKED IN VIOLATION
25 OF THE PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR’S PLEDGE EXECUTED UNDER § 8–504 OR § 8–507(D)
26 OF THIS SUBTITLE VACATES THE OFFICE OF ELECTOR, CREATING A VACANT
27 POSITION TO BE FILLED UNDER § 8–507 OF THIS SUBTITLE.
28 (F) THE STATE ADMINISTRATOR SHALL DISTRIBUTE BALLOTS AND
29 COLLECT BALLOTS FROM A SUBSTITUTE PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR AND REPEAT THE
30 PROCESS UNDER THIS SECTION, DECLARING AND FILLING VACANT POSITIONS AS
31 REQUIRED, AND RECORDING APPROPRIATELY COMPLETED BALLOTS FROM THE
32 SUBSTITUTED PRESIDENTIAL ELECTORS, UNTIL ALL OF THE STATE’S ELECTORAL
33 VOTES HAVE BEEN CAST AND RECORDED.
