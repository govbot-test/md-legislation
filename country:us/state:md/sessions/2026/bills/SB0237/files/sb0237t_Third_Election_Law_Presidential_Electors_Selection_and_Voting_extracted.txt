6 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That the Laws of Maryland read
7 as follows:
8 Article – Election Law
9 [8–504.] 8–505.
10 (a) (1) At the general election for President and Vice President of the United
11 States there shall be elected, in accordance with subsection (b) of this section, the number
12 of presidential electors to which this State is entitled.
13 (2) Presidential electors shall be elected at large by the voters of the entire
14 State.
15 (b) (1) The names of the candidates for the office of presidential elector may
16 not be printed on the ballot.
17 (2) A vote for the candidates for President and Vice President of a political
18 party shall be considered to be and counted as a vote for each of the NOMINEES FOR
19 presidential [electors] ELECTOR of the political party [nominated] SUBMITTED in
20 accordance with § 8–503 of this subtitle.
21 (3) A VOTE FOR AN UNAFFILIATED CANDIDATE FOR PRESIDENT AND
22 VICE PRESIDENT SHALL BE CONSIDERED TO BE AND COUNTED AS A VOTE FOR EACH
23 OF THE NOMINEES FOR PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR SUBMITTED BY THE UNAFFILIATED
24 PRESIDENTIAL CANDIDATE TO THE STATE BOARD IN ACCORDANCE WITH § 8–503 OF
25 THIS SUBTITLE.
26 [8–505.] 8–508.
27 (a) (1) The individuals elected to the office of presidential elector shall meet in
28 the State House in the City of Annapolis on the day provided by the Constitution and laws
29 of the United States.
30 (2) The conduct of the meeting shall be consistent with the requirements
31 of federal law.
32 (b) (1) Before proceeding to perform the duties of their office, the presidential

1 electors who are present shall fill any vacancy in the office of PRESIDENTIAL elector IN
2 ACCORDANCE WITH § 8–507 OF THIS SUBTITLE, whether the vacancy is caused by
3 absence or other reason.
4 (2) An individual appointed to fill a vacancy is entitled to all rights and
5 privileges of the duly elected electors.
6 (c) (1) After taking the oath prescribed by Article I, § 9 of the Maryland
7 Constitution before the Clerk of the Supreme Court of Maryland or, in the Clerk’s absence,
8 before one of the Clerk’s deputies, the presidential electors shall cast their votes for the
9 candidates for President and Vice President who received a plurality of the votes cast in
10 the State of Maryland.
11 (2) THE STATE ADMINISTRATOR SHALL PROVIDE EACH
12 PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR WITH A PRESIDENTIAL AND VICE PRESIDENTIAL BALLOT.
13 (3) EACH PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR SHALL MARK THE PRESIDENTIAL
14 ELECTOR’S PRESIDENTIAL AND VICE PRESIDENTIAL BALLOTS WITH THE
15 PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR’S SIGNATURE AND THE PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR’S
16 LEGIBLY PRINTED NAME.
17 (D) (1) EACH PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR SHALL PRESENT BOTH
18 COMPLETED BALLOTS TO THE STATE ADMINISTRATOR.
19 (2) THE STATE ADMINISTRATOR SHALL EXAMINE THE BALLOTS
20 PRESENTED UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION AND ACCEPT AS CAST ALL
21 BALLOTS OF PRESIDENTIAL ELECTORS WHOSE VOTES ARE CONSISTENT WITH THEIR
22 PLEDGES EXECUTED UNDER § 8–504 OR § 8–507(D) OF THIS SUBTITLE.
23 (3) THE STATE ADMINISTRATOR MAY NOT COUNT EITHER A
24 PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR’S PRESIDENTIAL OR VICE PRESIDENTIAL BALLOT IF THE
25 PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR HAS NOT MARKED BOTH BALLOTS OR HAS MARKED A
26 BALLOT IN VIOLATION OF THE PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR’S PLEDGE.
27 (E) A PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR WHO REFUSES TO PRESENT A BALLOT,
28 PRESENTS AN UNMARKED BALLOT, OR PRESENTS A BALLOT MARKED IN VIOLATION
29 OF THE PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR’S PLEDGE EXECUTED UNDER § 8–504 OR § 8–507(D)
30 OF THIS SUBTITLE VACATES THE OFFICE OF ELECTOR, CREATING A VACANT
31 POSITION TO BE FILLED UNDER § 8–507 OF THIS SUBTITLE.
32 (F) THE STATE ADMINISTRATOR SHALL DISTRIBUTE BALLOTS AND
33 COLLECT BALLOTS FROM A SUBSTITUTE PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR AND REPEAT THE

35 REQUIRED, AND RECORDING APPROPRIATELY COMPLETED BALLOTS FROM THE

8 Article – Election Law
9 [8–504.] 8–505.
10 (a) (1) At the general election for President and Vice President of the United
11 States there shall be elected, in accordance with subsection (b) of this section, the number
12 of presidential electors to which this State is entitled.
13 (2) Presidential electors shall be elected at large by the voters of the entire
14 State.
15 (b) (1) The names of the candidates for the office of presidential elector may
16 not be printed on the ballot.
17 (2) A vote for the candidates for President and Vice President of a political
18 party shall be considered to be and counted as a vote for each of the NOMINEES FOR
19 presidential [electors] ELECTOR of the political party [nominated] SUBMITTED in
20 accordance with § 8–503 of this subtitle.
21 (3) A VOTE FOR AN UNAFFILIATED CANDIDATE FOR PRESIDENT AND
22 VICE PRESIDENT SHALL BE CONSIDERED TO BE AND COUNTED AS A VOTE FOR EACH
23 OF THE NOMINEES FOR PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR SUBMITTED BY THE UNAFFILIATED
24 PRESIDENTIAL CANDIDATE TO THE STATE BOARD IN ACCORDANCE WITH § 8–503 OF
25 THIS SUBTITLE.
26 [8–505.] 8–508.
27 (a) (1) The individuals elected to the office of presidential elector shall meet in
28 the State House in the City of Annapolis on the day provided by the Constitution and laws
29 of the United States.
30 (2) The conduct of the meeting shall be consistent with the requirements
31 of federal law.
32 (b) (1) Before proceeding to perform the duties of their office, the presidential

1 electors who are present shall fill any vacancy in the office of PRESIDENTIAL elector IN
2 ACCORDANCE WITH § 8–507 OF THIS SUBTITLE, whether the vacancy is caused by
3 absence or other reason.
4 (2) An individual appointed to fill a vacancy is entitled to all rights and
5 privileges of the duly elected electors.
6 (c) (1) After taking the oath prescribed by Article I, § 9 of the Maryland
7 Constitution before the Clerk of the Supreme Court of Maryland or, in the Clerk’s absence,
8 before one of the Clerk’s deputies, the presidential electors shall cast their votes for the
9 candidates for President and Vice President who received a plurality of the votes cast in
10 the State of Maryland.
11 (2) THE STATE ADMINISTRATOR SHALL PROVIDE EACH
12 PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR WITH A PRESIDENTIAL AND VICE PRESIDENTIAL BALLOT.
13 (3) EACH PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR SHALL MARK THE PRESIDENTIAL
14 ELECTOR’S PRESIDENTIAL AND VICE PRESIDENTIAL BALLOTS WITH THE
15 PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR’S SIGNATURE AND THE PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR’S
16 LEGIBLY PRINTED NAME.
17 (D) (1) EACH PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR SHALL PRESENT BOTH
18 COMPLETED BALLOTS TO THE STATE ADMINISTRATOR.
19 (2) THE STATE ADMINISTRATOR SHALL EXAMINE THE BALLOTS
20 PRESENTED UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION AND ACCEPT AS CAST ALL
21 BALLOTS OF PRESIDENTIAL ELECTORS WHOSE VOTES ARE CONSISTENT WITH THEIR
22 PLEDGES EXECUTED UNDER § 8–504 OR § 8–507(D) OF THIS SUBTITLE.
23 (3) THE STATE ADMINISTRATOR MAY NOT COUNT EITHER A
24 PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR’S PRESIDENTIAL OR VICE PRESIDENTIAL BALLOT IF THE
25 PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR HAS NOT MARKED BOTH BALLOTS OR HAS MARKED A
26 BALLOT IN VIOLATION OF THE PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR’S PLEDGE.
27 (E) A PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR WHO REFUSES TO PRESENT A BALLOT,
28 PRESENTS AN UNMARKED BALLOT, OR PRESENTS A BALLOT MARKED IN VIOLATION
29 OF THE PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR’S PLEDGE EXECUTED UNDER § 8–504 OR § 8–507(D)
30 OF THIS SUBTITLE VACATES THE OFFICE OF ELECTOR, CREATING A VACANT
31 POSITION TO BE FILLED UNDER § 8–507 OF THIS SUBTITLE.
32 (F) THE STATE ADMINISTRATOR SHALL DISTRIBUTE BALLOTS AND
33 COLLECT BALLOTS FROM A SUBSTITUTE PRESIDENTIAL ELECTOR AND REPEAT THE
34 PROCESS UNDER THIS SECTION, DECLARING AND FILLING VACANT POSITIONS AS
35 REQUIRED, AND RECORDING APPROPRIATELY COMPLETED BALLOTS FROM THE
