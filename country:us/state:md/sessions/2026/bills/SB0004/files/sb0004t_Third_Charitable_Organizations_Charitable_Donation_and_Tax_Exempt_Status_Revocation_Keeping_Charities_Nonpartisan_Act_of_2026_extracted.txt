1 AN ACT concerning
2 Charitable Organizations – Charitable Donation and Tax–Exempt Status –
3 Revocation
4 (Keeping Charities Nonpartisan Act of 2026)
5 FOR the purpose of altering, for the purpose of certain provisions of law that regulate
6 charitable organizations, the definition of “charitable organization” to include a
7 certain person that is recognized by the Internal Revenue Service to receive
8 donations that are deductible from federal income tax and a certain private
9 foundation; prohibiting certain charitable organizations, including certain private
10 foundations, from participating in or intervening in a political campaign on behalf of
11 or in opposition to a candidate for public office; authorizing the Secretary of State
12 and the Attorney General, acting jointly, to order the revocation of certain
13 tax–exempt statuses of a charitable organization, including a certain private
14 foundation, under certain circumstances; requiring the Comptroller and the Director
15 of the State Department of Assessments and Taxation to revoke certain tax–exempt
16 statuses under certain circumstances; providing that a charitable organization,
17 including a certain private foundation, whose tax–exempt status is revoked does not
18 qualify for certain income, sales and use, and property tax exemptions; requiring the
19 Attorney General to monitor action by the federal government for a certain purpose;
20 and generally relating to charitable organizations.
21 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
22 Article – Business Regulation
23 Section 6–101(a), 6–402(a), and 6.5–101(a)
