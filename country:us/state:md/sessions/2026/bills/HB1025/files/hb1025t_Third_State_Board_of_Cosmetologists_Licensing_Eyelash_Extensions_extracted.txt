1 AN ACT concerning
2 State Board of Cosmetologists – Licensing – Eyelash Extensions
3 FOR the purpose of repealing the option for an applicant for a limited license to provide
4 eyelash extension services to pass a practical and written examination administered
5 by the State Board of Cosmetology in order to be licensed; and generally relating to
6 eyelash extension licensing and the State Board of Cosmetologists.
7 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
8 Article – Business Occupations and Professions
9 Section 5–305
10 Annotated Code of Maryland
11 (2018 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
12 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
13 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
14 Article – Business Occupations and Professions
15 5–305.
16 (a) To qualify for a limited license to practice cosmetology, an applicant shall be
17 an individual who meets the requirements of this section.
18 (b) An applicant for a limited license to provide esthetic services shall:

15 violence awareness.
16 (f) An applicant for a limited license to provide nail technician services shall:
17 (1) be at least 17 years old;
18 (2) have successfully completed a 9th grade education or the equivalent;
19 (3) have received training by:
20 (i) serving as a registered apprentice for at least 3 months as
21 provided under §§ 5–404 and 5–405 of this title; or
22 (ii) successfully completing at least 250 hours of instruction in
23 providing nail technician services in a cosmetology school approved by the State
24 Department of Education or the Maryland Higher Education Commission in consultation
25 with the Board; and
26 (4) have completed 1 hour of in–person or virtual training in domestic
27 violence awareness under § 5–305.1 of this subtitle.
28 (g) If an applicant is licensed to practice as an esthetician, eyelash extension
29 technician, hairstylist, hairstylist – blow drying technician, or nail technician in another
30 state or foreign country, the applicant meets the training requirements of this section if the
31 applicant has held a limited license in the other state or foreign country and has actively
32 practiced in the other state or foreign country for a period of 6 months.
