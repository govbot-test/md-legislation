A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Residential Property – Service Agreement – Defunct Service Providers
3 FOR the purpose of voiding certain service agreements for residential property entered into
4 before a certain date if the service provider is considered not in good standing with
5 the State Department of Assessments and Taxation on or after a certain date; and
6 generally relating to residential property and service agreements with defunct
7 service providers.
8 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
9 Article – Real Property
10 Section 14–901(a) and (d) and 14–903
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
13 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
14 Article – Real Property
15 Section 14–902(a)
16 Annotated Code of Maryland
17 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
18 BY adding to
19 Article – Real Property
20 Section 14–903.1
21 Annotated Code of Maryland
22 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
23 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
24 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
25 Article – Real Property
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from exist in g l aw . *sb0814*
2 SENATE BILL 814
1 14–901.
2 (a) In this subtitle the following words have the meanings indicated.
3 (d) (1) “Service agreement” means an agreement where a service provider
4 agrees to provide an individual services for:
5 (i) The maintenance of residential property that the individual
6 owns; or
7 (ii) The purchase or sale of residential property.
8 (2) “Service agreement” includes any memorandum, notice, agreement, or
9 similar document that is used by a service provider to cause a service agreement to be
10 recorded.
11 14–902.
12 (a) [This] EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN § 14–903.1 OF THIS SUBTITLE, THIS
13 subtitle applies only to a service agreement entered into on or after June 1, 2023.
14 14–903.
15 A service agreement, or any part of a service agreement, may not:
16 (1) Be effective for more than 1 year; or
17 (2) (i) Purport to run with the land or bind subsequent bona fide
18 purchasers;
19 (ii) Purport to create a lien, encumbrance, or security interest on the
20 residential property of a party to the service agreement; or
21 (iii) Allow the service provider to assign or transfer the right to
22 provide services under the service agreement to another person without notice or the
23 consent of a party to the service agreement.
24 14–903.1.
25 (A) THIS SECTION APPLIES ONLY TO A SERVICE AGREEMENT WITH A
26 SERVICE PROVIDER THAT HAS FORFEITED THE RIGHT OF THE SERVICE PROVIDER
27 TO CONDUCT BUSINESS IN THE STATE AND, ON OR AFTER JUNE 1, 2026, IS DEFUNCT
28 AND CONSIDERED NOT IN GOOD STANDING WITH THE STATE DEPARTMENT OF
29 ASSESSMENTS AND TAXATION.
SENATE BILL 814 3
1 (B) A SERVICE AGREEMENT, OR ANY PART OF A SERVICE AGREEMENT,
2 ENTERED INTO BEFORE JUNE 1, 2023, MAY NOT:
3 (1) BE EFFECTIVE FOR MORE THAN 2 YEARS; OR
4 (2) (I) PURPORT TO RUN WITH THE LAND OR BIND SUBSEQUENT
5 BONA FIDE PURCHASERS;
6 (II) PURPORT TO CREATE A LIEN, ENCUMBRANCE, OR SECURITY
7 INTEREST ON THE RESIDENTIAL PROPERTY OF A PARTY TO THE SERVICE
8 AGREEMENT; OR
9 (III) ALLOW THE SERVICE PROVIDER TO ASSIGN OR TRANSFER
10 THE RIGHT TO PROVIDE SERVICES UNDER THE SERVICE AGREEMENT TO ANOTHER
11 PERSON WITHOUT NOTICE OR THE CONSENT OF A PARTY TO THE SERVICE
12 AGREEMENT.
13 (C) A SERVICE AGREEMENT, OR ANY PART OF A SERVICE AGREEMENT, THAT
14 VIOLATES SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION SHALL BE CONSIDERED VOID AND
15 UNENFORCEABLE.
16 (D) (1) IF A SERVICE AGREEMENT THAT VIOLATES SUBSECTION (B) OF
17 THIS SECTION IS RECORDED, ANY PERSON WITH AN INTEREST IN THE RESIDENTIAL
18 PROPERTY THAT IS SUBJECT TO A LIEN ARISING FROM THE SERVICE AGREEMENT
19 MAY BRING AN ACTION AGAINST THE SERVICE PROVIDER IN THE CIRCUIT COURT OF
20 THE COUNTY WHERE THE RESIDENTIAL PROPERTY IS LOCATED TO REQUEST A
21 DETERMINATION THAT THE SERVICE AGREEMENT IS VOID AND UNENFORCEABLE
22 UNDER THIS SUBTITLE.
23 (2) A PERSON WHO BRINGS AN ACTION UNDER THIS SUBSECTION MAY
24 SEEK:
25 (I) COMPENSATORY DAMAGES; AND
26 (II) REASONABLE ATTORNEY’S FEES AND COSTS.
27 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect June
28 1, 2026.
