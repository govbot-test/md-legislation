A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Health Occupations – Pharmacists – Vaccination Orders
3 FOR the purpose of authorizing a pharmacist to order certain vaccinations for individuals
4 who are at least a certain age without having to also administer the vaccination; and
5 generally relating to the ordering of vaccinations by pharmacists.
6 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
7 Article – Health Occupations
8 Section 12–508
9 Annotated Code of Maryland
10 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
11 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
12 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
13 Article – Health Occupations
14 12–508.
15 (a) (1) Subject to paragraph (4) of this subsection, a pharmacist may order
16 [and] A VACCINATION FOR OR administer a vaccination to an individual who is at least 3
17 years old if:
18 (i) The vaccine is:
19 1. An influenza vaccine;
20 2. A COVID–19 vaccine; or
21 3. Used in response to a public health emergency;
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from exist in g l aw . *sb0773*
2 SENATE BILL 773
1 (ii) The pharmacist has completed a practical training program of at
2 least 20 hours that is approved by the Accreditation Council for Pharmacy Education and
3 includes:
4 1. Hands–on injection techniques;
5 2. Clinical evaluation of indications and contraindications of
6 vaccines; and
7 3. The recognition and treatment of emergency reactions to
8 vaccines;
9 (iii) The pharmacist has a current certificate in basic
10 cardiopulmonary resuscitation;
11 (iv) The pharmacist has completed a minimum of 2 hours of
12 continuing pharmaceutical education related to immunizations that is approved by the
13 Accreditation Council for Pharmacy Education as part of the license renewal requirements
14 under § 12–309 of this title;
15 (v) The pharmacist complies with the record–keeping and reporting
16 requirements in paragraph (5) of this subsection and any corresponding regulations
17 adopted by the Board; and
18 (vi) If the vaccination is administered to an individual under the age
19 of 18 years, OR ORDERED FOR ADMINISTRATION BY AN ADULT CAREGIVER TO AN
20 INDIVIDUAL UNDER THE AGE OF 18 YEARS, the pharmacist informs the child vaccination
21 patient and adult caregiver who is accompanying the child of the importance of well–child
22 visits with a pediatric primary care provider and refers the patient to a pediatric primary
23 care provider when appropriate.
24 (2) Subject to paragraph (4) of this subsection, a pharmacist who has met
25 the requirement of paragraph (1)(ii) through (vi) of this subsection may administer to an
26 individual who is at least 7 years old a vaccination that is:
27 (i) Recommended by the Centers for Disease Control and
28 Prevention’s Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices; or
29 (ii) Approved or authorized by the U.S. Food and Drug
30 Administration.
31 (3) Subject to paragraph (4) of this subsection, a pharmacist who has met
32 the requirement of paragraph (1)(ii), (iii), and (iv) of this subsection may administer to an
33 adult a vaccination that is recommended in the Centers for Disease Control and
34 Prevention’s Health Information for International Travel.
SENATE BILL 773 3
1 (4) (i) A pharmacist shall ORDER OR administer a vaccination under
2 paragraph (1), (2), or (3) of this subsection under a written protocol that:
3 1. Is vaccine specific; and
4 2. Meets criteria established by the Department, in
5 consultation with the Board, the Board of Physicians, and the Board of Nursing, in
6 regulation.
7 (ii) The recommendations of the Centers for Disease Control and
8 Prevention’s Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices cited in this section refer to
9 federal statutes, rules, and guidance in effect:
10 1. On December 31, 2024; or
11 2. At a later date to account for any new vaccines
12 recommended by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention’s Advisory Committee on
13 Immunization Practices after December 31, 2024.
14 (5) A pharmacist shall:
15 (i) Report all vaccinations administered OR ORDERED by the
16 pharmacist to the ImmuNet Program established under § 18–109 of the Health – General
17 Article;
18 (ii) If the vaccination has been administered in accordance with a
19 prescription, document at least one effort to inform the individual’s authorized prescriber
20 that the vaccination has been administered; and
21 (iii) Except for an influenza vaccination administered under
22 paragraph (1) of this subsection, if the authorized prescriber is not the individual’s primary
23 care provider or if the vaccination has not been administered in accordance with a
24 prescription, document at least one effort to inform the individual’s primary care provider
25 or other usual source of care that the vaccination has been administered.
26 (b) The Board shall:
27 (1) Set reasonable fees for the administration of vaccinations under this
28 section; and
29 (2) Adopt regulations that require a pharmacist to submit a registration
30 form to the Board that includes verification that the pharmacist:
31 (i) Has successfully completed a certification course approved by the
32 Board that included instruction in the guidelines and recommendations of the Centers for
33 Disease Control and Prevention regarding vaccinations in effect on December 31, 2024; and
4 SENATE BILL 773
1 (ii) Is certified in basic cardiopulmonary resuscitation and obtained
2 the certification through in–person classroom instruction.
3 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
4 October 1, 2026.
