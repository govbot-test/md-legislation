CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Labor and Employment – Mandatory Meetings on Religious or Political
3 Matters – Employee Attendance and Participation
4 (Maryland Worker Freedom Act)
5 FOR the purpose of prohibiting employers and their agents, representatives, and designees
6 from taking certain actions against an employee or applicant for employment
7 because the employee or applicant takes certain actions regarding
8 employer–sponsored meetings during which the employer communicates the opinion
9 of the employer regarding religious matters or political matters; authorizing an
10 employee to file a certain complaint with the Commissioner of Labor and Industry;
11 authorizing the Commissioner to take certain actions related to violations of certain
12 provisions of this Act; requiring employers to notify the employer’s employees in a
13 certain manner of the requirements and protections provided under certain
14 provisions of this Act; requiring the Commissioner to develop and make available a
15 certain poster and model notice; and generally relating to employee attendance and
16 participation in employer meetings on religious or political matters.

3 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
4 INDICATED.
5 (2) “POLITICAL MATTERS” MEANS MATTERS RELATING TO:
6 (I) ELECTIONS FOR POLITICAL OFFICE;
7 (II) POLITICAL PARTIES;
8 (III) PROPOSALS TO CHANGE:
9 1. LEGISLATION;
10 2. REGULATIONS; OR
11 3. PUBLIC POLICY; OR
12 (IV) THE DECISION TO JOIN OR SUPPORT A POLITICAL PARTY OR
13 POTENTIAL CIVIC, COMMUNITY, FRATERNAL, OR LABOR ORGANIZATION.
14 (3) “RELIGIOUS MATTERS” MEANS MATTERS RELATING TO
15 RELIGIOUS BELIEF, AFFILIATION, AND PRACTICE OR THE DECISION TO JOIN AND
16 SUPPORT A RELIGIOUS ORGANIZATION OR ASSOCIATION.
17 (B) THIS SECTION DOES NOT APPLY TO AN EMPLOYER THAT:
18 (1) IS A RELIGIOUS CORPORATION, ORGANIZATION, OR ASSOCIATION
19 OR AN EDUCATIONAL INSTITUTION OR SOCIETY THAT IS EXEMPT FROM THE
20 REQUIREMENTS OF TITLE VII OF THE CIVIL RIGHTS ACT OF 1964 UNDER 42 U.S.C.
21 § 2000E–1(A) OR IS EXEMPT FOR EMPLOYMENT DISCRIMINATION PROTECTIONS
22 UNDER STATE LAW;
23 (2) IS A POLITICAL ORGANIZATION THAT REQUIRES ITS EMPLOYEES
24 TO ATTEND AN EMPLOYER–SPONSORED MEETING OR TO PARTICIPATE IN
25 COMMUNICATIONS WITH THE EMPLOYER OR THE EMPLOYER’S AGENTS OR OTHER
26 REPRESENTATIVES FOR THE PURPOSE OF COMMUNICATING THE EMPLOYER’S
27 POLITICAL TENETS OR PURPOSES;

2 AN INSTRUCTOR TO ATTEND LECTURES ON POLITICAL OR RELIGIOUS MATTERS AS

15 (C) AN EMPLOYER, OR THE EMPLOYER’S AGENT, REPRESENTATIVE, OR
16 DESIGNEE, MAY NOT:
17 (1) DISCHARGE, DISCIPLINE, OR OTHERWISE PENALIZE OR
18 THREATEN TO DISCHARGE, DISCIPLINE, OR OTHERWISE PENALIZE AN EMPLOYEE
19 BECAUSE THE EMPLOYEE DECLINES TO ATTEND, PARTICIPATE IN, OR LISTEN TO
20 COMMUNICATIONS FROM THE EMPLOYER, OR THE EMPLOYER’S AGENT,
21 REPRESENTATIVE, OR DESIGNEE, IN AN EMPLOYER–SPONSORED MEETING DURING
22 WHICH THE EMPLOYER COMMUNICATES THE OPINION OF THE EMPLOYER
23 REGARDING RELIGIOUS MATTERS OR POLITICAL MATTERS; OR
24 (2) FAIL OR REFUSE TO HIRE AN APPLICANT FOR EMPLOYMENT AS A
25 RESULT OF THE APPLICANT’S REFUSAL TO ATTEND OR PARTICIPATE IN AN
26 EMPLOYER–SPONSORED MEETING DURING WHICH THE EMPLOYER COMMUNICATES
27 THE OPINION OF THE EMPLOYER REGARDING RELIGIOUS MATTERS OR POLITICAL
28 MATTERS.
29 (D) THIS SECTION DOES NOT PROHIBIT AN EMPLOYER:
30 (1) FROM COMMUNICATING INFORMATION THAT THE EMPLOYER IS
31 REQUIRED BY LAW TO COMMUNICATE;
32 (2) FROM CONDUCTING A MEETING THAT INVOLVES RELIGIOUS
33 MATTERS OR POLITICAL MATTERS IF ATTENDANCE AND PARTICIPATION ARE
34 VOLUNTARY;
