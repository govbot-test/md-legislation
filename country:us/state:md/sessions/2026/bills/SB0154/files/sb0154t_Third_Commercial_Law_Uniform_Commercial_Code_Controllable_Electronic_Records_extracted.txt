CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Commercial Law – Uniform Commercial Code – Controllable Electronic Records
3 FOR the purpose of adding Article 12 of the Uniform Commercial Code to the Maryland
4 Uniform Commercial Code to establish provisions governing the transfer of property
5 rights in certain controllable electronic records, controllable accounts, and
6 controllable payment intangibles; and generally relating to property rights under the
7 Maryland Uniform Commercial Code.
8 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
9 Article – Commercial Law
10 Section 1–101
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2013 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
13 BY adding to
14 Article – Commercial Law
15 Section 10A–101 through 10A–107 to be under the new title “Title 10A. Controllable
16 Electronic Records”

18 (2013 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

1 Article – Commercial Law
2 1–101.
3 (a) Titles 1 through [10] 10A of this article may be cited as the Maryland Uniform
4 Commercial Code.
5 (b) This title may be cited as Maryland Uniform Commercial Code – General
6 Provisions.
7 TITLE 10A. CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORDS.
8 10A–101.
9 THIS TITLE MAY BE CITED AS THE MARYLAND UNIFORM COMMERCIAL
10 CODE – CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORDS.
11 10A–102.
12 (A) IN THIS TITLE THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
13 INDICATED.
14 (B) (1) “ACCOUNT DEBTOR” MEANS A PERSON OBLIGATED ON AN
15 ACCOUNT, A CHATTEL PAPER, OR A GENERAL INTANGIBLE.
16 (2) “ACCOUNT DEBTOR” DOES NOT INCLUDE A PERSON OBLIGATED
17 TO PAY A NEGOTIABLE INSTRUMENT, EVEN IF THE NEGOTIABLE INSTRUMENT
18 EVIDENCES CHATTEL PAPER.
19 (C) (1) “CHATTEL PAPER” MEANS:
20 (I) A RIGHT TO PAYMENT OF A MONETARY OBLIGATION
21 SECURED BY SPECIFIC GOODS, IF THE RIGHT TO PAYMENT AND SECURITY
22 AGREEMENT ARE EVIDENCED BY A RECORD; OR
23 (II) A RIGHT TO PAYMENT OF A MONETARY OBLIGATION OWED
24 BY A LESSEE UNDER A LEASE AGREEMENT WITH RESPECT TO SPECIFIC GOODS AND
25 A MONETARY OBLIGATION OWED BY THE LESSEE IN CONNECTION WITH THE
26 TRANSACTION GIVING RISE TO THE LEASE, IF:
27 1. THE RIGHT TO PAYMENT AND THE LEASE AGREEMENT
28 ARE EVIDENCED BY A RECORD; AND
SENATE BILL 154 3
1 2. THE PREDOMINANT PURPOSE OF THE TRANSACTION
2 GIVING RISE TO THE LEASE WAS TO GIVE THE LESSEE THE RIGHT TO POSSESSION
3 AND USE OF THE GOODS.
4 (2) “CHATTEL PAPER” DOES NOT INCLUDE A RIGHT TO A PAYMENT
5 ARISING OUT OF A CHARTER OR OTHER CONTRACT INVOLVING THE USE OR HIRE OF
6 A VESSEL OR A RIGHT TO PAYMENT ARISING OUT OF THE USE OF A CREDIT OR
7 CHARGE CARD OR INFORMATION CONTAINED ON OR FOR USE WITH THE CARD.
8 (D) “CONTROLLABLE ACCOUNT” MEANS AN ACCOUNT EVIDENCED BY A
9 CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD THAT PROVIDES THAT THE ACCOUNT
10 DEBTOR UNDERTAKES TO PAY THE PERSON THAT HAS CONTROL OF THE
11 CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD IN ACCORDANCE WITH § 10A–105 OF THIS
12 TITLE.
13 (E) (1) “CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD” MEANS A RECORD
14 STORED IN AN ELECTRONIC MEDIUM THAT CAN BE SUBJECTED TO CONTROL UNDER
15 § 10A–105 OF THIS TITLE.
16 (2) “CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD” DOES NOT INCLUDE A
17 CONTROLLABLE ACCOUNT, A CONTROLLABLE PAYMENT INTANGIBLE, A DEPOSIT
18 ACCOUNT, AN ELECTRONIC COPY OF A RECORD EVIDENCING CHATTEL PAPER, AN
19 ELECTRONIC DOCUMENT OF TITLE, ELECTRONIC MONEY, INVESTMENT PROPERTY,
20 OR A TRANSFERABLE RECORD.
21 (F) “CONTROLLABLE PAYMENT INTANGIBLE” MEANS A PAYMENT
22 INTANGIBLE EVIDENCED BY A CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD THAT
23 PROVIDES THAT THE ACCOUNT DEBTOR UNDERTAKES TO PAY THE PERSON THAT
24 HAS CONTROL OF THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD IN ACCORDANCE WITH
25 § 10A–105 OF THIS TITLE.
26 (G) “DEPOSIT ACCOUNT” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 9–102 OF THIS
27 ARTICLE.
28 (H) “ELECTRONIC MONEY” MEANS MONEY IN AN ELECTRONIC FORM.
29 (I) “INVESTMENT PROPERTY” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 9–102 OF
30 THIS ARTICLE.
31 (J) “QUALIFYING PURCHASER” MEANS A PURCHASER OF A CONTROLLABLE
32 ELECTRONIC RECORD OR AN INTEREST IN A CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD
33 THAT OBTAINS CONTROL OF THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD FOR VALUE,
4 SENATE BILL 154
1 IN GOOD FAITH, AND WITHOUT NOTICE OF A CLAIM OF PROPERTY RIGHT IN THE
2 CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD.
3 (K) “TRANSFERABLE RECORDS” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 21–115 OF
4 THIS ARTICLE.
5 (L) “VALUE” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 3–303 OF THIS ARTICLE BUT
6 WITH RESPECT TO A CONTROLLABLE ACCOUNT, CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC
7 RECORD, OR CONTROLLABLE PAYMENT INTANGIBLE, RATHER THAN AN
8 INSTRUMENT.
9 10A–103.
10 (A) IF THERE IS A CONFLICT BETWEEN THIS TITLE AND TITLE 9 OF THIS
11 ARTICLE, TITLE 9 OF THIS ARTICLE GOVERNS.
12 (B) A TRANSACTION SUBJECT TO THIS TITLE IS ALSO SUBJECT TO ANY
13 OTHER APPLICABLE STATE LAW.
14 10A–104.
15 (A) THIS SECTION APPLIES TO THE ACQUISITION OF RIGHTS IN A
16 CONTROLLABLE ACCOUNT OR CONTROLLABLE PAYMENT INTANGIBLE, INCLUDING
17 THE RIGHTS AND BENEFITS PROVIDED UNDER SUBSECTIONS (C) THROUGH (E), (G),
18 AND (H) OF THIS SECTION OF A PURCHASER AND QUALIFYING PURCHASER, IN THE
19 SAME MANNER THIS SECTION APPLIES TO A CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD.
20 (B) TO DETERMINE WHETHER A PURCHASER OF A CONTROLLABLE
21 ACCOUNT OR A CONTROLLABLE PAYMENT INTANGIBLE IS A QUALIFYING
22 PURCHASER, THE PURCHASER OBTAINS CONTROL OF THE ACCOUNT OR PAYMENT
23 INTANGIBLE IF THE PURCHASER OBTAINS CONTROL OF THE CONTROLLABLE
24 ELECTRONIC RECORD THAT EVIDENCES THE ACCOUNT OR PAYMENT INTANGIBLE.
25 (C) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN THIS SECTION, A DETERMINATION ABOUT
26 WHETHER A PERSON ACQUIRES A RIGHT IN A CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD
27 AND THE TYPE OF RIGHT THE PERSON ACQUIRES CAN BE MADE USING APPLICABLE
28 STATE OR FEDERAL LAW.
29 (D) (1) A PURCHASER OF A CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD
30 ACQUIRES ALL RIGHTS IN THE RECORD THAT THE TRANSFEROR HAD THE
31 AUTHORITY TO TRANSFER.
SENATE BILL 154 5
1 (2) NOTWITHSTANDING PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION, A
2 PURCHASER OF A LIMITED INTEREST IN A CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD
3 SHALL ACQUIRE RIGHTS ONLY TO THE EXTENT OF THE INTEREST PURCHASED.
4 (E) A QUALIFYING PURCHASER ACQUIRES RIGHTS IN THE CONTROLLABLE
5 ELECTRONIC RECORD FREE OF A CLAIM OF A PROPERTY RIGHT IN THE
6 CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD.
7 (F) EXCEPT AS OTHERWISE PROVIDED IN A LAW NOT INCLUDED UNDER
8 THIS ARTICLE AND SUBSECTIONS (A) AND (E) OF THIS SECTION WITH RESPECT TO A
9 CONTROLLABLE ACCOUNT AND A CONTROLLABLE PAYMENT INTANGIBLE, A
10 QUALIFYING PURCHASER TAKES A RIGHT TO PAYMENT, RIGHT TO PERFORMANCE,
11 OR OTHER INTEREST IN PROPERTY EVIDENCED BY THE CONTROLLABLE
12 ELECTRONIC RECORD SUBJECT TO A CLAIM OF A PROPERTY RIGHT IN THE RIGHT TO
13 PAYMENT, RIGHT TO PERFORMANCE, OR OTHER INTEREST IN PROPERTY.
14 (G) AN ACTION MAY NOT BE ASSERTED AGAINST A QUALIFYING PURCHASER
15 BASED ON BOTH A PURCHASE BY THE QUALIFYING PURCHASER OF A
16 CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD AND A CLAIM OF PROPERTY RIGHT IN
17 ANOTHER CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD, WHETHER THE ACTION IS
18 FRAMED IN CONVERSION, REPLEVIN, CONSTRUCTIVE TRUST, EQUITABLE LIEN, OR
19 OTHER THEORY.
20 (H) THE FILING OF A FINANCING STATEMENT UNDER TITLE 9 OF THIS
21 ARTICLE IS NOT NOTICE OF A CLAIM OF A PROPERTY RIGHT IN A CONTROLLABLE
22 ELECTRONIC RECORD.
23 10A–105.
24 (A) A PERSON HAS CONTROL OF A CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD IF
25 THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD, A RECORD ATTACHED TO OR
26 LOGICALLY ASSOCIATED WITH THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD, OR A
27 SYSTEM IN WHICH THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD IS RECORDED:
28 (1) GIVES THE PERSON:
29 (I) THE AUTHORITY TO AVAIL THE PERSON OF SUBSTANTIALLY
30 ALL THE BENEFIT FROM THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD; AND
31 (II) SUBJECT TO SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION, EXCLUSIVE
32 POWER TO:
6 SENATE BILL 154
1 1. PREVENT OTHERS FROM AVAILING THEMSELVES OF
2 SUBSTANTIALLY ALL OF THE BENEFIT FROM THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC
3 RECORD; AND
4 2. TRANSFER CONTROL OF THE CONTROLLABLE
5 ELECTRONIC RECORD TO ANOTHER PERSON OR CAUSE ANOTHER PERSON TO
6 OBTAIN CONTROL OF ANOTHER CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD AS A RESULT
7 OF THE TRANSFER OF THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD; AND
8 (2) ENABLES THE PERSON READILY TO IDENTIFY THE PERSON IN ANY
9 WAY, INCLUDING BY NAME, IDENTIFYING NUMBER, CRYPTOGRAPHIC KEY, OFFICE,
10 OR ACCOUNT NUMBER AS HAVING THE POWERS SPECIFIED UNDER ITEM (1) OF THIS
11 SUBSECTION.
12 (B) SUBJECT TO SUBSECTION (C) OF THIS SECTION, POWER IS EXCLUSIVE
13 UNDER SUBSECTION (A)(1)(II) OF THIS SECTION, EVEN IF:
14 (1) THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD, A RECORD
15 ATTACHED TO OR LOGICALLY ASSOCIATED WITH THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC
16 RECORD, OR A SYSTEM IN WHICH THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD IS
17 RECORDED LIMITS THE USE OF THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD OR HAS
18 A PROTOCOL PROGRAMMED TO CAUSE A CHANGE, INCLUDING A TRANSFER OR LOSS
19 OF CONTROL OR A MODIFICATION OF BENEFITS AFFORDED BY THE CONTROLLABLE
20 ELECTRONIC RECORD; OR
21 (2) THE POWER IS SHARED WITH ANOTHER PERSON.
22 (C) THE POWER OF A PERSON IS NOT SHARED WITH ANOTHER PERSON
23 UNDER SUBSECTION (B)(2) OF THIS SECTION AND THE PERSON’S POWER IS NOT
24 EXCLUSIVE IF:
25 (1) THE PERSON CAN EXERCISE THE POWER ONLY IF THE POWER IS
26 ALSO EXERCISED BY THE OTHER PERSON; AND
27 (2) THE OTHER PERSON:
28 (I) CAN EXERCISE THE POWER WITHOUT EXERCISE OF THE
29 POWER BY THE PERSON; OR
30 (II) IS THE TRANSFEROR TO THE PERSON OF AN INTEREST IN
31 THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD OR A CONTROLLABLE ACCOUNT OR
32 CONTROLLABLE PAYMENT INTANGIBLE EVIDENCED BY THE CONTROLLABLE
33 ELECTRONIC RECORD.

1 (D) IF A PERSON HAS THE POWER SPECIFIED IN SUBSECTION (A)(1)(II) OF
2 THIS SECTION, THE POWERS ARE PRESUMED TO BE EXCLUSIVE.
3 (E) A PERSON HAS CONTROL OF A CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD IF
4 ANOTHER PERSON, OTHER THAN THE TRANSFEROR TO THE PERSON OF AN
5 INTEREST IN THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD OR A CONTROLLABLE
6 ACCOUNT OR CONTROLLABLE PAYMENT INTANGIBLE EVIDENCED BY THE
7 CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD:
8 (1) HAS CONTROL OF THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD
9 AND ACKNOWLEDGES THAT THE PERSON HAS CONTROL ON BEHALF OF THE PERSON;
10 OR
11 (2) OBTAINS CONTROL OF THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC
12 RECORD AFTER HAVING ACKNOWLEDGED THAT THE PERSON WILL OBTAIN CONTROL
13 OF THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD ON BEHALF OF THE PERSON.
14 (F) A PERSON THAT HAS CONTROL UNDER THIS SECTION IS NOT REQUIRED
15 TO ACKNOWLEDGE THAT THE PERSON HAS CONTROL ON BEHALF OF ANOTHER
16 PERSON.
17 (G) IF A PERSON ACKNOWLEDGES THAT THE PERSON HAS, OR WILL OBTAIN,
18 CONTROL ON BEHALF OF ANOTHER PERSON, UNLESS THE PERSON OTHERWISE
19 AGREES, OR A LAW OTHER THAN IN THIS TITLE OR TITLE 9 OF THIS ARTICLE
20 OTHERWISE PROVIDES, THE PERSON DOES NOT OWE A DUTY TO THE OTHER PERSON
21 AND IS NOT REQUIRED TO CONFIRM THE ACKNOWLEDGMENT TO ANY OTHER
22 PERSON.
23 10A–106.
24 (A) AN ACCOUNT DEBTOR ON A CONTROLLABLE ACCOUNT OR
25 CONTROLLABLE PAYMENT INTANGIBLE MAY DISCHARGE THE ACCOUNT DEBTOR’S
26 OBLIGATION BY PAYING:
27 (1) THE PERSON HAVING CONTROL OF THE CONTROLLABLE
28 ELECTRONIC RECORD THAT EVIDENCES THE CONTROLLABLE ACCOUNT OR
29 CONTROLLABLE PAYMENT INTANGIBLE; OR
30 (2) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION, A
31 PERSON THAT FORMERLY HAD CONTROL OF THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC
32 RECORD.

1 (B) SUBJECT TO SUBSECTION (D) OF THIS SECTION, THE ACCOUNT DEBTOR
2 MAY NOT DISCHARGE THE ACCOUNT DEBTOR’S OBLIGATION BY PAYING THE PERSON
3 THAT FORMERLY HAD CONTROL OF THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD IF
4 THE ACCOUNT DEBTOR RECEIVES A NOTIFICATION THAT:
5 (1) IS SIGNED BY A PERSON THAT FORMERLY HAD CONTROL OR THE
6 PERSON TO WHOM CONTROL WAS TRANSFERRED;
7 (2) REASONABLY IDENTIFIES THE CONTROLLABLE ACCOUNT OR
8 CONTROLLABLE PAYMENT INTANGIBLE;
9 (3) NOTIFIES THE ACCOUNT DEBTOR THAT CONTROL OF THE
10 CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD THAT EVIDENCES THE CONTROLLABLE
11 ACCOUNT OR CONTROLLABLE PAYMENT INTANGIBLE WAS TRANSFERRED;
12 (4) IDENTIFIES THE TRANSFEREE IN ANY REASONABLE WAY,
13 INCLUDING BY NAME, IDENTIFYING NUMBER, CRYPTOGRAPHIC KEY, OFFICE, OR
14 ACCOUNT NUMBER; AND
15 (5) PROVIDES A COMMERCIALLY REASONABLE METHOD BY WHICH
16 THE ACCOUNT DEBTOR IS TO PAY THE TRANSFEREE.
17 (C) AFTER RECEIPT OF A NOTIFICATION THAT COMPLIES WITH SUBSECTION
18 (B) OF THIS SECTION, THE ACCOUNT DEBTOR:
19 (1) MAY DISCHARGE THE ACCOUNT DEBTOR’S OBLIGATION BY
20 PAYING ACCORDING TO INSTRUCTIONS IN THE NOTIFICATION; AND
21 (2) MAY NOT DISCHARGE THE OBLIGATION BY PAYING A PERSON
22 THAT FORMERLY HAD CONTROL.
23 (D) SUBJECT TO SUBSECTION (H) OF THIS SECTION, NOTIFICATION IS
24 INEFFECTIVE UNDER SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION:
25 (1) UNLESS, BEFORE NOTIFICATION IS SENT, THE ACCOUNT DEBTOR
26 AND THE PERSON THAT AT THAT TIME HAD CONTROL OF THE CONTROLLABLE
27 ELECTRONIC RECORD THAT EVIDENCES THE CONTROLLABLE ACCOUNT OR
28 CONTROLLABLE PAYMENT INTANGIBLE AGREE IN A SIGNED RECORD TO A
29 COMMERCIALLY REASONABLE METHOD BY WHICH A PERSON MAY FURNISH
30 REASONABLE PROOF THAT CONTROL HAS BEEN TRANSFERRED;
31 (2) TO THE EXTENT AN AGREEMENT BETWEEN THE ACCOUNT DEBTOR
32 AND SELLER OF A PAYMENT INTANGIBLE LIMITS THE ACCOUNT DEBTOR’S DUTY TO

5 (I) DIVIDE A PAYMENT;

8 10A–107.
9 (A) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION, THE LOCAL
10 LAW OF THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD’S JURISDICTION GOVERNS A
11 MATTER COVERED BY THIS TITLE.
12 (B) FOR A CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD THAT EVIDENCES A
13 CONTROLLABLE ACCOUNT OR CONTROLLABLE PAYMENT INTANGIBLE, THE LOCAL
14 LAW OF THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD’S JURISDICTION GOVERNS A
15 MATTER COVERED BY § 10A–106 OF THIS TITLE UNLESS AN EFFECTIVE AGREEMENT
16 DETERMINES THAT THE LOCAL LAW OF ANOTHER JURISDICTION GOVERNS.
17 (C) THE FOLLOWING RULES DETERMINE A CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC
18 RECORD’S JURISDICTION UNDER THIS SECTION:
19 (1) IF THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD, OR A RECORD
20 ATTACHED TO OR LOGICALLY ASSOCIATED WITH THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC
21 RECORD THAT IS READILY AVAILABLE FOR REVIEW, EXPRESSLY PROVIDES THAT A
22 PARTICULAR JURISDICTION IS THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD’S
23 JURISDICTION, THEN FOR PURPOSES OF THIS TITLE, THAT JURISDICTION IS THE
24 CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD’S JURISDICTION;
25 (2) IF ITEM (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION DOES NOT APPLY AND THE
26 RULES OF THE SYSTEM IN WHICH THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD IS
27 RECORDED ARE READILY AVAILABLE FOR REVIEW AND EXPRESSLY PROVIDE THAT
28 A PARTICULAR JURISDICTION IS THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD’S
29 JURISDICTION, THEN FOR PURPOSES OF THIS TITLE, THAT JURISDICTION IS THE
30 CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD’S JURISDICTION;
31 (3) IF ITEMS (1) AND (2) OF THIS SUBSECTION DO NOT APPLY AND THE
32 CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD, OR A RECORD ATTACHED TO OR LOGICALLY
33 ASSOCIATED WITH THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD THAT IS READILY
SENATE BILL 154 11
1 AVAILABLE FOR REVIEW, EXPRESSLY PROVIDES THAT THE CONTROLLABLE
2 ELECTRONIC RECORD IS GOVERNED BY THE LAW OF A PARTICULAR JURISDICTION,
3 THEN THAT JURISDICTION IS THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD’S
4 JURISDICTION; AND
5 (4) IF ITEMS (1) THROUGH (3) OF THIS SUBSECTION DO NOT APPLY
6 AND THE RULES OF THE SYSTEM IN WHICH THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC
7 RECORD IS RECORDED ARE READILY AVAILABLE FOR REVIEW AND EXPRESSLY
8 PROVIDE THAT THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD OR SYSTEM IS
9 GOVERNED BY THE LAW OF A PARTICULAR JURISDICTION, THEN THAT
10 JURISDICTION IS THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD’S JURISDICTION.
11 (D) TO THE EXTENT SUBSECTIONS (A) AND (B) OF THIS SECTION PROVIDE
12 THAT THE LOCAL LAW OF THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD’S
13 JURISDICTION GOVERNS A MATTER COVERED BY THIS TITLE, THAT LAW GOVERNS
14 EVEN IF THE MATTER OR A TRANSACTION TO WHICH THE MATTER RELATES DOES
15 NOT BEAR ANY RELATION TO THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD’S
16 JURISDICTION.
17 (E) THE RIGHTS ACQUIRED UNDER § 10A–104 OF THIS TITLE BY A
18 PURCHASER OR QUALIFYING PURCHASER ARE GOVERNED BY THE LAW APPLICABLE
19 UNDER THIS SECTION AT THE TIME OF PURCHASE.

CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Commercial Law – Uniform Commercial Code – Controllable Electronic Records
3 FOR the purpose of adding Article 12 of the Uniform Commercial Code to the Maryland
4 Uniform Commercial Code to establish provisions governing the transfer of property
5 rights in certain controllable electronic records, controllable accounts, and
6 controllable payment intangibles; and generally relating to property rights under the
7 Maryland Uniform Commercial Code.
8 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
9 Article – Commercial Law
10 Section 1–101
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2013 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
13 BY adding to
14 Article – Commercial Law
15 Section 10A–101 through 10A–107 to be under the new title “Title 10A. Controllable
16 Electronic Records”
17 Annotated Code of Maryland
18 (2013 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
19 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
20 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

1 Article – Commercial Law
2 1–101.
3 (a) Titles 1 through [10] 10A of this article may be cited as the Maryland Uniform

5 (b) This title may be cited as Maryland Uniform Commercial Code – General
6 Provisions.
7 TITLE 10A. CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORDS.
8 10A–101.
9 THIS TITLE MAY BE CITED AS THE MARYLAND UNIFORM COMMERCIAL
10 CODE – CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORDS.
11 10A–102.
12 (A) IN THIS TITLE THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
13 INDICATED.
14 (B) (1) “ACCOUNT DEBTOR” MEANS A PERSON OBLIGATED ON AN
15 ACCOUNT, A CHATTEL PAPER, OR A GENERAL INTANGIBLE.
16 (2) “ACCOUNT DEBTOR” DOES NOT INCLUDE A PERSON OBLIGATED
17 TO PAY A NEGOTIABLE INSTRUMENT, EVEN IF THE NEGOTIABLE INSTRUMENT
18 EVIDENCES CHATTEL PAPER.
19 (C) (1) “CHATTEL PAPER” MEANS:
20 (I) A RIGHT TO PAYMENT OF A MONETARY OBLIGATION
21 SECURED BY SPECIFIC GOODS, IF THE RIGHT TO PAYMENT AND SECURITY
22 AGREEMENT ARE EVIDENCED BY A RECORD; OR
23 (II) A RIGHT TO PAYMENT OF A MONETARY OBLIGATION OWED
24 BY A LESSEE UNDER A LEASE AGREEMENT WITH RESPECT TO SPECIFIC GOODS AND
25 A MONETARY OBLIGATION OWED BY THE LESSEE IN CONNECTION WITH THE
26 TRANSACTION GIVING RISE TO THE LEASE, IF:
27 1. THE RIGHT TO PAYMENT AND THE LEASE AGREEMENT
28 ARE EVIDENCED BY A RECORD; AND

SENATE BILL 154 3
1 2. THE PREDOMINANT PURPOSE OF THE TRANSACTION
2 GIVING RISE TO THE LEASE WAS TO GIVE THE LESSEE THE RIGHT TO POSSESSION
3 AND USE OF THE GOODS.
4 (2) “CHATTEL PAPER” DOES NOT INCLUDE A RIGHT TO A PAYMENT
5 ARISING OUT OF A CHARTER OR OTHER CONTRACT INVOLVING THE USE OR HIRE OF
6 A VESSEL OR A RIGHT TO PAYMENT ARISING OUT OF THE USE OF A CREDIT OR
7 CHARGE CARD OR INFORMATION CONTAINED ON OR FOR USE WITH THE CARD.
8 (D) “CONTROLLABLE ACCOUNT” MEANS AN ACCOUNT EVIDENCED BY A
9 CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD THAT PROVIDES THAT THE ACCOUNT
10 DEBTOR UNDERTAKES TO PAY THE PERSON THAT HAS CONTROL OF THE
11 CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD IN ACCORDANCE WITH § 10A–105 OF THIS
12 TITLE.
13 (E) (1) “CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD” MEANS A RECORD
14 STORED IN AN ELECTRONIC MEDIUM THAT CAN BE SUBJECTED TO CONTROL UNDER
15 § 10A–105 OF THIS TITLE.
16 (2) “CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD” DOES NOT INCLUDE A
17 CONTROLLABLE ACCOUNT, A CONTROLLABLE PAYMENT INTANGIBLE, A DEPOSIT
18 ACCOUNT, AN ELECTRONIC COPY OF A RECORD EVIDENCING CHATTEL PAPER, AN
19 ELECTRONIC DOCUMENT OF TITLE, ELECTRONIC MONEY, INVESTMENT PROPERTY,
20 OR A TRANSFERABLE RECORD.
21 (F) “CONTROLLABLE PAYMENT INTANGIBLE” MEANS A PAYMENT
22 INTANGIBLE EVIDENCED BY A CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD THAT
23 PROVIDES THAT THE ACCOUNT DEBTOR UNDERTAKES TO PAY THE PERSON THAT
24 HAS CONTROL OF THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD IN ACCORDANCE WITH
25 § 10A–105 OF THIS TITLE.
26 (G) “DEPOSIT ACCOUNT” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 9–102 OF THIS
27 ARTICLE.
28 (H) “ELECTRONIC MONEY” MEANS MONEY IN AN ELECTRONIC FORM.
29 (I) “INVESTMENT PROPERTY” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 9–102 OF
30 THIS ARTICLE.
31 (J) “QUALIFYING PURCHASER” MEANS A PURCHASER OF A CONTROLLABLE
32 ELECTRONIC RECORD OR AN INTEREST IN A CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD
33 THAT OBTAINS CONTROL OF THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD FOR VALUE,

1 IN GOOD FAITH, AND WITHOUT NOTICE OF A CLAIM OF PROPERTY RIGHT IN THE
2 CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD.
3 (K) “TRANSFERABLE RECORDS” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 21–115 OF
4 THIS ARTICLE.
5 (L) “VALUE” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 3–303 OF THIS ARTICLE BUT
6 WITH RESPECT TO A CONTROLLABLE ACCOUNT, CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC
7 RECORD, OR CONTROLLABLE PAYMENT INTANGIBLE, RATHER THAN AN
8 INSTRUMENT.
9 10A–103.
10 (A) IF THERE IS A CONFLICT BETWEEN THIS TITLE AND TITLE 9 OF THIS
11 ARTICLE, TITLE 9 OF THIS ARTICLE GOVERNS.
12 (B) A TRANSACTION SUBJECT TO THIS TITLE IS ALSO SUBJECT TO ANY
13 OTHER APPLICABLE STATE LAW.
14 10A–104.
15 (A) THIS SECTION APPLIES TO THE ACQUISITION OF RIGHTS IN A
16 CONTROLLABLE ACCOUNT OR CONTROLLABLE PAYMENT INTANGIBLE, INCLUDING
17 THE RIGHTS AND BENEFITS PROVIDED UNDER SUBSECTIONS (C) THROUGH (E), (G),
18 AND (H) OF THIS SECTION OF A PURCHASER AND QUALIFYING PURCHASER, IN THE
19 SAME MANNER THIS SECTION APPLIES TO A CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD.
20 (B) TO DETERMINE WHETHER A PURCHASER OF A CONTROLLABLE
21 ACCOUNT OR A CONTROLLABLE PAYMENT INTANGIBLE IS A QUALIFYING
22 PURCHASER, THE PURCHASER OBTAINS CONTROL OF THE ACCOUNT OR PAYMENT
23 INTANGIBLE IF THE PURCHASER OBTAINS CONTROL OF THE CONTROLLABLE
24 ELECTRONIC RECORD THAT EVIDENCES THE ACCOUNT OR PAYMENT INTANGIBLE.
25 (C) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN THIS SECTION, A DETERMINATION ABOUT
26 WHETHER A PERSON ACQUIRES A RIGHT IN A CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD
27 AND THE TYPE OF RIGHT THE PERSON ACQUIRES CAN BE MADE USING APPLICABLE
28 STATE OR FEDERAL LAW.
29 (D) (1) A PURCHASER OF A CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD
30 ACQUIRES ALL RIGHTS IN THE RECORD THAT THE TRANSFEROR HAD THE
31 AUTHORITY TO TRANSFER.

1 (2) NOTWITHSTANDING PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION, A
2 PURCHASER OF A LIMITED INTEREST IN A CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD
3 SHALL ACQUIRE RIGHTS ONLY TO THE EXTENT OF THE INTEREST PURCHASED.
4 (E) A QUALIFYING PURCHASER ACQUIRES RIGHTS IN THE CONTROLLABLE
5 ELECTRONIC RECORD FREE OF A CLAIM OF A PROPERTY RIGHT IN THE
6 CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD.
7 (F) EXCEPT AS OTHERWISE PROVIDED IN A LAW NOT INCLUDED UNDER
8 THIS ARTICLE AND SUBSECTIONS (A) AND (E) OF THIS SECTION WITH RESPECT TO A
9 CONTROLLABLE ACCOUNT AND A CONTROLLABLE PAYMENT INTANGIBLE, A

11 OR OTHER INTEREST IN PROPERTY EVIDENCED BY THE CONTROLLABLE
12 ELECTRONIC RECORD SUBJECT TO A CLAIM OF A PROPERTY RIGHT IN THE RIGHT TO
13 PAYMENT, RIGHT TO PERFORMANCE, OR OTHER INTEREST IN PROPERTY.
14 (G) AN ACTION MAY NOT BE ASSERTED AGAINST A QUALIFYING PURCHASER
15 BASED ON BOTH A PURCHASE BY THE QUALIFYING PURCHASER OF A
16 CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD AND A CLAIM OF PROPERTY RIGHT IN
17 ANOTHER CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD, WHETHER THE ACTION IS
18 FRAMED IN CONVERSION, REPLEVIN, CONSTRUCTIVE TRUST, EQUITABLE LIEN, OR
19 OTHER THEORY.
20 (H) THE FILING OF A FINANCING STATEMENT UNDER TITLE 9 OF THIS
21 ARTICLE IS NOT NOTICE OF A CLAIM OF A PROPERTY RIGHT IN A CONTROLLABLE
22 ELECTRONIC RECORD.
23 10A–105.
24 (A) A PERSON HAS CONTROL OF A CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD IF
25 THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD, A RECORD ATTACHED TO OR
26 LOGICALLY ASSOCIATED WITH THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD, OR A
27 SYSTEM IN WHICH THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD IS RECORDED:
28 (1) GIVES THE PERSON:
29 (I) THE AUTHORITY TO AVAIL THE PERSON OF SUBSTANTIALLY
30 ALL THE BENEFIT FROM THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD; AND
31 (II) SUBJECT TO SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION, EXCLUSIVE
32 POWER TO:

1 1. PREVENT OTHERS FROM AVAILING THEMSELVES OF
2 SUBSTANTIALLY ALL OF THE BENEFIT FROM THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC
3 RECORD; AND

5 ELECTRONIC RECORD TO ANOTHER PERSON OR CAUSE ANOTHER PERSON TO
6 OBTAIN CONTROL OF ANOTHER CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD AS A RESULT
7 OF THE TRANSFER OF THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD; AND
8 (2) ENABLES THE PERSON READILY TO IDENTIFY THE PERSON IN ANY
9 WAY, INCLUDING BY NAME, IDENTIFYING NUMBER, CRYPTOGRAPHIC KEY, OFFICE,

14 (1) THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD, A RECORD

16 RECORD, OR A SYSTEM IN WHICH THE CONTROLLABLE ELECTRONIC RECORD IS

18 A PROTOCOL PROGRAMMED TO CAUSE A CHANGE, INCLUDING A TRANSFER OR LOSS
19 OF CONTROL OR A MODIFICATION OF BENEFITS AFFORDED BY THE CONTROLLABLE
20 ELECTRONIC RECORD; OR
21 (2) THE POWER IS SHARED WITH ANOTHER PERSON.
22 (C) THE POWER OF A PERSON IS NOT SHARED WITH ANOTHER PERSON
23 UNDER SUBSECTION (B)(2) OF THIS SECTION AND THE PERSON’S POWER IS NOT

25 (1) THE PERSON CAN EXERCISE THE POWER ONLY IF THE POWER IS
