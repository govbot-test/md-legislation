1 Article – Health – General
2 19–310.1.
3 (a) (1) This section applies to a nursing facility, as defined in § 19–301 of this
4 subtitle, that:
5 (i) Has 45 or more beds; and
6 (ii) Operates in the State.
7 (2) This section does not apply to a nursing home bed in a continuing care
8 retirement community that has obtained a certificate of registration to provide continuing
9 care under Title 10, Subtitle 4 of the Human Services Article.
10 (b) (1) The Department may impose a quality assessment on each freestanding
11 nursing facility subject to this section.
12 (2) The amount assessed in the aggregate on all nursing facilities may not
13 exceed 6.0% of the operating revenue for all nursing facilities subject to this section for the
14 previous fiscal quarter.
15 (3) The assessment authorized by this section shall be paid by each nursing
16 facility in accordance with this section.
17 (c) (1) On or before the 60th day after each quarter of the State fiscal year,
18 each nursing facility subject to this section shall pay to the Comptroller an amount
19 determined by the Department based on an amount per non–Medicare day of service for
20 the previous fiscal quarter.
21 (2) The assessment shall be based on an amount per patient day, not
22 including Medicare days.
23 (d) (1) [All] EXCEPT AS PROVIDED UNDER PARAGRAPH (3) OF THIS
24 SUBSECTION, ALL amounts collected by the State Comptroller under this section shall be
25 distributed to a special fund, to be used by the Department only to fund reimbursements to
26 nursing facilities under the Medicaid program.
27 (2) At least 65% of the funds allocated by the Department as
28 reimbursements to nursing facilities under this section shall be in addition to and may not
29 supplant funds already appropriated for this purpose.
30 (3) (I) IN FISCAL YEAR 2027 AND EACH FISCAL YEAR THEREAFTER,
31 THE GOVERNOR SHALL INCLUDE IN THE ANNUAL BUDGET BILL AT LEAST 3% OF THE
32 FUNDS COLLECTED BY THE DEPARTMENT UNDER THIS SECTION TO FUND THE

18 Article – Human Services
19 10–903.
20 (a) There is an Office of the Long–Term Care Ombudsman in the Department.
21 (b) (1) Unless the Secretary is subject to a conflict of interest that cannot be
22 removed, the Secretary shall select a State Long–Term Care Ombudsman.
23 (2) If the Secretary is subject to a conflict of interest that cannot be
24 removed, the Secretary shall designate another individual, who is not subject to a conflict
25 of interest, to select the State Long–Term Care Ombudsman.
26 (c) The State Long–Term Care Ombudsman:
27 (1) shall personally administer the Office;
28 (2) shall have expertise and experience in the field of long–term care
29 advocacy;
30 (3) may not have any conflict of interest with the position; and

1 (4) shall, on a full–time basis, perform duties related to the Program.
2 (d) The State Long–Term Care Ombudsman shall personally or through
3 designated ombudsmen:
4 (1) identify, investigate, and resolve complaints from any source made by,
5 or on behalf of, a resident relating to any action, inaction, or decision, that may adversely
6 affect the health, safety, welfare, or rights of a resident including the welfare and rights of
7 residents with respect to the appointment and activities of guardians and representative
8 payees, of:
9 (i) a provider, or a representative of a provider, of long–term care
10 services;
11 (ii) a public agency; or
12 (iii) a health or social service agency;
13 (2) represent the interests of residents before governmental agencies and
14 seek administrative, legal, and other remedies to protect the health, safety, welfare, and
15 rights of residents;
16 (3) provide information as appropriate to other agencies and the public
17 regarding the problems and concerns of residents;
18 (4) inform residents, family members, and others acting on behalf of
19 residents about how to access the assistance and services of the Office and the services and
20 assistance of other providers or agencies, including legal services;
21 (5) ensure regular and timely access to and response from the Office;
22 (6) comment on, facilitate public comment on, and recommend changes to
23 existing or proposed laws, rules, regulations, and other governmental policies and actions
24 that affect the health, safety, welfare, and rights of residents;
25 (7) provide technical support for the development of resident and family
26 councils to protect the well–being and rights of residents;
27 (8) provide for the education and training of ombudsmen;
28 (9) educate facilities, agencies, and staff members concerning the rights
29 and welfare of residents;
30 (10) promote the development of citizen organizations to advocate for the
31 well–being and rights of residents;
