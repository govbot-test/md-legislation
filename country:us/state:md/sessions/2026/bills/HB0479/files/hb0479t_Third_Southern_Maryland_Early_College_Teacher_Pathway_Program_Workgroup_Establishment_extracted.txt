10 the Maryland Higher Education Commission, the certain recommendations for a
11 Southern Maryland Early College Teacher Pathway Program and the a teacher
12 preparation pathway in a certain manner; establishing the Southern Maryland
13 Early College Teacher Pathway Program Workgroup; and generally relating to the
14 Southern Maryland Early College Teacher Pathway Program and the Southern
15 Maryland Early College Teacher Pathway Program Workgroup.

11 Preamble
12 WHEREAS, Maryland faces a critical shortage of qualified teachers, especially in
13 high–need schools and rural areas such as Southern Maryland where local school systems
14 report persistent vacancies in core subject areas and growing challenges in retaining
15 educators; and
16 WHEREAS, The Blueprint for Maryland’s Future calls for expanding high–quality
17 pathways into the teaching profession, increasing college readiness, and improving
18 workforce pipelines in every region of the State; and
19 WHEREAS, Nationally, early college programs have proven to increase college
20 enrollment, degree attainment, and workforce participation — particularly among
21 first–generation and low–income students — and can be leveraged to build stronger and
22 more diverse teacher pipelines; and
23 WHEREAS, Despite strong national evidence of success, Maryland lags behind most
24 states in providing early postsecondary opportunities for its students; and
25 WHEREAS, As of 2023, Maryland ranked low nationally for dual enrollment
26 participation as a percent of high school enrollment at 10% and ranked low nationally for
27 dual enrollment as a percent of undergraduate enrollment at 7%; and
28 WHEREAS, As of 2022, participation in dual enrollment and early college programs
29 in Maryland is overly concentrated at community colleges with only 5% of students enrolled
30 through public four–year institutions — compared to approximately 28% nationally —
31 limiting exposure to bachelor’s degree pathways, including teacher preparation programs;
32 and
33 WHEREAS, Southern Maryland’s geography, limited access to nearby four–year
34 institutions, and workforce needs make it an ideal region for a program that bridges high
35 schools, community colleges, and the University System of Maryland regional higher
36 education centers to create a seamless teacher preparation pathway; and
