WES MOORE, Governor Ch. 369
Chapter 369
(House Bill 1372)
AN ACT concerning
Public Health – Office of Health Care Quality Information and Maryland
Department of Health Centralization Commission
FOR the purpose of requiring the Maryland Health Care Commission, in collaboration with
the Office of Health Care Quality within the Maryland Department of Health, to
develop a process to receive and publish certain inspection information on a certain
website; establishing the Maryland Department of Health Centralization
Commission to provide certain advice and recommendations relating to the
relationship between the Maryland Department of Health and the health
occupations boards; requiring the Secretary of Information Technology to ensure
that minimum cybersecurity requirements are met by all health occupations boards
each health occupations board to annually report certain information to the
Department of Information Technology; and generally relating to the sharing of
inspection information and the oversight of health occupations boards in the State.

Article – Health Occupations
Subtitle 1. Definitions AND POLICY.
1–102.
(a) It is the policy of the State that health occupations should be regulated and
controlled as provided in this article to protect the health, safety, and welfare of the public.
(b) The health occupations boards established by this article, the majority of
whose members are licensed or certified under this article, are created to function as
independent boards, with the intent that a peer group is best qualified to regulate, control,
and otherwise discipline in a fair and unbiased manner the licensees or certificate holders
who practice in the State.
1–103.
(A) IN THIS SECTION, “COMMISSION” MEANS THE MARYLAND
DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH CENTRALIZATION COMMISSION.
(B) THERE IS A MARYLAND DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH CENTRALIZATION
COMMISSION.
(C) THE PURPOSE OF THE COMMISSION IS TO PROVIDE ONGOING ADVICE
AND RECOMMENDATIONS TO THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY ON HOW BEST TO IMPROVE
THE RELATIONSHIP INCREASE COLLABORATION BETWEEN THE DEPARTMENT AND
THE HEALTH OCCUPATIONS BOARDS TO ACHIEVE GREATER EFFICIENCY,
TRANSPARENCY, AND ACCOUNTABILITY.
(D) THE COMMISSION CONSISTS OF THE FOLLOWING MEMBERS:
(1) ONE MEMBER OF THE SENATE OF MARYLAND, APPOINTED BY THE
PRESIDENT OF THE SENATE;
(2) ONE MEMBER OF THE HOUSE OF DELEGATES, APPOINTED BY THE
SPEAKER OF THE HOUSE;
(3) THE SECRETARY, OR THE SECRETARY’S DESIGNEE;
(4) THE DEPUTY SECRETARY FOR PUBLIC HEALTH SERVICES;
