HOUSE BILL 1364
J3, J1 6lr0639
By: Delegate Pena–Melnyk
Introduced and read first time: February 13, 2026
Assigned to: Health
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Public Health – Mammograms – Arterial Calcification Notice
3 FOR the purpose of requiring certain centers where mammography testing is performed to
4 include in the screening results letter that is sent to a patient, as required by federal
5 law, a notice of the presence of arterial calcification as observed during the patient’s
6 mammogram; and generally relating to mammography testing.
7 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
8 Article – Health – General
9 Section 20–115
10 Annotated Code of Maryland
11 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
12 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
13 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
14 Article – Health – General
15 20–115.
16 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
17 (2) (i) “Center” means any facility that produces, develops, or
18 interprets:
19 1. Screening mammograms; or
20 2. Diagnostic mammograms.

12 (5) “Mammography testing” means the imaging of the breast with ionizing

14 (b) On or after July 1, 1992, a person may not perform mammography testing
15 unless:
16 (1) The individual performing mammography testing is qualified under
17 Title 14 of the Health Occupations Article; and
18 (2) The center where the mammography testing is performed:
19 (i) Is accredited or has applied for accreditation under the American
20 College of Radiology Screening Mammography Accreditation Program; and
21 (ii) Has obtained a certificate of approval from the federal Food and
22 Drug Administration as specified in the federal Mammography Quality Standards Act of
23 1992.
24 (c) (1) (i) This section does not apply if the federal Mammography Quality
25 Standards Act of 1992, or regulations adopted under the Act, requires a notice regarding
26 breast density to be included in the screening results letter that is sent to a patient.
27 (ii) This section may not be construed to:
28 1. Require a notice regarding breast density to be sent to a
29 patient that is inconsistent with the provisions of the federal Mammography Quality
30 Standards Act of 1992, or regulations adopted under the Act; or
31 2. Create a standard of care, obligation, or duty that provides
32 a basis for a cause of action.

1 (2) Subject to paragraph (3) of this subsection, a center where

4 results of your recent mammogram, including information about breast density.
5 If your mammogram shows that your breast tissue is dense, you should know that
6 dense breast tissue is a common finding and is not abnormal, with about half of women
7 having dense or highly dense breasts. However, dense breast tissue can make it harder to
8 find cancer on a mammogram and may also be associated with an increased risk of cancer.
