2 Adult Education – High School Diploma Pathways – GED Option Pilot Program
3 and MDiplomaWorks Pathway
4 FOR the purpose of establishing the GED Option Pilot Program; requiring the Maryland
5 Department of Labor, in consultation with certain entities, to establish criteria to
6 select certain local school systems to participate in the Pilot Program; establishing
7 qualifications for certain students selected to participate in the Pilot Program;
8 establishing the MDiplomaWorks pathway as an alternative method of earning a

24 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That the Laws of Maryland read
25 as follows:
26 Article – Labor and Employment
27 11–810.
28 (A) IN THIS SECTION, “MDIPLOMAWORKS PATHWAY” MEANS AN
29 ALTERNATIVE PATHWAY TO EARN A HIGH SCHOOL DIPLOMA IN THE STATE.

SENATE BILL 70 5
1 (B) THE MDIPLOMAWORKS PATHWAY IS A METHOD TO RECOGNIZE AND
2 CERTIFY HIGH SCHOOL DIPLOMA ACHIEVEMENT THROUGH DEMONSTRATED
3 ACADEMIC AND CAREER COMPETENCIES, INCLUDING WORKFORCE EXPERIENCE.
4 (C) AN INDIVIDUAL MAY OBTAIN A HIGH SCHOOL DIPLOMA THROUGH THE
5 MDIPLOMAWORKS PATHWAY IF THE INDIVIDUAL:
6 (1) HAS NOT OBTAINED A HIGH SCHOOL DIPLOMA;
7 (2) RESIDES IN THE STATE;
8 (3) IS NOT SUBJECT TO THE COMPULSORY SCHOOL ATTENDANCE
9 REQUIREMENT UNDER § 7–301 OF THE EDUCATION ARTICLE; AND
10 (4) HAS WITHDRAWN FROM A REGULAR FULL–TIME PUBLIC OR
11 PRIVATE SCHOOL PROGRAM.
12 (D) TO EARN A HIGH SCHOOL DIPLOMA THROUGH THE MDIPLOMAWORKS
13 PATHWAY, AN INDIVIDUAL SHALL DEMONSTRATE EQUIVALENCY TO A MARYLAND
14 HIGH SCHOOL DIPLOMA BY COMPLETION OF A COMBINATION OF THE FOLLOWING
15 COMPONENTS AS APPROVED BY THE DEPARTMENT:
16 (1) EARNING INDUSTRY–RECOGNIZED CREDENTIALS;
17 (2) DEMONSTRATING FOUNDATIONAL ACADEMIC SKILLS THROUGH
18 FORMAL ASSESSMENTS;
19 (3) COMPLETING CAREER AND TECHNICAL EDUCATION
20 COURSEWORK; AND
21 (4) COMPLETING AN ON–THE–JOB LEARNING EXPERIENCE.
22 (E) A HIGH SCHOOL DIPLOMA SHALL BE AWARDED TO AN INDIVIDUAL WHO
23 COMPLETES THE MDIPLOMAWORKS PATHWAY IN ACCORDANCE WITH THE
24 REGULATIONS ADOPTED BY THE SECRETARY AND THE STATE BOARD OF
25 EDUCATION.
26 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That, in calendar years 2027 and
27 2028, the Maryland Department of Labor shall publish on its website, and include in any
28 publications that describe how adults may obtain a high school diploma, information on the
29 MDiplomaWorks pathway established under § 11–810 of the Labor and Employment Article,
30 as enacted by Section 1 of this Act, in order to inform potentially eligible applicants about
31 the new pathway to obtain a high school diploma.
