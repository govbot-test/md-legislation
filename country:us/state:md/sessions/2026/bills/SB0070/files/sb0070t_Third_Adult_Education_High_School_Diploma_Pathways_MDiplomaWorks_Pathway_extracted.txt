1 AN ACT concerning
2 Adult Education – High School Diploma Pathways – GED Option Pilot Program
3 and MDiplomaWorks Pathway
4 FOR the purpose of establishing the GED Option Pilot Program; requiring the Maryland
5 Department of Labor, in consultation with certain entities, to establish criteria to
6 select certain local school systems to participate in the Pilot Program; establishing
7 qualifications for certain students selected to participate in the Pilot Program;
8 establishing the MDiplomaWorks pathway as an alternative method of earning a
9 high school diploma in the State; and generally relating to adult education and high
10 school diploma pathways.

6 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
7 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
8 Article – Labor and Employment
9 11–808.
10 (a) An individual may obtain a high school diploma by examination as provided
11 in this section if the individual:
12 (1) has not obtained a high school diploma;
13 (2) resides in this State; and
14 (3) except as provided in subsection (b) of this section:
15 (i) is not subject to compulsory school attendance under § 7–301 of
16 the Education Article; and
17 (ii) has withdrawn from a regular full–time public or private school
18 program.
19 (b) An individual is not subject to the requirements of subsection (a)(3) of this
20 section if the individual participates in:
21 (1) a GED Option Program administered by the State Department of
22 Education that creates a pathway to a high school diploma by examination for currently
23 enrolled high school English language learner students under the age of 21 years who have
24 experienced interrupted education and have a lower level of English proficiency than their
25 peers; OR
26 (2) THE GED OPTION PILOT PROGRAM ESTABLISHED UNDER §
27 11–808.1 OF THIS SUBTITLE.
28 11–808.1.
29 (A) IN THIS SECTION, “PILOT PROGRAM” MEANS THE GED OPTION PILOT
30 PROGRAM.
31 (B) (1) THERE IS A GED OPTION PILOT PROGRAM IN THE STATE.

1 (2) THE PURPOSE OF THE PILOT PROGRAM IS TO EXPAND THE GED
2 OPTION PROGRAM TO STUDENTS BEYOND ENGLISH LANGUAGE LEARNERS IN
3 SELECTED SCHOOL DISTRICTS TO ENSURE THAT THE STUDENTS HAVE A PATHWAY
4 TO OBTAIN A HIGH SCHOOL DIPLOMA AND ARE PREPARED TO ENTER
5 POSTSECONDARY PROGRAMMING OR THE WORKFORCE.
6 (C) THE PILOT PROGRAM SHALL BEGIN IN THE 2027–2028 SCHOOL YEAR
7 AND SHALL CONTINUE FOR 2 SCHOOL YEARS.
8 (D) THE DEPARTMENT:
9 (1) IN CONSULTATION WITH THE STATE DEPARTMENT OF
10 EDUCATION AND THE PUBLIC SCHOOL SUPERINTENDENTS’ ASSOCIATION OF
11 MARYLAND, SHALL DEVELOP CRITERIA, INCLUDING RECOGNITION OF GEOGRAPHIC
12 DIVERSITY, FOR THE SELECTION OF AT LEAST THREE LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEMS TO
13 PARTICIPATE IN THE PILOT PROGRAM; AND
14 (2) IN CONSULTATION WITH THE STATE DEPARTMENT OF
15 EDUCATION, SHALL USE THE CRITERIA DEVELOPED UNDER ITEM (1) OF THIS
16 SUBSECTION TO SELECT AT LEAST THREE LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEMS TO PARTICIPATE
17 IN THE PILOT PROGRAM.
18 (E) EACH COUNTY SUPERINTENDENT FROM A PARTICIPATING LOCAL
19 SCHOOL SYSTEM MAY SELECT UP TO 150 STUDENTS TO PARTICIPATE IN THE PILOT
20 PROGRAM.
21 (F) A STUDENT SELECTED TO PARTICIPATE IN THE PILOT PROGRAM:
22 (1) MAY BEGIN THE GED OPTION PROGRAM WHEN THE STUDENT IS
23 AT LEAST 17 YEARS OLD;
24 (2) MUST BE AT SIGNIFICANT RISK OF NOT SUCCESSFULLY
25 COMPLETING HIGH SCHOOL; AND
26 (3) SHALL TAKE THE HIGH SCHOOL DIPLOMA BY EXAMINATION
27 BEFORE THE CONCLUSION OF THE GED OPTION PROGRAM.
28 (G) THE DEPARTMENT AND THE STATE DEPARTMENT OF EDUCATION
29 SHALL EXPLORE OPTIONS FOR EXPANDING THE GED OPTION PROGRAM BASED ON
30 RESULTS OF THE PILOT PROGRAM.

19 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That the Laws of Maryland read
20 as follows:
21 Article – Labor and Employment
22 11–810.
23 (A) IN THIS SECTION, “MDIPLOMAWORKS PATHWAY” MEANS AN
24 ALTERNATIVE PATHWAY TO EARN A HIGH SCHOOL DIPLOMA IN THE STATE.
25 (B) THE MDIPLOMAWORKS PATHWAY IS A METHOD TO RECOGNIZE AND
26 CERTIFY HIGH SCHOOL DIPLOMA ACHIEVEMENT THROUGH DEMONSTRATED
27 ACADEMIC AND CAREER COMPETENCIES, INCLUDING WORKFORCE EXPERIENCE.
28 (C) AN INDIVIDUAL MAY OBTAIN A HIGH SCHOOL DIPLOMA THROUGH THE
29 MDIPLOMAWORKS PATHWAY IF THE INDIVIDUAL:
30 (1) HAS NOT OBTAINED A HIGH SCHOOL DIPLOMA;

SENATE BILL 70 5
1 (2) RESIDES IN THE STATE;
2 (3) IS NOT SUBJECT TO THE COMPULSORY SCHOOL ATTENDANCE
3 REQUIREMENT UNDER § 7–301 OF THE EDUCATION ARTICLE; AND
4 (4) HAS WITHDRAWN FROM A REGULAR FULL–TIME PUBLIC OR
5 PRIVATE SCHOOL PROGRAM.
6 (D) TO EARN A HIGH SCHOOL DIPLOMA THROUGH THE MDIPLOMAWORKS
7 PATHWAY, AN INDIVIDUAL SHALL DEMONSTRATE EQUIVALENCY TO A MARYLAND
8 HIGH SCHOOL DIPLOMA BY COMPLETION OF A COMBINATION OF THE FOLLOWING
9 COMPONENTS AS APPROVED BY THE DEPARTMENT:
10 (1) EARNING INDUSTRY–RECOGNIZED CREDENTIALS;
11 (2) DEMONSTRATING FOUNDATIONAL ACADEMIC SKILLS THROUGH
12 FORMAL ASSESSMENTS;
13 (3) COMPLETING CAREER AND TECHNICAL EDUCATION
14 COURSEWORK; AND
15 (4) COMPLETING AN ON–THE–JOB LEARNING EXPERIENCE.
16 (E) A HIGH SCHOOL DIPLOMA SHALL BE AWARDED TO AN INDIVIDUAL WHO
17 COMPLETES THE MDIPLOMAWORKS PATHWAY IN ACCORDANCE WITH THE
18 REGULATIONS ADOPTED BY THE SECRETARY AND THE STATE BOARD OF
19 EDUCATION.
20 SECTION 3. 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
21 October 1, 2026. Section 1 of this Act shall remain effective for a period of 4 years and, at
22 the end of September 30, 2030, Section 1 of this Act, with no further action required by the
23 General Assembly, shall be abrogated and of no further force and effect.
