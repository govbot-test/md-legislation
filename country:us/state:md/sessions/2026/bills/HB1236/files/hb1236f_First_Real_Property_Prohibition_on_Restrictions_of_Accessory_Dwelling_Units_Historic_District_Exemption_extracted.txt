A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Real Property – Prohibition on Restrictions of Accessory Dwelling Units –
3 Historic District Exemption
4 FOR the purpose of exempting from a prohibition on restrictions on use for accessory
5 dwelling units certain property located within a district designated to be of historic
6 significance; and generally relating to restrictions on use and accessory dwelling
7 units.
8 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
9 Article – Land Use
10 Section 4–501(b) and 8–105
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2012 Volume and 2025 Supplement)
13 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
14 Article – Real Property
15 Section 2–126
16 Annotated Code of Maryland
17 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
18 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
19 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
20 Article – Land Use
21 4–501.
22 (b) (1) “Accessory dwelling unit” means a secondary dwelling unit that is:
23 (i) on the same lot, parcel, or tract as a primary single–family
24 detached dwelling unit; and
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

2 HOUSE BILL 1236
1 (ii) not greater than 75% of the size of and subordinate in use to the
2 primary single–family detached dwelling unit.
3 (2) “Accessory dwelling unit” includes a structure that is:
4 (i) separate from the primary single–family detached dwelling unit;
5 or
6 (ii) attached as an addition to the primary single–family detached
7 dwelling unit.
8 8–105.
9 For the purposes of this title, each local jurisdiction may designate boundaries for
10 sites, structures, and districts that are considered to be of historic, archaeological, or
11 architectural significance, by following the procedures of the local jurisdiction for
12 establishing or changing zoning districts and classifications.
13 Article – Real Property
14 2–126.
15 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
16 (2) “Accessory dwelling unit” has the meaning stated in § 4–501 of the Land
17 Use Article.
18 (3) “Restriction on use” includes any covenant, restriction, or condition
19 contained in:
20 (i) A deed;
21 (ii) A declaration;
22 (iii) A contract;
23 (iv) The bylaws or rules of a homeowners association;
24 (v) A security instrument; or
25 (vi) Any other instrument affecting:
26 1. The transfer or sale of real property; or
27 2. Any other interest in real property.
HOUSE BILL 1236 3
1 (b) (1) Except as provided in paragraph (2)(ii) of this subsection, if a property
2 owner has the exclusive right to use the property and abides by all applicable laws and
3 regulations, a restriction on use regarding land use may not impose or act to impose an
4 unreasonable limitation on the ability of the property owner to develop or offer for rent an
5 accessory dwelling unit.
6 (2) For the purpose of paragraph (1) of this subsection, an unreasonable
7 limitation:
8 (i) Includes a limitation that prohibits, either explicitly or by effect
9 of the restrictions, the development of an accessory dwelling unit; and
10 (ii) Does not include a limitation on the short–term rental of an
11 accessory dwelling unit.
12 (c) This section does not apply to a restriction on use on [historic]:
13 (1) HISTORIC property that is listed in or determined by the Director of
14 the Maryland Historical Trust to be eligible for inclusion in the Maryland Register of
15 Historic Properties; OR
16 (2) PROPERTY LOCATED WITHIN A DISTRICT DESIGNATED BY THE
17 APPLICABLE LOCAL JURISDICTION TO BE OF HISTORIC SIGNIFICANCE IN
18 ACCORDANCE WITH § 8–105 OF THE LAND USE ARTICLE.
19 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
20 October 1, 2026.
