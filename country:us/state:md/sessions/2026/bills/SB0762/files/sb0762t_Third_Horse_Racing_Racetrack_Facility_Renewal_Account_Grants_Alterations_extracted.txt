CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Horse Racing – Racetrack Facility Renewal Account Grants – Alterations
3 FOR the purpose of authorizing, rather than requiring, a certain racing licensee to receive
4 a grant for a capital construction plan from the Racetrack Facility Renewal Account
5 without providing and expending a matching fund; requiring the racing licensee that
6 receives the grant without providing and expending a matching fund to conduct live
7 racing in the State for a certain number of years; requiring the State Racing
8 Commission to recapture certain grant funds under certain circumstances and in a
9 certain manner; and generally relating to grants from the Racetrack Facility
10 Renewal Account.

20 Annotated Code of Maryland
21 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

11 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,

15 as follows:
16 Article – State Government
17 9–1A–01.
18 (a) In this subtitle the following words have the meanings indicated.
19 (W–1) “RACING LICENSEE” MEANS THE HOLDER OF A LICENSE ISSUED BY THE
20 STATE RACING COMMISSION TO HOLD A RACE MEETING IN THE STATE UNDER
21 TITLE 11 OF THE BUSINESS REGULATION ARTICLE.
22 9–1A–09.
23 [(a) In this section, “racing licensee” means the holder of a license issued by the
24 State Racing Commission to hold a race meeting in the State under Title 11 of the Business
25 Regulation Article.]
26 [(b)] (A) As a condition of eligibility for funding under § 9–1A–29 of this subtitle,
27 a racing licensee shall:
28 (1) (i) 1. for Rosecroft Raceway, FOR ANY YEAR THAT FUNDING IS
29 PROVIDED, conduct a minimum of 60 annual live racing days unless otherwise agreed to
30 by the racing licensee and the organization that represents the majority of licensed
31 standardbred owners and trainers in the State or unless the racing licensee is prevented
32 by weather, acts of God, or other circumstances beyond the racing licensee’s control; and
33 [(ii)] 2. for Ocean Downs Racetrack, FOR ANY YEAR THAT
34 FUNDING IS PROVIDED, conduct a minimum of 40 annual live racing days unless

1 otherwise agreed to by the racing licensee and the organization that represents the majority
2 of licensed standardbred owners and trainers in the State or unless the racing licensee is
3 prevented by weather, acts of God, or other circumstances beyond the racing licensee’s
4 control; AND
5 (II) IF THE RACING LICENSEE RECEIVES FUNDING WITHOUT
6 PROVIDING AND EXPENDING A MATCHING FUND, AGREE TO CONDUCT LIVE RACING
7 DAYS IN THE STATE FOR AT LEAST 10 YEARS AFTER THE CONSTRUCTION PLAN
8 REQUIRED UNDER § 9–1A–29(E) OF THIS SUBTITLE IS COMPLETE;
9 (2) develop and submit to the State Racing Commission a multiyear plan
10 to improve the quality and marketing of horse racing at racetrack locations owned or
11 operated by the racing licensee in Maryland, which shall include:
12 (i) goals, indicators, and timelines for specific actions that will be
13 taken by the racing licensee to improve the quality and marketing of the horse racing
14 industry in Maryland; and
15 (ii) a master plan for capital improvements that reflects, at a
16 minimum:
17 1. commitments that have been made to the State Racing
18 Commission;
19 2. updates to any prior master plan specifically identifying
20 capital improvements and expenditures made on or after January 1, 2018; and
21 3. an ongoing investment in capital maintenance and
22 improvements in the horse racing facilities; AND
23 (3) for each year that funding is requested, spend at least the following
24 minimum amounts for capital maintenance and improvements, which may include
25 amounts provided as a matching fund [as required] IF THE RACING LICENSEE ELECTS
26 TO PROVIDE AND EXPEND A MATCHING FUND under § 9–1A–29(e)(2) of this subtitle:
27 (i) for Rosecroft Raceway, $300,000; and
28 (ii) for Ocean Downs Racetrack, $300,000.
29 [(c)] (B) As part of the capital maintenance and improvement items in the plan
30 submitted under subsection [(b)(2)] (A)(2) of this section, a racing licensee shall include
31 any improvements necessary to ensure that the condition of any part of the racetrack
32 facility where individuals reside is satisfactory for human habitation and meets minimum
33 housing and sanitation standards in the county where the facility is located.

9 racing schedule; and

17 (a) There is a Racetrack Facility Renewal Account under the authority of the

20 Commission may use the services of a certified public accountant to review an eligible
21 request for OR THE EXPENDITURES OF a grant under this section.
22 (ii) [The holder of a license to hold a race meeting in the State] A
23 RACING LICENSEE that has requested a grant under this section shall reimburse the State
24 Racing Commission for any expenditures for services under subparagraph (i) of this
25 paragraph.
26 (c) Except as provided in subsection (d)(1) of this section, funds from the Account
27 shall be used to provide a grant to the [holder of a license to hold a race meeting in the
28 State] RACING LICENSEE for racetrack facility capital construction and improvements.

30 in the State] RACING LICENSEE shall[:
31 (1)] submit a capital construction plan to be implemented COMPLETED
32 within a specified time frame to the State Racing Commission for approval[; and].

1 (2) SUBJECT TO § 9–1A–09(A)(1)(II) OF THIS SUBTITLE AND THE
2 APPROVAL OF THE GRANT FOR THE CAPITAL CONSTRUCTION PLAN BY THE STATE
3 RACING COMMISSION, THE RACING LICENSEE MAY provide and expend a matching
4 fund.
5 (f) (1) After a grant has been provided under this section, the State Racing
6 Commission shall:
7 [(1)] (I) in consultation with the Department of General Services,
8 monitor the implementation of the approved capital construction plan; and
9 (II) ON WRITTEN REQUEST FROM THE GRANTEE, IN
10 CONSULTATION WITH THE DEPARTMENT OF GENERAL SERVICES, CERTIFY IN
11 WRITING THAT THE GRANTEE HAS COMPLETED THE APPROVED CAPITAL
12 CONSTRUCTION PLAN; AND
13 [(2)] (II) (III) make provisions for recapture of grant money if the capital
14 construction plan is not implemented COMPLETED within the time frame approved by the
15 State Racing Commission.
16 (2) IF A RACING LICENSEE RECEIVES A GRANT FOR A CAPITAL
17 CONSTRUCTION PLAN WITHOUT EXPENDING A MATCHING FUND AND DOES NOT
18 COMPLETE THE CAPITAL CONSTRUCTION PLAN OR CONDUCT LIVE RACING DAYS IN
19 THE STATE FOR AT LEAST 10 YEARS AFTER THE CONSTRUCTION PLAN IS COMPLETE,
20 THE STATE RACING COMMISSION SHALL:
21 (I) IF THE RACING LICENSEE DOES NOT CONDUCT LIVE RACING
22 DAYS IN THE STATE FOR AT LEAST 10 YEARS AFTER THE CONSTRUCTION PLAN IS
23 COMPLETE, CALCULATE THE AMOUNT TO BE REPAID ON A PRO RATA BASIS; AND
24 BASIS AND RECAPTURE THE GRANT MONEY; OR
25 (II) RECAPTURE THE GRANT MONEY IN THE AMOUNT AS
26 CALCULATED UNDER ITEM (I) OF THIS PARAGRAPH.
27 (h) (1) The State Racing Commission shall adopt regulations to implement the
28 provisions of this section, including regulations to:
29 (i) address minimum criteria for the types of improvements to be
30 made by the [holder of a license] RACING LICENSEE; [and]
31 (ii) establish a formula to allocate funds under subsection (d)(2) of
32 this section between Rosecroft Raceway and Ocean Downs Race Course; AND

1 (III) ESTABLISH GUIDELINES FOR A RACING LICENSEE THAT
2 REQUESTS A GRANT FOR A CAPITAL CONSTRUCTION PLAN WITHOUT EXPENDING A
3 MATCHING FUND.
4 (2) If Rosecroft Raceway is closed and no longer used for live racing the
5 unencumbered fund balance, including accrued interest, of the amount available to
6 Rosecroft Raceway from the Racetrack Facility Renewal Account under subsection (d)(1)(ii)
7 of this section shall be made available to Ocean Downs Race Course.

9 1, 2026.
