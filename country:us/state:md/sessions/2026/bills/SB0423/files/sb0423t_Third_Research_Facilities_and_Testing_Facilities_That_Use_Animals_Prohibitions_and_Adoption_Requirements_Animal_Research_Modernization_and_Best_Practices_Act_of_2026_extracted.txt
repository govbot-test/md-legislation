CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Research Facilities and Testing Facilities That Use Animals – Prohibitions and
3 Adoption Requirements
4 (Animal Research Modernization and Best Practices Act of 2026)
5 FOR the purpose of prohibiting a research facility or testing facility from using certain dogs
6 and cats for research or testing purposes; prohibiting a research facility or testing
7 facility from performing certain procedures on dogs or cats; prohibiting a testing
8 facility from using traditional animal test methods under certain circumstances;
9 applying certain provisions of law regarding the adoption of dogs and cats used for
10 scientific research purposes to testing facilities; and generally relating to research
11 facilities and testing facilities that use animals in research, education, or testing.
12 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
13 Article – Agriculture
14 Section 15–101 to be under the amended title “Title 15. Research Facilities and
15 Testing Facilities That Use Animals”
16 Annotated Code of Maryland
17 (2016 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
18 BY adding to
19 Article – Agriculture
20 Section 15–101 through 15–103, 15–105, and 15–106
21 Annotated Code of Maryland
22 (2016 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

1 Preamble
2 WHEREAS, Scientific research and medical innovation are essential to protecting
3 public health and advancing knowledge; and
4 WHEREAS, The State has a compelling interest in ensuring that scientific research
5 and medical innovation are conducted in a manner that is ethical, responsible, and
6 consistent with evolving scientific standards; and
7 WHEREAS, Advances in technology and methodology have increasingly made
8 reducing, refining, or replacing the use of live animals in laboratory testing a possibility;
9 and
10 WHEREAS, The unnecessary or excessive use of animals in research undermines
11 public confidence and fails to reflect best practices in modern science; and
12 WHEREAS, It is the policy of the State to require that animal testing be employed
13 only when scientifically justified and when no reasonably available alternative methods
14 exist; and
15 WHEREAS, When animal testing is necessary, it should be conducted using the
16 minimum number of animals required and adhere to standards that prioritize humane
17 treatment and oversight; and
18 WHEREAS, Establishing clear statutory standards for the necessity, minimization,
19 and review of animal laboratory testing will promote scientific integrity, ethical
20 responsibility, and public trust; now, therefore,

1 (b) This section applies to a dog or cat that, in the determination of an attending
