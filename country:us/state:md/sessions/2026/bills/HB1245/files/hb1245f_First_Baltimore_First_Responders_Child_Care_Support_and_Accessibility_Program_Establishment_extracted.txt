4 FOR the purpose of establishing the Baltimore First Responders Child Care Support and
5 Accessibility Program within the State Department of Education’s Office of Child

7 child care allowances to first responders; and generally relating to the Baltimore
8 First Responders Child Care Support and Accessibility Program.

1 (B) (1) THERE IS A BALTIMORE FIRST RESPONDERS CHILD CARE
2 SUPPORT AND ACCESSIBILITY PROGRAM.
3 (2) THE PURPOSE OF THE PROGRAM IS TO SUPPORT AND EXPAND A
4 PILOT PROGRAM IN BALTIMORE CITY THAT PROVIDES MONTHLY CHILD CARE
5 ALLOWANCES TO FIRST RESPONDERS TO ACCESS QUALITY CHILD CARE THAT
6 ALIGNS WITH THE DEMANDS OF A CAREER IN PUBLIC SAFETY.
7 (3) THE OFFICE SHALL ADMINISTER THE PROGRAM IN
8 CONSULTATION WITH:
9 (I) THE BALTIMORE POLICE DEPARTMENT;
10 (II) THE BALTIMORE CITY FIRE DEPARTMENT, INCLUDING
11 EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES PERSONNEL; AND
12 (III) ANY NONPROFIT ORGANIZATION PARTNERS.
13 (C) (1) FOR FISCAL YEAR 2028 AND EACH FISCAL YEAR THEREAFTER,
14 THE GOVERNOR SHALL INCLUDE IN THE ANNUAL BUDGET BILL AN APPROPRIATION
15 OF $600,000 TO THE PROGRAM.
16 (2) THE FUNDS SHALL BE USED TO PROVIDE CHILD CARE
17 ALLOWANCES TO FIRST RESPONDERS IN BALTIMORE CITY TO:
18 (I) SUPPORT DEPENDENT CHILDREN IN EARLY CHILDHOOD
19 DEVELOPMENT;
20 (II) RECRUIT AND RETAIN FIRST RESPONDERS; AND
21 (III) PROMOTE PUBLIC SAFETY.
22 (D) ON OR BEFORE SEPTEMBER 1, 2028, AND EACH SEPTEMBER 1
23 THEREAFTER, THE OFFICE SHALL REPORT TO THE GOVERNOR AND, IN
24 ACCORDANCE WITH § 2–1257 OF THE STATE GOVERNMENT ARTICLE, THE GENERAL
25 ASSEMBLY ON THE PROGRAM, ON:
26 (1) THE NUMBER OF FIRST RESPONDERS WHO RECEIVED A CHILD
27 CARE ALLOWANCE UNDER THE PROGRAM DURING THE IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING
28 FISCAL YEAR DISAGGREGATED BY LOCAL PUBLIC SAFETY AGENCY THAT PROVIDES
29 EMERGENCY RESPONSE SERVICES;
