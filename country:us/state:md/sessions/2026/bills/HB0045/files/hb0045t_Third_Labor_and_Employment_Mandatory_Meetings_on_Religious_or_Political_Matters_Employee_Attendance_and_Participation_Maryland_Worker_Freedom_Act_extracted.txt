CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Labor and Employment – Mandatory Meetings on Religious or Political
3 Matters – Employee Attendance and Participation
4 (Maryland Worker Freedom Act)
5 FOR the purpose of prohibiting employers and their agents, representatives, and designees
6 from taking certain actions against an employee or applicant for employment
7 because the employee or applicant takes certain actions regarding
8 employer–sponsored meetings during which the employer communicates the opinion
9 of the employer regarding religious matters or political matters; authorizing an
10 employee to file a certain complaint with the Commissioner of Labor and Industry;
11 authorizing the Commissioner to take certain actions related to violations of certain
12 provisions of this Act; requiring employers to notify the employer’s employees in a
13 certain manner of the requirements and protections provided under certain
14 provisions of this Act; requiring the Commissioner to develop and make available a
15 certain poster and model notice; and generally relating to employee attendance and
16 participation in employer meetings on religious or political matters.
17 BY adding to
18 Article – Labor and Employment
19 Section 3–718
20 Annotated Code of Maryland
21 (2025 Replacement Volume)

1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – Labor and Employment
4 3–718.
5 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
6 INDICATED.
7 (2) “POLITICAL MATTERS” MEANS MATTERS RELATING TO:
8 (I) ELECTIONS FOR POLITICAL OFFICE;
9 (II) POLITICAL PARTIES;
10 (III) PROPOSALS TO CHANGE:
11 1. LEGISLATION;
12 2. REGULATIONS; OR
13 3. PUBLIC POLICY; OR
14 (IV) THE DECISION TO JOIN OR SUPPORT A POLITICAL PARTY OR
15 POTENTIAL CIVIC, COMMUNITY, FRATERNAL, OR LABOR ORGANIZATION.
16 (3) “RELIGIOUS MATTERS” MEANS MATTERS RELATING TO
17 RELIGIOUS BELIEF, AFFILIATION, AND PRACTICE OR THE DECISION TO JOIN AND
18 SUPPORT A RELIGIOUS ORGANIZATION OR ASSOCIATION.
19 (B) THIS SECTION DOES NOT APPLY TO AN EMPLOYER THAT:
20 (1) IS A RELIGIOUS CORPORATION, ORGANIZATION, OR ASSOCIATION
21 OR AN EDUCATIONAL INSTITUTION OR SOCIETY THAT IS EXEMPT FROM THE
22 REQUIREMENTS OF TITLE VII OF THE CIVIL RIGHTS ACT OF 1964 UNDER 42 U.S.C.
23 § 2000E–1(A) OR IS EXEMPT FOR EMPLOYMENT DISCRIMINATION PROTECTIONS
24 UNDER STATE LAW;
25 (2) IS A POLITICAL ORGANIZATION THAT REQUIRES ITS EMPLOYEES
26 TO ATTEND AN EMPLOYER–SPONSORED MEETING OR TO PARTICIPATE IN
27 COMMUNICATIONS WITH THE EMPLOYER OR THE EMPLOYER’S AGENTS OR OTHER
28 REPRESENTATIVES FOR THE PURPOSE OF COMMUNICATING THE EMPLOYER’S
29 POLITICAL TENETS OR PURPOSES;

24 (2) FAIL OR REFUSE TO HIRE AN APPLICANT FOR EMPLOYMENT AS A
25 RESULT OF THE APPLICANT’S REFUSAL TO ATTEND OR PARTICIPATE IN AN
26 EMPLOYER–SPONSORED MEETING DURING WHICH THE EMPLOYER COMMUNICATES
27 THE OPINION OF THE EMPLOYER REGARDING RELIGIOUS MATTERS OR POLITICAL
28 MATTERS.
29 (D) THIS SECTION DOES NOT PROHIBIT AN EMPLOYER:
30 (1) FROM COMMUNICATING INFORMATION THAT THE EMPLOYER IS
31 REQUIRED BY LAW TO COMMUNICATE;
