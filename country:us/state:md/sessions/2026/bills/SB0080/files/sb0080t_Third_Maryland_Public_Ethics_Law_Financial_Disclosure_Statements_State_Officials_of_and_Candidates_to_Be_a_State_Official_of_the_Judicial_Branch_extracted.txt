CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Maryland Public Ethics Law – Financial Disclosure Statements – State Officials
3 of and Candidates to Be a State Official of the Judicial Branch
4 FOR the purpose of clarifying certain duties of the Commission on Judicial Disabilities and
5 the Judicial Ethics Committee; requiring the Administrative Office of the Courts or
6 another body designated by the Supreme Court of Maryland to administer and
7 implement certain provisions of law relating to financial disclosure statements for
8 State officials of the Judicial Branch and candidates to be a State official of the
9 Judicial Branch and maintain and make available to the public the records and
10 statements of State officials of the Judicial Branch and candidates to be a State
11 official of the Judicial Branch; repealing a requirement that the Supreme Court of
12 Maryland transmit copies of certain statements to the State Ethics Commission; and
13 generally relating to statements for State officials of and candidates to be a State
14 official of the Judicial Branch.
15 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
16 Article – General Provisions
17 Section 5–104 and 5–606
18 Annotated Code of Maryland
19 (2019 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
20 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
21 Article – General Provisions

10 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
11 Article – General Provisions
12 5–104.
13 (a) Except as provided in subsections (b) and (c) of this section and in § 5–871 of
14 this title, this title shall be administered and implemented by the Ethics Commission.
15 (b) The Joint Ethics Committee, acting as an advisory body, shall administer and
16 implement Subtitle 5 of this title as [it] THAT SUBTITLE applies to members of the General
17 Assembly.
18 (c) (1) The Commission on Judicial Disabilities, the Judicial Ethics
19 Committee, or another body designated by the Supreme Court of Maryland, acting as an
20 advisory body, shall administer and implement [Subtitles 5 and 6] SUBTITLE 5 of this title
21 as [those subtitles apply] THAT SUBTITLE APPLIES to State officials of the Judicial
22 Branch AND CANDIDATES TO BE A STATE OFFICIAL OF THE JUDICIAL BRANCH.
23 (2) THE ADMINISTRATIVE OFFICE OF THE COURTS OR ANOTHER
24 BODY DESIGNATED BY THE SUPREME COURT OF MARYLAND, ACTING AS AN
25 ADVISORY BODY, SHALL ADMINISTER AND IMPLEMENT SUBTITLE 6 OF THIS TITLE
26 AS THAT SUBTITLE APPLIES TO STATE OFFICIALS OF THE JUDICIAL BRANCH AND
27 CANDIDATES TO BE A STATE OFFICIAL OF THE JUDICIAL BRANCH.
28 5–606.
29 (a) (1) (i) Except as provided in paragraph (3) of this subsection OR
30 SUBSECTION (C) OF THIS SECTION, the Ethics Commission and the Joint Ethics
31 Committee shall maintain the statements submitted under this subtitle and, during
32 normal office hours, make the statements available to the public for examination and
33 copying.
SENATE BILL 80 3
1 (ii) Except as provided in paragraph (2) of this subsection, the Ethics
2 Commission and the Joint Ethics Committee may charge a reasonable fee and adopt
3 administrative procedures for the examination and copying of a statement.
4 (2) Except as provided in paragraph (3) of this subsection, for statements
5 submitted on or after January 1, 2019, the Ethics Commission shall make freely available
6 to the public on the Internet, through an online registration program, a [financial
7 disclosure] statement required under § 5–601(a) of this subtitle and a preliminary
8 disclosure required under § 5–602(c) of this subtitle that is filed by:
9 (i) a State official OF THE EXECUTIVE BRANCH OR THE
10 LEGISLATIVE BRANCH;
11 (ii) a candidate for office as a State official OF THE EXECUTIVE
12 BRANCH OR THE LEGISLATIVE BRANCH; or
13 (iii) a secretary of a principal department in the Executive Branch.
14 (3) The Ethics Commission and the Joint Ethics Committee may not:
15 (i) provide public access to a portion of a statement that is filed after
16 January 1, 2019, and that includes an individual’s home address that the individual has
17 identified as the individual’s home address; or
18 (ii) post on the Internet information related to consideration
19 received that is reported under § 5–607(j) of this subtitle.
20 (b) (1) The Ethics Commission and the Joint Ethics Committee shall maintain
21 a record of:
22 (i) the name and home address of each individual who examines or
23 copies a statement under SUBSECTION (A) OF this section; and
24 (ii) the name of the individual whose statement was examined or
25 copied.
26 (2) On the request of the individual whose statement was examined or
27 copied, the Ethics Commission or the Joint Ethics Committee shall forward to that
28 individual a copy of the record specified in paragraph (1) of this subsection.
29 (C) THE ADMINISTRATIVE OFFICE OF THE COURTS OR ANOTHER BODY
30 DESIGNATED BY THE SUPREME COURT OF MARYLAND SHALL:
31 (1) MAINTAIN THE RECORDS OF STATE OFFICIALS OF THE JUDICIAL
32 BRANCH AND CANDIDATES TO BE A STATE OFFICIAL OF THE JUDICIAL BRANCH;
33 AND

1 (2) MAKE THE STATEMENTS SUBMITTED UNDER THIS SUBTITLE
2 AVAILABLE TO THE PUBLIC FOR EXAMINATION AND COPYING DURING NORMAL
3 OFFICE HOURS.
4 5–610.
5 (a) In accordance with its administrative authority over the Judicial Branch
6 under the Maryland Constitution, the Supreme Court of Maryland shall adopt and
7 administer rules that require each individual specified in § 5–601(b) of this subtitle to file
8 a statement periodically that discloses, as a public record, the information concerning the
9 individual’s financial affairs that the court considers necessary or appropriate to promote
10 continued trust and confidence in the integrity of the Judicial Branch.
11 [(c) Within 30 days after receiving a statement under this section, the Supreme
12 Court of Maryland or its designee shall transmit a copy of the statement to the Ethics
13 Commission.]
14 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
15 1, 2026.
