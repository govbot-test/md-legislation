3 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That:
4 (a) In this section, “foster youth” has the meaning stated in § 5–304 of the Human
5 Services Article.
6 (b) On or before December 1, 2026, the State Department of Education, in
7 cooperation with the Department of Human Services, shall develop and submit a report to
8 the General Assembly, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government Article, on
9 improving outcomes for students in the State child welfare system.
10 (c) The report required under subsection (b) of this section shall:
11 (1) identify each State agency that provides services for foster youth;
12 (2) specifically for children and foster youth students, identify needs and
13 gaps in services that could improve educational outcomes;
14 (3) identify best practices from other states that have demonstrated
15 success with college and career readiness for foster youth;
16 (4) provide recommendations for improving educational outcomes for foster
17 youth students in the State;
18 (5) evaluate the effectiveness of the information reported under § 5–1312
19 of the Family Law Article; and
20 (6) provide recommendations for improving data collection and reporting
21 requirements on foster youth students.
22 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
23 October 1, 2026. Section 2 of this Act shall remain effective for a period of 1 year and, at
24 the end of September 30, 2027, Section 2 of this Act, with no further action required by the
25 General Assembly, shall be abrogated and of no further force and effect.
