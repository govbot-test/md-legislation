CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Local School Systems State Department of Education – Sexual Abuse and
3 Sexual Misconduct – Misconduct Model Response Policy and After–Action
4 Review – Requirements
5 FOR the purpose of requiring the State Department of Education to develop a model sexual
6 abuse and sexual misconduct response policy that includes, at minimum, certain
7 components; requiring each local school system to adopt a certain response policy,
8 provide certain notices in a certain manner, and conduct a certain after–action
9 review under certain circumstances and post a certain link on its website; and
10 generally relating to sexual abuse and sexual misconduct response policies.
11 BY adding to
12 Article – Education
13 Section 4–148
14 Annotated Code of Maryland
15 (2025 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
16 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
17 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
18 Article – Education
19 4–148.

3 (2) “CREDIBLE ALLEGATION” MEANS A SPECIFIC AND PLAUSIBLE
4 REPORT OF SEXUAL MISCONDUCT OR SEXUAL ABUSE, MADE BY A PERSON WITH

9 MISCONDUCT RESPONSE POLICY.

22 (VII) ANY OTHER INCIDENT DESIGNATED BY THE LOCAL SCHOOL
23 SYSTEM AS REQUIRING A FORMAL AFTER–ACTION REVIEW.

25 MISCONDUCT RESPONSE POLICY FOR USE BY LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEMS TO RESPOND

16 TO ENSURE TIMELY RESOLUTION OF IDENTIFIED ISSUES; AND
17 4. METHODS FOR ENSURING LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM
18 ACCOUNTABILITY AFTER AN AFTER–ACTION REVIEW HAS BEEN CONDUCTED AND
19 ISSUES REQUIRING RESOLUTION HAVE BEEN IDENTIFIED.

6 (E) A LAW ENFORCEMENT AGENCY OR A CHILD PROTECTIVE SERVICES UNIT
7 MAY REQUEST THAT CERTAIN DETAILS OF A SERIOUS INCIDENT BE WITHHELD FROM

9 (F) (1) A LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM SHALL NOTIFY PARENTS AND

15 (I) CONFIRM THAT A CREDIBLE ALLEGATION WAS RECEIVED;
16 (II) INDICATE WHETHER THE EMPLOYEE WAS REMOVED FROM

18 (III) DESCRIBE THE SAFETY PRECAUTIONS BEING TAKEN BY THE
19 LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM; AND
20 (IV) LIST REFERRALS TO AVAILABLE COUNSELING AND
21 SUPPORT RESOURCES, IF ANY.
22 (3) A LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM MAY NOT DISCLOSE THE NAME OF THE
23 EMPLOYEE UNDER INVESTIGATION IN THE NOTICE SENT TO PARENTS AND
24 GUARDIANS UNDER THIS SUBSECTION.
25 (G) (1) ON THE CONCLUSION OF A CHILD SEXUAL ABUSE OR SEXUAL
26 MISCONDUCT CASE OR A SERIOUS INCIDENT, A LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM SHALL
27 CONDUCT AN AFTER–ACTION REVIEW WITHIN 45 DAYS OF THE CONCLUSION OF THE
28 INCIDENT TO:
29 (I) ANALYZE HOW THE INCIDENT OCCURRED;
30 (II) IDENTIFY SYSTEMIC AND ENVIRONMENTAL CONDITIONS
31 THAT CONTRIBUTED TO THE INCIDENT;

1 (III) IDENTIFY GAPS IN PROCEDURES, COMMUNICATION,

7 (VI) EVALUATE COMPLIANCE WITH TRANSPARENCY
8 REQUIREMENTS; AND
9 (VII) IDENTIFY AREAS FOR PROCESS IMPROVEMENTS.
10 (2) A WRITTEN REDACTED REPORT OF THE FINDINGS OF THE
11 AFTER–ACTION REVIEW CONDUCTED UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION
12 MAY BE PROVIDED TO THE STATE BOARD AND THE STATE SUPERINTENDENT.
