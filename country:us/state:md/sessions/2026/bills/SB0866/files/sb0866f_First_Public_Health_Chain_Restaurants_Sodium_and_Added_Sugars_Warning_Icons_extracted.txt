A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Public Health – Chain Restaurants – Sodium and Added Sugars Warning Icons
3 FOR the purpose of requiring the Maryland Department of Health to adopt certain
4 regulations relating to the sale of food items with a high added sugars content or
5 high sodium content by a chain restaurant by a certain date and to administer a
6 certain continuing education campaign within a certain time; requiring certain chain
7 restaurants to display certain icons next to food items with a high added sugars
8 content or high sodium content on each menu or menu board; and generally relating
9 to chain restaurants and high sodium and added sugars menu icons.

15 21–330.3.
16 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
17 INDICATED.
18 (2) “CHAIN RESTAURANT” MEANS A FOOD SERVICE FACILITY THAT IS
19 PART OF A CHAIN WITH 20 OR MORE LOCATIONS DOING BUSINESS UNDER THE SAME
20 NAME, REGARDLESS OF OWNERSHIP STRUCTURE, AND OFFERING FOR SALE
21 SUBSTANTIALLY SIMILAR MENU ITEMS.
22 (3) “HIGH ADDED SUGARS CONTENT” MEANS 25 MILLIGRAMS OR
23 MORE OF ADDED SUGARS PER SERVING.
24 (4) “HIGH SODIUM CONTENT” MEANS 1,150 MILLIGRAMS OR MORE OF
25 SODIUM PER SERVING.
26 (5) “MENU” OR “MENU BOARD” MEANS THE PRIMARY WRITING
27 SOURCE FROM WHICH A CUSTOMER MAKES AN ORDER SELECTION.
28 (6) “POINT OF SELECTION” MEANS A PLACE WHERE A CUSTOMER MAY
29 VIEW THE MENU AND MAKE A SELECTION TO PURCHASE A MENU ITEM.
30 (7) (I) “STANDARD MENU ITEM” MEANS A FOOD OR BEVERAGE
31 ITEM THAT IS LISTED ON A MENU OR MENU BOARD.
4 SENATE BILL 866
1 (II) “STANDARD MENU ITEM” DOES NOT INCLUDE A FOOD OR
2 BEVERAGE ITEM THAT APPEARS ON A MENU OR MENU BOARD FOR LESS THAN 60
3 CALENDAR DAYS PER YEAR.
4 (B) ON OR BEFORE JANUARY 1, 2027, THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ADOPT
5 REGULATIONS TO:
6 (1) ESTABLISH A HIGH SODIUM CONTENT WARNING ICON AND
7 ACCOMPANYING TEXT;
8 (2) ESTABLISH A HIGH ADDED SUGARS CONTENT WARNING ICON AND
9 ACCOMPANYING TEXT;
10 (3) ESTABLISH A MINIMUM SIZE FOR EACH WARNING ICON THAT IS AT
11 LEAST AS LARGE AS THE LARGEST TEXT CHARACTER USED IN THE NAME OF THE
12 MENU ITEM THAT THE ICON WILL BE DISPLAYED NEXT TO ON THE MENU OR MENU
13 BOARD; AND
14 (4) IMPLEMENT THIS SECTION.
15 (C) CONCURRENT WITH THE ADOPTION OF REGULATIONS UNDER
16 SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION, THE DEPARTMENT SHALL PUBLISH GUIDANCE
17 ON ITS WEBSITE THAT DETAILS THE PURPOSE OF AND HOW A CHAIN RESTAURANT
18 MAY COMPLY WITH THE REQUIREMENTS OF THIS SECTION.
19 (D) DURING CALENDAR YEAR 2027, THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ADMINISTER
20 A CONTINUING EDUCATION CAMPAIGN THAT EXPLAINS THE PURPOSE OF AND HOW
21 A CHAIN RESTAURANT MAY COMPLY WITH THE REQUIREMENTS OF THIS SECTION.
22 (E) BEGINNING JANUARY 1, 2028, EACH CHAIN RESTAURANT IN THE STATE
23 SHALL DISPLAY ON EACH MENU OR MENU BOARD:
24 (1) NEXT TO EACH STANDARD MENU ITEM WITH A HIGH SODIUM
25 CONTENT, THE HIGH SODIUM CONTENT WARNING ICON AND ACCOMPANYING TEXT
26 ESTABLISHED IN REGULATIONS ADOPTED UNDER SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS
27 SECTION; AND
28 (2) NEXT TO EACH STANDARD MENU ITEM WITH A HIGH ADDED
29 SUGARS CONTENT, THE HIGH ADDED SUGARS CONTENT WARNING ICON AND
30 ACCOMPANYING TEXT ESTABLISHED IN REGULATIONS ADOPTED UNDER
31 SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION.
SENATE BILL 866 5
1 (F) (1) IF THE CHAIN RESTAURANT IS REQUIRED TO DISPLAY A WARNING
2 ICON AND ACCOMPANYING TEXT UNDER SUBSECTION (E)(1) OF THIS SECTION, THE
3 FOLLOWING TEXT SHALL BE PROMINENTLY, CLEARLY, AND CONSPICUOUSLY
4 DISPLAYED AT THE POINT OF SELECTION:
5 “THE HIGH SODIUM CONTENT WARNING ICON INDICATES A MENU ITEM WITH
6 A SODIUM (SALT) CONTENT THAT EXCEEDS HALF THE TOTAL DAILY RECOMMENDED
7 LIMIT. DIETS HIGHER IN SODIUM ARE ASSOCIATED WITH AN INCREASED RISK OF
8 DEVELOPING HIGH BLOOD PRESSURE, WHICH IS A MAJOR CAUSE OF HEART DISEASE
9 AND STROKE.”.
10 (2) IF THE CHAIN RESTAURANT IS REQUIRED TO DISPLAY A WARNING
11 ICON AND ACCOMPANYING TEXT UNDER SUBSECTION (E)(2) OF THIS SECTION, THE
12 FOLLOWING TEXT SHALL BE PROMINENTLY, CLEARLY, AND CONSPICUOUSLY
13 DISPLAYED AT THE POINT OF SELECTION:
14 “THE HIGH ADDED SUGARS CONTENT WARNING ICON INDICATES A MENU ITEM
15 WITH A SUGAR CONTENT THAT EXCEEDS HALF THE TOTAL DAILY RECOMMENDED
16 LIMIT. DIETS WITH HIGHER AMOUNTS OF ADDED SUGARS OVER TIME MAY INCREASE
17 THE RISK OF TYPE 2 DIABETES, WEIGHT GAIN, AND TOOTH DECAY.”.
18 (G) ON OR BEFORE JULY 1, 2032, THE DEPARTMENT SHALL SUBMIT A
19 REPORT TO THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY, IN ACCORDANCE WITH § 2–1257 OF THE
20 STATE GOVERNMENT ARTICLE, THAT:
21 (1) REVIEWS ANY EVIDENCE OF THE IMPACT OF THE REQUIREMENTS
22 ESTABLISHED UNDER THIS SECTION, INCLUDING:
23 (I) WHETHER AND TO WHAT EXTENT ANY STANDARD MENU
24 ITEMS WERE REFORMULATED; AND
25 (II) WHETHER AND HOW CONSUMER BEHAVIOR CHANGED; AND
26 (2) RECOMMENDS ANY ADDITIONAL NUTRITION–RELATED MENU
27 WARNING ICONS THAT SHOULD BE CONSIDERED.
28 21–1214.
29 (a) (1) Except as provided in paragraph (2) of this subsection, any person who
30 violates any provision of Subtitle 3 of this title or any rule or regulation adopted under
31 Subtitle 3 of this title is guilty of a misdemeanor and on conviction is subject to:
32 (i) For a first offense, a fine not exceeding $1,000 or imprisonment
33 not exceeding 90 days, or both; and
6 SENATE BILL 866
1 (ii) For a second offense, a fine not exceeding $2,500 or imprisonment
2 not exceeding 1 year, or both.
3 (2) A person who violates § 21–330.1 OR § 21–330.3 of this title is not
4 subject to paragraph (1) of this subsection.
5 (b) In addition to any criminal penalties imposed under this section, a person who
6 violates any provision of Subtitle 3 of this title or any rule or regulation adopted under
7 Subtitle 3 of this title or any term, condition, or limitation of any license or registration
8 issued under Subtitle 3 of this title:
9 (1) Is liable for a civil penalty not exceeding $5,000, to be collected in a civil
10 action in the District Court for any county; and
11 (2) May be enjoined from continuing the violation.
12 (c) Each day on which a violation occurs is a separate violation under this section.
13 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
14 1, 2026.
