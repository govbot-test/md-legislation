CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Local School Systems State Department of Education – Sexual Abuse and
3 Sexual Misconduct – Misconduct Model Response Policy and After–Action
4 Review – Requirements
5 FOR the purpose of requiring the State Department of Education to develop a model sexual
6 abuse and sexual misconduct response policy that includes, at minimum, certain
7 components; requiring each local school system to adopt a certain response policy,
8 provide certain notices in a certain manner, and conduct a certain after–action
9 review under certain circumstances and post a certain link on its website; and
10 generally relating to sexual abuse and sexual misconduct response policies.
11 BY adding to
12 Article – Education
13 Section 4–148
14 Annotated Code of Maryland
15 (2025 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
16 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
17 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
18 Article – Education
19 4–148.

2 HOUSE BILL 1168
1 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION, THE FOLLOWING TERMS HAVE THE MEANINGS
2 INDICATED.
3 (2) “CREDIBLE ALLEGATION” MEANS A SPECIFIC AND PLAUSIBLE
4 REPORT OF SEXUAL MISCONDUCT OR SEXUAL ABUSE, MADE BY A PERSON WITH
5 APPARENT KNOWLEDGE, BY A SCHOOL EMPLOYEE OR CONTRACTOR OR VOLUNTEER
6 THAT IS SUFFICIENT TO WARRANT TEMPORARY PROTECTIVE ACTION BY THE LOCAL
7 SCHOOL SYSTEM.
8 (3) “RESPONSE RESPONSE POLICY” MEANS A SEXUAL ABUSE AND
9 MISCONDUCT RESPONSE POLICY.
10 (4) “SERIOUS INCIDENT” MEANS AN EVENT THAT POSES A
11 SUBSTANTIAL THREAT TO A STUDENT OR STAFF MEMBER’S SAFETY OR SCHOOL
12 ENVIRONMENT AND REQUIRES IMMEDIATE ADMINISTRATIVE OR
13 LAW–ENFORCEMENT RESPONSE, INCLUDING:
14 (I) CREDIBLE ALLEGATIONS OF SEXUAL MISCONDUCT OR
15 SEXUAL ABUSE THAT RESULT IN REMOVAL FROM STUDENT CONTACT FOR MORE
16 THAN 10 DAYS;
17 (II) PHYSICAL ASSAULT;
18 (III) OFFENSES INVOLVING A WEAPON;
19 (IV) THREATS OF VIOLENCE;
20 (V) CHILD ABUSE OR NEGLECT;
21 (VI) EVENTS THAT REQUIRE EMERGENCY RESPONSE; AND
22 (VII) ANY OTHER INCIDENT DESIGNATED BY THE LOCAL SCHOOL
23 SYSTEM AS REQUIRING A FORMAL AFTER–ACTION REVIEW.
24 (B) (1) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL DEVELOP A MODEL SEXUAL ABUSE AND
25 MISCONDUCT RESPONSE POLICY FOR USE BY LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEMS TO RESPOND
26 TO CREDIBLE SCHOOL–RELATED ALLEGATIONS OF SEXUAL ABUSE AND SEXUAL
27 MISCONDUCT.
28 (2) THE MODEL RESPONSE POLICY DEVELOPED UNDER PARAGRAPH
29 (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION SHALL, AT MINIMUM, INCLUDE THE FOLLOWING
30 COMPONENTS:
HOUSE BILL 1168 3
1 (I) A COMMUNICATIONS PLAN;
2 (II) AN E–MAIL AND OTHER ELECTRONIC COMMUNICATION
3 DOCUMENTS RETENTION POLICY;
4 (III) A REQUIREMENT TO LINK TO A CENTRALIZED RESOURCE
5 PLATFORM; AND
6 (IV) AN AFTER–ACTION REVIEW PLAN.
7 (3) THE MODEL RESPONSE POLICY MAY NOT IMPEDE OR
8 COMPROMISE THE ABILITY OF A SCHOOL, A SCHOOL SYSTEM, A LAW ENFORCEMENT
9 AGENCY, OR A PROSECUTOR TO CONDUCT A THOROUGH AND UNBIASED
10 INVESTIGATION INTO AN ALLEGATION OF SEXUAL ABUSE OR SEXUAL MISCONDUCT.
11 (I) A REQUIREMENT THAT A PARENT OR GUARDIAN RECEIVE
12 PROMPT NOTIFICATION THAT THE STUDENT HAS MADE A CREDIBLE ALLEGATION OF
13 A SERIOUS INCIDENT;
14 (II) A COMMUNICATIONS PLAN THAT PROVIDES LOCAL SCHOOL
15 SYSTEMS WITH THE INFORMATION AUTHORIZED TO BE SHARED WITH THE PUBLIC
16 AT EACH STAGE OF THE RESPONSE PROCESS;
17 (III) A REQUIREMENT THAT, SUBJECT TO SUBSECTION (E) OF
18 THIS SECTION, NOTIFICATION OF AN ALLEGATION OF CHILD ABUSE BY AN
19 EMPLOYEE OF THE LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM BE PROVIDED TO THE CHILD’S PARENT
20 OR GUARDIAN AS SOON AS POSSIBLE AND NOT LATER THAN WITHIN 3 SCHOOL DAYS;
21 AND
22 (IV) RECORD RETENTION REQUIREMENTS AND GUIDELINES
23 DEVELOPED IN COLLABORATION WITH THE STATE ARCHIVES, INCLUDING:
24 1. A REQUIREMENT THAT ALL E–MAIL AND ELECTRONIC
25 RECORDS REGARDING A CREDIBLE ALLEGATION OF SEXUAL ABUSE OR SEXUAL
26 MISCONDUCT BY AN EMPLOYEE OF THE LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM BE RETAINED BY
27 THE LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM FOR A MINIMUM OF 3 YEARS;
28 2. PROCEDURES FOR THE MAINTENANCE, SECURITY,
29 AND TRANSFER OF RECORDS; AND
30 3. GUIDELINES REGARDING RETAINING RECORDS FOR
31 CRIMINAL, CIVIL, OR ADMINISTRATIVE INVESTIGATIONS.
4 HOUSE BILL 1168
1 (C) (1) THE COMPONENTS OF THE MODEL RESPONSE POLICY
2 DEVELOPED UNDER SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION SHALL INCLUDE THE
3 INFORMATION REQUIRED UNDER THIS SUBSECTION AND BE DEVELOPED IN
4 COLLABORATION WITH STATE AND LOCAL GOVERNMENTAL ENTITIES, COMMUNITY
5 STAKEHOLDERS, AND ANY OTHER RELEVANT ENTITIES DETERMINED BY THE
6 DEPARTMENT.
7 (2) THE COMMUNICATIONS PLAN SHALL INCLUDE BEST PRACTICES
8 FOR PROVIDING TIMELY, RELEVANT, AND APPROPRIATE INFORMATION THROUGH
9 NOTIFICATION AND RESPONSES TO PARENTS OF AFFECTED STUDENTS AND
10 COMMUNITY STAKEHOLDERS AND LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM CONTACT INFORMATION.
11 (3) THE E–MAIL AND OTHER ELECTRONIC COMMUNICATION
12 DOCUMENTS RETENTION POLICY SHALL INCLUDE REQUIREMENTS FOR THE
13 RETENTION AND MAINTENANCE OF A LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM’S E–MAILS AND OTHER
14 ELECTRONIC COMMUNICATION DOCUMENTS FOR A MINIMUM OF 1 YEAR.
15 (4) A CENTRALIZED, STATEWIDE RESOURCE PLATFORM, DEVELOPED
16 IN ACCORDANCE WITH THE MODEL RESPONSE POLICY, SHALL INCLUDE
17 INFORMATION AND PROVIDE LINKS TO RESOURCES REGARDING CHILD SEXUAL
18 ABUSE AND SEXUAL MISCONDUCT IN A SCHOOL ENVIRONMENT.
19 (5) AN AFTER–ACTION REVIEW PLAN, DEVELOPED IN ACCORDANCE
20 WITH THE MODEL RESPONSE POLICY, SHALL REQUIRE A LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM TO
21 CONDUCT A STRUCTURED, COLLABORATIVE DEBRIEFING PROCESS TO IDENTIFY,
22 ASSESS, EVALUATE, AND RESOLVE ANY ISSUE WITH ANY ACTION TAKEN BY AN
23 ENTITY INVOLVED IN A SCHOOL–RELATED CASE OF SEXUAL ABUSE AND SEXUAL
24 MISCONDUCT TO IMPROVE FUTURE PERFORMANCE.
25 (C) (D) (1) ON OR BEFORE JULY SEPTEMBER 1, 2027, EACH LOCAL
26 SCHOOL SYSTEM SHALL ADOPT A RESPONSE POLICY BASED ON THE MODEL POLICY
27 DEVELOPED BY THE DEPARTMENT UNDER SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION.
28 (2) EACH LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM SHALL POST IN A CONSPICUOUS
29 PLACE ON ITS WEBSITE A LINK TO THE CENTRALIZED RESOURCE PLATFORM
30 DEVELOPED UNDER SUBSECTION (C) OF THIS SECTION.
31 (3) EACH LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM’S RESPONSE POLICY MAY NOT
32 IMPEDE OR COMPROMISE THE ABILITY OF A SCHOOL, THE LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM,
33 A LAW ENFORCEMENT AGENCY, OR A PROSECUTOR TO CONDUCT A THOROUGH AND
34 UNBIASED INVESTIGATION INTO AN ALLEGATION OF SEXUAL ABUSE OR SEXUAL
35 MISCONDUCT.
HOUSE BILL 1168 5
1 (D) EACH LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM SHALL ESTABLISH A TIME–STAMPED
2 WEBPAGE ON ITS WEBSITE REGARDING A SERIOUS INCIDENT THAT:
3 (1) CONFIRMS THE NATURE OF THE INCIDENT;
4 (2) PROVIDES NONIDENTIFYING INFORMATION ABOUT THE STATUS
5 OF THE ONGOING INVESTIGATION;
6 (3) PROVIDES LINKS TO RELEVANT RESOURCES FOR PARENTS AND
7 GUARDIANS FOR COUNSELING AND SUPPORT RESOURCES;
8 (4) PROVIDES AN UPDATE WHEN THERE IS A MATERIAL CHANGE,
9 INCLUDING THE CONCLUSION OF THE INVESTIGATION OR AN ONGOING CRIMINAL
10 CASE; AND
11 (5) CONTAINS SUPPORTING AND NONINVESTIGATIVE INFORMATION
12 ONLY.
13 (E) A LAW ENFORCEMENT AGENCY OR A CHILD PROTECTIVE SERVICES UNIT
14 MAY REQUEST THAT CERTAIN DETAILS OF A SERIOUS INCIDENT BE WITHHELD FROM
15 THE PUBLIC OR THAT PUBLIC COMMUNICATION BE DELAYED.
16 (F) (1) A LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM SHALL NOTIFY PARENTS AND
17 GUARDIANS THAT THE LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM HAS RECEIVED A CREDIBLE
18 ALLEGATION AS SOON AS FEASIBLE AFTER THE LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM IS MADE
19 AWARE OF THE CREDIBLE ALLEGATION.
20 (2) THE NOTICE REQUIRED UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS
21 SUBSECTION SHALL:
22 (I) CONFIRM THAT A CREDIBLE ALLEGATION WAS RECEIVED;
23 (II) INDICATE WHETHER THE EMPLOYEE WAS REMOVED FROM
24 STUDENT CONTACT;
25 (III) DESCRIBE THE SAFETY PRECAUTIONS BEING TAKEN BY THE
26 LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM; AND
27 (IV) LIST REFERRALS TO AVAILABLE COUNSELING AND
28 SUPPORT RESOURCES, IF ANY.
6 HOUSE BILL 1168
1 (3) A LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM MAY NOT DISCLOSE THE NAME OF THE
2 EMPLOYEE UNDER INVESTIGATION IN THE NOTICE SENT TO PARENTS AND
3 GUARDIANS UNDER THIS SUBSECTION.
4 (G) (1) ON THE CONCLUSION OF A CHILD SEXUAL ABUSE OR SEXUAL
5 MISCONDUCT CASE OR A SERIOUS INCIDENT, A LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM SHALL
6 CONDUCT AN AFTER–ACTION REVIEW WITHIN 45 DAYS OF THE CONCLUSION OF THE
7 INCIDENT TO:
8 (I) ANALYZE HOW THE INCIDENT OCCURRED;
9 (II) IDENTIFY SYSTEMIC AND ENVIRONMENTAL CONDITIONS
10 THAT CONTRIBUTED TO THE INCIDENT;
11 (III) IDENTIFY GAPS IN PROCEDURES, COMMUNICATION,
12 SUPERVISION, OR CULTURE;
13 (IV) EVALUATE THE TIMELINESS OF PARENT AND GUARDIAN
14 NOTIFICATION;
15 (V) EXAMINE COORDINATION WITH LAW ENFORCEMENT AND
16 CHILD PROTECTIVE SERVICES;
17 (VI) EVALUATE COMPLIANCE WITH TRANSPARENCY
18 REQUIREMENTS; AND
19 (VII) IDENTIFY AREAS FOR PROCESS IMPROVEMENTS.
20 (2) A WRITTEN REDACTED REPORT OF THE FINDINGS OF THE
21 AFTER–ACTION REVIEW CONDUCTED UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION
22 MAY BE PROVIDED TO THE STATE BOARD AND THE STATE SUPERINTENDENT.
23 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
24 1, 2026.
