A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Education – Posthumous Diploma Awards – Authorized
3 FOR the purpose of authorizing local school systems to award standard high school
4 diplomas, posthumous honorary diplomas, and in memoriam diplomas to certain
5 deceased students, subject to certain requirements; and generally relating to the
6 posthumous awarding of diplomas.
7 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
8 Article – Education
9 Section 7–205(c)
10 Annotated Code of Maryland
11 (2025 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
12 BY adding to
13 Article – Education
14 Section 7–206
15 Annotated Code of Maryland
16 (2025 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
17 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
18 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
19 Article – Education
20 7–205.
21 (c) (1) Each student who graduates from a public high school shall receive the
22 same type of diploma or certificate, regardless of the high school attended or the course
23 taken.
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

2 HOUSE BILL 1274
1 (2) The diploma or certificate shall state that the student has completed a
2 program of studies satisfactorily in accordance with the requirements of the State Board.
3 7–206.
4 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
5 INDICATED.
6 (2) “IN MEMORIAM DIPLOMA” MEANS A CEREMONIAL DIPLOMA
7 RECOGNIZING A STUDENT’S ENROLLMENT AND MEMBERSHIP IN THE SCHOOL
8 COMMUNITY.
9 (3) “POSTHUMOUS HONORARY DIPLOMA” MEANS A CEREMONIAL
10 DIPLOMA HONORING A STUDENT ENROLLED IN SECONDARY EDUCATION.
11 (4) “STANDARD HIGH SCHOOL DIPLOMA” MEANS THE DIPLOMA OR
12 CERTIFICATE AWARDED TO A STUDENT UNDER § 7–205 OF THIS SUBTITLE.
13 (B) A LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM MAY AWARD A STANDARD HIGH SCHOOL
14 DIPLOMA POSTHUMOUSLY TO A DECEASED STUDENT WHO WAS, AT THE TIME OF THE
15 STUDENT’S DEATH:
16 (1) A RESIDENT OF THE COUNTY;
17 (2) ENROLLED IN GRADE 12 IN A PUBLIC SCHOOL;
18 (3) WITHIN A CERTAIN NUMBER OF CREDITS FROM COMPLETING THE
19 CREDITS REQUIRED FOR GRADUATION; AND
20 (4) IN GOOD ACADEMIC STANDING.
21 (C) A LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM MAY AWARD A POSTHUMOUS HONORARY
22 DIPLOMA TO A DECEASED STUDENT WHO WAS, AT THE TIME OF THE STUDENT’S
23 DEATH:
24 (1) A RESIDENT OF THE COUNTY;
25 (2) ENROLLED IN GRADE 11 OR 12 IN A PUBLIC SCHOOL; AND
26 (3) WITHIN A CERTAIN NUMBER OF CREDITS FROM COMPLETING THE
27 CREDITS REQUIRED FOR GRADUATION.
28 (D) A LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM MAY AWARD AN IN MEMORIAM DIPLOMA TO A
29 DECEASED STUDENT WHO WAS, AT THE TIME OF THE STUDENT’S DEATH:
HOUSE BILL 1274 3
1 (1) A RESIDENT OF THE COUNTY; OR
2 (2) ENROLLED IN ANY GRADE FROM KINDERGARTEN THROUGH
3 GRADE 10 IN A PUBLIC SCHOOL.
4 (E) (1) SUBJECT TO PARAGRAPH (2) OF THIS SUBSECTION, A LOCAL
5 SCHOOL SYSTEM SHALL DETERMINE THE NUMBER OF CREDITS A STUDENT IS
6 REQUIRED TO COMPLETE TO BE AWARDED:
7 (I) A STANDARD HIGH SCHOOL DIPLOMA UNDER SUBSECTION
8 (B) OF THIS SECTION; AND
9 (II) A POSTHUMOUS HONORARY DIPLOMA UNDER SUBSECTION
10 (C) OF THIS SECTION.
11 (2) THE NUMBER OF CREDITS A STUDENT IS REQUIRED TO COMPLETE
12 TO BE AWARDED A STANDARD HIGH SCHOOL DIPLOMA SHALL BE SIGNIFICANTLY
13 HIGHER THAN THE NUMBER OF CREDITS REQUIRED FOR A POSTHUMOUS HONORARY
14 DIPLOMA.
15 (F) A LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM SHALL AWARD A DIPLOMA UNDER THIS
16 SECTION ONLY ON WRITTEN REQUEST OF:
17 (1) A PARENT OR LEGAL GUARDIAN OF THE DECEASED STUDENT; OR
18 (2) IF THERE IS NO SURVIVING PARENT OR LEGAL GUARDIAN, THE
19 DECEASED STUDENT’S NEXT OF KIN.
20 (G) (1) EXCEPT FOR A DIPLOMA AWARDED UNDER SUBSECTION (B) OF
21 THIS SECTION, A DIPLOMA AWARDED UNDER THIS SECTION IS CEREMONIAL IN
22 NATURE AND DOES NOT CONFER LEGAL RIGHTS OR STANDARDS ASSOCIATED WITH
23 ACADEMIC COMPLETION.
24 (2) THE AWARDING OF ANY DIPLOMA UNDER THIS SECTION MAY NOT
25 BE INCLUDED IN THE COMPUTATION OF GRADUATION RATES MAINTAINED BY THE
26 COUNTY BOARD OR THE DEPARTMENT.
27 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
28 1, 2026.
