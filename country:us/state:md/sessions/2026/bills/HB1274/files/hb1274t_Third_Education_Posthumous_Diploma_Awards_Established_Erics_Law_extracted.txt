1 AN ACT concerning
2 Education – Posthumous Diploma Awards – Authorized Established
3 (Eric’s Law)
4 FOR the purpose of authorizing requiring local school systems to award standard high
5 school diplomas, posthumous honorary diplomas, and in memoriam diplomas to
6 certain deceased students, subject to certain requirements; and generally relating to
7 the posthumous awarding of diplomas.

9 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
10 INDICATED.
11 (2) “IN MEMORIAM DIPLOMA” MEANS A CEREMONIAL DIPLOMA
12 RECOGNIZING A STUDENT’S ENROLLMENT AND MEMBERSHIP IN THE SCHOOL
13 COMMUNITY.
14 (3) “POSTHUMOUS HONORARY DIPLOMA” MEANS A CEREMONIAL
15 DIPLOMA HONORING A STUDENT ENROLLED IN SECONDARY EDUCATION.
16 (4) “STANDARD HIGH SCHOOL DIPLOMA” MEANS THE DIPLOMA OR
17 CERTIFICATE AWARDED TO A STUDENT UNDER § 7–205 OF THIS SUBTITLE.
18 (B) A LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM MAY AWARD A STANDARD HIGH SCHOOL
19 DIPLOMA POSTHUMOUSLY TO A DECEASED STUDENT WHO WAS, AT THE TIME OF THE
20 STUDENT’S DEATH:
21 (1) A RESIDENT OF THE COUNTY;
22 (2) ENROLLED IN GRADE 12 IN A PUBLIC SCHOOL;
23 (3) WITHIN A CERTAIN NUMBER OF CREDITS FROM COMPLETING THE
24 CREDITS REQUIRED FOR GRADUATION; AND
25 (4) IN GOOD ACADEMIC STANDING.
26 (C) A LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM MAY AWARD A POSTHUMOUS HONORARY
27 DIPLOMA TO A DECEASED STUDENT WHO WAS, AT THE TIME OF THE STUDENT’S
28 DEATH:
29 (1) A RESIDENT OF THE COUNTY;
