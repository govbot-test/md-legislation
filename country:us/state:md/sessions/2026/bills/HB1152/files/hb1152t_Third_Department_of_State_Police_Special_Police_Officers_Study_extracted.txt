CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Department of State Police – Special Police Officers – Study
3 FOR the purpose of requiring the Department of State Police to conduct a certain study
4 regarding special police officers; and generally relating to special police officers.
5 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
6 That:
7 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
8 (2) “Department” means the Department of State Police.
9 (3) “Special police officer” has the meaning stated in § 3–301 of the Public
10 Safety Article.
11 (b) The Department shall study and make recommendations on the employment
12 and use of special police officers, including:
13 (1) the commissioning process;
14 (2) employment requirements;
15 (3) special police operations, training, and equipment;
16 (4) the jurisdiction of special police officers; and

1 (5) regulatory oversight to suspend or revoke the commission of special
2 police officers.
3 (c) In conducting the study required under subsection (b) of this section, the
4 Department shall:
5 (1) survey and document the licensing of and regulatory requirements for
6 special police officers and the equivalent in surrounding states; and
7 (2) analyze best practices for the use of special police officers by public and
8 private employers in the State and surrounding states by considering:
9 (i) the number of special police officers in the State and surrounding
10 states;
11 (ii) mandated training for special police officers, including firearms
12 training;
13 (iii) the impact of the use of special police officers on public safety;
14 and
15 (iv) recorded complaints.
16 (d) In carrying out the study, the Department shall consult with:
17 (1) special police officers;
18 (2) employers of special police officers, including local or county
19 governments that employ special police officers;
20 (3) a representative of the Maryland Chiefs of Police Association;
21 (4) a representative of the Maryland Sheriffs’ Association;
22 (5) a representative of the Maryland State Police Licensing Division;
23 (6) a representative of the Maryland State’s Attorneys’ Association;
24 (7) a representative of the Office of the Secretary of State; and
25 (8) a representative of a security training academy that provides training
26 for special police officers.
27 (e) On or before December 1, 2026, the Department shall submit a report of its
28 findings and recommendations to the Governor and, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the
29 State Government Article, the General Assembly.
HOUSE BILL 1152 3
1 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
2 1, 2026.
