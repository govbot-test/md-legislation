WES MOORE, Governor Ch. 867
Chapter 867
AN ACT concerning
Maryland Medical Assistance Program – Registered Behavior Technicians –
Reimbursement
FOR the purpose of requiring the Maryland Department of Health to provide
reimbursement for applied behavior analysis services provided by registered
behavior technicians under the Maryland Medical Assistance Program; prohibiting,
subject to a certain exception, the Department from requiring as a condition of
reimbursement that an individual receive a certain certification to provide applied
behavior analysis services as a registered behavior technician to Program recipients;
and generally relating to the Maryland Medical Assistance Program and applied
behavior analysis services.
BY adding to
Article – Health – General

Section 2:
Section 15–114.2
Annotated Code of Maryland
(2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

Section 3:

That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
Article – Health – General
15–114.2.
(A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
INDICATED.
(2) (I) “APPLIED BEHAVIOR ANALYSIS” MEANS THE DESIGN,
IMPLEMENTATION, AND EVALUATION OF ENVIRONMENTAL MODIFICATIONS USING
BEHAVIORAL STIMULI AND CONSEQUENCES TO PRODUCE SOCIALLY SIGNIFICANT
IMPROVEMENTS IN HUMAN BEHAVIOR.
(II) “APPLIED BEHAVIOR ANALYSIS” INCLUDES THE DIRECT
OBSERVATIONS, MEASUREMENT, AND FUNCTIONAL ANALYSIS OF THE RELATIONS
BETWEEN ENVIRONMENT AND BEHAVIOR.
(3) “AUTISM SPECTRUM DISORDER” MEANS A GROUP OF
DEVELOPMENTAL DISORDERS:

(II) CHARACTERIZED BY RESTRICTED, REPETITIVE PATTERNS
OF BEHAVIOR, INTERESTS, OR ACTIVITIES; AND
(III) WHOSE SYMPTOMS CAUSE CLINICALLY SIGNIFICANT
IMPAIRMENT IN SOCIAL, OCCUPATIONAL, OR OTHER IMPORTANT AREAS OF

(II) MEETS THE REQUIREMENTS FOR PROVIDING BEHAVIOR
ANALYSIS SERVICES UNDER THE PROGRAM ESTABLISHED BY THE DEPARTMENT IN
REGULATION.
(4) (5) “BOARD CERTIFIED BEHAVIOR ANALYST” MEANS A
LICENSED PROFESSIONAL WHO:
(I) RENDERS AND PROVIDES DIRECTION FOR APPLIED
BEHAVIOR ANALYSIS SERVICES THAT ARE PROVIDED TO INDIVIDUALS WITH AUTISM
SPECTRUM DISORDER; AND
(II) HAS OBTAINED A BOARD CERTIFIED BEHAVIOR ANALYST
CERTIFICATION.

MEANS A LICENSED PROFESSIONAL WHO:

(I) RENDERS AND PROVIDES DIRECTION FOR APPLIED
BEHAVIOR ANALYSIS TREATMENTS THAT ARE PROVIDED TO INDIVIDUALS WITH
AUTISM SPECTRUM DISORDER; AND
(II) HAS OBTAINED A BOARD CERTIFIED BEHAVIOR
ANALYST–DOCTORAL CERTIFICATION.
(6) “REGISTERED BEHAVIOR TECHNICIAN” MEANS A
PARAPROFESSIONAL WHO DELIVERS APPLIED BEHAVIOR ANALYSIS SERVICES THAT
ARE PROVIDED TO INDIVIDUALS WITH AUTISM SPECTRUM DISORDER UNDER THE
SUPERVISION OF:
(I) A LICENSED PSYCHOLOGIST;
(II) A BOARD CERTIFIED BEHAVIOR ANALYST–DOCTORAL; OR
(III) A BOARD CERTIFIED BEHAVIOR ANALYST.
(B) (1) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL PROVIDE REIMBURSEMENT FOR
APPLIED BEHAVIOR ANALYSIS SERVICES PROVIDED BY REGISTERED BEHAVIOR
TECHNICIANS TO PROGRAM RECIPIENTS WITH AUTISM SPECTRUM DISORDER.
(2) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED UNDER PARAGRAPH (3) OF THIS
SUBSECTION, THE DEPARTMENT MAY NOT REQUIRE AS A CONDITION OF
REIMBURSEMENT UNDER THE PROGRAM THAT AN INDIVIDUAL RECEIVE A
CERTIFICATION FROM THE BEHAVIOR ANALYST CERTIFICATION BOARD OR OTHER
A CREDENTIALING ENTITY RECOGNIZED BY THE DEPARTMENT TO PROVIDE APPLIED
BEHAVIOR ANALYSIS SERVICES AS A REGISTERED BEHAVIOR TECHNICIAN.
(3) THE DEPARTMENT MAY REQUIRE AS A CONDITION OF
REIMBURSEMENT UNDER THE PROGRAM THAT AN INDIVIDUAL OBTAIN A CURRENT
CERTIFICATION ISSUED BY THE BEHAVIOR ANALYST CERTIFICATION BOARD OR
OTHER A CREDENTIALING ENTITY RECOGNIZED BY THE DEPARTMENT TO PROVIDE
APPLIED BEHAVIOR ANALYSIS SERVICES AS A REGISTERED BEHAVIOR TECHNICIAN
WITHIN 150 DAYS AFTER THE INDIVIDUAL BEGINS PROVIDING APPLIED BEHAVIOR
ANALYSIS SERVICES TO PROGRAM RECIPIENTS AS A REGISTERED BEHAVIOR
TECHNICIAN AND MAINTAIN THE CERTIFICATION.
(C) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ADOPT REGULATIONS NECESSARY TO CARRY
OUT THIS SECTION.

SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That:
(a) If, on or before June 30, 2027, the U.S. Department of Health and Human

Assembly, shall be abrogated and of no further force and effect.
(b) The Maryland Department of Health shall notify the Department of Legislative
Services within 5 days after receiving the advisement or notice from the U.S. Department of
Health and Human Services described under subsection (a) of this section.
