A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Maryland Medical Assistance Program – Registered Behavior Technicians –
3 Reimbursement
4 FOR the purpose of requiring the Maryland Department of Health to provide
5 reimbursement for applied behavior analysis services provided by registered
6 behavior technicians under the Maryland Medical Assistance Program; prohibiting,
7 subject to a certain exception, the Department from requiring as a condition of
8 reimbursement that an individual receive a certain certification to provide applied
9 behavior analysis services as a registered behavior technician to Program recipients;
10 and generally relating to the Maryland Medical Assistance Program and applied
11 behavior analysis services.
12 BY adding to
13 Article – Health – General
14 Section 15–114.2
15 Annotated Code of Maryland
16 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
17 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
18 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
19 Article – Health – General
20 15–114.2.
21 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
22 INDICATED.
23 (2) (I) “APPLIED BEHAVIOR ANALYSIS” MEANS THE DESIGN,
24 IMPLEMENTATION, AND EVALUATION OF ENVIRONMENTAL MODIFICATIONS USING
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

2 SENATE BILL 898
1 BEHAVIORAL STIMULI AND CONSEQUENCES TO PRODUCE SOCIALLY SIGNIFICANT
2 IMPROVEMENTS IN HUMAN BEHAVIOR.
3 (II) “APPLIED BEHAVIOR ANALYSIS” INCLUDES THE DIRECT
4 OBSERVATIONS, MEASUREMENT, AND FUNCTIONAL ANALYSIS OF THE RELATIONS
5 BETWEEN ENVIRONMENT AND BEHAVIOR.
6 (3) “AUTISM SPECTRUM DISORDER” MEANS A GROUP OF
7 DEVELOPMENTAL DISORDERS:
8 (I) CHARACTERIZED BY PERSISTENT DEFICITS IN SOCIAL
9 COMMUNICATION AND SOCIAL INTERACTION ACROSS MULTIPLE CONTEXTS;
10 (II) CHARACTERIZED BY RESTRICTED, REPETITIVE PATTERNS
11 OF BEHAVIOR, INTERESTS, OR ACTIVITIES; AND
12 (III) WHOSE SYMPTOMS CAUSE CLINICALLY SIGNIFICANT
13 IMPAIRMENT IN SOCIAL, OCCUPATIONAL, OR OTHER IMPORTANT AREAS OF
14 CURRENT FUNCTIONING.
15 (4) “BOARD CERTIFIED BEHAVIOR ANALYST” MEANS A LICENSED
16 PROFESSIONAL WHO:
17 (I) RENDERS AND PROVIDES DIRECTION FOR APPLIED
18 BEHAVIOR ANALYSIS SERVICES THAT ARE PROVIDED TO INDIVIDUALS WITH AUTISM
19 SPECTRUM DISORDER; AND
20 (II) HAS OBTAINED A BOARD CERTIFIED BEHAVIOR ANALYST
21 CERTIFICATION.
22 (5) “BOARD CERTIFIED BEHAVIOR ANALYST–DOCTORAL” MEANS A
23 LICENSED PROFESSIONAL WHO:
24 (I) RENDERS AND PROVIDES DIRECTION FOR APPLIED
25 BEHAVIOR ANALYSIS TREATMENTS THAT ARE PROVIDED TO INDIVIDUALS WITH
26 AUTISM SPECTRUM DISORDER; AND
27 (II) HAS OBTAINED A BOARD CERTIFIED BEHAVIOR
28 ANALYST–DOCTORAL CERTIFICATION.
29 (6) “REGISTERED BEHAVIOR TECHNICIAN” MEANS A
30 PARAPROFESSIONAL WHO DELIVERS APPLIED BEHAVIOR ANALYSIS SERVICES THAT
SENATE BILL 898 3
1 ARE PROVIDED TO INDIVIDUALS WITH AUTISM SPECTRUM DISORDER UNDER THE
2 SUPERVISION OF:
3 (I) A LICENSED PSYCHOLOGIST;
4 (II) A BOARD CERTIFIED BEHAVIOR ANALYST–DOCTORAL; OR
5 (III) A BOARD CERTIFIED BEHAVIOR ANALYST.
6 (B) (1) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL PROVIDE REIMBURSEMENT FOR
7 APPLIED BEHAVIOR ANALYSIS SERVICES PROVIDED BY REGISTERED BEHAVIOR
8 TECHNICIANS TO PROGRAM RECIPIENTS WITH AUTISM SPECTRUM DISORDER.
9 (2) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED UNDER PARAGRAPH (3) OF THIS
10 SUBSECTION, THE DEPARTMENT MAY NOT REQUIRE AS A CONDITION OF
11 REIMBURSEMENT UNDER THE PROGRAM THAT AN INDIVIDUAL RECEIVE A
12 CERTIFICATION FROM THE BEHAVIOR ANALYST CERTIFICATION BOARD OR OTHER
13 CREDENTIALING ENTITY TO PROVIDE APPLIED BEHAVIOR ANALYSIS SERVICES AS A
14 REGISTERED BEHAVIOR TECHNICIAN.
15 (3) THE DEPARTMENT MAY REQUIRE AS A CONDITION OF
16 REIMBURSEMENT UNDER THE PROGRAM THAT AN INDIVIDUAL OBTAIN A CURRENT
17 CERTIFICATION ISSUED BY THE BEHAVIOR ANALYST CERTIFICATION BOARD OR
18 OTHER CREDENTIALING ENTITY TO PROVIDE APPLIED BEHAVIOR ANALYSIS
19 SERVICES AS A REGISTERED BEHAVIOR TECHNICIAN WITHIN 150 DAYS AFTER THE
20 INDIVIDUAL BEGINS PROVIDING APPLIED BEHAVIOR ANALYSIS SERVICES TO
21 PROGRAM RECIPIENTS AS A REGISTERED BEHAVIOR TECHNICIAN AND MAINTAIN
22 THE CERTIFICATION.
23 (C) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ADOPT REGULATIONS NECESSARY TO CARRY
24 OUT THIS SECTION.
25 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall be construed to
26 apply only prospectively and may not be applied or interpreted to have any effect on or
27 application to any individual who provides applied behavior analysis services before the
28 effective date of this Act.
29 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
30 October 1, 2026.
