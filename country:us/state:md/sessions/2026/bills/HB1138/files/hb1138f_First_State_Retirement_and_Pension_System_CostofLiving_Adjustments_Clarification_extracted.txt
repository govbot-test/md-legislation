8 (c) (1) Except as provided in paragraph (2) of this subsection, and subject to
9 subsection (f) of this section, to determine the cost–of–living rate used to adjust an
10 allowance in a fiscal year, the Board of Trustees shall:
11 (i) subtract the Consumer Price Index for the calendar year ending
12 December 31 in the second preceding fiscal year from the Consumer Price Index for the
13 calendar year ending December 31 in the preceding fiscal year; and
14 (ii) divide the amount determined under item (i) of this paragraph
15 by the Consumer Price Index for the calendar year ending December 31 in the second
16 preceding fiscal year.
17 (2) The cost–of–living rate used to adjust an allowance in a fiscal year may
18 not exceed any cost–of–living rate cap that is applicable to the allowance under § 29–404(c),
19 § 29–405(c), § 29–406(c), or § 29–408(c) of this subtitle.
20 (d) (1) A cost–of–living adjustment payable in a fiscal year shall be determined
21 as provided in this subsection.
22 (2) For a compound cost–of–living adjustment, the cost–of–living
23 adjustment as of July 1 of a fiscal year shall be determined by multiplying the
24 cost–of–living rate determined in subsection (c) of this section by the sum of:
25 (i) the initial allowance; and
26 (ii) the accumulated cost–of–living adjustment amount.
27 (3) For a simple cost–of–living adjustment, the cost–of–living adjustment
28 as of July 1 of a fiscal year shall be determined by multiplying the cost–of–living rate
29 determined in subsection (c) of this section by the initial allowance.
