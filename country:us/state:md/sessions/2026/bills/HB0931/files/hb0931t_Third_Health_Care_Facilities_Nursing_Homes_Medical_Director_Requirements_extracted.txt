CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Health Care Facilities – Nursing Homes – Medical Director Requirements
3 FOR the purpose of requiring a physician, in order to be designated as a medical director
4 of a nursing home, to hold a certain medical director certification or be making active
5 progress toward obtaining the certification; requiring the Maryland Department of
6 Health to establish and maintain an online directory of nursing home medical
7 directors; each nursing home to publish on the nursing home’s website certain
8 information for the medical director of the nursing home; and generally relating to
9 nursing home medical directors.

1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – Health – General
4 19–1401.
5 (a) In this subtitle the following words have the meanings indicated.
6 (e) (1) “Nursing home” means a facility that offers nonacute inpatient care to
7 patients suffering from a disease, chronic illness, condition, disability of advanced age, or
8 terminal disease requiring maximal nursing care without continuous hospital services and
9 who require medical services and nursing services rendered by or under the supervision of
10 a licensed nurse together with convalescent, restorative, or rehabilitative services.
11 (2) “Nursing home” does not mean a facility offering domiciliary care or
12 personal care as those terms are defined in § 19–301 of this title.
13 19–1411.
14 (a) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
15 INDICATED.
16 (2) “MEDICAL DIRECTOR” MEANS THE PHYSICIAN DESIGNATED BY A
17 NURSING HOME TO OVERSEE MEDICAL CARE AND CLINICAL POLICIES AT THE
18 NURSING HOME IN ACCORDANCE WITH FEDERAL AND STATE LAWS AND
19 REGULATIONS.
20 (3) “MEDICAL DIRECTOR CERTIFICATION” MEANS:
21 (I) A CERTIFIED MEDICAL DIRECTOR (CMD) CERTIFICATION
22 ISSUED BY THE POST–ACUTE AND LONG–TERM CARE MEDICAL ASSOCIATION
23 (PALTMED); OR
24 (II) A CREDENTIAL EQUIVALENT TO A CMD CERTIFICATION
25 APPROVED BY THE DEPARTMENT.
26 (B) (1) (I) Each nursing home shall designate a physician WHO MEETS
27 THE REQUIREMENTS OF SUBSECTION (C)(1) OF THIS SECTION to serve as medical
28 director.
29 (II) A PHYSICIAN MAY NOT BE A DESIGNATED MEDICAL
30 DIRECTOR AT MORE THAN TWO LICENSED NURSING HOMES.

1 (E) (1) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ESTABLISH AND MAINTAIN A PUBLICLY
2 ACCESSIBLE ONLINE DIRECTORY OF NURSING HOME MEDICAL DIRECTORS IN THE
3 STATE THAT INCLUDES EACH EACH NURSING HOME SHALL PUBLISH ON THE

7 (II) (2) PROFESSIONAL CREDENTIALS; AND
8 (III) (3) CERTIFICATION STATUS.
9 (2) EACH NURSING HOME LICENSED UNDER THIS SUBTITLE SHALL
10 SUBMIT THE NAME OF THE DESIGNATED MEDICAL DIRECTOR TO THE DEPARTMENT

14 SECTION, INCLUDING REGULATIONS THAT ESTABLISH:
15 (1) THE EDUCATIONAL AND EXPERIENTIAL REQUIREMENTS FOR A

17 CERTIFICATION; AND
18 (2) THE PROCESS FOR APPROVING A MEDICAL DIRECTOR
19 CERTIFICATION.
