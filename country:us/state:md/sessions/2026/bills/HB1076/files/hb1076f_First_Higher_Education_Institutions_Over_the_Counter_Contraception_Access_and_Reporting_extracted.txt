A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Higher Education Institutions – Over–the–Counter Contraception – Access and
3 Reporting
4 FOR the purpose of requiring each public senior higher education institution and
5 community college to submit a certain report on contraception access to the
6 Maryland Higher Education Commission each year; requiring the Commission to
7 submit a certain report on contraception access to the General Assembly each year;
8 requiring each community college to provide students with access to all methods of
9 over–the–counter contraception; and generally relating to public senior higher
10 education institutions and community colleges and contraception access
11 requirements.
12 BY adding to
13 Article – Education
14 Section 11–407.2
15 Annotated Code of Maryland
16 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
17 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
18 Article – Education
19 Section 16–111
20 Annotated Code of Maryland
21 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
22 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
23 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
24 Article – Education
25 11–407.2.

15 NOT INCLUDE:
16 1. THE UNIVERSITY OF MARYLAND CENTER FOR

18 2. THE UNIVERSITY OF MARYLAND GLOBAL CAMPUS.

20 THEREAFTER, ON A FORM PROVIDED BY THE COMMISSION, EACH PUBLIC SENIOR
21 HIGHER EDUCATION INSTITUTION AND COMMUNITY COLLEGE SHALL SUBMIT A
22 REPORT TO THE COMMISSION ON THE PROVISION OF OVER–THE–COUNTER

24 (1) HOW STUDENTS ARE PROVIDED ACCESS TO OVER–THE–COUNTER
25 CONTRACEPTION ON CAMPUS;

2 PROVIDED ON CAMPUS; AND

8 THE COMMISSION SHALL REPORT TO THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY, IN ACCORDANCE

10 PROVIDED UNDER SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION.
11 16–111.
12 (a) In this section, “over–the–counter contraception” means a contraceptive drug
13 or device approved by the federal Food and Drug Administration that can be obtained for
14 use without a prescription.
15 (b) (1) On or before August 1, 2025, each community college, in consultation
16 with students and student organizations, shall develop and implement a plan to provide
17 students with access to ALL METHODS OF over–the–counter contraception.
18 (2) The plan under paragraph (1) of this subsection shall include access to
19 over–the–counter contraception through:
20 (i) The student health center;
21 (ii) Retail establishments on campus;
22 (iii) Vending machines; or
23 (iv) Any other method that provides all students access when on
24 campus.
25 (c) [(1) On or before September 1, 2025, and each September 1 thereafter, on a
26 form provided by the Commission, each community college shall submit a report to the
27 Commission on implementation of the plan required under this section, including:
28 (i) How students are provided access to over–the–counter
29 contraception on campus;
30 (ii) Available information regarding the amount of over–the–counter
31 contraception provided on campus; and
4 HOUSE BILL 1076
1 (iii) A description of how the community college has consulted with
2 students and student organizations to develop and implement the plan under this section.
3 (2) On or before October 1, 2025, and each October 1 thereafter, the
4 Commission shall report to the General Assembly, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State
5 Government Article, on the information collected from the community colleges under
6 paragraph (1) of this subsection.
7 (d)] The Maryland Department of Health, on request, may provide assistance to a
8 community college in:
9 (1) Developing a plan under this section; and
10 (2) Consulting and collaborating with organizations with expertise in
11 providing access to over–the–counter contraception through vending machines.
12 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
13 1, 2026.
