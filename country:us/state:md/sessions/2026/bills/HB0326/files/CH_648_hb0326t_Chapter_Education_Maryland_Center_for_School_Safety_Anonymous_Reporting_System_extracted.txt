WES MOORE, Governor Ch. 648
Chapter 648
(House Bill 326)
AN ACT concerning
Education – Maryland Center for School Safety – Anonymous Reporting System
FOR the purpose of requiring the Maryland Center for School Safety to develop and
administer an anonymous reporting system; requiring the Center to develop
operational guidelines and procedures for the anonymous reporting system and to
provide certain training; requiring each local school system to participate in the
anonymous reporting system, subject to a certain exception; requiring a certain
custodian of records to deny inspection of information and materials related to the
anonymous reporting system; and generally relating to the school safety anonymous
reporting system.

7–1514.
(A) (1) THE CENTER SHALL DEVELOP AND ADMINISTER AN ANONYMOUS
REPORTING SYSTEM.
(2) THE ANONYMOUS REPORTING SYSTEM SHALL:
(I) COORDINATE THE RECEIPT OF INFORMATION RELATING TO
A SCHOOL OR STUDENT SAFETY CONCERN IN A MANNER THAT MAINTAINS THE
ANONYMITY OF THE INDIVIDUAL REPORTING THE INFORMATION; AND
(II) PROVIDE A MECHANISM FOR ANY REPORTED INFORMATION
TO BE FORWARDED TO SCHOOL OFFICIALS OR OTHER APPROPRIATE RESPONSE
PERSONNEL.
(3) THE CENTER SHALL:
(I) DEVELOP OPERATIONAL GUIDELINES AND PROCEDURES
FOR THE ANONYMOUS REPORTING SYSTEM; AND
(II) PROVIDE TRAINING SESSIONS FOR SCHOOL PERSONNEL ON
NAVIGATING THE ANONYMOUS REPORTING SYSTEM.
(B) (1) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN PARAGRAPH (2) OF THIS SUBSECTION,
BEGINNING JULY 1, 2027, EACH LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM SHALL PARTICIPATE IN THE
ANONYMOUS REPORTING SYSTEM DEVELOPED UNDER THIS SECTION.
(2) (I) A LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM MAY CHOOSE NOT TO
PARTICIPATE IN THE ANONYMOUS REPORTING SYSTEM ONLY IF THE LOCAL SCHOOL
SYSTEM, ON OR BEFORE JULY 1, 2027, OPERATES AND MAKES AVAILABLE TO THE

Article – General Provisions
4–314.1.
(a) Except as provided in subsection (b) of this section, a custodian shall deny
inspection of any record disclosing:
(1) a safety evaluation or school emergency plan developed under § 7–1510
of the Education Article;
(2) an emergency response policy developed under § 7–1509 of the
Education Article and § 3–520 of the Public Safety Article;
(3) guidelines for school resource officers and supplemental coverage by
local law enforcement agencies developed by the Maryland Center for School Safety under
§ 7–1508 of the Education Article;
(4) a plan to implement the Maryland Center for School Safety’s guidelines
adopted by a local school system under § 7–1508 of the Education Article; [or]
(5) any school mapping data produced under the School Mapping Data
Program in accordance with § 7–1510.1 of the Education Article; OR
(6) INFORMATION AND MATERIALS RELATED TO THE ANONYMOUS
REPORTING SYSTEM DEVELOPED UNDER § 7–1514 OF THE EDUCATION ARTICLE.
SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
1, 2026.
