1 AN ACT concerning
2 Education – Maryland Center for School Safety – Anonymous Reporting System
3 FOR the purpose of requiring the Maryland Center for School Safety to develop and
4 administer an anonymous reporting system; requiring the Center to develop
5 operational guidelines and procedures for the anonymous reporting system and to
6 provide certain training; requiring each local school system to participate in the
7 anonymous reporting system, subject to a certain exception; requiring a certain
8 custodian of records to deny inspection of information and materials related to the
9 anonymous reporting system; and generally relating to the school safety anonymous
10 reporting system.

21 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
22 Article – General Provisions

1 Section 4–314.1(a)
2 Annotated Code of Maryland
3 (2019 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
4 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
5 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
6 Article – Education
7 7–1502.
8 (g) The Center shall perform the following functions and duties:
9 (1) Provide technical assistance and consultation to local school systems,
10 State and local government, and community organizations on best practices for safe schools
11 and violence prevention;
12 (2) Develop a website containing a searchable database of definitive
13 research, books, videos, white papers, speakers, websites, and other school safety resources;
14 (3) Develop criteria that may be applied consistently and uniformly in local
15 school systems for coding unsafe incidents and serious or violent offenses;
16 (4) Research and recommend the use of common assessment tools to be
17 used to identify specific problems and needs of schools and neighborhoods to facilitate
18 intervention before assessed findings become problematic;
19 (5) Assist local school systems to conduct a thorough assessment of their
20 school safety data, school building layouts, and use of human resources for monitoring
21 purposes to determine the need for:
22 (i) Surveillance and other security technology; and
23 (ii) Innovations to maximize the use of human resources to monitor
24 activity and influence positive relationship building;
25 (6) Maintain and maximize relationships with emergency responders, law
26 enforcement personnel, parents, and other emergency preparedness stakeholders to ensure
27 seamless execution in an emergency event, including:
28 (i) Consolidate resources among stakeholders to maximize support
29 and secure necessary skills to ensure emergency plan implementation;
30 (ii) Conduct collaborative training and preparation exercises; and
31 (iii) Identify improvements and ensure nonduplication of effort in
32 emergency response procedures;
HOUSE BILL 326 3
1 (7) Provide safety information on traveling to and from school to parents
2 and students twice a year that includes data related to bus and pedestrian safety, strategies
3 for ensuring personal safety, efforts of the local school system or school to improve safety,
4 and information on the available options for reporting incidents or concerns;
5 (8) Utilize and update an existing clearinghouse of law enforcement
6 resources that are available to support school safety to ensure that it includes information
7 regarding the purpose and process for accessing available funding;
8 (9) Assist local school systems to improve and monitor traffic control
9 measures in the immediate vicinity of schools to reduce the potential for pedestrian and
10 vehicle accidents;
11 (10) Assist the Department to evaluate and update current data systems to
12 ensure they are best suited for providing useful information on school safety issues;
13 (11) Assist local school systems to monitor local school system and
14 individual school behavior data to ensure fairness in the application of consequences for
15 student misbehavior;
16 (12) Assist the Department and local school systems:
17 (i) To prepare an annual report that combines multiple school safety
18 data systems into one format for public review; and
19 (ii) To incorporate new data points into existing data collection
20 systems;
21 (13) Assist in the development of safety and security criteria for the design
22 and operation of school facilities;
23 (14) Assist local school systems to identify resources and implement training
24 for students and parents about relationship violence, identifying the signs of unhealthy
25 relationships, and preventing relationship violence;
26 (15) Provide technical assistance to local school systems in the review of
27 safety and security audits and the implementation of improvements in school facilities;
28 (16) Analyze data on school resource officers and develop guidelines and
29 training for local school systems as required under § 7–1508 of this subtitle;
30 (17) Certify school safety coordinators as required under § 7–1508 of this
31 subtitle;
4 HOUSE BILL 326
1 (18) Submit to the General Assembly and the Governor a summary of
2 reports on school resource officer and local law enforcement agency coverage in public
3 schools as required under § 7–1508 of this subtitle;
4 (19) Consult with local school systems on safety evaluations developed
5 under § 7–1510 of this subtitle;
6 (20) Review and comment on school emergency plans developed under §
7 7–1510 of this subtitle;
8 (21) Report to the General Assembly and the Governor on life–threatening
9 incidents as required under § 7–1510 of this subtitle;
10 (22) Collaborate with an institution of higher education or a designated
11 federal or national research entity to study the effectiveness and psychological impact of
12 active shooter safety drills or trainings by researching and analyzing the data collected
13 from local school systems in accordance with § 7–1509(f) of this subtitle;
14 (23) On or before October 1, 2024, identify best practices for active shooter
15 safety drills or trainings, and periodically update those identifications using the data
16 collected from local school systems in accordance with § 7–1509(f) of this subtitle; [and]
17 (24) Administer the Secure Schools Emergency Response Grant Program
18 under § 7–1513 of this subtitle; AND
19 (25) DEVELOP AND ADMINISTER THE ANONYMOUS REPORTING
20 SYSTEM IN ACCORDANCE WITH § 7–1514 OF THIS SUBTITLE.
21 7–1514.
22 (A) (1) THE CENTER SHALL DEVELOP AND ADMINISTER AN ANONYMOUS
23 REPORTING SYSTEM.
24 (2) THE ANONYMOUS REPORTING SYSTEM SHALL:
25 (I) COORDINATE THE RECEIPT OF INFORMATION RELATING TO
26 A SCHOOL OR STUDENT SAFETY CONCERN IN A MANNER THAT MAINTAINS THE
27 ANONYMITY OF THE INDIVIDUAL REPORTING THE INFORMATION; AND
28 (II) PROVIDE A MECHANISM FOR ANY REPORTED INFORMATION
29 TO BE FORWARDED TO SCHOOL OFFICIALS OR OTHER APPROPRIATE RESPONSE
30 PERSONNEL.
31 (3) THE CENTER SHALL:
HOUSE BILL 326 5
1 (I) DEVELOP OPERATIONAL GUIDELINES AND PROCEDURES
2 FOR THE ANONYMOUS REPORTING SYSTEM; AND
3 (II) PROVIDE TRAINING SESSIONS FOR SCHOOL PERSONNEL ON
4 NAVIGATING THE ANONYMOUS REPORTING SYSTEM.
5 (B) (1) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN PARAGRAPH (2) OF THIS SUBSECTION,
6 BEGINNING JULY 1, 2027, EACH LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM SHALL PARTICIPATE IN THE
7 ANONYMOUS REPORTING SYSTEM DEVELOPED UNDER THIS SECTION.
8 (2) (I) A LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM MAY CHOOSE NOT TO
9 PARTICIPATE IN THE ANONYMOUS REPORTING SYSTEM ONLY IF THE LOCAL SCHOOL
10 SYSTEM, ON OR BEFORE JULY 1, 2027, OPERATES AND MAKES AVAILABLE TO THE
11 PUBLIC A REPORTING SYSTEM THAT IS SUBSTANTIALLY SIMILAR TO THE
12 ANONYMOUS REPORTING SYSTEM DEVELOPED UNDER THIS SECTION.
13 (II) A LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM THAT CHOOSES NOT TO
14 PARTICIPATE IN THE ANONYMOUS REPORTING SYSTEM SHALL PROVIDE WRITTEN
15 NOTICE TO THE CENTER ON OR BEFORE DECEMBER 1, 2026.
16 Article – General Provisions
17 4–314.1.
18 (a) Except as provided in subsection (b) of this section, a custodian shall deny
19 inspection of any record disclosing:
20 (1) a safety evaluation or school emergency plan developed under § 7–1510
21 of the Education Article;
22 (2) an emergency response policy developed under § 7–1509 of the
23 Education Article and § 3–520 of the Public Safety Article;
24 (3) guidelines for school resource officers and supplemental coverage by
25 local law enforcement agencies developed by the Maryland Center for School Safety under
26 § 7–1508 of the Education Article;
27 (4) a plan to implement the Maryland Center for School Safety’s guidelines
28 adopted by a local school system under § 7–1508 of the Education Article; [or]
29 (5) any school mapping data produced under the School Mapping Data
30 Program in accordance with § 7–1510.1 of the Education Article; OR
31 (6) INFORMATION AND MATERIALS RELATED TO THE ANONYMOUS
32 REPORTING SYSTEM DEVELOPED UNDER § 7–1514 OF THE EDUCATION ARTICLE.
6 HOUSE BILL 326
1 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
2 1, 2026.
