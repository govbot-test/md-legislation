Introduced and read first time: February 11, 2026
Assigned to: Environment and Transportation
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Residential Solar Energy Systems – Local Inspections and Permitting
3 FOR the purpose of altering the definition of “solar permitting software” for purposes of
4 provisions regarding permitting and inspections of residential solar energy systems;
5 altering the date by which certain counties and municipalities must implement
6 certain solar permitting software; requiring, on or before a certain date, certain
7 counties and municipalities to authorize remote inspections of certain residential
8 solar energy systems; prohibiting a county or municipality from requiring a manual
9 review of certain permits and inspections; prohibiting a county and municipality
10 from setting a solar permitting fee for residential solar energy systems that exceeds
11 a certain amount; limiting review of a certain application to a determination
12 regarding health and safety requirements; authorizing the Attorney General to seek
13 judicial enforcement for failure to comply with this Act; and generally relating to
14 permitting and inspections of residential solar energy systems.
15 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
16 Article – Local Government
17 Section 1–1320
18 Annotated Code of Maryland
19 (2013 Volume and 2025 Supplement)
20 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
21 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
22 Article – Local Government
23 1–1320.
24 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
25 (2) “Administration” means the Maryland Energy Administration.

1 (3) “Residential energy storage system” means a system, on a residential
2 customer’s side of the meter, used to store electrical energy, or mechanical, chemical, or
3 thermal energy that was once electrical energy, for use as electrical energy at a later date
4 or in a process that offsets electricity use at peak times.
5 (4) “Residential solar energy system” means any configuration of solar
6 energy devices that collects and distributes solar energy for the purpose of generating
7 electricity and that has a single residential interconnection with the electrical grid.
8 (5) “SOLAR PERMITTING FEE” MEANS A FEE EQUAL TO THE SUM OF
9 ALL CHARGES IMPOSED BY A COUNTY OR MUNICIPALITY, INCLUDING CHARGES
10 IMPOSED BY A PROVIDER OF SOLAR PERMITTING SOFTWARE, IN CONNECTION WITH
11 AN APPLICATION FOR A RESIDENTIAL SOLAR ENERGY SYSTEM.
12 (6) “Solar permitting software” means[:
13 (i) the most recent version of a web–based platform, developed by
14 the National Renewable Energy Laboratory, that provides a standard portal for receiving
15 and processing residential solar energy system and residential energy storage system
16 permit information; or
17 (ii) automated software that functions to support the tracking and
18 approval of residential building permits for residential solar energy systems, residential
19 energy storage systems, main electrical panel upgrades, and main electrical panel devices]
20 SOFTWARE OR A COMBINATION OF SOFTWARE THAT:
21 (I) AUTOMATES PLAN REVIEW FOR RESIDENTIAL SOLAR
22 ENERGY SYSTEMS TO THE MOST RECENT VERSION OF THE MARYLAND BUILDING
23 PERFORMANCE STANDARDS, ELECTRICAL CODE, AND FIRE PREVENTION CODE, AS
24 APPLICABLE;
25 (II) PRODUCES CODE–COMPLIANT APPROVALS;
26 (III) ISSUES A CODE–COMPLIANT PERMIT;
27 (IV) ACCEPTS ONLINE PAYMENTS FOR ANY PERMITTING FEES
28 IMPOSED; AND
29 (V) ISSUES PERMITS OR PERMIT REVISIONS IMMEDIATELY ON
30 RECEIPT OF ONLINE SUBMISSION OF PERMITTING FEE PAYMENTS, IF APPLICABLE.
31 (b) This section applies to all counties and municipalities.

1 (c) (1) Subject to subsection [(d)] (E) of this section and except as provided in
2 subsection [(e)] (F) of this section, on or before August 1, [2025] 2027, each county and
3 municipality shall implement solar permitting software for features supporting the
4 tracking and approval of residential building permits for:
5 [(1)] (I) residential solar energy systems;
6 [(2)] (II) residential energy storage systems;
7 [(3)] (III) main electrical panel upgrades; and
8 [(4)] (IV) main electrical panel derates.
9 (2) (I) SUBJECT TO SUBSECTION (D)(1) OF THIS SECTION, ON OR
10 BEFORE AUGUST 1, 2027, EACH COUNTY AND MUNICIPALITY SHALL AUTHORIZE ANY
11 INSPECTION REQUIRED FOR A PROJECT PERMITTED BY SOLAR PERMITTING
12 SOFTWARE TO BE CONDUCTED REMOTELY THROUGH:
13 1. RECORDED VIDEO; OR
14 2. PHOTOGRAPH.
15 (II) A REMOTE INSPECTION CONDUCTED UNDER THIS
16 PARAGRAPH:
17 1. MAY NOT COST EXCEED $100; AND
18 2. SHALL BE COMPLETED WITHIN THE SAME TIME
19 FRAME AS AN IN–PERSON INSPECTION.
20 (III) A COUNTY OR MUNICIPALITY MAY NOT REQUIRE REMOTE
21 INSPECTIONS CONDUCTED UNDER THIS PARAGRAPH TO BE CONDUCTED MORE THAN
22 ONCE UNLESS THE FIRST INSPECTION RESULTED IN A FAILED INSPECTION.
23 (3) A COUNTY OR MUNICIPALITY MAY NOT REQUIRE MANUAL REVIEW
24 AT ANY TIME DURING THE PERMITTING AND INSPECTION PROCESS FOR PERMITS
25 APPROVED UNDER THIS SUBSECTION.
26 (4) BEGINNING AUGUST 1, 2027, A COUNTY OR MUNICIPALITY MAY
27 NOT SET A SOLAR PERMITTING FEE THAT EXCEEDS $200 FOR RESIDENTIAL SOLAR
28 ENERGY SYSTEMS.
29 (5) AN INSPECTOR MAY REVIEW CONSTRUCTION DOCUMENTS THAT
30 ARE PRODUCED BY THE SOLAR PERMITTING SOFTWARE.
4 HOUSE BILL 1104
1 (D) REVIEW OF A PERMIT APPLICATION TO INSTALL A RESIDENTIAL SOLAR
2 ENERGY GENERATING SYSTEM SHALL BE LIMITED TO A DETERMINATION WHETHER
3 THE APPLICATION MEETS ALL HEALTH AND SAFETY REQUIREMENTS UNDER STATE
4 AND LOCAL LAW.
5 [(d)] (E) A county or municipality may not be required to comply with the
6 requirements of subsection (c) of this section if:
7 (1) the county or municipality does not require a permit for:
8 (i) residential solar energy systems; or
9 (ii) residential solar energy systems paired with a residential solar
10 energy storage system; or
11 (2) as determined by the Administration, the automated software is no
12 longer updated or maintained.
13 [(e)] (F) The Administration shall delay the initial implementation or suspend
14 the requirements of subsection (c) of this section if there are insufficient State or federal
15 funds available to the Administration to provide financial support to a county or
16 municipality implementing solar permitting software as defined in subsection (a)(5)(i) of
17 this section.
18 (G) THE ATTORNEY GENERAL MAY SEEK JUDICIAL ENFORCEMENT AGAINST
19 A COUNTY OR MUNICIPALITY THAT FAILS TO COMPLY WITH THIS SECTION.
20 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
21 October 1, 2026.
