A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Education – Maryland Institute for Literacy and Equity – Establishment
3 FOR the purpose of establishing the Maryland Institute for Literacy and Equity organized
4 as a partnership between the University of Maryland, College Park Campus and
5 Morgan State University to serve as a State–funded research and technical
6 assistance center to promote literacy in the State; requiring the Governor to include
7 in the annual budget bill certain appropriations to the University of Maryland,
8 College Park Campus, Morgan State University, and the Institute; and generally
9 relating to the Maryland Institute for Literacy and Equity.
10 BY adding to
11 Article – Education
12 Section 24–1401 through 24–1405 to be under the new subtitle “Subtitle 14.
13 Maryland Institute for Literacy and Equity”
14 Annotated Code of Maryland
15 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
16 Preamble
17 WHEREAS, Maryland’s fourth grade reading scores have declined sharply over the
18 last decade and, as of 2024, the State is ranked 20 out of 50 U.S. states based on the
19 National Assessment of Educational Progress survey in reading; and
20 WHEREAS, Disparities in reading outcomes on the basis of race, ethnicity,
21 socioeconomic status, and language spoken at home contribute to broader social and
22 economic inequalities; and
23 WHEREAS, Literacy achievement is a primary civil rights concern, essential to
24 addressing unemployment, incarceration rates, poverty, and health outcomes; and

1 WHEREAS, Unlike neighboring states, Maryland lacks a dedicated
2 university–based center to provide research and technical assistance on effective literacy
3 practices; and
4 WHEREAS, The Maryland Institute for Literacy and Equity would bring together
5 two of Maryland’s flagship universities – Morgan State University, the State’s preeminent
6 public urban research university, and the University of Maryland, the State’s land–grant
7 university and strategic partner – thus situating the Maryland Institute for Literacy and
8 Equity as a uniquely qualified resource to serve the State’s diverse populations; now,
9 therefore,
10 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
11 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

28 (1) SUPPORT THE MISSION AND PRIORITIES OF THE DEPARTMENT

31 ENTITIES AND THE COMMUNITIES THEY SERVE TO ENHANCE AND EXPAND THE
32 IMPLEMENTATION OF EFFECTIVE LITERACY INSTRUCTION AND INTERVENTION;
