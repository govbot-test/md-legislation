WES MOORE, Governor Ch. 386
Chapter 386
AN ACT concerning
Part–Time State Employment Pilot Program Workgroup for an Inclusive State
Workplace – Established
(Maryland Workforce Retention, Recruitment, and Reentry Act)
FOR the purpose of establishing the Part–Time State Employment Pilot Program in the
Department of Budget and Management to promote and support the inclusivity of
State employment by increasing the availability of part–time positions with benefits
in State government; requiring State agencies selected to participate in the Program
to evaluate full–time position vacancies to determine whether the vacant positions
could be converted to part–time positions with benefits; and generally relating to the
Part–Time State Employment Pilot Program Workgroup for an Inclusive State
Workplace to create a strategy and implementation work plan regarding more
inclusive study and make recommendations regarding the expansion of part–time
State employment opportunities for individuals who are challenged by the
traditional full–time employment structure; requiring the Department of Budget and
Management, taking into consideration the Workgroup’s findings and
recommendations, to develop and distribute guidance to State agencies on expanding
part–time employment opportunities; and generally relating to the Workgroup for an
Inclusive State Workplace.
BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
Article – State Personnel and Pensions

Section 2:
Section 1–101(a), (f), and (j)
Annotated Code of Maryland
(2024 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
Article – State Personnel and Pensions

(3) set annual goals to expand part–time job opportunities with benefits,

(4) identify any potential statutory changes required to implement the

(5) identify and quantify the long–term economic benefits to the State from
increased labor force participation by caregivers, people with disabilities or other medical

expansion of part–time employment within the State Personnel Management System, the
University System of Maryland, and the Department of Transportation;
(ii) focus on the inclusive employment opportunities in State
government and removing obstacles for individuals challenged by full–time employment;
(iii) review the current policies and procedures concerning part–time
employment in State government;
(iv) analyze the budgetary incentives, recruitment best practices, and
support structures currently in place to encourage hiring of part–time, benefit–eligible
employees;
(v) study the economic and operational effects and quantify the
long–term economic benefits of part–time employment on households and State government;
(vi) suggest timelines and identify methods and associated metrics for
expanding part–time employment opportunities; and
(vii) identify any potential statutory changes required to implement
the steps identified by the Workgroup.
(h) On or before September 15, 2027, the Workgroup shall share its inclusive

State Government Article.

shall develop and distribute guidance to State agencies on expanding part–time employment
opportunities, taking into consideration the findings and recommendations of the
Workgroup.
