CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Division of Correction – Release Preparation Volunteer Services Program
3 FOR the purpose of establishing the Release Preparation Volunteer Services Program in
4 the Division of Correction for the purpose of providing access to volunteers, including
5 formerly incarcerated individuals and organizations led by formerly incarcerated
6 individuals, to State correctional facilities to assist incarcerated individuals with
7 release preparation; altering the purposes of the Cannabis Regulation and
8 Enforcement Fund to include providing funds to cover the costs of supporting the
9 Release Preparation Program; and generally relating to the Release Preparation
10 Volunteer Services Program.
11 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
12 Article – Alcoholic Beverages and Cannabis
13 Section 36–206
14 Annotated Code of Maryland
15 (2024 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

3 Article – Alcoholic Beverages and Cannabis
4 36–206.
5 (a) In this section, “Fund” means the Cannabis Regulation and Enforcement
6 Fund.
7 (b) There is a Cannabis Regulation and Enforcement Fund.
8 (c) The purpose of the Fund is to provide funds to cover the costs of:
9 (1) the operation of the Administration;
10 (2) administering and enforcing this title; [and]
11 (3) supporting the Social Equity Partnership Grant Program established
12 under § 1–323 of this article; AND
13 (4) SUPPORTING THE RELEASE PREPARATION PROGRAM
14 ESTABLISHED UNDER § 9–619 OF THE CORRECTIONAL SERVICES ARTICLE.
15 (d) The Administration shall administer the Fund.
16 (e) (1) The Fund is a special, nonlapsing fund that is not subject to § 7–302 of
17 the State Finance and Procurement Article.
18 (2) The State Treasurer shall hold the Fund separately, and the
19 Comptroller shall account for the Fund.
20 (f) The Fund consists of:
21 (1) fees distributed to the Fund under § 36–205 of this subtitle;
22 (2) revenue distributed to the Fund under § 2–1302.2 of the Tax – General
23 Article;
24 (3) interest earnings of the Fund; and
25 (4) any other money from any other source accepted for the benefit of the
26 Fund, in accordance with any conditions adopted by the Commission for the acceptance of
27 donations or gifts to the Fund.
28 (g) The Fund may be used for:

15 Article – Correctional Services
16 9–619.
17 (A) IN THIS SECTION, “PROGRAM” MEANS THE RELEASE PREPARATION
18 VOLUNTEER SERVICES PROGRAM.
19 (B) THERE IS A RELEASE PREPARATION VOLUNTEER SERVICES PROGRAM
20 IN THE DIVISION OF CORRECTION.
21 (C) (1) THE DIVISION OF CORRECTION SHALL ESTABLISH A PROTOCOL
22 FOR REGISTERING VOLUNTEERS, INCLUDING FORMERLY INCARCERATED
23 INDIVIDUALS AND ORGANIZATIONS LED BY FORMERLY INCARCERATED
24 INDIVIDUALS, IN THE PROGRAM TO HAVE ACCESS TO STATE CORRECTIONAL
25 FACILITIES FOR THE PURPOSE OF ASSISTING INCARCERATED INDIVIDUALS WITH
26 RELEASE PREPARATION.
27 (2) THE DIVISION OF CORRECTION SHALL SUBMIT THE PROTOCOL
28 ESTABLISHED UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION TO THE OFFICE OF THE
29 ATTORNEY GENERAL FOR APPROVAL.
