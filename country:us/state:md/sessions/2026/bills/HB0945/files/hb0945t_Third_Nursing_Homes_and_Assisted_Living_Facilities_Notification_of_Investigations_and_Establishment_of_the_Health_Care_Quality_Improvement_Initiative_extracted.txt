CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Nursing Homes – Complaints – Notification and Consultation
3 Nursing Homes and Assisted Living Facilities – Notification of Investigations
4 and Establishment of the Health Care Quality Improvement Initiative
5 FOR the purpose of requiring the Maryland Department of Health to notify and consult
6 with the local health department for the county in which a nursing home is located
7 if the Department initiates an investigation of a nursing home complaint alleging
8 actual harm; establishing the Health Care Quality Improvement Initiative to
9 identify strategies to improve the quality of care and the affordability and
10 accessibility of services in nursing homes and assisted living programs and
11 determine how the State can best use and share available data and expertise for
12 certain purposes; establishing a task force to carry out the purposes of the Initiative;
13 requiring the Department to staff the Initiative; and generally relating to nursing
14 home complaints homes and assisted living programs.
15 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
16 Article – Health – General
17 Section 19–1408
18 Annotated Code of Maryland
19 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
20 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
21 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

3 (a) (1) Subject to subsection (c) of this section, the Department shall make a
4 site visit and conduct a full survey of each licensed nursing home at least once per calendar
5 year.
6 (2) Unless otherwise required by federal law, all surveys shall be
7 unannounced.
8 (b) (1) Subject to [paragraph] PARAGRAPHS (2) AND (3) of this subsection,
9 the Department shall initiate an investigation of a nursing home complaint alleging actual
10 harm within 10 business days after receiving the complaint.
11 (2) If the Department receives a complaint against a nursing home alleging
12 immediate jeopardy to a resident, the Department:
13 (i) Shall make every effort to investigate the complaint within 24
14 hours after receiving the complaint; and
15 (ii) Shall investigate the complaint not later than 48 hours after
16 receiving the complaint.
17 (3) (I) IF THE DEPARTMENT INITIATES AN INVESTIGATION OF A
18 NURSING HOME COMPLAINT UNDER THIS SUBSECTION, THE DEPARTMENT SHALL:
19 (I) 1. NOTIFY IMMEDIATELY THE LOCAL HEALTH
20 DEPARTMENT FOR THE COUNTY IN WHICH THE NURSING HOME IS LOCATED WITHIN
21 24 HOURS AFTER INITIATING THE INVESTIGATION; AND
22 (II) 2. TO THE EXTENT PRACTICABLE WITHIN THE TIME
23 REQUIREMENTS ESTABLISHED UNDER PARAGRAPH (2) OF THIS SUBSECTION AND AS
24 PERMITTED BY FEDERAL LAW, CONSULT AND COORDINATE WITH THE LOCAL
25 HEALTH OFFICER DEPARTMENT ON THE COMPLAINT INVESTIGATION AND
26 RESPONSE.
27 (II) THIS PARAGRAPH MAY NOT BE CONSTRUED TO REQUIRE A
28 LOCAL HEALTH DEPARTMENT TO ASSIST WITH OR OTHERWISE ASSUME THE
29 NURSING HOME OVERSIGHT RESPONSIBILITIES OF THE DEPARTMENT AS REQUIRED
30 BY FEDERAL OR STATE LAW.
31 (c) If ownership of a licensed nursing home is transferred to a person that does
32 not own or operate another nursing home in the State at the time of the transfer, the
33 Department shall conduct:
HOUSE BILL 945 3
1 (1) The first full survey of the licensed nursing home as required under
2 subsection (a) of this section within 3 months after the date of transfer; and
3 (2) An unannounced, on–site follow–up survey of the licensed nursing
4 home that covers any deficiencies noted in the full survey within 120 days after the full
5 survey was completed.
6 (d) (1) Beginning July 1, 2025, and every 3 months thereafter, the Department
7 shall report to the Senate Finance Committee and the House Health [and Government
8 Operations] Committee, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government Article, the
9 number of surveys conducted in each county, disaggregated by the following types of
10 surveys:
11 (i) Standard survey;
12 (ii) Complain survey;
13 (iii) Follow–up survey;
14 (iv) Revisit survey;
15 (v) Special focus facility survey;
16 (vi) Abbreviated survey; and
17 (vii) Life safety code survey.
18 (2) Beginning July 1, 2025, and every 6 months thereafter, the Office of
19 Health Care Quality shall report to the local area agency on aging for each county the name
20 of each nursing home in the county that was surveyed in the immediately preceding 6
21 months and the date each survey was conducted.
22 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That:
23 (a) (1) There is a Health Care Quality Improvement Initiative.
24 (2) The purpose of the Initiative is to:
25 (i) identify strategies to improve the quality of care and the
26 affordability and accessibility of services in nursing homes and assisted living programs;
27 and
28 (ii) determine how the State can best use and share available data
29 and expertise for the purposes of analysis, monitoring, public transparency, and oversight
30 of nursing homes and assisted living programs.
4 HOUSE BILL 945
1 (3) The Initiative shall be carried out by a task force established in
2 accordance with subsection (b) of this section.
3 (b) The task force consists of the following members:
4 (1) one member of the Senate who is a member of the Senate Finance
5 Committee, appointed by the President of the Senate;
6 (2) one member of the House of Delegates who is a member of the House
7 Health Committee, appointed by the Speaker of the House;
8 (3) the Attorney General, or the Attorney General’s designee;
9 (4) the Secretary of Health, or the Secretary’s designee;
10 (5) the Secretary of Aging, or the Secretary’s designee;
11 (6) the Secretary of Human Services, or the Secretary’s designee;
12 (7) the Deputy Secretary for Public Health Services, or the Deputy
13 Secretary’s designee;
14 (8) the Deputy Secretary of Maryland Medicaid, or the Deputy Secretary’s
15 designee;
16 (9) the Executive Director of the Office of Health Care Quality, or the
17 Executive Director’s designee;
18 (10) the Executive Director of the Maryland Health Care Commission, or the
19 Executive Director’s designee; and
20 (11) the Chief Executive Officer of the Maryland Patient Safety Center, or
21 the Chief Executive Officer’s designee.
22 (c) The Secretary of Health shall designate the chair of the task force.
23 (d) The Maryland Department of Health shall provide staff for the task force.
24 (e) A member of the task force:
25 (1) may not receive compensation as a member of the task force; but
26 (2) is entitled to reimbursement for expenses under the Standard State
27 Travel Regulations, as provided in the State budget.
28 (f) The task force shall study:
HOUSE BILL 945 5
1 (1) the feasibility of quality standards and potential use of the Centers for
2 Medicaid and Medicare Quality Assurance and Performance Improvement framework for
3 maintaining and improving safety and quality for assisted living facilities; and
4 (2) barriers and policy recommendations regarding:
5 (i) recruitment and retention of the nursing home and assisted
6 living program workforce;
7 (ii) the quality of required staff training for nursing home and
8 assisted living program staff;
9 (iii) oversight of small assisted living programs, including whether a
10 different regulatory structure should be implemented;
11 (iv) post–acute care involving discharges from the hospital;
12 (v) sharing data that is transparent to the public; and
13 (vi) any other issues the task force determines will improve the
14 quality of care, oversight, and affordability of nursing homes and assisted living programs
15 and the accessibility of services in nursing homes and assisted living programs.
16 (g) To complete the work of the task force, the task force:
17 (1) shall engage with members of the health care industry, advocacy
18 organizations, and the nursing home and assisted living communities;
19 (2) may establish workgroups to conduct its work that may include the
20 stakeholders described in item (1) of this subsection;
21 (3) shall collaborate with the Maryland Health Care Commission
22 regarding the use and sharing of available data and expertise for the purposes of analysis,
23 monitoring, public transparency, and oversight of nursing homes and assisted living
24 programs;
25 (4) shall hold a virtual meeting to seek feedback and recommendations
26 from frontline workers and family members involved in the care of individuals receiving
27 care from nursing homes and assisted living programs; and
28 (5) comply with the meeting requirements established under subsection (h)
29 of this section.
30 (h) (1) The task force shall meet once every 3 months.
31 (2) At each meeting, the Office of Health Care Quality shall report data
32 from the immediately preceding 6–month period regarding:
6 HOUSE BILL 945
1 (i) the number of nursing homes and assisted living programs
2 inspected;
3 (ii) the number of complaints received regarding nursing homes and
4 assisted living programs;
5 (iii) the number of complaints investigated regarding nursing homes
6 and assisted living programs; and
7 (iv) de–identified aggregate data on citations issued to nursing
8 homes and assisted living programs.
9 (3) (i) Within 30 days after each meeting, the task force shall provide a
10 public update, consistent with applicable federal and State confidentiality laws and
11 privileges, that includes the date of the meeting, general topics discussed at the meeting,
12 and any nonconfidential next steps of the task force.
13 (ii) The public update required under subparagraph (i) of this
14 paragraph may be posted on the Maryland Department of Health’s website.
15 (i) (1) Subject to paragraph (2) of this subsection, the task force may not
16 request or receive complaint information for identifiable individual facilities, protected
17 health information, or survey evidence, including records, documentation, observations,
18 survey notes, interviews, or other materials collected or created by the Office of Health Care
19 Quality in connection with a survey, inspection, or complaint investigation of a facility.
20 (2) The task force may request or receive redacted statements of
21 deficiencies.
22 (j) The task force shall:
23 (1) compile feedback received under subsection (g)(4) of this section and
24 otherwise through the work of the task force from frontline workers and family members
25 involved in the care of individuals receiving care from nursing homes and assisted living
26 programs;
27 (2) develop recommendations for the Secretary of Health to further the
28 purpose of the Initiative established under subsection (a)(2) of this section;
29 (3) identify resources required to implement the recommendations
30 developed in accordance with item (2) of this subsection; and
31 (4) (i) on or before December 1, 2027, report its preliminary findings
32 and recommendations to the Governor and, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State
33 Government Article, the General Assembly; and
HOUSE BILL 945 7
1 (ii) on or before December 1, 2028, report its final findings and
2 recommendations to the Governor and, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State
3 Government Article, the General Assembly.
4 SECTION 2. 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
5 October 1, 2026. Section 2 of this Act shall remain effective for a period of 2 years and 9
6 months and, at the end of June 30, 2029, Section 2 of this Act, with no further action
7 required by the General Assembly, shall be abrogated and of no further force and effect.
