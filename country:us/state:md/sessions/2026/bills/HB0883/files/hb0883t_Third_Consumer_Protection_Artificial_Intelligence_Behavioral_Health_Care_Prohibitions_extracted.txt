CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Consumer Protection – Artificial Intelligence – Behavioral Health Care
3 Prohibitions
4 FOR the purpose of prohibiting a certain developer from making or knowingly causing
5 artificial intelligence to make certain representations or statements or to provide
6 certain services or experiences relating to behavioral health care, subject to a certain
7 civil penalty; requiring certain artificial intelligence sold, leased, given, or otherwise
8 provided to consumers in the State to include certain notice or certain protocols;
9 making a violation of this Act an unfair, abusive, or deceptive trade practice under
10 the Maryland Consumer Protection Act; authorizing the Attorney General to
11 investigate and bring an action for an alleged violation of this Act and requiring
12 revenues from civil penalties collected to be distributed to a certain fund; and
13 generally relating to artificial intelligence and behavioral health care.

11 14–5102.
12 (A) A DEVELOPER MAY NOT:
13 (1) MAKE, OR KNOWINGLY CAUSE ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE TO
14 MAKE, ANY REPRESENTATION OR STATEMENT THAT EXPLICITLY OR IMPLICITLY
15 INDICATES THAT THE ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE IS:
16 (I) A BEHAVIORAL HEALTH CARE PROVIDER; OR
17 (II) CAPABLE OF PROVIDING BEHAVIORAL HEALTH CARE; OR
18 (2) OFFER, SELL, LEASE, GIVE, OR OTHERWISE PROVIDE TO
19 CONSUMERS IN THE STATE ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE THAT IS SPECIFICALLY
20 PROGRAMMED OR DESIGNED TO PROVIDE A SERVICE OR AN EXPERIENCE THAT
21 WOULD CONSTITUTE THE PRACTICE OF BEHAVIORAL HEALTH CARE IF PROVIDED BY
22 A BEHAVIORAL HEALTH CARE PROVIDER.
23 (B) ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SOLD, LEASED, GIVEN, OR OTHERWISE
24 PROVIDED TO A CONSUMER IN THE STATE SHALL INCLUDE:
25 (1) CLEAR AND CONSPICUOUS NOTICE THAT THE CONSUMER IS NOT
26 COMMUNICATING WITH A HUMAN, DISPLAYED AT THE BEGINNING OF EACH USE; AND
27 (2) PROTOCOLS THAT:
