WES MOORE, Governor Ch. 634
Chapter 634
(Senate Bill 720)
AN ACT concerning
Education – Artificial Intelligence – Guidelines, Professional Development, and
Collaborative
(Artificial Intelligence Ready Schools Act)
FOR the purpose of requiring the State Department of Education to provide certain
guidance on artificial intelligence to certain groups through an online platform;
requiring the Department, in consultation with certain stakeholders, to separately
publish certain guidance for certain groups; requiring the Department to develop
certain strategies to implement certain guidelines and best practices; requiring each
county board of education to have a certain artificial intelligence policy on or before
a certain date; requiring each county board local school system to designate a certain
coordinator for the use of artificial intelligence in the local school system; requiring
Morgan State University or another 4–year institution of higher education in the
State to support the Department in a certain evaluation and to certify that certain
artificial intelligence tools are consistent with certain State guidelines each year the
Department to develop and publish a certain rubric and certain evaluative tools to
assist local school systems in the evaluation of artificial intelligence tools selected
for use by local school systems; requiring local school systems to procure artificial
intelligence tools consistent with certain provisions of law; requiring the
Department, in consultation with the Governor’s Workforce Development Board, to
ensure that artificial intelligence literacy is a component of workforce preparation
standards; requiring the Department to coordinate with the Maryland Center for
Computing Education and coordinators of professional learning and instructional
coaches to promote and implement certain training in artificial intelligence provide
certain professional development in artificial intelligence to educators and school
leaders; establishing the Maryland AI Education Collaborative on Artificial
Intelligence in K–12 Education; and generally relating to artificial intelligence in
primary and secondary education.
BY adding to
Article – Education
Section 7–2201 through 7–2204 to be under the new subtitle “Subtitle 22. Artificial
Intelligence in Education”

SUBTITLE 22. ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE IN EDUCATION.
7–2201.
IN THIS SUBTITLE, “ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE” HAS THE MEANING STATED
IN § 3.5–801 OF THE STATE FINANCE AND PROCUREMENT ARTICLE.
7–2202.
(A) (1) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL PROVIDE GUIDANCE ON ARTIFICIAL
INTELLIGENCE TO LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEMS, EDUCATORS, PARENTS, AND STUDENTS
THROUGH AN ONLINE PLATFORM THAT:
(I) PROMOTES THE SAFE, RESPONSIBLE, EQUITABLE, AND
ETHICAL USE OF ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE;
(II) PROMOTES ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE LITERACY
THROUGH COMPUTER SCIENCE EDUCATION;
(II) (III) EMPHASIZES STUDENTS AND TEACHERS AT THE
CENTER OF EDUCATIONAL TECHNOLOGY;
(III) (IV) PRIORITIZES THE EDUCATIONAL NEEDS OF
STUDENTS USING EVIDENCE–BASED APPROACHES AND METHODOLOGIES; AND
(IV) (V) ACKNOWLEDGES TO STATE AND FEDERAL
STANDARDS ADDRESSES HOW STATE AND FEDERAL PRIVACY AND ACCESSIBILITY
STANDARDS APPLY TO THE USE OF ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE INSTRUCTION IN
SCHOOLS.
(2) THE DEPARTMENT, IN CONSULTATION WITH OTHER INTERESTED
STAKEHOLDERS, SHALL PUBLISH SEPARATELY ON ITS WEBSITE GUIDANCE FOR
EACH OF THE FOLLOWING GROUPS:
(I) STUDENTS;
(II) EDUCATORS; AND
(III) ADMINISTRATORS; AND
(IV) PARENTS AND GUARDIANS.

(3) EACH YEAR, THE DEPARTMENT SHALL REVIEW THE GUIDANCE
AND UPDATE THE GUIDANCE AS NECESSARY.
(B) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL DEVELOP STRATEGIES TO ASSIST COUNTY
BOARDS AND COUNTY SUPERINTENDENTS TO IMPLEMENT THE GUIDELINES AND
BEST PRACTICES DEVELOPED BY THE DEPARTMENT UNDER THIS SECTION.
(C) ON OR BEFORE 120 DAYS FROM THE DATE THAT THE DEPARTMENT

AND

CONSISTENT WITH THE STATE GUIDELINES.
(E) (1) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL DEVELOP AND PUBLISH A RUBRIC AND
OTHER EVALUATIVE TOOLS, AS NECESSARY, TO ASSIST LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEMS IN

LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM.
(2) THE RUBRIC SHALL BE DESIGNED TO SUPPORT LOCAL SCHOOL
SYSTEMS IN DETERMINING WHETHER ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE TOOLS ARE
CONSISTENT WITH STATE GUIDELINES.
(3) EACH LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEM SHALL USE THE RUBRIC AND
OTHER EVALUATIVE TOOLS DEVELOPED BY THE DEPARTMENT IN THE REVIEW AND

7–2203.
(A) ON OR BEFORE JUNE 1, 2027, THE DEPARTMENT, IN CONSULTATION
WITH THE GOVERNOR’S WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT BOARD, SHALL ENSURE THAT
ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE LITERACY IS A COMPONENT OF WORKFORCE
PREPARATION STANDARDS AND COMPUTER SCIENCE STANDARDS FOR
KINDERGARTEN THROUGH GRADE 12.
(B) (1) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL COORDINATE WITH THE MARYLAND
CENTER FOR COMPUTING EDUCATION ESTABLISHED UNDER § 12–118 OF THIS
ARTICLE AND COORDINATORS OF PROFESSIONAL LEARNING AND INSTRUCTIONAL
COACHES TO PROMOTE TEACHER PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT TRAINING IN
ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE.
(2) THE TEACHER PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT TRAINING SHALL
BE IMPLEMENTED STATEWIDE IN A TRAIN–THE–TRAINER MODEL RELATED TO
ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE LITERACY AND TECHNICAL EXPERTISE, WITH THE
INTENT OF TRAINING EDUCATORS BY JULY 1, 2027.
(3) (I) TEACHERS IN THE TEACHER PROFESSIONAL
DEVELOPMENT TRAINING SHALL BE COMPENSATED FOR THE TRAINING WITH TIME,
MONEY, OR RECERTIFICATION CREDITS.
(II) TEACHERS MAY NOT BE CHARGED TO ATTEND OR
COMPLETE THE TRAINING.
(B) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL PROVIDE PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT IN
ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE TO EDUCATORS AND SCHOOL LEADERS THAT:

EDUCATORS AND SCHOOL LEADERS:
(I) FOR THE PROFESSIONAL USE OF ARTIFICIAL

(II) TO APPLY ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE TO INSTRUCTION;

(II) AS A COURSE THAT IS ELIGIBLE FOR CREDIT TOWARD
