M4, M2 6lr3257
CF HB 1212
By: Senator Mautz
Introduced and read first time: February 6, 2026
Assigned to: Education, Energy, and the Environment
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Workgroup on Seafood Marketing
3 FOR the purpose of requiring the Secretary of Agriculture and the Secretary of Natural
4 Resources jointly to convene a workgroup to examine challenges and opportunities
5 in the State related to seafood marketing; and generally relating to seafood
6 marketing.
7 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
8 That:
9 (a) The Secretary of Agriculture and the Secretary of Natural Resources jointly
10 shall convene a workgroup to examine challenges and opportunities in the State related to
11 seafood marketing.
12 (b) The workgroup convened under this section shall include:
13 (1) two members of the Senate of Maryland, appointed by the President of
14 the Senate;
15 (2) two members of the House of Delegates, appointed by the Speaker of
16 the House;
17 (3) the Secretary of Agriculture, or the Secretary’s designee;
18 (4) the Secretary of Natural Resources, or the Secretary’s designee; and
19 (5) (i) representatives of the seafood industry, selected by the Secretary
20 of Agriculture and the Secretary of Natural Resources; and
21 (ii) any other stakeholder considered necessary by the Secretary of
22 Agriculture and the Secretary of Natural Resources.
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from exist in g l aw . *sb0916*
2 SENATE BILL 916
1 (c) The Department of Agriculture and the Department of Natural Resources
2 jointly shall provide staff for the workgroup.
3 (d) On or before December 1, 2026, the Secretary of Agriculture and the Secretary
4 of Natural Resources jointly shall report the findings of the workgroup convened under this
5 section and any recommendations of the workgroup, including recommendations for
6 legislation, to the Governor and, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government
7 Article, the Senate Committee on Education, Energy, and the Environment and the House
8 Environment and Transportation Committee.
9 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect June
10 1, 2026. It shall remain effective for a period of 1 year and 1 month and, at the end of June
11 30, 2027, this Act, with no further action required by the General Assembly, shall be
12 abrogated and of no further force and effect.
