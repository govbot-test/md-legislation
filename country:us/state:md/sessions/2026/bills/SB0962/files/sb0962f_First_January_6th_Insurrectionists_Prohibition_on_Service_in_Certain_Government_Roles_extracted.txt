A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 January 6th Insurrectionists – Prohibition on Service in Certain Government
3 Roles
4 FOR the purpose of prohibiting individuals convicted of certain crimes in relation to the
5 attack on the United States Capitol on January 6, 2021, from serving in certain
6 government roles; requiring an appointing authority to take certain actions to
7 remove or terminate the employment of an individual serving in a role in violation
8 of this Act; and generally relating to individuals convicted of crimes in relation to the
9 attack on the United States Capitol on January 6, 2021.
10 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,

1 WHEREAS, The January 6th attack represented an unprecedented assault on
2 American democracy, resulting in significant injuries and death to courageous law
3 enforcement officers; and
4 WHEREAS, Individuals convicted of crimes related to the January 6th insurrection
5 engaged in violent, lawless, and seditious actions that deliberately sought to subvert our
6 democracy, intimidate government officials, and flout constitutional norms; and
7 WHEREAS, Public service in the State of Maryland requires fidelity to the United
8 States Constitution, respect for the rule of law, and a commitment to protecting the
9 integrity of government institutions against threats, both foreign and domestic; and
10 WHEREAS, Public service is a privilege, and allowing individuals convicted of
11 crimes in relation to the January 6th attack to hold positions of public trust would threaten
12 the functioning of government, undermine public confidence, and place the authority of the
13 State in the hands of those who have already sought to violently overthrow lawful
14 institutions; now, therefore,
15 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,

SENATE BILL 962 3
1 (A) THIS SECTION APPLIES TO AN INDIVIDUAL CONVICTED OF A CRIME IN
2 RELATION TO THE ATTACK ON THE UNITED STATES CAPITOL ON JANUARY 6, 2021,
3 REGARDLESS OF WHETHER THE INDIVIDUAL WAS SUBSEQUENTLY PARDONED.
4 (B) THIS SECTION DOES NOT APPLY TO AN INDIVIDUAL SERVING ON:
5 (1) A STANDING COMMITTEE, AS DEFINED IN § 2–101 OF THIS
6 ARTICLE; OR
7 (2) A COMMITTEE ESTABLISHED UNDER TITLE 2 OF THIS ARTICLE.
8 (C) NOTWITHSTANDING ANY OTHER PROVISION OF LAW, AN INDIVIDUAL
9 CONVICTED OF A CRIME IN RELATION TO THE ATTACK ON THE UNITED STATES
10 CAPITOL ON JANUARY 6, 2021, MAY NOT SERVE:
11 (1) ON A BOARD, COMMITTEE, COMMISSION, TASK FORCE, OR
12 WORKGROUP CREATED BY STATE LAW;
13 (2) IN THE EXECUTIVE SERVICE OF STATE GOVERNMENT, AS DEFINED
14 IN § 6–404 OF THE STATE PERSONNEL AND PENSIONS ARTICLE; OR
15 (3) IN A POSITION CATEGORIZED AS A SPECIAL APPOINTMENT UNDER
16 § 6–405 OF THE STATE PERSONNEL AND PENSIONS ARTICLE.
17 (D) IF AN APPOINTING AUTHORITY DETERMINES THAT AN INDIVIDUAL IS
18 SERVING IN A ROLE IN VIOLATION OF THIS SECTION, THE APPOINTING AUTHORITY
19 SHALL TAKE IMMEDIATE ACTION TO REMOVE OR TERMINATE THE EMPLOYMENT OF
20 THE INDIVIDUAL.
21 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
22 1, 2026.
