CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Metro Funding Modification Act of 2026
3 FOR the purpose of requiring the Governor to withhold a certain percentage of the annual
4 appropriation for grants to the Washington Suburban Transit District under certain
5 circumstances; altering the calculation of the amount that the Governor is required
6 to include in the annual State budget each year to provide grants to the Washington
7 Suburban Transit District to pay the capital costs of the Washington Metropolitan
8 Area Transit Authority; requiring the Governor to include an appropriation in the
9 annual State budget bill for the purpose of paying the State’s proportionate share of
10 the Metrorail Operating Subsidy Allocation Formula funding required under a
11 certain agreement; requiring the Department to withhold certain grants for any
12 fiscal year in which the Authority invests in a rail signaling system without reaching
13 certain collective bargaining agreements; making this Act contingent on enactment
14 of certain legislation by the Commonwealth of Virgina and the District of Columbia;
15 and generally relating to funding for the Washington Metropolitan Area Transit
16 Authority.
17 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,

5 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
6 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
7 Article – Transportation
8 10–205.
9 (f) (1) Except as provided in paragraph (2) of this subsection, the Governor
10 shall include an appropriation in the annual budget of at least the amount specified in
11 paragraph (4) of this subsection for the sole purpose of providing grants to the Washington
12 Suburban Transit District to pay the capital costs of the Washington Metropolitan Area
13 Transit Authority.
14 (2) (i) The Governor is not required to make the appropriation under
15 paragraph (1) of this subsection in a fiscal year unless the Department certifies to the
16 Governor in writing before the beginning of the immediately preceding fiscal year that the
17 Washington Metropolitan Area Transit Authority has submitted to the Department:
18 1. Performance and condition assessments and reports
19 regarding:
20 A. The safety and reliability of rapid heavy rail and bus
21 systems;
22 B. The financial performance of the Washington
23 Metropolitan Area Transit Authority as it relates to rail and bus operations, including fare
24 box recovery, service per rider, and cost per service hour;
25 C. The monthly ridership of rail and bus systems broken
26 down by Metrorail station, Metrorail line, bus route, and bus line;
27 D. Strategies to reduce costs and improve the Washington
28 Metropolitan Area Transit Authority’s operational efficiency; and
29 E. The comparison of annual capital investments and
30 approved budgets; and
31 2. The Washington Metropolitan Area Transit Authority’s:
32 A. Annual budget;
33 B. Annual independent financial audit;

1 C. Annual National Transit Database profile; and
2 D. Single audit reports issued in accordance with the
3 Uniform Administrative Requirements, Cost Principles, and Audit Requirements for
4 Federal Awards under 2 C.F.R. Part 200.
5 (ii) If the Commonwealth of Virginia or the District of Columbia
6 reduce the amount of dedicated capital funding for the Washington Metropolitan Area
7 Transit Authority, the Governor may reduce the appropriation under paragraph (1) of this
8 subsection by a proportional amount.
9 (iii) 1. The Governor shall withhold 35% of the appropriation
10 under paragraph (1) of this subsection if:
11 A. The Washington Metropolitan Area Transit Authority has
12 received a modified audit opinion as a result of an annual independent audit conducted in
13 accordance with Article XVI, Section 70 of the Washington Metropolitan Area Transit
14 Authority Compact under § 10–204 of this subtitle; and
15 B. The Department has not certified to the Governor in
16 writing before the beginning of the immediately preceding fiscal year that the Washington
17 Metropolitan Area Transit Authority has submitted in writing to the board of directors of
18 the Washington Metropolitan Area Transit Authority and the Maryland General Assembly
19 a satisfactory corrective plan that addresses the reasons for the modified audit opinion.
20 2. The Governor shall release the portion of the
21 appropriation withheld under subsubparagraph 1 of this subparagraph if the Washington
22 Metropolitan Area Transit Authority submits in writing to the board of directors of the
23 Washington Metropolitan Area Transit Authority and, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the
24 State Government Article, the Maryland General Assembly a satisfactory corrective action
25 plan that addresses the reasons for the modified audit opinion.

27 DIRECTORS OF THE WASHINGTON METROPOLITAN AREA TRANSIT AUTHORITY
28 SHALL APPROVE A RAIL SIGNALING AND AUTOMATION WORKFORCE TRANSITION
29 PLAN, DEVELOPED IN CONSULTATION WITH AFFECTED COLLECTIVE BARGAINING
30 UNITS.

13 OF $150,000,000.
14 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That:
15 (a) Section 1 of this Act is contingent on:
16 (1) the Commonwealth of Virginia enacting legislation that provides
17 dedicated capital funding for the Washington Metropolitan Area Transit Authority that
18 includes:
19 (i) a mandatory appropriation for the Commonwealth of Virginia’s
20 proportionate share of the Metrorail Operating Subsidy Allocation Formula dedicated
21 funding required by the Capital Funding Agreement Subsidy Allocation Formula, or any
22 successor agreement; and
23 (ii) an annual 3% increase to the mandatory appropriation; and
24 (2) the District of Columbia enacting legislation that provides dedicated
25 capital funding for the Washington Metropolitan Area Transit Authority that includes:
26 (i) a mandatory appropriation for the District of Columbia’s
27 proportionate share of the Metrorail Operating Subsidy Allocation Formula dedicated
28 funding required by the Capital Funding Agreement Subsidy Allocation Formula, or any
29 successor agreement; and
30 (ii) an annual 3% increase to the base amount of capital funding.
31 (b) The Department of Transportation shall notify the Department of Legislative
32 Services in writing within 5 days after both the Commonwealth of Virginia and the District
33 of Columbia have enacted legislation that meets the requirements of subsection (a) of this
34 section.

1 (c) Section 1 of this Act shall take effect on the date that the Department of
2 Legislative Services receives notice under subsection (b) of this section.

4 Act, this Act shall take effect June 1, 2026.
