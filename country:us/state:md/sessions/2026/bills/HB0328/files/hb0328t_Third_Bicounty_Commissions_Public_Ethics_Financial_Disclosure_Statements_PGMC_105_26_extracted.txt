CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Bicounty Commissions – Public Ethics – Financial Disclosure Statements
3 PG/MC 105–26
4 FOR the purpose of requiring an applicant for appointment as a commissioner of a bicounty
5 commission to submit a certain statement to the Prince George’s County Office of
6 Ethics and Accountability; requiring the chief administrative officer for Prince
7 George’s County to transmit the financial disclosure statements of commissioners or
8 appointed applicants of bicounty commissions to the Office; requiring the Office,
9 rather than the chief administrative officer for Prince George’s County, to retain the
10 statements, return the statements to certain applicants under certain
11 circumstances, provide certain notice to the State Ethics Commission, record certain
12 information of individuals examining or copying the statement, and maintain, make
13 available, and establish procedures governing the inspection of the statements; and
14 generally relating to financial disclosure statements for commissioners and
15 applicants for appointment as commissioners of bicounty commissions.
16 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
17 Article – General Provisions
18 Section 5–822, 5–824(c)(1) and (2), and 5–825(a)
19 Annotated Code of Maryland
20 (2019 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
21 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
22 Article – General Provisions

5 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
6 Article – General Provisions
7 5–822.
8 In this part, “commissioner” means a commissioner of a bicounty commission.
9 5–824.
10 (c) (1) Each commissioner shall file a financial disclosure statement
11 electronically with the Ethics Commission.
12 (2) Except as provided in paragraph (3) of this subsection, each
13 commissioner shall also print a paper copy of the electronically filed financial disclosure
14 statement and submit it to the chief administrative officer of the county from which the
15 commissioner is appointed.
16 5–825.
17 (a) An applicant for appointment as commissioner shall file the financial
18 disclosure statement required by this part electronically with the Ethics Commission.
19 (b) (1) Except as provided in paragraph (2) of this subsection, an applicant
20 shall also print a paper copy of the electronically filed statement and submit it to:
21 (I) the chief administrative officer of the county from which the
22 applicant seeks appointment; AND
23 (II) IN PRINCE GEORGE’S COUNTY, THE PRINCE GEORGE’S
24 COUNTY OFFICE OF ETHICS AND ACCOUNTABILITY.
25 5–826.
26 (a) (1) Except as provided in paragraph (2) of this subsection, the chief
27 administrative officer of a county shall transmit each financial disclosure statement of a
28 commissioner or appointed applicant to the executive director of the appropriate bicounty
29 commission AND, IN PRINCE GEORGE’S COUNTY, THE PRINCE GEORGE’S COUNTY
30 OFFICE OF ETHICS AND ACCOUNTABILITY.

1 (2) In Montgomery County, the county council shall transmit each
2 financial disclosure statement of a commissioner or appointed applicant to the
3 Maryland–National Capital Park and Planning Commission to the executive director of the
4 commission.
5 (b) The FINANCIAL DISCLOSURE STATEMENT SHALL BE RETAINED BY THE
6 FOLLOWING INDIVIDUALS AND ENTITIES FOR THE ENTIRE TERM OF OFFICE OF THE
7 COMMISSIONER:
8 (1) THE executive director [and];
9 (2) IN MONTGOMERY COUNTY, the chief administrative officer [shall
10 retain the statement for the entire term of office of the commissioner]; AND
11 (3) IN PRINCE GEORGE’S COUNTY, THE PRINCE GEORGE’S COUNTY
12 OFFICE OF ETHICS AND ACCOUNTABILITY.
13 (c) (1) [Within 15 days after an appointment to a bicounty commission has
14 become final, if] IF an applicant provided a paper copy of a financial disclosure
15 STATEMENT, THE FOLLOWING INDIVIDUALS AND ENTITIES, WITHIN 15 DAYS AFTER
16 AN APPOINTMENT TO A BICOUNTY COMMISSION HAS BECOME FINAL, SHALL RETURN
17 TO EACH APPLICANT WHO IS NOT APPOINTED THE ORIGINAL AND ALL COPIES OF
18 THE STATEMENT SUBMITTED BY THAT APPLICANT:
19 (I) the county council [and];
20 (II) IN MONTGOMERY COUNTY, the chief administrative officer [of
21 the county involved shall return to each applicant who is not appointed the original and all
22 copies of the statement submitted by that applicant]; AND
23 (III) IN PRINCE GEORGE’S COUNTY, THE PRINCE GEORGE’S
24 COUNTY OFFICE OF ETHICS AND ACCOUNTABILITY.
25 (2) On notification by the chief administrative officer [of the county
26 involved] FOR MONTGOMERY COUNTY OR THE PRINCE GEORGE’S COUNTY OFFICE
27 OF ETHICS AND ACCOUNTABILITY that an applicant was not appointed, the Ethics
28 Commission shall promptly delete the FINANCIAL DISCLOSURE statement electronically
29 filed by the applicant.
30 5–827.
31 (a) The Ethics Commission, the executive director of each bicounty commission,
32 [and] the chief administrative officer [of each county] FOR MONTGOMERY COUNTY, AND
33 THE PRINCE GEORGE’S COUNTY OFFICE OF ETHICS AND ACCOUNTABILITY:
4 HOUSE BILL 328
1 (1) shall maintain financial disclosure statements of commissioners and
2 appointed applicants received under this part;
3 (2) shall make the statements available to the public for examination and
4 copying during normal office hours; and
5 (3) may charge a reasonable fee and adopt reasonable administrative
6 procedures for the examination and copying of a statement.
7 (b) The Ethics Commission, the executive director of each bicounty commission,
8 [and] the chief administrative officer [of each county] FOR MONTGOMERY COUNTY, AND
9 THE PRINCE GEORGE’S COUNTY OFFICE OF ETHICS AND ACCOUNTABILITY shall
10 require that any person examining or copying a statement shall record:
11 (1) the person’s name and home address; and
12 (2) the name of the individual whose statement was examined or copied.
13 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
14 October 1, 2026.
