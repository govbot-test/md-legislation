1 AN ACT concerning
2 Income Tax – Subtraction Modification for Classroom Supplies Purchased by
3 Teachers – Alteration
4 FOR the purpose of altering a subtraction modification under the Maryland income tax for
5 unreimbursed expenses paid or incurred by certain teachers during a taxable year
6 for the purchase of certain classroom supplies to include certain teachers employed
7 in certain prekindergarten programs in the State; and generally relating to a
8 subtraction modification under the Maryland income tax for classroom supplies
9 purchased by teachers.

2 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
3 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
4 Article – Tax – General
5 10–208.
6 (a) In addition to the modification under § 10–207 of this subtitle, the amounts
7 under this section are subtracted from the federal adjusted gross income of a resident to
8 determine Maryland adjusted gross income.
9 (x) (1) In this subsection, “eligible teacher” means an individual who is
10 EMPLOYED ON A FULL–TIME BASIS FOR AN ACADEMIC YEAR ENDING DURING THE
11 TAXABLE YEAR AS:
12 (I) 1. A PREKINDERGARTEN CLASSROOM TEACHER IN A
13 PUBLICLY FUNDED PREKINDERGARTEN PROGRAM IN THE STATE AT AN ELIGIBLE
14 PUBLIC PROVIDER, AS DEFINED IN § 7–1A–01 OF THE EDUCATION ARTICLE; OR
15 2. A LEAD TEACHER OR CONSULTING TEACHER IN A
16 PUBLICLY FUNDED PREKINDERGARTEN PROGRAM AT AN ELIGIBLE PRIVATE
17 PROVIDER, AS DEFINED IN § 7–1A–01 OF THE EDUCATION ARTICLE; OR
18 (II) a kindergarten through grade 12 classroom teacher in an
19 elementary or secondary school in the State [on a full–time basis for an academic year
20 ending during the taxable year].
21 (2) Subject to paragraph (3) of this subsection, the subtraction allowed
22 under subsection (a) of this section includes up to $250 of the unreimbursed expenses paid
23 or incurred by an eligible teacher during a taxable year for the purchase of classroom
24 supplies if the supplies are used by:
25 (i) students in the classroom; or
26 (ii) the eligible teacher to prepare for or during classroom teaching.
27 (3) The amount allowed as a subtraction under paragraph (2) of this
28 subsection does not include an expense that is subtracted from federal adjusted gross
29 income under § 62 of the Internal Revenue Code.
30 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
31 1, 2026, and shall be applicable to all taxable years beginning after December 31, 2025.
