Section 1:
WES MOORE, Governor Ch. 93
Chapter 93
AN ACT concerning
Human Services – Department of Aging and Commission on Aging
(The Longevity Ready Maryland Act)
FOR the purpose of requiring the Secretary of Aging to lead implementation of the
Longevity Ready Maryland Plan; repealing, adding, and altering certain duties of
the Secretary to enable the implementation, reporting, and evolution of the
Longevity Ready Maryland Plan; altering the membership and duties of the
Commission on Aging; requiring the Department of Aging to staff the Commission;
and generally relating to the Longevity Ready Maryland Plan, the Department of
Aging, and the Commission on Aging.
BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
Article – Human Services

Section 2:
Section 10–101
Annotated Code of Maryland
(2019 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
(As enacted by Chapters 33 and 34 of 2025)
BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
Article – Human Services

Section 3:
Section 10–204, 10–206, and 10–208
Annotated Code of Maryland
(2019 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

Section 4:
SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
Article – Human Services
10–101.
(a) In this title the following words have the meanings indicated.
(b) “Area agency on aging” means the local agency that the Department
designates in accordance with the Older Americans Act of 1965 to administer the delivery
of a comprehensive and coordinated [plan] SYSTEM of social and other services and
activities for older adults in a planning and service area.
(c) “Community for a Lifetime” means a community certified under § 10–804 of
this title.
– 1 –
Ch. 93 2026 LAWS OF MARYLAND
(d) “Department” means the Department of Aging.
(E) “LONGEVITY READY MARYLAND PLAN” MEANS A MULTISECTOR PLAN
ON AGING THAT:
(1) TRANSFORMS PROGRAMS, SERVICES, POLICIES, INSTITUTIONS,
AND SYSTEMS TO ADDRESS THE GROWING POPULATION OF OLDER ADULTS; AND
(2) INCORPORATES STREAMLINED, INNOVATIVE APPROACHES TO
AGING SERVICES.
[(e)] (F) “Planning and service area” means an area of the State that the
Department designates in accordance with the Older Americans Act of 1965 for the
planning and administration of social, health, and other services for older adults.
[(f)] (G) “Secretary” means the Secretary of Aging.
10–204.
(a) The Secretary shall administer the programs and activities that the federal
government delegates to the State under the Older Americans Act of 1965 that are not
otherwise committed by law to another unit of State government.
(b) The Secretary:
(1) is a member of the Governor’s Executive Council; and
(2) shall cooperate with and receive the cooperation of State, federal, and
local governmental units to carry out the purposes of this title.
(c) The Secretary shall LEAD IMPLEMENTATION OF THE LONGEVITY READY
MARYLAND PLAN, WITH THE FOLLOWING GOALS:
(1) BUILDING A LONGEVITY ECOSYSTEM;
(2) PROMOTING ECONOMIC OPPORTUNITY;
(3) PREPARING STATE RESIDENTS TO AFFORD LONGEVITY; AND
(4) OPTIMIZING HEALTH, WELLNESS, AND MOBILITY.
(D) TO IMPLEMENT THE LONGEVITY READY MARYLAND PLAN, THE
SECRETARY SHALL:

WES MOORE, Governor Ch. 93
(1) evaluate the service needs of [seniors] OLDER ADULTS in the State;
(2) determine the extent to which existing public and private programs
[meet the needs of seniors], POLICIES, INSTITUTIONS, AND SYSTEMS SUPPORT OLDER
ADULTS;
(3) establish priorities for meeting the needs of [seniors] OLDER ADULTS;
AND
(4) coordinate, subject to existing law, assess and evaluate, and educate the
public and professionals about all State and local programs and services, both public and
private, that:
(I) relate and are important to the well–being of [seniors] OLDER
ADULTS in the State[, including programs and services in the areas of:
(i) income maintenance;
(ii) public health;
(iii) mental health;
(iv) housing and urban development;
(v) employment;
(vi) education;
(vii) recreation; and
(viii) rehabilitation of seniors with physical or mental disabilities; and
(5) develop a statewide plan incorporating local plans for a comprehensive
and coordinated system of health, social, and community services for seniors, including
housing, and institutional and noninstitutional care]; AND
(II) ARE DESIGNED TO ASSIST OLDER ADULTS AND FAMILY
CAREGIVERS IN LEADING INDEPENDENT AND DIGNIFIED LIVES IN THEIR OWN
HOMES AND COMMUNITIES.
[(d)] (E) The Secretary shall:
(1) represent the interests of [seniors] OLDER ADULTS by serving as an
advocate FOR POLICY, SYSTEMS, AND INSTITUTIONAL CHANGE at all levels of
– 3 –
Ch. 93 2026 LAWS OF MARYLAND
government AND WITHIN THE PRIVATE SECTOR IN SUPPORT OF THE LONGEVITY
READY MARYLAND PLAN;
(2) [consult with and advise the secretaries of the principal departments of
State government] EMPHASIZE CROSS–SECTOR COLLABORATION, INCLUDING
CONSULTING AND ADVISING UNITS OF STATE GOVERNMENT AND STATE BOARDS
AND COMMISSIONS about [the programs and services for seniors that are the primary
responsibility of those departments] ANY ACTION THE UNIT, BOARD, OR COMMISSION
MAY TAKE TO ASSIST IN IMPLEMENTING THE LONGEVITY READY MARYLAND PLAN;
(3) consult with the Commission on Aging on [all matters pertaining to
programs for seniors] IMPLEMENTATION OF THE LONGEVITY READY MARYLAND
PLAN;
(4) provide consultation and technical assistance to [communities and civic
groups] ORGANIZATIONS AND OTHER ENTITIES OR PERSONS developing local services
for [seniors] OLDER ADULTS IN PARTNERSHIP WITH AREA AGENCIES ON AGING AND
OTHER ORGANIZATIONS AND ENTITIES FUNDED BY THE DEPARTMENT;
(5) maintain a [clearinghouse of information] SYSTEM THAT PROVIDES
INFORMATION, REFERRALS, AND COUNSELING FOR OLDER ADULTS AND THEIR
CAREGIVERS related to the interests of [seniors] OLDER ADULTS AND THE
IMPLEMENTATION OF THE LONGEVITY READY MARYLAND PLAN; and
(6) review and recommend [policies] LEGISLATION AND OTHER POLICY
ACTION to the Governor on publicly funded plans [and], programs, INSTITUTIONS, AND
SYSTEMS that affect [seniors] OLDER ADULTS AND THE IMPLEMENTATION OF THE
LONGEVITY READY MARYLAND PLAN; AND
(7) MAINTAIN A PUBLICLY ACCESSIBLE RESOURCE ON THE
DEPARTMENT’S WEBSITE FOR MONITORING IMPLEMENTATION OF THE LONGEVITY
READY MARYLAND PLAN.
[(e)] (F) The Secretary may adopt regulations to carry out the provisions of law
that are within the jurisdiction of the Secretary.
10–206.
(a) On or before January 1 each year, the Secretary shall report to the Governor
and, subject to § 2–1257 of the State Government Article, to the General Assembly ON:
(1) THE ALLOCATION OF FUNDS MADE AVAILABLE FOR SENIOR
CITIZEN ACTIVITIES CENTERS DISTRIBUTED BY THE DEPARTMENT; AND

(2) A SUMMARY OF KEY ACTIVITIES FUNDED AT SENIOR CITIZEN
ACTIVITIES CENTERS.
[(b) The report required under subsection (a) of this section that shall include:
(1) with the advice and recommendation of the Commission on Aging:
(i) a description of the senior citizen activities centers in each
county;
(ii) the allocation and use of funds made available for senior citizen
activities centers;
(iii) the results of any studies; and
(iv) any recommendations for legislation; and
(2) information regarding the operation and performance of accessible
housing services counseling provided by the Aging and Disability Resource Center
Program.]
(B) (1) ON OR BEFORE JANUARY 1 EACH YEAR, THE SECRETARY SHALL
REPORT TO THE GOVERNOR AND, SUBJECT TO § 2–1257 OF THE STATE
GOVERNMENT ARTICLE, TO THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY.
(2) THE REPORT REQUIRED UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS
SUBSECTION SHALL INCLUDE A SUMMARY OF:
(I) THE PROGRESS MADE DURING THE IMMEDIATELY
PRECEDING YEAR TO IMPLEMENT THE MOST RECENTLY PUBLISHED
COMPREHENSIVE LONGEVITY READY MARYLAND PLAN;
(II) RECOMMENDATIONS FOR LEGISLATION, POLICY CHANGE,
OR OTHER RELATED PLANNING OR ADVOCACY EFFORTS MADE BY THE COMMISSION
ON AGING TO THE DEPARTMENT;
(III) ANY STUDY, SURVEY, OR OTHER FORM OF RESEARCH
CONDUCTED BY THE DEPARTMENT OR THE COMMISSION ON AGING; AND
(IV) ANY AVAILABLE RESEARCH AND BEST PRACTICES
REVIEWED BY THE COMMISSION ON AGING.
– 5 –
Ch. 93 2026 LAWS OF MARYLAND
(C) ON OR BEFORE JULY 1, 2029, AND EVERY 4 YEARS ON JULY 1
THEREAFTER, THE SECRETARY SHALL PROVIDE TO THE GOVERNOR AND, SUBJECT
TO § 2–1257 OF THE STATE GOVERNMENT ARTICLE, TO THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY
AN UPDATED COMPREHENSIVE LONGEVITY READY MARYLAND PLAN THAT:
(1) COMPLIES WITH § 10–204 OF THIS SUBTITLE;
(2) INCLUDES PROGRESS MADE ON EXISTING GOALS, OBJECTIVES,
AND STRATEGIES IDENTIFIED IN THE IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING LONGEVITY READY
MARYLAND PLAN;
(3) MAINTAINS EXISTING GOALS AND INCLUDES ANY ADDITIONAL
GOALS; AND
(4) UPDATES OBJECTIVES AND STRATEGIES IF APPROPRIATE BASED
ON EVOLVING CIRCUMSTANCES AND POPULATION PROJECTIONS; AND
(5) IS INFORMED BY THE STATE PLAN ON AGING AS REQUIRED BY
THE OLDER AMERICANS ACT OF 1965 AND OTHER RELEVANT STATE AND LOCAL
PLANS AND AGENCY COLLABORATIONS.
10–208.
(a) There is a Commission on Aging in the Department.
(b) (1) The Commission consists of [13] THE FOLLOWING members
[appointed by the Governor as follows]:
(i) [1 shall be a] ONE member of the Senate of Maryland, [who may
not vote] APPOINTED BY THE PRESIDENT OF THE SENATE;
(ii) [1 shall be a] ONE member of the [Maryland] House of
Delegates, [who may not vote] APPOINTED BY THE SPEAKER OF THE HOUSE; [and]
(iii) [11 shall be selected to reflect the geographic diversity of the
State and because of their interest in the needs of seniors] THE SECRETARY OF AGING,
OR THE SECRETARY’S DESIGNEE; AND
(IV) THE FOLLOWING MEMBERS, APPOINTED BY THE
GOVERNOR:

the Commission on October 1, [2007] 2026.
(3) A member of the Commission appointed under subsection [(b)(1)(iii)]
(B)(1)(IV) of this section may not serve more than two consecutive terms.
(4) At the end of a term, a member continues to serve until a successor is
appointed and qualifies.
(5) A member who is appointed after a term has begun serves only for the
rest of the term and until a successor is appointed and qualifies.
(6) A MEMBER WHO FAILS TO ATTEND AT LEAST 50% OF THE
MEETINGS DURING ANY CONSECUTIVE 12–MONTH PERIOD SHALL BE CONSIDERED
TO HAVE RESIGNED.

(I) shall designate [a] THE chair OF THE COMMISSION; AND

(1) may not receive compensation as a member of the Commission; but

SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That:
(a) The terms of the members of the Commission on Aging serving on October 1,
2026, shall continue until the expiration of the member’s term.
(b) The terms of the members of the Commission on Aging appointed in
accordance with § 10–208(b)(1)(iv) of the Human Services Article, as enacted by Section 1
of this Act, shall expire as follows:
(1) four in 2028;
(2) five in 2029; and
(3) five in 2030.
