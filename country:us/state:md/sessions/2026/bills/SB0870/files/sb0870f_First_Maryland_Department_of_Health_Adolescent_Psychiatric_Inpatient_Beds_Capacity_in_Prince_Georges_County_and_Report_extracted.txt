A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Maryland Department of Health – Adolescent Psychiatric Inpatient Beds –
3 Capacity in Prince George’s County and Report
4 FOR the purpose of requiring the Maryland Department of Health to ensure the
5 establishment and ongoing operation of a minimum number of licensed adolescent
6 psychiatric inpatient beds in Prince George’s County; requiring, under certain
7 circumstances, the Department to issue a request for proposals to establish
8 adolescent psychiatric inpatient beds in the county and to administer a certain grant
9 program; requiring the Department to report to the Governor and the General
10 Assembly on certain information related to adolescent psychiatric inpatient bed
11 capacity in the State; and generally relating to adolescent psychiatric inpatient bed
12 capacity.
13 BY adding to
14 Article – Health – General
15 Section 19–310.7
16 Annotated Code of Maryland
17 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
18 Preamble
19 WHEREAS, Maryland hospitals experience pediatric psychiatric overstay and
20 boarding pressures, and State law recognizes the occurrence of pediatric psychiatric
21 overstays and the need to transfer youth to appropriate treatment settings when clinically
22 indicated; and
23 WHEREAS, Prince George’s County has experienced reductions in adolescent
24 psychiatric inpatient service capacity, including the revocation of adolescent psychiatric
25 inpatient bed licenses; and
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from exist in g l aw . *sb0870*
2 SENATE BILL 870
1 WHEREAS, The Maryland Department of Health maintains a statewide psychiatric
2 bed registry and referral system to identify available inpatient psychiatric beds and to
3 coordinate behavioral health placement; and
4 WHEREAS, State and local behavioral health planning documents identify an
5 ongoing need to expand and strengthen the quality and availability of behavioral health
6 services for children and youth; now, therefore,
7 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
8 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
9 Article – Health – General
10 19–310.7.
11 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
12 INDICATED.
13 (2) “ADOLESCENT” MEANS AN INDIVIDUAL AT LEAST 12 YEARS OLD
14 AND UNDER THE AGE OF 17 YEARS.
15 (3) “ADOLESCENT PSYCHIATRIC INPATIENT BED” MEANS A UNIT OF
16 LICENSED INPATIENT PSYCHIATRIC CAPACITY THAT PROVIDES OVERNIGHT,
17 24–HOUR, FACILITY–BASED PSYCHIATRIC EVALUATION, STABILIZATION, AND
18 TREATMENT FOR ADOLESCENTS.
19 (B) (1) THE DEPARTMENT, IN CONSULTATION WITH THE PRINCE
20 GEORGE’S COUNTY LOCAL BEHAVIORAL HEALTH AUTHORITY, SHALL ENSURE THE
21 ESTABLISHMENT AND ONGOING OPERATION OF AT LEAST 24 ADOLESCENT
22 PSYCHIATRIC INPATIENT BEDS IN PRINCE GEORGE’S COUNTY BY:
23 (I) EXPANDING THE LICENSED INPATIENT BED CAPACITY AT
24 AN EXISTING INPATIENT FACILITY IN PRINCE GEORGE’S COUNTY;
25 (II) CONTRACTING WITH A PRIVATE OR NONPROFIT OPERATOR
26 THAT WILL ESTABLISH AND OPERATE A LICENSED ADOLESCENT PSYCHIATRIC
27 INPATIENT FACILITY; OR
28 (III) ESTABLISHING A PUBLIC–PRIVATE PARTNERSHIP TO
29 FINANCE AND OPERATE ADOLESCENT PSYCHIATRIC INPATIENT BEDS.
30 (2) IN CARRYING OUT PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION, THE
31 DEPARTMENT SHALL PRIORITIZE SOLUTIONS THAT:
SENATE BILL 870 3
1 (I) REDUCE PEDIATRIC PSYCHIATRIC EMERGENCY
2 DEPARTMENT BOARDING AND INPATIENT OVERSTAYS;
3 (II) ARE REFLECTED IN AND MEASURABLE THROUGH THE
4 STATE’S INPATIENT PSYCHIATRIC BED REGISTRY AND REFERRAL SYSTEM; AND
5 (III) ENSURE ACCESS REGARDLESS OF PAYER SOURCE,
6 INCLUDING THE MARYLAND MEDICAL ASSISTANCE PROGRAM.
7 (3) (I) IF THE DEPARTMENT DETERMINES THAT EXPANDING BED
8 CAPACITY UNDER PARAGRAPH (1)(I) OF THIS SUBSECTION WILL NOT MEET THE
9 DEADLINE ESTABLISHED UNDER PARAGRAPH (4) OF THIS SUBSECTION, ON OR
10 BEFORE JUNE 30, 2027, THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ISSUE A REQUEST FOR
11 PROPOSALS TO ESTABLISH THE ADOLESCENT PSYCHIATRIC INPATIENT BEDS.
12 (II) A REQUEST FOR PROPOSALS ISSUED UNDER
13 SUBPARAGRAPH (I) OF THIS PARAGRAPH SHALL BE DISTRIBUTED TO, AT A
14 MINIMUM, PRIVATE OR NONPROFIT OPERATORS WITH DEMONSTRATED
15 EXPERIENCE IN ESTABLISHING ADOLESCENT PSYCHIATRIC INPATIENT BED
16 CAPACITY IN THE STATE.
17 (4) ON OR BEFORE DECEMBER 31, 2028, THE DEPARTMENT SHALL
18 ENSURE THAT THE ADOLESCENT PSYCHIATRIC INPATIENT BEDS REQUIRED UNDER
19 PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION ARE FULLY OPERATIONAL AND AVAILABLE
20 FOR PATIENT ADMISSION.
21 (C) (1) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ADMINISTER A COMPETITIVE GRANT
22 PROGRAM TO ESTABLISH THE ADOLESCENT PSYCHIATRIC INPATIENT BEDS
23 REQUIRED UNDER SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION.
24 (2) (I) GRANT FUNDS AWARDED UNDER A PROGRAM
25 ADMINISTERED UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION MAY BE DISBURSED
26 ONLY TO:
27 1. HOSPITALS;
28 2. PSYCHIATRIC FACILITIES;
29 3. NONPROFIT HEALTH CARE PROVIDERS; OR
30 4. PUBLIC–PRIVATE PARTNERSHIPS.
4 SENATE BILL 870
1 (II) GRANT FUNDS AWARDED UNDER A PROGRAM
2 ADMINISTERED UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION MAY BE USED ONLY
3 FOR THE FOLLOWING CAPITAL COSTS ASSOCIATED WITH OPERATING THE
4 ADOLESCENT PSYCHIATRIC INPATIENT BEDS:
5 1. RENOVATION;
6 2. CONSTRUCTION;
7 3. SAFETY OR LIGATURE UPGRADES; OR
8 4. WORKFORCE START–UP EXPENSES.
9 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That:
10 (a) On or before October 1, 2027, and annually thereafter until 2030, the
11 Maryland Department of Health shall report to the Governor and, in accordance with §
12 2–1257 of the State Government Article, the General Assembly on:
13 (1) the number and location of licensed adolescent psychiatric inpatient
14 beds by county;
15 (2) metrics for pediatric psychiatric emergency department boarding and
16 overstay;
17 (3) any barriers to staffing licensed adolescent psychiatric inpatient beds,
18 including licensure barriers; and
19 (4) recommendations for rate or regulatory changes needed to maintain
20 sufficient capacity of licensed adolescent psychiatric inpatient beds.
21 (b) The Department shall use available data systems, including the State
22 inpatient psychiatric registry and referral system, to generate data for the report required
23 under subsection (a) of this section.
24 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
25 1, 2026.
