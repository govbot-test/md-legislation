18 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
19 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
20 Article – Tax – General
21 10–208.
22 (a) In addition to the modification under § 10–207 of this subtitle, the amounts
23 under this section are subtracted from the federal adjusted gross income of a resident to
24 determine Maryland adjusted gross income.
25 (d) (1) In this subsection:
26 (i) “enhanced agricultural management equipment” means:
27 1. a planter or drill that:
28 A. is commonly known as a “no–till” planter or drill; and
29 B. is designed to minimize the disturbance of the soil in
30 planting crops;
31 2. liquid manure soil injection equipment that is designed to
32 inject manure into the soil to reduce nutrient runoff;
HOUSE BILL 547 3
1 3. a deep no–till ripper that does not invert the soil profile
2 and is used to address compaction in high residue cropping systems;
3 4. poultry or livestock manure spreading equipment used by
4 a farm owner or tenant on farmland in accordance with a nutrient management plan
5 prepared by an individual licensed by the Secretary of Agriculture in accordance with Title
6 8, Subtitle 8 of the Agriculture Article if the manure spreading equipment is used:
7 A. to spread poultry manure and bedding from normal
8 poultry production with a capability of being calibrated to 1 ton per acre; or
9 B. to apply solid or liquid livestock waste;
10 5. vertical tillage equipment used to incorporate livestock
11 manure or poultry litter into the soil;
12 6. a global positioning system device used for management of
13 agricultural nutrient applications; [and]
14 7. an integrated optical sensing and nutrient application
15 system that measures crop status and applies the crop’s nitrogen requirements at variable
16 rates based on predicted in–season yield potential for the crop and the predicted
17 responsiveness of the crop to additional nitrogen; and
18 8. OTHER EQUIPMENT THAT THE SECRETARY OF
19 AGRICULTURE DETERMINES BY REGULATION TO QUALIFY AS ENHANCED
20 AGRICULTURAL MANAGEMENT EQUIPMENT; AND
21 (ii) “enhanced agricultural management equipment” includes
22 equipment that attaches to or is pulled by equipment listed in item (i) of this paragraph.
23 (2) Except as provided in paragraph (3) of this subsection, the subtraction
24 under subsection (a) of this section includes 100% of the expenses that a taxpayer incurs to
25 buy and install enhanced agricultural management equipment if:
26 (i) the equipment has a useful life of at least 4 years;
27 (ii) the taxpayer:
28 1. bought the equipment:
29 A. after December 31, 1985, if the equipment is a planter or
30 drill;
31 B. after December 31, 1989, if the equipment is liquid
32 manure soil injection equipment;
4 HOUSE BILL 547
1 C. after December 31, 1997, if the equipment is poultry or
2 livestock manure spreading equipment;
3 D. after December 31, 2001, if the equipment is a deep no–till
4 ripper that does not invert the soil profile; or
5 E. after December 31, 2012, if the equipment is a global
6 positioning system device used for management of agricultural nutrient applications or an
7 integrated optical sensing and nutrient application system;
8 2. owns the equipment for at least 3 years after the taxable
9 year in which the subtraction is made; and
10 3. uses the equipment in agricultural production; and
11 (iii) for liquid manure soil injection equipment, the equipment is:
12 1. used on land upon which farm products, as defined under
13 § 10–601 of the Agriculture Article, are raised; and
14 2. not used to inject sludge into the soil.
15 (3) The subtraction under subsection (a) of this section includes 50% of the
16 expenses that a taxpayer incurs to buy and install enhanced agricultural management
17 equipment that is vertical tillage equipment used to incorporate livestock manure or
18 poultry litter into the soil if:
19 (i) the equipment has a useful life of at least 4 years; and
20 (ii) the taxpayer:
21 1. bought the equipment after December 31, 2012;
22 2. owns the equipment for at least 3 years after the taxable
23 year in which the subtraction is made; and
24 3. uses the equipment in agricultural production.
25 (4) To qualify for the subtraction under paragraphs (2) and (3) of this
26 subsection, a taxpayer shall file a statement from the Department of Agriculture certifying
27 compliance with the requirements of this section.
28 (5) If the subtraction allowed under paragraphs (2) and (3) of this
29 subsection exceeds the Maryland taxable income that is computed without the modification
30 allowed under this subsection and the subtraction is not used for the taxable year, the

1 excess may be carried over to succeeding taxable years, not to exceed 5, until the full
2 amount of the subtraction is used.
3 (6) THE ON OR BEFORE DECEMBER 1, 2026, AND EACH DECEMBER 1
4 THEREAFTER, THE SECRETARY OF AGRICULTURE MAY ADOPT REGULATIONS TO
5 ESTABLISH SHALL REPORT TO THE GOVERNOR AND, IN ACCORDANCE WITH § 2–1257
6 OF THE STATE GOVERNMENT ARTICLE, THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY ON ADDITIONAL
7 TYPES OF EQUIPMENT THAT THE SECRETARY RECOMMENDS SHOULD QUALIFY AS
8 ENHANCED AGRICULTURAL MANAGEMENT EQUIPMENT UNDER PARAGRAPH (1)(I)8
9 OF THIS SUBSECTION.
10 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That the Laws of Maryland read
11 as follows:
12 Article – Tax – General
13 10–758.
14 (A) SUBJECT TO THE LIMITATIONS OF THIS SECTION, A TAXPAYER WHO IS A
15 PARENT OF A STILLBORN CHILD MAY CLAIM A CREDIT AGAINST THE STATE INCOME
16 TAX IN AN AMOUNT EQUAL TO $1,000 FOR EACH BIRTH FOR WHICH A CERTIFICATE
17 OF BIRTH RESULTING IN STILLBIRTH HAS BEEN ISSUED UNDER § 4–213.1 OF THE
18 HEALTH – GENERAL ARTICLE OR FOR WHICH A CERTIFICATE OF FETAL DEATH OR
19 ITS EQUIVALENT HAS BEEN ISSUED BY ANOTHER STATE.
20 (B) THE AMOUNT OF THE CREDIT SHALL BE REDUCED BY $50 FOR EACH
21 $1,000, OR FRACTION THEREOF, BY WHICH THE TAXPAYER’S FEDERAL ADJUSTED
22 GROSS INCOME EXCEEDS:
23 (1) $100,000 IN THE CASE OF AN INDIVIDUAL; OR
24 (2) $150,000 IN THE CASE OF A MARRIED COUPLE FILING A JOINT
25 INCOME TAX RETURN.
26 (C) THE CREDIT ALLOWED UNDER THIS SECTION MAY BE CLAIMED ONLY FOR
27 THE TAXABLE YEAR IN WHICH THE STILLBIRTH OCCURRED.
28 (D) IF THE CREDIT ALLOWED UNDER THIS SECTION IN ANY TAXABLE YEAR
29 EXCEEDS THE STATE INCOME TAX FOR THAT TAXABLE YEAR, THE TAXPAYER MAY
30 CLAIM A REFUND IN THE AMOUNT OF THE EXCESS.
31 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That Section 2 of this Act shall be
32 applicable to all taxable years beginning after December 31, 2026.
