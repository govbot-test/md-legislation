CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Economic Development – Rural Readiness Program and Rural Maryland
3 Capacity Building Fund – Establishment
4 FOR the purpose of establishing the Rural Readiness Program administered by the Rural
5 Maryland Council to provide assistance to rural communities to enhance their
6 capacity for economic development; requiring priority to be given when making
7 certain grants to entities that have completed the Rural Readiness Program;
8 establishing the Rural Maryland Capacity Building Fund as a special, nonlapsing
9 fund to be administered by the Council to provide grants to support certain planning
10 and capacity building initiatives; and generally relating to rural communities.

12 Article – Economic Development

18 Article – Economic Development

20 Annotated Code of Maryland
21 (2024 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

13 (B) THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY FINDS THAT:
14 (1) RURAL COMMUNITIES ARE OFTEN DISADVANTAGED WHEN
15 COMPETING FOR FINANCIAL AND OTHER RESOURCES DUE TO:
16 (I) A LACK OF CAPACITY AND POPULATION DENSITY
17 RESULTING IN LIMITED RESOURCES DUE TO A SMALLER TAX BASE; AND
18 (II) LIMITED ACCESS TO CRITICAL INFRASTRUCTURE,
19 INCLUDING BROADBAND AND TRANSPORTATION;
20 (2) A DEFICIENCY EXISTS IN THE ESSENTIAL ELEMENTS NEEDED FOR
21 RURAL COMMUNITIES TO THRIVE AND ADDRESS THE NEEDS OF THE RURAL
22 COMMUNITY;
23 (3) RURAL COMMUNITIES STRUGGLE TO RECRUIT AND RETAIN
24 SKILLED STAFF NEEDED TO IMPLEMENT AND MANAGE PROJECTS PARTICULARLY IN
25 TECHNICAL SUPPORT FOR ECONOMIC AND COMMUNITY DEVELOPMENT;
26 (4) RURAL COMMUNITIES EXPERIENCE CHALLENGES IN SOCIAL
27 COHESION AND NETWORKS THAT MAKE IT HARD TO MOBILIZE RESOURCES AND
28 COLLECTIVELY ADDRESS ISSUES;

5 WILL MANIFEST AS DISPARITIES IN HEALTH CARE ACCESS, EDUCATIONAL
6 ATTAINMENT, ECONOMIC OPPORTUNITIES, AND INFRASTRUCTURE DEVELOPMENT.
7 (C) THERE IS A RURAL READINESS PROGRAM ADMINISTERED BY THE
8 COUNCIL.

10 IN RURAL COMMUNITIES FOR ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT BY ASSISTING ELIGIBLE
11 APPLICANTS ACCEPTED INTO THE PROGRAM WITH GRANT APPLICATIONS AND
12 DEVELOPING PROJECTS THAT GENERATE REVENUE.
13 (2) THE COUNCIL SHALL ESTABLISH ASSESSMENTS, TOOLKITS, AND
14 OTHER RESOURCES TO SUPPORT THE PURPOSE OF THE PROGRAM.
15 (E) (1) ELIGIBLE APPLICANTS MAY APPLY TO THE COUNCIL TO
16 PARTICIPATE IN THE PROGRAM.
17 (2) THE COUNCIL’S EXECUTIVE BOARD SHALL ESTABLISH:
18 (I) CRITERIA TO EVALUATE ELIGIBLE APPLICANTS FOR

20 (II) PARTICIPATION GUIDELINES.

25 AND RURAL DEVELOPMENT ASSISTANCE BOARD ESTABLISHED IN § 2–206 OF THIS

1 (4) “ELIGIBLE APPLICANT” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 13–416

4 FUND.
5 (6) “RURAL COMMUNITY” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 2–207 OF
6 THIS SUBTITLE.
7 (B) (1) THERE IS A RURAL MARYLAND CAPACITY BUILDING FUND.
8 (2) THE PURPOSE OF THE FUND IS TO SUPPORT COLLABORATIVE
9 PLANNING AND CAPACITY BUILDING INITIATIVES THAT ADDRESS THE NEEDS OF
10 RURAL COMMUNITIES.
11 (3) THE COUNCIL SHALL ADMINISTER THE FUND.
12 (4) (I) THE FUND IS A SPECIAL, NONLAPSING FUND THAT IS NOT
13 SUBJECT TO § 7–302 OF THE STATE FINANCE AND PROCUREMENT ARTICLE.
14 (II) THE STATE TREASURER SHALL HOLD THE FUND
15 SEPARATELY, AND THE COMPTROLLER SHALL ACCOUNT FOR THE FUND.
16 (5) THE FUND CONSISTS OF:
17 (I) MONEY APPROPRIATED IN THE STATE BUDGET TO THE
18 FUND; AND
19 (II) ANY OTHER MONEY FROM ANY OTHER SOURCE ACCEPTED
20 FOR THE BENEFIT OF THE FUND.
21 (6) THE FUND MAY BE USED ONLY FOR GRANTS PROVIDED UNDER
22 THIS SECTION.
23 (7) (I) THE STATE TREASURER SHALL INVEST THE MONEY OF THE
24 FUND IN THE SAME MANNER AS OTHER STATE MONEY MAY BE INVESTED.
25 (II) ANY INTEREST EARNINGS OF THE FUND SHALL BE
26 CREDITED TO THE GENERAL FUND OF THE STATE.
27 (8) EXPENDITURES FROM THE FUND MAY BE MADE ONLY IN
28 ACCORDANCE WITH THE STATE BUDGET.
HOUSE BILL 461 7
1 (C) (1) THE BOARD SHALL:
2 (I) ESTABLISH PROCEDURES FOR APPLICATIONS FOR GRANTS
3 PROVIDED UNDER THIS SECTION;
4 (II) REVIEW APPLICATIONS FOR GRANTS; AND
5 (III) APPROVE GRANT RECIPIENTS AND THE AMOUNT OF THE
6 GRANT AWARD.
7 (2) GRANTS MAY NOT BE AWARDED TO ELIGIBLE APPLICANTS THAT
8 HAVE NOT COMPLETED THE RURAL READINESS PROGRAM ESTABLISHED UNDER §
9 13–416 OF THE ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT ARTICLE.
10 (3) GRANTS SHALL BE AWARDED ON A COMPETITIVE BASIS.
11 (4) THE BOARD SHALL GIVE PRIORITY TO ELIGIBLE APPLICANTS
12 THAT HAVE:
13 (I) MULTIJURISDICTIONAL AND MULTISECTOR PROJECTS; AND
14 (II) PROVIDED SUFFICIENT EVIDENCE OF A COMMITMENT TO A
15 FUNDING MATCH OF AT LEAST 20% OF THE GRANT AMOUNT.
16 (5) A GRANT AMOUNT MAY NOT EXCEED $50,000.
17 (D) ELIGIBLE APPLICANTS MUST APPLY FOR A GRANT IN A MANNER
18 CONSISTENT WITH THE PROCEDURES ESTABLISHED BY THE BOARD.
19 (E) (1) GRANT FUNDS MAY BE USED TO SUPPORT:
20 (I) COLLABORATIVE PLANNING AND CAPACITY BUILDING
21 INITIATIVES THAT ADVANCE EFFORTS TO ADDRESS THE NEEDS OF RURAL
22 COMMUNITIES, INCLUDING:
23 1. HOUSING;
24 2. BROADBAND SERVICES;
25 3. PLACEMAKING;
26 4. ENERGY;
8 HOUSE BILL 461
1 5. TRANSPORTATION;
2 6. HEALTH;
3 7. INFRASTRUCTURE;
4 8 ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT; AND
5 9. WORKFORCE;
6 (II) READINESS FOR INVESTMENT IN AND DEVELOPMENT OF A
7 RURAL COMMUNITY OR PROPERTY IN A RURAL COMMUNITY, INCLUDING:
8 1. DEVELOPMENT OF PROPERTY INVENTORIES;
9 2. SITE DEVELOPMENT STUDIES;
10 3. SITE MATERIAL DEVELOPMENT;
11 4. SITE IMPLEMENTATION OR LAND ASSEMBLY
12 ACTIVITIES; AND
13 5. FEASIBILITY OR MARKET STUDIES;
14 (III) PARTNERSHIPS AND PLANNING TO BUILD READINESS FOR
15 FUTURE PROJECTS AND COLLABORATIVE INITIATIVES; AND
16 (IV) CROSS–SECTOR COLLABORATION FOR COMMUNITY AND
17 STAKEHOLDER ENGAGEMENT THAT FOSTERS PLANNING AND DIALOGUE.
18 (2) GRANT FUNDS MAY NOT BE USED TO:
19 (I) PAY OR REIMBURSE THE GRANTEE FOR EMPLOYEE SALARY,
20 WAGES, BENEFITS, OR OTHER INDIRECT COSTS OF THE GRANTEE THAT ARE NOT
21 RELATED TO THE PROJECT FOR WHICH A GRANT WAS AWARDED;
22 (II) FUND OPERATIONAL ACTIVITIES AT A FOR–PROFIT ENTITY;
23 (III) PURCHASE OR LEASE REAL PROPERTY; OR
24 (IV) SUPPORT A NONRURAL AREA.

1 (F) ON OR BEFORE OCTOBER 1 EACH YEAR, THE COUNCIL SHALL SUBMIT
2 TO THE GOVERNOR AND, IN ACCORDANCE WITH § 2–1257 OF THE STATE
3 GOVERNMENT ARTICLE, THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY A REPORT ON:
4 (1) THE NUMBER OF GRANTS MADE IN THE PREVIOUS FISCAL YEAR;
5 (2) THE NAME OF EACH GRANT RECIPIENT; AND
6 (3) THE PURPOSE AND USE OF EACH GRANT.
