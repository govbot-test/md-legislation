CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Health Occupations – Massage Therapy – Advertising
3 FOR the purpose of prohibiting licensed massage therapists, registered massage
4 practitioners, and third parties directed by licensed massage therapists or registered
5 massage practitioners from placing, publishing, or distributing certain
6 advertisements; requiring certain advertisements for massage therapy to include
7 certain information; and generally relating to massage therapy advertising.
8 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
9 Article – Health Occupations
10 Section 6–308(a)(11)
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
13 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
14 Article – Health Occupations
15 Section 6–401
16 Annotated Code of Maryland
17 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
18 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
19 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

1 Article – Health Occupations

3 (a) Subject to the hearing provisions of § 6–309 of this subtitle, the Board may
4 deny a license or registration to an applicant, reprimand a licensee or registration holder,
5 place any licensee or registration holder on probation, or suspend or revoke the license of a
6 licensee or the registration of a registration holder if the applicant, licensee, or registration
7 holder:
8 (11) Has violated any provision of this title;
9 6–401.
10 (a) The Board shall adopt regulations to [establish]:
11 (1) ESTABLISH standards for advertising or soliciting by licensed massage
12 therapists or registered massage practitioners;
13 (2) DEFINE SPECIFIC LANGUAGE AND CONTENT PROHIBITED UNDER
14 THIS SECTION; AND
15 (3) ESTABLISH PROCEDURES FOR REPORTING VIOLATIONS OF THIS
16 SECTION BY A LICENSED MASSAGE THERAPIST, REGISTERED MASSAGE
17 PRACTITIONER, OR MEMBER OF THE PUBLIC.
18 (b) For purposes of this section, the mailing of notices to patients to inform them
19 of times for periodic appointments is not advertising or soliciting.
20 (C) (1) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN PARAGRAPH (2) OF THIS SUBSECTION,
21 AN ADVERTISEMENT FOR MASSAGE THERAPY SERVICES SHALL INCLUDE THE FULL
22 NAME, AS STATED ON THE LICENSE OR REGISTRATION APPLICATION SUBMITTED TO
23 THE BOARD, AND LICENSE NUMBER OR REGISTRATION NUMBER OF THE LICENSED
24 MASSAGE THERAPIST OR REGISTERED MASSAGE PRACTITIONER PROVIDING THE
25 MASSAGE THERAPY SERVICES ADVERTISED.
26 (2) AN ADVERTISEMENT FOR MASSAGE THERAPY SERVICES PLACED,
27 PUBLISHED, OR DISTRIBUTED BY A BUSINESS THAT EMPLOYS OR CONTRACTS WITH
28 MULTIPLE LICENSED MASSAGE THERAPISTS OR REGISTERED MASSAGE
29 PRACTITIONERS IS NOT REQUIRED TO INCLUDE THE NAMES AND LICENSE OR
30 REGISTRATION NUMBERS OF EACH LICENSED MASSAGE THERAPIST OR REGISTERED
31 MASSAGE PRACTITIONER EMPLOYED OR CONTRACTED WITH IF AN ADVERTISEMENT
32 FOR MASSAGE THERAPY SERVICES LISTS THE NAME OF A LICENSED MASSAGE
33 THERAPIST OR A REGISTERED MASSAGE PRACTITIONER, THE NAME DISPLAYED ON
HOUSE BILL 975 3
1 THE ADVERTISEMENT MUST BE CONSISTENT WITH THE NAME OF THE INDIVIDUAL
2 THAT IS ON THE LICENSE OR REGISTRATION ISSUED BY THE BOARD.
3 [(c)] (D) [A] SUBJECT TO SUBSECTION (C) OF THIS SECTION, A licensed
4 massage therapist or registered massage practitioner may use a trade name in connection
5 with the practice of massage therapy provided that:
6 (1) The use of the trade name is not deceptive or misleading;
7 (2) The advertisement in which the trade name appears includes the name
8 of the licensed massage therapist or registered massage practitioner or the name of the
9 business entity providing the massage therapy services being advertised, as long as the
10 advertisement includes the name of a licensed massage therapist or registered massage
11 practitioner;
12 (3) The name of the licensed massage therapist or registered massage
13 practitioner providing massage therapy services appears on the billing invoices, stationery,
14 and any receipt given to a patient; and
15 (4) Treatment records are maintained that clearly identify the licensed
16 massage therapist or registered massage practitioner who has performed the massage
17 therapy service for the patient.
18 (E) (1) A LICENSED MASSAGE THERAPIST, REGISTERED MASSAGE
19 PRACTITIONER, OR A THIRD PARTY ACTING ON BEHALF OF A LICENSED MASSAGE
20 THERAPIST OR REGISTERED MASSAGE PRACTITIONER MAY NOT PLACE, PUBLISH, OR
21 DISTRIBUTE, OR CAUSE TO BE PLACED, PUBLISHED, OR DISTRIBUTED, AN
22 ADVERTISEMENT FOR MASSAGE THERAPY SERVICES:
23 (I) THAT STATES PROSTITUTION SERVICES, ESCORT SERVICES,
24 OR SEXUAL SERVICES ARE AVAILABLE;
25 (II) ON A WEBSITE KNOWN FOR ADVERTISING PROSTITUTION
26 SERVICES, ESCORT SERVICES, OR SEXUAL SERVICES; OR
27 (III) THAT INDICATES OR IMPLIES, THROUGH LANGUAGE OR
28 OTHER CONTENT, THAT SEXUAL ACTIVITY IS PART OF THE MASSAGE THERAPY
29 SERVICE.
30 (2) FOR AN ONLINE ADVERTISEMENT, EACH DAY ON WHICH AN
31 ADVERTISEMENT THAT VIOLATES PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION IS VISIBLE
32 TO THE PUBLIC CONSTITUTES A SEPARATE VIOLATION OF THIS SECTION.
4 HOUSE BILL 975
1 (3) FAILURE TO REMOVE A PHYSICAL SIGN OR BILLBOARD PLACED IN
2 PUBLIC VIEW THAT VIOLATES PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION CONSTITUTES A
3 VIOLATION OF THIS SECTION.
4 (F) ALL ADVERTISEMENTS FOR LICENSED MASSAGE THERAPISTS OR
5 REGISTERED MASSAGE PRACTITIONERS SHALL INCLUDE THE FULL NAME AS
6 LICENSED AND LICENSE NUMBER OF THE LICENSED MASSAGE THERAPIST OR
7 REGISTERED MASSAGE PRACTITIONER PROVIDING THE ADVERTISED MASSAGE
8 THERAPY SERVICES.
9 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That if, on the effective date of this
10 Act, a physical sign or billboard that is in violation of § 6–401(e)(1) of the Health
11 Occupations Article, as enacted by Section 1 of this Act, remains visible to the public, the
12 licensed massage therapist, registered massage practitioner, or third party acting on behalf
13 of a licensed massage therapist or registered massage practitioner that placed or caused
14 the physical sign or billboard to be placed shall remove the physical sign or billboard or
15 cause the physical sign or billboard to be removed before May 1, 2027.
16 SECTION 2. 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
17 October 1, 2026.
Approved:
