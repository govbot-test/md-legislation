CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Vehicle Laws – Out–of–State Vehicles – Improper Registration
3 FOR the purpose of authorizing the Motor Vehicle Administration and agents and
4 employees of State agencies and political subdivisions to take possession of certain
5 vehicle registration plates; altering a certain policy of the State; authorizing the
6 Motor Vehicle Administrator to enter into reciprocal agreements, arrangements, or
7 declarations to provide for reciprocal enforcement of certain automated enforcement
8 violations; exempting certain vehicles from the requirement to be registered in the
9 State; exempting certain vehicles from a certain tax; prohibiting a title service agent
10 from selling or offering for sale a certain vehicle registration; establishing a certain
11 process for bringing vehicles owned by State residents that are improperly registered
12 in another state into compliance with the Maryland Vehicle Law; and generally
13 relating to improperly registered out–of–state vehicles.
14 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
15 Article – Transportation
16 Section 12–110(a), 12–402, 13–402(a) and (c), 15–109, 15–606, and 26–301(b)

18 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
19 BY adding to
20 Article – Transportation
21 Section 12–411.1, 13–810(a)(26), and 26–301(b–1)
22 Annotated Code of Maryland

28 (2) (I) IF THE MOTOR VEHICLE OWNER PROVIDES PROOF
29 ACCEPTABLE TO THE ADMINISTRATION THAT THE MOTOR VEHICLE IS NOT
30 REQUIRED TO BE REGISTERED IN THE STATE, IF APPLICABLE AS DETERMINED BY
31 THE ADMINISTRATION, THE MOTOR VEHICLE OWNER SHALL OBTAIN AND DISPLAY
32 ON THE MOTOR VEHICLE A NONRESIDENT PERMIT ISSUED BY THE
33 ADMINISTRATION.
8 SENATE BILL 111
1 (II) IF THE MOTOR VEHICLE OWNER FAILS, WITHIN 60 DAYS
2 AFTER ISSUANCE OF A WARNING UNDER THIS SUBSECTION, TO PROPERLY REGISTER
3 THE MOTOR VEHICLE AND DISPLAY VALID REGISTRATION PLATES ON THE MOTOR
4 VEHICLE OR PROVIDE PROOF ACCEPTABLE TO THE ADMINISTRATION THAT
5 REGISTRATION IN THE STATE IS NOT REQUIRED, THE ADMINISTRATION SHALL
6 BEGIN TO ASSESS A CIVIL FINE AGAINST THE MOTOR VEHICLE OF $7 PER DAY FOR
7 UP TO 60 DAYS OR UNTIL THE MOTOR VEHICLE IS PROPERLY REGISTERED AND
8 DISPLAYING VALID REGISTRATION PLATES OR THE ADMINISTRATION DETERMINES
9 THAT THE MOTOR VEHICLE IS NOT SUBJECT TO REGISTRATION IN THE STATE,
10 WHICHEVER OCCURS FIRST.
11 (3) (I) THE ADMINISTRATION SHALL NOTIFY THE APPROPRIATE
12 LOCAL JURISDICTION IF A MOTOR VEHICLE OWNER FAILS, WITHIN 120 DAYS AFTER
13 ISSUANCE OF A WARNING UNDER THIS SUBSECTION, TO PROPERLY REGISTER THE
14 MOTOR VEHICLE AND DISPLAY VALID REGISTRATION PLATES ON THE MOTOR
15 VEHICLE OR SATISFY THE ADMINISTRATION THAT THE MOTOR VEHICLE IS NOT
16 SUBJECT TO REGISTRATION IN THE STATE.
17 (II) THE APPROPRIATE COUNTY STATE’S ATTORNEY ATTORNEY
18 MAY BRING A CIVIL ACTION IN REM AGAINST A MOTOR VEHICLE FOR WHICH THE
19 MOTOR VEHICLE OWNER FAILS UNDER THIS SUBSECTION TO PROPERLY REGISTER
20 AND DISPLAY VALID REGISTRATION PLATES OR TO SATISFY THE ADMINISTRATION
21 THAT THE MOTOR VEHICLE IS NOT SUBJECT TO REGISTRATION IN THE STATE.
22 (III) IN A CIVIL ACTION IN REM BROUGHT UNDER THIS
23 SUBSECTION, THE APPROPRIATE COUNTY STATE’S ATTORNEY ATTORNEY MAY SEEK
24 IMMOBILIZATION OF THE MOTOR VEHICLE BY TOWING OR REMOVAL AND
25 IMPOUNDMENT OR BY BOOTING.
