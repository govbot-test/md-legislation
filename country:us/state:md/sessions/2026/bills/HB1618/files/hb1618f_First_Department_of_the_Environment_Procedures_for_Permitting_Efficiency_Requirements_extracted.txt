HOUSE BILL 1618
M3 6lr3580
By: Delegate Qi
Introduced and read first time: February 20, 2026
Assigned to: Rules and Executive Nominations
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Department of the Environment – Procedures for Permitting Efficiency –
3 Requirements
4 FOR the purpose of requiring the Department of the Environment, in coordination with the
5 Department of Commerce, to adopt procedures on or before a certain date to
6 streamline the permitting process for certain entities and certain permits; and
7 generally relating to permitting efficiency for environmental permits.
8 BY adding to
9 Article – Environment
10 Section 1–608
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2013 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
13 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
14 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
15 Article – Environment
16 1–608.
17 (A) IN THIS SECTION, “DEDICATED NAVIGATOR” MEANS A REPRESENTATIVE
18 FROM THE DEPARTMENT OR FROM A PUBLIC OR PRIVATE ASSISTANCE AGENCY OR
19 ORGANIZATION WHO:
20 (1) IS IN PERSONAL CONTACT WITH A PERMIT APPLICANT; AND
21 (2) ASSISTS THE APPLICANT IN THE PERMIT APPLICATION PROCESS.
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

2 HOUSE BILL 1618
1 (B) THIS SECTION APPLIES TO APPLICATIONS FOR PERMITS ISSUED IN
2 ACCORDANCE WITH TITLE 2, TITLE 4, TITLE 9, AND TITLE 16 OF THIS ARTICLE BY A
3 PERSON CONDUCTING OR OPERATING A TRADE OR BUSINESS THAT IS PRIMARILY
4 ENGAGED IN ACTIVITIES THAT, IN ACCORDANCE WITH THE NORTH AMERICAN
5 INDUSTRIAL CLASSIFICATION SYSTEM (NAICS), UNITED STATES MANUAL,
6 UNITED STATES OFFICE OF MANAGEMENT AND BUDGET, 2012 EDITION, WOULD BE
7 INCLUDED IN SECTOR 31, 32, OR 33.
8 (C) (1) ON OR BEFORE OCTOBER 1, 2027, THE DEPARTMENT, IN
9 COORDINATION WITH THE DEPARTMENT OF COMMERCE, SHALL ADOPT
10 PROCEDURES TO STREAMLINE THE PERMITTING PROCESS IN ACCORDANCE WITH
11 THIS SECTION.
12 (2) A STREAMLINED PERMITTING PROCESS ADOPTED UNDER THIS
13 SECTION SHALL, AT A MINIMUM:
14 (I) REQUIRE THE DEPARTMENT TO ISSUE A DETERMINATION
15 ON A PERMIT APPLICATION WITHIN 60 DAYS AFTER THE DATE ON WHICH THE
16 DEPARTMENT RECEIVES THE PERMIT APPLICATION;
17 (II) PROVIDE FOR A DEDICATED NAVIGATOR; AND
18 (III) ESTABLISH A SINGLE DIGITAL PORTAL THROUGH WHICH
19 PERMIT APPLICATIONS MAY BE SUBMITTED.
20 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
21 October 1, 2026.
