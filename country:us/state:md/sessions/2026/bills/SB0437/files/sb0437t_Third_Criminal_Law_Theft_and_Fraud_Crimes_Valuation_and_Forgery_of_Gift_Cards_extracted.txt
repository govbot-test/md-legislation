1 AN ACT concerning
2 Criminal Law – Theft and Fraud Crimes – Valuation and Forgery of Gift Cards
3 FOR the purpose of providing for the valuation of a gift card for the purpose of determining
4 value for crimes involving theft; prohibiting a person, with the intent to defraud
5 another, from altering or tampering with a gift card or its packaging; and generally
6 relating to crimes involving gift cards.

2 SENATE BILL 437
1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – Commercial Law
4 14–4901.
5 (a) In this subtitle the following words have the meanings indicated.
6 (b) “Closed–loop gift card” means a card, code, or device that is:
7 (1) Issued to a consumer on a prepaid basis primarily for personal, family,
8 or household purposes in a specified amount, regardless of whether that amount may be
9 increased or reloaded in exchange for payment; and
10 (2) Redeemable on presentation by a consumer at a single merchant or a
11 group of affiliated merchants.
12 (e) “Open–loop gift card” means a card, code, or device that is:
13 (1) Issued to a consumer on a prepaid basis primarily for personal, family,
14 or household purposes in a specified amount, regardless of whether that amount may be
15 increased or reloaded in exchange for payment;
16 (2) Payment card network branded; and
17 (3) (i) Redeemable on presentation at multiple unaffiliated merchants
18 for goods or services within the payment card network; or
19 (ii) Usable at an automated teller machine.
20 Article – Criminal Law
21 7–103.
22 (a) In this section, “value” means:
23 (1) the market value of the property or service at the time and place of the
24 crime; or
25 (2) if the market value cannot satisfactorily be ascertained, the cost of the
26 replacement of the property or service within a reasonable time after the crime.
27 (b) Except as provided in § 7–104.1 of this subtitle, the value of property or service
28 under this part shall be determined in accordance with this section.
