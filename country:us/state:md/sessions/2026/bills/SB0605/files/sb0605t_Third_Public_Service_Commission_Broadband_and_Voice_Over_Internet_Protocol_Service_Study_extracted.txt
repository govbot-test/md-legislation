CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Public Service Commission – Broadband and Voice Over Internet Protocol
3 Service – Oversight Study
4 (Broadband Accountability and Affordability Act)
5 FOR the purpose of authorizing requiring the Public Service Commission to exercise certain
6 oversight of broadband service and voice over Internet protocol service; requiring the
7 Commission to assess the adequacy of certain plans for each Internet service
8 provider; authorizing the Commission to conduct certain evaluations or audits and
9 require certain remedial action under certain circumstances; conduct a study and
10 make recommendations on the potential scope, structure, and implementation of
11 Commission oversight of broadband service and voice over Internet protocol service;
12 and generally relating to broadband service and voice over Internet protocol service.
13 BY adding to
14 Article – Public Utilities
15 Section 5–107
16 Annotated Code of Maryland
17 (2025 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
18 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
19 Article – Public Utilities
20 Section 8–601
21 Annotated Code of Maryland
22 (2025 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

amendment. *sb0605*
2 SENATE BILL 605
1 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
2 Article – Public Utilities
3 Section 8–602
4 Annotated Code of Maryland
5 (2025 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
6 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
7 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
8 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
9 (2) (i) “Broadband service” means a mass–market retail service that
10 provides the capability to transmit data to and receive data from all or substantially all
11 Internet endpoints, including any capabilities that are incidental to and enable the
12 operation of communications services provided by an Internet service provider.
13 (ii) “Broadband service” does not include dial–up Internet service.
14 (3) “Internet service provider” means an entity that provides broadband
15 service to a customer in the State through any of the following modes:
16 (i) wireline;
17 (ii) fixed wireless;
18 (iii) mobile wireless; or
19 (iv) satellite.
20 (4) (i) “Voice over Internet protocol service” or “VoIP service” means
21 any service that:
22 1. enables real–time two–way voice communications that
23 originate from or terminate to the subscriber end user’s location requiring Internet protocol
24 or any successor protocol to Internet protocol; and
25 2. requires a broadband connection from the user’s location.
26 (ii) “Voice over Internet protocol service” or “VoIP service” includes
27 any service that allows users generally to receive calls that originate on the public switched
28 telephone network and to terminate calls to the public switched telephone network.
29 (b) The Public Service Commission shall conduct a study and make
30 recommendations regarding the potential scope, structure, and implementation of
31 Commission oversight of broadband service and VoIP service in the State, including
32 considering:
SENATE BILL 605 3
1 (1) the extent to which federal jurisdiction preempts State regulation of
2 broadband service and VoIP service;
3 (2) an appropriate regulatory framework to implement oversight of
4 broadband service and VoIP service that encompasses:
5 (i) standards for Internet service provider resiliency and reliability,
6 including the requirement to submit outage data;
7 (ii) standards for Internet service provider emergency preparedness;
8 (iii) public disclosure reporting requirements for the availability,
9 deployment, pricing, and adoption of broadband services and VoIP services;
10 (iv) a customer complaint process at the Commission; and
11 (v) a publicly accessible resource that contains information on
12 broadband services, including prices;
13 (3) the applicability of the current regulatory framework for landline
14 telecommunications to the regulation of broadband services and VoIP services;
15 (4) the expected cost to the Commission to implement the appropriate
16 regulatory framework and obtain the resources necessary to regulate broadband services
17 and VoIP services;
18 (5) statutory mechanisms and regulatory mechanisms utilized in other
19 states to oversee broadband services and VoIP services; and
20 (6) any legislative changes that must be enacted to establish the
21 Commission’s oversight authority.
22 (c) On or before December 31, 2026, the Public Service Commission shall report,
23 in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government Article, its findings and
24 recommendations to the Senate Committee on Education, Energy, and the Environment,
25 the House Environment and Transportation Committee, and the General Assembly.
26 Article – Public Utilities
27 5–107.
28 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
29 INDICATED.
30 (2) (I) “BROADBAND SERVICE” MEANS A MASS–MARKET RETAIL
31 SERVICE THAT PROVIDES THE CAPABILITY TO TRANSMIT DATA TO AND RECEIVE

1 DATA FROM ALL OR SUBSTANTIALLY ALL INTERNET ENDPOINTS, INCLUDING ANY
2 CAPABILITIES THAT ARE INCIDENTAL TO AND ENABLE THE OPERATION OF
3 COMMUNICATIONS SERVICES PROVIDED BY A WIRELINE, FIXED WIRELESS, MOBILE
4 WIRELESS BROADBAND, OR SATELLITE SERVICE PROVIDER.
5 (II) “BROADBAND SERVICE” DOES NOT INCLUDE DIAL–UP
6 INTERNET SERVICE.
7 (3) “INTERNET SERVICE PROVIDER” MEANS AN ENTITY THAT
8 PROVIDES BROADBAND SERVICE TO A CUSTOMER IN THE STATE.
9 (4) “VOICE OVER INTERNET PROTOCOL SERVICE” OR “VOIP
10 SERVICE” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 8–601 OF THIS ARTICLE.
11 (B) THE COMMISSION MAY EXERCISE OVERSIGHT OF BROADBAND SERVICE
12 AND VOIP SERVICE.
13 (C) THE COMMISSION SHALL ADOPT REGULATIONS TO IMPLEMENT
14 EFFECTIVE OVERSIGHT OF THE QUALITY, RELIABILITY, AND RESILIENCE OF
15 BROADBAND SERVICE AND VOIP SERVICE, INCLUDING:
16 (1) ESTABLISHING STANDARDS TO ENSURE THE RESILIENCE AND
17 RELIABILITY OF BROADBAND INFRASTRUCTURE;
18 (2) REQUIRING EACH INTERNET SERVICE PROVIDER TO:
19 (I) SUBMIT INFORMATION RELATING TO NETWORK
20 RELIABILITY, INCLUDING REPORTING OUTAGES;
21 (II) MAINTAIN NETWORKS SUFFICIENTLY IN ORDER TO ENSURE
22 RELIABLE, SAFE, AND ADEQUATE SERVICES;
23 (III) REPORT DATA ON THE AVAILABILITY, DEPLOYMENT,
24 PRICING, AND ADOPTION OF BROADBAND SERVICES AND VOIP SERVICES; AND
25 (IV) SUBMIT TO THE COMMISSION THE PROVIDER’S PLANS FOR
26 EMERGENCY PREPAREDNESS AND POSTEMERGENCY NETWORK RESTORATION;
27 (3) ESTABLISHING A SYSTEM FOR ASSESSING THE ADEQUACY OF
28 INTERNET SERVICE PROVIDERS’ PLANS FOR EMERGENCY PREPAREDNESS AND
29 POSTEMERGENCY NETWORK RESTORATION; AND
SENATE BILL 605 5
1 (4) ESTABLISHING MINIMUM REQUIREMENTS FOR BACKUP POWER
2 GENERATION.
3 (D) THE COMMISSION SHALL ASSESS THE ADEQUACY OF EACH INTERNET
4 SERVICE PROVIDER’S PLAN FOR EMERGENCY PREPAREDNESS AND
5 POSTEMERGENCY NETWORK RESTORATION IN ACCORDANCE WITH REGULATIONS
6 ADOPTED UNDER THIS SECTION.
7 (E) (1) ON RECEIPT OF A COMPLAINT FROM A CUSTOMER OR BASED ON
8 DATA COLLECTED FROM PROVIDERS, THE COMMISSION MAY CONDUCT AN
9 EVALUATION OR AUDIT OF AN INTERNET SERVICE PROVIDER’S FACILITIES AND
10 INFRASTRUCTURE TO ASSESS SERVICE QUALITY, PUBLIC SAFETY CONCERNS, OR
11 NETWORK RESILIENCE.
12 (2) THE COMMISSION MAY HOLD A HEARING AS PART OF THE
13 EVALUATION OR AUDIT AUTHORIZED UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION.
14 (3) IF THE COMMISSION DETERMINES THAT THE PRACTICES,
15 FACILITIES, OR SERVICES OF AN INTERNET SERVICE PROVIDER ARE UNJUST,
16 UNREASONABLE, UNSAFE, IMPROPER, OR INADEQUATE TO ENSURE NETWORK
17 RELIABILITY, RESILIENCE, AND PUBLIC SAFETY IN ACCORDANCE WITH THE
18 STANDARDS ESTABLISHED UNDER SUBSECTION (C) OF THIS SECTION, THE
19 COMMISSION MAY REQUIRE THE PROVIDER TO UNDERTAKE REMEDIAL ACTIONS.
20 (F) ON OR BEFORE JANUARY 1, 2028, AND EACH JANUARY 1 THEREAFTER,
21 THE COMMISSION SHALL REPORT TO THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY, IN ACCORDANCE
22 WITH § 2–1257 OF THE STATE GOVERNMENT ARTICLE, ON PROGRESS MADE
23 TOWARD IMPROVING SERVICE QUALITY, PUBLIC SAFETY, AND NETWORK
24 RESILIENCE.
25 (G) (1) NOTHING IN THIS SECTION MAY BE CONSTRUED TO LIMIT ANY
26 RIGHT OR AUTHORITY GRANTED TO THE OFFICE OF STATEWIDE BROADBAND
27 UNDER TITLE 6.5 OF THE HOUSING AND COMMUNITY DEVELOPMENT ARTICLE.
28 (2) THE COMMISSION MAY COORDINATE WITH THE OFFICE OF
29 STATEWIDE BROADBAND WHEN COLLECTING DATA TO CARRY OUT THE PROVISIONS
30 OF THIS SECTION.

10 (a) [The] EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN § 5–107 OF THIS ARTICLE, THE
11 Commission does not have jurisdiction over the regulation of VoIP service, including the
12 imposition of regulatory fees, certification requirements, and the filing or approval of
13 tariffs.
14 (b) Nothing in this subtitle may be construed to:
15 (1) require or prohibit the assessment of 9–1–1 fees in accordance with §
16 1–310 of the Public Safety Article on VoIP;
17 (2) require or prohibit the assessment of fees for telecommunications relay
18 service under Title 3, Subtitle 8 of the State Finance and Procurement Article;
19 (3) require or prohibit the payment of any switched network access rates
20 or other intercarrier compensation rates that may be determined to apply;
21 (4) relieve a company that is otherwise subject to § 8–201 of this title of its
22 obligation to provide telephone lifeline service over local exchange access lines that are
23 subject to the Commission’s jurisdiction;
24 (5) exempt VoIP service from generally applicable State and federal laws
25 relating to public safety, consumer protection, and unfair and deceptive trade practices, or
26 to exempt VoIP service from the authority of the Division of Consumer Protection in the
27 Office of the Attorney General; or
28 (6) remove the Commission’s jurisdiction over circuit switched local
29 exchange access service.
30 (c) A company that moves a customer from a Commission–approved tariff service
31 to VoIP service shall notify the customer that the Commission does not have jurisdiction
32 over the regulation of VoIP service and that complaints about VoIP service may be filed
33 with the Division of Consumer Protection in the Office of the Attorney General.
