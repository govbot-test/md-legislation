2 Vehicle Laws – Automotive Repair Facilities Motor Vehicle Administration –
3 Advanced Driver Assistance Systems Repairs – Study
4 FOR the purpose of requiring certain persons to be licensed by the Motor Vehicle
5 Administration in order to perform a recalibration of an advanced driver assistance
6 system; establishing certain requirements for an automotive repair facility that
7 performs advanced driver assistance system recalibrations; prohibiting an insurance
8 company from taking certain actions relating to repairs or recalibrations of an
9 advanced driver assistance system and requiring an insurance estimate to include
10 certain information; establishing that a violation of certain provisions of this Act are
11 unfair, abusive, or deceptive trade practices subject to certain enforcement and
12 penalty provisions under the Maryland Consumer Protection Act; and generally
13 relating to automotive repair facilities the Motor Vehicle Administration to study
14 and report on the repair and training requirements of advanced driver assistance
15 systems; and generally relating to the repair and training requirements of advanced
16 driver assistance systems.

23 driver assistance systems;

25 assistance systems;
26 (3) training requirements for advanced driver assistance systems; and
27 (4) statistics on accidents related to advanced driver assistance systems.

29 Administration shall consult with representatives from:

11 RECOMMENDATION REGARDING THE REPAIR, MAINTENANCE, OR REPLACEMENT OF
12 MOTOR VEHICLE COMPONENTS, ISSUED BY AN ORIGINAL EQUIPMENT
13 MANUFACTURER.
14 (F) “RECALIBRATION” MEANS THE PROCESS OF TESTING, MEASURING, AND
15 ADJUSTING ADVANCED DRIVER ASSISTANCE SYSTEM COMPONENTS TO ENSURE THE
16 COMPONENTS FUNCTION ACCORDING TO THE ORIGINAL EQUIPMENT
17 MANUFACTURER’S SPECIFICATIONS.
18 (G) “REPAIR PROCEDURE” MEANS THE DOCUMENTED METHODS AND
19 SPECIFICATIONS FOR THE REPAIR OF A MOTOR VEHICLE, ISSUED BY AN ORIGINAL
20 EQUIPMENT MANUFACTURER.
21 15–1102.
22 (A) THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY FINDS THAT:
23 (1) ADVANCED DRIVER ASSISTANCE SYSTEMS:
24 (I) ARE INCREASINGLY COMMON IN MOTOR VEHICLES; AND
25 (II) PLAY A CRITICAL ROLE IN MOTOR VEHICLE SAFETY;
26 (2) PROPER CALIBRATION OF ADVANCED DRIVER ASSISTANCE
27 SYSTEM COMPONENTS IS ESSENTIAL TO ENSURE THE SYSTEMS ARE FUNCTIONING
28 CORRECTLY;
29 (3) ORIGINAL EQUIPMENT MANUFACTURER REPAIR PROCEDURES
30 AND POSITION STATEMENTS PROVIDE CRITICAL SAFETY GUIDELINES REGARDING
31 ADVANCED DRIVER ASSISTANCE SYSTEMS;

5 FEATURES; AND
6 (5) PROFESSIONAL STANDARDS AND LICENSING OF INDIVIDUALS
7 AND BUSINESSES THAT PERFORM ADVANCED DRIVER ASSISTANCE SYSTEM
