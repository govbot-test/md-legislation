CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Family Law and State Government Human Services – Child Protection and the
3 Office of the Child Welfare Ombudsman Guardianship Assistance Program and
4 State Foster Youth Ombudsman – Establishment
5 (Kanaiyah’s Law)
6 FOR the purpose of requiring the Secretary of Human Services to establish and maintain
7 a Guardianship Assistance Program to promote the placement and maintenance of
8 children in permanent guardianship homes by providing guardianship assistance to
9 guardians of minor children; establishing the State Foster Youth Ombudsman in the
10 Department of Human Services; requiring that a juvenile court include in an order
11 granting guardianship of a child to a specific individual a requirement that the
12 individual provide certain information to a local department of social services under
13 certain circumstances; establishing procedures for the review of certain
14 guardianships of certain children by the juvenile court under certain circumstances;
15 prohibiting the Social Services Administration from allowing the placement of
16 certain children in unlicensed settings; expanding the list of individuals who are
17 required to obtain a certain criminal history records check to include adults living in
18 the home of a child’s guardian; establishing the Office of the Child Welfare
19 Ombudsman in the Office of the Attorney General; exempting from disclosure under
20 the Maryland Public Information Act records related to certain complaints;
21 prohibiting a person from certain willful interference and discriminatory and
22 retaliatory acts relating to complaints filed with the Office; and generally relating to
23 child protection and the Office of the Child Welfare Ombudsman the Guardianship
24 Assistance Program and the State Foster Youth Ombudsman.

8 Preamble
9 WHEREAS, Child abuse and neglect is a serious and reprehensible problem in
10 society; and
11 WHEREAS, The protection of children from abuse and neglect by applying
12 prevention measures and observing best practices in treating children who are abused and
13 neglected must be one of Maryland’s highest public policy priorities; and
14 WHEREAS, The child welfare system must protect and serve Maryland’s children in
15 a manner that keeps them safe and healthy and promotes their well–being; and
16 WHEREAS, The children and families served by the child welfare system, as well as
17 the public, must have a high level of confidence that the system will act in a child’s best
18 interests and will respond to the child’s needs in a timely and professional manner; and
19 WHEREAS, To engender this high level of confidence in the child welfare system, it
20 is important that children and families who become involved in the system, mandatory
21 reporters, and the general public have a well–publicized, easily accessible, and transparent
22 complaint process for voicing concerns regarding the child welfare system along with the
23 expectation that those concerns, once voiced, will be heard and addressed in a timely and
24 appropriate manner; and
25 WHEREAS, To improve child welfare outcomes and to foster best practices, there
26 must be effective accountability mechanisms, including the review and evaluation of
27 concerns voiced by children and families, mandatory reporters, persons involved in the
28 child welfare system, and members of the general public, that provide policymakers with
29 the information necessary to formulate systemic changes, where appropriate; now,
30 therefore,

CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Family Law and State Government Human Services – Child Protection and the
3 Office of the Child Welfare Ombudsman Guardianship Assistance Program and
4 State Foster Youth Ombudsman – Establishment
5 (Kanaiyah’s Law)
6 FOR the purpose of requiring the Secretary of Human Services to establish and maintain
7 a Guardianship Assistance Program to promote the placement and maintenance of
8 children in permanent guardianship homes by providing guardianship assistance to
9 guardians of minor children; establishing the State Foster Youth Ombudsman in the
10 Department of Human Services; requiring that a juvenile court include in an order
11 granting guardianship of a child to a specific individual a requirement that the
12 individual provide certain information to a local department of social services under
13 certain circumstances; establishing procedures for the review of certain
14 guardianships of certain children by the juvenile court under certain circumstances;
15 prohibiting the Social Services Administration from allowing the placement of
16 certain children in unlicensed settings; expanding the list of individuals who are
17 required to obtain a certain criminal history records check to include adults living in
18 the home of a child’s guardian; establishing the Office of the Child Welfare
19 Ombudsman in the Office of the Attorney General; exempting from disclosure under
20 the Maryland Public Information Act records related to certain complaints;
21 prohibiting a person from certain willful interference and discriminatory and
22 retaliatory acts relating to complaints filed with the Office; and generally relating to
23 child protection and the Office of the Child Welfare Ombudsman the Guardianship
24 Assistance Program and the State Foster Youth Ombudsman.

8 Preamble
9 WHEREAS, Child abuse and neglect is a serious and reprehensible problem in
10 society; and
11 WHEREAS, The protection of children from abuse and neglect by applying
12 prevention measures and observing best practices in treating children who are abused and
13 neglected must be one of Maryland’s highest public policy priorities; and
14 WHEREAS, The child welfare system must protect and serve Maryland’s children in
15 a manner that keeps them safe and healthy and promotes their well–being; and
16 WHEREAS, The children and families served by the child welfare system, as well as
17 the public, must have a high level of confidence that the system will act in a child’s best
18 interests and will respond to the child’s needs in a timely and professional manner; and
19 WHEREAS, To engender this high level of confidence in the child welfare system, it
20 is important that children and families who become involved in the system, mandatory
21 reporters, and the general public have a well–publicized, easily accessible, and transparent
22 complaint process for voicing concerns regarding the child welfare system along with the
23 expectation that those concerns, once voiced, will be heard and addressed in a timely and
24 appropriate manner; and
25 WHEREAS, To improve child welfare outcomes and to foster best practices, there
26 must be effective accountability mechanisms, including the review and evaluation of
27 concerns voiced by children and families, mandatory reporters, persons involved in the
28 child welfare system, and members of the general public, that provide policymakers with
29 the information necessary to formulate systemic changes, where appropriate; now,
30 therefore,
