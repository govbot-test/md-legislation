2 Public and Nonpublic Schools – Student Elopement – Locative Devices
3 FOR the purpose of authorizing certain public and nonpublic schools to coordinate a
4 student’s voluntary use of a certain locative device and monitor the student’s location
5 using the locative device for certain purposes and under certain circumstances;
6 establishing certain privacy and data protections for information obtained through
7 a student’s voluntary use of a certain locative device; requiring the State Department
8 of Education to issue certain advisory guidance to certain public and nonpublic
9 schools; and generally relating to student wandering and elopement behavior.

2 HOUSE BILL 1182
1 Article – Education
2 7–11A–01.
3 (a) In this subtitle the following words have the meanings indicated.
4 (b) “Behavior intervention plan” has the meaning stated in § 7–1101 of this title.
5 (c) “Elopement” means the act of a student leaving the school campus without the
6 permission of a school staff member who has responsibility for the student.
7 (d) “IEP” means an individualized education program as defined under the
8 federal Individuals with Disabilities Education Act.
9 (e) “Nonpublic school” has the meaning stated in § 7–1101 of this title.
10 (f) “School” means:
11 (1) A public elementary, middle, or high school;
12 (2) The Maryland School for the Blind;
13 (3) The Maryland School for the Deaf; and
14 (4) A nonpublic school.
15 (g) “School campus” means the buildings and grounds of a school, including a
16 playground.
17 (h) (1) “Wandering” means the act of a student leaving a classroom or area on
18 the school campus, but not leaving the school campus, without the permission of a school
19 staff member who has responsibility for the student.
20 (2) “Wandering” does not include a student leaving a classroom or area on
21 the school campus under a flash pass or another standing arrangement that has been
22 approved by a school administrator.
23 7–11A–02.
24 (a) Each time a student attempts or accomplishes an elopement, the school shall
25 notify the student’s parent or guardian of the elopement incident on the same day as the
26 elopement incident.
27 (b) At least one time each year, a school or a student’s IEP team shall review and
28 address with relevant stakeholders a student’s wandering or elopement behavior in
29 updating the student’s IEP or behavior intervention plan.

4 7–11A–03.
5 (A) IN THIS SECTION, “LOCATIVE DEVICE” MEANS A WEARABLE OR
6 PORTABLE ELECTRONIC DEVICE THAT IS CAPABLE OF LOCATING A STUDENT
7 DURING AN ELOPEMENT INCIDENT.
8 (B) SUBJECT TO SUBSECTION (C) OF THIS SECTION, A SCHOOL MAY
9 COORDINATE A STUDENT’S VOLUNTARY USE OF A LOCATIVE DEVICE AND MONITOR
10 THE STUDENT’S LOCATION USING THE LOCATIVE DEVICE ONLY IF:
11 (1) THE STUDENT HAS A DOCUMENTED HISTORY OF WANDERING OR
12 ELOPEMENT BEHAVIOR IN THE STUDENT’S IEP OR BEHAVIOR INTERVENTION PLAN;
13 AND
14 (2) THE STUDENT’S PARENT OR GUARDIAN PROVIDES INFORMED,
15 WRITTEN CONSENT TO:
16 (I) THE STUDENT’S VOLUNTARY USE OF THE LOCATIVE
17 DEVICE; AND
18 (II) THE SCHOOL’S MONITORING OF THE STUDENT’S LOCATION
19 USING THE LOCATIVE DEVICE.
20 (C) (1) A SCHOOL MAY MONITOR A STUDENT’S LOCATION USING A
21 LOCATIVE DEVICE UNDER THIS SECTION ONLY FOR WANDERING OR ELOPEMENT
22 BEHAVIOR.
23 (2) A SCHOOL SHALL:
24 (I) LIMIT INFORMATION OBTAINED THROUGH A STUDENT’S
25 VOLUNTARY USE OF A LOCATIVE DEVICE IN ACCORDANCE WITH THIS SECTION TO
26 INFORMATION THAT IS REASONABLY NECESSARY TO ADDRESS OR RESPOND TO A
27 STUDENT’S WANDERING OR ELOPEMENT BEHAVIOR; AND
28 (II) MAINTAIN ANY INFORMATION OBTAINED THROUGH A
29 STUDENT’S USE OF A LOCATIVE DEVICE IN ACCORDANCE WITH APPLICABLE
30 FEDERAL AND STATE EDUCATION PRIVACY LAWS.

1 (3) INFORMATION OBTAINED THROUGH A STUDENT’S VOLUNTARY
2 USE OF A LOCATIVE DEVICE IN ACCORDANCE WITH THIS SECTION:
3 (I) MAY BE ACCESSED ONLY BY:
4 1. PERSONNEL IDENTIFIED IN THE STUDENT’S IEP OR

7 DURING AN ELOPEMENT INCIDENT;

9 ADDRESS OR RESPOND TO A STUDENT’S WANDERING OR ELOPEMENT BEHAVIOR OR
10 AS REQUIRED BY LAW; AND
11 (III) SHALL BE PROTECTED BY EMPLOYING REASONABLE
12 ADMINISTRATIVE, TECHNICAL, AND PHYSICAL SAFEGUARDS.
13 (D) (1) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL ISSUE ADVISORY GUIDANCE TO
14 SCHOOLS ON:
15 (I) INCORPORATING A STUDENT’S VOLUNTARY USE OF A
16 LOCATIVE DEVICE IN ACCORDANCE WITH THIS SECTION IN THE STUDENT’S IEP OR
17 BEHAVIOR INTERVENTION PLAN; AND
18 (II) PRIVACY AND DATA PROTECTION CONSIDERATIONS
19 ASSOCIATED WITH USE OF A LOCATIVE DEVICE.
20 (2) THE DEPARTMENT’S GUIDANCE UNDER THIS SUBSECTION SHALL
21 GRANT A SCHOOL DISCRETION AS TO WHETHER AND HOW TO IMPLEMENT A
22 STUDENT’S VOLUNTARY USE OF A LOCATIVE DEVICE IN ACCORDANCE WITH THIS
23 SECTION.
24 (E) NOTHING IN THIS SECTION MAY BE CONSTRUED TO:
25 (1) REQUIRE A SCHOOL TO PROVIDE, PURCHASE, REIMBURSE,
26 MANAGE, OR MONITOR A LOCATIVE DEVICE OR A RELATED SERVICE PLAN; OR
27 (2) REQUIRE A PARENT, GUARDIAN, OR STUDENT TO PURCHASE OR
28 USE A LOCATIVE DEVICE, INCLUDING AS A CONDITION OF ENROLLMENT IN A SCHOOL
29 OR FOR THE PROVISION OF ANY SERVICE.
