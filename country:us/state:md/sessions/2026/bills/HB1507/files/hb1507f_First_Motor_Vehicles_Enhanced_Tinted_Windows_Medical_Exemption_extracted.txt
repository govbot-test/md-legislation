A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Motor Vehicles – Enhanced Tinted Windows – Medical Exemption
3 FOR the purpose of authorizing an out–of–state physician licensed to practice medicine to
4 issue a certification for a medical exemption for enhanced tinted motor vehicle
5 windows; repealing the time limit applicable to a certification for a medical
6 exemption for enhanced tinted motor vehicle windows; and generally relating to
7 medical exemptions for enhanced tinted motor vehicle windows.
8 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
9 Article – Transportation
10 Section 22–406(i)
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
13 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
14 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
15 Article – Transportation
16 22–406.
17 (i) (1) Except as provided in paragraph (4) of this subsection, a person may
18 not operate a vehicle registered under § 13–912, § 13–913, § 13–917, or § 13–937 of this
19 article on a highway in this State if:
20 (i) In the case of a vehicle registered under § 13–912 of this article,
21 there is affixed to any window of the vehicle any tinting materials added to the window
22 after manufacture of the vehicle that do not allow a light transmittance through the window
23 of at least 35%; and
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from exist in g l aw . *hb1507*
2 HOUSE BILL 1507
1 (ii) In the case of a vehicle registered under § 13–913, § 13–917, or §
2 13–937 of this article, there is affixed to any window to the immediate right or left of the
3 driver any window tinting materials added after manufacture of the vehicle that do not
4 allow a light transmittance through the window of at least 35%.
5 (2) If a police officer observes that a vehicle is being operated in violation
6 of paragraph (1) of this subsection, the officer may stop the driver of the vehicle and, in
7 addition to a citation charging the driver with the offense, issue to the driver a safety
8 equipment repair order in accordance with the provisions of § 23–105 of this article.
9 (3) A person may not install on a window of a vehicle any window tinting
10 material that does not comply with the light transmittance requirements specified in
11 paragraph (1) of this subsection.
12 (4) (i) A person who must be protected from the sun for medical reasons
13 is exempt from the provisions of paragraph (1) of this subsection if the owner has, in the
14 vehicle at the time the vehicle is stopped by a police officer, a written certification in the
15 manner and format required by the Automotive Safety Enforcement Division of the
16 Department of State Police that details the owner’s medical need for tinted windows with
17 a light transmittance of less than the allowed 35%, from a physician licensed to practice
18 medicine [in the State].
19 (ii) [1. Except as provided in subsubparagraph 2 of this
20 subparagraph, a written certification under this paragraph shall be valid for a period of
21 time that the licensed physician determines the owner needs the enhanced tinted windows,
22 not to exceed 2 years.
23 2. If the licensed physician determines that the owner’s
24 medical condition is permanent, a written certification under this paragraph shall be valid
25 indefinitely.
26 (iii)] This subsection does not apply to tinting materials that:
27 1. Are affixed in such a manner so as to be easily removed;
28 and
29 2. Are being used to protect a child less than 10 years of age
30 from the sun.
31 [(iv)] (III) Nothing in this subsection may be construed to:
32 1. Allow any tinting materials to be added to the windshield
33 of a vehicle below the AS1 line or below 5 inches from the top of the windshield;
34 2. Prohibit a person from operating the vehicle while the
35 person for whom the written certification is required is not present in the vehicle, provided
36 that the written certification is in the vehicle; or
HOUSE BILL 1507 3
1 3. Alter or restrict the authority of the Administrator to
2 adopt regulations regarding vehicle windows, except with respect to the light transmittance
3 requirements specified in this section.
4 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
5 October 1, 2026.
