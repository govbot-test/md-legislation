1 AN ACT concerning
2 Political Subdivisions – Third–Party Road Improvements – Satisfactory
3 Completion
4 State Highway Administration – Third–Party Road Improvements – Required
5 Work Schedule
6 FOR the purpose of prohibiting a political subdivision from issuing a use and occupancy
7 permit to the holder of a permit to complete certain improvements on certain State
8 roads until the improvement is completed to the satisfaction of the State Highway
9 Administration and the political subdivision; authorizing the State Highway
10 Administration to require certain nongovernmental applicants for certain permits to
11 submit a schedule for the work to be performed under the permit; requiring a person
12 issued a certain permit to perform the permitted work in accordance with a certain
13 work schedule; authorizing the Administration to revoke a certain permit under
14 certain circumstances; providing that certain permit holders and its sureties are
15 liable for certain damages; and generally relating to State road improvements.
16 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
17 Article – Transportation
18 Section 8–646(a)
19 Annotated Code of Maryland
20 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
21 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
22 Article – Transportation

2 HOUSE BILL 669
1 Section 8–646(b)
2 Annotated Code of Maryland
3 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
4 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
5 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
6 Article – Transportation
7 8–646.
8 (a) Except as permitted by this section or in accordance with a permit obtained
9 from the Administration, a person may not:
10 (1) Make an opening in any State highway;
11 (2) Place any structure on any State highway;
12 (3) Change or renew any structure placed on any State highway;
13 (4) Dig up any State highway for any purpose, including the placement of
14 pipes, sewers, poles, wires, or rails;
15 (5) Plant or remove any tree on any State highway; or
16 (6) Place any obstruction or improvement on any State highway.
17 (b) (1) The Administration may issue a permit for work otherwise prohibited
18 by subsection (a) of this section.
19 (2) Work done under the permit shall be performed to the satisfaction of
20 the Administration and under its supervision.
21 (3) If the work done under the permit will be performed within 1 mile of
22 Washington Metropolitan Area Transit Authority rail or bus rapid transit stations or
23 Maryland Transit Administration rail or bus rapid transit stations, including Maryland
24 Area Regional Commuter (MARC) stations, the person to whom the permit is issued or by
25 whom the work is done shall maintain a safe alternative pedestrian path at the work site
26 in accordance with regulations adopted under this section.
27 (4) The person to whom the permit is issued or by whom the work is done
28 shall pay the cost of replacing the highway in as good a condition as before the work was
29 done.
30 (5) (i) The Administration shall require a nongovernment applicant for
31 a permit issued under this subsection who is a developer to submit a performance bond,
32 letter of credit, or other surety acceptable to the Administration.
HOUSE BILL 669 3
1 (ii) The Administration shall require a nongovernment applicant for
2 a permit issued under this subsection to submit a payment bond, letter of credit, or other
3 surety acceptable to the Administration if:
4 1. The amount of the improvement is estimated to exceed
5 $100,000;
6 2. The project is financed, in whole or in part, by private
7 funds; and
8 3. The entire improvement is located outside the applicant’s
9 property.
10 (6) A POLITICAL SUBDIVISION MAY NOT ISSUE A USE AND OCCUPANCY
11 PERMIT TO A NONGOVERNMENT HOLDER OF A PERMIT AUTHORIZED UNDER THIS
12 SECTION WHO IS REQUIRED TO COMPLETE WORK OTHERWISE PROHIBITED BY
13 SUBSECTION (A) OF THIS SECTION ON A STATE HIGHWAY THAT CARRIES AN
14 AVERAGE TRAFFIC VOLUME OF MORE THAN 25,000 VEHICLES A DAY UNTIL THE
15 WORK IS COMPLETED TO THE SATISFACTION OF THE ADMINISTRATION AND THE
16 POLITICAL SUBDIVISION.
17 (6) (I) THE ADMINISTRATION MAY REQUIRE A
18 NONGOVERNMENTAL APPLICANT FOR A PERMIT ISSUED UNDER THIS SUBSECTION
19 TO SUBMIT, IN A MANNER THAT THE ADMINISTRATION REQUIRES, A SCHEDULE FOR
20 THE WORK TO BE PERFORMED UNDER THE PERMIT.
21 (II) A PERSON ISSUED A PERMIT UNDER THIS SUBSECTION WHO
22 IS REQUIRED TO SUBMIT A SCHEDULE TO THE ADMINISTRATION UNDER
23 SUBPARAGRAPH (I) OF THIS PARAGRAPH:
24 1. SHALL COMPLETE ALL WORK IN ACCORDANCE WITH
25 THE SCHEDULE OR ANY EXTENSION THE ADMINISTRATION APPROVES; AND
26 2. IF THE PERSON FAILS TO COMPLETE THE WORK IN
27 ACCORDANCE WITH THE SCHEDULE OR ANY EXTENSION THE ADMINISTRATION
28 APPROVES, IS:
29 A. SUBJECT TO REVOCATION OF THE PERMIT; AND
30 B. LIABLE, WITH ITS SURETIES, FOR ALL DAMAGES
31 SUSTAINED BY THE STATE AS A RESULT OF THE FAILURE, INCLUDING ANY COSTS
32 INCURRED BY THE ADMINISTRATION TO COMPLETE THE WORK.
4 HOUSE BILL 669
1 (III) THE ADMINISTRATION SHALL PROVIDE WRITTEN NOTICE
2 TO A PERSON WHOSE PERMIT IS REVOKED UNDER THIS PARAGRAPH.
3 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
4 October 1, 2026.
