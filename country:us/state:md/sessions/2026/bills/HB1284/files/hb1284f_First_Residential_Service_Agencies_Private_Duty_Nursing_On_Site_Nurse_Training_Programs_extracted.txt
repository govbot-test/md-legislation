A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Residential Service Agencies – Private Duty Nursing – On–Site Nurse Training
3 Programs
4 FOR the purpose of requiring the Maryland Department of Health to adopt regulations
5 that authorize a residential service agency to provide training to a licensed nurse
6 after hire; authorizing a residential service agency that provides private duty
7 nursing services to employ a licensed nurse who does not meet certain requirements
8 under certain circumstances; requiring a residential service agency to provide
9 training and a competency evaluation for certain nurses; and generally relating to
10 residential service agencies.

1 (a) The Department shall adopt regulations that set standards for the care,
2 treatment, health, safety, welfare, and comfort of individuals who receive home health care
3 services through a residential service agency.
4 (b) The regulations shall provide for the licensing of residential service agencies.
5 (c) The regulations shall include provisions that:
6 (1) Provide for the establishment of residential service agencies;
7 (2) Establish qualifications for licensure;
8 (3) Set minimum standards for individuals who provide home health care
9 services through a residential service agency; [and]
10 (4) Require the residential service agency to screen and verify the
11 character references of all home health care providers that are employed by the residential
12 service agency; AND
13 (5) AUTHORIZE A RESIDENTIAL SERVICE AGENCY TO PROVIDE
14 TRAINING TO A LICENSED NURSE AFTER HIRE IN ACCORDANCE WITH § 19–4A–03.2
15 OF THIS SUBTITLE.
16 19–4A–03.2.
17 (A) A RESIDENTIAL SERVICE AGENCY THAT PROVIDES PRIVATE DUTY
18 NURSING SERVICES MAY EMPLOY A LICENSED NURSE WHO DOES NOT MEET THE
19 CLINICAL EXPERIENCE REQUIREMENTS ESTABLISHED BY THE DEPARTMENT IF THE
20 RESIDENTIAL SERVICE AGENCY PROVIDES TRAINING AND A COMPETENCY
21 EVALUATION IN ACCORDANCE WITH THIS SECTION.
22 (B) A RESIDENTIAL SERVICE AGENCY THAT SEEKS TO EMPLOY A NURSE
23 DESCRIBED UNDER SUBSECTION (A) OF THIS SECTION SHALL:
24 (1) ESTABLISH AND IMPLEMENT A TRAINING PROGRAM DESIGNED TO
25 ENSURE SAFE AND COMPETENT CARE BASED ON PATIENT NEEDS;
26 (2) ENSURE THE NURSE RECEIVES CLINICAL SUPERVISION BY A
27 REGISTERED NURSE QUALIFIED IN PRIVATE DUTY NURSING SERVICES;
28 (3) ASSIGN THE NURSE TO A QUALIFIED PRECEPTOR OR CLINICAL
29 SUPERVISOR UNTIL COMPETENCY IS DEMONSTRATED;
30 (4) DOCUMENT SATISFACTORY COMPLETION OF REQUIRED
31 COMPETENCY STANDARDS IN THE NURSE’S PERSONNEL FILE; AND

1 (5) CONDUCT COMPETENCY EVALUATIONS OF THE NURSE:
2 (I) ANNUALLY; OR
3 (II) FOR DISCIPLINARY PURPOSES, ON A MORE FREQUENT
4 SCHEDULE AS REQUIRED BY THE DEPARTMENT.
5 (C) A TRAINING PROGRAM AND COMPETENCY EVALUATION REQUIRED
