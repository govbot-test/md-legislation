1 AN ACT concerning
2 Criminal Law – Counterfeiting and Possession of Counterfeit Lease of Real
3 Property – Penalty
4 FOR the purpose of prohibiting a person with the intent to defraud another from creating,
5 aiding in the creation of, or possessing a counterfeit lease of real property or rental
6 agreement relating to real property; and generally relating to counterfeiting and
7 possession of a counterfeit lease of real property or counterfeit rental agreement
8 relating to real property.

2 HOUSE BILL 765
1 (a) A person, with intent to defraud another, may not counterfeit, cause to be
2 counterfeited, or willingly aid or assist in counterfeiting any:
3 (1) bond;
4 (2) check;
5 (3) deed;
6 (4) draft;
7 (5) endorsement or assignment of a bond, draft, check, or promissory note;
8 (6) entry in an account book or ledger;
9 (7) LEASE OF REAL PROPERTY;
10 (8) letter of credit;
11 [(8)] (9) negotiable instrument;
12 [(9)] (10) power of attorney;
13 [(10)] (11) promissory note;
14 [(11)] (12) release or discharge for money or property;
15 (13) RENTAL AGREEMENT RELATING TO REAL PROPERTY;
16 [(12)] (14) title to a motor vehicle;
17 [(13)] (15) waiver or release of mechanics’ lien; or
18 [(14)] (16) will or codicil.
19 (b) A person may not knowingly, willfully, and with fraudulent intent possess a
20 counterfeit of any of the items listed in subsection (a) of this section.
21 (c) (1) A person who violates subsection (a) of this section is guilty of a felony
22 and on conviction is subject to imprisonment not exceeding 10 years or a fine not exceeding
23 $1,000 or both.
24 (2) A person who violates subsection (b) of this section is guilty of a
25 misdemeanor and on conviction is subject to imprisonment not exceeding 3 years or a fine
26 not exceeding $1,000 or both.

HOUSE BILL 765 3
1 (d) Notwithstanding any other provision of law, the prosecution of an alleged
2 violation of this section or for an alleged violation of a crime based on an act that establishes
3 a violation of this section may be commenced in any county in which:
4 (1) an element of the crime occurred;
5 (2) the deed or other alleged counterfeit instrument is recorded in the
6 county land records, filed with the clerk of the circuit court, or filed with the register of
7 wills;
8 (3) the victim resides; or
9 (4) if the victim is not an individual, the victim conducts business.
10 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
11 October 1, 2026.
