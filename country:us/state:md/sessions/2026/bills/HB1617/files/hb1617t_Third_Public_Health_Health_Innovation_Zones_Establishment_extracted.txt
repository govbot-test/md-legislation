CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Public Health – Health Innovation Zones – Establishment
3 FOR the purpose of authorizing the local zoning authority in a county to designate a health
4 innovation zone if an area meets certain criteria; requiring a county, if the local
5 zoning authority designates a health innovation zone, to establish, by law, certain
6 incentives; requiring the Prince George’s County Council to designate a health
7 innovation zone in certain areas; authorizing the Prince George’s County Council to
8 designate additional health innovation zones in certain areas; requiring authorizing
9 the Governor, subject to the limitations of the State budget, to include an
10 appropriation in the annual budget bill for the Maryland Economic Development
11 Corporation to encourage certain entities to locate in a health innovation zone in
12 Prince George’s County; exempting certain entities from local zoning and land use
13 laws; and generally relating to health innovation zones.
14 BY adding to
15 Article – Health – General
16 Section 20–14A–01 through 20–14A–04 to be under the new subtitle “Subtitle 14A.
17 Health Innovation Zones”
18 Annotated Code of Maryland
19 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

2 HOUSE BILL 1617
1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – Health – General
4 SUBTITLE 14A. HEALTH INNOVATION ZONES.
5 20–14A–01.
6 IN THIS SUBTITLE, “HEALTH INNOVATION ZONE” MEANS A CONTIGUOUS
7 GEOGRAPHIC AREA THAT:
8 (1) DEMONSTRATES MEASURABLE AND DOCUMENTED HEALTH
9 DISPARITIES AND POOR HEALTH OUTCOMES, INCLUDING HIGH RATES OF:
10 (I) MATERNAL HEALTH COMPLICATIONS;
11 (II) NEWBORN HEALTH COMPLICATIONS; AND
12 (III) CHRONIC AND INFECTIOUS DISEASES;
13 (2) IS POPULATED BY:
14 (I) COMMUNITIES OF COLOR;
15 (II) COMMUNITIES WITH LOW–INCOME HOUSEHOLDS;
16 (III) COMMUNITIES WITH A HIGH IMMIGRANT POPULATION; OR
17 (IV) ECONOMICALLY DISADVANTAGED COMMUNITIES; AND
18 (3) IS SMALL ENOUGH TO ALLOW FOR THE INCENTIVES UNDER THIS
19 SUBTITLE TO HAVE A SIGNIFICANT IMPACT ON IMPROVING HEALTH OUTCOMES AND
20 REDUCING HEALTH DISPARITIES, INCLUDING RACIAL, ETHNIC, GEOGRAPHIC, AND
21 DISABILITY–RELATED HEALTH DISPARITIES IN RURAL COMMUNITIES.
22 20–14A–02.
23 (A) SUBJECT TO § 20–14A–03 OF THIS SUBTITLE, THE LOCAL ZONING
24 AUTHORITY OF A COUNTY MAY DESIGNATE A HEALTH INNOVATION ZONE IF THE
25 AREA TO BE DESIGNATED:
26 (1) IS A FOOD OR HEALTH CARE DESERT;
HOUSE BILL 1617 3
1 (2) HAS A HIGH CONCENTRATION OF:
2 (I) CANCER;
3 (II) DIABETES;
4 (III) HEART DISEASE; OR
5 (IV) HYPERTENSION; AND
6 (3) HAS A LOW PREVALENCE OF GROCERY STORES, PHARMACIES,
7 PRIMARY CARE PROVIDERS, AND SPECIALTY CARE PROVIDERS.
8 (B) IF A LOCAL ZONING AUTHORITY OF A COUNTY DESIGNATES A HEALTH
9 INNOVATION ZONE UNDER SUBSECTION (A) OF THIS SECTION, THE COUNTY SHALL
10 ESTABLISH, BY LAW, INCENTIVES THAT WILL BE PROVIDED TO GROCERY STORES,
11 PHARMACIES, PRIMARY CARE PROVIDERS, AND SPECIALTY CARE PROVIDERS THAT
12 LOCATE IN THE HEALTH INNOVATION ZONE.
13 20–14A–03.
14 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
15 INDICATED.
16 (2) “MEDCO” MEANS THE MARYLAND ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT
17 CORPORATION.
18 (3) “PRINCE GEORGE’S COUNTY BLUE LINE CORRIDOR” HAS THE
19 MEANING STATED IN § 10–601 OF THE ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT ARTICLE.
20 (B) THIS SECTION APPLIES ONLY TO PRINCE GEORGE’S COUNTY.
21 (C) THE PRINCE GEORGE’S COUNTY COUNCIL SHALL DESIGNATE A
22 HEALTH INNOVATION ZONE IN THE FOLLOWING AREAS:
23 (1) PRINCE GEORGE’S COUNTY BLUE LINE CORRIDOR; AND
24 (2) THE AREA LOCATED WITHIN 2 MILES OF THE BORDER BETWEEN
25 PRINCE GEORGE’S COUNTY AND THE DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA.
26 (D) THE PRINCE GEORGE’S COUNTY COUNCIL MAY DESIGNATE
27 ADDITIONAL HEALTH INNOVATION ZONES IF THE AREA TO BE DESIGNATED:
4 HOUSE BILL 1617
1 (1) IS A FOOD OR HEALTH CARE DESERT;
2 (2) HAS A HIGH CONCENTRATION OF:
3 (I) CANCER;
4 (II) DIABETES;
5 (III) HEART DISEASE; OR
6 (IV) HYPERTENSION; AND
7 (3) HAS A LOW PREVALENCE OF GROCERY STORES, PHARMACIES,
8 PRIMARY CARE PROVIDERS, AND SPECIALTY CARE PROVIDERS.
9 (E) BEGINNING IN FISCAL YEAR 2028, THE GOVERNOR SHALL MAY,
10 SUBJECT TO THE LIMITATIONS OF THE STATE BUDGET, INCLUDE IN THE ANNUAL
11 BUDGET BILL AN APPROPRIATION OF AT LEAST $5,000,000 TO MEDCO TO
12 ENCOURAGE GROCERY STORES, PHARMACIES, PRIMARY CARE PROVIDERS, AND
13 SPECIALTY CARE PROVIDERS TO LOCATE IN A HEALTH INNOVATION ZONE
14 ESTABLISHED UNDER THIS SECTION.
15 (F) BEGINNING JULY 1, 2028, MEDCO SHALL PROVIDE FUNDING TO
16 ENCOURAGE GROCERY STORES, PHARMACIES, PRIMARY CARE PROVIDERS, AND
17 SPECIALTY CARE PROVIDERS TO ESTABLISH A BUSINESS IN A HEALTH INNOVATION
18 ZONE ESTABLISHED UNDER THIS SECTION.
19 (G) MEDCO SHALL LOOK FOR ADDITIONAL FUNDING SOURCES TO
20 ENCOURAGE HEALTH INNOVATION BUSINESS GROWTH IN A HEALTH INNOVATION
21 ZONE ESTABLISHED UNDER THIS SECTION.
22 20–14A–04.
23 A GROCERY STORE, PHARMACY, PRIMARY CARE PROVIDER, AND SPECIALTY
24 CARE PROVIDER LOCATED IN A HEALTH INNOVATION ZONE ESTABLISHED UNDER
25 THIS SUBTITLE ARE NOT SUBJECT TO LOCAL ZONING OR LAND USE LAWS.
26 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
27 October 1, 2026.
