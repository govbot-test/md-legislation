CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Judiciary Department – Judicial Removal – Disqualification From Future
3 Judicial Office
4 FOR the purpose of providing that a justice or judge removed or retired from office on
5 recommendation of the Commission on Judicial Disabilities is not eligible for future
6 judicial office.
7 BY proposing an amendment to the Maryland Constitution
8 Article IV – Judiciary Department
9 Section 4B
10 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
11 (Three–fifths of all the members elected to each of the two Houses concurring), That it be
12 proposed that the Maryland Constitution read as follows:
13 Article IV – Judiciary Department
14 4B.
15 (a) (1) The Commission on Judicial Disabilities has the power to:
16 (i) Investigate complaints against any justice or judge of the
17 Supreme Court of Maryland, any intermediate courts of appeal, the circuit courts, the
18 District Court of Maryland, or the orphans’ court; and

1 (ii) Conduct hearings concerning such complaints, administer oaths
2 and affirmations, issue process to compel the attendance of witnesses and the production
3 of evidence, and require persons to testify and produce evidence by granting them immunity
4 from prosecution or from penalty or forfeiture.
5 (2) The Commission has the power to issue a reprimand and the power to
6 recommend to the Supreme Court of Maryland the removal, censure, or other appropriate
7 disciplining of a justice or judge or, in an appropriate case, retirement.
8 (3) All proceedings, testimony, and evidence before the Commission shall
9 be confidential and privileged, except as provided by rule of the Supreme Court of
10 Maryland; the record and any proceeding filed with the Supreme Court of Maryland shall
11 lose its confidential character, except as ordered by the Supreme Court of Maryland.
12 (4) No justice or judge shall participate as a member of the Commission in
13 any proceedings involving that justice’s or judge’s own conduct, and the Governor shall
14 appoint another justice or judge as a substitute member of the Commission for those
15 proceedings.
16 (5) The Supreme Court of Maryland shall prescribe by rule the means to
17 implement and enforce the powers of the Commission and the practice and procedure before
18 the Commission.
19 (b) (1) Upon any recommendation of the Commission, the Supreme Court of
20 Maryland, after a hearing and upon a finding of misconduct while in office, or of persistent
21 failure to perform the duties of the office, or of conduct prejudicial to the proper
22 administration of justice, may remove the justice or judge from office or may censure or
23 otherwise discipline the justice or judge, or the Supreme Court of Maryland, after hearing
24 and upon a finding of disability which is or is likely to become permanent and which
25 seriously interferes with the performance of the justice’s or judge’s duties, may retire the
26 justice or judge from office.
27 (2) A justice or judge removed under this section, and the justice’s or
28 judge’s surviving spouse, shall have the rights and privileges accruing from the justice’s or
29 judge’s judicial service only to the extent prescribed by the order of removal.
30 (3) A justice or judge retired under this section shall have the rights and
31 privileges prescribed by law for other retired justices or judges.
32 (4) A JUSTICE OR JUDGE REMOVED OR RETIRED UNDER THIS
33 SECTION IS NOT ELIGIBLE FOR FUTURE JUDICIAL OFFICE.
34 (5) No justice of the Supreme Court of Maryland shall sit in judgment in
35 any hearing involving that justice’s own conduct.

13 each ballot there shall be printed the words “For the Constitutional Amendment” and
14 “Against the Constitutional Amendment”, as now provided by law. Immediately after the
15 election, all returns shall be made to the Governor of the vote for and against the proposed
16 amendment, as directed by Article XIV of the Maryland Constitution, and further
17 proceedings held in accordance with Article XIV.
