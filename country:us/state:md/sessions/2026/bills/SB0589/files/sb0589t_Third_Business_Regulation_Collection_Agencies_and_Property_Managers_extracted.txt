1 AN ACT concerning
2 Business Regulation – Collection Agencies – Licensure Exemption for Property
3 Managers
4 Business Regulation – Collection Agencies and Property Managers
5 FOR the purpose of exempting property managers engaged in certain activities from the
6 requirement to hold a license to do business as a collection agency from the State
7 Collection Agency Licensing Board; and generally relating to property managers,
8 collection agencies, and the State Collection Agency Licensing Board prohibiting
9 enforcement of certain provisions of law regulating collection agencies against
10 property managers pending the resolution of a certain judicial case; and generally
11 relating to property managers and debt collection.
12 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
13 Article – Business Regulation
14 Section 7–102
15 Annotated Code of Maryland
16 (2024 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
17 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
18 Article – Business Regulation
19 Section 7–301(a)
20 Annotated Code of Maryland
21 (2024 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

2 SENATE BILL 589
1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 (a) In this section, “property manager” means a person responsible for the
4 management and maintenance of real property, including:
5 (1) finding tenants;
6 (2) collecting rent, utilities, and fees;
7 (3) paying expenses and applicable taxes;
8 (4) maintaining utilities and systems; and
9 (5) taking any necessary actions to manage and operate real property.
10 (b) Title 7 of the Business Regulation Article may not be enforced against a
11 property manager until the resolution of case number C–15–CV–25–000340, Christine
12 Smith v. Bozzuto Management Company, including any appellate action.
13 Article – Business Regulation
14 7–102.
15 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
16 (2) “Common ownership” means direct or indirect ownership of more than
17 50% of a person.
18 (3) “Principal business” means a business activity of a person that
19 comprises more than 50% of the total business activities of the person.
20 (4) “PROPERTY MANAGER” MEANS A PERSON RESPONSIBLE FOR THE
21 MANAGEMENT AND MAINTENANCE OF REAL PROPERTY, INCLUDING:
22 (I) FINDING TENANTS;
23 (II) COLLECTING RENT, UTILITIES, AND FEES;
24 (III) PAYING EXPENSES AND APPLICABLE TAXES;
25 (IV) MAINTAINING UTILITIES AND SYSTEMS; AND
26 (V) TAKING ANY NECESSARY ACTIONS TO MANAGE AND
27 OPERATE REAL PROPERTY.
SENATE BILL 589 3
1 (b) This title does not apply to:
2 (1) a bank;
3 (2) a federal or State credit union;
4 (3) a mortgage lender;
5 (4) a person acting under an order of a court of competent jurisdiction;
6 (5) a licensed real estate broker, or an individual acting on behalf of the
7 real estate broker, in the collection of rent or allied charges for property;
8 (6) a savings and loan association;
9 (7) a title company as to its escrow business;
10 (8) a trust company;
11 (9) a lawyer who is collecting a debt for a client, unless the lawyer has an
12 employee who:
13 (i) is not a lawyer; and
14 (ii) is engaged primarily to solicit debts for collection or primarily
15 makes contact with a debtor to collect or adjust a debt through a procedure identified with
16 the operation of a collection agency; [or]
17 (10) a person who is collecting a debt for another person if:
18 (i) both persons are related by common ownership;
19 (ii) the person who is collecting a debt does so only for those persons
20 to whom it is related by common ownership;
21 (iii) the principal business of the person who is collecting a debt is not
22 the collection of debts; and
23 (iv) before collecting a debt, the person files with the Board:
24 1. the correct name of the person;
25 2. an address and telephone number of a contact person; and
26 3. the name of the person’s resident agent; OR
4 SENATE BILL 589
1 (11) A PROPERTY MANAGER COLLECTING PAYMENT FOR RENT,
2 UTILITIES, OR FEES FROM A RESIDENTIAL TENANT ON BEHALF OF A PROPERTY
3 OWNER IF:
4 (I) PAYMENT IS COLLECTED ONLY DURING THE TERM OF THE
5 LEASE OR A HOLDOVER PERIOD; AND
6 (II) THE COLLECTION OF DEBTS, INCLUDING FOR RENT,
7 UTILITIES, AND FEES IS NOT THE PRIMARY PURPOSE OF THE PROPERTY MANAGER
8 WITH RESPECT TO THE PROPERTY.
9 7–301.
10 (a) Except as otherwise provided in this title, a person must have a license
11 whenever the person does business as a collection agency in the State.
12 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
13 October 1, 2026.
