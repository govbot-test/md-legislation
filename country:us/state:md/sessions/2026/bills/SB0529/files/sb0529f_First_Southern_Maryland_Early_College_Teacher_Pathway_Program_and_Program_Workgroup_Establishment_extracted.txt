A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Southern Maryland Early College Teacher Pathway Program and Program
3 Workgroup – Establishment
4 FOR the purpose of establishing the Southern Maryland Early College Teacher Pathway
5 Program; requiring the State Department of Education to administer and
6 implement, in consultation with the Maryland Higher Education Commission, the
7 Program subject to a certain condition; requiring a Program student to be considered
8 a dually enrolled student for a certain purpose; requiring the University System of
9 Maryland to develop, in consultation with the Department and the Commission, the
10 Program and the teacher preparation pathway; establishing the Southern Maryland
11 Early College Teacher Pathway Program Workgroup; and generally relating to the
12 Southern Maryland Early College Teacher Pathway Program and the Southern
13 Maryland Early College Teacher Pathway Program Workgroup.
14 BY adding to
15 Article – Education
16 Section 7–2201 through 7–2204 to be under the new subtitle “Subtitle 22. Southern
17 Maryland Early College Teacher Pathway Program”
18 Annotated Code of Maryland
19 (2025 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
20 BY adding to
21 Article – Education
22 Section 12–124
23 Annotated Code of Maryland
24 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
25 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
26 Article – Education
27 Section 15–127
28 Annotated Code of Maryland

2 SENATE BILL 529
1 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
2 Preamble
3 WHEREAS, Maryland faces a critical shortage of qualified teachers, especially in
4 high–need schools and rural areas such as Southern Maryland where local school systems
5 report persistent vacancies in core subject areas and growing challenges in retaining
6 educators; and
7 WHEREAS, The Blueprint for Maryland’s Future calls for expanding high–quality
8 pathways into the teaching profession, increasing college readiness, and improving
9 workforce pipelines in every region of the State; and
10 WHEREAS, Nationally, early college programs have proven to increase college
11 enrollment, degree attainment, and workforce participation — particularly among
12 first–generation and low–income students — and can be leveraged to build stronger and
13 more diverse teacher pipelines; and
14 WHEREAS, Despite strong national evidence of success, Maryland lags behind most
15 states in providing early postsecondary opportunities for its students; and
16 WHEREAS, As of 2023, Maryland ranked low nationally for dual enrollment
17 participation as a percent of high school enrollment at 10% and ranked low nationally for
18 dual enrollment as a percent of undergraduate enrollment at 7%; and
19 WHEREAS, As of 2022, participation in dual enrollment and early college programs
20 in Maryland is overly concentrated at community colleges with only 5% of students enrolled
21 through public four–year institutions — compared to approximately 28% nationally —
22 limiting exposure to bachelor’s degree pathways, including teacher preparation programs;
23 and
24 WHEREAS, Southern Maryland’s geography, limited access to nearby four–year
25 institutions, and workforce needs make it an ideal region for a program that bridges high
26 schools, community colleges, and the University System of Maryland regional higher
27 education centers to create a seamless teacher preparation pathway; and
28 WHEREAS, Establishing a Southern Maryland Early College Teacher Pathway
29 Program will align with the Blueprint’s goals; now, therefore,
30 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
31 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
32 Article – Education

34 PROGRAM.

1 7–2201.
2 IN THIS SUBTITLE, “PROGRAM” MEANS THE SOUTHERN MARYLAND EARLY
3 COLLEGE TEACHER PATHWAY PROGRAM.
4 7–2202.
5 (A) THERE IS A SOUTHERN MARYLAND EARLY COLLEGE TEACHER
6 PATHWAY PROGRAM.
7 (B) THE PURPOSE OF THE PROGRAM IS TO:
8 (1) CREATE A STRUCTURED, CREDIT–BEARING PATHWAY FOR HIGH
9 SCHOOL STUDENTS TO BEGIN PREPARATION TO TEACH PRIMARY AND SECONDARY
10 EDUCATION;
11 (2) EXPAND ACCESS TO AND ENSURE THE AFFORDABILITY OF
12 HIGHER EDUCATION FOR HIGH SCHOOL STUDENTS WHO ARE:
13 (I) HISTORICALLY UNDERREPRESENTED IN HIGHER
14 EDUCATION; AND
15 (II) FROM RURAL AREAS OF THE STATE;
16 (3) BUILD A STRONG AND DIVERSE TEACHER PIPELINE TO SERVE THE
17 STATE’S PUBLIC PRIMARY AND SECONDARY SCHOOLS; AND
18 (4) SERVE AS A MODEL TO BE REPLICATED FOR REGIONAL
19 IMPLEMENTATION STATEWIDE.
20 (C) THE DEPARTMENT, IN CONSULTATION WITH THE MARYLAND HIGHER
21 EDUCATION COMMISSION, SHALL ADMINISTER AND IMPLEMENT THE PROGRAM ON
22 COMPLETION OF THE UNIVERSITY SYSTEM OF MARYLAND’S DEVELOPMENT OF THE
23 PROGRAM IN ACCORDANCE WITH § 12–124 OF THIS ARTICLE.
24 7–2203.
25 A PROGRAM STUDENT SHALL BE CONSIDERED A DUALLY ENROLLED STUDENT
26 FOR PURPOSES OF § 15–127 OF THIS ARTICLE AND MAY NOT BE REQUIRED TO PAY
27 ANY COST THAT IS RELATED TO ENROLLMENT OR PARTICIPATION IN THE PROGRAM,
28 INCLUDING TUITION AND MANDATORY FEES.
29 7–2204.

14 (a) (1) In this section, “dually enrolled student” means a student who is dually
15 enrolled in:
16 (i) A secondary school in the State; and
17 (ii) An institution of higher education in the State.
18 (2) “Dually enrolled student” includes a student enrolled in a credit or
19 noncredit certificate or license program, course, or sequence of courses that leads to
20 certification or licensure at an institution of higher education in the State.
21 (b) A public institution of higher education may not charge tuition to a dually
22 enrolled student.
23 (c) Subject to subsection (d) of this section, for each dually enrolled student who
24 is enrolled in a public school in the county, the county board shall pay 75% of the cost of
25 tuition for a public institution of higher education in the State.
26 (d) (1) If there is an agreement before July 1, 2020, between a public school
27 and a public institution of higher education in which the public institution of higher
28 education charges less than 75% of the cost of tuition to a dually enrolled student, the
29 county board shall pay the cost of tuition under the existing agreement.
30 (2) Every 2 years, a public school and a public institution of higher
31 education may evaluate and modify an agreement made under paragraph (1) of this
32 subsection.
