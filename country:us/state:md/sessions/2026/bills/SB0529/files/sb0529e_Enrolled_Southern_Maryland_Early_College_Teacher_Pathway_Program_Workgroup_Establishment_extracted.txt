1 AN ACT concerning
2 Southern Maryland Early College Teacher Pathway Program and Program
3 Workgroup – Establishment
4 FOR the purpose of establishing the Southern Maryland Early College Teacher Pathway
5 Program; requiring the State Department of Education to administer and
6 implement, in consultation with the Maryland Higher Education Commission, the
7 Program subject to a certain condition; requiring a Program student to be considered
8 a dually enrolled student for a certain purpose; requiring the University System of
9 Maryland to develop, in consultation with the State Department of Education and
10 the Maryland Higher Education Commission, the certain recommendations for a
11 Southern Maryland Early College Teacher Pathway Program and the a teacher
12 preparation pathway in a certain manner; establishing the Southern Maryland
13 Early College Teacher Pathway Program Workgroup; and generally relating to the
14 Southern Maryland Early College Teacher Pathway Program and the Southern
15 Maryland Early College Teacher Pathway Program Workgroup.

18 WHEREAS, Maryland faces a critical shortage of qualified teachers, especially in
19 high–need schools and rural areas such as Southern Maryland where local school systems
20 report persistent vacancies in core subject areas and growing challenges in retaining
21 educators; and
22 WHEREAS, The Blueprint for Maryland’s Future calls for expanding high–quality
23 pathways into the teaching profession, increasing college readiness, and improving
24 workforce pipelines in every region of the State; and
25 WHEREAS, Nationally, early college programs have proven to increase college
26 enrollment, degree attainment, and workforce participation — particularly among
27 first–generation and low–income students — and can be leveraged to build stronger and
28 more diverse teacher pipelines; and
29 WHEREAS, Despite strong national evidence of success, Maryland lags behind most
30 states in providing early postsecondary opportunities for its students; and
31 WHEREAS, As of 2023, Maryland ranked low nationally for dual enrollment
32 participation as a percent of high school enrollment at 10% and ranked low nationally for
33 dual enrollment as a percent of undergraduate enrollment at 7%; and
34 WHEREAS, As of 2022, participation in dual enrollment and early college programs
35 in Maryland is overly concentrated at community colleges with only 5% of students enrolled
36 through public four–year institutions — compared to approximately 28%

17 and a public institution of higher education in which the public institution of higher
18 education charges less than 75% of the cost of tuition to a dually enrolled student, the
19 county board shall pay the cost of tuition under the existing agreement.
20 (2) Every 2 years, a public school and a public institution of higher
21 education may evaluate and modify an agreement made under paragraph (1) of this
22 subsection.
