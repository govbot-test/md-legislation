CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Consumer Protection – Unsolicited Loans
3 FOR the purpose of prohibiting a creditor person from sending a check or other negotiable
4 instrument to an individual under certain circumstances; establishing that an
5 individual who receives a check or other negotiable instrument is not liable for the
6 amount of the check or negotiable instrument under certain circumstances; and
7 generally relating to extensions of consumer credit.
8 BY adding to
9 Article – Commercial Law
10 Section 14–1330
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2025 Replacement Volume)

2 SENATE BILL 582
1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – Commercial Law
4 14–1330.
5 (A) IN THIS SECTION, “CREDITOR” MEANS A PERSON THAT ENGAGES IN
6 WHOLE OR IN PART IN THE BUSINESS OF MAKING LOANS OR OTHER EXTENSIONS OF
7 CREDIT FOR PERSONAL, FAMILY, OR HOUSEHOLD PURPOSES THIS SECTION DOES
8 NOT APPLY TO A PERSON THAT SENDS A TO AN INDIVIDUAL:
9 (1) A CONVENIENCE CHECK TO AN INDIVIDUAL FOR THE SOLE
10 PURPOSE OF ACCESSING AN EXISTING CREDIT LINE OF THE INDIVIDUAL; OR
11 (2) A PRESCREENED FIRM OFFER OF UNSECURED CREDIT SUBJECT TO
12 REGULATION UNDER THE FEDERAL FAIR CREDIT REPORTING ACT.
13 (B) A CREDITOR PERSON MAY NOT SEND A CHECK OR OTHER NEGOTIABLE
14 INSTRUMENT TO AN INDIVIDUAL IF:
15 (1) (I) THE CHECK OR OTHER NEGOTIABLE INSTRUMENT IS SENT
16 IN CONNECTION WITH AN OFFER TO EXTEND SECURED CREDIT TO THE INDIVIDUAL;
17 AND
18 (2) (II) THE INDIVIDUAL HAS NOT SUBMITTED AN APPLICATION
19 FOR OR OTHERWISE REQUESTED THE EXTENSION OF SECURED CREDIT BEFORE
20 RECEIVING THE CHECK OR OTHER NEGOTIABLE INSTRUMENT; OR
21 (2) THE CHECK OR OTHER NEGOTIABLE INSTRUMENT IS SENT AS
22 PART OF AN UNSOLICITED OFFER TO PURCHASE REAL PROPERTY.
23 (C) AN INDIVIDUAL WHO IS SENT A CHECK OR OTHER NEGOTIABLE
24 INSTRUMENT IN VIOLATION OF SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION IS NOT LIABLE
25 FOR THE AMOUNT OF THE CHECK OR OTHER NEGOTIABLE INSTRUMENT UNLESS THE
26 INDIVIDUAL ACTUALLY RECEIVES AND NEGOTIATES THE CHECK OR OTHER
27 NEGOTIABLE INSTRUMENT.
28 (D) A CREDITOR PERSON THAT VIOLATES THIS SECTION IS GUILTY OF A
29 MISDEMEANOR AND ON CONVICTION SUBJECT TO A FINE NOT EXCEEDING $500.
30 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
31 October 1, 2026.
