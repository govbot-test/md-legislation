2 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
3 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
4 Article – Education
5 1–101.
6 (a) In this article, unless the context requires otherwise, the following words have
7 the meanings indicated.
8 (f) “Department” means the State Department of Education.
9 7–455.
10 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
11 INDICATED.
12 (2) “DONOR REGISTRY” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 4–501 OF THE
13 ESTATES AND TRUSTS ARTICLE.
14 (3) “PROCUREMENT ORGANIZATION” HAS THE MEANING STATED IN §
15 4–501 OF THE ESTATES AND TRUSTS ARTICLE.
16 (B) IF THE DEPARTMENT DEVELOPS, UPDATES, OR APPROVES
17 CURRICULUM GUIDANCE, INSTRUCTIONAL RESOURCES, OR PROFESSIONAL
18 DEVELOPMENT MATERIALS REGARDING ORGAN, EYE, AND TISSUE DONATION FOR
19 USE BY COUNTY BOARDS, THE DEPARTMENT SHALL CONSULT WITH:
20 (1) THE QUALIFIED NONPROFIT ENTITY THAT OPERATES THE DONOR
21 REGISTRY UNDER § 4–516 OF THE ESTATES AND TRUSTS ARTICLE; AND
22 (2) PROCUREMENT ORGANIZATIONS SERVING IN THE STATE.
23 (C) THE DEPARTMENT MAY USE OR REFERENCE EXISTING EDUCATIONAL
24 MATERIALS THAT WERE PREVIOUSLY DEVELOPED BY OR BEING USED BY THE DONOR
25 REGISTRY OR A PROCUREMENT ORGANIZATION IF THE EDUCATIONAL MATERIALS
26 ARE:
27 (1) AGE APPROPRIATE;
28 (2) EVIDENCE–BASED AND MEDICALLY ACCURATE; AND

5 4–501.
6 (a) In this subtitle the following words have the meanings indicated.
7 (h) “Donor registry” means a database that contains records of anatomical gifts
8 and amendments to or revocations of anatomical gifts as provided in § 4–516 of this subtitle.
9 (r) “Organ procurement organization” means a person designated by the
10 Secretary of the United States Department of Health and Human Services as an organ
11 procurement organization.
12 (v) “Procurement organization” means an eye bank, an organ procurement
