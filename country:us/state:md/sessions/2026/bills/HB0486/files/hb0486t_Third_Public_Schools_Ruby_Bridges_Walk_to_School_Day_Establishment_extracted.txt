CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Public Schools – Ruby Bridges Walk to School Day and Maryland School
3 Pedestrian Awareness Day – Establishment
4 FOR the purpose of designating a certain date as Ruby Bridges Walk to School Day;
5 designating a certain day as Maryland School Pedestrian Awareness Day; requiring
6 the State Department of Education to make resources available in a certain manner
7 for a program of education for Ruby Bridges Walk to School Day and to plan
8 Maryland School Pedestrian Awareness Day; and generally relating to Ruby Bridges
9 Walk to School Day and Maryland School Pedestrian Awareness Day.
10 BY adding to
11 Article – Education
12 Section 7–137 and 7–138
13 Annotated Code of Maryland
14 (2025 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
15 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
16 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
17 Article – Education
18 7–137.
19 (A) THE PURPOSE OF THIS SECTION IS TO DESIGNATE NOVEMBER 14 EACH
20 YEAR AS “RUBY BRIDGES WALK TO SCHOOL DAY” TO COMMEMORATE RUBY
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from existing law.
Underlining indicates amendments to bill.
Strike out indicates matter stricken from the bill by amendment or deleted from the law by
amendment. *hb0486*
2 HOUSE BILL 486
1 BRIDGES, WHO, ON NOVEMBER 14, 1960, BECAME THE FIRST AFRICAN AMERICAN
2 STUDENT TO INTEGRATE AN ALL–WHITE ELEMENTARY SCHOOL IN NEW ORLEANS,
3 LOUISIANA.
4 (B) (1) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL MAKE RESOURCE MATERIALS
5 PUBLICLY AVAILABLE TO ASSIST LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEMS IN PLANNING A PROGRAM
6 OF EDUCATION FOR RUBY BRIDGES WALK TO SCHOOL DAY.
7 (2) THE RESOURCE MATERIALS PROVIDED BY THE DEPARTMENT IN
8 ACCORDANCE WITH PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION:
9 (I) MAY INCLUDE INSTRUCTIONAL MATERIALS, INCLUDING
10 AGE–APPROPRIATE LESSON PLANS, DISCUSSION GUIDES, HISTORICAL DOCUMENTS,
11 AND MULTIMEDIA RESOURCES; AND
12 (II) SHALL INCLUDE RESOURCES TO ASSIST TEACHERS TO
13 ENGAGE STUDENTS IN DISCUSSIONS ABOUT CIVIL RIGHTS AND SOCIAL JUSTICE.
14 7–138.
15 (A) THE PURPOSE OF THIS SECTION IS TO DESIGNATE THE SECOND
16 WEDNESDAY IN OCTOBER EACH YEAR AS “MARYLAND SCHOOL PEDESTRIAN
17 AWARENESS DAY” TO ENCOURAGE LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEMS, IN COLLABORATION
18 WITH THE COUNTY OR MUNICIPALITY, TO PROMOTE SAFE PEDESTRIAN ROUTES TO
19 AND FROM SCHOOLS.
20 (B) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL MAKE RESOURCE MATERIALS PUBLICLY
21 AVAILABLE TO ASSIST LOCAL SCHOOL SYSTEMS IN PLANNING MARYLAND SCHOOL
22 PEDESTRIAN AWARENESS DAY.
23 (C) THE RESOURCE MATERIALS PROVIDED BY THE DEPARTMENT IN
24 ACCORDANCE WITH SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION MAY INCLUDE:
25 (1) PEDESTRIAN SAFETY BEST PRACTICES;
26 (2) AGE–APPROPRIATE PEDESTRIAN SAFETY LESSON PLANS;
27 (3) MODEL WALK AUDIT TEMPLATES AND DISCUSSION GUIDES;
28 (4) AVAILABLE GRANT RESOURCES; AND
29 (5) ANY OTHER MATERIALS RELATED TO PEDESTRIAN AWARENESS.
HOUSE BILL 486 3
1 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
2 1, 2026.
Approved:
