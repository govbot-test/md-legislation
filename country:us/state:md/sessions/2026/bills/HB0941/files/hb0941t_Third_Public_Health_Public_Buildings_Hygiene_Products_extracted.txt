2 Public Health – Public Buildings – Hygiene Products
3 FOR the purpose of requiring public restrooms in public buildings to provide an adequate
4 supply of certain hygiene products; and generally relating to hygiene products in
5 public buildings.

2 HOUSE BILL 941
1 (A) IN THIS SUBTITLE THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
2 INDICATED.
3 (B) “MENSTRUAL HYGIENE PRODUCTS” MEANS APPROPRIATELY SIZED
4 TAMPONS, SANITARY NAPKINS, AND SANITARY PADS FOR USE IN CONNECTION WITH
5 THE MENSTRUAL CYCLE.
6 (C) (1) “PUBLIC BUILDING” MEANS A BUILDING, A STRUCTURE, OR AN
7 IMPROVED AREA THAT IS OWNED, LEASED, OR OPERATED BY THE STATE OR A
8 POLITICAL SUBDIVISION OF THE STATE.
9 (2) “PUBLIC BUILDING” INCLUDES:
10 (I) A PUBLIC MASS TRANSPORTATION ACCOMMODATION, SUCH
11 AS A TERMINAL OR STATION, THAT IS SUPPORTED BY PUBLIC FUNDS; AND
12 (II) AN IMPROVEMENT OF A PUBLIC AREA USED FOR
13 GATHERING OR AMUSEMENT, INCLUDING A PUBLIC PARK OR RECREATION CENTER.
14 (3) “PUBLIC BUILDING” DOES NOT INCLUDE A FACILITY THAT IS
15 PRIMARILY USED TO PROVIDE PRIMARY OR SECONDARY EDUCATION.
16 (D) (1) “PUBLIC RESTROOM” MEANS A SANITARY FACILITY AVAILABLE
17 TO THE GENERAL PUBLIC THAT CONTAINS AT LEAST ONE TOILET OR URINAL.
18 (2) “PUBLIC RESTROOM” DOES NOT INCLUDE A SANITARY FACILITY
19 AVAILABLE TO THE GENERAL PUBLIC THAT CONTAINS ONLY A COMPOSTING TOILET
20 OR A PIT TOILET.
21 2–1002.
22 EACH PUBLIC RESTROOM IN EACH PUBLIC BUILDING SHALL PROVIDE AN
23 ADEQUATE SUPPLY OF:
24 (1) HAND SOAP;
25 (2) TOILET PAPER;
26 (3) TOWELS OR OTHER HAND DRYING DEVICES;
27 (4) WATER;
28 (5) WASTE CONTAINERS; AND

1 (6) MENSTRUAL HYGIENE PRODUCTS.
2 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
