2 Recovery Residences – Certification – Requirement
3 FOR the purpose of requiring, beginning on a certain date, rather than authorizing, a
4 recovery residence to be certified by a credentialing entity before operating in the
5 State; and generally relating to the certification of recovery residences.

15 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

17 Article – Health – General
18 Section 19–2501.1
19 Annotated Code of Maryland
20 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

2 SENATE BILL 876
1 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
2 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
3 Article – Health – General
4 7.5–101.
5 (a) In this title the following words have the meanings indicated.
6 (o) “Recovery residence” means a service that:
7 (1) Provides alcohol–free and illicit–drug–free housing to individuals with
8 substance–related disorders or addictive disorders or co–occurring mental disorders and
9 substance–related disorders or addictive disorders; and
10 (2) Does not include clinical treatment services.
11 7.5–401.
12 (a) (1) Except as otherwise provided in this section, a behavioral health
13 program shall be licensed by the Secretary before program services may be provided in this
14 State.
15 (2) PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION DOES NOT APPLY TO A
16 RECOVERY RESIDENCE.
17 (b) The Secretary may exempt the following persons from the licensure
18 requirements of this section:
19 (2) Alcoholics Anonymous, Narcotics Anonymous, [recovery residences,]
20 peer support services, family support services, or other similar organizations, if the
21 organization holds meetings or provides support services but does not provide any type of
22 treatment;
23 19–2501.
24 (a) In this subtitle the following words have the meanings indicated.
25 (c) “Certified recovery residence” means a recovery residence that holds a
26 certificate of compliance.
27 (e) “Recovery residence” has the meaning stated in § 7.5–101 of this article.
28 19–2501.1.
SENATE BILL 876 3
1 BEGINNING JANUARY JUNE 1, 2027, A RECOVERY RESIDENCE SHALL BE
2 CERTIFIED BY A CREDENTIALING ENTITY APPROVED BY THE DEPARTMENT UNDER
3 § 19–2502 OF THIS SUBTITLE BEFORE OPERATING IN THE STATE.
4 19–2502.
5 (a) The Department shall approve a credentialing entity to develop and
6 administer a certification process for recovery residences.
7 (b) The credentialing entity shall:
8 (1) Establish recovery residence certification requirements;
9 (2) Establish processes to administer the application, certification, and
10 recertification process;
11 (3) Establish processes to monitor and inspect a recovery residence;
12 (4) Conduct an on–site inspection of a recovery residence:
13 (i) Before issuing a certificate of compliance; and
14 (ii) At least once during each certification renewal period; and
15 (5) Issue a certificate of compliance on approval of the application process
16 and the inspection of the recovery residence.
17 (c) A certificate of compliance issued by the credentialing entity is valid for 1 year
18 from the date of issuance.
19 (d) The credentialing entity may revoke the certificate of compliance of a certified
20 recovery residence if the credentialing entity finds that the recovery residence is not in
21 compliance with the requirements established by the credentialing entity.
22 19–2503.
23 (a) (1) On or before October 1, 2017, the credentialing entity shall submit a list
24 to the Department of the recovery residences that have obtained a certificate of compliance.
25 (2) THE CREDENTIALING ENTITY SHALL SUBMIT AN UPDATED LIST TO
26 THE DEPARTMENT AS NECESSARY.
27 (b) [(1) On or before November 1, 2017, the] THE Department shall publish
28 AND MAINTAIN on its website:
4 SENATE BILL 876
1 [(i)] (1) A list of each credentialing entity and the contact
2 information for the credentialing entity; and
3 [(ii)] (2) A list of each CERTIFIED recovery residence operating in
4 each county in the State.
5 [(2) The list published under paragraph (1)(ii) of this subsection shall
6 indicate whether the owner of a recovery residence has received a valid certificate of
7 compliance.]
8 (c) (1) [On or before November 1, 2017, a] EACH credentialing entity shall
9 publish AND MAINTAIN on its website a list of each recovery residence that holds a valid
10 certificate of compliance.
11 (2) The list published under paragraph (1) of this subsection shall include
12 only the owner of the recovery residence and the contact information of the owner.
13 19–2504.
14 (a) A person may not advertise, represent, or imply to the public that a recovery
15 residence is a certified recovery residence unless the recovery residence has obtained a
16 certificate of compliance under this subtitle.
17 (b) (1) A person who violates subsection (a) of this section is subject to a civil
18 penalty imposed by the Department not exceeding $1,000 for each offense.
19 (2) In setting the amount of a civil penalty under paragraph (1) of this
20 subsection, the Department shall consider the nature, number, and seriousness of the
21 violations, the ability of the [certified] recovery residence to pay the penalty, and any other
22 factors the Department determines are relevant.
23 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
24 October 1, 2026.
Approved:
