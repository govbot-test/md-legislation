WES MOORE, Governor Ch. 408
Chapter 408
AN ACT concerning
Procurement – Department of Transportation and Maryland Transportation
Authority Contracts – Board of Public Works Contract Authority – Study and
Report
FOR the purpose of authorizing the Board of Public Works to control certain procurement
contracts by the Department of Transportation and the Maryland Transportation
Authority valued at or above a certain amount; requiring the Board to adopt
regulations for the review of a contract modification for certain procurement
contracts by the Department or the Authority; establishing a workgroup to review
the procurement procedures for capital expenditures in connection with State roads,
bridges, or highways and to issue a certain report making certain recommendations;
and generally relating to procurement contracts and the Department of
Transportation and the Maryland Transportation Authority.
BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
Article – State Finance and Procurement

Section 2:
Section 4–415, 10–202, 12–101(a), 12–103, 12–107(a), 12–108, and 12–202(a)

(2021 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

BY adding to

Section 10:
SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
That:
(a) There is a Workgroup on Transportation Contract Approval Authority.
(b) The Workgroup consists of the following members:
(1) one member of the House of Delegates, appointed by the Speaker of the
House;
(2) one member of the Senate of Maryland, appointed by the President of the
Senate;
(3) the Comptroller, or the Comptroller’s designee;
– 6 –
WES MOORE, Governor Ch. 408
(4) the State Treasurer, or the State Treasurer’s designee;
(5) the Secretary of Transportation, or the Secretary’s designee;
(6) the Secretary of Social and Economic Mobility, or the Secretary’s
designee;
(7) a representative appointed by the Maryland Association of Counties; and
(8) the following members appointed by the Governor:
(i) a representative of the engineering and design services industry;
(ii) a representative of the transportation construction and materials
industry;
(iii) a member of the business community who has been certified as a
Disadvantaged Business Enterprise and as a Minority Business Enterprise; and
(iv) a representative of a State labor organization.
(c) The Governor shall designate the chair of the Workgroup.
(d) The Department of Transportation and the Board of Public Works shall jointly
provide staff for the Workgroup.
(e) A member of the Workgroup:
(1) may not receive compensation as a member of the Workgroup; but
(2) is entitled to reimbursement for expenses under the Standard State
Travel Regulations, as provided in the State budget.
(f) The Workgroup shall:
(1) study the existing approval authority structure for capital expenditures
by the Department of Transportation and the Maryland Transportation Authority in
connection with State roads, bridges, or highways, including a review of the justifications
for the existing approval authority structure and whether the structure continues to provide
public benefit;
(2) study the effect of altering the approval authority structure for capital
expenditures by the Department of Transportation and the Maryland Transportation
Authority in connection with State roads, bridges, or highways, including whether to require
– 7 –
Ch. 408 2026 LAWS OF MARYLAND
approval of the award of new contracts and change orders for these projects by the Board of
Public Works process;
(3) study the purpose, impact, and public value of the Board of Public
Works’ approval process for capital expenditures by the Department of Transportation and
the Maryland Transportation Authority in connection with State roads, bridges, or
highways;
(4) analyze alternative options for review and approval by the Board of
Public Works for the award of new contracts and change orders for road, bridge, or highway
projects that include:
(i) advance notice and opportunity to review and comment on
upcoming contracts conducted by the Department of Transportation or the Maryland
Transportation Authority, and the appropriate stage of the contract for these activities;
(ii) public reporting of contract and change order awards made by the
Department of Transportation or the Maryland Transportation Authority; or
(iii) appropriate thresholds for new contracts and contract change
orders or modifications related to capital expenditures by the Department of Transportation
and the Maryland Transportation Authority in connection with State roads, bridges, or
highways;
(5) examine the impact on contract award and completion timeline;
(6) evaluate the effect of expanding the current delegated authority for
capital expenditures by the Department of Transportation and the Maryland Transportation
Authority in connection with State roads, bridges, or highways to contracts and change
orders for maintenance and capital construction to support service on Maryland Transit
Administration fixed guideway systems and associated transit facilities and vehicles; and
(7) evaluate the impact of the existing approval authority structure and any
alternative approval authority structures on the ability to secure federal funding for roads,
bridges, or highways.
(g) On or before December 1, 2026, the Workgroup shall report its findings and
recommendations to the Governor and, in accordance with Section 2–1257 of the State
Government Article, the General Assembly.

Section 11:
SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
June 1, 2026. It shall remain effective for a period of 1 year and 1 month and, at the end of
June 30, 2027, this Act, with no further action required by the General Assembly, shall be
abrogated and of no further force and effect.
Approved by the Governor, May 12, 2026.

(4) analyze alternative options for review and approval by the Board of
Public Works for the award of new contracts and change orders for road, bridge, or highway
projects that include:
(i) advance notice and opportunity to review and comment on
upcoming contracts conducted by the Department of Transportation or the Maryland
Transportation Authority, and the appropriate stage of the contract for these activities;
(ii) public reporting of contract and change order awards made by the
Department of Transportation or the Maryland Transportation Authority; or
(iii) appropriate thresholds for new contracts and contract change
orders or modifications related to capital expenditures by the Department of Transportation
and the Maryland Transportation Authority in connection with State roads, bridges, or
highways;
(5) examine the impact on contract award and completion timeline;
(6) evaluate the effect of expanding the current delegated authority for
capital expenditures by the Department of Transportation and the Maryland Transportation
Authority in connection with State roads, bridges, or highways to contracts and change
orders for maintenance and capital construction to support service on Maryland Transit
Administration fixed guideway systems and associated transit facilities and vehicles; and
(7) evaluate the impact of the existing approval authority structure and any
alternative approval authority structures on the ability to secure federal funding for roads,
bridges, or highways.
(g) On or before December 1, 2026, the Workgroup shall report its findings and
recommendations to the Governor and, in accordance with Section 2–1257 of the State
Government Article, the General Assembly.
SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
June 1, 2026. It shall remain effective for a period of 1 year and 1 month and, at the end of
June 30, 2027, this Act, with no further action required by the General Assembly, shall be
abrogated and of no further force and effect.
Approved by the Governor, May 12, 2026.
