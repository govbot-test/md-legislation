2 Procurement – Department of Transportation and Maryland Transportation
3 Authority Contracts – Board of Public Works Contract Authority
4 FOR the purpose of authorizing the Board of Public Works to control certain procurement
5 contracts by the Department of Transportation and the Maryland Transportation
6 Authority valued at or above a certain amount; requiring the Board to adopt
7 regulations for the review of a contract modification for certain procurement
8 contracts by the Department or the Authority; and generally relating to procurement
9 contracts and the Department of Transportation and the Maryland Transportation
10 Authority.

5 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
6 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
7 Article – State Finance and Procurement
8 4–415.
9 (a) This section does not apply to any capital expenditures by the Department of
10 Transportation or the Maryland Transportation Authority in connection with State roads,
11 bridges, or highways VALUED BELOW $500,000 $5,000,000.
12 (b) Before execution, a contract for the acquisition of land shall be:
13 (1) reviewed by the Secretary of General Services; and
14 (2) except as provided in § 12–204 of this article, after that review,
15 approved by the Board of Public Works.
16 (c) (1) This subsection does not apply to any capital expenditure VALUED
17 BELOW $500,000 $5,000,000 by:
18 (i) the Maryland Aviation Administration;
19 (ii) the Maryland Port Administration; or
20 (iii) the Maryland Transit Administration.
21 (2) At least 40 days before the Board of Public Works may act on a land
22 acquisition, the Division shall give written notice of a potential acquisition of land:
23 (i) to the governing body of the county in which the land is located;
24 and
25 (ii) if the land is located within a municipal corporation, to the
26 governing body of the municipal corporation.
27 (3) Within 30 days after receiving notice under this subsection, the
28 governing body may submit written comments to the Division.
HOUSE BILL 587 3
1 (4) THE BOARD OF PUBLIC WORKS SHALL ADOPT REGULATIONS FOR
2 THE REVIEW OF A CONTRACT MODIFICATION VALUED ABOVE $1,000,000 FOR A
3 CAPITAL EXPENDITURE IDENTIFIED IN PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION.
4 (d) The Board of Public Works shall supervise the expenditure of any money that
5 the General Assembly appropriates for the acquisition of land.
6 10–202.
7 (a) This section does not apply to property to be acquired in connection with State
8 roads, bridges, or highways VALUED BELOW $500,000 $5,000,000.
9 (b) Subject to Title 12 of the Real Property Article, the Board may condemn
10 property for State use.
11 (c) The Department of General Services, with the approval of the Board, shall
12 adopt regulations in accordance with Title 10, Subtitle 1 of the State Government Article
13 that establish a uniform method of determining the value of any applicable life estate
14 discount for property condemned in accordance with this section.
15 (D) THE BOARD OF PUBLIC WORKS SHALL ADOPT REGULATIONS FOR THE
16 REVIEW OF A CONTRACT MODIFICATION VALUED ABOVE $1,000,000 FOR AN
17 ACQUISITION IDENTIFIED IN SUBSECTION (A) OF THIS SECTION.
18 12–101.
19 (a) This section does not apply to:
20 (1) capital expenditures by the Department of Transportation or the
21 Maryland Transportation Authority, in connection with State roads, bridges, or highways,
22 as provided in § 12–202 of this title, VALUED BELOW $500,000 $5,000,000; or
23 (2) procurements by the Department of General Services for the purpose of
24 modernizing cybersecurity infrastructure for the State valued below $1,000,000.
25 (b) (1) The Board may control procurement by units.
26 (2) To implement the provisions of this Division II, the Board may:
27 (i) set policy;
28 (ii) adopt regulations, in accordance with Title 10, Subtitle 1 of the
29 State Government Article; and
30 (iii) establish internal operational procedures consistent with this
31 Division II.
4 HOUSE BILL 587
1 (3) The Board shall ensure that the regulations of the primary
2 procurement units provide for procedures that are consistent with this Division II and Title
3 13, Subtitle 4 of the State Personnel and Pensions Article and, to the extent the
4 circumstances of a particular type of procurement or a particular unit do not require
5 otherwise, are substantially the same.
6 (4) The Board may delegate any of its authority that it determines to be
7 appropriate for delegation and may require prior Board approval for specified procurement
8 actions.
9 (5) Except as limited by the Maryland Constitution, the Board may
10 exercise any control authority conferred on a primary procurement unit by this Division II
11 and, to the extent that its action conflicts with the action of the primary procurement unit,
12 the action of the Board shall prevail.
13 (E) THE BOARD OF PUBLIC WORKS SHALL ADOPT REGULATIONS FOR THE
14 REVIEW OF A CONTRACT MODIFICATION VALUED ABOVE $1,000,000 FOR A CAPITAL
15 EXPENDITURE IDENTIFIED IN SUBSECTION (A)(1) OF THIS SECTION.
16 12–103.
17 (a) This section does not apply to capital expenditures by the Department of
18 Transportation or the Maryland Transportation Authority, in connection with State roads,
19 bridges, or highways, as provided in § 12–202 of this title, VALUED BELOW $500,000
20 $5,000,000.
21 (b) Notwithstanding any delegation of authority under § 12–101 of this subtitle,
22 the Board retains full authority to engage in procurement of services and supplies for itself.
23 (c) By resolution, the Board may authorize a Board member or a unit to exercise
24 all or any part of the authority of the Board for a specific Board procurement or for a
25 particular class of Board procurements.
26 (D) THE BOARD OF PUBLIC WORKS SHALL ADOPT REGULATIONS FOR THE
27 REVIEW OF A CONTRACT MODIFICATION VALUED ABOVE $1,000,000 FOR A CAPITAL
28 EXPENDITURE IDENTIFIED IN SUBSECTION (A) OF THIS SECTION.
29 12–107.
30 (a) This section does not apply to capital expenditures by the Department of
31 Transportation or the Maryland Transportation Authority, in connection with State roads,
32 bridges, or highways, as provided in § 12–202 of this title, VALUED BELOW $500,000
33 $5,000,000.
