2 HOUSE BILL 1151
1 (a) The Division of Health Care Financing and Medicaid within the Maryland
2 Department of Health Maryland Health Care Commission and the Maryland Medical
3 Assistance Program jointly shall study the impact of requiring the Maryland Medical
4 Assistance Program to provide reimbursement for room and board services provided by a
5 hospice house, as defined in COMAR 10.07.22.02.
6 (b) The study required under subsection (a) of this section shall:
7 (1) quantify analyze overall Maryland Medical Assistance Program
8 savings per enrollee death as a result of the use of hospice care; and associated with hospice
9 care utilization during the last 6 months of life compared with costs associated with other
10 services provided to Program enrollees during the last 6 months of life, including the impact
11 on:
12 (i) avoided inpatient hospital stays;
13 (ii) emergency department utilization; and
14 (iii) intensive treatments near the end of life;
15 (2) analyze the projected fiscal impact of the provision of reimbursement
16 for hospice house room and board services, including any potential offsets or net savings;
17 (3) examine how the provision of reimbursement for room and board
18 services would:
19 (i) affect hospice hospital readmission rates;
20 (ii) improve hospice length of stay;
21 (iii) improve early access to hospice services;
22 (iv) result in a reduction in deaths in hospitals; and
23 (iv) (v) result in a reduction in emergency department utilization; and
24 (4) include a comparison of other states that provide reimbursement for
25 hospice house room and board service, including a comparison of:
26 (i) payment models;
27 (ii) utilization outcomes; and
28 (iii) cost impacts.
