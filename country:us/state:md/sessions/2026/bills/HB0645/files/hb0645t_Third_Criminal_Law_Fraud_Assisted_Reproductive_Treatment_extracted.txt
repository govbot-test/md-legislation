CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Criminal Law – Fraud – Assisted Reproductive Treatment
3 FOR the purpose of prohibiting a person from intentionally or knowingly providing certain
4 assisted reproductive treatment to another using the person’s sperm or ovum
5 without the other’s written consent to assisted reproductive treatment using the
6 person’s sperm or ovum; establishing a civil cause of action for a violation of this Act;
7 and generally relating to fraud.
8 BY adding to
9 Article – Criminal Law
10 Section 8–906
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
13 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
14 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
15 Article – Criminal Law
16 8–906.
17 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION, “ASSISTED REPRODUCTIVE TREATMENT”
18 MEANS A METHOD OF CAUSING PREGNANCY OTHER THAN THROUGH SEXUAL
19 INTERCOURSE.

2 HOUSE BILL 645
1 (2) “ASSISTED REPRODUCTIVE TREATMENT” INCLUDES:
2 (I) DONATION OF EGGS OR SPERM;
3 (II) DONATION OF EMBRYOS;
4 (III) INTRACYTOPLASMIC SPERM INJECTION;
5 (IV) INTRAUTERINE OR INTRACERVICAL INSEMINATION; AND
6 (V) IN VITRO FERTILIZATION AND EMBRYO TRANSFER.
7 (B) (1) A PERSON MAY NOT INTENTIONALLY OR KNOWINGLY PROVIDE
8 ASSISTED REPRODUCTIVE TREATMENT TO ANOTHER USING THE PERSON’S SPERM
9 OR OVUM WITHOUT THE OTHER’S WRITTEN CONSENT TO ASSISTED REPRODUCTIVE
10 TREATMENT USING THE PERSON’S SPERM OR OVUM.
11 (2) A PERSON WHO VIOLATES PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION
12 IS GUILTY OF A FELONY AND ON CONVICTION IS SUBJECT TO IMPRISONMENT NOT
13 EXCEEDING 10 YEARS OR A FINE NOT EXCEEDING $5,000 OR BOTH.
14 (C) (1) A PERSON WHO VIOLATES SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION IS
15 LIABLE FOR CIVIL DAMAGES IN AN ACTION BROUGHT BY:
16 (I) THE WOMAN WHO GIVES BIRTH TO A CHILD AFTER
17 RECEIVING THE ASSISTED REPRODUCTIVE TREATMENT;
18 (II) THE SPOUSE OR DOMESTIC PARTNER OF THE WOMAN WHO
19 GIVES BIRTH TO A CHILD AFTER RECEIVING THE ASSISTED REPRODUCTIVE
20 TREATMENT; OR
21 (III) THE CHILD BORN AS A RESULT OF THE ASSISTED
22 REPRODUCTIVE TREATMENT.
23 (2) A CHILD BORN AS A RESULT OF ASSISTED REPRODUCTIVE
24 TREATMENT PROVIDED BY A PERSON IN VIOLATION OF SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS
25 SECTION IS ENTITLED TO A QUALIFIED PROTECTIVE ORDER ALLOWING THE CHILD
26 ACCESS TO THE PERSONAL MEDICAL RECORDS AND HEALTH HISTORY OF THE
27 PERSON.
28 (3) A CIVIL ACTION UNDER THIS SECTION SHALL BE FILED:

HOUSE BILL 645 3
1 (I) WITHIN 10 YEARS AFTER THE CHILD BORN AS A RESULT OF
2 THE ASSISTED REPRODUCTIVE TREATMENT REACHES THE AGE OF 18 YEARS;
3 (II) WITHIN 20 YEARS AFTER THE DAY ON WHICH THE ASSISTED
4 REPRODUCTIVE TREATMENT WAS PROVIDED; OR
5 (III) WITHIN 2 YEARS AFTER THE DAY ON WHICH AN INDIVIDUAL
6 FIRST DISCOVERS SUFFICIENT EVIDENCE THROUGH DNA ANALYSIS THAT THE
7 INDIVIDUAL HAS A CAUSE OF ACTION AGAINST A PERSON UNDER THIS SECTION.
8 (4) A PLAINTIFF WHO PREVAILS IN AN ACTION BROUGHT UNDER THIS
9 SECTION IS ENTITLED TO:
10 (I) THE COSTS OF THE ASSISTED REPRODUCTIVE TREATMENT;
11 (II) LIQUIDATED DAMAGES NOT TO EXCEED $50,000;
12 (III) ECONOMIC AND NONECONOMIC COMPENSATORY DAMAGES;
13 AND
14 (IV) (III) REASONABLE ATTORNEY’S FEES AND COSTS.
15 (D) THIS SECTION MAY NOT BE CONSTRUED TO PROHIBIT A PERSON FROM
16 PURSUING ANY OTHER REMEDY PROVIDED BY LAW.
17 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
18 October 1, 2026.
