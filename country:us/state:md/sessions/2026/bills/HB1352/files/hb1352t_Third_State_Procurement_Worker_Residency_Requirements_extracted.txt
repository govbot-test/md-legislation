2 State Procurement – Worker Residency Requirements
3 FOR the purpose of requiring certain contractors to ensure that a certain percentage of
4 work hours on certain projects are performed by State residents; requiring the
5 Department of Labor to enforce certain worker residency requirements; providing
6 that a person may be debarred from entering into a contract with the State for a
7 certain period of time under certain circumstances; and generally relating to State
8 procurement and worker residency requirements.

18 SUBTITLE 6B. WORKER RESIDENCY REQUIREMENTS.
19 17–6B–01.
20 (A) IN THIS SUBTITLE THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
21 INDICATED.
22 (B) “APPRENTICESHIP HOUR” MEANS 1 HOUR OF TIME SPENT IN AN
23 APPRENTICESHIP PROGRAM AND MAY INCLUDE OBSERVATION, PERFORMING WORK,
24 OR WORK–RELATED DISCUSSION.
25 (C) “COVERED LARGE PROJECT” MEANS A PUBLIC WORK CONTRACT
26 SUBJECT TO SUBTITLE 2 OF THIS TITLE THAT IS VALUED AT $5,000,000 OR MORE.
27 (D) “DEPARTMENT” MEANS THE MARYLAND DEPARTMENT OF LABOR.
28 (E) “REGISTERED APPRENTICESHIP PROGRAM” MEANS AN
29 APPRENTICESHIP PROGRAM REGISTERED WITH THE DIVISION OF WORKFORCE
30 DEVELOPMENT AND ADULT LEARNING WITHIN THE DEPARTMENT.

21 SUBJECT TO THIS SUBTITLE SHALL PROVIDE TO THE UNIT AND THE DEPARTMENT A
22 STATEMENT ATTESTING THAT THE CONTRACTOR SHALL MEET THE REQUIREMENTS
23 OF THIS SECTION FOR THE APPLICABLE PROJECT.
