A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Consumer Protection – Artificial Intelligence Toys
3 (Artificial Intelligence Toy Safety Act)
4 FOR the purpose of establishing certain child safety and data privacy requirements for
5 certain toys featuring artificial intelligence; requiring an artificial intelligence toy to
6 contain certain labeling; prohibiting the manufacturer of an artificial intelligence toy
7 from using certain data for certain marketing; establishing the Artificial Intelligence
8 Toy Safety Review Panel in the Consumer Protection Division of the Office of the
9 Attorney General to monitor manufacturer compliance with this Act and conduct
10 independent audits and safety assessments relating to artificial intelligence toys;
11 establishing that a violation of this Act is an unfair, abusive, or deceptive trade
12 practice subject to certain enforcement and penalty provisions under the Maryland
13 Consumer Protection Act; and generally relating to artificial intelligence toys.
14 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
15 Article – Commercial Law
16 Section 13–301(14)(xlvii)
17 Annotated Code of Maryland
18 (2025 Replacement Volume)
19 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
20 Article – Commercial Law
21 Section 13–301(14)(xlviii)
22 Annotated Code of Maryland
23 (2025 Replacement Volume)
24 BY adding to
25 Article – Commercial Law
26 Section 13–301(14)(xlix); and 14–5101 through 14–5108 to be under the new subtitle
27 “Subtitle 51. Artificial Intelligence Toy Safety Act”

16 (B) “ADAPTIVE CONTENT” MEANS CONTENT OR RESPONSES GENERATED OR
17 MODIFIED USING ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE THAT CHANGE BASED ON USER INPUT,

19 (C) “ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE TOY” MEANS ANY TOY, DEVICE, OR
20 PRODUCT SOLD TO A CONSUMER IN THE STATE THAT:
21 (1) EMPLOYS ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE, MACHINE LEARNING,
22 ADAPTIVE ALGORITHMS, CONVERSATIONAL ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE,
23 BEHAVIORAL MODELING, OR SIMILAR COMPUTATIONAL PROCESSES; AND
24 (2) IS MARKETED AND SOLD TO, OR PRIMARILY USED BY, A CHILD.
25 (D) (1) “CHILD USER DATA” MEANS INFORMATION COLLECTED OR
26 INFERRED FROM A CHILD’S INTERACTION WITH AN ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE TOY.
27 (2) “CHILD USER DATA” INCLUDES AUDIO, VIDEO, GEOLOCATION,
28 BIOMETRIC DATA, TEXT, BEHAVIORAL PATTERNS, AND RECORDED SPEECH.

17 (6) THE CAPABILITY TO SECURELY UPDATE SOFTWARE OR
18 FIRMWARE TO ADDRESS VULNERABILITIES OR IMPROVE SAFETY.

4 CONTINUING TO BE MARKETED OR SOLD IN THE STATE; AND

8 ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE TOY SOLD OR MARKETED IN THE STATE ON OR BEFORE

10 14–5104.
11 (A) (1) SUBJECT TO SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION, A
12 MANUFACTURER MAY ONLY COLLECT THE MINIMUM CHILD USER DATA NECESSARY
13 FOR THE OPERATION OF THE CORE FUNCTIONALITY OF AN ARTIFICIAL
14 INTELLIGENCE TOY.
15 (2) CHILD USER DATA COLLECTED BY A MANUFACTURER SHALL BE
16 ENCRYPTED.
17 (B) AN ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE TOY MANUFACTURER MAY NOT:
18 (1) SELL, LEASE, OR OTHERWISE TRANSFER CHILD USER DATA TO A
19 THIRD PARTY;
20 (2) USE CHILD USER DATA TO TRAIN UNRELATED ARTIFICIAL
21 INTELLIGENCE MODELS OR SYSTEMS;
22 (3) USE CHILD USER DATA FOR TARGETED ADVERTISING; OR
23 (4) RETAIN CHILD USER DATA FOR MORE THAN 12 MONTHS UNLESS
24 THE MANUFACTURER OBTAINS RENEWED PARENTAL CONSENT.
25 (C) A MANUFACTURER SHALL ENSURE THAT THE PARENT OR LEGAL
26 GUARDIAN OF A CHILD USER OF AN ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE TOY HAS THE ABILITY
27 TO:
28 (1) EASILY ACCESS, REVIEW, DOWNLOAD, AND DELETE ALL CHILD
29 USER DATA COLLECTED BY AN ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE TOY; AND
6 HOUSE BILL 1261
1 (2) EASILY DISABLE CHILD USER DATA COLLECTION WITHOUT
2 DISABLING THE CORE FUNCTIONALITY OF THE ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE TOY.
3 (D) IF A DATA BREACH OR SUSPECTED DATA BREACH INVOLVING CHILD
4 USER DATA OCCURS, A MANUFACTURER SHALL NOTIFY EACH READILY
5 IDENTIFIABLE PARENT OR GUARDIAN OF A CHILD ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE TOY
6 USER WITHIN 48 HOURS AFTER THE MANUFACTURER DISCOVERING THE DATA
7 BREACH OR SUSPECTED DATA BREACH.
8 14–5105.
9 (A) AN ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE TOY SOLD OR DISTRIBUTED IN THE
10 STATE SHALL INCLUDE A CLEAR AND CONSPICUOUS LABEL:
11 (1) STATING THAT THE PRODUCT USES ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE;
12 AND
13 (2) DIRECTLY OR THROUGH THE USE OF A QUICK RESPONSE CODE:
14 (I) IDENTIFYING ANY TYPE OF DATA COLLECTED BY THE
15 ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE TOY; AND
16 (II) IF INTERACTIONS WITH THE ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE
17 TOY ARE STORED, TRANSMITTED, OR USED TO GENERATE ADAPTIVE CONTENT,
18 PROVIDING INFORMATION ON HOW THE INTERACTIONS ARE STORED,
19 TRANSMITTED, OR USED.
20 (B) A MANUFACTURER THAT PRODUCES, DISTRIBUTES, IMPORTS, OR SELLS
21 AN ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE TOY IN THE STATE SHALL MAKE PUBLICLY
22 AVAILABLE AN EASILY ACCESSIBLE ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SAFETY SUMMARY
23 REPORT USING PLAIN, EASILY UNDERSTANDABLE LANGUAGE IDENTIFYING:
24 (1) THE AGE RANGE THAT THE ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE TOY IS
25 INTENDED FOR;
26 (2) RISKS IDENTIFIED IN THE USE OF THE ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE
27 SAFETY ASSESSMENT COMPLETED UNDER § 14–5103 OF THIS SUBTITLE;
28 (3) THE RESULTS OF ANY TESTING OF THE ARTIFICIAL
29 INTELLIGENCE TOY; AND
30 (4) DIRECTIONS THAT THE PARENT OR GUARDIAN OF A CHILD USER
31 MAY USE TO CONTROL OR LIMIT DATA COLLECTION OR ADAPTIVE BEHAVIORS.

28 MANUFACTURER COMPLIANCE, THE RESULTS OF INDEPENDENT AUDITS, AND
29 POTENTIAL STATUTORY CHANGES.
