1 AN ACT concerning
2 Mental Health Law – Definition of Danger to the Life or Safety of the Individual
3 or of Others – Definition and Reports on Emergency Evaluation Petitions –
4 Definition
5 (Right to Treatment)
6 FOR the purpose of defining “danger to the life or safety of the individual or of others” for
7 purposes of the involuntary admission of individuals with mental disorders to a
8 facility or Veterans’ Administration hospital and the emergency evaluation of
9 individuals with mental disorders; requiring the Behavioral Health Administration
10 to submit to the General Assembly certain reports regarding the use of petitions for
11 emergency evaluation; and generally relating to involuntary admissions and
12 emergency evaluations of individuals with mental disorders.
13 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,

5 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
6 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
7 Article – Health – General
8 10–601.
9 (a) In this subtitle the following words have the meanings indicated.
10 (b) “Clinical social worker” means an individual who is licensed under Title 19 of
11 the Health Occupations Article to practice clinical social work.
12 (C) “DANGER TO THE LIFE OR SAFETY OF THE INDIVIDUAL OR OF OTHERS”
13 MEANS THERE IS, AS A RESULT OF A MENTAL DISORDER, A SUBSTANTIAL RISK,
14 WHETHER OR NOT THE RISK IS IMMINENT BASED ON CONDUCT THAT IS RECENT AND
15 RELEVANT TO THE DANGER THAT THE INDIVIDUAL MAY PRESENT AND IN
16 CONSIDERATION OF THE INDIVIDUAL’S CURRENT CONDITION AND, IF AVAILABLE,
17 RECENT PERSONAL, MEDICAL, AND PSYCHIATRIC HISTORY, THAT AS A RESULT OF
18 THE MENTAL DISORDER THE INDIVIDUAL WILL:
19 (1) CAUSE SERIOUS BODILY HARM TO THE INDIVIDUAL OR ANOTHER
20 INDIVIDUAL; OR
21 (2) ENGAGE IN CONDUCT THAT WILL RESULT IN CRIMINAL JUSTICE
22 INVOLVEMENT;
23 (3) (2) BE UNABLE, EXCEPT FOR REASONS OF INDIGENCE, TO
24 PROVIDE FOR THE INDIVIDUAL’S BASIC NEEDS, INCLUDING FOOD, CLOTHING,
25 SHELTER, MEDICAL CARE, SELF–PROTECTION, OR SAFETY, TO SUCH A DEGREE AS
26 TO CREATE A SUBSTANTIAL RISK OF SERIOUS BODILY HARM, SERIOUS ILLNESS, OR
27 DEATH IN THE NEAR FUTURE.; OR
28 (4) SUFFER SUBSTANTIAL DETERIORATION OF THE INDIVIDUAL’S
29 JUDGMENT, REASONING, OR ABILITY TO CONTROL BEHAVIOR, PROVIDED THAT THE
30 INDIVIDUAL IS CURRENTLY SUBSTANTIALLY IMPAIRED IN THE INDIVIDUAL’S
31 ABILITY TO MAKE A RATIONAL AND INFORMED DECISION AS TO WHETHER TO
32 SUBMIT TO TREATMENT, THAT WILL LIKELY RESULT IN THE INDIVIDUAL MEETING
33 ONE OF THE CRITERIA ENUMERATED UNDER THIS SUBSECTION.
