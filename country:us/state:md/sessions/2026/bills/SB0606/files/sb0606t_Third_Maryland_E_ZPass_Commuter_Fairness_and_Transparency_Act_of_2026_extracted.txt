1 AN ACT concerning
2 Maryland E–ZPass Commuter Fairness and Transparency Act of 2026
3 FOR the purpose of requiring the Maryland Transportation Authority to submit a certain
4 report on E–ZPass discount plans and unused commuter trips that contains certain
5 data; requiring the Authority to provide recommendations for lowering the amount
6 of unused commuter trips; requiring the Authority, under certain circumstances, to
7 submit a certain corrective action plan or eliminate certain forfeitures; and generally
8 relating to unused commuter trips and the Maryland Transportation Authority.
9 Preamble
10 WHEREAS, The Maryland Transportation Authority operates the E–ZPass, an
11 all–electronic tolling system on Maryland toll facilities, which is used daily by hundreds of
12 thousands of Maryland residents, commuters, and visitors; and
13 WHEREAS, Under the current system, a missed toll payment as low as $4 can result
14 in the assessment of administrative fees and penalties that represent a disproportionate
15 percentage increase over the original toll amount, creating significant financial burdens for
16 motorists; and
17 WHEREAS, These fees and penalties often accumulate rapidly, particularly for
18 individuals who may be unaware of an unpaid toll due to address changes, mail delivery
19 issues, or delays in notification; and
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.

2 SENATE BILL 606
1 WHEREAS, Maryland’s transition to all–electronic tolling, with the elimination of
2 cash payment options, has removed a critical access point for individuals who are unbanked
3 or underbanked, including residents of communities that experience banking deserts and
4 limited access to traditional financial services; and
5 WHEREAS, The all–electronic tolling system effectively requires access to bank
6 accounts, credit cards, or online payment platforms, which may not be readily available to
7 all Marylanders, thereby creating inequitable barriers to compliance; and
8 WHEREAS, The E–ZPass system charges consumers for unused or expired trips
9 when toll transactions are not utilized within a shortened, State–imposed time frame,
10 resulting in the loss of prepaid funds without corresponding transportation services; and
11 WHEREAS, Maryland has shortened the allowable time frame in which prepaid toll
12 trips may be used, which has the effect of depriving consumers of funds already paid and
13 disproportionately impacting working families, seniors, and individuals with irregular
14 travel patterns; and
15 WHEREAS, The cumulative effect of penalty structures, shortened usage windows,
16 and all–electronic payment systems has resulted in consumers being systematically
17 charged fees that exceed the original cost of the toll, rather than being encouraged or
18 supported in timely compliance; and
19 WHEREAS, These practices raise concerns regarding fairness, transparency,
20 proportionality, and consumer protection, particularly for low–income motorists and
21 residents of communities with limited access to banking and digital infrastructure; and
22 WHEREAS, The General Assembly has an interest in ensuring that tolling and
23 transportation revenue systems operate in a manner that is equitable, transparent,
24 consumer–focused, and proportionate, while still meeting the operational needs of the
25 State; now, therefore,
26 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
27 That:
28 (a) On or before September 1, 2026, the Maryland Transportation Authority shall
29 submit a report to the Senate Budget and Taxation Committee and the House
30 Appropriations Committee, in accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government Article.
31 (b) The report required under subsection (a) of this section shall:
32 (1) analyze data from calendar years 2023, 2024, and 2025 and provide:
33 (i) the number of E–ZPass discount plans issued annually;
34 (ii) the number and percentage of discount plans with unused
35 commuter trips;
SENATE BILL 606 3
1 (iii) the average number of unused commuter trips per plan;
2 (iv) the aggregate dollar value of unused commuter trips by year;
3 (v) any available demographic, geographic, and usage trends; and
4 (vi) any administrative, operational, and statutory factors that
5 contribute to increased unused commuter trips;
6 (2) include recommendations for lowering the amount of unused commuter
7 trips, which may include:
8 (i) extending the allowable usage window for prepaid trips on
9 discount plans;
10 (ii) allowing rollovers or credits for unused trips;
11 (iii) refund or partial refund mechanisms;
12 (iv) automatic transfer of unused trips to account balances; and
13 (v) alternative discount plan structures that reduce forfeiture of
14 prepaid funds; and
15 (3) if during 2023, 2024, or 2025 more than 25% of all E–ZPass discount
16 plans have unused commuter trips, include a corrective action plan to reduce or eliminate
17 the forfeiture of unused commuter trips and mitigate consumer harm.
18 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
19 1, 2026. It shall remain effective for a period of 1 year and, at the end of June 30, 2027, this
20 Act, with no further action required by the General Assembly, shall be abrogated and of no
21 further force and effect.
