1 AN ACT concerning
2 Maryland Automobile Insurance Fund – Affordability – Purpose of Fund and
3 Authorized Program Program and Industry Automobile Insurance Association
4 Assessments
5 FOR the purpose of altering the purpose of the Maryland Automobile Insurance Fund to
6 specify that the financial security provided by the Fund be provided at rates that are
7 affordable; altering the cap on a certain assessment allocation percentage required
8 to be calculated by the Board of Directors of the Industry Automobile Insurance
9 Association; authorizing the Executive Director of the Maryland Automobile
10 Insurance Fund to establish a certain affordability program subject to certain
11 requirements and modification under certain circumstances; and generally relating
12 to the Maryland Automobile Insurance Fund.
13 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
14 Article – Insurance

4 20–521.
5 (A) IN THIS SECTION, “AFFORDABILITY PROGRAM” MEANS A PROGRAM
6 THAT LIMITS THE MAXIMUM PREMIUM RATE FOR A POLICY OF PRIVATE PASSENGER
7 AUTO INSURANCE ISSUED BY THE FUND IN AN AMOUNT THAT MAY BE INADEQUATE.
8 (B) SUBJECT TO REVIEW AND APPROVAL BY THE COMMISSIONER, THE
9 EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR MAY ESTABLISH AN AFFORDABILITY PROGRAM IF:
10 (1) RATES ARE NOT EXCESSIVE OR UNFAIRLY DISCRIMINATORY; AND
11 (2) THE AFFORDABILITY PROGRAM DOES NOT RESULT IN:
12 (I) AN OVERALL INADEQUACY GREATER THAN 20% OF THE
13 FUND’S NET WRITTEN PREMIUM; OR
14 (II) AN ASSESSMENT ALLOCATION PERCENTAGE THAT EXCEEDS
15 THE LIMIT ESTABLISHED IN § 20–405(D) OF THIS TITLE.
16 (C) AN INDIVIDUAL IS ELIGIBLE FOR A POLICY THROUGH THE
17 AFFORDABILITY PROGRAM IF THE INDIVIDUAL:
18 (1) MEETS THE ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS FOR POLICYHOLDERS
19 UNDER § 20–502 OF THIS SUBTITLE; AND
20 (2) HAS A HOUSEHOLD INCOME THAT IS NOT MORE THAN 250% OF
21 THE FEDERAL POVERTY LEVEL.
22 (D) THE COMMISSIONER MAY REQUIRE THE FUND TO MODIFY THE
23 AFFORDABILITY PROGRAM IF THE COMMISSIONER DETERMINES THAT:
24 (1) THE RATES OFFERED THROUGH THE AFFORDABILITY PROGRAM
25 DO NOT MEET THE REQUIREMENTS OF THIS SECTION;
26 (2) THE AFFORDABILITY PROGRAM WILL NO LONGER MEET THE
27 CONDITION ESTABLISHED UNDER SUBSECTION (B)(2)(I) OR (II) OF THIS SECTION;
28 OR
29 (3) THE AFFORDABILITY PROGRAM VIOLATES OTHER PROVISIONS OF
30 THIS ARTICLE TO WHICH THE FUND IS SUBJECT.
