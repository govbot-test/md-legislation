CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Employment Standards – Firefighters – Payment of Wages and Payroll
3 Information
4 FOR the purpose of requiring governmental units that employ firefighters to compute
5 overtime pay in a certain manner; requiring counties and municipalities to provide
6 certain payroll information to each firefighter employed by the county or
7 municipality; authorizing a firefighter or the firefighter’s exclusive representative to
8 initiate a certain grievance if a county or municipality does not provide the payroll
9 information as required or wages due; and generally relating to wages and payroll
10 information for firefighters.

1 Article – Labor and Employment
2 3–420.
3 (A) IN THIS SECTION, “EMPLOYER” INCLUDES A GOVERNMENTAL UNIT
4 THAT EMPLOYS A FIREFIGHTER.
5 [(a)] (B) Except as otherwise provided in this section, an employer shall compute
6 the wage for overtime under § 3–415 of this subtitle on the basis of each hour over 40 hours
7 that an employee works during 1 workweek.
8 [(b)] (C) Notwithstanding § 3–415(b)(2) of this subtitle, an employer that is not
9 a nonprofit organization and is a concert promoter, legitimate theater, music festival, music
10 pavilion, or theatrical show shall pay overtime for a craft or trade employee as required in
11 subsection [(a)] (B) of this section.
12 [(c)] (D) The wage for overtime may be computed on the basis of each hour over
13 60 hours that an employee works during 1 workweek for an employee who:
14 (1) is engaged in agriculture; and
15 (2) is exempt from the overtime provisions of the federal Act.
16 [(d)] (E) The wage for overtime may be computed on the basis of each hour over
17 48 hours that an employee works during 1 workweek:
18 (1) for an employee of a bowling establishment; and
19 (2) for an employee of an institution that:
20 (i) is not a hospital; but
21 (ii) is engaged primarily in the care of individuals who:
22 1. are aged, intellectually disabled, or sick or have a mental
23 disorder; and
24 2. reside at the institution.
25 (F) (1) THE WAGE FOR OVERTIME FOR A FIREFIGHTER EMPLOYED BY A
26 GOVERNMENTAL UNIT SHALL BE COMPUTED ON THE BASIS OF EACH HOUR OVER 168
27 HOURS THAT THE FIREFIGHTER WORKS DURING A 28–DAY WORK PERIOD.
28 (2) IN CALCULATING HOURS WORKED UNDER PARAGRAPH (1) OF
29 THIS SUBSECTION, THE EMPLOYER:

13 Article – Local Government
14 1–210.
15 (A) EACH COUNTY AND MUNICIPALITY SHALL PROVIDE FOR EACH
16 FIREFIGHTER EMPLOYED BY THE COUNTY OR MUNICIPALITY:
17 (1) AT THE TIME OF HIRING AND AT THE TIME OF ANY CHANGE OF THE
18 FIREFIGHTER’S RATE OF PAY, NOTICE OF:
19 (I) THE FIREFIGHTER’S RATE OF PAY;
20 (II) THE REGULAR PAY PERIODS; AND
21 (III) THE FIREFIGHTER’S OVERTIME RATE OF PAY; AND
22 (2) FOR EACH PAY PERIOD, A STATEMENT OF THE HOURS WORKED,
23 GROSS EARNINGS, AND ANY DEDUCTIONS FROM THE GROSS EARNINGS.
24 (B) IF A COUNTY OR MUNICIPALITY DOES NOT PROVIDE PAYROLL
25 INFORMATION IN ACCORDANCE WITH SUBSECTION (A) OF THIS SECTION OR WAGES
26 DUE, THE FIREFIGHTER OR THE FIREFIGHTER’S EXCLUSIVE REPRESENTATIVE MAY
27 INITIATE A GRIEVANCE UNDER THE GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE ESTABLISHED UNDER
28 ANY APPLICABLE GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE OR COLLECTIVE BARGAINING
29 AGREEMENT.
