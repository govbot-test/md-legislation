1 AN ACT concerning
2 Department of Aging – Aging Resilience Fund – Establishment
3 FOR the purpose of establishing the Aging Resilience Fund as a special, nonlapsing fund;
4 providing for the purposes of the Fund; requiring interest earnings of the Fund to be
5 credited to the Fund; requiring the Secretary of Aging to administer the Fund; and
6 generally relating to the Aging Resilience Fund.

13 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
14 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
15 Article – Human Services
16 10–101.
17 (a) In this title the following words have the meanings indicated.
18 (d) “Department” means the Department of Aging.
19 (f) “Secretary” means the Secretary of Aging.
20 10–204.
21 (a) The Secretary shall administer the programs and activities that the federal
22 government delegates to the State under the Older Americans Act of 1965 that are not
23 otherwise committed by law to another unit of State government.
24 (b) The Secretary:
25 (1) is a member of the Governor’s Executive Council; and
26 (2) shall cooperate with and receive the cooperation of State, federal, and
27 local governmental units to carry out the purposes of this title.
28 (c) The Secretary shall:
29 (1) evaluate the service needs of seniors in the State;
30 (2) determine the extent to which existing public and private programs
31 meet the needs of seniors;
HOUSE BILL 804 3
1 (3) establish priorities for meeting the needs of seniors;
2 (4) coordinate, subject to existing law, assess and evaluate, and educate
3 the public and professionals about all State and local programs and services, both public
4 and private, that relate and are important to the well–being of seniors in the State,
5 including programs and services in the areas of:
6 (i) income maintenance;
7 (ii) public health;
8 (iii) mental health;
9 (iv) housing and urban development;
10 (v) employment;
11 (vi) education;
12 (vii) recreation; and
13 (viii) rehabilitation of seniors with physical or mental disabilities; and
14 (5) develop a statewide plan incorporating local plans for a comprehensive
15 and coordinated system of health, social, and community services for seniors, including
16 housing and institutional and noninstitutional care.
17 (d) The Secretary shall:
18 (1) represent the interests of seniors by serving as an advocate at all levels
19 of government;
20 (2) consult with and advise the secretaries of the principal departments of
21 State government about the programs and services for seniors that are the primary
22 responsibility of those departments;
23 (3) consult with the Commission on Aging on all matters pertaining to
24 programs for seniors;
25 (4) provide consultation and technical assistance to communities and civic
26 groups developing local services for seniors;
27 (5) maintain a clearinghouse of information related to the interests of
28 seniors; and
4 HOUSE BILL 804
1 (6) review and recommend policies to the Governor on publicly funded
2 plans and programs that affect seniors.
3 (e) The Secretary may adopt regulations to carry out the provisions of law that
4 are within the jurisdiction of the Secretary.
5 (F) (1) IN THIS SUBSECTION, “FUND” MEANS THE AGING RESILIENCE
6 FUND.
7 (2) THERE IS AN AGING RESILIENCE FUND.
8 (3) THE PURPOSE OF THE FUND IS TO SUPPORT THE
9 IMPLEMENTATION OF THE DEPARTMENT’S MISSION UNDER THIS TITLE.
10 (4) THE SECRETARY SHALL ADMINISTER THE FUND.
11 (5) (I) THE FUND IS A SPECIAL, NONLAPSING FUND THAT IS NOT
12 SUBJECT TO § 7–302 OF THE STATE FINANCE AND PROCUREMENT ARTICLE.
13 (II) THE STATE TREASURER SHALL HOLD THE FUND
14 SEPARATELY, AND THE COMPTROLLER SHALL ACCOUNT FOR THE FUND.
15 (6) (I) THE FUND CONSISTS OF:
16 (I) 1. INVESTMENT EARNINGS OF THE FUND;
17 (II) 2. ANY FEES COLLECTED UNDER THIS SUBTITLE; AND
18 (III) 3. ANY OTHER MONEY FROM ANY OTHER SOURCE
19 ACCEPTED FOR THE BENEFIT OF THE FUND.
20 (II) MONEY RECEIVED BY THE DEPARTMENT FROM THE FUND
21 IN ACCORDANCE WITH THIS SECTION IS SUPPLEMENTAL TO AND IS NOT INTENDED
22 TO TAKE THE PLACE OF FUNDING THAT OTHERWISE WOULD BE APPROPRIATED IN
23 THE ANNUAL STATE OPERATING OR CAPITAL BUDGET BILL FOR PROJECTS OR
24 ACTIVITIES OF THE DEPARTMENT.
25 (7) THE FUND MAY BE USED FOR ADMINISTRATIVE EXPENSES THAT
26 ALIGN WITH THE DEPARTMENT’S MISSION UNDER THIS TITLE, INCLUDING:
27 (I) PERSONNEL;
28 (II) (I) PARTNERSHIP DEVELOPMENT; AND
HOUSE BILL 804 5
1 (III) (II) ANY PROGRAMS, SERVICES, OR ACTIVITIES WITHIN
2 THE DEPARTMENT.
3 (8) (I) THE STATE TREASURER SHALL INVEST THE MONEY OF THE
4 FUND IN THE SAME MANNER AS OTHER STATE MONEY MAY BE INVESTED.
5 (II) ANY INTEREST EARNINGS OF THE FUND SHALL BE
6 CREDITED TO THE FUND.
7 (9) EXPENDITURES FROM THE FUND MAY BE MADE ONLY IN
8 ACCORDANCE WITH THE STATE BUDGET.
9 (10) (I) NOTWITHSTANDING THE PROVISIONS OF § 7–213 OF THE
10 STATE FINANCE AND PROCUREMENT ARTICLE, THE GOVERNOR MAY NOT REDUCE
11 AN APPROPRIATION FOR THE FUND IN THE STATE BUDGET AS APPROVED BY THE
12 GENERAL ASSEMBLY.
13 (II) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN THIS SUBSECTION, MONEY
14 APPROPRIATED FOR THE FUND SHALL REMAIN IN THE FUND.
15 (11) ON OR BEFORE JANUARY 1 EACH YEAR, THE SECRETARY SHALL
16 REPORT TO THE SENATE FINANCE COMMITTEE AND THE HOUSE HEALTH
17 COMMITTEE, IN ACCORDANCE WITH § 2–1257 OF THE STATE GOVERNMENT
18 ARTICLE, ON THE ADMINISTRATION OF THE FUND FOR THE PREVIOUS FISCAL YEAR,
19 INCLUDING:
20 (I) THE TOTAL AMOUNT IN THE FUND;
21 (II) THE TOTAL NUMBER OF DEPOSITS MADE TO THE FUND,
22 INCLUDING AMOUNTS AND SOURCES; AND
23 (III) THE TOTAL NUMBER OF EXPENDITURES MADE FROM THE
24 FUND, INCLUDING AMOUNTS AND PURPOSES.
25 Article – State Finance and Procurement
26 6–226.
27 (a) (2) (i) This paragraph does not apply in fiscal years 2024 through 2028.
28 (ii) Notwithstanding any other provision of law, and unless
29 inconsistent with a federal law, grant agreement, or other federal requirement or with the
30 terms of a gift or settlement agreement, net interest on all State money allocated by the
31 State Treasurer under this section to special funds or accounts, and otherwise entitled to
6 HOUSE BILL 804
1 receive interest earnings, as accounted for by the Comptroller, shall accrue to the General
2 Fund of the State.
3 (iii) The provisions of subparagraph (ii) of this paragraph do not
4 apply to the following funds:
5 212. the Department of Social and Economic Mobility Special
6 Fund; [and]
7 213. the Population Health Improvement Fund; AND
8 214. THE AGING RESILIENCE FUND.
9 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
10 1, 2026.
