6 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
7 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
8 Article – Public Safety
9 14–110.5.
10 (a) (1) In this section the following words have the meanings indicated.
11 (2) “Fund” means the State Disaster Recovery Fund.
12 (3) “Local Disaster Declaration” means a formal request for State disaster
13 relief and recovery assistance made as a result of an emergency by the senior elected official
14 through the local organization for emergency management.
15 (f) (1) The Governor may include in the annual budget bill an appropriation
16 to the Fund.
17 (2) The Fund shall consist of:
18 (i) money appropriated in the State budget to the Fund;
19 (ii) money transferred to the Fund from:
20 1. the Catastrophic Event Account in accordance with §
21 7–324 of the State Finance and Procurement Article; AND
22 2. THE REVENUE STABILIZATION ACCOUNT IN
23 ACCORDANCE WITH § 7–311 OF THE STATE FINANCE AND PROCUREMENT ARTICLE;
24 (iii) repayments of principal and interest from loans made from the
25 Fund;
26 (iv) reimbursements from the federal government or other legal
27 entities for disaster recovery assistance expenditures made from the Fund;
28 (v) interest earnings of the Fund; and
29 (vi) any other money from any other source accepted for the benefit
30 of the Fund.

5 (g) (1) (I) Unless the transfer would result in an Account balance below 5%
6 of the estimated General Fund revenues for the fiscal year in which the transfer is made,
7 if authorized by an act of the General Assembly or specifically authorized in the State
8 budget bill as enacted, the Governor may transfer funds from the Account to General Fund
9 revenues as necessary to support the operation of State government on a temporary basis.
10 [(2)] (II) If the transfer would result in an Account balance below 5% of
11 the estimated General Fund revenues for the fiscal year in which the transfer is made, the
12 Governor may transfer funds from the Account to General Fund revenues only if the
13 transfer is authorized by an act of the General Assembly other than the State budget bill.
