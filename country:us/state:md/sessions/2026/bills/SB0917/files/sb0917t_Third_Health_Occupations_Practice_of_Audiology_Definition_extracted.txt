2 SENATE BILL 917
1 (a) In this title the following words have the meanings indicated.
2 (b) “Audiologist” means an individual who practices audiology.
3 (q) (1) “Practice audiology” means to:
4 (i) Evaluate, diagnose, manage, and treat auditory or vestibular
5 conditions in the human ear;
6 (ii) Prescribe, order, sell, dispense, or fit hearing aids to an
7 individual for the correction or relief of a condition for which hearing aids are worn;
8 (iii) Prescribe, order, sell, dispense, or externally fit a sound
9 processor to an osseo–integrated device for the correction or relief of a condition for which
10 osseo–integrated devices are worn; and
11 (iv) Prescribe, order, sell, dispense, or externally fit a sound
12 processor to a cochlear implant for the correction or relief of a condition for which cochlear
13 implants are worn.
14 (2) “Practice audiology” includes:
15 (i) The conducting of health screenings RELATED TO AUDITORY
16 OR VESTIBULAR CONDITIONS IN THE HUMAN EAR;
17 (ii) The removal of a foreign body from the external auditory canal
18 that is not impacted to the point it requires anesthesia;
19 (iii) The removal of cerumen from the external auditory canal that is
20 not impacted to the point it requires anesthesia;
21 (iv) The ordering of cultures and bloodwork testing as it relates to
22 the auditory or vestibular conditions in the human ear;
23 (v) The ordering and performing of in–office, nonradiographic
24 scanning or imaging of the external auditory canal AS IT RELATES TO THE AUDITORY OR
25 VESTIBULAR CONDITIONS IN THE HUMAN EAR; and
26 (vi) The ordering of radiographic imaging as it relates to the auditory
27 or vestibular conditions in the human ear.
28 (3) “Practice audiology” does not include:
29 (i) Surgery using an instrument, including a laser, a scalpel, a
30 needle, cautery, a cryoprobe, or a suture, in which human tissue is cut, burned, vaporized,
