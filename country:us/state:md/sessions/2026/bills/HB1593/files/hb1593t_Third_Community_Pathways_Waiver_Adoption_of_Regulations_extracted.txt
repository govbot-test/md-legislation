CHAPTER ______
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Developmental Disabilities Administration – Self–Directed Services Manual –
3 Public Comment
4 Community Pathways Waiver – Adoption of Regulations
5 FOR the purpose of requiring the Deputy Secretary for Developmental Disabilities to
6 provide notice to the public before making changes to the Self–Directed Services
7 Manual; requiring the Deputy Secretary to receive and consider comments on the
8 proposed changes from the public for at least a certain period; prohibiting the Deputy
9 Secretary from adopting the proposed changes until the public comment period has
10 ended; and generally relating to the Self–Directed Services Manual the Secretary of
11 Health to adopt regulations governing the Community Pathways Waiver, including
12 regulations pertaining to the delivery of self–directed services; and generally relating
13 to the Community Pathways Waiver.
14 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
15 Article – Health – General
16 Section 7–101(a), (b), (f), and (w), and (x)
17 Annotated Code of Maryland
18 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
19 BY adding to
20 Article – Health – General

2 HOUSE BILL 1593
1 Section 7–409.1
2 Annotated Code of Maryland
3 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
4 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
5 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
6 Article – Health – General
7 7–101.
8 (a) In this title the following words have the meanings indicated.
9 (b) “Administration” means the Developmental Disabilities Administration.
10 (f) “Deputy Secretary” means the Deputy Secretary for Developmental
11 Disabilities.
12 (w) (1) “Self–directed services” means waiver program services:
13 (i) For which participants or their representatives have
14 decision–making authority over and take direct responsibility for management of the
15 services with the assistance of available supports chosen by the participant or the
16 participant’s representative; and
17 (ii) That are provided in a manner that:
18 1. Furthers the right of individuals and their
19 representatives, as applicable, to make choices about and direct all aspects of their lives,
20 including by choosing and controlling the delivery of waiver services, who provides the
21 services, and how services are provided; and
22 2. Recognizes that all individuals have the capacity to make
23 choices and may be supported in making choices.
24 (2) “Self–directed services” includes services that:
25 (i) Provide an individual with:
26 1. The decision–making employer authority to recruit, hire,
27 train, supervise, and dismiss the employees and vendors who furnish the individual’s
28 services; and
29 2. The budget authority over how funds in a budget are spent
30 within the total approved annual budget;
HOUSE BILL 1593 3
1 (ii) Maximize the opportunities of an individual to live as
2 independently as possible in the most inclusive community–based setting of the
3 individual’s choice;
4 (iii) Empower an individual to exercise choice and control over
5 needed long–term services and supports; and
6 (iv) Help to maintain and improve the individual’s health and quality
7 of life in the individual’s community.
8 (x) “Services” means residential, day, or other services that provide for
9 evaluation, diagnosis, treatment, care, supervision, assistance, or attention to individuals
10 with developmental disability and that promote habilitation of these individuals.
11 7–409.1.
12 (A) THE DEPUTY SECRETARY SHALL PROVIDE NOTICE TO THE PUBLIC
13 BEFORE MAKING CHANGES TO THE SELF–DIRECTED SERVICES MANUAL
14 DEVELOPED BY THE ADMINISTRATION.
15 (B) IF THE DEPUTY SECRETARY PROPOSES TO MAKE CHANGES TO THE
16 SELF–DIRECTED SERVICES MANUAL, THE DEPUTY SECRETARY SHALL RECEIVE
17 AND CONSIDER COMMENTS ON THE PROPOSED CHANGES FROM THE PUBLIC FOR A
18 PERIOD OF AT LEAST 90 DAYS AFTER THE DATE NOTICE IS PROVIDED TO THE PUBLIC
19 UNDER SUBSECTION (A) OF THIS SECTION.
20 (C) THE DEPUTY SECRETARY MAY NOT ADOPT THE PROPOSED CHANGES
21 UNTIL AFTER THE PUBLIC COMMENT PERIOD REQUIRED UNDER SUBSECTION (B) OF
22 THIS SECTION HAS ENDED. THE SECRETARY SHALL ADOPT REGULATIONS
23 GOVERNING THE COMMUNITY PATHWAYS WAIVER, INCLUDING REGULATIONS
24 PERTAINING TO THE DELIVERY OF SELF–DIRECTED SERVICES.
25 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
26 October 1, 2026.
Approved:
