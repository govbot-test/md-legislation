2 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
3 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
4 Article – Commercial Law
5 13–301.
6 Unfair, abusive, or deceptive trade practices include any:
7 (14) Violation of a provision of:
8 (xlviii) Section 13–411.1(c)(2) of the Transportation Article; [or]
9 (15) Act or omission that relates to a residential building and that is
10 chargeable as a misdemeanor under or otherwise violates a provision of the Energy
11 Conservation Building Standards Act, Title 7, Subtitle 4 of the Public Utilities Article; OR
12 (16) FAILURE TO DISCLOSE THAT THE PRICE OF A CONSUMER GOOD
13 INCLUDES A TARIFF IMPOSED ON THE CONSUMER GOOD OR ON COMPONENTS OF
14 THE CONSUMER GOOD AND THE AMOUNT OF THE TARIFF.
15 Article – Economic Development
16 2.5–111.
17 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
18 INDICATED.
19 (2) “LOCAL SOURCE” MEANS A BUSINESS ENTITY THAT:
20 (I) IS HEADQUARTERED IN THE STATE; OR
21 (II) HAS A PRINCIPAL PLACE OF BUSINESS LOCATED IN THE
22 STATE.
23 (3) “PORTAL” MEANS THE MARYLAND LOCAL SOURCING PORTAL.
24 (4) “TARIFF–IMPACTED GOODS” MEANS COMMODITIES, SUPPLIES,
25 OR RAW MATERIALS IDENTIFIED BY THE SECRETARY AS SUBJECT TO FEDERAL
26 IMPORT DUTIES OR TRADE VOLATILITY.
27 (B) (1) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL DEVELOP AND MAINTAIN A
28 SEARCHABLE ONLINE DATABASE, TO BE KNOWN AS THE MARYLAND LOCAL

1 SOURCING PORTAL, TO CONNECT BUSINESSES IN THE STATE WITH LOCAL SOURCES
2 FOR TARIFF–IMPACTED GOODS AND OTHER SUPPLIES.
3 (2) THE PORTAL SHALL BE SEARCHABLE BY NORTH AMERICAN
4 INDUSTRY CLASSIFICATION SYSTEM CODES.
5 (3) THE PORTAL SHALL COMPLY WITH:
6 (I) STATE AND FEDERAL ACCESSIBILITY STANDARDS FOR
7 DIGITAL CONTENT; AND
8 (II) THE DATA MINIMIZATION AND SECURITY STANDARDS OF
9 THE MARYLAND ONLINE DATA PRIVACY ACT.
10 (C) (1) PARTICIPATION IN THE PORTAL BY A LOCAL SOURCE SHALL BE
11 VOLUNTARY.
12 (2) THE PORTAL SHALL ENABLE A LOCAL SOURCE TO LIST:
13 (I) THE CATEGORIES OF SUPPLIES OR INVENTORY HELD IN THE
14 STATE; AND
15 (II) A STATUS INDICATOR SIGNIFYING THAT INVENTORY IS
16 CURRENTLY AVAILABLE FOR PURCHASE.
17 (3) THE PORTAL IS NOT REQUIRED TO DISPLAY SPECIFIC
18 QUANTITIES OR PROPRIETARY STOCK LEVELS.
19 (D) THE DEPARTMENT MAY COORDINATE WITH THE STATE DEPARTMENT
20 OF ASSESSMENTS AND TAXATION, THE OFFICE OF THE COMPTROLLER, AND THE
21 DEPARTMENT OF INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY TO:
22 (1) IDENTIFY POTENTIAL LOCAL SOURCES FOR INCLUSION IN THE
23 PORTAL; AND
24 (2) LEVERAGE EXISTING DIGITAL INFRASTRUCTURE TO MINIMIZE
25 THE COST OF DEVELOPING AND MAINTAINING THE PORTAL.
26 (E) THE STATE, THE DEPARTMENT, AND THEIR EMPLOYEES SHALL BE
27 HELD HARMLESS FOR ANY CLAIMS, DAMAGES, OR LOSSES ARISING FROM:
28 (1) THE ACCURACY OR TIMELINESS OF DATA SUBMITTED BY A
29 VOLUNTARY PARTICIPANT; OR

1 (2) ANY TRANSACTION OR AGREEMENT ENTERED INTO BETWEEN
2 PRIVATE PARTIES USING THE PORTAL.
3 (F) ON OR BEFORE DECEMBER 31 EACH YEAR, THE DEPARTMENT SHALL
4 REPORT TO THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY, IN ACCORDANCE WITH § 2–1257 OF THE
5 STATE GOVERNMENT ARTICLE, ON THE USE AND EFFECTIVENESS OF THE PORTAL.
6 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
7 October 1, 2026. It shall remain effective for a period of 5 years and, at the end of September
8 30, 2031, this Act, with no further action required by the General Assembly, shall be
9 abrogated and of no further force and effect.
