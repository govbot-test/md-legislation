A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Consumer Protection – Disclosure of Tariffs and Local Sourcing
3 FOR the purpose of prohibiting a person from not making a certain disclosure on the price
4 of a consumer good; requiring the Department of Commerce to develop and maintain
5 a certain database to connect businesses in the State with local sources for supplies
6 and inventory; and generally relating to the establishment of the Maryland Local
7 Sourcing Portal.

11 Article – Economic Development
12 2.5–111.
13 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS
14 INDICATED.
15 (2) “LOCAL SOURCE” MEANS A BUSINESS ENTITY THAT:
16 (I) IS HEADQUARTERED IN THE STATE; OR
17 (II) HAS A PRINCIPAL PLACE OF BUSINESS LOCATED IN THE
18 STATE.
19 (3) “PORTAL” MEANS THE MARYLAND LOCAL SOURCING PORTAL.
20 (4) “TARIFF–IMPACTED GOODS” MEANS COMMODITIES, SUPPLIES,
21 OR RAW MATERIALS IDENTIFIED BY THE SECRETARY AS SUBJECT TO FEDERAL
22 IMPORT DUTIES OR TRADE VOLATILITY.
23 (B) (1) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL DEVELOP AND MAINTAIN A
24 SEARCHABLE ONLINE DATABASE, TO BE KNOWN AS THE MARYLAND LOCAL
25 SOURCING PORTAL, TO CONNECT BUSINESSES IN THE STATE WITH LOCAL SOURCES
26 FOR TARIFF–IMPACTED GOODS AND OTHER SUPPLIES.
27 (2) THE PORTAL SHALL BE SEARCHABLE BY NORTH AMERICAN
28 INDUSTRY CLASSIFICATION SYSTEM CODES.

1 (3) THE PORTAL SHALL COMPLY WITH:
2 (I) STATE AND FEDERAL ACCESSIBILITY STANDARDS FOR
3 DIGITAL CONTENT; AND
4 (II) THE DATA MINIMIZATION AND SECURITY STANDARDS OF
5 THE MARYLAND ONLINE DATA PRIVACY ACT.
6 (C) (1) PARTICIPATION IN THE PORTAL BY A LOCAL SOURCE SHALL BE
7 VOLUNTARY.
8 (2) THE PORTAL SHALL ENABLE A LOCAL SOURCE TO LIST:
9 (I) THE CATEGORIES OF SUPPLIES OR INVENTORY HELD IN THE
10 STATE; AND
11 (II) A STATUS INDICATOR SIGNIFYING THAT INVENTORY IS
12 CURRENTLY AVAILABLE FOR PURCHASE.
13 (3) THE PORTAL IS NOT REQUIRED TO DISPLAY SPECIFIC
14 QUANTITIES OR PROPRIETARY STOCK LEVELS.
15 (D) THE DEPARTMENT MAY COORDINATE WITH THE STATE DEPARTMENT
16 OF ASSESSMENTS AND TAXATION, THE OFFICE OF THE COMPTROLLER, AND THE
17 DEPARTMENT OF INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY TO:
18 (1) IDENTIFY POTENTIAL LOCAL SOURCES FOR INCLUSION IN THE
19 PORTAL; AND
20 (2) LEVERAGE EXISTING DIGITAL INFRASTRUCTURE TO MINIMIZE
21 THE COST OF DEVELOPING AND MAINTAINING THE PORTAL.
22 (E) THE STATE, THE DEPARTMENT, AND THEIR EMPLOYEES SHALL BE
23 HELD HARMLESS FOR ANY CLAIMS, DAMAGES, OR LOSSES ARISING FROM:
24 (1) THE ACCURACY OR TIMELINESS OF DATA SUBMITTED BY A
25 VOLUNTARY PARTICIPANT; OR
26 (2) ANY TRANSACTION OR AGREEMENT ENTERED INTO BETWEEN
27 PRIVATE PARTIES USING THE PORTAL.
