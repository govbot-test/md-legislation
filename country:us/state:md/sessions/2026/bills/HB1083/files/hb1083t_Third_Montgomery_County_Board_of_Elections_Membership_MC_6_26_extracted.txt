2 Montgomery County Board of Elections – Membership
3 MC 6–26
4 FOR the purpose of altering the membership of the Montgomery County Board of Elections;
5 altering the votes required for the county board to appoint an election director; and
6 generally relating to the Montgomery County Board of Elections.
7 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
8 Article – Election Law
9 Section 2–201, 2–202(e), and 11–301(f)
10 Annotated Code of Maryland
11 (2022 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)
12 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
13 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:
14 Article – Election Law
15 2–201.
16 (a) (1) There is a county board of elections in each county of the State.
17 (2) Each local board and its staff is subject to the direction and authority
18 of the State Board and is accountable to the State Board for its actions in all matters

2 law.
3 (b) (1) (i) Except as provided in paragraphs (2) and (3) of this subsection,
4 each local board consists of five regular members.
5 (ii) Three regular members shall be of the majority party, and two
6 regular members shall be of the principal minority party.
7 (2) (i) In Prince George’s County, the local board consists of five regular
8 members and three substitute members.
9 (ii) Four regular members and two substitute members shall be of
10 the majority party, and one regular member and one substitute member shall be of the
11 principal minority party.
12 (3) (i) In Montgomery County, the local board consists of [five] SEVEN
13 regular members [and two substitute members].
14 (ii) [Three] FOUR regular members [and one substitute member]
15 shall be of the majority party, and [two] THREE regular members [and one substitute
16 member] shall be of the principal minority party.
17 (c) Each regular member of a local board and each substitute member of a local
18 board shall:
19 (1) be appointed in accordance with subsection (g) of this section;
20 (2) be a registered voter in the county for which the individual is appointed
21 for the 5 years immediately preceding the appointment; and
22 (3) be eligible for reappointment.
23 (d) (1) The term of a member is 4 years and begins on the first Monday in June
24 of each year following a gubernatorial election.
25 (2) At the end of a term, a member continues to serve until a successor is
26 appointed and qualifies.
27 (e) Before taking office, a member shall take and subscribe to the oath prescribed
28 in Article I, § 9 of the Maryland Constitution.
29 (f) The Governor may remove a member for incompetence, misconduct, or other
30 good cause, upon written charges stating the Governor’s grounds for dismissal and after
31 affording the member notice and an ample opportunity to be heard.
HOUSE BILL 1083 3
1 (g) (1) The Governor shall request the county central committee representing
2 the majority party or the principal minority party, as appropriate, to submit a list of at
3 least four eligible individuals from which the Governor may make an appointment of a
4 regular member of a local board or a substitute member of the local board.
5 (2) The Governor may reject all of the nominees if the Governor determines
6 them to be unfit or incompetent, in which case the Governor shall notify the State Board in
7 writing and request an additional list of at least four eligible nominees from the county
8 central committee. A third list may be requested in the same manner.
9 (3) If a list containing the names of four eligible nominees is not submitted
10 within 20 days of a request or if all the nominees on three lists are rejected, the Governor
11 may appoint any eligible person who is a member of the appropriate political party.
12 (4) (i) Except as provided in subparagraph (ii) of this paragraph, each
13 appointment shall be subject to confirmation by the Senate of Maryland.
14 (ii) In Caroline, Dorchester, and Kent counties, if there is no resident
15 Senator of the particular county, the confirmation required under subparagraph (i) of this
16 paragraph shall be by the House of Delegates of Maryland.
17 (iii) If an appointee is rejected, the Governor shall make another
18 appointment from the list or lists submitted under paragraphs (1) and (2) of this subsection.
19 If a list is not provided, or the nominees on three lists are rejected, the Governor may
20 appoint an eligible individual as provided in paragraph (3) of this subsection.
21 (h) (1) Except in [Montgomery County and] Prince George’s County, if a
22 vacancy occurs on the local board, the Governor shall appoint an eligible person from the
23 same political party as the predecessor member to fill the vacancy in accordance with
24 subsection (g) of this section for the remainder of the unexpired term and until a successor
25 is appointed and qualifies.
26 (2) An appointment made while the General Assembly is not in session
27 shall be considered temporary until the appointee is confirmed by the General Assembly.
28 (i) (1) This subsection applies only in [Montgomery County and] Prince
29 George’s County.
30 (2) In the event of the absence of a regular member or a vacancy in the
31 office of a regular member, the substitute member of the same political party shall exercise
32 the powers and duties of a regular member until the regular member returns or the vacancy
33 is filled as prescribed in this subsection.
34 (3) Subject to paragraph (4) of this subsection, if a member of a local board
35 dies, resigns, is removed, or becomes ineligible:
4 HOUSE BILL 1083
1 (i) the substitute member belonging to the same political party shall
2 become a regular member of the local board; and
3 (ii) the Governor shall appoint an eligible person from the same
4 political party to be the new substitute member.
5 (4) [In Prince George’s County, if] IF a vacancy occurs on the local board
6 among the members from the majority party, the Governor shall designate one of the
7 substitute members from that party to fill the vacancy.
8 (5) If a substitute member of a local board becomes a regular member as
9 provided in paragraph (3)(i) of this subsection, dies, resigns, is removed, or becomes
10 ineligible when the confirming legislative body is not in session, the Governor shall appoint
11 an eligible person from the same political party as the predecessor substitute member to
12 fill the vacancy. That individual shall serve until the earlier of:
13 (i) the adjournment of the next session of the General Assembly; or
14 (ii) the appointment of another individual to fill the same vacancy.
15 (j) A board shall meet within 20 days after the beginning of the term to elect one
16 of its regular members as president.
17 2–202.
18 (e) In Montgomery County, the local board shall require an affirmative vote of
19 not fewer than [three] FOUR duly confirmed regular members of the local board, at least
20 one of whom shall be a member of the principal minority party, to appoint an election
21 director.
22 11–301.
23 (f) In [Montgomery County and] Prince George’s County, if a member is not
24 present at the scheduled time for vote canvassing, a substitute member of the board of
25 canvassers may be sworn in.
26 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That each substitute member of
27 the Montgomery County Board of Elections in office on the effective date of this Act shall
28 become a regular member on the effective date of this Act for the remainder of the member’s
29 term and until a successor is appointed and qualifies.
30 SECTION 3. 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
31 June 1, 2026 7, 2027.
