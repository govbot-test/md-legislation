By: Delegates Kaiser, Kaufman, Palakovich Carr, Rose, Watson, and Wims
Introduced and read first time: February 12, 2026
Assigned to: Government, Labor, and Elections
A BILL ENTITLED
1 AN ACT concerning
2 Department of Information Technology – Recruitment, Retention, and
3 Compensation of Information Technology and Cybersecurity Professionals –
4 Independent Study
5 FOR the purpose of requiring the Department of Information Technology to procure the
6 services of a third–party entity to conduct a certain independent study; and generally
7 relating to the recruitment, retention, and compensation of information technology
8 and cybersecurity professionals in the State.
9 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
10 That:
11 (a) The Department of Information Technology shall procure the services of a
12 third–party entity to conduct an independent study that includes:
13 (1) a comparison of compensation of information technology and
14 cybersecurity professionals at State agencies with other comparable positions:
15 (i) at federal and local levels; and
16 (ii) in the private sector;
17 (2) an evaluation of the impact of salary adjustments and any other
18 compensation benefits or incentives offered by the State for information technology and
19 cybersecurity professionals; and
20 (3) recommendations for improving recruitment and retention of the State
21 technology and cybersecurity workforce.
EXPLANATION: CAPITALS INDICATE MATTER ADDED TO EXISTING LAW.
[Brackets] indicate matter deleted from exist in g l aw . *hb1335*
2 HOUSE BILL 1335
1 (b) On or before November 1, 2027, the Department of Information Technology
2 shall submit the study under subsection (a) of this section to the Governor and, in
3 accordance with § 2–1257 of the State Government Article, the General Assembly.
4 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
5 1, 2026. It shall remain effective for a period of 2 years and, at the end of June 30, 2028,
6 this Act, with no further action required by the General Assembly, shall be abrogated and
7 of no further force and effect.
